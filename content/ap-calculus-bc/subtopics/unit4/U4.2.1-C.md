A conical tank with height 6 m and top radius 3 m is being filled with water at a rate of $2$ m³/min. When the water height is $4$ m, at what rate is the water level rising?

## Options
A) $\frac{1}{2\pi}$ m/min

B) $\frac{1}{\pi}$ m/min

C) $\frac{2}{\pi}$ m/min

D) $\frac{1}{4\pi}$ m/min

## Answer
A

## Explanation
By similar triangles, $r=\frac{h}{2}$. Volume $V=\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2h=\frac{1}{3}\pi(\frac{h}{2})^2h=\frac{\pi h^3}{12}$. Differentiate: $\frac{dV}{dt}=\frac{\pi h^2}{4}\frac{dh}{dt}$. Given $\frac{dV}{dt}=2$ and $h=4$: $2=\frac{\pi(16)}{4}\frac{dh}{dt}=4\pi\frac{dh}{dt}$. So $\frac{dh}{dt}=\frac{2}{4\pi}=\frac{1}{2\pi}$ m/min. Option B forgets the similar triangle ratio. Option C miscalculates the volume derivative. Option D has an extra factor error.
