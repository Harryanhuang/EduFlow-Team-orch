The radius of a circle is increasing at a rate of $2$ cm/s. At what rate is the area increasing when the radius is $5$ cm?

## Options
A) $10\pi$ cm²/s

B) $20\pi$ cm²/s

C) $25\pi$ cm²/s

D) $4\pi$ cm²/s

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$A=\pi r^2$. Differentiate with respect to $t$: $\frac{dA}{dt}=2\pi r\frac{dr}{dt}$. Given $\frac{dr}{dt}=2$ and $r=5$: $\frac{dA}{dt}=2\pi(5)(2)=20\pi$ cm²/s. Option A uses only one factor of $r$ or $\frac{dr}{dt}$. Option C computes the area, not its rate of change. Option D forgets the radius.
