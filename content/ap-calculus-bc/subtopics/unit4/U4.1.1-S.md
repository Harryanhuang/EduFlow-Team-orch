The graph of $f$ is increasing and concave down on the interval $[a,b]$. Which must be true?

## Options
A) The average rate of change on $[a,b]$ equals $f'(a)$.

B) The average rate of change on $[a,b]$ is greater than $f'(b)$.

C) The average rate of change on $[a,b]$ is less than $f'(a)$ but greater than $f'(b)$.

D) The average rate of change on $[a,b]$ is greater than both $f'(a)$ and $f'(b)$.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
For an increasing, concave down function, the slope of the tangent line decreases as $x$ increases. The secant slope (average rate of change) lies between the tangent slopes at the endpoints: $f'(b)<\text{average}<f'(a)$. Option A is generally false. Option B reverses the inequality. Option D is wrong because the average lies between, not above both.
