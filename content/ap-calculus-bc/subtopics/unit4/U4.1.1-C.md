The position of a particle is given by $s(t)=t^3-6t^2+9t$. What is the average velocity over the time interval during which the instantaneous velocity is zero?

## Options
A) $-2$

B) $0$

C) $3$

D) $9$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Instantaneous velocity $v(t)=s'(t)=3t^2-12t+9=3(t-1)(t-3)$. It is zero at $t=1$ and $t=3$. Average velocity over $[1,3]$ is $\frac{s(3)-s(1)}{3-1}=\frac{0-4}{2}=-2$. Option B confuses average velocity with the fact that $v=0$ at the endpoints. Option C is $v(2)=-3$ miscomputed. Option D is $v(0)=9$.
