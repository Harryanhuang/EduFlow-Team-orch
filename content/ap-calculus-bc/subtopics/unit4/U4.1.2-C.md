A particle moves along a line with velocity $v(t)=3t^2-12t+9$ for $0\leq t\leq5$. At what time in this interval is the speed of the particle the greatest?

## Options
A) $t=0$

B) $t=2$

C) $t=3$

D) $t=5$

## Answer
D

## Explanation
Speed is $|v(t)|$. $v(t)=3(t-1)(t-3)$, with zeros at $t=1$ and $t=3$. Critical values of velocity occur at endpoints $t=0,5$ and at $t=1,3$ (where speed is 0). Evaluate: $|v(0)|=9$, $|v(1)|=0$, $|v(2)|=|12-24+9|=3$, $|v(3)|=0$, $|v(5)|=|75-60+9|=24$. The greatest speed is $24$ at $t=5$. Option A has speed 9. Option B is a local minimum of speed. Option C has speed 0.
