At noon, two ships are at the same point. Ship A sails east at 15 km/h and Ship B sails north at 20 km/h. At what rate is the distance between the ships changing at 2 PM?

## Options
A) $5$ km/h

B) $25$ km/h

C) $35$ km/h

D) $625$ km/h

## Answer
B

## Explanation
After $t$ hours, the horizontal separation is $15t$ km and the vertical separation is $20t$ km. The distance between them is $D=\sqrt{(15t)^2+(20t)^2}=\sqrt{625t^2}=25t$. Therefore $\frac{dD}{dt}=25$ km/h for all $t$, including at 2 PM. Option A is the speed difference. Option C is the sum of speeds. Option D is $D^2$ at $t=1$ misused.
