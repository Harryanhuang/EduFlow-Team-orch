What is $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{x^2}{e^x}$?

## Options
A) $2$

B) $0$

C) $\infty$

D) $1$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
As $x\to\infty$, this is $\frac{\infty}{\infty}$. Apply L'Hôpital's Rule: $\lim\frac{2x}{e^x}$, still $\frac{\infty}{\infty}$. Apply again: $\lim\frac{2}{e^x}=0$. Option A is the limit of the second numerator. Option C is incorrect because exponential growth dominates polynomial growth. Option D is not justified.
