The cost $C$ (in dollars) of producing $x$ units is $C(x)=500+10x+0.01x^2$. If production is increasing at a rate of $35$ units per week when $x=1000$, at what rate is the cost increasing?

## Options
A) $500$ dollars/week

B) $700$ dollars/week

C) $1050$ dollars/week

D) $30000$ dollars/week

## Answer
C

## Explanation
$\frac{dC}{dt}=C'(x)\frac{dx}{dt}=(10+0.02x)(35)$. At $x=1000$: $\frac{dC}{dt}=(10+20)(35)=30\cdot35=1050$ dollars/week. Option A uses only $C'(1000)=30$ and multiplies by wrong rate. Option B is $20\times35$ (ignoring constant marginal cost). Option D uses total cost $C(1000)$ instead of rate.
