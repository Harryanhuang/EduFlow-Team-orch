A spherical balloon is being inflated so that its volume increases at a rate of $100$ cm³/s. When the radius is $5$ cm, at what rate is the radius increasing?

## Options
A) $\frac{1}{\pi}$ cm/s

B) $\frac{1}{4\pi}$ cm/s

C) $\frac{4}{\pi}$ cm/s

D) $\frac{1}{5\pi}$ cm/s

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$V=\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$. Differentiate: $\frac{dV}{dt}=4\pi r^2\frac{dr}{dt}$. Given $\frac{dV}{dt}=100$ and $r=5$: $100=4\pi(25)\frac{dr}{dt}=100\pi\frac{dr}{dt}$. So $\frac{dr}{dt}=\frac{1}{\pi}$ cm/s. Option B has an extra factor of $\frac{1}{4}$. Option C inverts the formula. Option D uses the wrong radius relationship.
