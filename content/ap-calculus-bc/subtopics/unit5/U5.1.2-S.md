What is the absolute maximum value of $f(x)=x^3-3x+2$ on $[-2,2]$?

## Options
A) $4$

B) $2$

C) $0$

D) $-2$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$f'(x)=3x^2-3=3(x-1)(x+1)$, zeros at $x=\pm1$. Evaluate: $f(-2)=-8+6+2=0$, $f(-1)=1-3(-1)+2=4$, $f(1)=1-3+2=0$, $f(2)=8-6+2=4$. The absolute maximum is $4$ (attained at both $x=-1$ and $x=2$). Option B is $f(0)=2$, a local minimum. Option C is the local min values. Option D is not attained.
