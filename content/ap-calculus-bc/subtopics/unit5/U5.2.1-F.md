For which interval is $f(x)=x^3-3x^2+2$ decreasing?

## Options
A) $(-\infty,0)$

B) $(0,2)$

C) $(2,\infty)$

D) $(-\infty,1)$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$f'(x)=3x^2-6x=3x(x-2)$. This is negative when $x\in(0,2)$ (between the roots). So $f$ is decreasing on $(0,2)$. Option A: $f'>0$ here ($f$ increasing). Option C: $f'>0$ here. Option D: $f'>0$ for $x<0$ and $f'<0$ for $0<x<1$, so not monotone on this entire interval.
