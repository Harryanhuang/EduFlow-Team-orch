The function $g$ is continuous on $[0,2]$, with $g(0)=1$ and $g(2)=5$. What is the strongest conclusion guaranteed by the Extreme Value Theorem?

## Options
A) There exists $c\in(0,2)$ with $g(c)=3$.

B) $g$ attains a maximum and minimum on $[0,2]$.

C) There exists $c\in(0,2)$ with $g'(c)=2$.

D) $g$ is differentiable on $(0,2)$.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
EVT guarantees that a continuous function on a closed interval attains both its maximum and minimum values. Option A is guaranteed by IVT (intermediate value), not EVT. Option C is guaranteed by MVT, not EVT. Option D is not guaranteed by any theorem — continuity does not imply differentiability.
