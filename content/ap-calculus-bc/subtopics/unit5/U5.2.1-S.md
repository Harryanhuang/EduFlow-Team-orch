Let $f(x)=x^4-4x^3+4x^2$. Which statement is true about the critical points?

## Options
A) Local maximum at $x=0$ and local minimum at $x=2$.

B) Local maximum at $x=1$ and local minima at $x=0$ and $x=2$.

C) No local extrema.

D) Local minimum at $x=1$ only.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$f'(x)=4x^3-12x^2+8x=4x(x^2-3x+2)=4x(x-1)(x-2)$. Critical points at $x=0,1,2$. Sign analysis: $x<0$: $f'<0$; $0<x<1$: $f'>0$; $1<x<2$: $f'<0$; $x>2$: $f'>0$. So: $x=0$: $f'$ goes $-\to+$, local min. $x=1$: $f'$ goes $+\to-$, local max. $x=2$: $f'$ goes $-\to+$, local min. Option A reverses the extrema. Option C is wrong — there are extrema. Option D misses two critical points.
