What is $\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{3x^2 + 1}{x^2 - 5}$?

## Options
A) 0
B) 1
C) 3
D) The limit does not exist.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
When $x \to \infty$, divide numerator and denominator by the highest power of $x$ (which is $x^2$):
$\frac{3x^2+1}{x^2-5} = \frac{3 + 1/x^2}{1 - 5/x^2} \to \frac{3+0}{1-0} = 3$.
When degrees are equal, the limit is the ratio of leading coefficients: 3/1 = 3. Option A (0) would be correct if the numerator had lower degree. Option B (1) is the ratio of the $x^2$ coefficients only if both were 1. Option D is wrong — the limit exists and equals 3.
