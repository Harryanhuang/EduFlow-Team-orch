What is $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos(2x)}{x^2}$?

## Options
A) 0
B) 1
C) 2
D) 4

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Using the identity $1 - \cos(2x) = 2\sin^2(x)$: $\frac{1-\cos(2x)}{x^2} = \frac{2\sin^2(x)}{x^2} = 2\left(\frac{\sin x}{x}\right)^2$. As $x \to 0$: $\frac{\sin x}{x} \to 1$, so $2(1)^2 = 2$. Option A (0) is $\lim (1-\cos x)/x$ (first power). Option B (1) is $\lim \sin(x)/x$. Option D (4) would be for $(1-\cos(4x))/x^2$ or similar.
