Which interval guarantees that $x^3 - x - 1 = 0$ has at least one solution?

## Options
A) $[0, 1]$
B) $[1, 2]$
C) $[-1, 0]$
D) $[2, 3]$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Let $g(x) = x^3 - x - 1$. This is a polynomial, hence continuous everywhere.
$g(1) = 1 - 1 - 1 = -1 < 0$.
$g(2) = 8 - 2 - 1 = 5 > 0$.
Since $g(1) < 0$ and $g(2) > 0$, and $g$ is continuous on $[1, 2]$, by IVT there exists $c \in (1, 2)$ such that $g(c) = 0$. Option A: $g(0) = -1$, $g(1) = -1$ — same sign, IVT doesn't apply. Option C: $g(-1) = -1$, $g(0) = -1$ — same sign. Option D: $g(2) = 5$, $g(3) = 23$ — same sign. Only interval $[1, 2]$ has a sign change.
