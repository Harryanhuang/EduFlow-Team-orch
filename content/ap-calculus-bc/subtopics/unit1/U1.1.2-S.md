What is $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{|x|}{x}$?

## Options
A) 0
B) 1
C) -1
D) The limit does not exist.

## Answer
D

## Explanation
From the right ($x > 0$): $|x| = x$, so $\frac{|x|}{x} = \frac{x}{x} = 1$. Thus $\lim_{x \to 0^+} \frac{|x|}{x} = 1$. From the left ($x < 0$): $|x| = -x$, so $\frac{|x|}{x} = \frac{-x}{x} = -1$. Thus $\lim_{x \to 0^-} \frac{|x|}{x} = -1$. Since $1 \neq -1$, the two-sided limit does not exist. Option A (0) would be correct for $\lim_{x \to 0} |x|/x$ if both sides agreed at 0. Option B (1) is the right limit only. Option C (-1) is the left limit only.
