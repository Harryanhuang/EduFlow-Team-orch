For what value of $k$ is the function $f(x) = \begin{cases} x^2 & x \leq 1 \\ kx + 2 & x > 1 \end{cases}$ continuous at $x = 1$?

## Options
A) $k = 1$
B) $k = -1$
C) $k = 2$
D) $k = 3$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For continuity at $x = 1$, we need: $\lim_{x\to1^-} f(x) = \lim_{x\to1^+} f(x) = f(1)$.
Left limit: $\lim_{x\to1^-} x^2 = 1$.
$f(1) = 1^2 = 1$ (using the $x \leq 1$ piece).
Right limit: $\lim_{x\to1^+} (kx+2) = k + 2$.
Setting right limit = left limit: $k + 2 = 1 \implies k = -1$.
Option A (k=1) gives right limit = 3, not 1. Option C (k=2) gives right limit = 4. Option D (k=3) gives right limit = 5. Only k = -1 makes the function continuous.
