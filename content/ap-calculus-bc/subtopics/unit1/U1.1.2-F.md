For the piecewise function $f(x) = \begin{cases} x+1 & x < 2 \\ 5 & x = 2 \\ 3x-1 & x > 2 \end{cases}$, what is $\lim_{x \to 2} f(x)$?

## Options
A) 3
B) 5
C) The limit does not exist.
D) 2

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Left limit: $\lim_{x \to 2^-} f(x) = \lim_{x \to 2^-} (x+1) = 3$. Right limit: $\lim_{x \to 2^+} f(x) = \lim_{x \to 2^+} (3x-1) = 5$. Since $3 \neq 5$, the two-sided limit does not exist. Option A (3) is only the left limit. Option B (5) is only the right limit (and equals $f(2)$). Option D (2) is the x-value, not the limit.
