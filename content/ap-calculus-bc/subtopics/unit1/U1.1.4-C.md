What is $\lim_{x \to 0} x \sin\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)$?

## Options
A) 0
B) 1
C) The limit does not exist (oscillation).
D) The limit does not exist (unbounded).

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Although $\sin(1/x)$ oscillates, it is bounded: $-1 \leq \sin(1/x) \leq 1$. Therefore $-|x| \leq x\sin(1/x) \leq |x|$. As $x \to 0$: $\lim_{x\to0}(-|x|) = 0$ and $\lim_{x\to0}|x| = 0$. By the Squeeze Theorem, $\lim_{x\to0} x\sin(1/x) = 0$. The oscillation is "squeezed out" because the amplitude $|x|$ shrinks to zero. Option B (1) is $\lim_{x\to0} \sin(x)/x$, a different limit. Option C is tempting — $\sin(1/x)$ alone has no limit, but the factor of $x$ forces convergence. Option D is wrong — the function is bounded.
