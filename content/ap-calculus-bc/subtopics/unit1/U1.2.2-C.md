What is $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan(5x)}{\sin(2x)}$?

## Options
A) $\frac{2}{5}$
B) 1
C) $\frac{5}{2}$
D) The limit does not exist.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Rewrite: $\frac{\tan(5x)}{\sin(2x)} = \frac{\tan(5x)}{5x} \cdot \frac{2x}{\sin(2x)} \cdot \frac{5x}{2x} = \frac{\tan(5x)}{5x} \cdot \frac{2x}{\sin(2x)} \cdot \frac{5}{2}$. As $x \to 0$: $\frac{\tan(5x)}{5x} \to 1$ and $\frac{2x}{\sin(2x)} \to 1$. So the limit is $1 \cdot 1 \cdot \frac{5}{2} = \frac{5}{2}$. Option A (2/5) inverts the ratio. Option B (1) ignores the coefficients. Option D is wrong — both numerator and denominator → 0, and the ratio has a finite limit.
