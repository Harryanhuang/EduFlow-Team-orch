Given $\lim_{x \to 3} f(x) = 4$ and $\lim_{x \to 3} g(x) = -2$, what is $\lim_{x \to 3} [f(x) \cdot g(x) + f(x)]$?

## Options
A) -10
B) -4
C) 2
D) 6

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Using limit laws: $\lim [f(x) \cdot g(x) + f(x)] = (\lim f(x))(\lim g(x)) + \lim f(x) = (4)(-2) + 4 = -8 + 4 = -4$. Option A (-10) uses wrong arithmetic. Option C (2) might compute fg - f = -8-4. Option D (6) adds incorrectly: 4 + (-2) + 4 = 6 but forgets multiplication.
