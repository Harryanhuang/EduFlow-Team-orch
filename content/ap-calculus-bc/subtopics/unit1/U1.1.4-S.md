Why does $\lim_{x \to 0} \sin\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)$ not exist?

## Options
A) The function grows without bound.
B) The function oscillates between -1 and 1 infinitely often as $x \to 0$.
C) The left and right limits are different finite numbers.
D) The function is not defined for any $x$ near 0.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
As $x \to 0$, $1/x \to \pm\infty$, and $\sin$ of a very large number oscillates between -1 and 1 without settling on any particular value. The function doesn't approach any single number — it keeps oscillating. This is different from growing unboundedly (A) — the function IS bounded. It's also different from left≠right (C) — both sides oscillate. Option D is wrong — $\sin(1/x)$ is defined for all $x \neq 0$.
