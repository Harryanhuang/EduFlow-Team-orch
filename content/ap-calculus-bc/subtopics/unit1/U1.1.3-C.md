If $2 - x^2 \leq f(x) \leq 2\cos(x)$ for all $x$ near 0, what is $\lim_{x \to 0} f(x)$?

## Options
A) 0
B) 1
C) 2
D) Cannot be determined.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Check both bounding functions: $\lim_{x \to 0} (2 - x^2) = 2 - 0 = 2$. $\lim_{x \to 0} 2\cos(x) = 2(1) = 2$. Since both bounds approach 2, by the Squeeze Theorem, $\lim_{x \to 0} f(x) = 2$. Option A (0) ignores the constant term. Option B (1) might evaluate $\cos(0) = 1$ without the factor of 2. Option D is wrong — the Squeeze Theorem gives a definite answer when both bounds agree.
