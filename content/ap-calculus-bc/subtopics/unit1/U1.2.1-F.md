What is $\lim_{x \to 3} \frac{x^2 - 9}{x - 3}$?

## Options
A) 0
B) 3
C) 6
D) The limit does not exist.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Direct substitution gives $\frac{9-9}{3-3} = \frac{0}{0}$ (indeterminate). Factor: $\frac{x^2-9}{x-3} = \frac{(x-3)(x+3)}{x-3} = x+3$ for $x \neq 3$. So $\lim_{x\to3} \frac{x^2-9}{x-3} = \lim_{x\to3}(x+3) = 6$. Option A (0) substitutes incorrectly. Option B (3) is the x-value. Option D is wrong — after simplification, the limit exists.
