The table shows values of $f(x)$ near $x = 3$:

| $x$ | 2.9 | 2.99 | 2.999 | 3.001 | 3.01 | 3.1 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $f(x)$ | 4.1 | 4.01 | 4.001 | 3.999 | 3.99 | 3.9 |

What is $\lim_{x \to 3} f(x)$?

## Options
A) 3.0
B) 3.9
C) 4.0
D) The limit does not exist.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
From the left ($x = 2.9, 2.99, 2.999$): $f(x) = 4.1, 4.01, 4.001 \to 4.0$. From the right ($x = 3.001, 3.01, 3.1$): $f(x) = 3.999, 3.99, 3.9 \to 4.0$. Both sides approach 4.0, so the limit is 4.0. Option A (3.0) confuses the $x$-value with the limit. Option B (3.9) is just the last right-side entry. Option D is wrong — both sides converge to the same value.
