What is $\lim_{x \to -\infty} \frac{2x + 1}{x^3 - 4}$?

## Options
A) 0
B) 2
C) $-\infty$
D) The limit does not exist.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The numerator has degree 1 and the denominator has degree 3. Since deg(numerator) < deg(denominator), the limit is 0 as $x \to -\infty$. Equivalently, divide by $x^3$: $\frac{2/x^2 + 1/x^3}{1 - 4/x^3} \to \frac{0+0}{1-0} = 0$. Option B (2) uses the leading coefficient of the numerator but ignores the degree difference. Option C ($-\infty$) would be correct if the numerator had higher degree. Option D is wrong — the limit exists and equals 0. The function has a horizontal asymptote at $y = 0$.
