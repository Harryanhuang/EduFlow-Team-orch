What is $\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^3 - 8}{x^2 - 4}$?

## Options
A) 0
B) 2
C) 3
D) 4

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Direct substitution: $\frac{8-8}{4-4} = \frac{0}{0}$. Factor numerator and denominator:
$x^3 - 8 = (x-2)(x^2 + 2x + 4)$
$x^2 - 4 = (x-2)(x+2)$
Cancel: $\frac{(x-2)(x^2+2x+4)}{(x-2)(x+2)} = \frac{x^2+2x+4}{x+2}$ for $x \neq 2$.
$\lim_{x\to2} \frac{x^2+2x+4}{x+2} = \frac{4+4+4}{4} = \frac{12}{4} = 3$. Option A (0) ignores the numerator. Option B (2) is the x-value. Option D (4) is just the denominator value at x=2.
