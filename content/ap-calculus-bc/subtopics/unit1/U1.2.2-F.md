What is $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin(3x)}{x}$?

## Options
A) 0
B) 1
C) 3
D) $\frac{1}{3}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Rewrite: $\frac{\sin(3x)}{x} = 3 \cdot \frac{\sin(3x)}{3x}$. Let $u = 3x$; as $x \to 0$, $u \to 0$. So $\lim_{x\to0} 3 \cdot \frac{\sin(3x)}{3x} = 3 \cdot \lim_{u\to0}\frac{\sin u}{u} = 3 \cdot 1 = 3$. Option A (0) would be correct if the numerator were $1-\cos(3x)$. Option B (1) is $\lim \sin(x)/x$. Option D (1/3) divides instead of multiplies.
