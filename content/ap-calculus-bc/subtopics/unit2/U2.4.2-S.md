| $x$ | $1.0$ | $1.5$ | $2.0$ | $2.5$ | $3.0$ |
|-----|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| $f(x)$ | $3.0$ | $4.2$ | $5.0$ | $5.3$ | $5.0$ |

Based on the table, which is the best estimate for $f'(2)$?

## Options
A) $\dfrac{5.0-3.0}{2.0-1.0}=2.0$

B) $\dfrac{5.3-4.2}{2.5-1.5}=1.1$

C) $\dfrac{5.0-4.2}{2.0-1.5}=1.6$

D) $\dfrac{5.0-5.0}{3.0-2.0}=0.0$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The best estimate uses the smallest symmetric interval around $x=2$: the average of the forward and backward difference. Option B uses $\frac{f(2.5)-f(1.5)}{2.5-1.5}=\frac{5.3-4.2}{1.0}=1.1$, which is a centered difference over the smallest available interval. Option A uses a wider interval (less accurate). Option C uses only the backward difference. Option D uses only the forward difference. The centered difference (B) is the most accurate estimate.
