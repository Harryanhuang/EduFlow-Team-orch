What is the equation of the tangent line to $y=x^3-2x$ at $x=1$?

## Options
A) $y=x-1$

B) $y=x-2$

C) $y=x$

D) $y=3x-4$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$f(1)=1^3-2(1)=-1$, so the point of tangency is $(1,-1)$. The derivative $f'(x)=3x^2-2$ gives $f'(1)=3-2=1$. Using point-slope form: $y-(-1)=1\cdot(x-1)$, so $y=x-2$. Option A has the wrong $y$-intercept. Option C misses the vertical shift. Option D uses $f'(1)=3$ incorrectly by forgetting the $-2$ term in the derivative.
