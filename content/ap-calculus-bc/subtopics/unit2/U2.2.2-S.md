What is $\dfrac{d}{dx}\left[2^x\right]$?

## Options
A) $x\cdot 2^{x-1}$

B) $2^x$

C) $2^x \ln 2$

D) $2^x \log 2$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
For $a^x$ where $a>0$ and $a\neq1$: $\frac{d}{dx}[a^x]=a^x\ln a$. With $a=2$: $\frac{d}{dx}[2^x]=2^x\ln 2$. Option A incorrectly applies the power rule (treating $x$ as a constant exponent). Option B omits the $\ln 2$ factor (that's only correct for base $e$). Option D uses $\log$ (base 10) instead of $\ln$ (natural log).
