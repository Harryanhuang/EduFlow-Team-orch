If $f(x)=x^2\sin x$, what is $f'(x)$?

## Options
A) $2x\cos x$

B) $2x\sin x + x^2\cos x$

C) $2x\sin x - x^2\cos x$

D) $x^2\cos x$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By the product rule: $f'(x)=(x^2)'\sin x+x^2(\sin x)'=2x\sin x+x^2\cos x$. Option A omits the second term of the product rule. Option C uses a minus sign (that's the quotient rule pattern). Option D only has the second term.
