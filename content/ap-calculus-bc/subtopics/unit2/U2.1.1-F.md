Using the limit definition, what is the derivative of $f(x) = x^2$?

## Options
A) $x$
B) $2x$
C) $x^2$
D) $2x + h$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$f'(x) = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{(x+h)^2 - x^2}{h} = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{x^2+2xh+h^2-x^2}{h} = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{2xh+h^2}{h} = \lim_{h\to0}(2x+h) = 2x$. Option A (x) forgets the factor of 2. Option C (x²) is f(x) itself, not f'(x). Option D (2x+h) is the difference quotient before taking the limit.
