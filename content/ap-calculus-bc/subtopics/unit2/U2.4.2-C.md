The graph of $f$ has the following properties: $f$ is increasing on $(0,3)$ and concave up on $(0,3)$, and $f$ has a local maximum at $x=5$. Which of the following must be true?

## Options
A) $f'(1)<f'(2)<f'(3)$

B) $f'(1)>f'(2)>f'(3)$

C) $f''(1)>0$ and $f''(2)<0$

D) $f'(5)>0$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Since $f$ is increasing on $(0,3)$: $f'(x)>0$ on $(0,3)$. Since $f$ is concave up on $(0,3)$: $f''(x)>0$, meaning $f'$ is increasing. Therefore $f'(1)<f'(2)<f'(3)$. Option B contradicts $f'$ being increasing. Option C claims $f''(2)<0$, contradicting concave up. Option D claims $f'(5)>0$, but at a local maximum $f'(5)=0$.
