Let $g(x)=x^2e^x$. What is $g''(0)$?

## Options
A) $0$

B) $1$

C) $2$

D) $4$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
$g'(x)=2xe^x+x^2e^x=e^x(x^2+2x)$. Then $g''(x)=e^x(x^2+2x)+e^x(2x+2)=e^x(x^2+4x+2)$. At $x=0$: $g''(0)=e^0(0+0+2)=2$. Option A confuses $g''(0)$ with $g(0)=0$. Option B gives $g'(0)=0$ miscomputed. Option D doesn't match any derivative value at 0.
