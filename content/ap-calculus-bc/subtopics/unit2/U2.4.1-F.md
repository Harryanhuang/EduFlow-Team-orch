Which of the following is true?

## Options
A) If $f$ is continuous at $x=a$, then $f$ is differentiable at $x=a$.

B) If $f$ is differentiable at $x=a$, then $f$ is continuous at $x=a$.

C) Continuity and differentiability at a point are equivalent conditions.

D) A function can be differentiable at a point without being defined there.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Differentiability implies continuity (the contrapositive of "discontinuous ⟹ non-differentiable"). But continuity does NOT imply differentiability — for example, $f(x)=|x|$ is continuous at $x=0$ but not differentiable there (sharp corner). Option A is the common misconception. Option C is wrong for the same reason. Option D is impossible — differentiability requires the function to be defined.
