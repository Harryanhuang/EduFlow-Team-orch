Let $p(x)=2x^3-6\sqrt{x}+1$. What is $p'(1)$?

## Options
A) $0$

B) $3$

C) $6$

D) $9$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Rewrite $p(x)=2x^3-6x^{1/2}+1$. Then $p'(x)=6x^2-3x^{-1/2}$. Evaluate at $x=1$: $p'(1)=6(1)^2-3(1)^{-1/2}=6-3=3$. Option A confuses $p'(1)$ with $p(1)=2-6+1=-3$. Option C forgets the second term. Option D adds rather than subtracts.
