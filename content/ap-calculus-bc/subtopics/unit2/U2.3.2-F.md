If $f(x)=x^4-3x^2+5x$, what is $f''(x)$?

## Options
A) $4x^3-6x+5$

B) $12x^2-6$

C) $12x$

D) $4x^2-6$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$f'(x)=4x^3-6x+5$, then $f''(x)=12x^2-6$. Option A is $f'(x)$, not $f''(x)$. Option C is $f'''(x)$, the third derivative. Option D mixes exponents incorrectly.
