Let $f(x)=\begin{cases}x^2 & x\geq 1\\2x-1 & x<1\end{cases}$. Is $f$ differentiable at $x=1$?

## Options
A) Yes, because $f$ is continuous at $x=1$.

B) Yes, because the left and right derivatives both equal 2.

C) No, because the left derivative is 2 and the right derivative is 2 but the function is piecewise.

D) No, because $f$ is not differentiable at a boundary of a piecewise function.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Check continuity: $\lim_{x\to1^-}f(x)=2(1)-1=1$ and $f(1)=1^2=1$. Continuous. Check derivatives: left derivative of $2x-1$ is $2$; right derivative of $x^2$ at $x=1$ is $2(1)=2$. Since left derivative = right derivative $=2$, $f$ is differentiable at $x=1$ with $f'(1)=2$. Option A gives a correct conclusion but wrong reasoning (continuity alone is insufficient to guarantee differentiability). Option C is factually incorrect — equal left and right derivatives mean the function IS differentiable. Option D states a false rule — piecewise functions can be differentiable at boundaries.
