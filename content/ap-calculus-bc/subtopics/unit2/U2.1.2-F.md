If $f'(3)=5$, what is the slope of the tangent line to $y=f(x)$ at $x=3$?

## Options
A) $5$

B) $\frac{1}{5}$

C) $3$

D) Cannot be determined from the given information

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The derivative $f'(a)$ gives exactly the slope of the tangent line to $y=f(x)$ at $x=a$. Since $f'(3)=5$, the tangent slope at $x=3$ is $5$. Option B confuses slope with reciprocal. Option C confuses the $x$-value with the slope. Option D is incorrect — $f'(3)$ directly provides the tangent slope.
