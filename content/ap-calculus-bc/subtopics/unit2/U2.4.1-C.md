For what value of $a$ is $f(x)=\begin{cases}x^2+a & x\leq 1\\2x & x>1\end{cases}$ differentiable at $x=1$?

## Options
A) $a=0$

B) $a=1$

C) $a=2$

D) $a=-1$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For differentiability, need both continuity and equal derivatives.

**Continuity:** $\lim_{x\to1^-}f(x)=1^2+a=1+a$ and $\lim_{x\to1^+}f(x)=2(1)=2$. So $1+a=2$, giving $a=1$.

**Equal derivatives:** Left derivative $=2x\big|_{x=1}=2$. Right derivative $=2$. Equal ✓.

With $a=1$: continuous ($1+1=2$) and differentiable ($2=2$). Option A fails continuity ($1+0\neq2$). Option C fails continuity ($1+2\neq2$). Option D fails continuity ($1-1\neq2$).
