The tangent line to $y=x^2$ at $x=a$ (where $a>0$) is perpendicular to the line $y=-\frac{1}{4}x+3$. What is $a$?

## Options
A) $2$

B) $1$

C) $\frac{1}{2}$

D) $4$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The line $y=-\frac{1}{4}x+3$ has slope $-\frac{1}{4}$. A perpendicular line has slope $4$ (negative reciprocal). The tangent to $y=x^2$ at $x=a$ has slope $f'(a)=2a$. Set $2a=4$, so $a=2$. Option B gives slope $2\neq4$. Option C gives slope $1\neq4$. Option D gives slope $8\neq4$.
