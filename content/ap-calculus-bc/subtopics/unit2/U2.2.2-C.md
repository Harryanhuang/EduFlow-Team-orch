What is $\dfrac{d}{dx}[\sec x]$?

## Options
A) $\csc x \cot x$

B) $\sec x \tan x$

C) $-\sec x \tan x$

D) $\sec^2 x$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$\sec x=\frac{1}{\cos x}$. Using the chain rule or quotient rule: $\frac{d}{dx}[\sec x]=\frac{\sin x}{\cos^2 x}=\sec x\tan x$. Option A is the derivative of $\csc x$ (with a negative sign). Option C adds an incorrect negative sign. Option D is the derivative of $\tan x$, not $\sec x$.
