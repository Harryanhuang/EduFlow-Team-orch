If $f(x)=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}}$, what is $f'(x)$?

## Options
A) $-\dfrac{1}{2}x^{-3/2}$

B) $-\dfrac{1}{2}x^{-1/2}$

C) $\dfrac{1}{2}x^{-3/2}$

D) $-x^{-3/2}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Rewrite $f(x)=x^{-1/2}$. By the power rule: $f'(x)=-\frac{1}{2}x^{-1/2-1}=-\frac{1}{2}x^{-3/2}$. This equals $\frac{-1}{2x^{3/2}}$. Option B has the wrong exponent ($-\frac{1}{2}$ instead of $-\frac{3}{2}$). Option C has the wrong sign. Option D is missing the $\frac{1}{2}$ factor.
