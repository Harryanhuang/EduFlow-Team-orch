What is $\dfrac{d}{dx}\left[\dfrac{x+1}{x-1}\right]$?

## Options
A) $\dfrac{2}{(x-1)^2}$

B) $\dfrac{-2}{(x-1)^2}$

C) $\dfrac{1}{(x-1)^2}$

D) $\dfrac{2}{x-1}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Quotient rule with $f=x+1$, $g=x-1$: $f'=1$, $g'=1$. Numerator: $f'g-fg'=1\cdot(x-1)-(x+1)\cdot1=x-1-x-1=-2$. So $\frac{d}{dx}=\frac{-2}{(x-1)^2}$. Option A has the wrong sign (swapped the order of subtraction). Option C miscalculates the numerator. Option D forgets to square the denominator.
