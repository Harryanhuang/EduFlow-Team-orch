If $x^2+y^2=25$, what is $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$?

## Options
A) $\frac{x}{y}$

B) $-\frac{x}{y}$

C) $\frac{y}{x}$

D) $-2x-2y$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Differentiate implicitly: $2x+2y\frac{dy}{dx}=0$. Solve for $\frac{dy}{dx}$: $\frac{dy}{dx}=-\frac{x}{y}$. Option A has the wrong sign. Option C inverts the ratio. Option D differentiates term by term without solving.
