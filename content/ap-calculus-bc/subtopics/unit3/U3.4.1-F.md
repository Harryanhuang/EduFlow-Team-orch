If $y=x^x$ for $x>0$, what is $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$?

## Options
A) $x^x$

B) $x^x(1+\ln x)$

C) $x\cdot x^{x-1}$

D) $x^x\ln x$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Take natural log: $\ln y=x\ln x$. Differentiate implicitly: $\frac{1}{y}\frac{dy}{dx}=\ln x+x\cdot\frac{1}{x}=\ln x+1$. Multiply by $y$: $\frac{dy}{dx}=y(1+\ln x)=x^x(1+\ln x)$. Option A is just $y$. Option C applies the power rule incorrectly (treating $x$ as constant exponent). Option D forgets the $1$ term.
