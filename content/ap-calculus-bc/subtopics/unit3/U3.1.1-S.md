What is $\dfrac{d}{dx}\left[e^{x^2}\right]$?

## Options
A) $e^{x^2}$

B) $2x e^{x^2}$

C) $x^2 e^{x^2}$

D) $2e^{x^2}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By the chain rule: $\frac{d}{dx}[e^{u}]=e^{u}\cdot u'$ where $u=x^2$. So $\frac{d}{dx}[e^{x^2}]=e^{x^2}\cdot2x=2xe^{x^2}$. Option A forgets the chain rule. Option C confuses the exponent with its derivative. Option D forgets to retain the factor $x$.
