Let $f(x)=x^2\cdot\sin(x^3)$. What is $f'(x)$?

## Options
A) $2x\sin(x^3)+3x^4\cos(x^3)$

B) $2x\sin(x^3)+x^2\cos(x^3)$

C) $2x\cos(x^3)$

D) $x^2\sin(3x^2)$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
By the product rule: $f'(x)=(x^2)'\sin(x^3)+x^2(\sin(x^3))'=2x\sin(x^3)+x^2\cdot\cos(x^3)\cdot3x^2=2x\sin(x^3)+3x^4\cos(x^3)$. Option B forgets the chain rule on $\sin(x^3)$. Option C drops the first term and mishandles the chain rule. Option D incorrectly differentiates the argument.
