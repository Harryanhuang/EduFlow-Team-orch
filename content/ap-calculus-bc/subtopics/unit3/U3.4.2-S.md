A curve is defined by $x=\sin t$, $y=\cos(2t)$. What is the slope of the tangent line at $t=\frac{\pi}{6}$?

## Options
A) $-\sqrt{3}$

B) $-2$

C) $-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$

D) $\sqrt{3}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$\frac{dx}{dt}=\cos t$ and $\frac{dy}{dt}=-2\sin(2t)$. So $\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{-2\sin(2t)}{\cos t}$. At $t=\frac{\pi}{6}$: $\sin(\frac{\pi}{3})=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ and $\cos(\frac{\pi}{6})=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$. Thus $\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{-2\cdot\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}=-2$. Option A uses $\sin(2t)=\frac{1}{2}$ incorrectly. Option C forgets to cancel $\sqrt{3}$ properly. Option D has the wrong sign.
