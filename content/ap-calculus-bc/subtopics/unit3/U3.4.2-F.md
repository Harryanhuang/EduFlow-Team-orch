A curve is defined by $x=t^2$, $y=t^3$. What is $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$?

## Options
A) $\frac{3t}{2}$

B) $\frac{2}{3t}$

C) $\frac{3t^2}{2}$

D) $\frac{2t}{3t^2}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$\frac{dx}{dt}=2t$ and $\frac{dy}{dt}=3t^2$. So $\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{dy/dt}{dx/dt}=\frac{3t^2}{2t}=\frac{3t}{2}$. Option B inverts the ratio. Option C forgets to simplify by canceling a factor of $t$. Option D is an unsimplified inverted form.
