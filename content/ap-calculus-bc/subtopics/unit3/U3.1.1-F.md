What is $\dfrac{d}{dx}\left[(3x+1)^4\right]$?

## Options
A) $4(3x+1)^3$

B) $12(3x+1)^3$

C) $12(3x+1)^4$

D) $4(3x+1)^4$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Let $u=3x+1$. By the chain rule: $\frac{d}{dx}[u^4]=4u^3\cdot u'=4(3x+1)^3\cdot3=12(3x+1)^3$. Option A forgets to multiply by the inner derivative $3$. Option C multiplies by $3$ but keeps the wrong power. Option D forgets the chain rule entirely.
