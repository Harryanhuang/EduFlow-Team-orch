If $f(x)=\sin^3 x$, what is $f'\left(\frac{\pi}{3}\right)$?

## Options
A) $\frac{3}{4}$

B) $\frac{3\sqrt{3}}{4}$

C) $\frac{9}{8}$

D) $-\frac{9}{8}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Rewrite $f(x)=[\sin x]^3$. Apply chain rule: $f'(x)=3\sin^2 x\cdot\cos x$. At $x=\frac{\pi}{3}$: $\sin(\frac{\pi}{3})=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ and $\cos(\frac{\pi}{3})=\frac{1}{2}$. So $f'\left(\frac{\pi}{3}\right)=3\cdot\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^2\cdot\frac{1}{2}=3\cdot\frac{3}{4}\cdot\frac{1}{2}=\frac{9}{8}$. Option A uses $\sin^2=\frac{3}{4}$ without the factor 3. Option B keeps $\sqrt{3}$. Option D has the wrong sign.
