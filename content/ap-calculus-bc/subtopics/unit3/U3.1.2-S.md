What is $\dfrac{d}{dx}\left[\tan(3x)\right]$?

## Options
A) $\sec^2(3x)$

B) $3\sec^2(3x)$

C) $3\tan^2(3x)$

D) $\sec(3x)\tan(3x)$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$\frac{d}{dx}[\tan u]=\sec^2 u\cdot u'$. With $u=3x$, $u'=3$, so $\frac{d}{dx}[\tan(3x)]=3\sec^2(3x)$. Option A forgets the inner derivative. Option C uses the wrong identity. Option D gives the derivative of $\sec(3x)$, not $\tan(3x)$.
