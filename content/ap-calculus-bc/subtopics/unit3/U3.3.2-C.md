Let $h(x)=\arcsin\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)$. What is $h'(1)$?

## Options
A) $\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$

B) $\frac{1}{2\sqrt{3}}$

C) $\frac{1}{\sqrt{4-x^2}}$

D) $\frac{1}{2}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$h'(x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-(x/2)^2}}\cdot\frac{1}{2}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{4-x^2}}$. At $x=1$: $h'(1)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{4-1}}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$. Option B includes an extra factor of $\frac{1}{2}$. Option C is the general derivative expression, not the value at $x=1$. Option D is the inner derivative only.
