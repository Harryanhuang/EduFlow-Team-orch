The function $f$ is differentiable and has an inverse. The table below gives selected values.

| $x$ | $1$ | $2$ | $3$ | $4$ |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| $f(x)$ | $3$ | $5$ | $7$ | $9$ |
| $f'(x)$ | $2$ | $4$ | $6$ | $8$ |

What is $\left(f^{-1}\right)'(7)$?

## Options
A) $\frac{1}{6}$

B) $\frac{1}{4}$

C) $6$

D) $3$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$f^{-1}(7)=3$ because $f(3)=7$. By the inverse function theorem: $(f^{-1})'(7)=\frac{1}{f'(3)}=\frac{1}{6}$. Option B uses $f'(2)=4$ (wrong preimage). Option C inverts the formula. Option D gives $f^{-1}(7)=3$, not the derivative.
