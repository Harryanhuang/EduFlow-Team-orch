What is $\dfrac{d}{dx}\left[\sin(2x)\right]$?

## Options
A) $\cos(2x)$

B) $2\cos(2x)$

C) $-\cos(2x)$

D) $\cos(x)$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By the chain rule: $\frac{d}{dx}[\sin(u)]=\cos(u)\cdot u'$ with $u=2x$, so $u'=2$. Thus $\frac{d}{dx}[\sin(2x)]=\cos(2x)\cdot2=2\cos(2x)$. Option A forgets the inner derivative. Option C has the wrong sign. Option D incorrectly changes the argument.
