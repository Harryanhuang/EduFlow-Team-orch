A curve is defined by $x=t^2$, $y=t^3+1$. What is $\dfrac{d^2y}{dx^2}$?

## Options
A) $\frac{3}{4t}$

B) $\frac{3}{2}$

C) $6t$

D) $\frac{3}{4}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
First, $\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{3t^2}{2t}=\frac{3t}{2}$. Then $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{3t}{2}\right)\bigg/\frac{dx}{dt}=\frac{3/2}{2t}=\frac{3}{4t}$. Option B is $\frac{d}{dt}(\frac{3t}{2})$, missing the division by $\frac{dx}{dt}$. Option C is $\frac{d^2y}{dt^2}$. Option D ignores the $t$ in the denominator.
