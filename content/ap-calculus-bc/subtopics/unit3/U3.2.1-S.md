For the curve $x^2+xy+y^2=7$, what is the slope of the tangent line at the point $(1,2)$?

## Options
A) $-\frac{4}{5}$

B) $\frac{4}{5}$

C) $-\frac{1}{2}$

D) $\frac{1}{2}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Differentiate implicitly: $2x+y+x\frac{dy}{dx}+2y\frac{dy}{dx}=0$. Group terms: $(x+2y)\frac{dy}{dx}=-2x-y$, so $\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{-2x-y}{x+2y}$. At $(1,2)$: $\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{-2-2}{1+4}=-\frac{4}{5}$. Option B has the wrong sign. Option C and D result from algebraic errors in grouping terms.
