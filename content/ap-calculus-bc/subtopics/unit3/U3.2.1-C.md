For the curve $x^3+y^3=6xy$, find $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$.

## Options
A) $\dfrac{2y-x^2}{y^2-2x}$

B) $\dfrac{x^2-2y}{2x-y^2}$

C) $\dfrac{2y+x^2}{y^2+2x}$

D) $\dfrac{y^2-2x}{2y-x^2}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Differentiate implicitly: $3x^2+3y^2\frac{dy}{dx}=6y+6x\frac{dy}{dx}$. Collect $\frac{dy}{dx}$ terms: $3y^2\frac{dy}{dx}-6x\frac{dy}{dx}=6y-3x^2$. Factor: $(3y^2-6x)\frac{dy}{dx}=6y-3x^2$. Divide by 3: $(y^2-2x)\frac{dy}{dx}=2y-x^2$. So $\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{2y-x^2}{y^2-2x}$. Option B has sign errors in both numerator and denominator. Option C has all signs wrong. Option D inverts the fraction.
