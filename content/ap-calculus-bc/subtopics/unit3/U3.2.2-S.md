For the curve $x^2-y^2=3$, find $\dfrac{d^2y}{dx^2}$ at the point $(2,1)$.

## Options
A) $\frac{3}{1}$

B) $-\frac{3}{1}$

C) $\frac{3}{x^3}$

D) $-\frac{3}{y^3}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
First, $2x-2y\frac{dy}{dx}=0$, so $\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{x}{y}$. Differentiate again: $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=\frac{y\cdot1-x\cdot\frac{dy}{dx}}{y^2}=\frac{y-x(\frac{x}{y})}{y^2}=\frac{y^2-x^2}{y^3}$. At $(2,1)$: $\frac{1-4}{1}=-3$. So the answer is $-3$ or $-\frac{3}{1}$. Option A has the wrong sign. Option C is the general form but not evaluated. Option D has the wrong general form.
