If $y=(\sin x)^x$ for $0<x<\pi$, what is $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$?

## Options
A) $(\sin x)^x\left(\ln(\sin x)+x\cot x\right)$

B) $(\sin x)^x\left(x\cot x\right)$

C) $x(\sin x)^{x-1}\cos x$

D) $(\sin x)^x\ln(\sin x)$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Take natural log: $\ln y=x\ln(\sin x)$. Differentiate: $\frac{y'}{y}=\ln(\sin x)+x\cdot\frac{\cos x}{\sin x}=\ln(\sin x)+x\cot x$. So $y'=y[\ln(\sin x)+x\cot x]=(\sin x)^x[\ln(\sin x)+x\cot x]$. Option B misses the $\ln(\sin x)$ term. Option C incorrectly applies the power rule (treating $x$ as constant exponent). Option D misses the $x\cot x$ term.
