What is $\dfrac{d}{dx}\left[\arctan(x^2)\right]$?

## Options
A) $\frac{2x}{1+x^2}$

B) $\frac{2x}{1+x^4}$

C) $\frac{1}{1+x^4}$

D) $\frac{x^2}{1+x^4}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By the chain rule: $\frac{d}{dx}[\arctan u]=\frac{u'}{1+u^2}$ with $u=x^2$, so $u'=2x$. Thus $\frac{d}{dx}[\arctan(x^2)]=\frac{2x}{1+(x^2)^2}=\frac{2x}{1+x^4}$. Option A has the wrong denominator (forgot to square $x^2$). Option C forgets the inner derivative. Option D both forgets the derivative and misplaces it.
