### Question Q-4.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A car manufacturer currently uses flow production. It is considering introducing a custom-build option for customers who want personalised specifications. Recommend how it should adapt its production approach. Justify your answer.
**Answer**: The manufacturer should adopt a hybrid approach. Keep flow production for standard models to maintain low costs and high volume. For custom-build orders, use flexible manufacturing systems (CAM-controlled robots that can switch between specifications on the same line) or set up a separate job-production cell for bespoke orders. This allows the business to capture the premium custom market without disrupting the efficiency of its main flow line. Justification: maintaining the flow line protects margins on the core business, while a custom option captures higher-margin niche demand.
**Explanation**: The solution must protect existing efficiency. A common error is suggesting the entire line switch to batch or job production, which would destroy the economies of scale.
