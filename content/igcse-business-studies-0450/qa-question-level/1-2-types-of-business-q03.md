### Question Q-1.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Compare the liability of a sole trader and a shareholder in a limited company.
**Answer**: A sole trader has unlimited liability — personal assets can be seized to pay business debts. A shareholder has limited liability — they can only lose the amount invested in shares.
**Explanation**: Unlimited vs limited liability. A common error is saying sole traders have limited liability.
