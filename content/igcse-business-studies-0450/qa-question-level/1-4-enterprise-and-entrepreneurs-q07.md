### Question Q-1.4-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate whether government should do more to support entrepreneurs.
**Answer**: Arguments for: entrepreneurs create jobs, drive innovation, and stimulate economic growth — government support (grants, training, tax breaks) can amplify these benefits, especially in deprived areas. Arguments against: resources may be wasted on businesses that fail, intervention may distort market forces, and support could create dependency rather than genuine enterprise. Government support is most effective when it addresses specific barriers (e.g., access to finance) rather than providing blanket subsidies. The level of support should reflect the economic benefits entrepreneurs generate.
**Explanation**: Support has benefits but also risks. A common error is one-sided evaluation.
