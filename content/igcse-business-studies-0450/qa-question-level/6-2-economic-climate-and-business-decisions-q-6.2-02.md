### Question Q-6.2-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is inflation? How is it measured?
**Answer**: Inflation is a sustained increase in the general price level of goods and services over time, reducing the purchasing power of money. It is measured using the Consumer Price Index (CPI), which tracks the cost of a representative basket of goods and services bought by a typical household. The inflation rate is the percentage change in the CPI over a period (usually one year).
**Explanation**: Inflation is about the average price level, not individual prices. A common error is confusing inflation with a single price increase — it must be economy-wide and sustained.
