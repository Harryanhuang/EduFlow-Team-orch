### Question Q-5.4-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is a debenture? How does it differ from an ordinary share?
**Answer**: A debenture is a type of long-term loan capital issued by companies, secured against the company's assets. Debenture holders receive a fixed rate of interest regardless of whether the company makes a profit. They differ from ordinary shares in that: 1) Debenture holders are lenders, not owners — they have no voting rights. 2) Interest on debentures is a fixed charge, while dividends on shares vary with profit. 3) Debentures must be repaid at a specified date; share capital does not. 4) In liquidation, debenture holders are paid before shareholders.
**Explanation**: Debentures are debt, not equity. A common error is treating debenture holders as part-owners of the company.
