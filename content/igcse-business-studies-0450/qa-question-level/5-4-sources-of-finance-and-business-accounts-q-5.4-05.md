### Question Q-5.4-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is trade credit? How does it help a business manage its cash flow?
**Answer**: Trade credit is when a supplier allows a business to receive goods or services now and pay for them at a later date (typically 30, 60, or 90 days). It is a short-term source of finance because the business can sell the goods and generate revenue before the payment is due. It helps cash flow by: 1) Deferring cash outflows. 2) Reducing the need for external borrowing. 3) Being readily available without formal application. However, discounts for early payment may be lost, and late payment can damage supplier relationships.
**Explanation**: Trade credit is the most common short-term finance for businesses. A common error is not recognising trade credit as a source of finance — it is effectively an interest-free loan from suppliers.
