### Question Q-6.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the difference between demand-pull and cost-push inflation?
**Answer**: Demand-pull inflation occurs when aggregate demand exceeds aggregate supply — too much money chasing too few goods. It is caused by strong consumer spending, government expenditure, or investment. Cost-push inflation occurs when production costs rise (e.g., higher wages, increased raw material prices, higher taxes), forcing businesses to raise prices to maintain margins. Both result in rising prices, but the causes and policy responses differ. Demand-pull can be addressed by reducing demand (higher interest rates); cost-push requires addressing supply-side constraints.
**Explanation**: The distinction matters for diagnosis and response. A common error is treating all inflation as the same phenomenon — the underlying cause determines the appropriate business and policy response.
