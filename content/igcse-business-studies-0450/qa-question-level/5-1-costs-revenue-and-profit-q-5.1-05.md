### Question Q-5.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A business has fixed costs of $12,000 per month, variable cost per unit of $5, and sells each unit for $15. Calculate total cost, total revenue, contribution per unit, and profit at a production level of 1,500 units per month.
**Answer**: Total cost = $12,000 + ($5 × 1,500) = $12,000 + $7,500 = $19,500. Total revenue = $15 × 1,500 = $22,500. Contribution per unit = $15 − $5 = $10. Total contribution = $10 × 1,500 = $15,000. Profit = total revenue − total cost = $22,500 − $19,500 = $3,000 (or profit = total contribution − fixed costs = $15,000 − $12,000 = $3,000).
**Explanation**: Both profit formulas should give the same result — use this as a check. A common error is forgetting to multiply variable cost per unit by the number of units.
