### Question Q-4.4-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare the impact of improving capacity utilisation versus improving labour productivity on a manufacturing business's profitability.
**Answer**: Improving capacity utilisation spreads fixed costs over more units, reducing average cost per unit and increasing the margin on each sale. It requires sufficient demand to absorb the extra output. Improving labour productivity reduces the variable cost per unit — each worker produces more, so labour cost per unit falls. This benefits profitability regardless of demand levels. Together, they are powerful: higher utilisation ensures output is sold, and higher productivity ensures each unit costs less to make. However, if demand is weak, improving utilisation may lead to unsold inventory, making productivity improvements the safer first priority.
**Explanation**: Both reduce costs but through different mechanisms. A common error is treating them as the same thing — utilisation addresses fixed cost absorption; productivity addresses labour efficiency.
