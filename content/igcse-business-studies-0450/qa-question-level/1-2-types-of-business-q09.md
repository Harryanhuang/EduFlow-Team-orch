### Question Q-1.2-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Discuss whether limited liability is the most important advantage of operating as a limited company compared to a sole trader.
**Answer**: Limited liability protects personal assets, which encourages investment and risk-taking — this is a significant advantage. However, other advantages may be equally important: easier access to finance through share sales, greater credibility with suppliers and customers, continuity of the business if owners change, and the ability to attract professional management. For some businesses, the ability to raise capital may matter more than limited liability. The most important advantage depends on the business's size, growth plans, and risk profile.
**Explanation**: Limited liability is important but not the only advantage. A common error is focusing solely on liability without considering finance, credibility, and continuity.
