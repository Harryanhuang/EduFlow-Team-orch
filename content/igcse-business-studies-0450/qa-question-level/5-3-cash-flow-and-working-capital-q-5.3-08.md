### Question Q-5.3-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A rapidly growing business has increasing sales but faces a worsening cash position. Analyse the causes and recommend actions.
**Answer**: This is 'overtrading' — growing faster than available finance can support. Causes: 1) More sales mean more inventory and receivables, tying up cash. 2) Fixed capacity means the business must pay for inputs before receiving customer payments. 3) Profit is growing but cash is being consumed by working capital expansion. Recommendations: 1) Arrange additional finance (overdraft for short-term gap, loan for expansion). 2) Tighten credit control — collect receivables faster. 3) Negotiate longer payment terms with suppliers. 4) Slow growth temporarily to match available cash. 5) Consider invoice factoring. Justification: the business is fundamentally viable but needs working capital to fund the growth cycle.
**Explanation**: Overtrading is a common cause of business failure despite growing profits. A common error is recommending cost-cutting when the underlying issue is growth outpacing cash.
