### Question Q-2.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare Herzberg's two-factor theory with Maslow's hierarchy of needs. Which theory provides more practical guidance for managers?
**Answer**: Herzberg identifies hygiene factors (pay, conditions, policies — prevent dissatisfaction) and motivators (recognition, responsibility, achievement — create satisfaction). Maslow's hierarchy has five sequential levels from physiological to self-actualisation. Both recognise money alone does not sustain motivation. Herzberg is more workplace-specific and directly distinguishes what prevents dissatisfaction from what creates motivation, making it more actionable for managers designing reward systems. However, Maslow's framework helps managers understand the broader range of employee needs. For practical management, Herzberg offers clearer guidance on job design, while Maslow helps with understanding individual employee circumstances.
**Explanation**: Herzberg's theory is more workplace-specific. A common error is treating hygiene factors as motivators — they only prevent dissatisfaction, they do not create it.
