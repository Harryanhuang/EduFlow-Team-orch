### Question Q-5.3-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why a profitable business can still run out of cash.
**Answer**: Profit is calculated on an accruals basis (revenue when earned, costs when incurred), while cash flow tracks actual money received and paid. A profitable business can run out of cash if: 1) Customers pay slowly (high receivables) while suppliers must be paid promptly. 2) The business has invested heavily in inventory or equipment. 3) Loan repayments are due. 4) Rapid growth requires upfront cash for materials and wages before customer payments arrive. Profit without cash is not immediately available to pay bills.
**Explanation**: Cash and profit are different measures. A common error is assuming profit = cash — the timing gap between earning and receiving is critical.
