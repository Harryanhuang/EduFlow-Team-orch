### Question Q-1.3-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Analyse whether corporate social responsibility (CSR) always benefits a business.
**Answer**: Benefits of CSR include improved brand image, customer loyalty, employee motivation, and positive relationships with the local community and government — all supporting long-term profitability. However, CSR involves costs such as investment in sustainable materials, community programmes, and environmental measures that may reduce short-term profit. Some CSR activities may not directly benefit the business or may be seen as superficial 'greenwashing'. Small businesses may struggle to afford CSR compared to large multinationals. CSR is most beneficial when it aligns with the business's core activities and values rather than being a superficial add-on.
**Explanation**: CSR has both benefits and costs. A common error is one-sided analysis that only lists benefits or ignores the cost implications.
