### Question Q-3.5-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A coffee shop chain faces rising coffee bean costs and increasing competition from a new budget chain. Recommend how it should adapt its pricing approach and justify your recommendation.
**Answer**: The chain should avoid a simple cost-plus increase across all products, as this risks losing price-sensitive customers to the budget competitor. Instead: 1) Apply selective price increases on premium drinks where customers are less price-sensitive. 2) Introduce a value range at competitive prices to defend market share. 3) Use bundle pricing (coffee + pastry) to increase perceived value. 4) Strengthen loyalty programmes to reduce switching. Justification: a differentiated pricing approach protects margins on premium items while defending volume against the budget competitor.
**Explanation**: A uniform price rise is risky when a budget competitor enters. A common error is passing all cost increases to customers without considering competitive positioning.
