### Question Q-4.4-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is labour productivity? How is it calculated?
**Answer**: Labour productivity measures the output produced per worker (or per hour worked). It is calculated as: total output ÷ number of workers (or total hours worked). For example, if a factory produces 1,000 units per day with 50 workers, labour productivity is 20 units per worker per day.
**Explanation**: Higher productivity means more output from the same input, which lowers average cost. A common error is confusing productivity with production — production is total output; productivity is output per unit of input.
