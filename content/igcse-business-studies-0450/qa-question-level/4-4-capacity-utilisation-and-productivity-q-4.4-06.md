### Question Q-4.4-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Calculate the capacity utilisation and labour productivity for a factory that has a maximum capacity of 5,000 units per week, currently produces 3,500 units, and employs 70 workers.
**Answer**: Capacity utilisation = (3,500 ÷ 5,000) × 100 = 70%. Labour productivity = 3,500 ÷ 70 = 50 units per worker per week. The factory has 30% unused capacity, meaning fixed costs are spread over fewer units than possible. If demand allows, increasing output would lower the average cost per unit.
**Explanation**: Always show the formula and working. A common error is swapping the numerator and denominator in the capacity utilisation formula — actual output always goes on top.
