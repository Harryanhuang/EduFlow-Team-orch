### Question Q-4.5-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is nearshoring? How does it compare to offshoring?
**Answer**: Nearshoring is relocating business processes or production to a nearby country rather than a distant one. For example, a US company moving production to Mexico rather than China. Compared to offshoring, nearshoring offers shorter supply chains, similar time zones, cultural and language proximity, and easier visits for quality control. However, cost savings may be less than with distant low-wage countries. It is a compromise between domestic production and distant offshoring.
**Explanation**: Nearshoring balances cost and control. A common error is treating nearshoring as just a form of offshoring — it is specifically chosen for geographic and cultural closeness.
