### Question Q-5.4-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain why a business might choose to raise finance through a combination of sources rather than relying on a single source.
**Answer**: Using multiple sources spreads risk and matches finance to specific needs. Long-term assets (buildings, machinery) should be funded by long-term sources (share capital, retained profit, long-term loans) to avoid maturity mismatch. Short-term working capital needs suit short-term sources (overdraft, trade credit). A mixed approach also avoids over-reliance on one lender or investor, keeps the cost of capital lower (debt is cheaper than equity but carries repayment risk), and maintains flexibility. For example, a business might use retained profit for part of an expansion, a bank loan for equipment, and an overdraft for working capital — each source matched to its purpose.
**Explanation**: Diversifying finance is analogous to diversifying investment — it reduces concentration risk. A common error is seeking one 'best' source when most businesses use several.
