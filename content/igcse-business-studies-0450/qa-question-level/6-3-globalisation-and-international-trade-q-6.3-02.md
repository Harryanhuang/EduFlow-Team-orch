### Question Q-6.3-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What are the main causes of globalisation?
**Answer**: Key causes include: 1) Trade liberalisation — reduction of tariffs and trade barriers through agreements (WTO, free trade areas). 2) Technological advances — container shipping, the internet, and communication technology reduce the cost of trading. 3) Financial deregulation — easier cross-border investment and capital flows. 4) Growth of multinational corporations (MNCs) operating across multiple countries. 5) Converging consumer preferences creating global markets for brands.
**Explanation**: Multiple forces drive globalisation simultaneously. A common error is attributing it to a single cause like technology — policy changes and corporate strategy are equally important.
