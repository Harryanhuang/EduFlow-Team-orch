### Question Q-3.5-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Explain cost-plus pricing with an example.
**Answer**: Cost-plus pricing sets the selling price by adding a fixed profit amount or percentage (mark-up) to the total cost of producing one unit. For example, if a product costs $10 to produce and the business adds a 30% mark-up, the selling price is $13.
**Explanation**: It is simple and ensures every sale makes a profit. A common error is confusing mark-up percentage with profit margin — a 30% mark-up on $10 gives a $3 profit, but the margin is $3/$13 = 23%.
