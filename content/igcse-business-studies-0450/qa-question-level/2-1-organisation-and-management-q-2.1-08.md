### Question Q-2.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare centralised and decentralised decision-making. To what extent should a large retail chain decentralise its decisions?
**Answer**: Centralisation keeps decision-making at senior levels — ensures consistency and control but is slow and demotivating. Decentralisation pushes decisions to lower levels — faster, more responsive, and motivating, but risks inconsistency. A large retail chain could decentralise operational decisions (e.g., local promotions, staffing rotas) to store managers who understand local markets, while centralising strategic decisions (e.g., brand strategy, major purchasing) to maintain consistency. Neither approach is inherently superior; most businesses use a mix depending on the decision type.
**Explanation**: Neither approach is inherently superior. A common error is treating centralisation and decentralisation as opposites rather than a continuum.
