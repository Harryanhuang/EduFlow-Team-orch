### Question Q-4.4-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain the relationship between capacity utilisation and unit costs.
**Answer**: When capacity utilisation increases, fixed costs are spread over a larger number of units, reducing the average cost per unit. This is one source of economies of scale. For example, a factory with $10,000 in monthly fixed costs producing 1,000 units has $10 fixed cost per unit; at 2,000 units it drops to $5 per unit. However, beyond a certain point, pushing utilisation towards 100% can increase costs — overtime pay, machine wear, and bottlenecks may offset the benefit.
**Explanation**: The relationship is not linear at very high utilisation levels. A common error is assuming that higher utilisation always lowers costs without limit.
