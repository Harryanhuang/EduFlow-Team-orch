### Question Q-4.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is flow production? Give an example.
**Answer**: Flow production (also called mass production) is the continuous large-scale production of identical products on an assembly line. Products move from one stage to the next without interruption. Examples include car manufacturing, soft drink bottling, and electronic device assembly.
**Explanation**: Flow production achieves the lowest cost per unit through economies of scale. A common error is thinking flow production means 'fast' — it means continuous and standardised, not necessarily quick to set up.
