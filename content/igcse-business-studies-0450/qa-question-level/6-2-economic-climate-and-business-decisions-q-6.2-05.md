### Question Q-6.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is GDP (gross domestic product)? How does GDP growth affect business planning?
**Answer**: GDP is the total value of all goods and services produced in a country in a given period. It measures the size and health of the economy. Rising GDP (economic growth) indicates expanding output and typically higher consumer income and spending — businesses plan for increased demand, investment, and hiring. Falling GDP signals contraction — businesses may delay investment, reduce inventory, and focus on cost control. GDP growth rates help businesses forecast market size and set realistic sales targets.
**Explanation**: GDP is the broadest indicator of economic conditions. A common error is equating GDP with business profits — GDP measures output, not profitability.
