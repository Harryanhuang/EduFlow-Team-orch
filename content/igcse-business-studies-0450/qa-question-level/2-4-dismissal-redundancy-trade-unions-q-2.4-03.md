### Question Q-2.4-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Give three reasons why an employee might be fairly dismissed.
**Answer**: 1) Gross misconduct (e.g., theft, violence). 2) Lack of capability or qualifications for the role. 3) Long-term illness preventing the employee from doing the job. 4) Some other substantial reason (e.g., a genuine business reorganisation).
**Explanation**: Fair dismissal must follow proper procedures. A common error is listing 'personal dislike' as a reason — this is not a fair ground.
