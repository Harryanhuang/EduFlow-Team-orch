### Question Q-3.5-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Distinguish between competitive pricing and predatory pricing.
**Answer**: Competitive pricing means setting prices at or near the level of rivals to avoid price wars — common in oligopoly markets. Predatory pricing means setting prices below cost deliberately to drive competitors out of the market, then raising prices once competition is reduced — this is illegal in many countries.
**Explanation**: Both involve looking at competitor prices, but the intent and legality differ. A common error is treating them as the same — predatory pricing requires prices below cost with anti-competitive intent.
