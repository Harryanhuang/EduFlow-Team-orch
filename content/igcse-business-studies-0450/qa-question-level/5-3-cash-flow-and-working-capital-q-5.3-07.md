### Question Q-5.3-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain how invoice factoring works and evaluate it as a cash flow solution for a small business.
**Answer**: Invoice factoring (or factoring) is when a business sells its outstanding receivables (invoices owed by customers) to a third-party factor at a discount. The factor pays the business immediately (typically 70-90% of the invoice value) and then collects payment directly from customers. Advantages: immediate cash without taking on debt; the factor handles credit control. Disadvantages: the business receives less than the full invoice value; customers may perceive the business as financially weak; it can be expensive for small invoice volumes. It is a practical solution for B2B businesses with long payment terms but may be unsuitable if customer relationships are sensitive.
**Explanation**: Factoring converts receivables to cash quickly but at a cost. A common error is confusing factoring with a loan — it is a sale of assets, not borrowing.
