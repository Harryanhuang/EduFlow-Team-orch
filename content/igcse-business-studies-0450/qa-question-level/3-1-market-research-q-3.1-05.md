### Question Q-3.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is a sample? Why is it important that a sample is representative?
**Answer**: A sample is a subset of the total population used in research. A representative sample accurately reflects the characteristics of the whole population. If the sample is biased, the results will not be reliable for the whole market.
**Explanation**: Sample quality determines data quality. A common error is thinking larger samples always mean better results — a large but biased sample is still unreliable.
