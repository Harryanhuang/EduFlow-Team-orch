### Question Q-3.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Analyse the differences between quantitative and qualitative research data and explain when each type is most useful.
**Answer**: Quantitative data is numerical — it can be measured and analysed statistically (e.g., '65% of respondents prefer product A'). Qualitative data is descriptive — it explores opinions, feelings and motivations (e.g., interview transcripts). Quantitative data shows 'what' is happening and is most useful when a business needs measurable evidence to support decisions, such as estimating market size. Qualitative data explains 'why' and is most useful when a business needs to understand customer motivations, such as during product development. Using only one type risks an incomplete picture.
**Explanation**: Both types complement each other. A common error is treating qualitative data as 'less useful' — it provides depth that numbers alone cannot.
