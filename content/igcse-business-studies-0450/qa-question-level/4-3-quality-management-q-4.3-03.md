### Question Q-4.3-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is Total Quality Management (TQM)? Describe its key principles.
**Answer**: TQM is a management approach where every employee is committed to maintaining high standards of quality in all aspects of operations. Key principles: 1) Quality is everyone's responsibility — from the shop floor to senior management. 2) Continuous improvement (kaizen) — always seeking to do things better. 3) Customer focus — quality is defined by the customer. 4) Right first time — aim for zero defects. 5) Teamwork and open communication.
**Explanation**: TQM is a cultural shift, not just a set of procedures. A common error is thinking TQM is only about the production department — it involves the entire organisation.
