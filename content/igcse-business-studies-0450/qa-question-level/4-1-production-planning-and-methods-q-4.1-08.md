### Question Q-4.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Analyse the risks of just-in-time production and evaluate how a business can reduce them.
**Answer**: Risks: 1) A single supplier delay can halt the entire production line. 2) No buffer stock to handle unexpected demand spikes. 3) Heavy dependence on supplier reliability and quality. 4) Vulnerable to external disruptions (weather, strikes, transport issues). Mitigation: 1) Use multiple suppliers for critical materials. 2) Maintain strong communication and shared IT systems with suppliers. 3) Keep a small safety stock of the most critical components. 4) Have contingency transport arrangements. Evaluation: JIT trades inventory cost for supply chain risk — businesses must assess whether their suppliers are reliable enough before adopting JIT, as the cost of a production halt far exceeds the savings from lower stock levels.
**Explanation**: JIT trades inventory cost for supply chain risk. A common error is presenting JIT as purely advantageous without acknowledging its vulnerability to disruption.
