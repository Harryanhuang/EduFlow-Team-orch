### Question Q-3.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate price skimming versus penetration pricing for a new technology product.
**Answer**: Price skimming works when the product is innovative, has few competitors, and targets early adopters willing to pay more — it recovers R&D costs quickly. Penetration pricing works when the market is competitive and price-sensitive — it builds volume fast. The choice depends on competition, product uniqueness, and business cash flow needs. Skimming risks attracting competitors; penetration risks establishing a low-price image that is hard to reverse.
**Explanation**: The best choice depends on market conditions. A common error is claiming one strategy is always superior.
