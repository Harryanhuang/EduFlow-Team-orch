### Question Q-6.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does high inflation affect a business's costs, pricing, and competitiveness?
**Answer**: High inflation increases input costs (raw materials, energy, wages), squeezing profit margins if prices cannot be raised proportionately. Businesses may raise prices to protect margins, but this risks losing price-sensitive customers. Frequent price changes create menu costs (the cost of updating prices, catalogues, and systems). If domestic inflation is higher than in competitor countries, exports become less competitive internationally. Workers may demand higher wages to maintain living standards, creating a wage-price spiral.
**Explanation**: Inflation affects both costs and revenues. A common error is thinking businesses can always pass inflation on to customers — price elasticity limits this.
