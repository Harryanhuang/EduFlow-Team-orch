### Question Q-5.4-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is retained profit? Why is it an important source of finance for established businesses?
**Answer**: Retained profit is the portion of net profit that is kept in the business rather than distributed to owners as dividends or drawings. It is an important source because: 1) It does not incur interest charges. 2) It does not dilute ownership or control. 3) It is readily available without application processes. 4) It signals financial strength to lenders. However, new businesses have no retained profit, and owners may prefer immediate returns.
**Explanation**: Retained profit is the cheapest long-term source of finance. A common error is confusing retained profit with cash — retained profit is an accounting figure; actual cash may be tied up in assets.
