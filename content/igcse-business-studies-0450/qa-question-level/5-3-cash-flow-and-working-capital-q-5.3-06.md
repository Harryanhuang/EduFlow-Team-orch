### Question Q-5.3-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe three methods a business can use to improve its cash flow.
**Answer**: 1) Speed up receivables collection — offer early-payment discounts, tighten credit terms, or chase overdue invoices more actively. 2) Delay payables — negotiate longer payment terms with suppliers (without damaging relationships). 3) Reduce inventory levels — avoid overstocking, use just-in-time ordering. 4) Arrange short-term finance — overdraft, short-term loan, or invoice factoring to cover temporary shortfalls. 5) Sell unused assets for cash. 6) Sale and leaseback — sell equipment or property and lease it back to release cash.
**Explanation**: Cash flow improvement targets either inflows (speed them up) or outflows (slow them down). A common error is suggesting long-term strategies (e.g., increase sales) for an immediate cash crisis.
