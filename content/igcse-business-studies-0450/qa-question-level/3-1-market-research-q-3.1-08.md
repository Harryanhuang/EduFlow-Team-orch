### Question Q-3.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate why a business might use a mixed-method approach to market research.
**Answer**: Using both primary and secondary, and both quantitative and qualitative methods, gives a more complete picture. Secondary research provides background cheaply; primary research fills specific gaps. Quantitative data identifies trends; qualitative data explains them. The cost is higher but the risk of bad decisions is reduced.
**Explanation**: Mixed methods reduce blind spots. A common error is listing methods without explaining why combining them is valuable.
