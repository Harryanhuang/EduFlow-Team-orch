### Question Q-4.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is just-in-time (JIT) production? What are its benefits?
**Answer**: Just-in-time production is a method where raw materials and components are delivered exactly when they are needed in the production process, so the business holds minimal or no inventory. Benefits include: 1) Lower storage and warehousing costs. 2) Less capital tied up in stock. 3) Reduced waste from obsolete or damaged inventory. 4) Encourages closer relationships with reliable suppliers.
**Explanation**: JIT requires reliable suppliers and good planning. A common error is confusing JIT with just-in-case — the latter holds buffer stock as insurance against supply disruption.
