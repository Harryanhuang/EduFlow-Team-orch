### Question Q-1.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A sole trader wants to expand and needs $500,000. Evaluate the most suitable business structure for this expansion.
**Answer**: Converting to a private limited company would allow raising capital through share sales to family and friends while retaining control. A PLC could raise the most capital from the stock exchange but requires more regulation and risks loss of control through takeover. Remaining a sole trader avoids complexity but limits capital to personal savings and loans. The best option depends on whether the owner prioritises control (stay private) or maximum capital (go public).
**Explanation**: Limited company for capital raising, but control trade-offs matter. A common error is saying one option is always best without justification.
