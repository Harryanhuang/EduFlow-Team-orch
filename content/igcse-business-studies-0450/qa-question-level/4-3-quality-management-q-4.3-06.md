### Question Q-4.3-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Compare quality control and quality assurance in terms of their approach, advantages, and disadvantages.
**Answer**: Quality control: inspects finished products to find defects. Advantage — catches faults before customers see them. Disadvantage — reactive, wastes resources on defective items, does not prevent recurrence. Quality assurance: builds quality into processes to prevent defects. Advantage — reduces waste and rework long-term, consistent quality. Disadvantage — requires significant investment in training, systems, and cultural change; benefits take time to materialise.
**Explanation**: Both have a role — assurance prevents, control catches. A common error is presenting one as universally superior — in practice, most businesses use both.
