### Question Q-4.3-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate whether a small manufacturer should implement a formal quality assurance system or rely on quality control inspection.
**Answer**: For a small manufacturer, the choice depends on product type and customer expectations. Quality control inspection has lower upfront costs and is easier to implement immediately — suitable if defect rates are low and products are simple. However, quality assurance delivers long-term savings through waste reduction and consistent quality, which is critical if the manufacturer supplies larger businesses that demand certification (e.g., ISO 9001). The initial investment in QA (training, documentation, process redesign) may strain a small business, but the competitive advantage and reduced rework often justify it. A hybrid approach — basic QC now, gradual QA adoption — is often the most practical path.
**Explanation**: Scale and resources matter. A common error is recommending full TQM to a small firm without acknowledging the implementation cost.
