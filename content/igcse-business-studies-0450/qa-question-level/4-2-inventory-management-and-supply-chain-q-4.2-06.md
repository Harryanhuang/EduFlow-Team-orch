### Question Q-4.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is stock rotation? Why is it important, particularly for perishable goods?
**Answer**: Stock rotation is the practice of using or selling older stock before newer stock. The most common method is FIFO (first in, first out). For perishable goods like food and medicine, stock rotation prevents items from passing their use-by or expiry dates, which would cause waste, financial loss, and potential health risks. It also ensures customers receive products with the maximum remaining shelf life.
**Explanation**: FIFO is the dominant method for perishables. A common error is confusing FIFO with LIFO — LIFO (last in, first out) is rarely used for physical goods and unsuitable for perishables.
