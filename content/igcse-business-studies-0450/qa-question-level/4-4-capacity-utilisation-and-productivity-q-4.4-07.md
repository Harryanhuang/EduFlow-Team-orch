### Question Q-4.4-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate whether a business should always aim for 100% capacity utilisation.
**Answer**: Not always. Operating at 100% leaves no flexibility for unexpected orders, machine maintenance, or peak seasonal demand. It can cause excessive machine wear, force overtime payments, and increase employee stress and turnover. Most businesses aim for 80-95% utilisation, leaving headroom for maintenance and demand spikes. However, capital-intensive industries (e.g., airlines, hotels) have high fixed costs and need utilisation close to 100% to be profitable. The ideal level depends on the industry, cost structure, and demand variability.
**Explanation**: 100% sounds ideal but creates operational risk. A common error is treating 100% as a universal target without considering maintenance needs and demand fluctuations.
