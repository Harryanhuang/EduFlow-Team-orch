### Question Q-5.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A business has current assets of $80,000 (cash $15,000, inventory $35,000, receivables $30,000) and current liabilities of $50,000 (payables $40,000, overdraft $10,000). Calculate its working capital and comment on its liquidity position.
**Answer**: Working capital = $80,000 − $50,000 = $30,000. This is positive, meaning the business has sufficient current assets to cover its short-term liabilities. However, $35,000 of the $80,000 in current assets is inventory, which may not be quickly convertible to cash. Quick liquidity depends on collecting receivables ($30,000) and available cash ($15,000) — only $45,000 against $50,000 of immediate liabilities. The business should monitor receivables collection and consider reducing inventory levels.
**Explanation**: Positive working capital does not guarantee immediate liquidity if assets are tied up in slow-moving inventory. A common error is not considering the composition of current assets.
