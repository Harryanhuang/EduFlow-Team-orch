### Question Q-5.2-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: How is the break-even point calculated using the formula method?
**Answer**: Break-even point (in units) = total fixed costs ÷ contribution per unit, where contribution per unit = selling price − variable cost per unit. For example, if fixed costs are $20,000, selling price is $50, and variable cost is $30: contribution per unit = $20; break-even = $20,000 ÷ $20 = 1,000 units.
**Explanation**: The formula shows how many units must be sold to cover all costs. A common error is dividing fixed costs by selling price instead of contribution.
