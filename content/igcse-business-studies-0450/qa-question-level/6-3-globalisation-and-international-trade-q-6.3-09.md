### Question Q-6.3-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate whether a local clothing manufacturer should begin exporting its products or focus exclusively on the domestic market.
**Answer**: Exporting advantages: 1) Access to a much larger customer base, reducing dependency on the domestic economy. 2) Economies of scale from higher production volumes. 3) Risk diversification — a downturn in one market may be offset by growth in another. 4) Enhanced brand reputation from international presence. Exporting challenges: 1) Tariffs and trade barriers in target markets. 2) Need to adapt products to local tastes, regulations, and sizing standards. 3) Higher distribution and logistics costs. 4) Currency fluctuation risk. 5) Need for local market knowledge and potentially local partners. Recommendation: begin with a limited export programme to one or two culturally similar, low-barrier markets while maintaining the domestic base. This limits risk while building export experience. Justification: the clothing industry benefits from brand building across markets, and starting small allows learning before committing significant resources.
**Explanation**: The decision balances growth opportunity against complexity and risk. A common error is either refusing to export at all or entering too many markets simultaneously without adequate preparation.
