### Question Q-1.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate whether a family business should remain a private limited company or become a public limited company.
**Answer**: Staying private retains family control, avoids public scrutiny, and has fewer regulatory requirements — important for maintaining the family vision. Going public raises more capital for growth, improves credibility, and allows shares to be traded easily. However, a PLC faces risk of hostile takeover, must publish detailed accounts, and may lose the personal culture of the family firm. The decision depends on whether capital needs outweigh the desire to maintain family control.
**Explanation**: Control vs capital trade-off. A common error is saying one is clearly better without balanced evaluation.
