### Question Q-5.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A business is deciding whether to accept a special order at a price below its normal selling price. Using the concept of contribution, explain when this would be financially worthwhile.
**Answer**: Accepting a lower-priced order is worthwhile if the order price exceeds the variable cost per unit — meaning it generates positive contribution. Since fixed costs are already covered by normal sales, any additional contribution flows directly to profit. For example, if variable cost is $8 per unit and the special order offers $10 per unit, each unit contributes $2 to profit. However, conditions apply: 1) The business must have spare capacity. 2) The order must not cannibalise normal-priced sales. 3) It should not set a price precedent that customers expect regularly.
**Explanation**: Contribution analysis separates short-term decisions from long-term pricing strategy. A common error is rejecting the order because the price is below total cost per unit, ignoring that fixed costs are already covered.
