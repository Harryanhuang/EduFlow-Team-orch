### Question Q-2.3-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare on-the-job and off-the-job training. Which approach is more suitable for a business introducing new technology?
**Answer**: On-the-job training takes place at the workplace while doing the actual job (e.g., coaching, job rotation). It is cheaper and job-specific but may pass on bad habits. Off-the-job training takes place away from work (e.g., college course, external workshop). It brings fresh ideas and expert trainers but is more expensive and may not be job-specific. For new technology, off-the-job training may be more suitable initially because external specialists can teach the technology properly without the pressure of daily production targets. However, follow-up on-the-job coaching is valuable to embed learning. A blended approach is usually most effective, combining expert instruction with practical workplace application.
**Explanation**: Many businesses use a blend of both. A common error is claiming one is always better — the choice depends on the job and budget.
