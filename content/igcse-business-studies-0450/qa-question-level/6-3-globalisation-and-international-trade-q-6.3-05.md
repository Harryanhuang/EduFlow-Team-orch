### Question Q-6.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is protectionism? Describe three methods of protectionism.
**Answer**: Protectionism is government policy to restrict imports and protect domestic industries from foreign competition. Methods: 1) Tariffs — taxes on imported goods, making them more expensive. 2) Quotas — limits on the quantity of a specific good that can be imported. 3) Subsidies — payments to domestic producers to lower their costs and make them more competitive. 4) Administrative barriers — complex regulations, safety standards, or paperwork that makes importing difficult. 5) Embargoes — complete bans on trade with specific countries.
**Explanation**: Protectionism is the opposite of free trade. A common error is thinking tariffs are the only tool — quotas, subsidies, and regulations are equally important.
