### Question Q-4.2-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate the impact of a global supply chain disruption (such as a pandemic or trade dispute) on a manufacturer that relies on just-in-time inventory management.
**Answer**: A JIT manufacturer is highly vulnerable to supply chain disruption because it holds minimal buffer stock. Any delay — port closures, supplier shutdowns, transport restrictions — can halt the entire production line within hours or days, causing lost sales, idle workers, and damaged customer relationships. However, JIT firms with strong supplier relationships may receive priority during shortages. Response strategies include: diversifying the supplier base across regions, increasing safety stock for critical components, and nearshoring key suppliers. Justification: JIT's efficiency advantage disappears when supply reliability collapses — resilience becomes more important than lean inventory.
**Explanation**: The trade-off between efficiency and resilience is central. A common error is dismissing JIT entirely — it works well in stable conditions but needs contingency planning.
