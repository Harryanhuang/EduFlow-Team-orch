### Question Q-4.5-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What are the risks of relocating a business to a new location? How can these risks be reduced?
**Answer**: Risks: 1) High one-off relocation costs (moving equipment, terminating leases, setting up new facilities). 2) Loss of experienced workers who are unwilling to relocate. 3) Disruption to operations and customer service during the transition. 4) Uncertainty about the new location — labour market, local regulations, community acceptance. 5) Damage to brand image if the move is seen as abandoning the local community. Mitigation: 1) Detailed planning and phased relocation. 2) Retention bonuses for key staff. 3) Maintaining a temporary presence at the old location during transition. 4) Thorough research and pilot visits to the new location.
**Explanation**: Relocation is disruptive even when well-planned. A common error is underestimating the human cost — losing skilled workers can be more damaging than the financial cost.
