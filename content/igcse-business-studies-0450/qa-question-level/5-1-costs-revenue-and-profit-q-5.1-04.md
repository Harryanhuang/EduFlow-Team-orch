### Question Q-5.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Distinguish between gross profit and net profit.
**Answer**: Gross profit = revenue − cost of sales (direct production costs such as raw materials and direct labour). It measures how efficiently a business produces its goods. Net profit = gross profit − all other expenses (overheads, administrative costs, interest, tax). It measures overall profitability after all costs. Gross profit is always higher than or equal to net profit.
**Explanation**: The gap between gross and net profit shows how much overhead the business carries. A common error is including overheads in the cost of sales when calculating gross profit.
