### Question Q-6.3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is free trade? What are its benefits?
**Answer**: Free trade is international trade without restrictions such as tariffs, quotas, or subsidies. Its benefits include: 1) Countries specialise in producing goods where they have a comparative advantage, increasing global output. 2) Consumers get lower prices and greater choice. 3) Businesses access cheaper raw materials and components. 4) Competition encourages innovation and efficiency. 5) Developing countries can grow through export-led growth.
**Explanation**: Free trade is based on the theory of comparative advantage. A common error is thinking free trade means no rules — it still requires agreements on standards, intellectual property, and dispute resolution.
