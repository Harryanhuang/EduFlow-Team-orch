# Topic 1: Measurement & Units (1.1–1.5)

## Q1
- **Question:** A student measures the length of a pencil with a metre rule and records 14.3 cm. The actual length is 14.8 cm. Calculate the absolute error and the percentage error.
- **Answer:** Absolute error = 0.5 cm; Percentage error = 3.4%
- **Explanation:** Absolute error = |measured − actual| = |14.3 − 14.8| = 0.5 cm. Percentage error = (0.5 / 14.8) × 100 = 3.38% ≈ 3.4%.
- **Type:** calculation
- **Difficulty:** easy

## Q2
- **Question:** What is the SI base unit for mass, and how does it differ from weight?
- **Answer:** The SI base unit for mass is the kilogram (kg). Mass is the amount of matter in an object and is constant everywhere; weight is the gravitational force on that mass (W = mg) and varies with gravitational field strength.
- **Explanation:** Mass is a scalar quantity measured in kg. Weight is a vector (force) measured in newtons (N). On Earth g ≈ 9.8 N/kg, so a 2 kg mass weighs 19.6 N.
- **Type:** concept
- **Difficulty:** easy

## Q3
- **Question:** A measuring cylinder initially contains 35.0 cm³ of water. After submerging a stone the level reads 52.5 cm³. The stone's mass is 42.0 g. Calculate its density.
- **Answer:** 2.4 g/cm³
- **Explanation:** Volume of stone = 52.5 − 35.0 = 17.5 cm³. Density = mass / volume = 42.0 / 17.5 = 2.4 g/cm³.
- **Type:** calculation
- **Difficulty:** easy

## Q4
- **Question:** Describe how you would use a micrometer screw gauge to measure the diameter of a thin wire, including how to account for zero error.
- **Answer:** Close the jaws gently on the wire and read the main scale + thimble scale. Before measuring, close the jaws fully and note the reading — this is the zero error. Subtract the zero error from all readings (if zero error is +0.02 mm, subtract 0.02 mm from each measurement).
- **Explanation:** A micrometer reads to 0.01 mm precision. Zero error correction ensures systematic errors are removed. Always take 3 readings at different points along the wire and average them.
- **Type:** practical
- **Difficulty:** medium

## Q5
- **Question:** A car travels 150 km in 2 hours. Convert this speed to m/s.
- **Answer:** 20.8 m/s
- **Explanation:** 150 km = 150 000 m; 2 h = 7200 s. Speed = 150 000 / 7200 = 20.83 m/s ≈ 20.8 m/s.
- **Type:** calculation
- **Difficulty:** easy

## Q6
- **Question:** A student reports the mass of a rock as 67.3456 g using a balance that reads to the nearest 0.1 g. Comment on the appropriateness of this recording.
- **Answer:** The recording is inappropriate. The balance only reads to 0.1 g, so the mass should be recorded as 67.3 g (3 significant figures). Extra digits imply false precision.
- **Explanation:** Measured values must reflect instrument precision. A balance reading to 0.1 g cannot justify 6 significant figures. This tests understanding of significant figures and precision.
- **Type:** application
- **Difficulty:** medium

## Q7
- **Question:** Convert the following: (a) 3.2 km to m, (b) 4500 g to kg, (c) 2.5 h to s.
- **Answer:** (a) 3200 m, (b) 4.5 kg, (c) 9000 s
- **Explanation:** (a) 3.2 × 1000 = 3200 m. (b) 4500 / 1000 = 4.5 kg. (c) 2.5 × 3600 = 9000 s.
- **Type:** calculation
- **Difficulty:** easy
