# Topic 2: Describing Motion (2.1–2.5)

## Q1
- **Question:** Define speed and velocity. State one key difference between them.
- **Answer:** Speed is the rate of change of distance (scalar). Velocity is the rate of change of displacement in a specified direction (vector). The key difference: velocity has direction, speed does not.
- **Explanation:** A car travelling at 60 km/h has a speed of 60 km/h. If it travels 60 km/h north, its velocity is 60 km/h north. Speed and velocity share the same unit (m/s) but velocity is directional.
- **Type:** concept
- **Difficulty:** easy

## Q2
- **Question:** A cyclist accelerates from rest to 12 m/s in 6 seconds. Calculate the acceleration.
- **Answer:** 2 m/s²
- **Explanation:** a = Δv / Δt = (12 − 0) / 6 = 2 m/s².
- **Type:** calculation
- **Difficulty:** easy

## Q3
- **Question:** A car's velocity-time graph shows a straight line from (0, 0) to (10 s, 30 m/s). What distance does the car travel in these 10 seconds?
- **Answer:** 150 m
- **Explanation:** Distance = area under the v-t graph = ½ × base × height = ½ × 10 × 30 = 150 m.
- **Type:** calculation
- **Difficulty:** medium

## Q4
- **Question:** Sketch and label a distance-time graph for an object that is stationary for 5 s, then moves at constant speed for 10 s.
- **Answer:** A horizontal line (zero gradient) for the first 5 s, then a straight line with positive constant gradient for the next 10 s.
- **Explanation:** A stationary object has constant distance → horizontal line (gradient = 0 = speed). Constant speed → straight line with constant positive gradient. The steeper the gradient, the faster the speed.
- **Type:** concept
- **Difficulty:** easy

## Q5
- **Question:** A ball is thrown vertically upwards at 20 m/s. Taking g = 10 m/s², calculate: (a) the time to reach maximum height, (b) the maximum height reached.
- **Answer:** (a) 2.0 s, (b) 20 m
- **Explanation:** (a) At max height v = 0. Using v = u + at: 0 = 20 + (−10)t → t = 2.0 s. (b) Using s = ut + ½at²: s = 20(2) + ½(−10)(4) = 40 − 20 = 20 m. Alternatively, v² = u² + 2as → 0 = 400 − 20s → s = 20 m.
- **Type:** calculation
- **Difficulty:** medium

## Q6
- **Question:** Explain why the gradient of a velocity-time graph gives acceleration, and the area under it gives distance.
- **Answer:** Gradient = Δv / Δt = acceleration by definition. Area = v × t = distance (since distance = speed × time for constant velocity; for changing velocity the area still represents the integral of velocity over time = displacement).
- **Explanation:** These are fundamental relationships in kinematics. For a curved v-t graph, the instantaneous gradient at any point gives instantaneous acceleration, and the total area gives total displacement.
- **Type:** concept
- **Difficulty:** medium

## Q7
- **Question:** A train travels 80 km at 40 km/h and then 120 km at 60 km/h. Calculate the average speed for the entire journey.
- **Answer:** 50 km/h
- **Explanation:** Total distance = 80 + 120 = 200 km. Time for first leg = 80/40 = 2 h. Time for second leg = 120/60 = 2 h. Total time = 4 h. Average speed = 200/4 = 50 km/h. (Note: not the simple average of 40 and 60.)
- **Type:** calculation
- **Difficulty:** medium
