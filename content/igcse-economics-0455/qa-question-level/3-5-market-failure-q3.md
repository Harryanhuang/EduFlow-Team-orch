### Question Q-3.5-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why a negative externality leads to market failure.
**Answer**: When a negative externality exists, the social cost of production exceeds the private cost. Producers only consider their private costs, so the market produces more than the socially optimal quantity. This overproduction creates a welfare loss because the additional units produced impose costs on third parties that exceed the benefits to consumers.
**Explanation**: Social cost > private cost leads to overproduction and welfare loss. A common error is saying negative externalities cause underproduction.
