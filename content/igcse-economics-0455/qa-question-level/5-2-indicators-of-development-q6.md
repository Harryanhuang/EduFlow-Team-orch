### Question Q-5.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain how life expectancy is used as a development indicator and what it reveals about a country.
**Answer**: Life expectancy at birth measures the average number of years a newborn is expected to live. It serves as a proxy for the health dimension of the HDI. A higher life expectancy indicates better healthcare, nutrition, clean water, sanitation, and overall living conditions. Countries with low life expectancy typically face problems such as high infant mortality, infectious diseases, and poor access to medical services.
**Explanation**: Life expectancy is a proxy for overall health and living conditions. A common error is assuming life expectancy measures only medical quality.
