### Question Q-4.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Distinguish between direct and indirect taxes, giving one example of each.
**Answer**: Direct taxes are levied on income or wealth and are paid directly by the person or firm on whom they are imposed. They cannot be passed on to someone else. Example: income tax. Indirect taxes are levied on goods and services and can be passed on to consumers in the form of higher prices. Example: value-added tax (VAT) or excise duty on cigarettes.
**Explanation**: Direct taxes are on income/wealth and cannot be shifted; indirect taxes are on spending and can be passed to consumers. A common error is classifying income tax as indirect or VAT as direct.
