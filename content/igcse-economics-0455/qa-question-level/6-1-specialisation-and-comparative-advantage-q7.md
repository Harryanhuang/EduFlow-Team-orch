### Question Q-6.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate whether a developing country should specialise in primary products or manufactured goods.
**Answer**: Primary products: easier to begin producing using existing natural resources, but are subject to high price volatility, low income elasticity of demand, and declining terms of trade. Manufactured goods: offer higher value added, more employment, and greater export earnings, but require skilled labour, infrastructure, and capital investment that developing countries may lack. A gradual shift from primary to manufacturing specialisation, as seen in the development paths of South Korea and China, may be the most effective strategy. The best choice depends on the country's resource endowment, stage of development, and access to international markets.
**Explanation**: Weigh trade-offs between primary and manufacturing sectors. A common error is arguing one sector is always superior without considering the country's specific circumstances.
