### Question Q-5.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Identify four limitations of the HDI as a measure of development.
**Answer**: (1) It does not measure inequality within a country — averages may hide large disparities between rich and poor. (2) It ignores environmental quality and sustainability. (3) It does not account for political freedom, human rights, or gender equality. (4) It excludes non-market activities such as unpaid care work and subsistence farming, which are important in many developing countries.
**Explanation**: The HDI is useful but incomplete. A common error is treating the HDI as a perfect or comprehensive measure of all aspects of development.
