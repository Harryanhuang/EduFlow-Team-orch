### Question Q-6.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the impact of trade protection on employment in the economy as a whole?
**Answer**: Trade protection may save jobs in the protected industry by shielding it from foreign competition, but it costs jobs elsewhere. Downstream industries face higher input costs and may cut employment. Export industries may lose jobs if trading partners retaliate with their own protectionist measures. The net effect on total employment across the economy is often negative or negligible.
**Explanation**: Protects some jobs but destroys others through higher costs and retaliation. A common error is assuming protection always increases total employment.
