### Question Q-2.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: If the price of coffee rises, what happens to the demand for tea? Explain.
**Answer**: Demand for tea increases — coffee and tea are substitutes. When coffee becomes more expensive, consumers switch to tea.
**Explanation**: Substitutes: price of one up → demand for the other up. A common error is saying demand for tea falls.
**Tags**: demand, substitutes, cross-price
