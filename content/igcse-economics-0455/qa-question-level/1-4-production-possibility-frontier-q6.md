### Question Q-1.4-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: On a PPF showing guns and butter, what happens when the economy moves from producing more guns to producing more butter?
**Answer**: The economy moves along the PPF curve. As more butter is produced, fewer guns can be produced — the opportunity cost of more butter is the quantity of guns forgone. This illustrates the trade-off between the two goods when resources are reallocated. The economy remains on the frontier (efficient), but the composition of output changes.
**Explanation**: Movement along the curve = reallocation of resources between two goods. A common error is showing a shift of the curve rather than a movement along it.
