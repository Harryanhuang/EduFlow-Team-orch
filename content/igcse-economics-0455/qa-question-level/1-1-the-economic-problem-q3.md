### Question Q-1.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A government has a budget of $10 billion. It can spend it on healthcare, education, or defence. Explain the economic problem this illustrates.
**Answer**: This illustrates scarcity (limited budget) and choice (must decide allocation). Spending on healthcare means less for education or defence — the opportunity cost of healthcare is the next best alternative forgone.
**Explanation**: Budget constraint forces trade-offs. A common error is saying the government can afford all three equally or ignoring the concept of opportunity cost in the analysis.
