### Question Q-4.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain the main roles of a central bank in an economy.
**Answer**: The central bank manages the country's monetary policy by controlling the money supply and setting interest rates. It acts as the government's bank (managing government accounts and debt), serves as the lender of last resort to commercial banks to prevent bank failures, issues banknotes and coins, and regulates the banking system to maintain financial stability.
**Explanation**: The central bank has multiple roles: monetary policy, government's bank, lender of last resort, currency issuer, and regulator. A common error is confusing the central bank with a commercial bank or only mentioning one of its functions.
