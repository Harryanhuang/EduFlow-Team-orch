### Question Q-3.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate why the PES for manufactured goods is generally higher than for agricultural goods.
**Answer**: Manufactured goods can be produced more quickly and flexibly as factories can increase shifts, hire more workers, and use spare capacity. Agricultural goods are constrained by biological processes, seasons, and land availability. However, manufacturing may also face capacity constraints when operating at full capacity, and some manufactured goods require long lead times for specialised components. The difference in PES largely reflects the greater ability of manufacturers to adjust output in the short run.
**Explanation**: Manufacturing is more flexible than agriculture. A common error is saying manufacturing PES is always high regardless of capacity utilisation.
