### Question Q-4.5-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate the view that some unemployment is necessary for a healthy economy.
**Answer**: Frictional unemployment can be considered beneficial: it reflects workers voluntarily leaving jobs to find better matches, which improves productivity and job satisfaction over time. It indicates a dynamic, flexible labour market where people have the freedom to change careers. Zero unemployment is unrealistic and may indicate a rigid labour market where workers are trapped in unsuitable jobs with no mobility. However, this argument only applies to low levels of frictional unemployment. Structural unemployment represents wasted human potential and requires costly retraining. Cyclical unemployment causes significant economic damage, poverty, and social problems during recessions. Long-term unemployment of any type leads to deskilling (hysteresis), making it even harder for workers to re-enter employment. Youth unemployment is particularly damaging as it affects lifetime earnings and career prospects. In conclusion, a small amount of frictional unemployment (around the natural rate) is consistent with a healthy economy, but any unemployment above this level represents economic waste and social harm that governments should actively address.
**Explanation**: Distinguish between frictional (arguably necessary) and structural/cyclical (harmful) unemployment. Consider hysteresis and youth unemployment. A common error is accepting all unemployment as necessary or rejecting any unemployment as harmful.
