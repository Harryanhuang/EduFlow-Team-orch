### Question Q-6.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What are the effects of a tariff on domestic producers in the importing country?
**Answer**: Domestic producers benefit from reduced foreign competition, allowing them to sell more output at higher prices and potentially expand production and employment. However, they may become less efficient over time due to lack of competitive pressure, and producers who rely on imported raw materials face higher input costs.
**Explanation**: Protected from competition in the short run but may become inefficient long-term. A common error is assuming domestic producers always benefit without drawbacks.
