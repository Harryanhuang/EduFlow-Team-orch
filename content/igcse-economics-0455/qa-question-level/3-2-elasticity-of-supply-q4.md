### Question Q-3.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is the supply of agricultural products often price inelastic in the short run?
**Answer**: Crops take time to grow and farmers cannot quickly increase supply in response to a price increase. Production is constrained by biological growing seasons and land availability.
**Explanation**: Time lag in production. A common error is saying supply is perfectly inelastic.
