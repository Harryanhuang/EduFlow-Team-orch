### Question Q-3.4-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What are barriers to entry? Give two examples and explain how they protect existing firms.
**Answer**: Barriers to entry are obstacles that prevent or make it difficult for new firms to enter a market. Examples: (1) Patents give legal protection that prevents competitors from copying a product. (2) High start-up costs (e.g. building factories) deter new entrants who lack capital. These protect existing firms by limiting competition, allowing them to maintain higher prices and profits.
**Explanation**: Obstacles to new entrants. A common error is confusing barriers to entry with barriers to exit.
