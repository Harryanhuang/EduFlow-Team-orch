### Question Q-1.4-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare the effects on a country's PPF of an increase in immigration versus an increase in automation technology. Justify which would have a greater long-term impact on productive capacity.
**Answer**: Immigration increases the quantity of labour, shifting the PPF outward — especially for labour-intensive goods. However, the shift depends on the skills of immigrants and may be uneven across sectors. Automation technology improves the productivity of existing workers, shifting the PPF outward — particularly for capital-intensive goods. Automation may cause a more pronounced shift in specific sectors. In the long term, automation may have greater impact because technological improvements compound over time (each innovation enables further innovation), while immigration increases are limited by population growth and policy. However, automation may increase structural unemployment, while immigration can fill labour shortages and support an ageing population. The greater long-term impact depends on the country's existing factor endowments and economic structure.
**Explanation**: Both shift the PPF outward but through different mechanisms and with different distributional effects. A common error is assuming both cause identical outward shifts without considering sectoral differences and long-term dynamics.
