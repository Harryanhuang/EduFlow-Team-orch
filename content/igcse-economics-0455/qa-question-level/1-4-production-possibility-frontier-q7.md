### Question Q-1.4-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate the usefulness of the PPF model for analysing real-world economic decisions.
**Answer**: The PPF simplifies a complex economy to two goods, making trade-offs, scarcity, and opportunity cost visually clear. It effectively illustrates the concepts of efficiency (on the curve), unemployment (inside), and growth (outward shift). However, real economies produce thousands of goods, not two, and resources are not always fully employed or perfectly transferable. The model assumes a fixed level of technology and does not capture income distribution, externalities, or the role of international trade. Despite these limitations, the PPF remains a powerful teaching and analytical tool for understanding fundamental economic principles and the cost of resource allocation choices.
**Explanation**: Useful for illustrating core concepts but simplified. A common error is treating the PPF as a literal description of an economy rather than a simplified analytical model.
