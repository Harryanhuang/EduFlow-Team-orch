### Question Q-1.4-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is the PPF usually drawn concave (bowed outwards) to the origin?
**Answer**: The bowed shape reflects increasing opportunity cost — as more of one good is produced, increasingly larger amounts of the other good must be given up. This occurs because resources are not equally suited to producing both goods. Workers and land specialised for one product are less efficient when redirected to another, so each additional unit costs more in terms of the forgone good.
**Explanation**: Increasing opportunity cost = bowed/concave shape. A common error is drawing a straight-line PPF (which assumes constant opportunity cost) or a convex shape.
