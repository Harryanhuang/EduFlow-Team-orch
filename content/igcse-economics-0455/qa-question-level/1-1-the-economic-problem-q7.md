### Question Q-1.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A country discovers large new oil reserves. Analyse whether this eliminates the problem of scarcity for that country.
**Answer**: No, scarcity is not eliminated. While the country has more of one resource, other resources (labour, capital, land for agriculture) remain limited, and human wants are still unlimited. The new wealth from oil may even create new wants (better infrastructure, higher living standards). Additionally, oil extraction uses other scarce resources and has environmental costs. Scarcity is a universal and permanent economic condition — having more of one resource does not resolve the fundamental mismatch between limited resources and unlimited wants.
**Explanation**: Scarcity is fundamental — more of one resource does not eliminate it because wants grow and other resources remain limited. A common error is assuming that resource wealth ends scarcity entirely.
