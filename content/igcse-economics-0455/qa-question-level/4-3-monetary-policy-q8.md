### Question Q-4.3-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Discuss whether a central bank should prioritise controlling inflation or promoting economic growth.
**Answer**: Prioritising inflation control (inflation targeting) provides price stability, which creates certainty for businesses to plan investment and for consumers to spend. Low and stable inflation prevents the erosion of savings and maintains international competitiveness. Many successful central banks (e.g., the ECB) have a primary inflation mandate. However, focusing solely on inflation may lead to overly tight monetary policy during downturns, increasing unemployment and slowing growth. The US Federal Reserve has a dual mandate (price stability and maximum employment), which allows it to respond to recessions more flexibly. In practice, the two goals are interconnected: stable inflation supports sustainable long-term growth, while excessive growth stimulus can create inflation that ultimately damages the economy. The optimal approach depends on the economic context — during high inflation periods, inflation control should dominate; during recessions, growth promotion is more urgent. A flexible inflation target with consideration for employment is generally the most balanced approach.
**Explanation**: Weigh price stability benefits against the risk of ignoring unemployment and growth. Consider dual mandate vs single mandate. A common error is arguing that only one objective matters without considering how they interact.
