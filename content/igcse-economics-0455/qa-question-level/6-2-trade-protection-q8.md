### Question Q-6.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare the effectiveness of tariffs and quotas as methods of trade protection.
**Answer**: Tariffs are taxes on imports that raise prices, while quotas are physical limits on import quantities. Both restrict imports and protect domestic producers, but they work differently. Tariffs generate government revenue and allow consumers to buy more if they are willing to pay the higher price. Quotas do not generate direct government revenue (unless licences are auctioned) and set a hard limit on quantity regardless of price. Quotas provide more certainty about import volumes but may lead to higher price increases than tariffs. Tariffs are more transparent and easier to negotiate in trade agreements, while quotas can be more restrictive but harder to administer. In practice, tariffs are generally preferred by the WTO as they are more transparent and allow market forces to determine quantities.
**Explanation**: Tariffs raise price and earn revenue; quotas cap quantity and may raise prices more. A common error is treating tariffs and quotas as identical in their effects.
