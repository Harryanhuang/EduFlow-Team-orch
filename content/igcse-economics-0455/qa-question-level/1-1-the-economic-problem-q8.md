### Question Q-1.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate whether technological advances reduce or increase the problem of scarcity.
**Answer**: Technology can increase productive efficiency (more output from same inputs), reducing scarcity for some goods — for example, mechanised farming increases food supply. However, technology also creates new wants (smartphones, internet services, electric vehicles) that did not previously exist, generating fresh demands on resources. Technology may also deplete resources faster (more efficient mining exhausts minerals sooner). Overall, while technology eases scarcity in specific areas, it cannot eliminate the fundamental economic problem because wants grow alongside capabilities. Scarcity persists regardless of technological progress.
**Explanation**: Technology increases supply but also creates new wants and can accelerate resource depletion. A common error is saying technology eliminates scarcity entirely, or that it only has positive effects.
