### Question Q-3.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Analyse how knowledge of PES could help a government decide whether to subsidise housing construction to address a housing shortage.
**Answer**: If the supply of housing is price inelastic (PES < 1), a subsidy to builders will have a limited effect on the quantity of housing supplied in the short run because construction takes time, land is scarce, and planning permissions create delays. The subsidy may mainly increase builders' profits and land prices rather than output. In the long run, supply becomes more elastic as new developments are planned and construction capacity increases, making the subsidy more effective. The government should consider whether the subsidy is better targeted at increasing construction capacity (training workers, releasing land) rather than just reducing costs for existing builders.
**Explanation**: Inelastic supply limits the output effect of subsidies in the short run. A common error is assuming subsidies always increase supply significantly regardless of elasticity.
