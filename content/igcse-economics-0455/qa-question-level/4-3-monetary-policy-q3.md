### Question Q-4.3-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain how an increase in interest rates affects consumer spending and business investment.
**Answer**: Higher interest rates increase the cost of borrowing, making loans for houses, cars, and other goods more expensive. This reduces consumer spending, especially on big-ticket items purchased on credit. Higher rates also increase the reward for saving, encouraging people to save rather than spend. For businesses, higher borrowing costs make investment projects less profitable, reducing spending on new equipment, factories, and expansion. Existing borrowers with variable-rate mortgages and loans face higher repayments, reducing their disposable income. Overall, higher interest rates reduce aggregate demand in the economy.
**Explanation**: Higher rates increase borrowing costs, reward saving, and reduce disposable income for variable-rate borrowers. A common error is saying higher interest rates increase spending or only affect one channel.
