### Question Q-2.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate whether an increase in consumer income always leads to an increase in demand.
**Answer**: For normal goods, higher income increases demand (rightward shift) as consumers buy more at every price. However, for inferior goods, higher income reduces demand as consumers switch to superior alternatives — e.g. switching from bus travel to car ownership, or from generic brands to premium brands. The proportion of income spent also matters: as incomes rise, the share spent on basic necessities may fall (Engel's law). Additionally, higher income may increase savings rather than spending if consumer confidence is low. Evaluation: the effect of income on demand depends on the type of good, consumer preferences, and the broader economic context.
**Explanation**: Requires distinguishing normal and inferior goods and considering broader factors. A common error is assuming all goods see higher demand when income rises.
