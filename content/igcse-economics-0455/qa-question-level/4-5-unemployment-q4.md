### Question Q-4.5-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain what structural unemployment is and what causes it.
**Answer**: Structural unemployment is long-term unemployment caused by a fundamental mismatch between the skills workers possess and the skills demanded by employers. It results from permanent changes in the structure of the economy, such as the decline of traditional industries (e.g., coal mining, shipbuilding), technological change making certain skills obsolete, globalisation shifting production to other countries, or changes in consumer demand patterns. Unlike frictional unemployment, structural unemployment cannot be solved simply by matching workers to existing vacancies — workers need to retrain, relocate, or acquire new skills, which takes significant time and investment.
**Explanation**: Structural unemployment is caused by a skills mismatch due to economic change. A common error is confusing it with cyclical unemployment or suggesting that more job advertisements will solve it.
