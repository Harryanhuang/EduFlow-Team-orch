### Question Q-1.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Distinguish between renewable and non-renewable resources with examples.
**Answer**: Renewable resources can be replenished naturally over time (solar energy, wind, forests if managed sustainably). Non-renewable resources are finite and cannot be replaced once used (oil, coal, natural gas). Overuse of renewable resources beyond their replenishment rate can make them effectively non-renewable.
**Explanation**: Renewable = replenished naturally. Non-renewable = finite supply. A common error is not recognising that water can be scarce locally, or classifying all natural resources as renewable.
