### Question Q-4.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is fiat money and why does it have value?
**Answer**: Fiat money is currency that has value because the government declares it to be legal tender. It has no intrinsic value (unlike gold or silver coins) and is not backed by a commodity. Its value depends on public confidence in the government and the central bank.
**Explanation**: Fiat money is government-decreed, not commodity-backed. A common error is saying fiat money is backed by gold reserves or has value because of the material it is made from.
