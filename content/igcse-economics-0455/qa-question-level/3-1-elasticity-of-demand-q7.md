### Question Q-3.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A firm with inelastic demand for its product is considering raising prices. Advise the firm.
**Answer**: Raising prices will increase total revenue because quantity demanded falls by a smaller percentage. However, the firm should consider long-run effects as consumers may find substitutes over time, making demand more elastic. The firm should also assess competitor responses and whether the price rise might damage brand reputation or customer loyalty.
**Explanation**: Price increase leads to revenue increase for inelastic demand, but consider long-run elasticity changes. A common error is ignoring long-run elasticity and competitor reactions.
