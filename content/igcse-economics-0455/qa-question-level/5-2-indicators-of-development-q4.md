### Question Q-5.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why the HDI is considered a better measure of development than GDP per capita alone.
**Answer**: The HDI is multi-dimensional — it includes health (life expectancy) and education (schooling) alongside income (GNI per capita). GDP per capita only measures average economic output and ignores how income is distributed and whether people are healthy or educated. Two countries with the same GDP per capita can have very different HDI scores if one invests more in healthcare and schools, making the HDI a more complete picture of human well-being.
**Explanation**: Multi-dimensional vs single dimension. A common error is claiming GDP per capita fully captures development.
