### Question Q-6.3-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How do changes in interest rates affect the exchange rate?
**Answer**: When a country raises its interest rates relative to other countries, foreign investors seeking higher returns move their savings into that country's banks and financial assets. This increases demand for the domestic currency, causing it to appreciate. Conversely, lower interest rates may lead to capital outflows as investors seek better returns abroad, increasing supply of the currency and causing depreciation.
**Explanation**: Higher interest rates attract foreign capital and increase currency demand. A common error is predicting depreciation when interest rates rise.
