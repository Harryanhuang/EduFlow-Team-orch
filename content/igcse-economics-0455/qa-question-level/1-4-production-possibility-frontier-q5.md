### Question Q-1.4-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A natural disaster destroys a significant number of factories in a country. Explain the effect on the country's PPF.
**Answer**: The PPF shifts inward — the economy can now produce less of both goods with the reduced capital stock. The destruction of factories means fewer capital goods available for production, reducing the economy's productive capacity. This is a loss of economic growth, not merely a movement along the existing curve.
**Explanation**: Fewer resources (capital destroyed) cause an inward shift of the PPF. A common error is showing a movement along the curve instead of a shift, or confusing a demand change with a supply change.
