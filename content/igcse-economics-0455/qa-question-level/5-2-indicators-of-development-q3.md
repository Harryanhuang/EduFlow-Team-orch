### Question Q-5.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is GNI per capita and how does it differ from GDP per capita?
**Answer**: GNI (Gross National Income) per capita is total income earned by a country's residents divided by the population. It includes income from abroad (such as remittances and profits from overseas investments). GDP per capita measures the value of goods and services produced within a country's borders regardless of who owns the factors of production. GNI is used in the HDI because it better reflects the income actually available to a country's citizens.
**Explanation**: GNI includes net income from abroad; GDP does not. A common error is using GDP and GNI interchangeably without recognising the distinction.
