### Question Q-4.3-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the money supply and why is it important for the economy?
**Answer**: The money supply is the total amount of money in circulation in an economy at a given time, including physical currency (notes and coins), bank deposits, and other liquid assets. It is important because changes in the money supply affect spending, inflation, and economic growth. Too much money growth can cause inflation; too little can lead to deflation and economic stagnation.
**Explanation**: Money supply includes cash plus bank deposits, not just physical cash. A common error is thinking the money supply only refers to notes and coins in circulation.
