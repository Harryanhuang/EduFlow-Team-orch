### Question Q-6.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Can a country have a comparative advantage in producing every good? Explain your reasoning.
**Answer**: No. If a country has a lower opportunity cost in producing one good, it must have a higher opportunity cost in producing another. Comparative advantage is relative: one country's advantage in a good necessarily means the other country has the advantage in the other good. It is mathematically impossible for one country to have the lowest opportunity cost in all goods.
**Explanation**: Opportunity costs are inversely related across goods. A common error is assuming a highly productive country can have a comparative advantage in everything.
