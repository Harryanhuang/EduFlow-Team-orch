### Question Q-6.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What are the effects of a tariff on consumers in the importing country?
**Answer**: Consumers face higher prices for both imported and domestically produced goods (domestic producers raise prices to match the tariff-inflated import price). This reduces consumer surplus, lowers real incomes, and restricts consumer choice. Consumers may also face reduced product variety as some foreign producers exit the market.
**Explanation**: Higher prices and less choice for consumers. A common error is saying consumers benefit from tariffs.
