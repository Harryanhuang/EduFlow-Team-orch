### Question Q-1.4-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Analyse whether an outward shift in a country's PPF always leads to an improvement in living standards.
**Answer**: An outward PPF shift indicates greater productive capacity, which could raise living standards through higher output and income. However, this is not guaranteed. The benefits may be distributed unequally — if growth concentrates among a wealthy elite, most citizens see no improvement. The shift may also involve negative externalities (pollution, resource depletion) that reduce quality of life. Additionally, if the shift is towards capital goods rather than consumer goods, current living standards may not improve even though future capacity increases. Population growth may absorb the gains, leaving per capita output unchanged. Therefore, while an outward PPF shift is a necessary condition for sustained improvement in living standards, it is not sufficient — distribution, environmental quality, and the composition of output also matter.
**Explanation**: Growth in productive capacity does not automatically translate to higher living standards. A common error is assuming that any outward PPF shift automatically improves welfare without considering distribution, externalities, and output composition.
