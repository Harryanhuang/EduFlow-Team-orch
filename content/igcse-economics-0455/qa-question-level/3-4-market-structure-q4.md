### Question Q-3.4-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is an oligopoly and why are firms in an oligopoly interdependent?
**Answer**: An oligopoly is a market dominated by a small number of large firms with high barriers to entry. Firms are interdependent because each firm's pricing and output decisions directly affect its rivals, leading to strategic behaviour where firms must anticipate competitors' reactions.
**Explanation**: Few large firms, interdependence due to market concentration. A common error is confusing oligopoly with monopolistic competition.
