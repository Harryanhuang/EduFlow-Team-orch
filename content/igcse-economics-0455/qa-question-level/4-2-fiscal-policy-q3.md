### Question Q-4.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain how expansionary fiscal policy is used to stimulate economic growth.
**Answer**: Expansionary fiscal policy involves increasing government spending and/or reducing taxation. Higher government spending directly increases aggregate demand (e.g., building schools, hospitals). Tax cuts leave consumers and businesses with more disposable income, increasing consumption and investment. This multiplier effect boosts output and employment. It is typically used during recessions to close a deflationary gap.
**Explanation**: Spend more and/or tax less to boost aggregate demand. A common error is saying expansionary policy involves cutting government spending.
