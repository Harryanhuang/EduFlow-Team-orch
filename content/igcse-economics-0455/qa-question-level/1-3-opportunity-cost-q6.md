### Question Q-1.3-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Country A can produce 10 tonnes of rice or 5 tonnes of wheat on the same land. Calculate the opportunity cost of each good.
**Answer**: Opportunity cost of 1 tonne of rice = 5/10 = 0.5 tonnes of wheat. Opportunity cost of 1 tonne of wheat = 10/5 = 2 tonnes of rice. To find opportunity cost, divide what is given up by what is gained.
**Explanation**: OC of rice = wheat forgone / rice gained = 5/10 = 0.5. OC of wheat = rice forgone / wheat gained = 10/5 = 2. A common error is mixing up which quantity goes in the numerator and denominator.
