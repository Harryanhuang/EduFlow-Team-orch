### Question Q-3.4-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate whether monopoly is always bad for consumers.
**Answer**: Monopolies can harm consumers by charging higher prices, restricting output, and having less incentive to be efficient due to lack of competition. However, monopolies are not always bad: they may benefit from economies of scale that lower costs and prices, they may invest more in research and development using supernormal profits, and natural monopolies (such as utilities) may be more efficient as a single provider than with duplicated infrastructure. The impact on consumers depends on whether the monopoly uses its market power exploitatively or invests in innovation and efficiency.
**Explanation**: Trade-off between higher prices and potential benefits. A common error is saying monopoly is always bad without considering economies of scale and innovation.
