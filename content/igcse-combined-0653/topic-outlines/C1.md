# Topic C1 — States of Matter

## Basic Info
- **Topic ID:** C1
- **Domain:** Chemistry
- **Core/Supplement:** Core
- **Weight in exam:** Foundational (often Paper 1 multiple choice basis questions)

## Knowledge Points
1. Kinetic theory: particles in solids, liquids, gases
2. Changes of state: melting, freezing, boiling, evaporation, condensation, sublimation
3. Gas pressure and its relationship to temperature and particle motion
4. Brownian motion as evidence for kinetic theory
5. Diffusion in gases and liquids
6. Heating and cooling curves
7. Relationships between pressure, volume, and temperature (qualitative only for 0653)

## Key Formulas / Facts
- Changes of state are physical changes, not chemical (no new substances formed)
- Rate of diffusion increases with temperature
- Gases diffuse faster than liquids (higher KE at same T)
- No fixed shape for liquids/gases; fixed shape only for solids
- Melting point and boiling point are characteristic properties

## Common Errors
- Confusing evaporation with boiling (evaporation occurs at any temperature, boiling at specific BP)
- Thinking changes of state create new substances
- Forgetting that particles themselves do not expand/contract — it's the spacing that changes
- Mixing up the direction of state changes on heating/cooling curves

## Difficulty Band: F:2|S:4|C:3
- F: identify states of matter, name state changes, basic particle arrangement descriptions
- S: explain changes of state using kinetic theory, diffusion rate, heating curves
- C: evaluate evaporation vs boiling, explain Brownian motion, relate pressure to particle collisions
