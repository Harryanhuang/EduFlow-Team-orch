# Topic B2 — Cell Structure and Organisation

## Basic Info
- **Topic ID:** B2
- **Domain:** Biology
- **Core/Supplement:** Core
- **Weight in exam:** Foundational (appears in both Paper 1 and Paper 2)

## Knowledge Points
1. Structure of animal, plant, and bacterial cells
2. Functions of cell organelles: nucleus, mitochondria, ribosomes, chloroplasts, cell membrane, cell wall, vacuole
3. Specialisation of cells (root hair cell, xylem vessel, palisade mesophyll, red blood cell, sperm cell, nerve cell)
4. Levels of organisation: cell → tissue → organ → organ system → organism
5. Differences between plant and animal cells
6. Prokaryotic vs eukaryotic cells

## Key Formulas / Facts
- Plant cells have: cell wall, chloroplasts, large central vacuole (not in animal cells)
- Animal cells have: no cell wall, no chloroplasts, small vacuoles (not large central)
- Bacterial cells: no nucleus (DNA in cytoplasm), no membrane-bound organelles
- Cell magnification = image size / actual size
- Cell surface area to volume ratio decreases as cell size increases

## Common Errors
- Confusing prokaryotic and eukaryotic cell features
- Forgetting that plant cells do NOT have centrioles
- Mixing up magnification formula direction
- Confusing cell specialisation with cell division

## Difficulty Band: F:2|S:4|C:3
- F: identify cell structures, basic differences animal/plant
- S: cell specialisation, magnification calculations, levels of organisation
- C: compare specialised cells, evaluate advantages of cell specialisation, SA:Vol ratio analysis
