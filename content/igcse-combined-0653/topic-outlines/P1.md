# Topic P1 — Motion and Forces

## Basic Info
- **Topic ID:** P1
- **Domain:** Physics
- **Core/Supplement:** Core
- **Weight in exam:** Medium-High (both Paper 1 and Paper 2)

## Knowledge Points
1. Distance and displacement (scalar vs vector)
2. Speed and velocity (scalar vs vector)
3. Acceleration: a = (v − u)/t
4. Distance-time graphs: gradient = speed
5. Velocity-time graphs: gradient = acceleration; area = distance travelled
6. Newton's First Law (inertia)
7. Newton's Second Law: F = ma
8. Newton's Third Law: action-reaction pairs
9. Momentum: p = mv
10. Conservation of momentum in collisions
11. Friction and its effects

## Key Formulas
- Speed = distance / time
- Velocity = displacement / time
- Acceleration = (final velocity − initial velocity) / time
- F = ma
- Momentum = mass × velocity
- Weight = mass × gravitational field strength (W = mg)

## Common Errors
- Confusing speed with velocity (direction matters for velocity)
- Misreading graphs: taking the y-value instead of gradient
- Forgetting to convert units (e.g., km/h to m/s)
- Applying F = ma without ensuring consistent units
- Confusing mass with weight

## Difficulty Band: F:2|S:4|C:3
- F: calculate speed, read distance-time graphs, identify scalar/vector quantities
- S: calculate acceleration, use F = ma, interpret velocity-time graphs
- C: conservation of momentum problems, multi-step kinematics, evaluate motion from graphs
