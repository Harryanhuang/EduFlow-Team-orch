# Topic B4 — Biological Molecules
## QA Question Level File

**1. [F]** Name the four main biological molecules found in living organisms.

**2. [F]** What is the molecular formula of glucose?

**3. [F]** What is the general formula of a carbohydrate?

**4. [F]** State one function of protein in the human body.

**5. [S]** Describe the difference between a monomer and a polymer, using starch as an example.

**6. [S]** Name the test used to test for the presence of starch in a food sample and describe the expected result.

**7. [S]** What is the difference between saturated and unsaturated lipids?

**8. [S]** Describe the Benedict's test for reducing sugars and the colour change observed.

**9. [S]** Explain why enzymes are specific to their substrates.

**10. [S]** State two properties of enzymes.

**11. [S]** Describe the biuret test for protein and the expected colour change.

**12. [S]** Explain why lipid tests require the sample to be dissolved in water first.

**13. [C]** Explain the lock-and-key model of enzyme action. Then evaluate this model, suggesting one way it has been improved.

**14. [C]** Describe the lock-and-key and induced-fit models of enzyme action. Evaluate each model.

**15. [C]** A student tested three food samples with Benedict's test. Sample A turned brick-red, Sample B stayed blue, Sample C turned green. Explain these results.

**16. [C]** Explain how enzymes work as catalysts, including reference to the role of the active site and the lowering of activation energy.

**17. [C]** Describe how temperature affects enzyme activity, explaining the underlying reasons at different temperature ranges.

**18. [C]** Evaluate the use of enzyme-based washing powders compared to conventional detergents. Consider their advantages and disadvantages.
