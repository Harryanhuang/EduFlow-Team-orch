# Topic B6 — Gas Exchange and Respiration
## QA Question Level File

**1. [F]** What is aerobic respiration?

**2. [F]** State the balanced symbol equation for aerobic respiration.

**3. [F]** What is anaerobic respiration?

**4. [F]** Name the gas exchange surface in humans.

**5. [S]** Describe what happens to the diaphragm during inhalation (breathing in).

**6. [S]** Explain why ventilation (breathing) is necessary for gas exchange.

**7. [S]** Describe what happens to the diaphragm and ribcage during forced exhalation (e.g., blowing out a candle).

**8. [S]** What is the function of the epiglottis?

**9. [S]** State three features of the alveoli that make them efficient for gas exchange.

**10. [S]** Why does breathing rate increase during exercise?

**11. [S]** Compare aerobic and anaerobic respiration in terms of (a) products and (b) energy yield.

**12. [S]** What is oxygen debt?

**13. [C]** A student breathes normally and then breathes as fast and deep as possible for 30 seconds. Predict and explain what happens to the blood pH in each case.

**14. [C]** Design an experiment to compare the rate of respiration in germinating seeds versus dry seeds. Describe the method, variables, and expected results.

**15. [C]** Explain the mechanism of gas exchange at the alveolus, including reference to partial pressures and diffusion.

**16. [C]** A person has a lung disease that causes the alveolar walls to become thicker. Explain the effects on gas exchange.

**17. [C]** Compare the gas exchange systems in a fish and a human, explaining how each is adapted to its environment.

**18. [C]** Explain why marathon runners often experience muscle cramps after finishing a race, while a sprinter may experience cramps during the race. Relate your answer to the type of respiration occurring in each case.
