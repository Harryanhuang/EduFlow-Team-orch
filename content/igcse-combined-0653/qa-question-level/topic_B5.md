# Topic B5 — Nutrition
## QA Question Level File

**1. [F]** What is photosynthesis?

**2. [F]** State two sources of light for photosynthesis.

**3. [F]** What are the two stages of photosynthesis?

**4. [F]** What is a food chain?

**5. [S]** Explain why plants need mineral ions for healthy growth. Give two examples.

**6. [S]** Describe the structure of the human digestive system, naming four main regions or organs involved in chemical digestion.

**7. [S]** What is the role of bile in digestion?

**8. [S]** Draw and label a diagram of the human alimentary canal, showing the mouth, oesophagus, stomach, small intestine, and large intestine.

**9. [S]** Explain how villi in the small intestine are adapted for absorption.

**10. [S]** What is the difference between a food chain and a food web?

**11. [S]** Explain how energy is lost at each stage of a food chain.

**12. [S]** State the balanced chemical equation for photosynthesis.

**13. [C]** Design an experiment to investigate the effect of light intensity on the rate of photosynthesis. Describe the method, variables, and expected results.

**14. [C]** Explain why a cactus plant has small, spiky leaves and a thick, fleshy stem.

**15. [C]** Compare the energy flows in a food chain and in an ecosystem. Explain why ecosystems are more stable than individual food chains.

**16. [C]** A farmer grows crops in a field and applies nitrogen fertiliser. Explain the possible consequences for the local ecosystem if excess fertiliser runs off into a nearby lake.

**17. [C]** Evaluate the environmental impact of a meat-based diet compared to a plant-based diet.

**18. [C]** Explain how decomposers recycle nutrients in an ecosystem. Include in your answer: the role of decomposition, the nutrients involved, and why this recycling is essential for ecosystem function.
