# Topic B3 — Movement in and out of Cells
## QA Question Level File

**1. [F]** Define diffusion.

**2. [F]** State two factors that affect the rate of diffusion.

**3. [F]** Give one example of diffusion in the human body.

**4. [F]** Define osmosis.

**5. [S]** A plant cell is placed in a dilute solution. State and explain what happens to the cell.

**6. [S]** A red blood cell is placed in distilled water. State and explain what happens.

**7. [S]** Explain the difference between diffusion and active transport.

**8. [S]** State two ways in which the small intestine is adapted for efficient absorption of digested food.

**9. [S]** Explain why plant cells do not burst when placed in water, while animal cells do.

**10. [S]** A potato chip is placed in concentrated salt solution. Describe and explain what happens.

**11. [S]** Name the process by which water is absorbed into the blood from the alimentary canal, and explain the mechanism.

**12. [S]** Explain how oxygen and carbon dioxide are exchanged in the lungs.

**13. [C]** Explain the role of the root hair cell in water uptake by plants. Include the terms osmosis, diffusion, and active transport in your answer.

**14. [C]** Design an experiment to investigate the effect of solution concentration on plant tissue. Describe the method, expected results, and conclusion.

**15. [C]** Compare the transport of oxygen in the blood with the transport of glucose to body cells. In your answer, include the mechanisms of transport and the reasons why each mechanism is necessary.

**16. [C]** Explain what is meant by water potential. Describe what happens to animal cells when placed in solutions with (a) higher, (b) lower, and (c) equal water potential compared to the cell cytoplasm.

**17. [C]** Evaluate the importance of active transport in the kidney in maintaining blood homeostasis. Include reference to osmoregulation and ion balance.

**18. [C]** A student states that "osmosis is just diffusion of water." Evaluate this statement. In your answer, discuss what osmosis has in common with diffusion and how it differs, with reference to membrane properties and water potential.
