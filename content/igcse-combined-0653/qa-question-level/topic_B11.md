# Topic B11 — Inheritance
## QA Question Level File

**1. [F]** What is a gene?

**2. [F]** What is the difference between genotype and phenotype?

**3. [F]** What is meant by a dominant allele?

**4. [F]** What is meant by a recessive allele?

**5. [S]** What is the difference between mitosis and meiosis?

**6. [S]** What is a Punnett square?

**7. [S]** In humans, the allele for brown eyes (B) is dominant over the allele for blue eyes (b). Cross a heterozygous brown-eyed parent with a homozygous blue-eyed parent. What are the offspring genotypes and phenotypes?

**8. [S]** State three ways meiosis introduces genetic variation.

**9. [S]** What is the difference between a gene and a chromosome?

**10. [S]** Explain the process of mitosis.

**11. [S]** A man with genotype IAi has blood type A. What does this tell you about the alleles IA and i?

**12. [S]** What is a monohybrid cross?

**13. [C]** In pea plants, tall (T) is dominant over short (t). Two heterozygous tall plants are crossed. Draw a Punnett square and give the ratio of genotypes and phenotypes in the offspring.

**14. [C]** Explain the differences between monohybrid and dihybrid crosses, using a Punnett square in your answer.

**15. [C]** A colour-blind man marries a woman who is a carrier for colour blindness. Colour blindness is caused by a recessive allele on the X chromosome. What are the possible genotypes of their offspring?

**16. [C]** A couple have four children. Three are girls and one is a boy. Explain this outcome in terms of genetics.

**17. [C]** Compare the structure of DNA and RNA.

**18. [C]** Evaluate the statement: "All genetic diseases can be cured using gene therapy."
