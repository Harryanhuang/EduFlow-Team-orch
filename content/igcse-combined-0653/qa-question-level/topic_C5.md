# Topic C5 — Chemical Reactions
## QA Question Level File

**1. [F]** Name the four main types of chemical reaction.

**2. [F]** What is oxidation?

**3. [F]** What is reduction?

**4. [F]** State the reactivity series of metals (potassium, sodium, calcium, magnesium, aluminium, zinc, iron, copper, silver, gold).

**5. [S]** Iron rusts when exposed to air and water. Write the word equation for this reaction.

**6. [S]** A more reactive metal displaces a less reactive metal from its salt solution. Give an example.

**7. [S]** What is a decomposition reaction?

**8. [S]** What is thermal decomposition?

**9. [S]** Give two signs that a chemical reaction has occurred.

**10. [S]** Explain why unreactivity of gold makes it suitable for jewellery.

**11. [S]** What is a combustion reaction?

**12. [S]** Iron is galvanised (coated with zinc). Explain why this protects the iron even if the coating is scratched.

**13. [C]** In the reaction Fe₂O₃ + 2Al → Al₂O₃ + 2Fe: identify which substance is oxidised and which is reduced.

**14. [C]** State the products of complete and incomplete combustion of methane.

**15. [C]** Electrolysis of molten aluminium oxide (Al₂O₃) produces aluminium. Explain the process.

**16. [C]** Evaluate the use of copper and iron in everyday applications, referring to their reactivity.

**17. [C]** Explain what happens during the electrolysis of acidified water (dilute sulfuric acid).

**18. [C]** A student says: "Aluminium is more reactive than iron, so aluminium should rust faster than iron." Evaluate this statement.
