# Topic B2 — Cell Structure and Organisation
## QA Question Level File

**1. [F]** Name three structures found in a plant cell but NOT in an animal cell.

**2. [F]** State two differences between a prokaryotic cell and a eukaryotic cell.

**3. [S]** A cell has a magnification of ×400 and the image size is 2 mm. Calculate the actual size of the specimen. Give your answer in micrometres (μm).

**4. [S]** Describe the function of the following organelles: (a) mitochondria (b) ribosomes (c) cell membrane.

**5. [S]** Explain why multicellular organisms need specialised cells rather than all cells being identical.

**6. [S]** The table shows some cells and their adaptations. Complete the table:
| Cell type | Adaptation | Function |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| Root hair cell | Long projection | (a) ... |
| Red blood cell | (b) ... | Carry oxygen |
| Nerve cell | Long axon | (c) ... |

**7. [C]** Compare the structure of a sperm cell with a root hair cell. Explain how each adaptation relates to its function.

**8. [C]** As a cell grows larger, its surface area to volume ratio decreases. Explain why this limits cell size and how this is overcome in multicellular organisms.

**9. [C]** A student examines a cell under a microscope and observes a cell wall, chloroplasts, and a large central vacuole. The student concludes the cell is a plant cell. Evaluate this conclusion.
