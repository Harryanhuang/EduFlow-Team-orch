# Topic P1 — Motion and Forces
## QA Question Level File

**1. [F]** A car travels 300 m in 20 s. Calculate its average speed.

**2. [F]** State two differences between a scalar quantity and a vector quantity. Give one example of each.

**3. [S]** A car accelerates from rest (0 m/s) to 20 m/s in 8 s. Calculate its acceleration.

**4. [S]** (a) Draw a D-T graph for a car: stationary, then constant speed, then decelerating to a stop. (b) What does the gradient of a D-T graph represent?

**5. [S]** A force of 500 N acts on a mass of 25 kg. Calculate the acceleration produced.

**6. [S]** Draw a V-T graph for a ball thrown vertically upwards and caught again at the same point. Label regions of positive acceleration, zero acceleration, and negative acceleration (deceleration).

**7. [C]** Two identical balls collide head-on with equal speeds and bounce off. Explain using the law of conservation of momentum why their velocities are reversed.

**8. [C]** A car at 20 m/s brakes uniformly at 5 m/s² to rest. Find: (a) braking distance (b) stopping time.

**9. [C]** Newton's third law: for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction. Use this to explain how a fish propels itself through water.

**10. [F]** State Newton's First Law of Motion.

**11. [F]** A boy pushes a box with a force of 30 N to the right, while his friend pushes it with 20 N to the left. What is the resultant force?

**12. [S]** A lorry of mass 8000 kg accelerates at 2 m/s². What is the driving force produced by the engine?

**13. [S]** A parachutist of mass 80 kg falls with constant velocity. What is the drag force acting on the parachutist?

**14. [S]** State the law of conservation of momentum.

**15. [S]** A car of mass 1200 kg travels at 15 m/s. Calculate its kinetic energy.

**16. [C]** Two trolleys A (2 kg, 3 m/s) and B (3 kg, 2 m/s) collide and stick together. Find their common velocity after the collision.

**17. [C]** A railway truck of mass 5000 kg moving at 8 m/s collides with a stationary truck of mass 3000 kg. They couple together. Calculate the speed after collision.

**18. [C]** Evaluate the safety features in vehicles that rely on Newton's laws of motion.
