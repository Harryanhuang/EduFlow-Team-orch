# Topic P2 — Work, Energy, and Power
## QA Question Level File

**1. [F]** State Newton's Second Law of Motion.

**2. [F]** What is work done?

**3. [F]** Calculate the work done when a force of 30 N moves an object 4 m.

**4. [F]** What is gravitational potential energy?

**5. [S]** Calculate the gravitational potential energy of a 3 kg mass raised 5 m above the ground (g = 10 m/s²).

**6. [S]** A car travels at constant speed along a flat road. Does the engine do work?

**7. [S]** What is the relationship between work done and energy?

**8. [S]** Define kinetic energy.

**9. [S]** Calculate the kinetic energy of a 2 kg object moving at 5 m/s.

**10. [S]** Define power.

**11. [S]** A person climbs stairs of height 4 m in 10 s. Their mass is 60 kg. Calculate their power output.

**12. [S]** State the law of conservation of energy.

**13. [C]** A pendulum swings from its highest point to its lowest point. Describe the energy transformations.

**14. [C]** A ball of mass 0.5 kg is dropped from a height of 10 m. Calculate: (a) its GPE at the top (b) its speed just before hitting the ground.

**15. [C]** Explain why a roller coaster car reaches its maximum speed at the bottom of the track.

**16. [C]** Two cars of the same mass travel at the same speed. One crashes into a barrier and stops. The other rolls up a ramp to rest. Compare the work done by the force in each case.

**17. [C]** Evaluate the design of a safety helmet in terms of energy transfer.

**18. [C]** A car of mass 1000 kg accelerates from 0 to 30 m/s. Calculate the increase in kinetic energy and explain what happens to this energy when the car brakes to a stop.
