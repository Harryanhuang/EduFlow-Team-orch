# Topic B10 — Reproduction
## QA Question Level File

**1. [F]** What is sexual reproduction?

**2. [F]** What is asexual reproduction?

**3. [F]** Name the male and female gametes in humans.

**4. [F]** State two differences between the male gamete (sperm) and the female gamete (ovum).

**5. [S]** Describe the structure and function of the flower in sexual reproduction in plants.

**6. [S]** Explain the process of pollination in flowering plants.

**7. [S]** Describe the process of fertilisation in flowering plants.

**8. [S]** What is the difference between pollination and fertilisation in plants?

**9. [S]** Name two male reproductive organs and state their functions.

**10. [S]** Describe the menstrual cycle in humans, naming the four main phases.

**11. [S]** What is the role of oestrogen in the menstrual cycle?

**12. [S]** Explain how a pollen grain is adapted for its function.

**13. [C]** Compare sexual and asexual reproduction in plants, including advantages and disadvantages.

**14. [C]** A plant produces many small seeds. Explain how this is an adaptation for survival and dispersal.

**15. [C]** Evaluate the use of artificial methods of plant propagation in agriculture and horticulture.

**16. [C]** Explain the role of hormones in the control of the menstrual cycle, including the interactions between FSH, LH, oestrogen, and progesterone.

**17. [C]** Describe how a sperm cell is adapted for fertilising the egg cell.

**18. [C]** A farmer wants to produce a large number of identical plants with a specific flower colour and disease resistance. Evaluate the most appropriate method of propagation for this purpose.
