# Question: Question Q-S5-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What does P(B|A) = P(B) mean?
**Answer**: It means events A and B are independent. Knowing that A has occurred does not change the probability of B occurring.
**Explanation**: Independent events: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B). A common error is confusing independence with mutual exclusivity.
