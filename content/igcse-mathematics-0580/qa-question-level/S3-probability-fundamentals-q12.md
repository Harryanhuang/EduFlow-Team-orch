### Question Q-S3-10
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A bag has 5 red, 3 blue, and 2 green balls. A ball is picked, its colour noted, then replaced. What is the probability of picking 2 different colours?
**Answer**: P(different) = 1 - P(same). P(same) = P(RR) + P(BB) + P(GG) = (5/10)² + (3/10)² + (2/10)² = 25/100 + 9/100 + 4/100 = 38/100. P(different) = 62/100 = 31/50.
**Explanation**: Easier to find P(same) and subtract from 1. With replacement, probabilities don't change. A common error is listing all pairs manually.
