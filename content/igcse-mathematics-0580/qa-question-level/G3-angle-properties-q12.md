### Question Q-G3-12
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In triangle ABC, the exterior angle at C is 120°. Angle A = 50°. Find angle B.
**Answer**: Exterior angle = sum of opposite interior angles. 120° = 50° + B, B = 70°.
**Explanation**: Exterior angle theorem: exterior = sum of two opposite interior angles. A common error is 120° - 50° = 70° without justification.
