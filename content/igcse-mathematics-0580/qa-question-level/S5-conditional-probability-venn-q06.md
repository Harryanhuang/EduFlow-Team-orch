# Question: Question Q-S5-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: If P(A) = 0.6, P(B) = 0.5, and P(A and B) = 0.3, find P(A or B).
**Answer**: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A and B) = 0.6 + 0.5 − 0.3 = 0.8.
**Explanation**: Addition formula for probability. A common error is adding instead of subtracting the intersection.
