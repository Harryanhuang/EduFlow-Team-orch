### Question Q-G6-10
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Triangle ABC has AB = AC. D is on BC such that BD = DC. Prove that AD is perpendicular to BC.
**Answer**: In triangles ABD and ACD: AB = AC (given), BD = DC (given), AD = AD (common). By SSS, triangles ABD and ACD are congruent. So angle ADB = angle ADC. Since they form a straight line, each = 90°. AD ⊥ BC.
**Explanation**: SSS congruence. Equal angles on a straight line = 90° each. A common error is assuming AD bisects angle A without proof.
