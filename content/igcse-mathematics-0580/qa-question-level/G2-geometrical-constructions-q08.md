### Question Q-G2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A loci problem: Draw the locus of points equidistant from two points A and B.
**Answer**: The perpendicular bisector of AB. All points on this line are equidistant from A and B.
**Explanation**: The locus of points equidistant from two points is the perpendicular bisector of the line joining them. A common error is drawing the line AB itself.
