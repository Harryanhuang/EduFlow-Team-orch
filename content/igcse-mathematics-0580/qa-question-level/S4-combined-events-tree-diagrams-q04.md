### Question Q-S4-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A biased coin has P(H) = 0.6. It is flipped twice. What is the probability of at least one head?
**Answer**: P(at least 1 H) = 1 - P(TT) = 1 - 0.4² = 1 - 0.16 = 0.84.
**Explanation**: At least one head = 1 - no heads. A common error is 0.6 + 0.6 = 1.2 (probabilities can't exceed 1).
