### Question Q-T3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In 3D trigonometry, a line AB is 10 cm long and makes an angle of 37° with the horizontal. Find the vertical height of B above A.
**Answer**: Vertical height = 10 sin 37° = 6.02 cm.
**Explanation**: In 3D trigonometry, sin θ = vertical component / full length, so vertical = length × sin θ. A common error is using cos instead.
