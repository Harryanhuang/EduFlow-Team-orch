### Question Q-N1-16
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Write 60 as a product of its prime factors in the form 2^a × 3^b × 5^c.
**Answer**: 2^2 × 3^1 × 5^1
**Explanation**: Use a prime factor tree or repeated division. 60 = 2 × 30 = 2 × 2 × 15 = 2 × 2 × 3 × 5. So 60 = 2^2 × 3 × 5. A common error is to write 60 = 6 × 10 and stop, not realising that 6 and 10 are not prime. Another error is omitting an exponent of 1 (writing 3 and 5 without the power).
