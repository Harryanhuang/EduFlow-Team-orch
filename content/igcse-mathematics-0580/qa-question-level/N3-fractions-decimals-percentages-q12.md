### Question Q-N3-11
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A shop increases all prices by 10%. A year later, it reduces all prices by 10%. Are the final prices the same as the original? Explain.
**Answer**: No. If original price = P, after +10%: P × 1.1. After -10%: P × 1.1 × 0.9 = P × 0.99. Final price is 99% of original (1% less). The percentages apply to different base amounts.
**Explanation**: 1.1 × 0.9 = 0.99, not 1. A common error is thinking +10% then -10% cancels out.
