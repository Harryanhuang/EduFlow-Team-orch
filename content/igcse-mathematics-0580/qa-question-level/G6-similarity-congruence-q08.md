### Question Q-G6-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In the diagram, AB is parallel to CD. Prove that triangle ABE is similar to triangle CDE.
**Answer**: Angle A = angle C (alternate angles, AB∥CD). Angle B = angle D (alternate angles). Angle E is common. By AAA, the triangles are similar.
**Explanation**: Two equal angles are enough (AAA similarity). Parallel lines give equal alternate angles. A common error is not identifying which theorem applies.
