### Question Q-G4-11
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In a circle, AB is a diameter. C is on the circumference. Angle CAB = 35°. Find angle ABC.
**Answer**: Angle ACB = 90° (angle in semicircle). In triangle ABC: 35° + 90° + ABC = 180°. ABC = 55°.
**Explanation**: Angle in semicircle = 90°. Triangle angles sum to 180°. A common error is 35° (thinking it's isosceles).
