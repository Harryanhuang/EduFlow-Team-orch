### Question Q-S4-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A coin is flipped three times. Draw a tree diagram. What is the probability of exactly 2 heads?
**Answer**: Outcomes with exactly 2 heads: HHT, HTH, THH. Each has probability 1/8. P = 3/8.
**Explanation**: Tree has 2³ = 8 outcomes. Each outcome = (1/2)³ = 1/8. Three outcomes have exactly 2 heads. A common error is 2/8 = 1/4.
**Tags**: probability, tree-diagram, three-coin

### Question Q-S4-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A bag has 3 red and 2 blue balls. Two balls are picked without replacement. Use a tree diagram to find P(one of each colour).
**Answer**: P(RB) = 3/5 × 2/4 = 6/20. P(BR) = 2/5 × 3/4 = 6/20. P(one each) = 6/20 + 6/20 = 12/20 = 3/5.
**Explanation**: Two paths give one of each: RB and BR. Add the probabilities. A common error is only calculating one path.
**Tags**: probability, tree-diagram, without-replacement

### Question Q-S4-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A biased coin has P(H) = 0.6. It is flipped twice. What is the probability of at least one head?
**Answer**: P(at least 1 H) = 1 - P(TT) = 1 - 0.4² = 1 - 0.16 = 0.84.
**Explanation**: At least one head = 1 - no heads. A common error is 0.6 + 0.6 = 1.2 (probabilities can't exceed 1).
**Tags**: probability, biased-coin, at-least

### Question Q-S4-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A spinner with 4 equal sections (1,2,3,4) is spun twice. What is the probability the sum is 5?
**Answer**: Pairs summing to 5: (1,4), (2,3), (3,2), (4,1). 4 out of 16. P = 4/16 = 1/4.
**Explanation**: Total outcomes = 4×4 = 16. List pairs summing to 5. A common error is 5/16.
**Tags**: probability, spinner, sum

### Question Q-S4-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Event A has probability 0.3 and event B has probability 0.5. If A and B are independent, find P(A and B).
**Answer**: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B) = 0.3 × 0.5 = 0.15.
**Explanation**: For independent events, multiply probabilities. A common error is 0.3 + 0.5 = 0.8.
**Tags**: probability, independent-events, and

### Question Q-S4-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A box contains 5 chocolates and 3 caramels. Sarah picks two at random without replacement. What is the probability both are the same type?
**Answer**: P(CC) = 5/8 × 4/7 = 20/56. P(AA) = 3/8 × 2/7 = 6/56. P(same) = 26/56 = 13/28.
**Explanation**: Both chocolates or both caramels. Add the two probabilities. A common error is only one path.
**Tags**: probability, without-replacement, same-type

### Question Q-S4-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A die is thrown and a coin is flipped. What is the probability of getting a 6 and a head?
**Answer**: P(6 and H) = 1/6 × 1/2 = 1/12.
**Explanation**: Independent events: multiply. A common error is 1/6 + 1/2 = 2/3.
**Tags**: probability, independent, combined

### Question Q-S4-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A bag has 4 red, 3 blue, and 3 green balls. Three balls are picked without replacement. What is the probability all three are different colours?
**Answer**: P(RBG) = 4/10 × 3/9 × 3/8 = 36/720. There are 3! = 6 orderings. P = 6 × 36/720 = 216/720 = 3/10.
**Explanation**: Any ordering of R, B, G works. Each ordering has the same probability (numerator: 4×3×3, denominator: 10×9×8). A common error is one ordering only.
**Tags**: probability, three-events, without-replacement

### Question Q-S4-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A machine produces defective items with probability 0.1. What is the probability that in a sample of 3 items, at least one is defective?
**Answer**: P(at least 1 defective) = 1 - P(none) = 1 - 0.9³ = 1 - 0.729 = 0.271.
**Explanation**: None defective: 0.9³. At least one = 1 - none. A common error is 0.1 × 3 = 0.3.
**Tags**: probability, at-least, complement

### Question Q-S4-10
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Two cards are drawn from a pack of 52 without replacement. What is the probability they are both aces?
**Answer**: P(AA) = 4/52 × 3/51 = 12/2652 = 1/221.
**Explanation**: First ace: 4/52. Second ace (without replacement): 3/51. A common error is 4/52 × 4/52 = 16/2704.
**Tags**: probability, cards, without-replacement
