### Question Q-G2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Describe how to construct the perpendicular bisector of a line segment AB using a compass and straightedge.
**Answer**: 1) Set compass to more than half of AB. 2) Draw arcs from A above and below AB. 3) With same radius, draw arcs from B. 4) Join the two intersection points. This line is the perpendicular bisector.
**Explanation**: The perpendicular bisector passes through the midpoint of AB at 90°. A common error is setting the compass too small (arcs don't intersect).
**Tags**: construction, perpendicular-bisector

### Question Q-G2-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Describe how to bisect an angle using a compass and straightedge.
**Answer**: 1) Draw an arc from the vertex crossing both arms. 2) From each crossing point, draw arcs inside the angle. 3) Join the vertex to where the arcs cross. This is the angle bisector.
**Explanation**: The angle bisector divides the angle into two equal parts. A common error is not keeping the compass radius the same for both arcs.
**Tags**: construction, angle-bisector

### Question Q-G2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Construct a triangle with sides 6 cm, 8 cm, and 10 cm. What type of triangle is this?
**Answer**: Right-angled triangle (6² + 8² = 36 + 64 = 100 = 10², satisfying Pythagoras).
**Explanation**: Draw base 8 cm, arc of 6 cm from one end, arc of 10 cm from the other. Intersection gives third vertex. It's right-angled (Pythagorean triple). A common error is not recognising it's right-angled.
**Tags**: construction, triangle, pythagorean-triple

### Question Q-G2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the construction of a perpendicular from a point P to a line l.
**Answer**: 1) Draw arcs from P crossing l at two points A and B. 2) From A and B, draw arcs below l that intersect. 3) Join P to the intersection point. This is the perpendicular.
**Explanation**: The perpendicular from P to l is the shortest distance. A common error is drawing a line from P to the nearest point on l without construction.
**Tags**: construction, perpendicular-from-point

### Question Q-G2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Construct a 60° angle using only a compass and straightedge.
**Answer**: 1) Draw a line segment AB. 2) Set compass to AB. 3) Draw an arc from A. 4) From where the arc crosses AB, draw another arc crossing the first. 5) Join A to the intersection. The angle is 60°.
**Explanation**: This constructs an equilateral triangle (all angles 60°). A common error is not keeping the compass radius constant.
**Tags**: construction, 60-degree-angle

### Question Q-G2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Construct a rectangle 8 cm by 5 cm using a ruler and set square.
**Answer**: 1) Draw base 8 cm. 2) At each end, construct perpendiculars. 3) Measure 5 cm up each perpendicular. 4) Join the top points.
**Explanation**: A rectangle has four right angles. Use set square or compass for perpendiculars. A common error is not ensuring the opposite sides are parallel.
**Tags**: construction, rectangle

### Question Q-G2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A loci problem: Draw the locus of points equidistant from two points A and B.
**Answer**: The perpendicular bisector of AB. All points on this line are equidistant from A and B.
**Explanation**: The locus of points equidistant from two points is the perpendicular bisector of the line joining them. A common error is drawing the line AB itself.
**Tags**: loci, perpendicular-bisector, equidistant

### Question Q-G2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Construct the locus of points that are 3 cm from a line segment AB of length 8 cm.
**Answer**: Two parallel lines 3 cm above and below AB, plus two semicircles of radius 3 cm at A and B.
**Explanation**: The locus forms a 'stadium' shape: parallel lines + semicircles at the ends. A common error is just drawing parallel lines without the semicircles.
**Tags**: loci, distance-from-line
