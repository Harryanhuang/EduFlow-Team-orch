### Question Q-M1-11
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Find the area of a triangle with base 14 cm and height 9 cm.
**Answer**: ½ × 14 × 9 = 63 cm²
**Explanation**: Area = ½ × base × height. A common error is 14 × 9 = 126 (forgetting the ½).
**Tags**: area, triangle

### Question Q-M2-11
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A cuboid has dimensions 4 cm × 6 cm × 10 cm. Find its total surface area.
**Answer**: 2(4×6 + 4×10 + 6×10) = 2(24+40+60) = 2(124) = 248 cm².
**Explanation**: SA = 2(lw + lh + wh). A common error is 4×6×10 = 240 (volume).
**Tags**: surface-area, cuboid

### Question Q-M1-12
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A rhombus has diagonals 10 cm and 6 cm. Find its area.
**Answer**: ½ × 10 × 6 = 30 cm²
**Explanation**: Area of rhombus = ½ × d₁ × d₂. A common error is 10 × 6 = 60.
**Tags**: area, rhombus, diagonals

### Question Q-M2-12
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A cone has slant height 13 cm and radius 5 cm. Find its volume.
**Answer**: Height = √(13²-5²) = √(169-25) = √144 = 12 cm. Volume = (1/3)π(25)(12) = 100π ≈ 314 cm³.
**Explanation**: Pythagoras for height: h² = l² - r². Then volume formula. A common error is using slant height in the volume formula.
**Tags**: volume, cone, pythagoras
