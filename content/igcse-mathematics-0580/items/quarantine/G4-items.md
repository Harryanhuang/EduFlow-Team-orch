# Items: G4

### Question Q-G4-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: State the circle theorem: The angle subtended by an arc at the centre is...
**Answer**: ...twice the angle subtended at the circumference (on the same arc).
**Explanation**: Angle at centre = 2 × angle at circumference. A common error is saying they are equal.
**Tags**: circle-theorems, angle-at-centre

### Question Q-G4-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: An angle at the circumference in a semicircle is...
**Answer**: ...90° (a right angle).
**Explanation**: The angle in a semicircle is always 90°. This is because the angle at the centre is 180° (straight line), and 180°/2 = 90°. A common error is 180°.
**Tags**: circle-theorems, semicircle

### Question Q-G4-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Angles in the same segment are...
**Answer**: ...equal.
**Explanation**: Any two angles subtended by the same arc at the circumference are equal. A common error is saying they are supplementary.
**Tags**: circle-theorems, same-segment

### Question Q-G4-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Opposite angles in a cyclic quadrilateral...
**Answer**: ...sum to 180°.
**Explanation**: In a cyclic quadrilateral (all vertices on the circle), opposite angles are supplementary. A common error is saying they are equal.
**Tags**: circle-theorems, cyclic-quadrilateral

### Question Q-G4-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: The angle between a tangent and a radius at the point of contact is...
**Answer**: ...90° (a right angle).
**Explanation**: A tangent is always perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact. A common error is 45° or 180°.
**Tags**: circle-theorems, tangent, radius

### Question Q-G4-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In a circle, the angle at the centre is 120°. What is the angle at the circumference on the same arc?
**Answer**: 120° ÷ 2 = 60°
**Explanation**: Angle at circumference = half the angle at centre. A common error is 120° (same).
**Tags**: circle-theorems, angle-at-centre, calculation

### Question Q-G4-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In a circle, O is the centre. A and B are on the circumference. Angle AOB = 80°. Find angle ACB where C is on the major arc.
**Answer**: Angle ACB = 80°/2 = 40° (angle at centre = 2 × angle at circumference).
**Explanation**: The angle subtended at the circumference is half the angle at the centre. A common error is 80°.
**Tags**: circle-theorems, centre-circumference

### Question Q-G4-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: ABCD is a cyclic quadrilateral. Angle A = 95°. Find angle C.
**Answer**: Angle C = 180° - 95° = 85° (opposite angles in a cyclic quadrilateral sum to 180°).
**Explanation**: Opposite angles are supplementary. A common error is angle C = 95° (thinking they are equal).
**Tags**: circle-theorems, cyclic-quadrilateral

### Question Q-G4-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: In a circle, a tangent at A meets a chord AB. The angle between the tangent and chord is 35°. Find the angle in the alternate segment.
**Answer**: 35° (angle between tangent and chord = angle in the alternate segment).
**Explanation**: Alternate segment theorem: the angle between tangent and chord equals the angle in the opposite segment. A common error is 90°-35° = 55°.
**Tags**: circle-theorems, alternate-segment

### Question Q-G4-10
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Two tangents from an external point P touch a circle at A and B. Angle APB = 50°. Find angle AOB where O is the centre.
**Answer**: Angle AOB = 180° - 50° = 130°. (OAP and OBP are 90° each. Quadrilateral OAPB: 90+90+50+AOB = 360, AOB = 130°.)
**Explanation**: Tangents from a point are equal. Radii to points of contact are perpendicular to tangents. Quadrilateral angles = 360°. A common error is 180°-50° = 130° but not justifying.
**Tags**: circle-theorems, tangents-from-point
