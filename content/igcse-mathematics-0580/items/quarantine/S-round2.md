### Question Q-S1-11
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Five numbers have a median of 8, a mode of 10, and a mean of 7. Give a possible set of five numbers.
**Answer**: Mean = 35 (5×7). Median = 8 (3rd number). Mode = 10 (appears most). One possibility: 3, 5, 8, 10, 10. Sum = 36... no. 2, 6, 8, 10, 10 → sum = 36. Need 35. Try 2, 5, 8, 10, 10 → sum = 35 ✓.
**Explanation**: Work from the constraints. Sum must be 35, middle is 8, 10 appears twice. A common error is not checking all conditions.
**Tags**: averages, mode, median, mean, reasoning

### Question Q-S2-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A cumulative frequency graph shows that 40 students scored below 60 marks and 60 students scored below 80 marks. How many scored between 60 and 80?
**Answer**: 60 - 40 = 20 students.
**Explanation**: Cumulative frequency at 80 minus cumulative frequency at 60. A common error is 60 + 40 = 100.
**Tags**: cumulative-frequency, between

### Question Q-S3-11
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A box contains 12 red and 8 blue pens. A pen is picked at random. What is the probability it is not blue?
**Answer**: P(not blue) = P(red) = 12/20 = 3/5.
**Explanation**: Not blue means red. 12 red out of 20 total. A common error is 8/20 = 2/5.
**Tags**: probability, complement, pens

### Question Q-S4-11
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A bag has 6 red and 4 green balls. Two are picked with replacement. What is the probability they are different colours?
**Answer**: P(RG) = 6/10 × 4/10 = 24/100. P(GR) = 4/10 × 6/10 = 24/100. P(different) = 48/100 = 12/25.
**Explanation**: Two orderings: RG and GR. With replacement, probabilities stay the same. A common error is one path only.
**Tags**: probability, with-replacement, different

### Question Q-S1-12
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A grouped frequency table shows: 0-10 (5), 10-20 (8), 20-30 (12), 30-40 (5). Estimate the mean using midpoints.
**Answer**: Midpoints: 5, 15, 25, 35. Sum of (midpoint × frequency): 5×5+15×8+25×12+35×5 = 25+120+300+175 = 620. Total frequency = 30. Mean ≈ 620/30 ≈ 20.7.
**Explanation**: Use midpoints as estimates. A common error is using the class boundaries instead of midpoints.
**Tags**: mean, grouped-data, midpoint

### Question Q-S3-12
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: The probability that a student passes Maths is 0.7 and passes English is 0.8. The probability they pass both is 0.6. What is the probability they pass at least one?
**Answer**: P(M or E) = P(M) + P(E) - P(M and E) = 0.7 + 0.8 - 0.6 = 0.9.
**Explanation**: Inclusion-exclusion formula. A common error is 0.7 + 0.8 = 1.5 (not subtracting the overlap).
**Tags**: probability, inclusion-exclusion, at-least
