### Question Q-M2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Calculate the volume of a cuboid with dimensions 5 cm × 3 cm × 8 cm.
**Answer**: 5 × 3 × 8 = 120 cm³
**Explanation**: Volume = length × width × height. A common error is 5+3+8 = 16.
**Tags**: volume, cuboid

### Question Q-M2-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Calculate the volume of a cylinder with radius 4 cm and height 10 cm. Use π = 3.14.
**Answer**: π × 4² × 10 = 3.14 × 16 × 10 = 502.4 cm³
**Explanation**: Volume = πr²h. A common error is 2πrh (curved surface area).
**Tags**: volume, cylinder

### Question Q-M2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Find the surface area of a cube with side 5 cm.
**Answer**: 6 × 5² = 6 × 25 = 150 cm²
**Explanation**: A cube has 6 identical faces. Each face = 5² = 25 cm². A common error is 5³ = 125 (volume).
**Tags**: surface-area, cube

### Question Q-M2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Calculate the volume of a cone with radius 3 cm and height 7 cm. Use π = 3.14.
**Answer**: (1/3) × π × 3² × 7 = (1/3) × 3.14 × 9 × 7 = 65.94 cm³
**Explanation**: Volume = (1/3)πr²h. A common error is omitting the 1/3.
**Tags**: volume, cone

### Question Q-M2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Find the surface area of a sphere with radius 5 cm. Use π = 3.14.
**Answer**: 4 × π × 5² = 4 × 3.14 × 25 = 314 cm²
**Explanation**: Surface area = 4πr². A common error is 2πr² (hemisphere without base) or (4/3)πr³ (volume).
**Tags**: surface-area, sphere

### Question Q-M2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A triangular prism has a triangular cross-section with base 6 cm, height 4 cm, and length 10 cm. Find the volume.
**Answer**: Area of triangle = ½ × 6 × 4 = 12 cm². Volume = 12 × 10 = 120 cm³.
**Explanation**: Volume = area of cross-section × length. A common error is 6 × 4 × 10 = 240 (not halving for the triangle).
**Tags**: volume, triangular-prism

### Question Q-M2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Find the volume of a sphere with radius 3 cm. Give your answer in terms of π.
**Answer**: (4/3) × π × 3³ = (4/3) × π × 27 = 36π cm³
**Explanation**: Volume = (4/3)πr³. 27 × 4/3 = 36. A common error is 4π × 3² = 36π (using surface area formula).
**Tags**: volume, sphere, in-terms-of-pi

### Question Q-M2-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A cylinder has volume 200π cm³ and radius 5 cm. Find its height.
**Answer**: π × 25 × h = 200π, 25h = 200, h = 8 cm.
**Explanation**: πr²h = 200π, h = 200/(25) = 8. A common error is h = 200/5 = 40 (not squaring the radius).
**Tags**: volume, cylinder, find-height

### Question Q-M2-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A hemisphere has radius 6 cm. Find its total surface area (including the base).
**Answer**: Curved surface: 2πr² = 2π(36) = 72π. Base: πr² = 36π. Total = 108π ≈ 339.3 cm².
**Explanation**: Total surface area of hemisphere = 3πr² (curved + flat base). A common error is 2πr² (curved only).
**Tags**: surface-area, hemisphere

### Question Q-M2-10
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A solid is made by placing a hemisphere on top of a cylinder. Both have radius 3 cm. The cylinder has height 10 cm. Find the total volume.
**Answer**: Cylinder: π(3²)(10) = 90π. Hemisphere: (2/3)π(3³) = 18π. Total = 108π ≈ 339.3 cm³.
**Explanation**: Add cylinder volume and hemisphere volume. Hemisphere = (2/3)πr³. A common error is (4/3)πr³ (full sphere).
**Tags**: volume, composite, hemisphere
