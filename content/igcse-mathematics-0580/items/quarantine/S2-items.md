### Question Q-S2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: In a bar chart, the bar for 'Red' has height 15 and the bar for 'Blue' has height 10. How many more people chose Red than Blue?
**Answer**: 15 - 10 = 5 more people.
**Explanation**: Read the heights from the chart and find the difference. A common error is adding: 15 + 10 = 25.
**Tags**: bar-chart, reading-data

### Question Q-S2-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In a pie chart, the sector for 'Football' has an angle of 90°. If 30 people chose Football, how many people were surveyed in total?
**Answer**: 90°/360° = 1/4 of the total. Total = 30 × 4 = 120 people.
**Explanation**: The angle represents a fraction of the total. 90/360 = 1/4. A common error is 30 × 360/90 = 120 (correct, but some do 30 × 90 = 2700).
**Tags**: pie-chart, total-from-sector

### Question Q-S2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A pictogram uses a symbol to represent 5 people. The row for 'Monday' has 3 full symbols and 1 half symbol. How many people on Monday?
**Answer**: 3.5 × 5 = 17.5. Since people must be whole numbers, this suggests 17 or 18 (likely 17 or 18 depending on context).
**Explanation**: Each symbol = 5. 3.5 symbols = 17.5. In real data this might round. A common error is 3 × 5 = 15 (ignoring the half symbol).
**Tags**: pictogram, reading-data

### Question Q-S2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A stem-and-leaf diagram shows: 1|2 3 5, 2|0 4 7 8, 3|1 6. How many data values are there? What is the median?
**Answer**: 10 values. Ordered: 12, 13, 15, 20, 24, 27, 28, 31, 36. Wait — 1|2 = 12. Values: 12,13,15,20,24,27,28,31,36. That's 9. Median = 5th value = 24.
**Explanation**: Read stem|leaf as full numbers. Median of 9 values = 5th. A common error is not reading the stem correctly.
**Tags**: stem-and-leaf, median, reading-data

### Question Q-S2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A scatter graph shows points trending from bottom-left to top-right. What type of correlation is this?
**Answer**: Positive correlation. As x increases, y tends to increase.
**Explanation**: Bottom-left to top-right = positive correlation. A common error is negative (top-left to bottom-right).
**Tags**: scatter-graph, correlation

### Question Q-S2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A pie chart has sectors: 120°, 80°, 60°, and x°. Find x and the fraction represented by the 120° sector.
**Answer**: x = 360 - 120 - 80 - 60 = 100°. Fraction for 120° = 120/360 = 1/3.
**Explanation**: All sectors sum to 360°. Fraction = angle/360. A common error is 120/100 = 1.2.
**Tags**: pie-chart, missing-angle, fraction

### Question Q-S2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Draw a line of best fit on a scatter graph. Describe what it represents.
**Answer**: A line of best fit passes through the middle of the points, roughly balancing points above and below. It represents the general trend of the data.
**Explanation**: The line should have roughly equal numbers of points above and below. It does not need to pass through any specific point. A common error is connecting the first and last points.
**Tags**: scatter-graph, line-of-best-fit

### Question Q-S2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A histogram has unequal class widths. The frequency density for the class 10-20 is 5, and for 20-40 is 3. Which class has the higher frequency?
**Answer**: Frequency = frequency density × class width. Class 10-20: 5 × 10 = 50. Class 20-40: 3 × 20 = 60. The second class has higher frequency.
**Explanation**: In a histogram, area (not height) represents frequency. A common error is comparing heights directly (5 > 3, but 50 < 60).
**Tags**: histogram, frequency-density, unequal-widths
