### Question Q-S3-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A fair six-sided die is thrown. What is the probability of getting an even number?
**Answer**: Even numbers: 2, 4, 6 (3 out of 6). P(even) = 3/6 = 1/2.
**Explanation**: Count favourable outcomes ÷ total outcomes. A common error is 3/6 = 0.3 (not 0.5).
**Tags**: probability, die, even

### Question Q-S3-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A bag contains 3 red balls and 7 blue balls. One ball is picked at random. What is the probability it is red?
**Answer**: P(red) = 3/10.
**Explanation**: Favourable (red) = 3, total = 10. A common error is 3/7 (red:blue instead of red:total).
**Tags**: probability, bag, single-event

### Question Q-S3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A fair coin is flipped twice. What is the probability of getting exactly one head?
**Answer**: Outcomes: HH, HT, TH, TT. Exactly one head: HT, TH. P = 2/4 = 1/2.
**Explanation**: List all outcomes. HT and TH each have exactly one head. A common error is 1/4 (counting only HT).
**Tags**: probability, coin, two-events

### Question Q-S3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: The probability it rains tomorrow is 0.3. What is the probability it does not rain?
**Answer**: P(not rain) = 1 - 0.3 = 0.7.
**Explanation**: P(not A) = 1 - P(A). A common error is 1 - 0.3 = 0.3.
**Tags**: probability, complement

### Question Q-S3-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A card is picked from a standard pack of 52 cards. What is the probability it is a heart or an ace?
**Answer**: P(heart or ace) = P(heart) + P(ace) - P(heart and ace) = 13/52 + 4/52 - 1/52 = 16/52 = 4/13.
**Explanation**: Subtract the overlap (ace of hearts counted twice). A common error is 13/52 + 4/52 = 17/52 (double counting).
**Tags**: probability, cards, or, overlap

### Question Q-S3-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A spinner has sections: Red (probability 0.4), Blue (0.35), Green (0.25). What is the probability of Red or Green?
**Answer**: 0.4 + 0.25 = 0.65. Red and Green are mutually exclusive.
**Explanation**: For mutually exclusive events, P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B). A common error is 0.4 × 0.25.
**Tags**: probability, spinner, mutually-exclusive

### Question Q-S3-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Two dice are thrown. What is the probability that the total is 7?
**Answer**: Favourable outcomes: (1,6), (2,5), (3,4), (4,3), (5,2), (6,1) = 6 out of 36. P = 6/36 = 1/6.
**Explanation**: List all pairs summing to 7. Total outcomes = 6×6 = 36. A common error is 7/36.
**Tags**: probability, two-dice, sum

### Question Q-S3-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A bag has 4 red and 6 blue balls. Two balls are picked without replacement. What is the probability both are red?
**Answer**: P(RR) = 4/10 × 3/9 = 12/90 = 2/15.
**Explanation**: First red: 4/10. Second red (without replacement): 3/9. Multiply for 'and'. A common error is 4/10 × 4/10 = 16/100 (with replacement).
**Tags**: probability, without-replacement, two-events

### Question Q-S3-10
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A bag has 5 red, 3 blue, and 2 green balls. A ball is picked, its colour noted, then replaced. What is the probability of picking 2 different colours?
**Answer**: P(different) = 1 - P(same). P(same) = P(RR) + P(BB) + P(GG) = (5/10)² + (3/10)² + (2/10)² = 25/100 + 9/100 + 4/100 = 38/100. P(different) = 62/100 = 31/50.
**Explanation**: Easier to find P(same) and subtract from 1. With replacement, probabilities don't change. A common error is listing all pairs manually.
**Tags**: probability, with-replacement, complement
