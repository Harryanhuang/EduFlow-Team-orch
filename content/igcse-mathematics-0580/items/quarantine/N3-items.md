# Items: N3 — Fractions, decimals, percentages
### Question Q-N3-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Convert 3/8 to a decimal and a percentage.
**Answer**: Decimal: 0.375, Percentage: 37.5%
**Explanation**: Divide the numerator by the denominator: 3 ÷ 8 = 0.375. Multiply by 100 to get percentage: 0.375 × 100 = 37.5%. A common error is dividing 8 by 3 instead of 3 by 8.
**Tags**: fractions, decimals, percentages, conversion

### Question Q-N3-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Write 0.6 as a fraction in its simplest form.
**Answer**: 3/5
**Explanation**: 0.6 = 6/10. Simplify by dividing numerator and denominator by their HCF (2): 6/10 = 3/5. A common error is writing 6/10 without simplifying.
**Tags**: decimals, fractions, simplification

### Question Q-N3-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Calculate 2/3 + 1/4. Give your answer as a fraction in its simplest form.
**Answer**: 11/12
**Explanation**: Find a common denominator: LCM of 3 and 4 is 12. 2/3 = 8/12, 1/4 = 3/12. 8/12 + 3/12 = 11/12. A common error is adding numerators and denominators directly: 2/3 + 1/4 = 3/7.
**Tags**: fractions, addition, common-denominator

### Question Q-N3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A shirt costs $48 after a 20% discount. What was the original price?
**Answer**: $60
**Explanation**: If 80% of original = $48, then original = $48 ÷ 0.8 = $60. A common error is calculating 20% of $48 and adding it back ($48 + $9.60 = $57.60), which is wrong because the 20% was taken from the original, not the sale price.
**Tags**: percentages, reverse-percentage, discount

### Question Q-N3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Write 2/5, 0.45, and 42% in order from smallest to largest.
**Answer**: 2/5 (= 0.40), 42% (= 0.42), 0.45
**Explanation**: Convert all to decimals: 2/5 = 0.4, 42% = 0.42, 0.45 = 0.45. Order: 0.4, 0.42, 0.45. A common error is not converting to the same form before comparing.
**Tags**: ordering, fractions, decimals, percentages

### Question Q-N3-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Calculate 1 1/2 × 2 2/3. Give your answer as a mixed number.
**Answer**: 4
**Explanation**: Convert to improper fractions: 1 1/2 = 3/2, 2 2/3 = 8/3. Multiply: (3×8)/(2×3) = 24/6 = 4. A common error is multiplying whole numbers and fractions separately.
**Tags**: fractions, multiplication, mixed-numbers

### Question Q-N3-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A price of $350 increases by 15%. Calculate the new price.
**Answer**: $402.50
**Explanation**: New price = $350 × 1.15 = $402.50. Alternatively: 15% of $350 = $52.50, new price = $350 + $52.50 = $402.50. A common error is subtracting instead of adding, or calculating 15% incorrectly.
**Tags**: percentages, percentage-increase, price

### Question Q-N3-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Write 0.072 recurring (0.0727272...) as a fraction.
**Answer**: 4/55
**Explanation**: Let x = 0.0727272... Then 100x = 7.272727... and x = 0.072727... Subtract: 99x = 7.2, so x = 7.2/99 = 72/990 = 4/55. A common error is treating it as 0.072 (terminating) = 72/1000.
**Tags**: recurring-decimals, fractions, algebraic-method

### Question Q-N3-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Express 45 cm as a percentage of 1.5 m.
**Answer**: 30%
**Explanation**: Convert to the same unit: 1.5 m = 150 cm. (45/150) × 100 = 30%. A common error is using 1.5 instead of 150: (45/1.5) = 3000%.
**Tags**: percentages, units, conversion

### Question Q-N3-10
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Simplify: (3/4) ÷ (2/5)
**Answer**: 15/8 or 1 7/8
**Explanation**: To divide fractions, multiply by the reciprocal: (3/4) × (5/2) = 15/8. A common error is dividing numerators and denominators: 3÷2 / 4÷5.
**Tags**: fractions, division, reciprocal

### Question Q-N3-11
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A shop increases all prices by 10%. A year later, it reduces all prices by 10%. Are the final prices the same as the original? Explain.
**Answer**: No. If original price = P, after +10%: P × 1.1. After -10%: P × 1.1 × 0.9 = P × 0.99. Final price is 99% of original (1% less). The percentages apply to different base amounts.
**Explanation**: 1.1 × 0.9 = 0.99, not 1. A common error is thinking +10% then -10% cancels out.
**Tags**: percentages, compound-change, reasoning
