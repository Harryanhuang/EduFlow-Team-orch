### Question Q-M1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Calculate the area of a rectangle with length 12 cm and width 7 cm.
**Answer**: 12 × 7 = 84 cm²
**Explanation**: Area = length × width. A common error is 12 + 7 = 19 (perimeter of two sides).
**Tags**: area, rectangle

### Question Q-M1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Calculate the perimeter of a triangle with sides 5 cm, 12 cm, and 13 cm.
**Answer**: 5 + 12 + 13 = 30 cm
**Explanation**: Perimeter = sum of all sides. A common error is using the area formula instead.
**Tags**: perimeter, triangle

### Question Q-M1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Calculate the area of a trapezium with parallel sides 8 cm and 12 cm, height 5 cm.
**Answer**: ½(8 + 12) × 5 = ½ × 20 × 5 = 50 cm²
**Explanation**: Area of trapezium = ½(a+b)h. A common error is (a+b)×h without the ½.
**Tags**: area, trapezium

### Question Q-M1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Find the area of a circle with radius 7 cm. Give your answer to 1 decimal place. Use π = 3.142.
**Answer**: π × 7² = 3.142 × 49 = 153.958 ≈ 154.0 cm²
**Explanation**: Area = πr². A common error is πd (circumference formula) or π × 7 = 22.0.
**Tags**: area, circle

### Question Q-M1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A composite shape is made of a rectangle (10 cm × 6 cm) with a semicircle on top (diameter 10 cm). Find the total area.
**Answer**: Rectangle: 60 cm². Semicircle: ½π(5)² = ½π(25) ≈ 39.27 cm². Total ≈ 99.3 cm².
**Explanation**: Add the areas. Semicircle radius = 5 (half of 10). A common error is using diameter instead of radius for the semicircle.
**Tags**: area, composite, semicircle

### Question Q-M1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Find the circumference of a circle with diameter 14 cm. Use π = 22/7.
**Answer**: πd = (22/7) × 14 = 44 cm
**Explanation**: Circumference = πd. With π = 22/7, the 7 cancels with 14. A common error is πr² (area formula).
**Tags**: circumference, circle, pi

### Question Q-M1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A garden is 15 m by 10 m. A path 1 m wide runs around the outside. Find the area of the path.
**Answer**: Outer rectangle: 17 × 12 = 204 m². Garden: 15 × 10 = 150 m². Path: 204 - 150 = 54 m².
**Explanation**: Outer dimensions add 1 m on each side: 15+2 = 17, 10+2 = 12. Path area = outer - inner. A common error is 15×1 + 10×1 = 25 (not accounting for corners).
**Tags**: area, path, composite

### Question Q-M1-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Find the area of a parallelogram with base 10 cm and height 6 cm.
**Answer**: 10 × 6 = 60 cm²
**Explanation**: Area = base × height (perpendicular height, not slant). A common error is using the slant height instead of the perpendicular height.
**Tags**: area, parallelogram

### Question Q-M1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A rectangular field has a perimeter of 60 m. Its length is twice its width. Find its area.
**Answer**: 2(l+w) = 60, l = 2w. 2(2w+w) = 60, 6w = 60, w = 10, l = 20. Area = 20 × 10 = 200 m².
**Explanation**: Set up equations from the information. A common error is perimeter = l+w = 60 (missing the 2).
**Tags**: area, perimeter, algebra

### Question Q-M1-10
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A sector of a circle has radius 6 cm and angle 60°. Find the area of the sector.
**Answer**: (60/360) × π × 6² = (1/6) × π × 36 = 6π ≈ 18.85 cm².
**Explanation**: Area of sector = (angle/360) × πr². A common error is (60/360) × π × 6 (using r, not r²).
**Tags**: area, sector, circle
