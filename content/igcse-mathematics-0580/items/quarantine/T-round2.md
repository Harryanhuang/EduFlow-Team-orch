### Question Q-T1-11
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In a right-angled triangle, the two shorter sides are 7 cm and 24 cm. Find the hypotenuse.
**Answer**: √(49 + 576) = √625 = 25 cm.
**Explanation**: Pythagoras: c² = a² + b². 7-24-25 is a Pythagorean triple. A common error is 7+24 = 31.

### Question Q-T1-12
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A right-angled triangle has hypotenuse 10 cm and one angle 35°. Find the area.
**Answer**: Opposite = 10×sin35° ≈ 5.74, adjacent = 10×cos35° ≈ 8.19. Area = ½×5.74×8.19 ≈ 23.5 cm².
**Explanation**: Find both legs using sin and cos, then ½×base×height. A common error is ½×10×10 = 50.
**Tags**: trigonometry, area, right-angled
