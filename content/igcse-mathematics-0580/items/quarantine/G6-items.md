### Question Q-G6-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: State the four conditions for triangle congruence.
**Answer**: SSS (three sides equal), SAS (two sides and included angle), ASA/AAS (two angles and a side), RHS (right angle, hypotenuse, one other side).
**Explanation**: These are the four congruence tests. A common error is SSA (which is not sufficient for congruence).
**Tags**: congruence, conditions

### Question Q-G6-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Two triangles are similar. One has sides 3, 4, 5. The other has sides 6, 8, 10. What is the scale factor?
**Answer**: 6/3 = 8/4 = 10/5 = 2. Scale factor = 2.
**Explanation**: Corresponding sides are in the same ratio. A common error is 6-3 = 3 (using difference, not ratio).
**Tags**: similarity, scale-factor

### Question Q-G6-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Triangle ABC is similar to triangle DEF. AB = 4 cm, DE = 6 cm, BC = 5 cm. Find EF.
**Answer**: Scale factor = 6/4 = 1.5. EF = 5 × 1.5 = 7.5 cm.
**Explanation**: Ratio of corresponding sides is constant. EF/BC = DE/AB = 6/4. A common error is EF = 5+2 = 7 (adding the difference).
**Tags**: similarity, corresponding-sides

### Question Q-G6-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Two similar shapes have areas 20 cm² and 80 cm². Find the linear scale factor.
**Answer**: Area scale factor = 80/20 = 4. Linear scale factor = √4 = 2.
**Explanation**: Area ratio = (linear ratio)². Linear ratio = √(area ratio). A common error is linear scale factor = 4 (not taking the square root).
**Tags**: similarity, area, scale-factor

### Question Q-G6-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Two similar solids have volumes in the ratio 27:8. Find the ratio of their surface areas.
**Answer**: Linear ratio = ∛(27/8) = 3/2. Area ratio = (3/2)² = 9/4.
**Explanation**: Volume ratio = (linear ratio)³. Area ratio = (linear ratio)². Linear = ∛(27/8) = 3/2. Area = (3/2)² = 9/4. A common error is area ratio = 27/8.
**Tags**: similarity, volume, surface-area

### Question Q-G6-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Prove that two triangles are congruent using SAS. Triangle ABC: AB = 5, BC = 7, angle B = 60°. Triangle DEF: DE = 5, EF = 7, angle E = 60°.
**Answer**: AB = DE (5), BC = EF (7), angle B = angle E (60°). The angle is between the two sides. Therefore, by SAS, the triangles are congruent.
**Explanation**: SAS requires two sides and the included angle. The angle must be between the two given sides. A common error is using a non-included angle.
**Tags**: congruence, SAS, proof

### Question Q-G6-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In the diagram, AB is parallel to CD. Prove that triangle ABE is similar to triangle CDE.
**Answer**: Angle A = angle C (alternate angles, AB∥CD). Angle B = angle D (alternate angles). Angle E is common. By AAA, the triangles are similar.
**Explanation**: Two equal angles are enough (AAA similarity). Parallel lines give equal alternate angles. A common error is not identifying which theorem applies.
**Tags**: similarity, parallel-lines, proof

### Question Q-G6-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A cone has radius 3 cm and height 8 cm. A similar cone has radius 4.5 cm. Find its height.
**Answer**: Scale factor = 4.5/3 = 1.5. Height = 8 × 1.5 = 12 cm.
**Explanation**: Corresponding lengths are in the same ratio. A common error is 8 + 1.5 = 9.5.
**Tags**: similarity, cone, corresponding-lengths

### Question Q-G6-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Two similar cylinders have heights 6 cm and 9 cm. The smaller has volume 200 cm³. Find the volume of the larger.
**Answer**: Linear ratio = 9/6 = 3/2. Volume ratio = (3/2)³ = 27/8. Volume = 200 × 27/8 = 675 cm³.
**Explanation**: Volume ratio = (linear ratio)³. 200 × (27/8) = 675. A common error is 200 × 3/2 = 300 (linear, not volume).
**Tags**: similarity, cylinder, volume

### Question Q-G6-10
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Triangle ABC has AB = AC. D is on BC such that BD = DC. Prove that AD is perpendicular to BC.
**Answer**: In triangles ABD and ACD: AB = AC (given), BD = DC (given), AD = AD (common). By SSS, triangles ABD and ACD are congruent. So angle ADB = angle ADC. Since they form a straight line, each = 90°. AD ⊥ BC.
**Explanation**: SSS congruence. Equal angles on a straight line = 90° each. A common error is assuming AD bisects angle A without proof.
**Tags**: congruence, isosceles, proof
