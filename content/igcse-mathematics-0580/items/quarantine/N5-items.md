### Question Q-N5-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Simplify the ratio 24:36 to its simplest form.
**Answer**: 2:3
**Explanation**: HCF of 24 and 36 is 12. 24÷12 = 2, 36÷12 = 3. Ratio = 2:3. A common error is dividing by a common factor that isn't the HCF, giving 4:6 (not simplest).
**Tags**: ratio, simplification

### Question Q-N5-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Divide $60 in the ratio 2:3.
**Answer**: $24 and $36
**Explanation**: Total parts = 2+3 = 5. One part = $60÷5 = $12. First share = 2×$12 = $24. Second share = 3×$12 = $36. Check: $24+$36 = $60. A common error is $20 and $40.
**Tags**: ratio, sharing, proportion

### Question Q-N5-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A recipe for 4 people needs 300g of flour. How much flour is needed for 10 people?
**Answer**: 750g
**Explanation**: Per person: 300÷4 = 75g. For 10 people: 75×10 = 750g. Or using ratio: 300/4 = x/10, x = 300×10/4 = 750. A common error is 300+10 = 310 (adding instead of scaling).
**Tags**: proportion, recipe, scaling

### Question Q-N5-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: The ratio of boys to girls in a class is 5:4. There are 27 students. How many are boys?
**Answer**: 15 boys
**Explanation**: Total parts = 5+4 = 9. One part = 27÷9 = 3. Boys = 5×3 = 15. A common error is boys = 5/9 × 27 = 15 (correct method) but confusing which is which.
**Tags**: ratio, sharing, word-problem

### Question Q-N5-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A car travels 240 km using 20 litres of petrol. Calculate its fuel consumption in km per litre.
**Answer**: 12 km/litre
**Explanation**: 240 km ÷ 20 litres = 12 km/litre. A common error is 20÷240 = 0.083 (litres per km instead of km per litre).
**Tags**: rate, fuel-consumption

### Question Q-N5-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Map scale is 1:50,000. Two towns are 8.5 cm apart on the map. Find the real distance in km.
**Answer**: 8.5 × 50,000 = 425,000 cm = 4,250 m = 4.25 km
**Explanation**: Real distance = 8.5 × 50,000 = 425,000 cm. Convert: ÷100 = 4,250 m, ÷1000 = 4.25 km. A common error is forgetting to convert cm to km.
**Tags**: ratio, scale, map, conversion

### Question Q-N5-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: If y is directly proportional to x and y = 15 when x = 3, find y when x = 7.
**Answer**: y = kx where k = 15/3 = 5. When x = 7: y = 5×7 = 35.
**Explanation**: Find constant of proportionality: k = y/x = 15/3 = 5. Then y = 5x. When x=7: y = 35. A common error is adding: 15+4 = 19 (not proportional).
**Tags**: direct-proportion, constant, algebra

### Question Q-N5-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: If y is inversely proportional to x and y = 4 when x = 6, find y when x = 8.
**Answer**: y = k/x where k = 4×6 = 24. When x = 8: y = 24/8 = 3.
**Explanation**: y = k/x, k = y×x = 4×6 = 24. y = 24/x. When x=8: y = 24/8 = 3. A common error is treating it as direct proportion: y = 4×8/6.
**Tags**: inverse-proportion, constant

### Question Q-N5-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: 3 workers can complete a job in 8 days. How long would 6 workers take (working at the same rate)?
**Answer**: 4 days
**Explanation**: Inverse proportion: more workers = less time. 3×8 = 24 worker-days. 24÷6 = 4 days. A common error is direct proportion: 6/3 × 8 = 16 days (wrong — doubling workers halves time).
**Tags**: inverse-proportion, work-rate

### Question Q-N5-10
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A and B are in the ratio 3:5. B and C are in the ratio 2:7. Find the ratio A:B:C.
**Answer**: A:B = 3:5 and B:C = 2:7. Make B the same: A:B = 6:10, B:C = 10:35. So A:B:C = 6:10:35.
**Explanation**: Make B equal in both ratios. LCM of 5 and 2 is 10. A:B = 6:10, B:C = 10:35. Combined: 6:10:35. A common error is writing 3:5:7.
**Tags**: ratio, combined-ratio
