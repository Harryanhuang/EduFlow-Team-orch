### Question Q-T1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: In a right-angled triangle, the opposite side to angle θ is 3 and the hypotenuse is 5. Find sin θ.
**Answer**: sin θ = 3/5 = 0.6
**Explanation**: SOH: sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse. A common error is adjacent/hypotenuse (cos).
**Tags**: trigonometry, sine, soh-cah-toa

### Question Q-T1-02

**Answer**: tan θ = 3/4 = 0.75

### Question Q-T1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In a right-angled triangle, the hypotenuse is 13 cm and one angle is 30°. Find the length of the opposite side.
**Answer**: sin 30° = opposite/13, opposite = 13 × sin 30° = 13 × 0.5 = 6.5 cm.
**Explanation**: sin 30° = 0.5. Opposite = hypotenuse × sin θ. A common error is using cos or tan.
**Tags**: trigonometry, finding-side, sine

### Question Q-T1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In a right-angled triangle, the adjacent is 7 cm and the hypotenuse is 25 cm. Find the angle θ.
**Answer**: cos θ = 7/25 = 0.28, θ = cos⁻¹(0.28) ≈ 73.7°.
**Explanation**: cos θ = adjacent/hypotenuse. Use inverse cos. A common error is sin⁻¹(7/25).
**Tags**: trigonometry, finding-angle, cosine

### Question Q-T1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A ladder 5 m long leans against a wall. The foot of the ladder is 1.5 m from the wall. What angle does the ladder make with the ground?
**Answer**: cos θ = 1.5/5 = 0.3, θ = cos⁻¹(0.3) ≈ 72.5°.
**Explanation**: Adjacent = 1.5, hypotenuse = 5. cos θ = 1.5/5. A common error is sin⁻¹(1.5/5).
**Tags**: trigonometry, ladder, word-problem

### Question Q-T1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In a right-angled triangle, angle θ = 40° and the opposite side is 8 cm. Find the adjacent side.
**Answer**: tan 40° = 8/adjacent, adjacent = 8/tan 40° ≈ 8/0.8391 ≈ 9.53 cm.
**Explanation**: tan θ = opposite/adjacent. Rearrange: adjacent = opposite/tan θ. A common error is 8 × tan 40°.
**Tags**: trigonometry, finding-side, tangent

### Question Q-T1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Verify that sin²30° + cos²30° = 1.
**Answer**: sin 30° = 0.5, cos 30° = √3/2 ≈ 0.866. sin²30° = 0.25, cos²30° = 0.75. 0.25 + 0.75 = 1.
**Explanation**: Pythagorean identity: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 for all θ. A common error is sin² + cos² = 2.
**Tags**: trigonometry, identity, pythagorean

### Question Q-T1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: From the top of a 30 m cliff, the angle of depression to a boat is 25°. How far is the boat from the base of the cliff?
**Answer**: tan 25° = 30/d, d = 30/tan 25° ≈ 30/0.4663 ≈ 64.3 m.
**Explanation**: Angle of depression = angle of elevation from boat. tan θ = opposite/adjacent. A common error is 30 × tan 25°.
**Tags**: trigonometry, angle-of-depression, word-problem

### Question Q-T1-10
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A rectangular field is 100 m by 60 m. Find the angle that the diagonal makes with the longer side.
**Answer**: tan θ = 60/100 = 0.6, θ = tan⁻¹(0.6) ≈ 31.0°.
**Explanation**: Opposite = 60 (width), adjacent = 100 (length). tan⁻¹(60/100). A common error is tan⁻¹(100/60) (swapping opposite and adjacent).
