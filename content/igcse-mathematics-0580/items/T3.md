# Mathematics 0580 — T3 3D Trigonometry and Bearings
## Items File

**Item 1 [F]**
Question: What is a bearing? Express a bearing as a three-digit number.
Answer: A bearing is a direction measured clockwise from North, written as 000° to 360°. North = 000°, East = 090°, South = 180°.
Difficulty: F
Topic: T3
Explanation: Always write three digits. A common error is writing 90° instead of 090°.
Tags: bearings, trigonometry, navigation, direction

**Item 2 [F]**
Question: Sketch a diagram showing a bearing of 125° from point A. Describe the direction.
Answer: Draw a North line at A. Measure 125° clockwise from North. The direction is in the South-East quadrant, between South (180°) and East (090°).
Difficulty: F
Topic: T3
Explanation: Clockwise from North. A common error is measuring anticlockwise instead.
Tags: bearings, trigonometry, diagram, clock face

**Item 3 [S]**
Question: A ship sails 12 km on a bearing of 045° from point A. How far north and east has it travelled?
Answer: North = 12 cos 45° = 8.49 km. East = 12 sin 45° = 8.49 km.
Difficulty: S
Topic: T3
Explanation: North = d cos θ, east = d sin θ. A common error is swapping the formulas.
Tags: bearings, trigonometry, components, vector resolution

**Item 4 [S]**
Question: From point A, point B is 8 km away on a bearing of 120°. Find the coordinates of B relative to A.
Answer: North = 8 cos 120° = −4 km (4 km South). East = 8 sin 120° = 6.93 km East.
Difficulty: S
Topic: T3
Explanation: cos bearing = north component, sin bearing = east component. Watch the sign: cos 120° is negative.
Tags: bearings, trigonometry, coordinates, negative components

**Item 5 [S]**
Question: In 3D trigonometry, a line AB is 10 cm long and makes an angle of 37° with the horizontal. Find the vertical height of B above A.
Answer: Vertical height = 10 sin 37° = 6.02 cm.
Difficulty: S
Topic: T3
Explanation: sin θ = vertical component / full length, so vertical = length × sin θ. A common error is using cos.
Tags: 3D trigonometry, elevation, horizontal, vertical component

**Item 6 [S]**
Question: From point A, a point C is 5 km north of B and 3 km east of A. Using the bearing from A to C, find the distance AC.
Answer: North = 5 km, East = 3 km. AC = √(5² + 3²) = √34 = 5.83 km.
Difficulty: S
Topic: T3
Explanation: Treat north and east as perpendicular components. Use Pythagoras for the resultant distance AC.
Tags: bearings, Pythagoras, resultant distance, trigonometry

**Item 7 [C]**
Question: A tower at B has an angle of elevation of 15° from point A. The tower height is 60 m. A second measurement is taken from point C, which is 20 m further from the tower along the same line. Find the angle of elevation from C.
Answer: AB = 60 / tan 15° = 223.9 m. AC = 243.9 m. tan θ = 60 / 243.9 → θ = 13.8°.
Difficulty: C
Topic: T3
Explanation: tan θ = opposite / adjacent. Set up two right triangles sharing the tower height. A common error is mixing up the two measurement positions.
Tags: 3D trigonometry, elevation, angle of elevation, two-triangle problem

**Item 8 [C]**
Question: A plane flies 400 km on a bearing of 030° and then 300 km on a bearing of 120°. Find the bearing and distance of the plane from its starting point.
Answer: North total = 196.4 km, East total = 459.8 km. Distance = √(196.4² + 459.8²) = 498.2 km. Bearing = tan⁻¹(459.8/196.4) = 066.9°.
Difficulty: C
Topic: T3
Explanation: Resolve both legs into north and east components, add them, then use Pythagoras for distance and tan⁻¹ for bearing. A common error is adding the angles directly instead of resolving to components first.
Tags: bearings, vector addition, resultant, composite journey, trigonometry

**Item 9 [C]**
Question: Point P is at (3, 4, 0) and point Q is at (6, 7, 5) in a 3D coordinate system. Find the angle that line PQ makes with the horizontal plane.
Answer: PQ = (3, 3, 5). Length of PQ = √43 = 6.56. Horizontal component = √(3²+3²) = √18 = 4.24. tan θ = 5/4.24 → θ = 49.8°.
Difficulty: C
Topic: T3
Explanation: In 3D, the angle with the horizontal uses the vertical component and the horizontal projection. A common error is using the full length as the denominator instead of the horizontal component.
Tags: 3D trigonometry, coordinates, angle with horizontal, vector
