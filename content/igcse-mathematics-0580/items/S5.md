# Mathematics 0580 — S5 Conditional Probability & Venn Diagrams Items

## Item 1
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the formula for conditional probability P(A and B)?
**Answer**: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B|A) = P(B) × P(A|B).
**Explanation**: Multiply probability of A by probability of B given A. A common error is just multiplying P(A) × P(B) (only works if independent).
**Tags**: conditional-probability, probability, formula

## Item 2
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What does P(A ∪ B) mean?
**Answer**: P(A or B or both) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A ∩ B).
**Explanation**: Union = OR. Intersection = AND. A common error is adding without subtracting the overlap.
**Tags**: probability, notation, union, intersection

## Item 3
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Bag has 5 red and 3 blue balls. Two drawn without replacement. Find P(both red).
**Answer**: P(first red) = 5/8. P(second red | first red) = 4/7. P(both red) = 5/8 × 4/7 = 5/14.
**Explanation**: Conditional without replacement. A common error is using 5/8 × 4/8 (with replacement).
**Tags**: conditional-probability, probability, without-replacement

## Item 4
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: n(A) = 20, n(B) = 15, n(A ∩ B) = 8, n(U) = 40. Find n(A ∪ B).
**Answer**: n(A ∪ B) = 20 + 15 − 8 = 27.
**Explanation**: Union formula. A common error is forgetting to subtract the overlap.
**Tags**: Venn-diagram, probability, sets

## Item 5
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What does P(B|A) = P(B) mean?
**Answer**: A and B are independent. Knowing A has occurred does not change P(B).
**Explanation**: Independence check. A common error is confusing with mutual exclusivity.
**Tags**: independence, probability, conditional

## Item 6
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: P(A) = 0.6, P(B) = 0.5, P(A and B) = 0.3. Find P(A or B).
**Answer**: P(A or B) = 0.6 + 0.5 − 0.3 = 0.8.
**Explanation**: Addition formula. A common error is adding instead of subtracting the intersection.
**Tags**: probability, addition-rule, union

## Item 7
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Disease affects 1 in 100. Test 95% accurate. Find P(disease | positive).
**Answer**: P(pos) = (0.95×0.01) + (0.05×0.99) = 0.059. P(disease|pos) = 0.0095/0.059 = 16.1%.
**Explanation**: Bayes' theorem. A common error is forgetting false positives in P(positive).
**Tags**: Bayes-theorem, probability, conditional

## Item 8
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: P(A) = 0.4, P(B) = 0.5, P(A ∩ B) = 0.2. Find P(A ∩ B').
**Answer**: P(A ∩ B') = P(A) − P(A ∩ B) = 0.4 − 0.2 = 0.2.
**Explanation**: In A but not in B. A common error is confusing with union.
**Tags**: Venn-diagram, probability, complement

## Item 9
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: P(A) = 3/4, P(B) = 2/3, P(A ∩ B) = 1/2. Are A and B independent?
**Answer**: P(A) × P(B) = 1/2 = P(A ∩ B). Yes, independent.
**Explanation**: Independent if P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B). A common error is assuming without checking.
**Tags**: independence, probability, conditional
