# Mathematics 0580 — S6 Cumulative Frequency, Box Plots, Scatter Graphs Items

## Item 1
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is cumulative frequency?
**Answer**: Cumulative frequency is the running total of frequencies. Add each frequency to the sum of all previous frequencies.
**Explanation**: CF accumulates down a grouped frequency table. A common error is restarting the sum at each class.
**Tags**: cumulative-frequency, statistics, data

## Item 2
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is a box plot and what does it show?
**Answer**: Shows minimum, Q1, median, Q3, maximum. The box spans Q1 to Q3 with a line at the median.
**Explanation**: Five-number summary. A common error is confusing Q1 with the minimum.
**Tags**: box-plot, statistics, quartile

## Item 3
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Draw a scatter graph and describe the types of correlation.
**Answer**: Positive (both increase), negative (one up, one down), no correlation. Strong = points close to a line.
**Explanation**: Direction and strength. A common error is confusing direction with strength.
**Tags**: scatter-graph, correlation, statistics

## Item 4
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the equation of the line of best fit?
**Answer**: y = mx + c. Gradient m = rate of change. Intercept c = y-value when x = 0.
**Explanation**: Linear regression. A common error is guessing without using data points.
**Tags**: line-of-best-fit, regression, statistics

## Item 5
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: From a cumulative frequency curve, find the median and IQR.
**Answer**: Median at CF = n/2. Q1 at CF = n/4, Q3 at CF = 3n/4. IQR = Q3 − Q1.
**Explanation**: Read from CF curve. A common error is finding the value instead of the position.
**Tags**: cumulative-frequency, quartile, median, IQR

## Item 6
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What does standard deviation measure?
**Answer**: Spread of data around the mean. σ = √(Σ(x − x̄)²/n). Small σ = data close to the mean.
**Explanation**: Dispersion measure. A common error is confusing variance with standard deviation.
**Tags**: standard-deviation, statistics, spread

## Item 7
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Two data sets have the same mean but different SDs. What does this tell you?
**Answer**: Larger SD = more spread from the mean. Same average but more variability.
**Explanation**: SD measures dispersion. A common error is saying the means differ.
**Tags**: standard-deviation, statistics, comparison

## Item 8
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare a box plot and a histogram for presenting data.
**Answer**: Histogram shows distribution shape. Box plot shows five-number summary. Histogram for shape; box plot for comparing sets.
**Explanation**: Different purposes. A common error is using the wrong graph for the question.
**Tags**: box-plot, histogram, statistics, comparison

## Item 9
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Find mean and SD of: 4, 6, 6, 7, 8, 10, 12.
**Answer**: Mean = 53/7 = 7.57. σ = √7.63 = 2.76.
**Explanation**: Mean = Σx/n. SD = √(Σ(x − x̄)²/n). A common error is forgetting to square the deviations.
**Tags**: standard-deviation, mean, statistics

## Item 10
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A bag contains 3 red balls and 2 blue balls. One ball is drawn at random. What is the probability of drawing a red ball?
**Answer**: P(red) = 3/5.
**Explanation**: Probability = favourable outcomes / total outcomes. A common error is forgetting to count all possible outcomes.
**Tags**: basic-probability, probability, fractions

## Item 11
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Two coins are tossed. List all possible outcomes and find the probability of getting exactly one head.
**Answer**: Outcomes: HH, HT, TH, TT. P(exactly one head) = 2/4 = 1/2.
**Explanation**: Sample space has 4 equally likely outcomes. A common error is not listing all outcomes systematically.
**Tags**: basic-probability, outcomes, coins

## Item 12
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A dice is rolled. What is the probability of getting an even number or a number greater than 3?
**Answer**: P(even or >3) = P(even) + P(>3) − P(even and >3) = 1/2 + 1/2 − 2/6 = 2/3.
**Explanation**: Use inclusion-exclusion for "or". A common error is adding P(A) and P(B) without subtracting the overlap.
**Tags**: basic-probability, union, dice

## Item 13
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A drawer contains 4 red socks and 6 black socks. Two socks are drawn without replacement. Draw a tree diagram and find the probability that both socks are red.
**Answer**: P(R then R) = (4/10) × (3/9) = 12/90 = 2/15.
**Explanation**: Without replacement means the second probability changes. A common error is using (4/10)² for both draws.
**Tags**: tree-diagram, probability, without-replacement

## Item 14
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A bag has 5 white and 3 green balls. A ball is drawn and not replaced. Then a second ball is drawn. Find P(green then green).
**Answer**: P(G1) = 3/8. P(G2 | G1) = 2/7. P(G and G) = (3/8) × (2/7) = 3/28.
**Explanation**: Conditional probability on the second draw. A common error is forgetting to reduce the total after the first draw.
**Tags**: tree-diagram, conditional-probability, without-replacement

## Item 15
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: In a class, 60% of students play football, 40% play basketball, and 20% play both. Find P(football given basketball).
**Answer**: P(F | B) = P(F and B) / P(B) = 0.20 / 0.40 = 0.5.
**Explanation**: P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B). A common error is confusing P(A|B) with P(B|A).
**Tags**: conditional-probability, Venn-diagram, set-theory

## Item 16
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A disease affects 1 in 1000 people. The test is 99% accurate. A person tests positive. What is the actual probability they have the disease?
**Answer**: P(disease | positive) = (0.001 × 0.99) / (0.001 × 0.99 + 0.999 × 0.01) ≈ 0.090 or 9%.
**Explanation**: Bayesian probability — most people overestimate. A common error is ignoring false positives.
**Tags**: conditional-probability, Bayes-theorem, real-world-probability
