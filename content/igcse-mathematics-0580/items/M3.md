# Mathematics 0580 — M3 Arc Length, Sector Area, Radian Measure Items

## Item 1
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the formula for the arc length of a sector of a circle?
**Answer**: Arc length = (θ/360) × 2πr = rθ (when θ is in radians).
**Explanation**: Fraction of the circumference. A common error is using the diameter instead of the radius.
**Tags**: arc-length, circle, sector, trigonometry

## Item 2
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Calculate the area of a sector with radius 6 cm and angle 60°.
**Answer**: Area = (60/360) × π × 6² = 6π ≈ 18.85 cm².
**Explanation**: Fraction of full circle area. A common error is forgetting to square the radius.
**Tags**: sector-area, circle, area

## Item 3
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Convert 150° to radians.
**Answer**: 150° × (π/180) = (5π/6) radians ≈ 2.618 radians.
**Explanation**: Multiply by π/180. A common error is multiplying by 180/π.
**Tags**: radian-measure, conversion, trigonometry

## Item 4
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A sector has radius 8 cm and arc length 6 cm. Find the angle in radians.
**Answer**: Arc length = rθ → θ = 6/8 = 0.75 radians.
**Explanation**: Use arc length formula rθ. A common error is dividing the wrong way.
**Tags**: arc-length, radian, sector

## Item 5
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Find the area of a sector with radius 10 cm and angle 2.5 radians.
**Answer**: Area = (1/2) × r² × θ = 0.5 × 100 × 2.5 = 125 cm².
**Explanation**: Formula (1/2)r²θ for radians. A common error is using degrees formula.
**Tags**: sector-area, radians, area

## Item 6
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the formula for the area of a segment of a circle?
**Answer**: Area of segment = Area of sector − Area of triangle = (1/2)r²(θ − sinθ) when θ is in radians.
**Explanation**: Sector minus triangle. A common error is forgetting to convert to radians.
**Tags**: segment, circle, trigonometry

## Item 7
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: The arc length of a sector is 12 cm and its area is 48 cm². Find the radius.
**Answer**: Area = (1/2) × r × arc length → 6r = 48 → r = 8 cm.
**Explanation**: Use area = (1/2) × r × arc length. A common error is using circumference formula.
**Tags**: arc-length, sector-area, problem-solving

## Item 8
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A cone has base radius 5 cm and slant height 13 cm. Find the total surface area.
**Answer**: CSA = πrl = 65π. Base = πr² = 25π. Total = 90π ≈ 282.7 cm².
**Explanation**: CSA + base. A common error is forgetting the base.
**Tags**: cone, surface-area, trigonometry

## Item 9
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A frustum has radii 8 cm and 12 cm, height 10 cm. Find volume.
**Answer**: V = (1/3)πh(R² + Rr + r²) = (1/3)π(10)(304) = (3040/3)π ≈ 3183 cm³.
**Explanation**: Frustum formula. A common error is using the wrong radii.
**Tags**: frustum, volume, cone
