### Question Q-N1-15
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Find the highest common factor (HCF) of 24 and 36.
**Answer**: 12
**Explanation**: List the factors of each number. Factors of 24: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24. Factors of 36: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36. The largest factor common to both is 12. A common error is to find the LCM (72) instead of the HCF, or to stop at a smaller common factor like 6.
**Tags**: hcf, factors, number-theory

### Question Q-N1-16
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Write 60 as a product of its prime factors in the form 2^a × 3^b × 5^c.
**Answer**: 2^2 × 3^1 × 5^1
**Explanation**: Use a prime factor tree or repeated division. 60 = 2 × 30 = 2 × 2 × 15 = 2 × 2 × 3 × 5. So 60 = 2^2 × 3 × 5. A common error is to write 60 = 6 × 10 and stop, not realising that 6 and 10 are not prime. Another error is omitting an exponent of 1 (writing 3 and 5 without the power).
**Tags**: prime-factorisation, prime-numbers, index-notation
