# Question Q-4.2-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Explain why sound waves can travel through solids but not through a vacuum, while light waves can travel through both.

**Answer**: Sound waves are mechanical waves that require particles to transfer energy through vibrations. Solids have particles that can vibrate, but a vacuum has no particles. Light waves are electromagnetic and do not require a medium; they can travel through empty space.

**Explanation**: The key distinction is mechanical vs electromagnetic waves. A common error is saying "sound needs air" when it actually needs any medium with particles.
