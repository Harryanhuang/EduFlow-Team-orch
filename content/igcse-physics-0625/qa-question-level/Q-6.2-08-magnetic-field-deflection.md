# Question Q-6.2-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A beam of radioactive particles passes through a magnetic field. Alpha particles curve in one direction, beta particles curve in the opposite direction, and gamma rays do not curve. Explain these observations.

**Answer**: Alpha particles are positively charged (+2) and beta particles are negatively charged (-1), so the magnetic field exerts a force on both in opposite directions (Fleming's left-hand rule). Gamma rays carry no charge, so no magnetic force acts on them and they travel in a straight line.

**Explanation**: Charged particles moving through a magnetic field experience a force perpendicular to both their velocity and the field direction. The force direction depends on the sign of the charge. A common error is forgetting that the direction of deflection depends on the charge sign.
