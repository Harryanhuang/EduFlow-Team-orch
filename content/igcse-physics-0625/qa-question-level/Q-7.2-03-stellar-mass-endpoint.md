# Question Q-7.2-03

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: What property of a star determines whether it ends its life as a white dwarf, neutron star, or black hole? Describe the endpoint for each mass range.

**Answer**: The initial mass of the star determines its fate. Low to medium mass stars (like the Sun, up to about 8 solar masses) end as white dwarfs after shedding their outer layers as a planetary nebula. High mass stars (8 to about 25 solar masses) undergo a supernova explosion and leave behind a neutron star. Very high mass stars (above about 25 solar masses) also go supernova but collapse further to form a black hole.

**Explanation**: Mass is the determining factor. More massive stars have stronger gravity and can compress matter further. A common error is thinking all stars become black holes or that the Sun will explode as a supernova (it is not massive enough).
