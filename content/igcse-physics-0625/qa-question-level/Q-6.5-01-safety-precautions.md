# Question Q-6.5-01

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: State three safety precautions that should be taken when handling radioactive materials in a school laboratory.

**Answer**: (1) Minimise time of exposure — handle sources quickly. (2) Maximise distance — use tongs or forceps, never touch directly. (3) Use shielding — store sources in lead-lined containers and place lead shields between the source and people.

**Explanation**: The three principles of radiation protection are time, distance, and shielding. Reducing any one factor reduces the total dose received. A common error is saying "wear a lab coat" — this does not protect against penetrating radiation.
