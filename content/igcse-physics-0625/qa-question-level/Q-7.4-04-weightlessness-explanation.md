# Question Q-7.4-04

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Astronauts orbiting Earth in the International Space Station appear to be "weightless." Explain why this is the case. Is there gravity at the ISS's altitude?

**Answer**: Astronauts feel weightless because they are in continuous free fall around Earth. Both the spacecraft and everything inside it are falling at the same rate towards Earth's centre, but their high orbital velocity means they keep "missing" the Earth. There is no contact force (normal force) pushing up on them, so they experience the sensation of weightlessness. Yes, there is still significant gravity at the ISS altitude (about 90% of surface gravity) — without it, the station could not stay in orbit.

**Explanation**: Weightlessness does not mean zero gravity. It means free fall with no supporting force. A common error is claiming "there is no gravity in space" or "the ISS is too far for Earth's gravity to reach it."
