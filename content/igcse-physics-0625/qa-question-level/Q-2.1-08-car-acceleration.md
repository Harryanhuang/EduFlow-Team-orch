# Question Q-2.1-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A car of mass 1200 kg accelerates from rest to 30 m/s in 10 seconds. Calculate: (a) the acceleration, (b) the resultant force, (c) the distance travelled during this time, (d) if the driving force is 5000 N, what is the frictional force?

**Answer**: (a) a = (30-0)/10 = 3 m/s². (b) F = ma = 1200 × 3 = 3600 N. (c) s = ½(u+v)t = ½(0+30)×10 = 150 m. (d) Driving force - friction = resultant force: 5000 - f = 3600, f = 1400 N.

**Explanation**: Combine F=ma with kinematic equations. Part (d) requires recognizing that the driving force must overcome friction AND provide the resultant force for acceleration. A common error is equating driving force with resultant force.
