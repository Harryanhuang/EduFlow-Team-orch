# Question Q-7.3-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: What is dark matter? State one piece of observational evidence that suggests it exists.

**Answer**: Dark matter is invisible matter that does not emit, absorb, or reflect electromagnetic radiation but exerts gravitational influence. It makes up approximately 27% of the universe's total mass-energy (compared to only 5% normal matter). Evidence: galaxies rotate faster than can be explained by visible matter alone — outer stars orbit at nearly constant speed regardless of distance from the centre, indicating additional unseen mass providing extra gravitational force. Other evidence includes gravitational lensing being stronger than visible mass accounts for, and the motion of galaxy clusters.

**Explanation**: Dark matter is inferred from its gravitational effects on visible matter. A common error is confusing dark matter with black holes or antimatter. It is not simply "matter we haven't discovered" but matter that interacts gravitationally but not electromagnetically.
