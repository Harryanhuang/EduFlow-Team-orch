# Question Q-6.5-04

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: Explain how gamma radiation is used to sterilise medical instruments such as syringes and surgical gloves.

**Answer**: Medical instruments are sealed in plastic packaging and then exposed to a controlled dose of gamma radiation from a source such as cobalt-60. The gamma rays kill bacteria, viruses, and other microorganisms by damaging their DNA, effectively sterilising the instruments without heat damage. This allows single-use items to be sterilised in their final packaging.

**Explanation**: Gamma radiation penetrates packaging and kills microbes throughout. Unlike heat sterilisation, it does not damage heat-sensitive plastics. A common error is saying it "cleans" the instruments — sterilisation is not the same as cleaning.
