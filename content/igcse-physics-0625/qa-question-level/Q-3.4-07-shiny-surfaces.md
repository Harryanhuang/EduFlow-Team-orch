# Question Q-3.4-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A student claims that shiny surfaces are good insulators because they reflect heat. Evaluate this statement.

**Answer**: The statement is partially correct but uses wrong terminology. Shiny/silvered surfaces are good reflectors of infrared radiation, which reduces radiative heat transfer. However, "insulator" refers to materials that resist conduction. A shiny surface can still conduct heat well if it's metal. The correct statement is: shiny surfaces are poor absorbers and emitters of radiation, making them effective at reducing radiative heat loss.

**Explanation**: Reflection of radiation ≠ insulation against conduction. A common error is confusing "reflector" with "insulator" — these describe different heat transfer mechanisms.
