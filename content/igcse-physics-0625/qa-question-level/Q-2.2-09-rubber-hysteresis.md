# Question Q-2.2-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A student plots a force-extension graph for a rubber band. The graph is curved throughout and does not pass through the origin when the load is removed (hysteresis). Explain what this means and why rubber does not obey Hooke's law.

**Answer**: Rubber does not obey Hooke's law because its molecular structure involves uncoiling of polymer chains rather than simple bond stretching. The curved graph means extension is not proportional to force. Hysteresis (different loading and unloading paths) means energy is dissipated as heat during the cycle — the area between the curves represents energy lost.

**Explanation**: Hooke's law applies to materials where extension is proportional to force within the elastic limit. Rubber's non-linear behaviour is due to its polymer structure. A common error is saying rubber "doesn't work" — it does store energy, just not linearly.
