# Question Q-3.2-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Two metal blocks X and Y are in thermal contact. Block X is initially at 80°C and block Y is at 20°C. Describe what happens to the temperatures of both blocks until they reach thermal equilibrium.

**Answer**: Thermal energy flows from the hotter block X to the cooler block Y. Block X cools down (loses energy) and block Y warms up (gains energy). This continues until both blocks reach the same intermediate temperature, at which point they are in thermal equilibrium and there is no net energy transfer. The final temperature depends on the masses and specific heat capacities of the blocks.

**Explanation**: Energy transfer continues until temperatures equalise. A common error is thinking that the final temperature will be exactly the average (50°C) — it depends on the thermal properties of each block, not just the initial temperatures.
