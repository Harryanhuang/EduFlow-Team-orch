# Question Q-7.2-02

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: What happens during the main-sequence stage of a star? Explain why this stage is stable.

**Answer**: During the main-sequence stage, hydrogen nuclei fuse into helium in the core, releasing enormous amounts of energy. This stage is stable because the outward pressure from the hot gases produced by fusion exactly balances the inward gravitational pull trying to collapse the star. This equilibrium can last billions of years.

**Explanation**: The balance between fusion pressure pushing outward and gravity pulling inward maintains stability. A common error is saying the star is "burning like fire" — fusion is a nuclear process, not combustion.
