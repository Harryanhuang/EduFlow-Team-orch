# Question Q-1.4-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A uniform metre rule is balanced at the 50 cm mark. A 2 N weight is hung at the 10 cm mark. Where must a 5 N weight be placed to balance the rule?

**Answer**: 66 cm mark

**Explanation**: Taking moments about the pivot: 2 N × (50 - 10) cm = 80 N·cm anticlockwise. For balance: 5 N × d = 80, d = 16 cm from pivot. Position = 50 + 16 = 66 cm mark. The principle of moments: sum of clockwise moments = sum of anticlockwise moments. A common error is measuring from the wrong end of the rule.
