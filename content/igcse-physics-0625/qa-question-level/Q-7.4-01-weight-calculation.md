# Question Q-7.4-01

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: An astronaut has a mass of 70 kg. Calculate her weight on Earth (g = 10 N/kg) and on Mars (g = 3.7 N/kg). Explain the difference.

**Answer**: On Earth: W = mg = 70 × 10 = 700 N. On Mars: W = mg = 70 × 3.7 = 259 N. The astronaut weighs less on Mars because Mars has a weaker gravitational field strength (lower g) due to its smaller mass compared to Earth. Her mass remains 70 kg on both planets.

**Explanation**: Weight depends on the local gravitational field; mass is intrinsic. A common error is using Earth's g for both calculations or confusing mass with weight.
