# Question Q-3.2-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A mercury-in-glass thermometer is inaccurate because the bore is slightly wider than intended. Will this thermometer read higher or lower than the true temperature? Explain your reasoning.

**Answer**: It will read lower than the true temperature. For a given temperature increase, the mercury expands by a fixed volume. In a wider bore, this same volume produces a smaller rise in the column length. Since the scale is calibrated assuming a narrower bore, the thermometer will indicate a smaller temperature change than actually occurred, giving a reading that is too low.

**Explanation**: A wider bore means less column movement per degree. A common error is confusing bore width with overall accuracy — the issue is about sensitivity, not whether the thermometer works at all.
