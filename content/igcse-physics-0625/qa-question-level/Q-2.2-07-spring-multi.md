# Question Q-2.2-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A spring of natural length 15 cm extends to 20 cm when a 4 N load is attached. Calculate: (a) the spring constant, (b) the extension when a 6 N load is attached, (c) the total length with the 6 N load, (d) the energy stored in the spring with the 4 N load.

**Answer**: (a) x = 20-15 = 5 cm. k = F/x = 4/5 = 0.8 N/cm. (b) x = F/k = 6/0.8 = 7.5 cm. (c) Total length = 15 + 7.5 = 22.5 cm. (d) Energy = ½Fx = ½ × 4 × 0.05 = 0.1 J (using metres: x = 0.05 m).

**Explanation**: Remember to use metres for energy calculations (J = N·m). A common error is using cm for the energy calculation, giving an answer 100× too large.
