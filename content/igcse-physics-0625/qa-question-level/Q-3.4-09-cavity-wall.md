# Question Q-3.4-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Explain why a cavity wall (two layers of brick with an air gap between) is a better insulator than a solid brick wall of the same total thickness.

**Answer**: The cavity wall is better because: (1) The air gap is a poor conductor (air is an insulator), reducing conductive heat transfer. (2) The narrow gap prevents convection currents from forming, reducing convective heat transfer. (3) If the inner surfaces are lined with foil, radiation is also reduced. A solid brick wall conducts heat directly through the material with no insulating layer.

**Explanation**: Multiple mechanisms are reduced: conduction (air gap), convection (narrow space), and possibly radiation (foil lining). A common error is saying "air is a perfect insulator" — it's just much worse than solid material at conducting.
