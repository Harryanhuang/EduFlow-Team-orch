# Question Q-5.3-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A student says, "In a parallel circuit the current is the same in every branch." Explain why this is not always true.

**Answer**: The p.d. is the same across each parallel branch, but branch current depends on resistance. Lower resistance branches take larger currents. Total current is the sum of branch currents.

**Explanation**: Parallel circuits share voltage, not necessarily current.
