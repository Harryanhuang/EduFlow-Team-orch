# Question Q-1.3-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Two skydivers of equal mass jump from the same height. Skydiver A falls in a streamlined position; Skydiver B spreads out. Compare their terminal velocities and explain which reaches the ground first.

**Answer**: Skydiver A (streamlined) has less air resistance at any given speed, so must reach a higher speed before air resistance equals weight. Skydiver A has a higher terminal velocity and reaches the ground first. Skydiver B (spread out) has more air resistance, reaches a lower terminal velocity, and takes longer.

**Explanation**: Terminal velocity depends on the balance of weight and air resistance. Same weight, different air resistance → different terminal velocities. A common error is thinking mass is the only factor — shape and surface area matter equally.
