# Question Q-5.4-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A 12 V supply gives a current of 0.30 A through a wire. The wire is replaced with one of the same material and length but half the cross-sectional area. Calculate the new current.

**Answer**: Original R = 12/0.30 = 40 ohm. Halving area doubles resistance, so new R = 80 ohm. New current = 12/80 = 0.15 A.

**Explanation**: This combines Ohm law with area dependence.
