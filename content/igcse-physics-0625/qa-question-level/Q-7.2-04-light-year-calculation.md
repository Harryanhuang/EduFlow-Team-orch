# Question Q-7.2-04

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is a light-year? Calculate how far light travels in one year. (Speed of light c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s)

**Answer**: A light-year is the distance light travels in one year through vacuum. Distance = speed × time = 3 × 10⁸ m/s × 365 × 24 × 3600 s ≈ 9.46 × 10¹⁵ m (about 9.5 trillion kilometres or 9.5 million million km).

**Explanation**: It is a unit of distance, not time. The calculation multiplies the speed of light by the number of seconds in a year. A common error is confusing a light-year with a measure of time or getting the calculation wrong by using incorrect time units.
