# Question Q-1.2-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A car accelerates uniformly from rest at 2 m/s² for 10 seconds, then travels at constant speed for 20 seconds, then decelerates uniformly to rest in 5 seconds. Calculate: (a) the maximum speed reached, (b) the total distance travelled, (c) the average speed for the whole journey.

**Answer**: (a) Maximum speed = 2 × 10 = 20 m/s. (b) Phase 1: s = ½ × 2 × 10² = 100 m. Phase 2: s = 20 × 20 = 400 m. Phase 3: s = ½ × (20 + 0) × 5 = 50 m. Total = 100 + 400 + 50 = 550 m. (c) Total time = 10 + 20 + 5 = 35 s. Average speed = 550 / 35 = 15.7 m/s.

**Explanation**: Break the journey into phases and treat each separately. The area under the speed-time graph gives distance. A common error is using s = vt for the acceleration phase instead of the correct formula.
