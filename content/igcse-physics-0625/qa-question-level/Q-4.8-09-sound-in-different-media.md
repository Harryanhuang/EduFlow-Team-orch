# Question Q-4.8-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Compare how sound travels through solids, liquids, and gases. Explain why the speed differs in each medium.

**Answer**: Sound travels fastest in solids, slower in liquids, and slowest in gases. In solids, particles are close together and strongly bonded, so vibrations transfer quickly. In liquids, particles are further apart. In gases, particles are widely spaced and collisions are less frequent, slowing energy transfer. The closer the particle spacing and stronger the bonds, the faster sound propagates.

**Explanation**: Particle spacing and bond strength determine speed. A common error is thinking sound travels fastest in gases because they are "lighter."
