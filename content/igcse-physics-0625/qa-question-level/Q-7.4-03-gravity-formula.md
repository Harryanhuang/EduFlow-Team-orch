# Question Q-7.4-03

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Planet X has twice the mass of Earth and twice the radius. Using the formula g = GM/r², calculate how the surface gravitational field strength on Planet X compares with that on Earth (g_Earth = 10 N/kg).

**Answer**: For Planet X: g_X = G(2M)/(2r)² = 2GM/4r² = (1/2)(GM/r²) = (1/2)g_Earth = 5 N/kg. The surface gravity on Planet X is half that of Earth. Although the doubled mass would increase gravity by a factor of 2, the doubled radius decreases it by a factor of 4 (since r² appears in the denominator). The radius effect dominates, giving a net factor of 1/2.

**Explanation**: Gravitational field strength depends on both mass and radius squared. A common error is saying g doubles because mass doubles, forgetting that radius has a squared inverse effect.
