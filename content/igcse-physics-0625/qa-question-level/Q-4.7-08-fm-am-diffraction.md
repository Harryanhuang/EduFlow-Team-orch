# Question Q-4.7-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: An FM radio station broadcasts at 96 MHz and an AM station at 1000 kHz. Calculate the wavelengths of both and explain which would diffract more around hills. (c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s)

**Answer**: FM: λ = c/f = 3 × 10⁸ / (96 × 10⁶) = 3.125 m. AM: λ = 3 × 10⁸ / (1000 × 10³) = 300 m. The AM wave has a much longer wavelength, so it diffracts more around obstacles like hills. Longer wavelengths diffract more than shorter ones.

**Explanation**: Diffraction increases with wavelength. A common error is thinking higher frequency means more diffraction.
