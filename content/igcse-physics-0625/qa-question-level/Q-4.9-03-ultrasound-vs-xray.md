# Question Q-4.9-03

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Why is ultrasound preferred over X-rays for imaging a developing fetus?

**Answer**: Ultrasound is non-ionising and does not damage living cells, making it safe for the developing fetus. X-rays are ionising radiation and could cause cell damage or mutations.

**Explanation**: Safety is the key factor. A common error is saying ultrasound gives a "clearer image."
