# Question Q-1.5-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A solid object floats in water with 60% of its volume submerged. Calculate the density of the object. (Water density = 1 g/cm³)

**Answer**: 0.6 g/cm³

**Explanation**: For a floating object, the fraction submerged = density of object / density of fluid. So density of object = 0.6 × 1 = 0.6 g/cm³. A common error is thinking the object's density equals the displaced water's density without considering the fraction.
