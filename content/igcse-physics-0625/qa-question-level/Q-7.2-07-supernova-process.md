# Question Q-7.2-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Describe the process of a supernova explosion. What conditions must be met for a supernova to occur, and what remains afterwards?

**Answer**: A supernova occurs in massive stars when nuclear fusion in the core produces iron. Iron cannot fuse to release energy, so fusion stops. Without outward fusion pressure, gravity causes the core to collapse catastrophically in milliseconds. The outer layers rebound off the dense core and explode outward in a tremendous burst of energy, briefly outshining an entire galaxy. Afterwards, the core remnant becomes either a neutron star (if the remaining mass is about 1.4 to 3 solar masses) or a black hole (if above about 3 solar masses).

**Explanation**: The key trigger is iron production halting fusion. A common error is saying the star "runs out of fuel" generally rather than specifically reaching iron in the fusion chain.
