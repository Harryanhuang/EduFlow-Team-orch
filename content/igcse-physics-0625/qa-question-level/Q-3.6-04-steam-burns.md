# Question Q-3.6-04

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: Why does steam at 100°C cause a more severe burn than water at 100°C?

**Answer**: Steam has additional latent heat of vaporisation. When steam condenses on the skin, it releases this latent heat as well as cooling down, transferring more energy than water at the same temperature.

**Explanation**: The extra latent heat makes steam more dangerous. A common error is saying steam is "hotter than 100°C."
