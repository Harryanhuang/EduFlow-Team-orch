# Question Q-6.5-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Gamma irradiation is used to preserve food by killing bacteria and insects. State two advantages of this method compared to chemical preservatives or heat treatment, and identify one concern consumers might have.

**Answer**: Advantages: (1) Food can be treated in its final packaging, preventing recontamination. (2) No residual chemicals are added, and nutritional value is largely preserved (unlike heat treatment which can destroy vitamins). Concern: Consumers may worry that irradiated food becomes radioactive itself, though this is incorrect — the gamma rays pass through without making the food radioactive. Another concern is potential formation of breakdown products whose health effects are not fully known.

**Explanation**: Irradiation kills pathogens without making food radioactive, similar to how X-rays pass through patients without leaving radiation behind. A common error is claiming the food becomes radioactive, which demonstrates a misunderstanding of the process.
