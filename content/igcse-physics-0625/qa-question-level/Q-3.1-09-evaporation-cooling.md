# Question Q-3.1-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Use the kinetic particle model to explain why evaporation causes cooling. Refer to the energies of the particles involved.

**Answer**: In a liquid, particles have a range of kinetic energies. The most energetic particles near the surface can escape into the air, becoming vapour. When these high-energy particles leave, the average kinetic energy of the remaining particles decreases. Since temperature is proportional to average kinetic energy, the temperature of the liquid falls. This is why evaporation causes cooling.

**Explanation**: Only the fastest particles escape, lowering the average KE of those left behind. A common error is saying "all particles leave" or that evaporation happens at a specific temperature (it occurs at any temperature, unlike boiling).
