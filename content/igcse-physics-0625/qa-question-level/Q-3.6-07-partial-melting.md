# Question Q-3.6-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A student places 0.2 kg of ice at 0°C in a beaker and supplies 80 000 J of energy. After heating, some ice remains. Calculate how much ice has melted. (Specific latent heat of fusion of ice = 334 000 J/kg)

**Answer**: 0.24 kg — but since only 0.2 kg was available, all 0.2 kg melts with energy remaining. Energy to melt all ice: Q = 0.2 × 334 000 = 66 800 J. Since 80 000 J > 66 800 J, all ice melts. Remaining energy = 80 000 - 66 800 = 13 200 J heats the water. Mass melted = 0.2 kg (all of it).

**Explanation**: First check if enough energy to melt all ice. A common error is blindly applying m = Q/L without checking if enough mass exists — getting 0.24 kg which exceeds the available 0.2 kg.
