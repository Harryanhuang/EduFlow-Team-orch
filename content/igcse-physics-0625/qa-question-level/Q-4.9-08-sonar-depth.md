# Question Q-4.9-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A ship uses sonar to measure ocean depth. It sends an ultrasound pulse downward and detects the echo 0.8 seconds later. If the speed of sound in seawater is 1520 m/s, calculate: (a) the depth of the water, (b) the time taken for the pulse to reach the seabed.

**Answer**: (a) Total distance = 1520 × 0.8 = 1216 m. Depth = 1216 / 2 = 608 m. (b) Time to reach seabed = 0.8 / 2 = 0.4 s.

**Explanation**: The pulse travels down and back, so depth is half the total distance. A common error in (a) is giving 1216 m as the depth instead of halving.
