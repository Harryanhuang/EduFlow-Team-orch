# Question Q-2.4-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A pump lifts 200 kg of water through a height of 15 m in 2 minutes. The motor has a power input of 500 W. Calculate: (a) the useful work done, (b) the useful power output, (c) the efficiency of the pump. (g = 10 N/kg)

**Answer**: (a) Work = mgh = 200 × 10 × 15 = 30 000 J. (b) Power = W/t = 30 000/120 = 250 W. (c) Efficiency = 250/500 × 100% = 50%.

**Explanation**: Remember to convert minutes to seconds for power. A common error is using 2 minutes directly (30 000/2 = 15 000 W), giving an efficiency over 100%.
