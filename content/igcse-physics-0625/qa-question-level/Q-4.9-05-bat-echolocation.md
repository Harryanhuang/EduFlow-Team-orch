# Question Q-4.9-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A bat emits ultrasound at a frequency of 50 kHz. Explain how the bat uses this to locate prey in the dark.

**Answer**: The bat emits ultrasound pulses and listens for the echoes reflected off objects (including prey). By measuring the time delay between emission and detection of the echo, the bat can determine the distance to the object. This process is called echolocation.

**Explanation**: Echolocation relies on echo timing. A common error is thinking bats "see" with ultrasound rather than using echo timing.
