# Question Q-1.2-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A ball is thrown vertically upwards with an initial speed of 15 m/s. How long does it take to reach its highest point? (g = 10 m/s²)

**Answer**: 1.5 s

**Explanation**: At the highest point, the speed is 0. Using v = u - gt: 0 = 15 - 10t, so t = 15/10 = 1.5 s. A common error is using v = u + gt and getting a negative time, or forgetting that gravity acts downwards (opposing the motion).
