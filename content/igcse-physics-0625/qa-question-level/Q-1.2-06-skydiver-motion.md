# Question Q-1.2-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Describe the motion of a skydiver from jumping until reaching terminal velocity, in terms of forces.

**Answer**: Initially, weight is much greater than air resistance, so the skydiver accelerates downwards. As speed increases, air resistance increases. When air resistance equals weight, the resultant force is zero and the skydiver falls at constant terminal velocity.

**Explanation**: The key is understanding the changing balance of forces. A common error is saying "air resistance becomes greater than weight" at terminal velocity (they are equal, not greater).
