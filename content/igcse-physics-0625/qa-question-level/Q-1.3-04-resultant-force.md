# Question Q-1.3-04

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A skydiver of weight 700 N falls through the air. At one instant, the air resistance is 400 N. Calculate the resultant force and describe the motion at this point.

**Answer**: Resultant force = 700 - 400 = 300 N downwards. The skydiver is still accelerating downwards (but at a reduced rate compared to free fall).

**Explanation**: The resultant force is still downwards, so acceleration continues. A common error is saying the skydiver is "slowing down" — the acceleration has decreased, not the speed.
