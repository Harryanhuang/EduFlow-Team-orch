# Question Q-2.1-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Explain why an object moving at constant velocity has zero resultant force acting on it.

**Answer**: According to Newton's first law, if velocity is constant (no acceleration), the resultant force must be zero. All forces are balanced.

**Explanation**: Constant velocity means no net force. A common error is saying there is "no force" — there may be forces, but they balance.
