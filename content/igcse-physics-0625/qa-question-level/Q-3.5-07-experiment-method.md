# Question Q-3.5-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Describe an experiment to determine the specific heat capacity of a metal block. Include the apparatus needed, measurements taken, and one precaution to improve accuracy.

**Answer**: Apparatus: metal block, insulation, heater of known power, thermometer, stopwatch, power supply. Method: (1) Measure mass of block. (2) Insert heater and thermometer into block. (3) Record initial temperature. (4) Switch on heater for a measured time t. (5) Record final temperature. Calculation: Energy = Pt, ΔT = T_final - T_initial, c = Pt/(mΔT). Precaution: Insulate the block to minimise heat loss to surroundings. Other precautions: stir if using liquid, ensure good thermal contact between heater and block.

**Explanation**: The key equation is c = Q/(mΔT) where Q = Pt. A common error is not mentioning insulation, which causes systematic error (overestimate of c).
