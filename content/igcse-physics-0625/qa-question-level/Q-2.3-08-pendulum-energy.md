# Question Q-2.3-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A pendulum bob of mass 0.2 kg is pulled to one side so that it is 0.15 m higher than its lowest position. It is released. Calculate: (a) its maximum kinetic energy, (b) its maximum speed, (c) its speed when it is 0.05 m above the lowest position. (g = 10 N/kg)

**Answer**: (a) Max KE = initial PE = mgh = 0.2 × 10 × 0.15 = 0.3 J. (b) ½mv² = 0.3, v² = 0.3 × 2 / 0.2 = 3, v = √3 ≈ 1.73 m/s. (c) PE at 0.05 m = 0.2 × 10 × 0.05 = 0.1 J. KE = 0.3 - 0.1 = 0.2 J. ½ × 0.2 × v² = 0.2, v² = 2, v = √2 ≈ 1.41 m/s.

**Explanation**: Energy converts between PE and KE as the pendulum swings. At any point: total energy = PE + KE. A common error is using the full height for part (c) instead of the remaining height difference.
