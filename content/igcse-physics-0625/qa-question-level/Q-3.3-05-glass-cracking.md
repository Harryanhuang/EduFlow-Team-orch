# Question Q-3.3-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Why is the glass of a drinking glass more likely to crack when boiling water is poured into a thick glass than a thin glass?

**Answer**: In thick glass, the inner surface heats and expands rapidly while the outer surface remains cool. The uneven expansion creates stress that cracks the glass. Thin glass heats more evenly.

**Explanation**: Differential expansion causes stress. A common error is saying "thick glass holds more heat."
