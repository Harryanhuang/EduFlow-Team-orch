# Question Q-3.6-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: An electric kettle boils 0.5 kg of water at 100°C. Calculate the energy needed to convert all the water to steam at 100°C. If the kettle is rated at 2400 W, calculate the minimum time required. (Specific latent heat of vaporisation of water = 2.26 × 10^6 J/kg)

**Answer**: Energy: Q = mL = 0.5 × 2.26 × 10^6 = 1.13 × 10^6 J (1 130 000 J). Time: t = Q/P = 1 130 000/2400 = 471 s (approximately 7.9 min).

**Explanation**: For boiling at constant temperature, use Q = mL (not Q = mcΔT). A common error is trying to use specific heat capacity when there is no temperature change — the water is already at 100°C.
