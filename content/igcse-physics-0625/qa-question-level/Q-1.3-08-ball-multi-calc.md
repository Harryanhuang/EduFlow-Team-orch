# Question Q-1.3-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A ball is thrown vertically upwards from ground level with a speed of 20 m/s. (g = 10 m/s²). Calculate: (a) the maximum height reached, (b) the time to reach maximum height, (c) the total time before it returns to ground level, (d) its speed when it is 15 m above the ground on the way up.

**Answer**: (a) v² = u² - 2gs: 0 = 400 - 20s, s = 20 m. (b) v = u - gt: 0 = 20 - 10t, t = 2 s. (c) Total time = 2 × 2 = 4 s (symmetry). (d) v² = u² - 2gs = 400 - 2(10)(15) = 400 - 300 = 100, v = 10 m/s.

**Explanation**: Use the kinematic equations systematically. Part (d) requires solving for v given s. A common error is using s = ½gt² for part (d), which gives time, not speed.
