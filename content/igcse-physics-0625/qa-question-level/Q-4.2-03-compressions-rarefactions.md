# Question Q-4.2-03

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: What are compressions and rarefactions in a sound wave?

**Answer**: Compressions are regions where particles are pushed close together (high pressure). Rarefactions are regions where particles are spread apart (low pressure). They alternate as the sound wave travels.

**Explanation**: These replace "crests" and "troughs" for longitudinal waves. A common error is calling them crests and troughs.
