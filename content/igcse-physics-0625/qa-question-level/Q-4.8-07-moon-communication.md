# Question Q-4.8-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Explain why astronauts on the Moon cannot communicate by speaking directly to each other, even though they can see each other clearly.

**Answer**: The Moon has no atmosphere (it is essentially a vacuum). Sound requires a medium (particles) to travel, so speech cannot propagate. However, light can travel through vacuum, so they can see each other. They must use radio communication (electromagnetic waves) which can travel through vacuum.

**Explanation**: The key difference between sound (mechanical) and light (EM) wave propagation. A common error is saying "there's no air" without explaining why that matters for sound.
