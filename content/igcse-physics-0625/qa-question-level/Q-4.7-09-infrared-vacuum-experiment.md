# Question Q-4.7-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Describe how you could demonstrate that infrared radiation can travel through a vacuum. Include a labelled diagram and explain what observations would confirm this.

**Answer**: Set up: Place an electric heater (infrared source) inside a bell jar connected to a vacuum pump. Place an infrared detector (e.g., thermopile) on the other side of the jar, connected to a voltmeter. Initially, with air in the jar, the detector registers IR. As air is pumped out, if the detector still registers IR (voltmeter shows a reading), this confirms IR can travel through vacuum. Observation: The voltmeter continues to show a reading even when the air is removed, proving IR propagates without a medium.

**Explanation**: Since EM waves don't need a medium, IR should still be detected in vacuum. A common error is placing the detector outside the jar where IR cannot reach it, or not explaining the control (air present first).
