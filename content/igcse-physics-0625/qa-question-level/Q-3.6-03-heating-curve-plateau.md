# Question Q-3.6-03

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A heating curve for water shows a flat section at 100°C. Explain why the temperature does not rise during this section even though heating continues.

**Answer**: The energy supplied is used to break the bonds between water molecules (change of state from liquid to gas), not to increase the kinetic energy (temperature) of the molecules.

**Explanation**: Energy goes into potential energy (bond breaking), not kinetic energy. A common error is saying "no energy is being absorbed."
