# Question Q-4.7-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Explain why UV radiation is more dangerous to living cells than visible light, even though both are electromagnetic waves travelling at the same speed.

**Answer**: UV radiation has a higher frequency than visible light, which means each photon carries more energy (E ∝ f). This higher energy can damage or destroy cells, causing sunburn or skin cancer. Visible light photons have insufficient energy to cause this damage. Both travel at c in vacuum, but the energy per photon differs.

**Explanation**: The danger comes from photon energy, not speed. A common error is saying "UV travels faster" or confusing intensity with photon energy.
