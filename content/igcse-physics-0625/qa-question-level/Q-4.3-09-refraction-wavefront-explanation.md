# Question Q-4.3-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Parallel wavefronts approach a boundary between two media. In medium A, the wavelength is 4 cm. In medium B, the wavelength is 3 cm. If the wavefronts hit the boundary at an angle, explain why the direction of the waves changes as they enter medium B.

**Answer**: The direction changes due to refraction. One edge of the wavefront enters medium B first and slows down (shorter wavelength means slower speed), while the other edge is still in medium A. This causes the wavefront to pivot, changing direction. The waves bend towards the normal when entering the slower medium.

**Explanation**: Refraction occurs because one part of the wavefront changes speed before another. A common error is saying the waves "bounce" off the boundary.
