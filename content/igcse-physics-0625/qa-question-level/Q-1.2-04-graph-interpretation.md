# Question Q-1.2-04

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: On a distance-time graph, what does a horizontal line represent? What does a steeper line represent compared to a shallower line?

**Answer**: A horizontal line means the object is stationary (not moving). A steeper line represents a greater speed compared to a shallower line.

**Explanation**: The gradient of a distance-time graph equals speed. Zero gradient = zero speed. Steeper gradient = higher speed. A common error is saying a horizontal line means "constant speed" without recognising that zero is a specific case.
