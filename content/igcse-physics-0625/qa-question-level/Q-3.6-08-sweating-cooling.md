# Question Q-3.6-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Explain why sweating is an effective cooling mechanism for the human body. Refer to the physics of phase change.

**Answer**: Sweat is mostly water secreted onto the skin. As it evaporates, it absorbs latent heat of vaporisation from the skin and body. This energy is used to change water from liquid to vapour, removing thermal energy from the body and producing a cooling effect. The process is enhanced by air movement (breeze/fan) which removes water vapour and allows more evaporation. In humid conditions, evaporation is slower because the air is already saturated with water vapour.

**Explanation**: Evaporation requires energy (latent heat) which comes from the body. A common error is saying sweat "cools the skin" directly — it's the phase change that removes energy, not the liquid itself.
