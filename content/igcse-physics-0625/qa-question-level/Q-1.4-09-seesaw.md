# Question Q-1.4-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A seesaw is 4 m long and pivoted at its centre. A child of mass 30 kg sits at one end. Where must a parent of mass 60 kg sit to balance the seesaw? Explain why the seesaw would still balance if both moved halfway towards the pivot.

**Answer**: Distance of child from pivot = 2 m. Moment = 30g × 2 = 60g N·m. Parent must produce same moment: 60g × d = 60g, d = 1 m from pivot. If both move halfway: child at 1 m (moment = 30g × 1 = 30g), parent at 0.5 m (moment = 60g × 0.5 = 30g). Still balanced because the ratio of distances (1:2) is maintained.

**Explanation**: The principle of moments depends on the product of force and distance. If both distances are scaled by the same factor, the moments remain in balance. A common error is thinking only the heavier person's position matters.
