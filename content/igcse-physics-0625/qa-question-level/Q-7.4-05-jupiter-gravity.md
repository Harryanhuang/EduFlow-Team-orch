# Question Q-7.4-05

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: The gravitational field strength on Jupiter is approximately 24 N/kg, much greater than Earth's 10 N/kg. Explain why Jupiter's surface gravity is so strong. If you stood on Jupiter (assuming it had a solid surface), how would your weight compare to your weight on Earth?

**Answer**: Jupiter's surface gravity is strong primarily because Jupiter has an enormous mass (about 318 times Earth's mass). Although Jupiter also has a much larger radius, the mass effect dominates, resulting in g ≈ 24 N/kg. If you stood on Jupiter, your weight would be W = m × 24, which is 2.4 times your weight on Earth (W = m × 10). Your mass would remain unchanged.

**Explanation**: Mass is the primary determinant of gravitational field strength. A common error is attributing Jupiter's strong gravity to its density or composition rather than its total mass.
