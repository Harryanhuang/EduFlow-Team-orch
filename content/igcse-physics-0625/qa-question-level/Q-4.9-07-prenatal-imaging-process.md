# Question Q-4.9-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: In prenatal imaging, an ultrasound scan produces an image of the fetus on a screen. Describe the process of how this image is formed, including what happens to the ultrasound waves.

**Answer**: The ultrasound transducer emits short pulses of high-frequency sound waves into the body. When these waves encounter boundaries between tissues of different densities (e.g., fluid to organ), some waves are reflected back as echoes while others continue deeper. The transducer detects the reflected echoes and measures their arrival times and amplitudes. A computer processes this data to build up a 2D image, with brighter areas indicating stronger reflections (denser tissue boundaries). Gel is used to ensure good coupling between the transducer and skin.

**Explanation**: Image formation involves pulse-echo timing and reflection at tissue boundaries. A common error is thinking the ultrasound "photographs" the fetus directly.
