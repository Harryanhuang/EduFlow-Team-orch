# Question Q-7.2-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: What is a nebula? Give two types of nebula and state how each is formed.

**Answer**: A nebula is a vast cloud of gas (mainly hydrogen and helium) and dust in space. Two types: (1) Star-forming nebulae (emission nebulae) — clouds of gas where new stars are born through gravitational collapse (e.g., Orion Nebula). (2) Planetary nebulae — shells of gas ejected by dying red giant stars when they shed their outer layers (e.g., Ring Nebula).

**Explanation**: Nebulae can be sites of star birth or remnants of stellar death. A common error is thinking all nebulae are the same type or confusing planetary nebulae with planets.
