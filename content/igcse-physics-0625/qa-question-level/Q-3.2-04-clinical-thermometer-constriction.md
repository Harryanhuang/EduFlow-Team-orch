# Question Q-3.2-04

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Explain why a clinical thermometer has a constriction in the capillary tube.

**Answer**: The constriction prevents the mercury from flowing back into the bulb when removed from the patient, so the reading can be taken after removal. The mercury must be shaken back down for the next use.

**Explanation**: The constriction breaks the mercury column. A common error is saying it "makes the thermometer more accurate."
