# Question Q-1.4-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: An object weighs 45 N on Planet X (g = 15 N/kg) and 29.4 N on Planet Y. Calculate: (a) the mass of the object, (b) the gravitational field strength on Planet Y.

**Answer**: (a) Mass = W/g = 45/15 = 3 kg. (b) g on Planet Y = W/m = 29.4/3 = 9.8 N/kg.

**Explanation**: Mass is constant everywhere — calculate it from one planet's data, then use it to find the other planet's g. A common error is dividing the two weights directly without using the mass.
