# Question Q-7.4-06

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Explain how the value of g (gravitational field strength) changes as you move from the surface of the Earth towards its centre. Assume Earth has uniform density for simplicity.

**Answer**: Inside a uniform spherical body, g decreases linearly with depth from the surface to the centre. At the surface, g = GM/R² (maximum). As you go deeper, only the mass beneath you contributes to the gravitational pull (the shell above exerts no net force). At the centre, g = 0 because mass surrounds you equally in all directions. For uniform density, g(r) = g_surface × (r/R), where r is distance from centre. So halfway to the centre, g is halved.

**Explanation**: This differs from the outside behaviour where g ∝ 1/r². Inside, the effective attracting mass decreases faster than r². A common error is thinking g increases towards the centre or stays constant underground.
