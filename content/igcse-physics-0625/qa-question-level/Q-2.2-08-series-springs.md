# Question Q-2.2-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Two springs are connected in series. Spring A has k = 10 N/cm and Spring B has k = 5 N/cm. A force of 20 N is applied. Calculate: (a) the extension of each spring, (b) the total extension, (c) the effective spring constant of the combination.

**Answer**: (a) Extension of A = 20/10 = 2 cm. Extension of B = 20/5 = 4 cm. (b) Total = 2 + 4 = 6 cm. (c) k_effective = F/total_x = 20/6 = 3.33 N/cm. Or: 1/k_eff = 1/10 + 1/5 = 3/10, k_eff = 10/3 = 3.33 N/cm.

**Explanation**: In series, each spring feels the full force. Extensions add. The effective spring constant is less than either individual constant. A common error is adding the spring constants (as for parallel).
