# Question Q-5.1-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Explain how the Earth's magnetic field allows a compass to show direction. Include why the north-seeking end of a compass points roughly towards geographic north.

**Answer**: A compass needle is a small magnet that aligns with Earth's magnetic field. The north-seeking end points along the field direction towards geographic north. This happens because the region near geographic north acts like a magnetic south pole, attracting the north-seeking end of the compass.

**Explanation**: Geographic north and magnetic pole naming can be confusing. A common error is saying Earth's magnetic north pole attracts the compass north pole.
