# Question Q-3.5-04

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: In an experiment to measure the specific heat capacity of a metal, the measured value is higher than the accepted value. Suggest two possible reasons.

**Answer**: (1) Heat loss to the surroundings means more energy was needed than calculated, giving an apparently higher c. (2) The heater was not fully in contact with the block, so some energy was lost to the air.

**Explanation**: Systematic errors that cause overestimation. A common error is saying "reading error" without specifying the direction of the effect.
