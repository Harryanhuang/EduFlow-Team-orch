# Question Q-7.2-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Why does a star appear red when it becomes a red giant?

**Answer**: As a star expands into a red giant, its surface area increases dramatically but its surface temperature decreases (to about 3000-4000 K). Cooler objects emit more light at longer wavelengths, which appear redder. The lower surface temperature shifts the peak emission towards the red end of the spectrum, giving the star a reddish appearance.

**Explanation**: Surface temperature determines colour: hotter = bluer, cooler = redder. A common error is saying the star is "cooling down to die" without connecting temperature to colour through Wien's law.
