# Question Q-3.3-04

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Which expands most when heated by the same amount: a solid, a liquid, or a gas? Explain.

**Answer**: A gas expands most. Gas particles are furthest apart and have the weakest intermolecular forces, so they move apart most easily when heated. Liquids expand more than solids but less than gases.

**Explanation**: The order is gas > liquid > solid. A common error is saying solids expand most because they are "most dense."
