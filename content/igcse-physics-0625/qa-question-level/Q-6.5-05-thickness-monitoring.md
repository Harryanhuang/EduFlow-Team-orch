# Question Q-6.5-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: How is the thickness of paper monitored during manufacture using radiation? Describe the setup and explain which type of radiation is used.

**Answer**: A beta radiation source is placed on one side of the moving paper sheet and a Geiger-Müller detector on the other. Beta particles pass through the paper; if the thickness increases, fewer particles reach the detector and the count rate drops. This triggers an automatic adjustment to reduce paper thickness. Beta is used because it is partially absorbed by paper — alpha would be completely stopped, and gamma would pass through almost unaffected.

**Explanation**: The system relies on the partial absorption of beta radiation by the paper. Changes in thickness produce measurable changes in count rate. A common error is selecting alpha (completely absorbed) or gamma (barely affected).
