# Question Q-2.2-04

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: What is meant by the "limit of proportionality" of a spring?

**Answer**: The maximum force up to which the extension is directly proportional to the force. Beyond this point, Hooke's law no longer applies.

**Explanation**: A common error is confusing it with the "breaking point" — the limit of proportionality is where linearity ends, not where the spring breaks.
