# Question Q-3.3-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Explain why overhead electrical cables are installed with some slack rather than being pulled tight between pylons.

**Answer**: Cables are installed with slack to allow for contraction in cold weather. If pulled tight in warm weather, the cables would contract when the temperature drops, creating tension that could snap the cable or damage the pylons. The slack allows the cable to shorten safely without excessive tension.

**Explanation**: Thermal contraction can create enormous tension. A common error is only mentioning expansion in hot weather — the real danger is contraction in cold weather pulling cables taut.
