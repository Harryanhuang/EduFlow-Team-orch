# Question Q-1.5-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A density bottle has a mass of 25 g when empty, 75 g when filled with water, and 70 g when filled with an unknown liquid. Calculate the density of the unknown liquid. (Water density = 1 g/cm³)

**Answer**: 0.9 g/cm³

**Explanation**: Mass of water = 75 - 25 = 50 g. Volume of bottle = 50 cm³ (since water density = 1 g/cm³). Mass of unknown liquid = 70 - 25 = 45 g. Density = 45/50 = 0.9 g/cm³. A common error is using the total mass (70 g) instead of the mass of the liquid alone.
