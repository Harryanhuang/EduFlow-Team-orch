# Question Q-3.2-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: State two advantages of a thermocouple thermometer over a liquid-in-glass thermometer.

**Answer**: (1) Can measure very high or very low temperatures (wider range). (2) Responds quickly to temperature changes (small thermal capacity).

**Explanation**: Thermocouples use voltage, not liquid expansion. A common error is saying they are "more accurate" — they may not be, but they have other advantages.
