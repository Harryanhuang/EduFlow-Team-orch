# Question Q-7.4-08

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Calculate the gravitational field strength on the surface of Mars. Given: Mars mass = 6.4 × 10²³ kg, Mars radius = 3400 km. (G = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²)

**Answer**: g = GM/r² = (6.67 × 10⁻¹¹)(6.4 × 10²³) / (3.4 × 10⁶)² = 4.27 × 10¹³ / 1.156 × 10¹³ ≈ 3.7 N/kg.

**Explanation**: First convert radius to metres: 3400 km = 3.4 × 10⁶ m. Then substitute into the formula. A common error is forgetting to square the radius or not converting km to m, giving an answer off by orders of magnitude.
