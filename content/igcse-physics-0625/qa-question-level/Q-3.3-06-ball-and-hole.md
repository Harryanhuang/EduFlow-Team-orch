# Question Q-3.3-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A copper ball has a diameter of exactly 5.00 cm at 20°C. It just fits through a circular hole in an aluminium plate. If both are heated to 100°C, will the ball still fit through the hole? Explain. (Copper expands more than aluminium.)

**Answer**: No, the ball will not fit. Both the ball and the hole expand, but copper expands more than aluminium. The ball's diameter increases more than the hole's diameter, so the ball becomes too large to pass through.

**Explanation**: Both objects expand, but the material with the greater expansion coefficient (copper) expands more. A common error is thinking the hole "shrinks" — holes expand as if filled with the same material.
