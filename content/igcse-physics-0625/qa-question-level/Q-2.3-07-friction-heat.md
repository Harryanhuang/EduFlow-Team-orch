# Question Q-2.3-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: When a book is pushed across a table at constant speed, work is done but the book does not gain kinetic energy. Where does the energy go?

**Answer**: The energy is converted to thermal energy (heat) due to friction between the book and the table, and possibly to sound energy.

**Explanation**: Friction converts mechanical energy to heat. A common error is saying the energy "disappears" or is "used up."
