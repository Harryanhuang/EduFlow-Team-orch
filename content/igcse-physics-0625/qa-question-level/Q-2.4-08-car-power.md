# Question Q-2.4-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A car travels at a constant speed of 25 m/s. The engine provides a driving force of 800 N. If the engine is 40% efficient, calculate: (a) the useful power output, (b) the total power input from fuel, (c) the rate of energy waste.

**Answer**: (a) P_useful = Fv = 800 × 25 = 20 000 W. (b) P_input = 20 000/0.40 = 50 000 W. (c) Waste = 50 000 - 20 000 = 30 000 W.

**Explanation**: At constant speed, driving force equals resistive forces. The engine's useful power goes into overcoming resistance; the rest is wasted. A common error is confusing input and output power in the efficiency formula.
