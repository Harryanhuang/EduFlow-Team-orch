# Question Q-2.1-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Two forces act on an object: 8 N north and 6 N east. Calculate the magnitude and direction of the resultant force. If the object has mass 2 kg, what is its acceleration?

**Answer**: Magnitude = √(8² + 6²) = √(64+36) = √100 = 10 N. Direction = tan⁻¹(8/6) = 53.1° north of east (or 36.9° east of north). Acceleration = F/m = 10/2 = 5 m/s² in the direction of the resultant.

**Explanation**: Use Pythagoras for perpendicular forces and trigonometry for direction. A common error is adding the forces directly (8+6=14 N) instead of using vector addition.
