# Question Q-7.4-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Define gravitational potential at a point. Explain why gravitational potential is negative near a planet and what happens to it as you move further away. State the value at infinity.

**Answer**: Gravitational potential at a point is the work done per unit mass to bring a small test mass from infinity to that point. It is negative near a planet because gravity is attractive — work must be done against gravity to move a mass away (the system has negative potential energy relative to infinity). As you move further from the planet, gravitational potential becomes less negative (increases towards zero). At infinity, gravitational potential is defined as zero.

**Explanation**: Negative potential indicates a bound state — energy must be added to escape. A common error is thinking negative potential means "negative energy exists" rather than understanding it as a reference convention where infinity = zero.
