# Question Q-4.8-04

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Sound cannot travel through a vacuum. Describe an experiment to demonstrate this.

**Answer**: Place a ringing bell inside a bell jar connected to a vacuum pump. As air is removed, the sound becomes fainter until it cannot be heard, even though the bell is still ringing (visible). This shows sound needs a medium.

**Explanation**: The visual confirmation that the bell still works is important. A common error is not mentioning the visual check.
