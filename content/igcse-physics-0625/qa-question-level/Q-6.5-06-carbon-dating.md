# Question Q-6.5-06

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Carbon-14 dating is used to estimate the age of archaeological samples. Explain the principle behind this technique and state one assumption it makes.

**Answer**: Living organisms maintain a constant ratio of carbon-14 to carbon-12 through exchange with the atmosphere. When they die, carbon-14 intake stops and the existing C-14 decays (half-life 5730 years). By measuring the remaining C-14 activity and comparing it to that of a living sample, the age can be calculated. One assumption: the atmospheric C-14/C-12 ratio has remained constant over time.

**Explanation**: The technique assumes equilibrium between production and decay of C-14 in the atmosphere. A common error is not stating any assumption, or assuming the decay rate changes over time (it is constant).
