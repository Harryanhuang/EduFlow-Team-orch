# Question Q-5.10-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A student says increasing the number of turns on the coil does not affect a d.c. motor because the current is unchanged. Explain why this is wrong.

**Answer**: More turns mean more lengths of wire experience force in the magnetic field, increasing the total turning effect on the coil, even if current in each turn is unchanged.

**Explanation**: Motor torque depends on current, field strength, coil area, and number of turns.
