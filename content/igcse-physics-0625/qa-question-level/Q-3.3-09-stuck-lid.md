# Question Q-3.3-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A metal lid on a glass jar is stuck. Running hot water over the lid makes it easier to open. Explain why this works, referring to the expansion of both materials.

**Answer**: Both the metal lid and glass neck expand when heated, but metals generally have a higher coefficient of thermal expansion than glass. The metal lid expands more than the glass, increasing the gap between them and making the lid looser. The thin metal also heats up faster than the thicker glass.

**Explanation**: Different materials expand at different rates. A common error is saying "only the lid expands" — the glass also expands, just less. The key is the differential expansion.
