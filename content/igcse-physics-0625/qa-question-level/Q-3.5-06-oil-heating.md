# Question Q-3.5-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A 200 g sample of oil at 25°C is heated with 8400 J of energy. The specific heat capacity of oil is 2100 J/(kg·°C). Calculate the final temperature of the oil.

**Answer**: 45°C

**Explanation**: Q = mcΔT, so ΔT = Q/(mc) = 8400/(0.2 × 2100) = 8400/420 = 20°C. Final temperature = 25 + 20 = 45°C. A common error is forgetting to convert grams to kilograms (using 200 instead of 0.2).
