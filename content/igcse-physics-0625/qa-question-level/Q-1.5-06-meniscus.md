# Question Q-1.5-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Why is it important to read the bottom of the meniscus when measuring liquid volume in a measuring cylinder?

**Answer**: The liquid surface curves due to adhesion to the cylinder walls. Reading the bottom of the meniscus (at eye level) gives the true volume. Reading the top or at an angle introduces a parallax error.

**Explanation**: The meniscus effect is caused by adhesion. A common error is saying "read the middle" or not mentioning eye level.
