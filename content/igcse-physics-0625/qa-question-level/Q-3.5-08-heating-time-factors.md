# Question Q-3.5-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A student claims that a substance with a higher specific heat capacity takes longer to heat up. Is this always true? Explain your answer, considering all relevant factors.

**Answer**: Not always. The time to heat up depends on: (1) specific heat capacity (higher c → more energy per degree → longer), (2) mass (more mass → more energy needed → longer), (3) power of heater (higher power → faster heating). For the same mass and heater, a substance with higher c does take longer. But comparing different masses or using different heaters could reverse this. The statement is only true when mass and heating power are kept constant.

**Explanation**: Multiple factors affect heating time. A common error is considering only specific heat capacity while ignoring mass and power.
