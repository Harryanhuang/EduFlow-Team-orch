# Question Q-1.5-03

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Describe how you would determine the density of an irregularly shaped rock that does not dissolve in water.

**Answer**: Measure the mass using a balance. Fill a measuring cylinder with water and record the initial volume. Submerge the rock and record the new volume. Volume of rock = final - initial. Density = mass / volume.

**Explanation**: The displacement method works for irregular objects. A common error is not specifying measuring the mass first or forgetting to subtract initial volume.
