# Question Q-1.5-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A student measures the density of a metal cube using a balance and a ruler. The cube has a mass of 54.0 g and each side measures 2.0 cm. The accepted density of the metal is 7.0 g/cm³. Calculate the student's result, the percentage error, and suggest which metal it is.

**Answer**: Volume = 2.0³ = 8.0 cm³. Student's density = 54.0/8.0 = 6.75 g/cm³. Percentage error = |7.0 - 6.75| / 7.0 × 100% = 3.6%. The metal is likely zinc (accepted density ~7.1 g/cm³) or an iron alloy.

**Explanation**: The volume calculation from dimensions assumes a perfect cube. A common error is using surface area (6 × 2²) instead of volume. The percentage error formula uses absolute difference over accepted value.
