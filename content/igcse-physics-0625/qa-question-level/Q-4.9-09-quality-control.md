# Question Q-4.9-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Explain why ultrasound is used for quality control in manufacturing (e.g., checking for cracks in metal), but visible light cannot be used for the same purpose.

**Answer**: Ultrasound has a much shorter wavelength than visible light when travelling through solids, allowing it to detect small internal defects. More importantly, ultrasound can penetrate opaque materials like metals, while visible light is absorbed or reflected at the surface and cannot pass through. Ultrasound waves reflect off internal boundaries (cracks, voids), producing detectable echoes that reveal the location and size of defects. Visible light cannot enter the material at all.

**Explanation**: Penetration and internal reflection are key. A common error is focusing only on wavelength without mentioning that light cannot penetrate metals.
