# Question Q-1.4-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A student says that a 1 kg object on Earth and a 1 kg object on the Moon have "the same weight." Is this correct? Explain.

**Answer**: No. They have the same mass (1 kg), but different weights because g is different. On Earth W = 1 × 10 = 10 N; on the Moon W = 1 × 1.6 = 1.6 N.

**Explanation**: The key distinction between mass and weight. A common error is agreeing with the student.
