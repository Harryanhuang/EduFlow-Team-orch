# Question Q-3.6-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Describe the shape of a cooling curve for molten lead as it cools from 400°C to 250°C. (Melting point of lead = 327°C)

**Answer**: The temperature decreases steadily from 400°C to 327°C, then remains constant at 327°C while lead solidifies (plateau), then decreases again from 327°C to 250°C as solid lead cools.

**Explanation**: The plateau occurs at the melting/freezing point. A common error is missing the plateau or placing it at the wrong temperature.
