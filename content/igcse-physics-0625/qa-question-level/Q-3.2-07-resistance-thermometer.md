# Question Q-3.2-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A resistance thermometer gives a resistance of 20 Ω at 0°C and 28 Ω at 100°C. When placed in a warm liquid, the resistance is 24 Ω. Calculate the temperature of the liquid.

**Answer**: 50°C

**Explanation**: The change in resistance is proportional to temperature change. Total resistance change = 28 - 20 = 8 Ω for 100°C. Measured change = 24 - 20 = 4 Ω. Temperature = (4/8) × 100 = 50°C. A common error is not recognising the linear relationship between resistance and temperature.
