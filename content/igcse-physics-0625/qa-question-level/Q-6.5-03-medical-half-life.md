# Question Q-6.5-03

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Iodine-131 (half-life 8 days) is used to treat thyroid conditions. Why is an isotope with a relatively short half-life preferred for medical diagnosis and treatment?

**Answer**: A short half-life means the radioactivity decreases rapidly inside the patient's body, minimising the total radiation dose received and reducing the risk of long-term damage. However, it must be long enough to allow the isotope to reach the target organ and complete the diagnostic scan or treatment before decaying significantly.

**Explanation**: There is a balance: short enough to minimise dose but long enough to be useful. A common error is simply stating "short half-life is always safer" without considering the required functional duration.
