# Question Q-1.3-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Explain why a parachute helps a skydiver land safely, in terms of forces.

**Answer**: Opening the parachute greatly increases air resistance (drag). This makes air resistance greater than weight temporarily, causing deceleration. A new, lower terminal velocity is reached, allowing a safe landing.

**Explanation**: The parachute increases surface area, increasing drag. A common error is saying the parachute "reduces gravity" or "reduces weight."
