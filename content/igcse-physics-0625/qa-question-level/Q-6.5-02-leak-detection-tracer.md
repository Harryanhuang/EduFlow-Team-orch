# Question Q-6.5-02

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: A radioactive isotope is used as a tracer to detect a leak in an underground water pipe. What type of radiation should the isotope emit and why?

**Answer**: Gamma radiation. It has high penetrating power and can pass through soil and pipe walls to be detected above ground with a Geiger counter. Alpha and beta radiation would be absorbed by the pipe and surrounding soil, making detection impossible.

**Explanation**: Penetrating power is essential for underground detection. Gamma rays can travel through metres of soil. A common error is suggesting beta radiation, which has insufficient penetrating power for this application.
