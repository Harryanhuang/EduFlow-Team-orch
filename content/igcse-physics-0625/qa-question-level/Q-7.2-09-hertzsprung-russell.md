# Question Q-7.2-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: On a Hertzsprung-Russell (H-R) diagram, stars are plotted according to their surface temperature and luminosity. Describe where the following would be found on an H-R diagram: (a) main-sequence stars, (b) red giants, (c) white dwarfs. Sketch the general shape.

**Answer**: (a) Main-sequence stars form a diagonal band from top-left (hot, luminous) to bottom-right (cool, dim). The Sun sits roughly in the middle. (b) Red giants are found in the top-right region: cool (red) but very luminous due to large size. (c) White dwarfs occupy the bottom-left: hot (white/blue) but dim due to small size. The diagram shows distinct groupings, not random distribution.

**Explanation**: The H-R diagram reveals stellar evolution pathways. Position depends on both temperature and size. A common error is placing red giants in the bottom-right (confusing low luminosity) or white dwarfs in the top-left (confusing high temperature with high luminosity).
