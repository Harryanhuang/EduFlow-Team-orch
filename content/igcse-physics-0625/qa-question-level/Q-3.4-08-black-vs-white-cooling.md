# Question Q-3.4-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Two identical cans, one painted black and one painted white, each contain equal volumes of water at 80°C. They are left in a room at 20°C. Which can cools faster? Explain the main mechanism and name two factors that affect the rate of cooling.

**Answer**: The black can cools faster. Black surfaces are better emitters of infrared radiation than white/shiny surfaces, so the black can radiate thermal energy more quickly. The main mechanism is radiation (since both are hotter than surroundings). Two factors affecting cooling rate: (1) surface area (larger area → faster cooling), (2) temperature difference (greater difference → faster cooling). Other factors: air movement (draughts increase cooling), insulation.

**Explanation**: Good emitters lose heat faster. A common error is saying the black can "absorbs more cold" — cooling is about emission, not absorption of cold.
