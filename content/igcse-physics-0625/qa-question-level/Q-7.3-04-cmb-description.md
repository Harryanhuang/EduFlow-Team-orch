# Question Q-7.3-04

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is the cosmic microwave background radiation (CMB)? Explain why it is found throughout all of space.

**Answer**: The CMB is low-energy electromagnetic radiation in the microwave region that fills all of space uniformly in every direction. It originated as high-energy gamma radiation from the extremely hot, dense early universe about 380 000 years after the Big Bang. As the universe expanded over billions of years, this radiation was stretched (red-shifted) to longer microwave wavelengths. It is found everywhere because it was emitted when the entire universe was hot and dense, not from a single point or explosion in space.

**Explanation**: The CMB is the "afterglow" of the Big Bang, detectable as a faint hiss of microwaves in any direction. A common error is thinking it comes from stars, the Sun, or human-made sources like microwave ovens.
