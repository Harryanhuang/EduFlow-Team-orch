# Question Q-7.3-02

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: What is red-shift? Explain how it provides evidence for the expansion of the universe.

**Answer**: Red-shift is the displacement of spectral lines from distant galaxies towards longer wavelengths (the red end of the spectrum). It occurs because the light waves are stretched as space itself expands while the light travels through it. The observation that almost all galaxies show red-shift, and that more distant galaxies show greater red-shift, indicates they are moving away from us — evidence that the universe is expanding uniformly in all directions.

**Explanation**: This is analogous to the Doppler effect for sound: a receding source has its waves stretched. In cosmology, it is space itself expanding rather than galaxies moving through static space. A common error is saying galaxies "look redder" due to dust or ageing rather than wavelength stretching.
