# Question Q-3.3-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A thermometer uses alcohol instead of mercury. State two reasons why alcohol might be preferred and one disadvantage of using alcohol.

**Answer**: Advantages: (1) Alcohol has a lower freezing point (-115°C) than mercury (-39°C), so it can measure very low temperatures. (2) Alcohol expands more per degree than mercury, giving better sensitivity. Disadvantage: Alcohol has a lower boiling point (78°C) than mercury (357°C), limiting its maximum temperature range.

**Explanation**: Alcohol vs mercury involves trade-offs. A common error is saying alcohol is "more accurate" — accuracy depends on calibration, not the liquid used.
