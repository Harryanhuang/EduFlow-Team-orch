# Question Q-2.2-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A force-extension graph for a spring shows a straight line from the origin up to 12 N, then curves. What does the curved section indicate?

**Answer**: The spring has exceeded its limit of proportionality and no longer obeys Hooke's law. The extension is no longer proportional to the force.

**Explanation**: The straight section represents Hooke's law; the curve shows non-linear behaviour. A common error is saying the spring "breaks" in the curved section.
