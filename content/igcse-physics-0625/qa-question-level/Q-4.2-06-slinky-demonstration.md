# Question Q-4.2-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A slinky spring is used to demonstrate waves. When one end is moved side to side, a wave travels along the spring. What type of wave is produced? Explain your answer.

**Answer**: A transverse wave is produced. The particles of the spring move perpendicular (side to side) to the direction of wave travel along the spring.

**Explanation**: Side-to-side motion creates transverse waves. A common error is confusing this with pushing and pulling (which creates longitudinal waves).
