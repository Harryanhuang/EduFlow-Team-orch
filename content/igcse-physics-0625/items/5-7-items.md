# Items: 5.7 — Power, energy, cost of electricity

### Question Q-5.7-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A 240 V mains supply powers a heater drawing a current of 8 A. Calculate the power of the heater.
**Answer**: 1920 W, or 1.92 kW.
**Explanation**: P = IV = 8 × 240 = 1920 W. Use P = IV because voltage and current are given.
**Tags**: power, P=IV, calculation

### Question Q-5.7-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A 100 W lamp is switched on for 5 hours. Calculate the energy transferred in kWh.
**Answer**: 0.5 kWh
**Explanation**: Energy = Pt = 0.1 kW × 5 h = 0.5 kWh. Convert W to kW before calculating kWh.
**Tags**: energy, kWh, calculation

### Question Q-5.7-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A family uses 450 kWh of electricity in one month. If the cost is $0.12 per kWh, calculate the total electricity bill.
**Answer**: $54
**Explanation**: Cost = 450 × 0.12 = $54. Electricity bills commonly use kWh directly.
**Tags**: electricity-cost, kWh, billing

### Question Q-5.7-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A 2 kW heater and a 500 W lamp are both used for 4 hours. Which costs more to run and by how much? Electricity costs $0.10 per kWh.
**Answer**: Heater: 8 kWh, cost $0.80. Lamp: 2 kWh, cost $0.20. The heater costs $0.60 more.
**Explanation**: Compare energy use first, then cost.
**Tags**: power, energy, cost, comparison

### Question Q-5.7-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: An appliance has a power rating of 750 W and a resistance of 8 ohm. Calculate the current flowing through it.
**Answer**: 9.69 A, approximately 9.7 A.
**Explanation**: P = I^2R, so I = sqrt(P/R) = sqrt(750/8) = 9.69 A. Use P = I^2R because power and resistance are given.
**Tags**: power, P=I2R, current, calculation

### Question Q-5.7-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Convert 2.5 kWh into joules.
**Answer**: 9.0 × 10^6 J
**Explanation**: 1 kWh = 3.6 × 10^6 J, so 2.5 kWh = 2.5 × 3.6 × 10^6 = 9.0 × 10^6 J. The kilo in kWh is part of the conversion.
**Tags**: kWh, joules, energy-conversion

### Question Q-5.7-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A 60 W lamp is replaced by a 9 W LED lamp. Both are used for 5 hours per day for 30 days. Electricity costs $0.20 per kWh. Calculate the monthly saving.
**Answer**: Old lamp energy = 0.060 × 150 = 9.0 kWh. LED energy = 0.009 × 150 = 1.35 kWh. Saving = 7.65 kWh, so cost saving = 7.65 × 0.20 = $1.53.
**Explanation**: Use total time and convert W to kW.
**Tags**: energy-saving, cost, LED, multi-step

### Question Q-5.7-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A kettle is rated 2.2 kW and takes 3 minutes to boil water. Calculate the energy transferred in joules and in kWh.
**Answer**: Time = 180 s = 0.05 h. Energy = 2200 × 180 = 396000 J. In kWh, energy = 2.2 × 0.05 = 0.11 kWh.
**Explanation**: Use seconds with watts for joules and hours with kW for kWh.
**Tags**: power, energy, joules, kWh

### Question Q-5.7-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A student says, "The appliance with the highest power rating always costs the most to run." Explain why this is not necessarily true.
**Answer**: Cost depends on energy transferred, E = Pt, not power alone. A high-power appliance used briefly may use less energy than a low-power appliance used for many hours.
**Explanation**: Running time matters as well as power.
**Tags**: power, energy, cost, misconception
