# Items: 4.9 — Ultrasound

### Question Q-4.9-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is ultrasound? State its frequency range.
**Answer**: Ultrasound is sound with a frequency above 20 000 Hz (20 kHz), above the upper limit of human hearing.
**Explanation**: Ultrasound is high-frequency sound, not a different type of wave. A common error is saying humans can hear it if it is loud enough.
**Tags**: ultrasound, frequency, definition

### Question Q-4.9-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Why is ultrasound preferred over X-rays for imaging a developing fetus?
**Answer**: Ultrasound is non-ionising and does not damage living cells, so it is safer for a developing fetus. X-rays are ionising radiation and can damage cells or DNA.
**Explanation**: Safety is the key reason. A common error is saying ultrasound is always clearer than X-rays.
**Tags**: ultrasound, safety, medical-imaging

### Question Q-4.9-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: An ultrasound probe sends a pulse into the body. The reflected echo is detected after 0.000 04 s. If the speed of ultrasound in tissue is 1500 m/s, calculate the depth of the reflecting surface.
**Answer**: 0.03 m, or 3 cm.
**Explanation**: Total distance = 1500 × 0.00004 = 0.06 m. The pulse travels down and back, so depth = 0.06 / 2 = 0.03 m.
**Tags**: ultrasound, depth, echo, calculation

### Question Q-4.9-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Give two industrial uses of ultrasound.
**Answer**: Detecting cracks or defects in metal structures, and cleaning delicate items. Ultrasound reflects from internal defects and produces vibrations that remove dirt from hard-to-reach places.
**Explanation**: Both examples must be industrial rather than medical. A common error is listing only scanning a fetus.
**Tags**: ultrasound, industrial-applications

### Question Q-4.9-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A bat emits ultrasound at a frequency of 50 kHz. Explain how the bat uses this to locate prey in the dark.
**Answer**: The bat emits ultrasound pulses and listens for echoes reflected from objects or prey. By measuring the time delay between emission and echo, it estimates distance. This is echolocation.
**Explanation**: Echolocation depends on echo timing. A common error is saying bats "see" directly with ultrasound.
**Tags**: ultrasound, echolocation, bat, nature

### Question Q-4.9-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: An ultrasound wave has a frequency of 2 MHz and travels through tissue at 1500 m/s. Calculate its wavelength.
**Answer**: 0.75 mm, or 7.5 × 10^-4 m.
**Explanation**: λ = v/f = 1500 / (2 × 10^6) = 0.00075 m = 0.75 mm. A common error is not converting MHz to Hz.
**Tags**: ultrasound, wavelength, calculation, unit-conversion

### Question Q-4.9-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: In prenatal imaging, an ultrasound scan produces an image of the fetus on a screen. Describe the process of how this image is formed, including what happens to the ultrasound waves.
**Answer**: The transducer emits short ultrasound pulses into the body. At boundaries between tissues, some waves are reflected as echoes and some continue deeper. The transducer detects the echoes and a computer uses their arrival times and strengths to build an image. Gel improves transmission between the transducer and skin.
**Explanation**: The image is produced from pulse-echo data, not by taking a direct photograph. A common error is ignoring reflection at tissue boundaries.
**Tags**: ultrasound, prenatal-imaging, medical-application, extended-response, explanation

### Question Q-4.9-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A ship uses sonar to measure ocean depth. It sends an ultrasound pulse downward and detects the echo 0.8 seconds later. If the speed of sound in seawater is 1520 m/s, calculate: (a) the depth of the water, (b) the time taken for the pulse to reach the seabed.
**Answer**: (a) Total distance = 1520 × 0.8 = 1216 m, so depth = 1216 / 2 = 608 m. (b) Time to reach the seabed = 0.8 / 2 = 0.4 s.
**Explanation**: The echo time is for the downward and upward journey. A common error is giving 1216 m as the depth.
**Tags**: sonar, depth-measurement, calculation, marine-application

### Question Q-4.9-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain why ultrasound is used for quality control in manufacturing, for example checking for cracks in metal, but visible light cannot be used for the same purpose.
**Answer**: Ultrasound can penetrate opaque materials such as metals and reflects from internal boundaries like cracks or voids. The reflected echoes reveal the position and size of defects. Visible light is mostly reflected or absorbed at the surface of metal and cannot show internal cracks.
**Explanation**: Penetration and internal reflection are the important points. A common error is only mentioning wavelength.
**Tags**: ultrasound, quality-control, ndt, comparison, extended-response
