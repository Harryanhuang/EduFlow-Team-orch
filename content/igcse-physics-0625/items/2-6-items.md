# Items: 2.6 — Pressure in liquids and gases

### Question Q-2.6-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A force of 200 N acts on an area of 4 m². Calculate the pressure.
**Answer**: 50 Pa
**Explanation**: p = F/A = 200/4 = 50 Pa. A common error is giving the answer in N/m instead of Pa (N/m²).
**Tags**: pressure, force, area, calculation

### Question Q-2.6-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Calculate the pressure at a depth of 10 m in water. (density of water = 1000 kg/m³, g = 10 N/kg)
**Answer**: 100 000 Pa (or 100 kPa)
**Explanation**: p = ρgh = 1000 × 10 × 10 = 100 000 Pa. A common error is using the wrong density or forgetting g.
**Tags**: liquid-pressure, p=ρgh, calculation

### Question Q-2.6-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A gas has a volume of 500 cm³ at a pressure of 200 kPa. If the temperature is constant, what is the volume when the pressure is increased to 400 kPa?
**Answer**: 250 cm³
**Explanation**: Boyle's Law: p₁V₁ = p₂V₂. 200 × 500 = 400 × V₂. V₂ = 100 000/400 = 250 cm³. A common error is thinking volume increases with pressure.
**Tags**: Boyle's-law, gas-pressure, volume, calculation

### Question Q-2.6-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Use the kinetic particle model to explain why increasing the temperature of a gas in a sealed container increases the pressure.
**Answer**: Higher temperature means particles move faster. They collide with the container walls more frequently and with greater force, increasing the pressure.
**Explanation**: Both frequency and force of collisions increase. A common error is only mentioning one of these.
**Tags**: kinetic-model, gas-pressure, temperature, explanation

### Question Q-2.6-05
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A U-tube manometer contains water. One side is connected to a gas supply and the water level on that side is 15 cm lower than the other side. Calculate the pressure of the gas above atmospheric pressure. (water density = 1000 kg/m³, g = 10 N/kg)
**Answer**: 3000 Pa (or 3 kPa)
**Explanation**: The height difference is 15 cm on one side + 15 cm on the other = 30 cm = 0.3 m total. p = ρgh = 1000 × 10 × 0.3 = 3000 Pa. A common error is using 15 cm instead of 30 cm.
**Tags**: manometer, pressure, height-difference, calculation

### Question Q-2.6-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why do the ears of a diver "pop" when diving deep underwater?
**Answer**: Pressure increases with depth (p = ρgh). The increased water pressure pushes on the eardrums. The "pop" is the equalising of pressure inside and outside the ear.
**Explanation**: Pressure in a liquid increases with depth. A common error is attributing it to temperature change.
**Tags**: liquid-pressure, depth, application

### Question Q-2.6-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A rectangular tank measures 2 m by 3 m and is filled with oil to a depth of 1.5 m. Calculate the total force exerted by the oil on the bottom of the tank. (density of oil = 900 kg/m³, g = 10 N/kg)
**Answer**: 81 000 N
**Explanation**: First calculate pressure: p = ρgh = 900 × 10 × 1.5 = 13 500 Pa. Then force: F = pA = 13 500 × (2 × 3) = 13 500 × 6 = 81 000 N. A common error is calculating only the pressure without converting to force.
**Tags**: liquid-pressure, force, multi-step, calculation

### Question Q-2.6-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: In an experiment to verify Boyle's Law, a student records the following data: at pressure 100 kPa the volume is 60 cm³; at pressure 150 kPa the volume is 42 cm³; at pressure 200 kPa the volume is 30 cm³. Show whether these results support Boyle's Law.
**Answer**: For Boyle's Law, pV should be constant. At 100 kPa: pV = 100 × 60 = 6000. At 150 kPa: pV = 150 × 42 = 6300. At 200 kPa: pV = 200 × 30 = 6000. The values are approximately constant (6000-6300), supporting Boyle's Law within experimental error.
**Explanation**: Students must calculate pV for each pair and compare. A common error is only checking two points or not commenting on the slight variation.
**Tags**: Boyle's-law, experimental-data, analysis

### Question Q-2.6-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why a balloon expands when taken from a cold room into a warm room, using the kinetic particle model. State any assumptions you make.
**Answer**: In the warm room, air particles gain kinetic energy and move faster. They collide with the balloon walls more frequently and with greater force, increasing the internal pressure. This causes the balloon to expand until the internal pressure equals the external pressure. Assumption: the amount of air in the balloon remains constant (no leakage).
**Explanation**: The explanation must link temperature to particle motion to pressure to volume change. A common error is not mentioning the balance between internal and external pressure.
**Tags**: kinetic-model, gas-laws, thermal-expansion, explanation
