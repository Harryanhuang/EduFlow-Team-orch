# Items: 3.1–3.6 — Waves (round 2)

### Question Q-4.1-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A wave has a frequency of 50 Hz and a wavelength of 4 m. Calculate its speed and period.
**Answer**: Speed = 200 m/s. Period = 0.02 s.
**Explanation**: v = fλ = 50 × 4 = 200 m/s. T = 1/f = 1/50 = 0.02 s. A common error is confusing period and frequency.
**Tags**: wave-speed, frequency, period, calculation

### Question Q-4.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: State the law of reflection and draw a labelled diagram showing the angle of incidence and angle of reflection.
**Answer**: The angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection. Both angles are measured between the ray and the normal (perpendicular) to the surface. The incident ray, reflected ray, and normal all lie in the same plane.
**Explanation**: Angles are measured from the normal, not the surface. A common error is measuring from the surface.
**Tags**: reflection, law-of-reflection, angles

### Question Q-4.3-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Light enters glass (n = 1.5) from air at an angle of incidence of 30°. Calculate the angle of refraction.
**Answer**: 19.5°
**Explanation**: n = sin(i)/sin(r), so sin(r) = sin(30°)/1.5 = 0.5/1.5 = 0.333. r = sin⁻¹(0.333) ≈ 19.5°. A common error is using sin(r) = n × sin(i).
**Tags**: refraction, Snell's-law, calculation

### Question Q-4.4-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the critical angle for glass with refractive index 1.5?
**Answer**: 41.8°
**Explanation**: sin(c) = 1/n = 1/1.5 = 0.667. c = sin⁻¹(0.667) ≈ 41.8°. A common error is using n = sin(c) instead of sin(c) = 1/n.
**Tags**: critical-angle, total-internal-reflection, calculation

### Question Q-4.5-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Give two differences between real and virtual images.
**Answer**: (1) A real image can be projected onto a screen; a virtual image cannot. (2) A real image is formed where light rays actually converge; a virtual image is formed where rays appear to come from. (Also: real images are inverted, virtual images are upright.)
**Explanation**: Convergence vs. apparent divergence is key. A common error is saying virtual images "don't exist."
**Tags**: real-image, virtual-image, comparison

### Question Q-4.6-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A converging lens of focal length 10 cm is used to form an image of an object placed 15 cm from the lens. Calculate the image distance and state whether the image is real or virtual.
**Answer**: 30 cm, real image.
**Explanation**: 1/f = 1/u + 1/v, so 1/10 = 1/15 + 1/v. 1/v = 1/10 - 1/15 = 3/30 - 2/30 = 1/30. v = 30 cm. Since v is positive, the image is real. A common error is getting the sign wrong.
**Tags**: lens, focal-length, image-distance, calculation
