### Question Q-5.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A current of 0.5 A flows through a resistor for 2 minutes. Calculate the charge that passes through it.
**Answer**: 60 C
**Explanation**: Q = It = 0.5 × (2 × 60) = 0.5 × 120 = 60 C. A common error is using 2 instead of 120 seconds.
**Tags**: charge, current, time, calculation

### Question Q-5.2-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A 12 V battery is connected to a 4 Ω resistor. Calculate the power dissipated by the resistor.
**Answer**: 36 W
**Explanation**: P = V²/R = 144/4 = 36 W. Alternatively: I = V/R = 12/4 = 3 A, P = VI = 12 × 3 = 36 W. A common error is using P = V/R.
**Tags**: power, voltage, resistance, calculation

### Question Q-5.3-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Two resistors, 6 Ω and 3 Ω, are connected in parallel. Calculate the total resistance.
**Answer**: 2 Ω
**Explanation**: 1/R_total = 1/6 + 1/3 = 1/6 + 2/6 = 3/6 = 1/2. R_total = 2 Ω. A common error is adding them (9 Ω).
**Tags**: parallel-resistors, total-resistance, calculation

### Question Q-5.4-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Why are lamps in a house wired in parallel and not in series? Give two reasons.
**Answer**: (1) Each lamp receives the full supply voltage and operates at full brightness. In series, the voltage is shared. (2) If one lamp fails, the others continue to work. In series, one failure breaks the circuit.
**Explanation**: Parallel = independent operation. A common error is giving only one reason.
**Tags**: parallel-circuit, domestic-wiring, reasons

### Question Q-5.5-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the function of a fuse in a circuit?
**Answer**: A fuse contains a thin wire that melts and breaks the circuit if the current exceeds the rated value. This prevents overheating and protects the appliance and user from damage or electric shock.
**Explanation**: It's a deliberate weak link. A common error is saying it "regulates voltage."
**Tags**: fuse, safety, circuit-protection

### Question Q-5.6-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Describe the pattern of magnetic field lines around a straight current-carrying wire.
**Answer**: Concentric circles centred on the wire, with the plane of the circles perpendicular to the wire. The direction is given by the right-hand grip rule.
**Explanation**: Circular, not radial. A common error is drawing straight lines.
**Tags**: magnetic-field, current-carrying-wire, field-lines

### Question Q-5.7-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A transformer has 200 turns on the primary coil and 800 turns on the secondary. If the input voltage is 12 V, what is the output voltage? What type of transformer is this?
**Answer**: 48 V. This is a step-up transformer.
**Explanation**: Vp/Vs = Np/Ns, so 12/Vs = 200/800 = 1/4. Vs = 48 V. More secondary turns → step-up. A common error is inverting the ratio.
**Tags**: transformer, voltage-ratio, step-up, calculation
