# Items: 2.1–2.6 — Thermal physics (round 2)

### Question Q-3.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Explain why a metal bar expands when heated, in terms of particles.
**Answer**: When heated, the metal particles gain kinetic energy and vibrate more vigorously. This increased vibration pushes neighbouring particles further apart, causing the bar to expand in all directions.
**Explanation**: More vibration → larger separation → expansion. A common error is saying particles "get bigger."
**Tags**: thermal-expansion, particle-model

### Question Q-3.2-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A gas in a sealed container is heated from 27°C to 327°C. What happens to the pressure? Explain in terms of particles.
**Answer**: Temperature in Kelvin doubles (300 K → 600 K), so pressure doubles (if volume is constant). Particles move faster, collide with the walls more frequently and with greater force, increasing pressure.
**Explanation**: Must convert to Kelvin. A common error is using Celsius directly.
**Tags**: gas-laws, pressure, Kelvin, particle-model

### Question Q-3.3-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Name the three methods of thermal energy transfer and give one example of each.
**Answer**: Conduction (heat flowing through a metal rod), convection (hot air rising above a radiator), radiation (heat from the Sun reaching Earth).
**Explanation**: Each method has a distinct mechanism. A common error is confusing convection with conduction.
**Tags**: thermal-transfer, conduction, convection, radiation

### Question Q-3.4-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Calculate the energy required to heat 500 g of water from 20°C to 80°C. (c_water = 4200 J/kg·K)
**Answer**: 126000 J (126 kJ)
**Explanation**: ΔE = mcΔθ = 0.5 × 4200 × (80 - 20) = 0.5 × 4200 × 60 = 126000 J. A common error is using 500 instead of 0.5 kg.
**Tags**: specific-heat, calculation, energy

### Question Q-3.5-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why does evaporation cause cooling?
**Answer**: The fastest-moving (highest energy) molecules at the surface escape during evaporation. This lowers the average kinetic energy of the remaining molecules, which means the temperature decreases.
**Explanation**: High-energy molecules leave → average KE drops → temperature drops. A common error is saying evaporation "absorbs cold."
**Tags**: evaporation, cooling, explanation

### Question Q-3.6-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A substance is heated at a constant rate. Its temperature rises, then stays constant for a while, then rises again. What is happening during the constant-temperature period?
**Answer**: The substance is changing state (melting or boiling). The energy supplied is used to break intermolecular bonds (latent heat) rather than increase temperature.
**Explanation**: Latent heat = energy for state change at constant temperature. A common error is saying "no energy is being supplied."
**Tags**: latent-heat, state-change, heating-curve
