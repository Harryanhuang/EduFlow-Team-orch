# Items: 4.3 — Wavefronts, reflection, refraction

### Question Q-4.3-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: State the law of reflection.
**Answer**: The angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection (i = r). The incident ray, reflected ray, and normal all lie in the same plane.
**Explanation**: Both parts must be stated. A common error is only stating i = r without the plane condition.
**Tags**: reflection, law, definition

### Question Q-4.3-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A ray of light strikes a plane mirror at an angle of 30° to the mirror surface. What is the angle of reflection?
**Answer**: 60°
**Explanation**: The angle of incidence is measured from the normal, not the mirror. i = 90° - 30° = 60°. By the law of reflection, r = 60°. A common error is answering 30°.
**Tags**: reflection, angle, normal

### Question Q-4.3-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why a straight stick appears bent when partially immersed in water.
**Answer**: Light from the underwater part of the stick is refracted (bent) as it passes from water into air. The rays bend away from the normal, so the underwater part appears to be at a different position, making the stick look bent.
**Explanation**: Refraction at the water-air interface causes the apparent displacement. A common error is saying "reflection" instead of refraction.
**Tags**: refraction, apparent-bending, explanation

### Question Q-4.3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: When a wave passes from deep water to shallow water, what happens to its speed, frequency, and wavelength?
**Answer**: Speed decreases, frequency stays the same, wavelength decreases. The frequency is determined by the source and does not change when the wave enters a different medium.
**Explanation**: Frequency is invariant across boundaries. A common error is saying frequency changes.
**Tags**: refraction, wave-properties, depth-change

### Question Q-4.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is a wavefront? Draw the direction of wave travel relative to a wavefront.
**Answer**: A wavefront is a line or surface connecting points on a wave that are in the same phase (e.g., all at a crest). The direction of wave travel is perpendicular to the wavefront.
**Explanation**: Wavefronts are like contour lines on a map. A common error is drawing wave travel parallel to the wavefront.
**Tags**: wavefront, definition, direction

### Question Q-4.3-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A ray of light is incident on a plane mirror at an angle of 45° to the normal. What is the angle between the incident ray and the reflected ray?
**Answer**: 90°
**Explanation**: Angle of incidence = 45°, angle of reflection = 45°. The angle between incident and reflected rays = 45° + 45° = 90°. A common error is answering 45° (the angle of incidence alone).
**Tags**: reflection, angle-calculation, geometry

### Question Q-4.3-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A periscope uses two plane mirrors placed at 45° to the horizontal. If the object is 2 m above the top mirror and the bottom mirror is 1.5 m below the top mirror, how far above the observer's eye does the image appear?
**Answer**: 3.5 m
**Explanation**: In a plane mirror, the image distance equals the object distance. The total vertical distance from object to eye = 2 m + 1.5 m = 3.5 m. Each mirror produces a virtual image at the same distance behind it. A common error is adding distances incorrectly.
**Tags**: periscope, plane-mirror, multi-step, application

### Question Q-4.3-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Water waves approach a harbour wall with a gap. The waves have a wavelength of 2 m. Describe what happens to the waves after they pass through: (a) a narrow gap (0.5 m wide), (b) a wide gap (10 m wide). Name this phenomenon.
**Answer**: This is diffraction. (a) With a narrow gap (smaller than wavelength), the waves spread out significantly in a semicircular pattern. (b) With a wide gap (much larger than wavelength), the waves pass through with minimal spreading; only the edges diffract slightly.
**Explanation**: Diffraction is most pronounced when the gap size is comparable to or smaller than the wavelength. A common error is thinking wider gaps cause more diffraction.
**Tags**: diffraction, wavefront, gap-size, harbour

### Question Q-4.3-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Parallel wavefronts approach a boundary between two media. In medium A, the wavelength is 4 cm. In medium B, the wavelength is 3 cm. If the wavefronts hit the boundary at an angle, explain why the direction of the waves changes as they enter medium B.
**Answer**: The direction changes due to refraction. One edge of the wavefront enters medium B first and slows down (shorter wavelength means slower speed), while the other edge is still in medium A. This causes the wavefront to pivot, changing direction. The waves bend towards the normal when entering the slower medium.
**Explanation**: Refraction occurs because one part of the wavefront changes speed before another. A common error is saying the waves "bounce" off the boundary.
**Tags**: refraction, wavefront, speed-change, explanation
