# Items: 3.3 — Thermal expansion

### Question Q-3.3-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Use the kinetic particle model to explain why a metal rod expands when heated.
**Answer**: When heated, the particles gain energy and vibrate more vigorously. The increased vibration pushes neighbouring particles further apart, increasing the average separation and causing the rod to expand.
**Explanation**: The particles themselves don't get bigger — their spacing increases. A common error is saying "the particles expand."
**Tags**: thermal-expansion, kinetic-model, explanation

### Question Q-3.3-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A bimetallic strip is made of brass and iron. When heated, which way does it bend? Explain. (Brass expands more than iron.)
**Answer**: It bends towards the iron side. Brass expands more than iron, so the brass side becomes longer, causing the strip to curve with the brass on the outside of the curve.
**Explanation**: The side that expands more is always on the outside of the bend. A common error is saying it bends towards the brass.
**Tags**: bimetallic-strip, thermal-expansion, application

### Question Q-3.3-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Give two examples of where thermal expansion is a nuisance in everyday life and how it is dealt with.
**Answer**: (1) Railway tracks: gaps left between sections to allow for expansion in hot weather. (2) Bridges: expansion joints (rollers) allow the bridge to expand and contract without damage.
**Explanation**: Both examples need a problem and a solution. A common error is only listing the problem.
**Tags**: thermal-expansion, application, everyday-life

### Question Q-3.3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Which expands most when heated by the same amount: a solid, a liquid, or a gas? Explain.
**Answer**: A gas expands most. Gas particles are furthest apart and have the weakest intermolecular forces, so they move apart most easily when heated. Liquids expand more than solids but less than gases.
**Explanation**: The order is gas > liquid > solid. A common error is saying solids expand most because they are "most dense."
**Tags**: thermal-expansion, states-of-matter, comparison

### Question Q-3.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is the glass of a drinking glass more likely to crack when boiling water is poured into a thick glass than a thin glass?
**Answer**: In thick glass, the inner surface heats and expands rapidly while the outer surface remains cool. The uneven expansion creates stress that cracks the glass. Thin glass heats more evenly.
**Explanation**: Differential expansion causes stress. A common error is saying "thick glass holds more heat."
**Tags**: thermal-expansion, glass, cracking, application

### Question Q-3.3-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A copper ball has a diameter of exactly 5.00 cm at 20°C. It just fits through a circular hole in an aluminium plate. If both are heated to 100°C, will the ball still fit through the hole? Explain. (Copper expands more than aluminium.)
**Answer**: No, the ball will not fit. Both the ball and the hole expand, but copper expands more than aluminium. The ball's diameter increases more than the hole's diameter, so the ball becomes too large to pass through.
**Explanation**: Both objects expand, but the material with the greater expansion coefficient expands more. A common error is thinking the hole "shrinks"; holes expand as if filled with the same material.
**Tags**: thermal-expansion, hole-expansion, comparison

### Question Q-3.3-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A thermometer uses alcohol instead of mercury. State two reasons why alcohol might be preferred and one disadvantage of using alcohol.
**Answer**: Alcohol may be preferred because it has a lower freezing point than mercury, so it can measure very low temperatures, and because it expands more per degree, giving better sensitivity. A disadvantage is that alcohol has a lower boiling point, limiting its maximum temperature range.
**Explanation**: Alcohol and mercury involve trade-offs. A common error is saying alcohol is "more accurate"; accuracy depends on calibration.
**Tags**: thermometer, alcohol, mercury, comparison

### Question Q-3.3-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain why overhead electrical cables are installed with some slack rather than being pulled tight between pylons.
**Answer**: Cables are installed with slack to allow for contraction in cold weather. If pulled tight in warm weather, the cables would contract when the temperature drops, creating tension that could snap the cable or damage the pylons.
**Explanation**: Thermal contraction can create very large tension. A common error is only mentioning expansion in hot weather; the main danger is contraction in cold weather pulling cables taut.
**Tags**: thermal-expansion, contraction, cables, application

### Question Q-3.3-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A metal lid on a glass jar is stuck. Running hot water over the lid makes it easier to open. Explain why this works, referring to the expansion of both materials.
**Answer**: Both the metal lid and glass neck expand when heated, but metals generally expand more than glass. The metal lid expands more than the glass, increasing the gap between them and making the lid looser.
**Explanation**: Different materials expand at different rates. A common error is saying only the lid expands; the glass also expands, just less.
**Tags**: thermal-expansion, differential-expansion, application, lid
