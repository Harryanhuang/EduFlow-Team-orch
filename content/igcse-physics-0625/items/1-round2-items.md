### Question Q-1.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Convert 2.5 km to metres, and 3.2 hours to seconds.
**Answer**: 2.5 km = 2500 m. 3.2 hours = 3.2 × 3600 = 11520 s.
**Explanation**: Multiply by the conversion factor. A common error is dividing instead of multiplying.
**Tags**: unit-conversion, SI-units

### Question Q-1.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A student measures the length of a pencil five times: 15.2 cm, 15.4 cm, 15.3 cm, 15.8 cm, 15.3 cm. Identify the outlier and calculate the average excluding it.
**Answer**: 15.8 cm is the outlier. Average of the remaining four: (15.2 + 15.4 + 15.3 + 15.3) / 4 = 61.2 / 4 = 15.3 cm.
**Explanation**: Outliers skew averages. A common error is including the outlier.
**Tags**: measurement, average, outlier

### Question Q-1.2-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A car accelerates uniformly from 10 m/s to 30 m/s in 8 seconds. Calculate the distance travelled during this time.
**Answer**: 160 m
**Explanation**: Average speed = (10 + 30)/2 = 20 m/s. Distance = 20 × 8 = 160 m. Alternatively: a = (30-10)/8 = 2.5 m/s², s = ut + ½at² = 10×8 + ½×2.5×64 = 80 + 80 = 160 m. A common error is using only the final speed.
**Tags**: uniform-acceleration, distance, calculation

### Question Q-1.3-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the difference between mass and weight?
**Answer**: Mass is the amount of matter in an object (measured in kg, constant everywhere). Weight is the force of gravity on that mass (measured in N, varies with gravitational field strength). W = mg.
**Explanation**: Mass ≠ weight. A common error is using them interchangeably.
**Tags**: mass, weight, difference

### Question Q-1.3-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A spring has a spring constant of 40 N/m. Calculate the extension when a 2 kg mass is hung from it. (g = 10 N/kg)
**Answer**: 0.5 m (50 cm)
**Explanation**: Force = weight = mg = 2 × 10 = 20 N. F = kx, so x = F/k = 20/40 = 0.5 m. A common error is using the mass directly instead of converting to force.
**Tags**: Hooke's-law, spring, extension

### Question Q-1.4-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A 500 kg car travelling at 20 m/s brakes to a stop in 4 seconds. Calculate the braking force.
**Answer**: -2500 N (magnitude 2500 N)
**Explanation**: a = (0 - 20)/4 = -5 m/s². F = ma = 500 × (-5) = -2500 N. The negative sign indicates the force opposes the motion. A common error is forgetting the negative sign.
**Tags**: F=ma, braking-force, calculation

### Question Q-1.5-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A motor does 6000 J of work in 30 seconds. Calculate its power output.
**Answer**: 200 W
**Explanation**: P = E/t = 6000/30 = 200 W. A common error is multiplying instead of dividing.
**Tags**: power, work, calculation

### Question Q-1.5-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A 2 kg ball is dropped from a height of 5 m. Calculate its speed just before hitting the ground. (g = 10 N/kg, ignore air resistance)
**Answer**: 10 m/s
**Explanation**: GPE = mgh = 2 × 10 × 5 = 100 J. All GPE converts to KE: ½mv² = 100. v² = 200/2 = 100. v = 10 m/s. A common error is forgetting to take the square root.
**Tags**: energy-conservation, GPE, KE, speed
