# Items: 4.4 — Light: plane mirrors, ray diagrams

### Question Q-4.4-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: State three characteristics of the image formed by a plane mirror.
**Answer**: (1) Virtual, (2) Same size as the object, (3) Laterally inverted (left-right reversed). Also: same distance behind the mirror as the object is in front, upright.
**Explanation**: At least three are needed. A common error is saying "real" or "inverted."
**Tags**: plane-mirror, image, characteristics

### Question Q-4.4-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: An object is placed 15 cm in front of a plane mirror. Where is the image formed? If the object moves 5 cm closer to the mirror, what is the new distance between the object and its image?
**Answer**: Image is 15 cm behind the mirror. After moving: object is 10 cm from mirror, image is 10 cm behind. Distance between object and image = 20 cm.
**Explanation**: Object and image are equidistant from the mirror. A common error is giving the image distance from the mirror instead of the object-image separation.
**Tags**: plane-mirror, image-position, calculation

### Question Q-4.4-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Draw a ray diagram to show how an eye sees the image of a point object in a plane mirror.
**Answer**: Draw two rays from the object to the mirror, reflect them according to i = r, and extend the reflected rays backwards behind the mirror. They meet at the virtual image position.
**Explanation**: At least two rays are needed, with arrowheads showing direction. A common error is drawing rays from the image to the mirror.
**Tags**: ray-diagram, plane-mirror, drawing

### Question Q-4.4-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does a periscope work?
**Answer**: A periscope uses two plane mirrors placed at 45° angles, parallel to each other. Light from the object reflects off the top mirror, then the bottom mirror, and into the viewer's eye, allowing viewing over obstacles.
**Explanation**: Two reflections at 45° redirect the light. A common error is saying it uses lenses.
**Tags**: periscope, plane-mirror, application

### Question Q-4.4-05
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the difference between a real image and a virtual image?
**Answer**: A real image is formed where light rays actually converge and can be projected onto a screen. A virtual image is formed where rays appear to diverge from and cannot be projected onto a screen.
**Explanation**: The screen test is the key difference. A common error is saying virtual images are "not real" — they are real perceptions but not projectable.
**Tags**: real-image, virtual-image, definition

### Question Q-4.4-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A person stands 2 m in front of a tall plane mirror. Can they see their full body if the mirror is only half their height? Explain.
**Answer**: Yes. To see the full body, the mirror only needs to be half the person's height. Light from the top of the head reflects off the top of the mirror, and light from the feet reflects off the bottom. The required mirror height is always half the object height regardless of distance.
**Explanation**: This is a standard result from ray diagrams. A common error is thinking the mirror must be as tall as the person.
**Tags**: plane-mirror, ray-diagram, minimum-height

### Question Q-4.4-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A clock shows 3:00 when viewed in a plane mirror. What time does the actual clock show?
**Answer**: 9:00
**Explanation**: A plane mirror laterally inverts the image (left-right reversal). When a clock showing 3:00 is reflected, the positions of 3 and 9 swap. The mirror image of 3:00 looks like 9:00. A common error is not recognizing that clock faces are reversed in mirrors.
**Tags**: plane-mirror, lateral-inversion, clock, application

### Question Q-4.4-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Two plane mirrors are placed at right angles (90°) to each other. An object is placed between them. How many images are formed and explain why.
**Answer**: Three images are formed. Each mirror forms one image directly (2 images). Additionally, light reflects from one mirror to the other, forming a third image. The number of images = (360°/angle between mirrors) - 1 = (360°/90°) - 1 = 3.
**Explanation**: Multiple reflections create additional images. A common error is saying only 2 images (one per mirror).
**Tags**: plane-mirror, multiple-reflections, perpendicular, extended-response

### Question Q-4.4-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Describe how you would use a pin, a plane mirror, and a ruler to find the position of the image of the pin. Include a ray diagram in your answer.
**Answer**: Place the pin in front of the mirror. Look at its image from different positions. Place a second pin (search pin) behind the mirror so it appears to coincide with the image (no parallax — they move together when you change viewing position). Measure the distances: object distance = image distance. Draw rays from the object to the mirror and extend reflected rays backward to locate the image.
**Explanation**: The no-parallax method confirms the image position. A common error is not mentioning the parallax check or drawing rays incorrectly.
**Tags**: plane-mirror, experiment, no-parallax, ray-diagram, practical
