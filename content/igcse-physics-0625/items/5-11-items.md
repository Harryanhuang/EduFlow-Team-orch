# Items: 5.11 — Electromagnets, relay and loudspeaker

### Question Q-5.11-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is an electromagnet?
**Answer**: An electromagnet is a magnet produced by an electric current, usually using a coil of wire around a soft iron core.
**Explanation**: It can be switched on and off by controlling the current.
**Tags**: electromagnet, definition, current

### Question Q-5.11-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: State two ways to make an electromagnet stronger.
**Answer**: Increase the current, add more turns to the coil, or use a soft iron core.
**Explanation**: Each change increases the magnetic field produced by the coil.
**Tags**: electromagnet, strength, factors

### Question Q-5.11-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is soft iron used as the core of an electromagnet?
**Answer**: Soft iron is easily magnetised when current flows and easily demagnetised when current is switched off.
**Explanation**: This makes the electromagnet controllable.
**Tags**: soft-iron, electromagnet, core

### Question Q-5.11-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how a relay uses an electromagnet to switch on a separate circuit.
**Answer**: A small current in the coil magnetises the core and attracts an iron armature. This closes contacts in a separate circuit, allowing a larger current to flow there.
**Explanation**: A relay allows a low-current control circuit to operate a high-current circuit.
**Tags**: relay, electromagnet, switch

### Question Q-5.11-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Give one advantage of using a relay in a control circuit.
**Answer**: A small or low-voltage current can safely switch a larger current or a separate circuit. It can also isolate the control circuit from the output circuit.
**Explanation**: Relays provide electrical isolation and control.
**Tags**: relay, control-circuit, safety

### Question Q-5.11-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the energy changes in a loudspeaker.
**Answer**: Electrical energy in the varying current is transferred to kinetic energy of the cone and then to sound energy in the air.
**Explanation**: The cone vibrations make pressure variations in the air.
**Tags**: loudspeaker, energy-transfer, sound

### Question Q-5.11-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain how a loudspeaker produces sound from an alternating current signal.
**Answer**: The a.c. in the voice coil changes direction and size. In the magnetic field of a permanent magnet, the coil experiences a changing force, so it moves back and forth. The attached cone vibrates and produces sound waves.
**Explanation**: The frequency of vibration follows the frequency of the electrical signal.
**Tags**: loudspeaker, alternating-current, motor-effect

### Question Q-5.11-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A relay coil is connected to a temperature sensor circuit. Explain how it could switch on a fan when temperature rises.
**Answer**: As temperature rises, the sensor circuit increases current through the relay coil. When the current is large enough, the electromagnet attracts the armature and closes the fan circuit.
**Explanation**: The relay links a sensor/control circuit to a separate output circuit.
**Tags**: relay, thermistor, fan-control

### Question Q-5.11-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare a permanent magnet and an electromagnet for use in a scrap-yard crane.
**Answer**: An electromagnet is better because it can be switched on to lift steel and switched off to release it. A permanent magnet would be difficult to release and its strength is less easily controlled.
**Explanation**: Control of magnetism is the key advantage.
**Tags**: electromagnet, permanent-magnet, crane, comparison
