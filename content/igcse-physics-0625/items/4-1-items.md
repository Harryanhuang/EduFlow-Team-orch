# Items: 4.1 — Wave properties

### Question Q-4.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A wave has a frequency of 50 Hz and a wavelength of 4 m. Calculate its speed.
**Answer**: 200 m/s
**Explanation**: v = fλ = 50 × 4 = 200 m/s. A common error is dividing frequency by wavelength.
**Tags**: wave-speed, frequency, wavelength, v=fλ

### Question Q-4.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A wave on a string has a period of 0.02 s. Calculate its frequency.
**Answer**: 50 Hz
**Explanation**: f = 1/T = 1/0.02 = 50 Hz. A common error is multiplying instead of taking the reciprocal.
**Tags**: frequency, period, T=1/f

### Question Q-4.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: From a wave diagram, the distance between two adjacent crests is 0.8 m and 4 waves pass a point in 2 seconds. Calculate the wave speed.
**Answer**: 1.6 m/s
**Explanation**: Wavelength = 0.8 m. Frequency = 4/2 = 2 Hz. v = fλ = 2 × 0.8 = 1.6 m/s. A common error is using 4 waves as the frequency directly without dividing by time.
**Tags**: wave-speed, wave-diagram, frequency

### Question Q-4.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the amplitude of a wave?
**Answer**: The maximum displacement of a particle from its rest (equilibrium) position, measured from the rest position to a crest or trough.
**Explanation**: Amplitude is half the crest-to-trough distance. A common error is saying it is the distance from crest to trough.
**Tags**: amplitude, definition, wave-properties

### Question Q-4.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A sound wave travels at 340 m/s in air. A musical note has a frequency of 512 Hz. Calculate the wavelength.
**Answer**: 0.66 m (or 0.664 m)
**Explanation**: λ = v/f = 340/512 = 0.664 m. A common error is multiplying instead of dividing.
**Tags**: wavelength, sound, v=fλ, calculation

### Question Q-4.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A wave transfers energy from point A to point B. Does matter also move from A to B? Explain.
**Answer**: No. A wave transfers energy only, not matter. The particles of the medium oscillate about their equilibrium positions but do not travel with the wave.
**Explanation**: This is a fundamental property of waves. A common error is saying water waves carry water forward.
**Tags**: wave, energy-transfer, conceptual

### Question Q-4.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A radio station broadcasts at a frequency of 100 MHz. If the wavelength of these radio waves is 3 m, calculate the speed of the electromagnetic waves. Give your answer in standard form.
**Answer**: 3 × 10^8 m/s
**Explanation**: Convert 100 MHz to 100 × 10^6 Hz. Then v = fλ = (100 × 10^6) × 3 = 3 × 10^8 m/s. A common error is forgetting that 1 MHz = 10^6 Hz.
**Tags**: wave-speed, radio-waves, standard-form, EM-spectrum

### Question Q-4.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A wave generator produces 15 waves in 3 seconds. If the wavelength is 0.4 m, calculate: (a) the frequency, (b) the speed of the wave.
**Answer**: (a) 5 Hz; (b) 2 m/s
**Explanation**: Frequency = number of waves/time = 15/3 = 5 Hz. Then v = fλ = 5 × 0.4 = 2 m/s. A common error is using 15 Hz directly without dividing by time.
**Tags**: frequency, wave-speed, multi-step, calculation

### Question Q-4.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A displacement-distance graph of a wave shows that the distance between consecutive peaks is 1.2 m and the maximum displacement is 0.3 m. If the wave speed is 6 m/s, calculate the frequency of the wave.
**Answer**: 5 Hz
**Explanation**: From the graph, wavelength λ = 1.2 m and amplitude = 0.3 m. Using v = fλ, rearrange to f = v/λ = 6/1.2 = 5 Hz. A common error is confusing amplitude with wavelength.
**Tags**: wave-graph, frequency, interpretation, multi-step
