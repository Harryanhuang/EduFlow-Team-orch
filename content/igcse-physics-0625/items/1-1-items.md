# Items: 1.1 — Physical quantities, SI units, measuring length and time

### Question Q-1.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A student uses a metre rule to measure the length of a notebook and records the result as 245 mm. Write this length in metres.
**Answer**: 0.245 m
**Explanation**: Convert millimetres to metres by dividing by 1000. So 245 mm = 245 / 1000 m = 0.245 m. A common error is dividing by 100 or moving the decimal point the wrong number of places.
**Tags**: si-units, unit-conversion, length-measurement

### Question Q-1.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Convert 3.2 km into metres and then into centimetres.
**Answer**: 3200 m, 320 000 cm
**Explanation**: 3.2 km = 3.2 × 1000 = 3200 m. Then 3200 m = 3200 × 100 = 320 000 cm. A common error is multiplying by 10 instead of 100 for m to cm conversion.
**Tags**: si-units, unit-conversion, distance

### Question Q-1.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A vernier caliper has a zero error of +0.03 cm. The main scale reading is 2.4 cm and the vernier scale reading is 0.06 cm. What is the corrected measurement?
**Answer**: 2.43 cm
**Explanation**: Total reading = 2.4 + 0.06 = 2.46 cm. Corrected reading = 2.46 - 0.03 = 2.43 cm (subtract positive zero error). A common error is adding the zero error instead of subtracting it.
**Tags**: vernier-calipers, zero-error, length-measurement

### Question Q-1.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A micrometer screw gauge shows a reading of 5.78 mm. The instrument has a negative zero error of -0.02 mm. What is the true measurement?
**Answer**: 5.80 mm
**Explanation**: Corrected reading = measured reading - zero error = 5.78 - (-0.02) = 5.78 + 0.02 = 5.80 mm. For negative zero error, you add the correction. A common error is subtracting 0.02 instead of adding.
**Tags**: micrometer, zero-error, length-measurement

### Question Q-1.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A student measures the time for 20 oscillations of a pendulum as 36.5 s. Calculate the period of one oscillation.
**Answer**: 1.825 s
**Explanation**: Period = total time / number of oscillations = 36.5 / 20 = 1.825 s. Measuring multiple oscillations and averaging reduces random error. A common error is multiplying instead of dividing.
**Tags**: time-measurement, period, pendulum, averaging

### Question Q-1.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A student records five measurements of a wire's diameter using a micrometer: 0.52 mm, 0.54 mm, 0.51 mm, 0.53 mm, 0.55 mm. Calculate the average diameter and estimate the uncertainty.
**Answer**: 0.53 mm, uncertainty ± 0.02 mm
**Explanation**: Average = (0.52 + 0.54 + 0.51 + 0.53 + 0.55) / 5 = 2.65 / 5 = 0.53 mm. Uncertainty = (max - min) / 2 = (0.55 - 0.51) / 2 = ± 0.02 mm. A common error is using only the range without dividing by 2.
**Tags**: averaging, uncertainty, repeated-measurement, micrometer

### Question Q-1.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Write the following in order from smallest to largest: 500 μm, 0.3 mm, 2 × 10⁻⁴ m, 0.05 cm.
**Answer**: 2 × 10⁻⁴ m, 0.3 mm, 500 μm and 0.05 cm (equal)
**Explanation**: Convert all to metres: 500 μm = 5 × 10⁻⁴ m, 0.05 cm = 5 × 10⁻⁴ m, 0.3 mm = 3 × 10⁻⁴ m, 2 × 10⁻⁴ m. Ordering: 2 × 10⁻⁴ m (0.0002 m) < 3 × 10⁻⁴ m (0.3 mm) < 5 × 10⁻⁴ m (500 μm = 0.05 cm). Note: 500 μm and 0.05 cm are equal.
**Tags**: si-prefixes, unit-conversion, ordering

### Question Q-1.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain why a micrometer screw gauge is more suitable than a metre rule for measuring the diameter of a thin wire.
**Answer**: A micrometer can measure to 0.01 mm precision while a metre rule only measures to 1 mm. For a thin wire (typically less than 1 mm), the metre rule lacks sufficient precision and the wire diameter is too small to read accurately on a metre rule scale.
**Explanation**: The choice of measuring instrument depends on the size of the quantity and the required precision. A common error is saying the metre rule "cannot measure" small objects — it can, but not with sufficient precision.
**Tags**: instrument-selection, precision, micrometer

### Question Q-1.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A student measures the diameter of a metal sphere five times and gets: 12.4 mm, 12.6 mm, 12.5 mm, 12.3 mm, 12.7 mm. The micrometer has a zero error of +0.1 mm. Calculate the corrected average diameter and the uncertainty of the measurement.
**Answer**: 12.4 mm, uncertainty ± 0.2 mm
**Explanation**: Average of raw readings = (12.4 + 12.6 + 12.5 + 12.3 + 12.7) / 5 = 62.5 / 5 = 12.5 mm. Corrected average = 12.5 - 0.1 = 12.4 mm (subtract positive zero error). Uncertainty = (12.7 - 12.3) / 2 = ± 0.2 mm. A common error is applying the zero error correction to each individual reading before averaging — the result is the same, but it is more efficient to correct the average.
**Tags**: zero-error, averaging, uncertainty, micrometer, multi-step
