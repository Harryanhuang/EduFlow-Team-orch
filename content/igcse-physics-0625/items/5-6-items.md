# Items: 5.6 — Capacitors: charge storage, time-delay, smoothing

### Question Q-5.6-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What does a capacitor store?
**Answer**: A capacitor stores electric charge and therefore electrical potential energy on its plates.
**Explanation**: It stores charge, not current.
**Tags**: capacitor, charge, definition

### Question Q-5.6-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A capacitor stores 0.02 C of charge when connected to a 12 V supply. Calculate its capacitance.
**Answer**: 0.00167 F, or 1670 microF.
**Explanation**: C = Q/V = 0.02/12 = 0.00167 F. Convert to microfarads by multiplying by 10^6 if needed.
**Tags**: capacitance, C=Q/V, calculation

### Question Q-5.6-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the function of a capacitor in a smoothing circuit?
**Answer**: It charges during peaks of rectified a.c. and discharges during gaps, reducing voltage ripple and producing a steadier d.c. output.
**Explanation**: The rectifier converts a.c.; the capacitor smooths.
**Tags**: capacitor, smoothing, rectification

### Question Q-5.6-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how a capacitor can be used in a time-delay circuit.
**Answer**: The capacitor charges through a resistor. The time to reach a switching threshold depends on R and C, so it can trigger a component after a delay.
**Explanation**: Larger R or C gives a longer delay.
**Tags**: capacitor, time-delay, RC-circuit

### Question Q-5.6-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Sketch and describe how p.d. across a capacitor changes as it charges through a resistor from a d.c. supply.
**Answer**: The p.d. starts at 0 V, rises quickly at first, then rises more slowly and approaches the supply voltage.
**Explanation**: Charging is curved, not linear.
**Tags**: capacitor, charging-curve, voltage

### Question Q-5.6-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A 470 microF capacitor is connected to a 6 V supply. Calculate the charge stored when fully charged.
**Answer**: 0.00282 C, or 2.82 mC.
**Explanation**: Q = CV = 470 × 10^-6 × 6 = 0.00282 C. Convert microfarads to farads before calculating.
**Tags**: capacitance, charge, unit-conversion

### Question Q-5.6-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A 1000 microF capacitor is charged to 9 V and then discharged through a lamp. Explain how lamp brightness changes with time.
**Answer**: The lamp is brightest at first because capacitor p.d. and current are largest. As the capacitor discharges, p.d. and current fall, so the lamp gradually dims and goes out.
**Explanation**: Discharge is gradual rather than instant.
**Tags**: capacitor, discharge, lamp, explanation

### Question Q-5.6-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A student wants a longer time delay in an RC timing circuit. Suggest two circuit changes and explain why they work.
**Answer**: Increase the resistance or increase the capacitance. A larger R reduces charging current; a larger C needs more charge for the same p.d. Both increase RC.
**Explanation**: Delay depends on the time constant RC.
**Tags**: RC-circuit, time-constant, design

### Question Q-5.6-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare the output of a rectifier circuit with and without a smoothing capacitor.
**Answer**: Without the capacitor, the output is pulsating d.c. with large ripple. With the capacitor, it charges near peaks and discharges between them, making the output smoother with smaller ripple.
**Explanation**: Smoothing reduces ripple but does not make a perfect constant voltage.
**Tags**: smoothing, rectifier, capacitor, comparison
