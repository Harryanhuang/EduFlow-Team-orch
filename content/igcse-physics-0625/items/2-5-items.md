# Items: 2.5 — Energy resources and electricity generation

### Question Q-2.5-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Classify the following as renewable or non-renewable: coal, solar, wind, natural gas, tidal, nuclear.
**Answer**: Non-renewable: coal, natural gas, nuclear. Renewable: solar, wind, tidal.
**Explanation**: Nuclear is non-renewable because uranium is a finite resource. A common error is classifying nuclear as renewable because it doesn't produce CO₂.
**Tags**: energy-classification, renewable, non-renewable

### Question Q-2.5-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Describe the energy transfers in a coal-fired power station.
**Answer**: Chemical energy (coal) → thermal energy (burning) → kinetic energy (steam turning turbine) → electrical energy (generator).
**Explanation**: Each step in the chain must be identified. A common error is missing the intermediate steps or confusing thermal and chemical energy.
**Tags**: power-station, energy-chain, coal

### Question Q-2.5-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Compare the advantages and disadvantages of wind power and nuclear power for electricity generation.
**Answer**: Wind: advantages — renewable, no pollution, low running cost. Disadvantages — intermittent, requires specific locations, visual impact. Nuclear: advantages — high output, reliable, low CO₂. Disadvantages — radioactive waste, risk of accidents, high decommissioning cost.
**Explanation**: Both need balanced pros and cons. A common error is only listing one side.
**Tags**: energy-comparison, wind, nuclear

### Question Q-2.5-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is electricity transmitted at high voltage across the National Grid?
**Answer**: High voltage means low current for the same power (P = IV). Lower current reduces energy lost as heat in the cables (P_loss = I²R), making transmission more efficient.
**Explanation**: The key is I²R losses. A common error is saying "high voltage is faster" or "more powerful."
**Tags**: electricity-transmission, high-voltage, efficiency

### Question Q-2.5-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the main energy source for almost all energy on Earth? Explain.
**Answer**: The Sun. Solar energy drives photosynthesis (food/fossil fuels), the water cycle (hydroelectric), wind patterns, and directly provides heat and light.
**Explanation**: Fossil fuels are stored solar energy. A common error is saying "coal" or "nuclear."
**Tags**: sun, energy-source, conceptual

### Question Q-2.5-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A solar panel receives 5000 J of solar energy per second and converts 750 J into useful electrical energy. Calculate the efficiency of the solar panel.
**Answer**: 15%
**Explanation**: Efficiency = useful output / total input × 100% = 750/5000 × 100% = 15%. A common error is giving 0.15 without converting to a percentage.
**Tags**: efficiency, solar, calculation

### Question Q-2.5-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain how a nuclear fission chain reaction works in a nuclear reactor, and describe the role of control rods in maintaining a steady rate of fission.
**Answer**: When a uranium nucleus absorbs a neutron, it splits into two smaller nuclei, releasing energy and 2-3 neutrons. These neutrons can trigger further fissions, creating a chain reaction. Control rods absorb excess neutrons to ensure only one neutron from each fission causes another fission, maintaining a steady rate.
**Explanation**: The chain reaction must be controlled to prevent runaway reactions. A common error is not explaining both the neutron production and the absorption mechanism of control rods.
**Tags**: nuclear-fission, chain-reaction, control-rods

### Question Q-2.5-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A pumped storage hydroelectric system stores water in an upper reservoir 200 m above the turbines. If 500 000 kg of water flows through the turbines in 1 hour, calculate the maximum electrical energy that could be generated. (g = 10 N/kg)
**Answer**: 1.0 × 10⁹ J (or 1000 MJ)
**Explanation**: Gravitational potential energy = mgh = 500 000 × 10 × 200 = 1.0 × 10⁹ J. This assumes 100% conversion efficiency. A common error is forgetting to convert hours to seconds when calculating power separately.
**Tags**: hydroelectric, gravitational-potential, calculation

### Question Q-2.5-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A country currently generates 60% of its electricity from coal, 25% from natural gas, 10% from nuclear, and 5% from renewables. The government plans to reduce carbon emissions by closing all coal plants and replacing them with a mix of nuclear and offshore wind. Evaluate this plan, considering reliability, cost, and environmental impact.
**Answer**: Advantages: Closing coal plants significantly reduces CO₂ emissions. Nuclear provides reliable baseload power. Wind is renewable with no fuel costs. Disadvantages: Nuclear has high construction and decommissioning costs, plus waste disposal issues. Wind is intermittent and requires backup capacity or energy storage. Loss of coal may reduce energy security if nuclear/wind cannot fully compensate.
**Explanation**: A balanced evaluation must consider multiple factors. A common error is only discussing one energy source or ignoring the intermittency problem of wind power.
**Tags**: energy-mix, policy-analysis, evaluation
