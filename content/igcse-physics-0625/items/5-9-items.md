# Items: 5.9 — Electromagnetic induction, generators and transformers

### Question Q-5.9-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: State what is meant by electromagnetic induction.
**Answer**: Electromagnetic induction is the production of an emf, and possibly a current, when a conductor cuts magnetic field lines or when the magnetic field through a coil changes.
**Explanation**: A changing magnetic field is required.
**Tags**: electromagnetic-induction, emf, definition

### Question Q-5.9-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Give two ways to increase the induced emf in a coil.
**Answer**: Move the magnet or coil faster; use a stronger magnet; use more turns on the coil; insert an iron core.
**Explanation**: Each method increases the rate of change of magnetic flux linkage.
**Tags**: induced-emf, coil, factors

### Question Q-5.9-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A magnet is pushed into a coil connected to a sensitive galvanometer. Describe what is observed and explain why.
**Answer**: The galvanometer deflects while the magnet is moving, showing an induced current. It returns to zero when the magnet stops because the magnetic field through the coil is no longer changing.
**Explanation**: Induction happens during change, not just because a magnet is nearby.
**Tags**: coil, magnet, galvanometer, induction

### Question Q-5.9-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why the induced current reverses direction when a magnet is pulled out of a coil instead of pushed in.
**Answer**: Pulling the magnet out changes the magnetic field through the coil in the opposite way, so the induced emf and current reverse direction.
**Explanation**: The direction depends on whether flux linkage is increasing or decreasing.
**Tags**: induced-current, direction, Lenz-law

### Question Q-5.9-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how a simple a.c. generator works.
**Answer**: A coil rotates in a magnetic field and cuts field lines. The changing flux through the coil induces an alternating emf. Slip rings and brushes connect the rotating coil to the external circuit.
**Explanation**: The output alternates because each side of the coil swaps direction through the field every half-turn.
**Tags**: generator, alternating-current, induction

### Question Q-5.9-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A transformer has 200 turns on the primary coil and 1000 turns on the secondary coil. The primary voltage is 12 V. Calculate the secondary voltage.
**Answer**: Vs/Vp = Ns/Np = 1000/200 = 5, so Vs = 12 × 5 = 60 V.
**Explanation**: This is a step-up transformer.
**Tags**: transformer, turns-ratio, voltage

### Question Q-5.9-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain why a transformer works with a.c. but not with steady d.c.
**Answer**: A transformer requires a changing magnetic field in the core to induce an emf in the secondary coil. A.c. continually changes direction and size, but steady d.c. produces a constant magnetic field after switching on, so no continuous emf is induced.
**Explanation**: Changing flux is essential for transformer action.
**Tags**: transformer, ac, dc, induction

### Question Q-5.9-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A transformer is used to transmit electrical power over long distances at high voltage. Explain why this reduces energy loss in cables.
**Answer**: For a given power, increasing voltage reduces current because P = IV. Cable heating loss is I^2R, so a smaller current greatly reduces energy lost as thermal energy.
**Explanation**: High voltage transmission is used to reduce current, not because high voltage itself saves energy.
**Tags**: power-transmission, transformer, energy-loss

### Question Q-5.9-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A transformer is 90 percent efficient. It supplies 45 W to the secondary circuit from a 12 V primary supply. Calculate the input power and primary current.
**Answer**: Efficiency = output/input, so input power = 45/0.90 = 50 W. Primary current = P/V = 50/12 = 4.17 A.
**Explanation**: Use efficiency before calculating input current.
**Tags**: transformer, efficiency, power, current
