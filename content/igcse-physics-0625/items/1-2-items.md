# Items: 1.2 — Speed, velocity, acceleration, graphs

### Question Q-1.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A car increases its speed from 10 m/s to 25 m/s in 5.0 s. Calculate its acceleration.
**Answer**: 3.0 m/s²
**Explanation**: Use acceleration = change in velocity / time. The change in velocity is 25 - 10 = 15 m/s. Then 15 / 5.0 = 3.0 m/s². Students often give the speed change without dividing by time, or omit the squared unit.
**Tags**: acceleration, kinematics, formula-application

### Question Q-1.2-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A cyclist travels at a constant speed of 8 m/s for 3 minutes. Calculate the distance travelled.
**Answer**: 1440 m
**Explanation**: Distance = speed × time = 8 × (3 × 60) = 8 × 180 = 1440 m. A common error is forgetting to convert minutes to seconds, giving 8 × 3 = 24 m.
**Tags**: speed, distance, unit-conversion, constant-speed

### Question Q-1.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A speed-time graph shows a straight line from (0, 0) to (10 s, 20 m/s). Calculate the acceleration and the total distance travelled.
**Answer**: Acceleration = 2.0 m/s², Distance = 100 m
**Explanation**: Acceleration = gradient = 20 / 10 = 2.0 m/s². Distance = area under graph = ½ × 10 × 20 = 100 m. A common error is using distance = speed × time with the final speed (20 × 10 = 200 m) instead of the area.
**Tags**: speed-time-graph, acceleration, area-under-graph

### Question Q-1.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: On a distance-time graph, what does a horizontal line represent? What does a steeper line represent compared to a shallower line?
**Answer**: A horizontal line means the object is stationary (not moving). A steeper line represents a greater speed compared to a shallower line.
**Explanation**: The gradient of a distance-time graph equals speed. Zero gradient = zero speed. Steeper gradient = higher speed. A common error is saying a horizontal line means "constant speed" without recognising that zero is a specific case.
**Tags**: distance-time-graph, speed, gradient, stationary

### Question Q-1.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A ball is thrown vertically upwards with an initial speed of 15 m/s. How long does it take to reach its highest point? (g = 10 m/s²)
**Answer**: 1.5 s
**Explanation**: At the highest point, the speed is 0. Using v = u - gt: 0 = 15 - 10t, so t = 15/10 = 1.5 s. A common error is using v = u + gt and getting a negative time, or forgetting that gravity acts downwards (opposing the motion).
**Tags**: free-fall, gravity, vertical-motion, kinematics

### Question Q-1.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the motion of a skydiver from jumping until reaching terminal velocity, in terms of forces.
**Answer**: Initially, weight is much greater than air resistance, so the skydiver accelerates downwards. As speed increases, air resistance increases. When air resistance equals weight, the resultant force is zero and the skydiver falls at constant terminal velocity.
**Explanation**: The key is understanding the changing balance of forces. A common error is saying "air resistance becomes greater than weight" at terminal velocity (they are equal, not greater).
**Tags**: terminal-velocity, forces, air-resistance, free-fall

### Question Q-1.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Distinguish between speed and velocity.
**Answer**: Speed is a scalar quantity (magnitude only) measured in m/s. Velocity is a vector quantity (magnitude and direction) also measured in m/s. Two objects can have the same speed but different velocities if moving in different directions.
**Explanation**: The key distinction is that velocity includes direction. A common error is defining them as the same thing or saying velocity is "speed with units."
**Tags**: speed, velocity, scalar, vector

### Question Q-1.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A car travels 120 km in the first 2 hours, then 180 km in the next 3 hours. Calculate the average speed for the whole journey.
**Answer**: 60 km/h
**Explanation**: Total distance = 120 + 180 = 300 km. Total time = 2 + 3 = 5 hours. Average speed = 300 / 5 = 60 km/h. A common error is averaging the two speeds (60 + 60) / 2, which happens to give the same answer here but is not the correct method in general.
**Tags**: average-speed, distance, time

### Question Q-1.2-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A car accelerates uniformly from rest at 2 m/s² for 10 seconds, then travels at constant speed for 20 seconds, then decelerates uniformly to rest in 5 seconds. Calculate: (a) the maximum speed reached, (b) the total distance travelled, (c) the average speed for the whole journey.
**Answer**: (a) 20 m/s. (b) 550 m. (c) 15.7 m/s.
**Explanation**: Phase 1: v = 2 × 10 = 20 m/s, s = ½ × 2 × 100 = 100 m. Phase 2: s = 20 × 20 = 400 m. Phase 3: s = ½ × 20 × 5 = 50 m. Total distance = 550 m, total time = 35 s. Average speed = 550/35 = 15.7 m/s. A common error is using s = vt for the acceleration phase instead of the correct formula.
**Tags**: multi-stage-motion, speed-time-graph, average-speed, challenge
