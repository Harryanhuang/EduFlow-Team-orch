# Items: 2.1 — Force, resultant force, F=ma

### Question Q-2.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A force of 12 N acts on an object of mass 3 kg. Calculate the acceleration.
**Answer**: 4 m/s²
**Explanation**: F = ma, so a = F/m = 12/3 = 4 m/s². A common error is multiplying instead of dividing (12 × 3 = 36).
**Tags**: F=ma, force, acceleration

### Question Q-2.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the resultant force on a car of mass 1000 kg accelerating at 2.5 m/s²?
**Answer**: 2500 N
**Explanation**: F = ma = 1000 × 2.5 = 2500 N. A common error is giving the answer in kg·m/s instead of N.
**Tags**: F=ma, force, calculation

### Question Q-2.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A box is pushed with a force of 50 N to the right and experiences a friction force of 15 N to the left. What is the resultant force?
**Answer**: 35 N to the right
**Explanation**: Resultant = 50 - 15 = 35 N in the direction of the larger force. A common error is adding the forces (65 N) instead of subtracting opposing forces.
**Tags**: resultant-force, opposing-forces

### Question Q-2.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A 5 kg object is acted on by two forces: 30 N to the right and 10 N to the left. Calculate the acceleration and state its direction.
**Answer**: 4 m/s² to the right
**Explanation**: Resultant force = 30 - 10 = 20 N to the right. a = F/m = 20/5 = 4 m/s² to the right. A common error is not stating the direction of acceleration.
**Tags**: resultant-force, F=ma, direction

### Question Q-2.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why an object moving at constant velocity has zero resultant force acting on it.
**Answer**: According to Newton's first law, if velocity is constant (no acceleration), the resultant force must be zero. All forces are balanced.
**Explanation**: Constant velocity means no net force. A common error is saying there is "no force" — there may be forces, but they balance.
**Tags**: Newton-first-law, resultant-force, constant-velocity

### Question Q-2.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A trolley of mass 2 kg is pushed with a force of 10 N. If the friction is 2 N, how long will it take to reach a speed of 6 m/s from rest?
**Answer**: 1.5 s
**Explanation**: Resultant force = 10 - 2 = 8 N. a = 8/2 = 4 m/s². v = u + at: 6 = 0 + 4t, t = 6/4 = 1.5 s.
**Explanation**: Common error: forgetting to subtract friction before calculating acceleration.
**Tags**: F=ma, friction, kinematics, multi-step

### Question Q-2.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: What is the unit of force? Define it in terms of base units.
**Answer**: The newton (N). 1 N = 1 kg·m/s².
**Explanation**: From F = ma, the unit of force is kg × m/s² = N. A common error is saying 1 N = 1 kg.
**Tags**: units, newton, definition

### Question Q-2.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A car of mass 1200 kg accelerates from rest to 30 m/s in 10 seconds. Calculate: (a) the acceleration, (b) the resultant force, (c) the distance travelled during this time, (d) if the driving force is 5000 N, what is the frictional force?
**Answer**: (a) 3 m/s². (b) 3600 N. (c) 150 m. (d) 1400 N.
**Explanation**: (a) a = 30/10 = 3 m/s². (b) F = 1200 × 3 = 3600 N. (c) s = ½(0+30)×10 = 150 m. (d) 5000 - f = 3600, f = 1400 N. A common error is equating driving force with resultant force.
**Tags**: F=ma, kinematics, friction, multi-step, challenge

### Question Q-2.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Two forces act on an object: 8 N north and 6 N east. Calculate the magnitude and direction of the resultant force. If the object has mass 2 kg, what is its acceleration?
**Answer**: Magnitude = 10 N. Direction = 53.1° north of east. Acceleration = 5 m/s².
**Explanation**: Magnitude = √(8² + 6²) = 10 N. Direction = tan⁻¹(8/6) = 53.1°. Acceleration = 10/2 = 5 m/s². A common error is adding the forces directly (8+6=14 N) instead of using vector addition.
**Tags**: resultant-force, vectors, Pythagoras, F=ma, challenge
