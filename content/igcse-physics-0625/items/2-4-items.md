# Items: 2.4 — Power, efficiency

### Question Q-2.4-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A motor does 600 J of work in 12 seconds. Calculate its power output.
**Answer**: 50 W
**Explanation**: P = W/t = 600/12 = 50 W. A common error is multiplying instead of dividing.
**Tags**: power, work, time, calculation

### Question Q-2.4-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A crane lifts a 500 N load through a height of 10 m in 20 s. Calculate the power of the crane.
**Answer**: 250 W
**Explanation**: Work = Fs = 500 × 10 = 5000 J. Power = 5000/20 = 250 W. A common error is using the force directly without calculating work first.
**Tags**: power, work, crane, calculation

### Question Q-2.4-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A machine has a power input of 1000 W and a useful power output of 750 W. Calculate its efficiency as a percentage.
**Answer**: 75%
**Explanation**: Efficiency = useful output / total input × 100% = 750/1000 × 100% = 75%. A common error is giving 0.75 without the percentage sign.
**Tags**: efficiency, power, percentage

### Question Q-2.4-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A car engine is 30% efficient. If the fuel provides 50 000 J of energy per second, calculate the useful power output.
**Answer**: 15 000 W (or 15 kW)
**Explanation**: Useful output = 30% of 50 000 = 0.30 × 50 000 = 15 000 J/s = 15 000 W. A common error is calculating the wasted energy instead of the useful output.
**Tags**: efficiency, power, percentage, calculation

### Question Q-2.4-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: An electric heater has an efficiency of 100%. Explain why this is possible for a heater but not for a motor.
**Answer**: A heater's purpose is to produce thermal energy, so all electrical energy input becomes useful heat output. A motor must produce kinetic energy, and some energy is always lost as heat and sound.
**Explanation**: Efficiency depends on what the "useful" output is. A common error is saying "no device can be 100% efficient" — heaters can be.
**Tags**: efficiency, heater, conceptual

### Question Q-2.4-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A motor with power 200 W lifts a 400 N load at constant speed. Calculate the speed of lifting.
**Answer**: 0.5 m/s
**Explanation**: P = Fv, so v = P/F = 200/400 = 0.5 m/s. A common error is trying to use P = W/t without knowing the time.
**Tags**: power, P=Fv, lifting-speed, calculation

### Question Q-2.4-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A pump lifts 200 kg of water through a height of 15 m in 2 minutes. The motor has a power input of 500 W. Calculate: (a) the useful work done, (b) the useful power output, (c) the efficiency of the pump. (g = 10 N/kg)
**Answer**: (a) 30 000 J. (b) 250 W. (c) 50%.
**Explanation**: (a) Work = mgh = 200 × 10 × 15 = 30 000 J. (b) Power = 30 000/120 = 250 W. (c) Efficiency = 250/500 × 100% = 50%. A common error is using 2 minutes directly (30 000/2), giving an efficiency over 100%.
**Tags**: power, work, efficiency, pump, multi-step, challenge

### Question Q-2.4-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A car travels at a constant speed of 25 m/s. The engine provides a driving force of 800 N. If the engine is 40% efficient, calculate: (a) the useful power output, (b) the total power input from fuel, (c) the rate of energy waste.
**Answer**: (a) 20 000 W. (b) 50 000 W. (c) 30 000 W.
**Explanation**: (a) P = Fv = 800 × 25 = 20 000 W. (b) Input = 20 000/0.40 = 50 000 W. (c) Waste = 50 000 - 20 000 = 30 000 W. A common error is confusing input and output power in the efficiency formula.
**Tags**: power, P=Fv, efficiency, energy-waste, challenge

### Question Q-2.4-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: An escalator lifts 60 people per minute, each of average mass 70 kg, through a vertical height of 5 m. The motor is 65% efficient. Calculate: (a) the useful power output of the motor, (b) the power input required. (g = 10 N/kg)
**Answer**: (a) 3500 W. (b) 5385 W.
**Explanation**: Total mass/min = 60 × 70 = 4200 kg. Work/min = 4200 × 10 × 5 = 210 000 J. Useful power = 210 000/60 = 3500 W. Input = 3500/0.65 = 5385 W. A common error is using 60 people as the total mass.
**Tags**: power, work, efficiency, escalator, multi-step, challenge
