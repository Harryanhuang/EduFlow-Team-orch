# Items: 3.4 — Heat transfer: conduction, convection, radiation

### Question Q-3.4-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Explain why metals are good conductors of heat.
**Answer**: Metals have free electrons that can move through the structure. When heated, these electrons gain kinetic energy and rapidly transfer it through the metal, in addition to the vibration transfer between neighbouring atoms.
**Explanation**: Free electrons are the key. A common error is only mentioning particle vibrations (which applies to all solids, not just metals).
**Tags**: conduction, metals, free-electrons

### Question Q-3.4-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Describe how a convection current forms in a room heated by a radiator.
**Answer**: Air near the radiator heats up, expands, becomes less dense, and rises. Cooler, denser air sinks to take its place. This creates a circulating current of warm air rising and cool air sinking.
**Explanation**: The cycle of heating, rising, cooling, and sinking creates the current. A common error is saying "hot air rises" without explaining why (density).
**Tags**: convection, convection-current, heating

### Question Q-3.4-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why are solar heating panels often painted black?
**Answer**: Black/dark surfaces are good absorbers of infrared radiation. Painting the panels black maximises the absorption of solar radiation, heating the water inside more efficiently.
**Explanation**: Good absorbers are also good emitters. A common error is saying black "attracts heat."
**Tags**: radiation, absorption, solar-heating

### Question Q-3.4-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain how a vacuum flask (Thermos) keeps a hot drink hot. Refer to all three methods of heat transfer.
**Answer**: The silvered walls reflect infrared radiation, reducing radiative heat loss. The vacuum between the double walls prevents conduction and convection (no particles to transfer heat). The stopper prevents convection currents escaping from the top.
**Explanation**: All three methods must be addressed. A common error is saying the vacuum prevents radiation — it doesn't (the silvered surface does).
**Tags**: vacuum-flask, conduction, convection, radiation, explanation

### Question Q-3.4-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why does a carpet feel warmer than a tile floor even when both are at the same temperature?
**Answer**: Carpet is a poor conductor (insulator), so it does not conduct heat away from your feet quickly. Tile is a better conductor and removes heat from your feet faster, feeling colder.
**Explanation**: It's about the rate of heat transfer, not actual temperature. A common error is saying the carpet "is warmer."
**Tags**: conduction, insulator, sensation, application

### Question Q-3.4-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In which state of matter does convection occur? Explain why it cannot occur in solids.
**Answer**: Convection occurs in liquids and gases (fluids). It cannot occur in solids because the particles are fixed in position and cannot flow to create convection currents.
**Explanation**: Convection requires bulk movement of particles. A common error is saying convection can occur in solids "slowly."
**Tags**: convection, states-of-matter, explanation

### Question Q-3.4-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A student claims that shiny surfaces are good insulators because they reflect heat. Evaluate this statement.
**Answer**: The statement is partly correct but uses the wrong term. Shiny surfaces are good reflectors and poor absorbers/emitters of infrared radiation, reducing radiative heat transfer. However, an insulator is a material that reduces conduction; a shiny metal surface may still conduct heat well.
**Explanation**: Reflection of radiation is not the same as insulation against conduction. A common error is using "reflector" and "insulator" as if they mean the same thing.
**Tags**: radiation, reflection, insulation, terminology

### Question Q-3.4-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Two identical cans, one painted black and one painted white, each contain equal volumes of water at 80°C. They are left in a room at 20°C. Which can cools faster? Explain the main mechanism and name two factors that affect the rate of cooling.
**Answer**: The black can cools faster because black surfaces are better emitters of infrared radiation. The main mechanism is radiation. Factors affecting cooling rate include surface area, temperature difference, air movement, and insulation.
**Explanation**: Good emitters lose heat faster. A common error is saying the black can "absorbs more cold"; cooling is due to energy emission.
**Tags**: radiation, emission, cooling-rate, factors

### Question Q-3.4-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain why a cavity wall, made of two layers of brick with an air gap between, is a better insulator than a solid brick wall of the same total thickness.
**Answer**: The air gap is a poor conductor, so conduction is reduced. The narrow gap also limits convection currents. If reflective foil is included, radiation is reduced too. A solid brick wall conducts heat more directly through the material.
**Explanation**: Cavity walls reduce more than one heat transfer route. A common error is saying air is a perfect insulator; it is simply a poor conductor compared with brick.
**Tags**: insulation, cavity-wall, conduction, convection, radiation
