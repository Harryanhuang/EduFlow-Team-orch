A mixture contains 10.0 g of sodium chloride, $\mathrm{NaCl}$. What is the mass of chlorine present in this amount of $\mathrm{NaCl}$?

(Atomic masses: Na = 22.99 g/mol, Cl = 35.45 g/mol)

## Options
A. 6.07 g
B. 10.0 g
C. 17.3 g
D. 35.5 g

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Step 1: Calculate molar mass of $\mathrm{NaCl}$:

$$M_{\mathrm{NaCl}} = 22.99 + 35.45 = 58.44 \text{ g/mol}$$

Step 2: Calculate mass fraction of chlorine:

$$\text{Mass fraction Cl} = \frac{35.45}{58.44} = 0.607$$

Step 3: Calculate mass of Cl in 10.0 g sample:

$$\text{Mass of Cl} = 10.0 \text{ g} \times 0.607 = 6.07 \text{ g}$$

Option B incorrectly assumes all mass is chlorine.
Option C incorrectly uses only the fraction of sodium.
Option D uses the atomic mass of Cl directly without considering the compound.
