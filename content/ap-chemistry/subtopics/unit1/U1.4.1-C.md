A 50.0 g mixture contains $\mathrm{Na_2SO_4}$, $\mathrm{K_2SO_4}$, and $\mathrm{(NH_4)_2SO_4}$. Analysis shows the mixture contains 11.24 g of sulfur. If the mass of $\mathrm{Na_2SO_4}$ equals the mass of $\mathrm{K_2SO_4}$, what is the mass of $\mathrm{(NH_4)_2SO_4}$ in the mixture?

(Atomic masses: N = 14.01 g/mol, H = 1.01 g/mol, Na = 22.99 g/mol, K = 39.10 g/mol, S = 32.07 g/mol, O = 16.00 g/mol)

## Options
A. 13.2 g
B. 18.5 g
C. 26.4 g
D. 31.5 g

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Let $x$ = mass of $\mathrm{Na_2SO_4}$ = mass of $\mathrm{K_2SO_4}$, and $z$ = mass of $\mathrm{(NH_4)_2SO_4}$.

Total mass: $x + x + z = 50.0$, so $2x + z = 50.0$ ... (1)

Calculate molar masses and sulfur mass fractions:
- $M_{\mathrm{Na_2SO_4}} = 2(22.99) + 32.07 + 4(16.00) = 142.05$ g/mol; S fraction = $\frac{32.07}{142.05} = 0.226$
- $M_{\mathrm{K_2SO_4}} = 2(39.10) + 32.07 + 4(16.00) = 174.27$ g/mol; S fraction = $\frac{32.07}{174.27} = 0.184$
- $M_{\mathrm{(NH_4)_2SO_4}} = 2(14.01) + 8(1.01) + 32.07 + 4(16.00) = 132.17$ g/mol; S fraction = $\frac{32.07}{132.17} = 0.243$

Sulfur mass: $0.226x + 0.184x + 0.243z = 11.24$ ... (2)

Simplify: $0.410x + 0.243z = 11.24$ ... (2)

From (1): $z = 50.0 - 2x$

Substitute into (2):
$$0.410x + 0.243(50.0 - 2x) = 11.24$$
$$0.410x + 12.15 - 0.486x = 11.24$$
$$-0.076x = -0.91$$
$$x = 11.8 \text{ g}$$

Therefore: $z = 50.0 - 2(11.8) = 26.4$ g

Check: $0.410(11.8) + 0.243(26.4) = 4.84 + 6.42 = 11.26 \approx 11.24$ g S. Confirmed.

Options A, B, and D result from calculation errors or incorrect stoichiometry.
