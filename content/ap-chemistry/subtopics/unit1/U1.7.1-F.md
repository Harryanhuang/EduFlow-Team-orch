Which of the following atoms has the largest atomic radius?

## Options
A. $\mathrm{F}$
B. $\mathrm{Cl}$
C. $\mathrm{Na}$
D. $\mathrm{K}$

## Answer
D

## Explanation
Two periodic trends govern atomic radius:
1. Atomic radius increases down a group (additional electron shells increase size).
2. Atomic radius decreases across a period (increasing effective nuclear charge pulls electrons inward).

Comparing the options:
- F (period 2) and Cl (period 3) are in Group 17.
- Na (period 3) and K (period 4) are in Group 1.

Group 1 elements are on the left of the periodic table and have the largest radii in their periods. Between Na (period 3) and K (period 4), potassium has an additional electron shell, making it larger.

K has the largest atomic radius: it is farthest down and farthest left among the options.

Option A (F) is small (high up, far right).
Option B (Cl) is larger than F but still right side.
Option C (Na) is large but one period above K in the same group.
