What is the ground-state electron configuration of a neutral silicon atom (Z = 14)?

## Options
A. $1s^2 2s^2 2p^6 3s^2 3p^2$
B. $1s^2 2s^2 2p^6 3s^2 3p^4$
C. $1s^2 2s^2 2p^8 3s^2$
D. $1s^2 2s^2 2p^6 3s^1 3p^3$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Silicon has 14 electrons. Fill orbitals following the Aufbau principle:

- $1s^2$: 2 electrons (total: 2)
- $2s^2$: 2 electrons (total: 4)
- $2p^6$: 6 electrons (total: 10)
- $3s^2$: 2 electrons (total: 12)
- $3p^2$: 2 electrons (total: 14)

Configuration: $1s^2 2s^2 2p^6 3s^2 3p^2$

Option B has 16 electrons (matches sulfur, not silicon).
Option C violates the p orbital maximum capacity (6, not 8).
Option D distributes 3s and 3p electrons incorrectly (violates Aufbau).
