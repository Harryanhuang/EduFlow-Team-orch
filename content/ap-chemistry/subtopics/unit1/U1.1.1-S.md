A sample of calcium carbonate, $\mathrm{CaCO_3}$, has a mass of 25.0 g. How many moles of $\mathrm{CaCO_3}$ are present in this sample?

(Atomic masses: Ca = 40.08 g/mol, C = 12.01 g/mol, O = 16.00 g/mol)

## Options
A. 0.250 mol
B. 0.400 mol
C. 2.50 mol
D. 4.00 mol

## Answer
A

## Explanation
First, calculate the molar mass of $\mathrm{CaCO_3}$:

$$M_{\mathrm{CaCO_3}} = 40.08 + 12.01 + 3(16.00) = 100.09 \text{ g/mol}$$

Now apply $n = \frac{m}{M}$:

$$n = \frac{25.0 \text{ g}}{100.09 \text{ g/mol}} = 0.250 \text{ mol}$$

Option B (0.400 mol) would result from incorrectly calculating molar mass as 62.5 g/mol.
Option C (2.50 mol) results from using 10.0 g/mol as molar mass.
Option D (4.00 mol) results from dividing by atomic mass of Ca only.
