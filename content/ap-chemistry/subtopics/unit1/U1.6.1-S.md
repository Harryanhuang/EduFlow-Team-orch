A photoelectron spectrum shows three peaks. Reading from highest to lowest binding energy, the relative peak heights are in the ratio 2 : 2 : 1. Which neutral element does this spectrum most likely represent?

## Options
A. Lithium (Z = 3)
B. Boron (Z = 5)
C. Carbon (Z = 6)
D. Nitrogen (Z = 7)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Three peaks indicate three occupied subshells. The heights give the relative number of electrons in each subshell, reading from highest binding energy (closest to nucleus) to lowest.

Peaks from highest to lowest binding energy correspond to: $1s$, $2s$, $2p$.

Height ratio 2 : 2 : 1 means:
- $1s$: 2 electrons
- $2s$: 2 electrons
- $2p$: 1 electron

Total electrons = $2 + 2 + 1 = 5$

This corresponds to boron, with configuration $1s^2 2s^2 2p^1$.

Option A (Li, $1s^2 2s^1$) would show only two peaks with ratio 2 : 1.
Option C (C, $1s^2 2s^2 2p^2$) would show ratio 2 : 2 : 2.
Option D (N, $1s^2 2s^2 2p^3$) would show ratio 2 : 2 : 3.
