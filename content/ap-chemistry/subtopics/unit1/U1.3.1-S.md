A compound is found to contain 40.0% carbon, 6.7% hydrogen, and 53.3% oxygen by mass. What is the empirical formula of this compound?

(Atomic masses: C = 12.01 g/mol, H = 1.01 g/mol, O = 16.00 g/mol)

## Options
A. $\mathrm{CHO}$
B. $\mathrm{CH_2O}$
C. $\mathrm{C_2H_4O_2}$
D. $\mathrm{C_3H_6O_3}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Assume 100 g of compound, so masses are: 40.0 g C, 6.7 g H, 53.3 g O.

Convert to moles:
$$n_C = \frac{40.0 \text{ g}}{12.01 \text{ g/mol}} = 3.33 \text{ mol}$$

$$n_H = \frac{6.7 \text{ g}}{1.01 \text{ g/mol}} = 6.63 \text{ mol}$$

$$n_O = \frac{53.3 \text{ g}}{16.00 \text{ g/mol}} = 3.33 \text{ mol}$$

Divide by smallest value (3.33 mol):
$$\text{C: } \frac{3.33}{3.33} = 1, \quad \text{H: } \frac{6.63}{3.33} = 2, \quad \text{O: } \frac{3.33}{3.33} = 1$$

Empirical formula: $\mathrm{CH_2O}$

Option A has incorrect H ratio.
Options C and D are molecular formulas (multiples of the empirical formula).
