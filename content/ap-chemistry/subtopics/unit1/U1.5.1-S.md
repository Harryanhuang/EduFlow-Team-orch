What is the electron configuration of the $\mathrm{Fe^{3+}}$ ion?

## Options
A. $[\mathrm{Ar}] 3d^5$
B. $[\mathrm{Ar}] 4s^2 3d^3$
C. $[\mathrm{Ar}] 3d^3 4s^2$
D. $[\mathrm{Ar}] 4s^1 3d^4$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Iron (Fe, Z = 26) has ground-state configuration: $[\mathrm{Ar}] 4s^2 3d^6$

For $\mathrm{Fe^{3+}}$, remove 3 electrons. Electrons are removed from highest $n$ orbitals first. For transition metals, $4s$ electrons are removed before $3d$ electrons:

Remove 2 electrons from $4s$: $[\mathrm{Ar}] 3d^6$
Remove 1 electron from $3d$: $[\mathrm{Ar}] 3d^5$

Configuration of $\mathrm{Fe^{3+}}$: $[\mathrm{Ar}] 3d^5$

Option B incorrectly keeps $4s$ electrons (would have 29 electrons total).
Option C has the same electron count as B with wrong notation order.
Option D has only 2 electrons removed, giving $\mathrm{Fe^{2+}}$ configuration.
