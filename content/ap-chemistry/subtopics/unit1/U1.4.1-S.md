A 20.0 g mixture contains only $\mathrm{NaCl}$ and $\mathrm{KCl}$. The total mass of chlorine in the mixture is 11.0 g. What is the mass of $\mathrm{NaCl}$ in the mixture?

(Atomic masses: Na = 22.99 g/mol, K = 39.10 g/mol, Cl = 35.45 g/mol)

## Options
A. 5.85 g
B. 8.60 g
C. 11.4 g
D. 14.2 g

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Let $x$ = mass of $\mathrm{NaCl}$ and $y$ = mass of $\mathrm{KCl}$.

Equation 1 (total mass): $x + y = 20.0$ g

For Equation 2, calculate mass fractions of Cl in each compound:
- In $\mathrm{NaCl}$: $\frac{35.45}{58.44} = 0.607$
- In $\mathrm{KCl}$: $\frac{35.45}{74.55} = 0.476$

Equation 2 (Cl mass): $0.607x + 0.476y = 11.0$

From Equation 1: $y = 20.0 - x$

Substitute into Equation 2:
$$0.607x + 0.476(20.0 - x) = 11.0$$
$$0.607x + 9.52 - 0.476x = 11.0$$
$$0.131x = 1.48$$
$$x = 11.4 \text{ g}$$

Check: $\mathrm{KCl} = 20.0 - 11.4 = 8.6$ g; Cl from each = $0.607(11.4) + 0.476(8.6) = 6.92 + 4.09 = 11.0$ g. Confirmed.

Options A, B, and D result from algebraic errors or incorrect mass fraction calculations.
