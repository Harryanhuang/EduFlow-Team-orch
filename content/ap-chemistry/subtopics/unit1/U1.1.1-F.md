How many moles of water molecules are present in a sample containing $3.011 \times 10^{23}$ molecules of $\mathrm{H_2O}$?

## Options
A. 0.500 mol
B. 1.00 mol
C. 2.00 mol
D. $1.81 \times 10^{47}$ mol

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Using the relationship $n = \frac{N}{N_A}$:

$$n = \frac{3.011 \times 10^{23} \text{ molecules}}{6.022 \times 10^{23} \text{ molecules/mol}}$$

$$n = 0.500 \text{ mol}$$

The given number of molecules is exactly half of Avogadro's number, so the answer is 0.500 mol.

Option B would require $6.022 \times 10^{23}$ molecules.
Option C would require $1.204 \times 10^{24}$ molecules.
Option D incorrectly multiplies instead of dividing by Avogadro's number.
