How many oxygen atoms are present in a 4.40 g sample of carbon dioxide, $\mathrm{CO_2}$?

(Atomic masses: C = 12.01 g/mol, O = 16.00 g/mol)

## Options
A. $6.02 \times 10^{22}$ atoms
B. $1.20 \times 10^{23}$ atoms
C. $2.65 \times 10^{23}$ atoms
D. $1.20 \times 10^{24}$ atoms

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Step 1: Calculate molar mass of $\mathrm{CO_2}$:

$$M_{\mathrm{CO_2}} = 12.01 + 2(16.00) = 44.01 \text{ g/mol}$$

Step 2: Calculate moles of $\mathrm{CO_2}$:

$$n_{\mathrm{CO_2}} = \frac{4.40 \text{ g}}{44.01 \text{ g/mol}} = 0.100 \text{ mol}$$

Step 3: Calculate number of $\mathrm{CO_2}$ molecules:

$$N_{\text{molecules}} = 0.100 \text{ mol} \times 6.022 \times 10^{23} \text{ molecules/mol} = 6.02 \times 10^{22} \text{ molecules}$$

Step 4: Since each $\mathrm{CO_2}$ molecule contains 2 oxygen atoms:

$$N_{\text{O atoms}} = 6.02 \times 10^{22} \text{ molecules} \times \frac{2 \text{ O atoms}}{1 \text{ molecule}} = 1.20 \times 10^{23} \text{ O atoms}$$

Option A gives the number of molecules, not atoms.
Option C would result from incorrect molar mass calculation.
Option D incorrectly multiplies by 20 instead of 2 oxygen atoms per molecule.
