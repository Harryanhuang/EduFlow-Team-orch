A hydrocarbon contains 85.7% carbon and 14.3% hydrogen by mass. The molar mass of the compound is determined to be 84.0 g/mol. What is the molecular formula?

(Atomic masses: C = 12.01 g/mol, H = 1.01 g/mol)

## Options
A. $\mathrm{CH_2}$
B. $\mathrm{C_2H_4}$
C. $\mathrm{C_4H_8}$
D. $\mathrm{C_6H_{12}}$

## Answer
D

## Explanation
Step 1: Find empirical formula. Assume 100 g sample:

$$n_C = \frac{85.7 \text{ g}}{12.01 \text{ g/mol}} = 7.14 \text{ mol}$$

$$n_H = \frac{14.3 \text{ g}}{1.01 \text{ g/mol}} = 14.2 \text{ mol}$$

Divide by smallest (7.14 mol):
$$\text{C: } \frac{7.14}{7.14} = 1, \quad \text{H: } \frac{14.2}{7.14} = 2$$

Empirical formula: $\mathrm{CH_2}$

Step 2: Calculate empirical formula mass:
$$M_{\text{empirical}} = 12.01 + 2(1.01) = 14.03 \text{ g/mol}$$

Step 3: Find multiplier:
$$n = \frac{M_{\text{molecular}}}{M_{\text{empirical}}} = \frac{84.0}{14.03} = 6.0$$

Step 4: Molecular formula = $(\mathrm{CH_2})_6 = \mathrm{C_6H_{12}}$

Option A is the empirical formula only.
Option B ($n = 2$) would have molar mass 28 g/mol.
Option C ($n = 4$) would have molar mass 56 g/mol.
