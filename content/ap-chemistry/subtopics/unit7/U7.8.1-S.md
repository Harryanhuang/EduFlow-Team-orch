The solubility product of $\mathrm{BaSO_4}$ is $K_{sp} = 1.1 \times 10^{-10}$ at 25 °C. What is the molar solubility of $\mathrm{BaSO_4}$ in pure water?

$$\mathrm{BaSO_4(s) \rightleftharpoons Ba^{2+}(aq) + SO_4^{2-}(aq)}$$

## Options
A. $1.1 \times 10^{-10}$ M
B. $1.1 \times 10^{-5}$ M
C. $1.0 \times 10^{-5}$ M
D. $5.5 \times 10^{-11}$ M

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Let $s$ = molar solubility. Dissolving $s$ mol/L of $\mathrm{BaSO_4}$ gives one $\mathrm{Ba^{2+}}$ and one $\mathrm{SO_4^{2-}}$ per formula unit:

$$[\mathrm{Ba^{2+}}] = s, \qquad [\mathrm{SO_4^{2-}}] = s$$

Write the solubility product:

$$K_{sp} = [\mathrm{Ba^{2+}}][\mathrm{SO_4^{2-}}] = s^2$$

Solve for $s$:

$$s = \sqrt{K_{sp}} = \sqrt{1.1 \times 10^{-10}} = 1.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ M}$$

Option A is the value of $K_{sp}$ itself, not the solubility.
Option B has an incorrect square root.
Option D incorrectly divides $K_{sp}$ by 2.
