The reaction $\mathrm{PCl_5(g) \rightleftharpoons PCl_3(g) + Cl_2(g)}$ is at equilibrium with $[\mathrm{PCl_5}] = 0.20\ \mathrm{M}$, $[\mathrm{PCl_3}] = 0.40\ \mathrm{M}$, and $[\mathrm{Cl_2}] = 0.20\ \mathrm{M}$, giving $K_c = 0.40$. Suddenly, enough $\mathrm{Cl_2}$ is added to raise its concentration to $0.80\ \mathrm{M}$ (other concentrations momentarily unchanged). Which statement correctly describes the system immediately after the addition?

## Options
A. $Q = 1.6$; the reaction shifts left (toward $\mathrm{PCl_5}$).
B. $Q = 0.40$; the system remains at equilibrium.
C. $Q = 1.6$; the reaction shifts right (toward products).
D. $Q = 0.10$; the reaction shifts right (toward products).

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Immediately after adding $\mathrm{Cl_2}$, compute the reaction quotient with the new concentrations:

$$Q = \frac{[\mathrm{PCl_3}][\mathrm{Cl_2}]}{[\mathrm{PCl_5}]} = \frac{(0.40)(0.80)}{0.20} = \frac{0.32}{0.20} = 1.6$$

Compare to $K_c$:

$$Q = 1.6 > K_c = 0.40$$

Since $Q > K$, there is too much product, so the reaction shifts **left** (reverse), consuming $\mathrm{PCl_3}$ and $\mathrm{Cl_2}$ to re-form $\mathrm{PCl_5}$ until $Q = K$ again. This matches the Le Chatelier prediction: adding a product drives the equilibrium toward reactants.

Option B uses the pre-addition $Q$.
Option C has the correct $Q$ but the wrong direction.
Option D miscalculates $Q$.
