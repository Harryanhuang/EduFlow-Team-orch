For the reaction $\mathrm{A(g) \rightleftharpoons B(g)}$, a chemist records the following concentrations:

| Time (s) | [A] (M) | [B] (M) |
|----------|---------|---------|
| 0        | 1.00    | 0.00    |
| 20       | 0.60    | 0.40    |
| 40       | 0.45    | 0.55    |
| 60       | 0.40    | 0.60    |
| 80       | 0.40    | 0.60    |
| 100      | 0.40    | 0.60    |

At approximately what time does the reaction first reach equilibrium?

## Options
A. 0 s
B. 20 s
C. 40 s
D. 60 s

## Answer
D

## Explanation
A system is at equilibrium when the concentrations of all species stop changing with time. From the data, both [A] and [B] continue to change up through 40 s, but from 60 s onward the values remain constant at [A] = 0.40 M and [B] = 0.60 M.

$$[A] = 0.40 \text{ M}, \quad [B] = 0.60 \text{ M} \quad (\text{constant from } 60 \text{ s})$$

Therefore equilibrium is first established at about 60 s.

Option B (20 s) and Option C (40 s) are points where concentrations are still changing.
Note that the curves never have to be equal — equilibrium is defined by *constant* concentrations, not equal ones.
