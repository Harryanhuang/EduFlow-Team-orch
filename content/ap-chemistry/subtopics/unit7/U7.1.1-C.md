An elementary reversible reaction $\mathrm{A(g) \rightleftharpoons B(g)}$ has a forward rate constant $k_f = 8.0 \times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{s^{-1}}$ and a reverse rate constant $k_r = 2.0 \times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{s^{-1}}$. At equilibrium, what is the ratio $\dfrac{[B]}{[A]}$?

## Options
A. 0.25
B. 1.0
C. 4.0
D. 16

## Answer
C

## Explanation
For an elementary reaction, equilibrium is the dynamic condition where the forward and reverse rates are equal:

$$k_f[A] = k_r[B]$$

Rearranging gives the ratio of product to reactant, which is the equilibrium constant:

$$K = \frac{[B]}{[A]} = \frac{k_f}{k_r} = \frac{8.0 \times 10^{-2}}{2.0 \times 10^{-2}} = 4.0$$

Because $k_f > k_r$, the forward reaction is faster at equal concentrations, so the system accumulates more product than reactant; $[B] = 4.0\,[A]$.

Option A is the inverted ratio $k_r/k_f$.
Option B would require equal rate constants.
Option D incorrectly squares the ratio.
