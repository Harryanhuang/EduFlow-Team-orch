For the decomposition $\mathrm{COCl_2(g) \rightleftharpoons CO(g) + Cl_2(g)}$, $K_c = 2.5 \times 10^{-4}$. A flask is filled with $[\mathrm{COCl_2}]_0 = 0.10\ \mathrm{M}$ and no products. What is the equilibrium concentration of CO?

## Options
A. $2.5 \times 10^{-5}$ M
B. $5.0 \times 10^{-3}$ M
C. $2.5 \times 10^{-4}$ M
D. $1.6 \times 10^{-2}$ M

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Set up the ICE table (let $x = [\mathrm{CO}]$ formed):

| | $\mathrm{COCl_2}$ | $\mathrm{CO}$ | $\mathrm{Cl_2}$ |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | 0.10 | 0 | 0 |
| C | $-x$ | $+x$ | $+x$ |
| E | $0.10-x$ | $x$ | $x$ |

Write the equilibrium expression:

$$K_c = \frac{[\mathrm{CO}][\mathrm{Cl_2}]}{[\mathrm{COCl_2}]} = \frac{x \cdot x}{0.10 - x} = 2.5 \times 10^{-4}$$

Because $K_c$ is small, assume $x \ll 0.10$, so $0.10 - x \approx 0.10$:

$$\frac{x^2}{0.10} = 2.5 \times 10^{-4}$$

$$x^2 = 2.5 \times 10^{-5}$$

$$x = \sqrt{2.5 \times 10^{-5}} = 5.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ M}$$

Check: $x/0.10 = 5\% $, so the approximation is acceptable. Thus $[\mathrm{CO}] = 5.0 \times 10^{-3}$ M.

Option A is $x^2$, not $x$.
Option C is simply the value of $K_c$.
Option D results from omitting the square root.
