Consider the following equilibrium:

$$\mathrm{CaCO_3(s) \rightleftharpoons CaO(s) + CO_2(g)}$$

Which expression correctly represents the equilibrium constant $K_c$ for this reaction?

## Options
A. $K_c = \dfrac{[\mathrm{CaO}][\mathrm{CO_2}]}{[\mathrm{CaCO_3}]}$
B. $K_c = [\mathrm{CO_2}]$
C. $K_c = \dfrac{[\mathrm{CO_2}]}{[\mathrm{CaCO_3}]}$
D. $K_c = \dfrac{1}{[\mathrm{CO_2}]}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The equilibrium constant expression includes only gaseous and aqueous species. Pure solids and pure liquids have effectively constant activity and are **excluded** from the expression.

In this reaction, $\mathrm{CaCO_3}$ and $\mathrm{CaO}$ are both pure solids, so they are omitted. The only species that appears is the gas $\mathrm{CO_2}$:

$$K_c = [\mathrm{CO_2}]$$

Option A and Option C incorrectly include the solids.
Option D inverts the expression, which would correspond to the reverse reaction.
