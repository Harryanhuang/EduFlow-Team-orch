The solubility product of $\mathrm{PbI_2}$ is $K_{sp} = 1.4 \times 10^{-8}$ at 25 °C. What is the molar solubility, $s$, of $\mathrm{PbI_2}$ in pure water?

$$\mathrm{PbI_2(s) \rightleftharpoons Pb^{2+}(aq) + 2\,I^-(aq)}$$

## Options
A. $1.2 \times 10^{-4}$ M
B. $1.5 \times 10^{-3}$ M
C. $2.4 \times 10^{-3}$ M
D. $3.0 \times 10^{-3}$ M

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Let $s$ = molar solubility. From the dissociation stoichiometry, dissolving $s$ mol/L of $\mathrm{PbI_2}$ produces:

$$[\mathrm{Pb^{2+}}] = s, \qquad [\mathrm{I^-}] = 2s$$

Write the solubility product expression, applying the exponent of 2 to the iodide term:

$$K_{sp} = [\mathrm{Pb^{2+}}][\mathrm{I^-}]^2 = (s)(2s)^2 = 4s^3$$

Solve for $s$:

$$4s^3 = 1.4 \times 10^{-8}$$

$$s^3 = 3.5 \times 10^{-9}$$

$$s = \sqrt[3]{3.5 \times 10^{-9}} = 1.5 \times 10^{-3} \text{ M}$$

Option A omits the coefficient/exponent handling.
Option C reports $[\mathrm{I^-}] = 2s$ instead of $s$.
Option D comes from solving $s^3 = K_{sp}$ without the factor of 4.
