Solid silver chromate dissolves slightly in water according to:

$$\mathrm{Ag_2CrO_4(s) \rightleftharpoons 2\,Ag^+(aq) + CrO_4^{2-}(aq)}$$

Which expression correctly represents the solubility product, $K_{sp}$?

## Options
A. $K_{sp} = [\mathrm{Ag^+}][\mathrm{CrO_4^{2-}}]$
B. $K_{sp} = [\mathrm{Ag^+}]^2[\mathrm{CrO_4^{2-}}]$
C. $K_{sp} = \dfrac{[\mathrm{Ag^+}]^2[\mathrm{CrO_4^{2-}}]}{[\mathrm{Ag_2CrO_4}]}$
D. $K_{sp} = 2[\mathrm{Ag^+}][\mathrm{CrO_4^{2-}}]$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The solubility product is written like any equilibrium constant: include only the dissolved (aqueous) ions, each raised to the power of its stoichiometric coefficient. The pure solid is excluded.

From the balanced equation, $\mathrm{Ag^+}$ has a coefficient of 2 and $\mathrm{CrO_4^{2-}}$ has a coefficient of 1:

$$K_{sp} = [\mathrm{Ag^+}]^2[\mathrm{CrO_4^{2-}}]$$

Option A omits the exponent of 2 on $[\mathrm{Ag^+}]$.
Option C incorrectly includes the solid in the denominator.
Option D wrongly places the coefficient as a multiplier rather than an exponent.
