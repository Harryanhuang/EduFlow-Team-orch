For the reaction below, the equilibrium concentrations at a certain temperature are $[\mathrm{N_2}] = 0.50\ \mathrm{M}$, $[\mathrm{H_2}] = 0.20\ \mathrm{M}$, and $[\mathrm{NH_3}] = 0.40\ \mathrm{M}$.

$$\mathrm{N_2(g) + 3\,H_2(g) \rightleftharpoons 2\,NH_3(g)}$$

What is the value of $K_c$?

## Options
A. 4.0
B. 8.0
C. 40
D. 1.6

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Write the equilibrium expression, applying each species' coefficient as an exponent:

$$K_c = \frac{[\mathrm{NH_3}]^2}{[\mathrm{N_2}][\mathrm{H_2}]^3}$$

Substitute the equilibrium concentrations:

$$K_c = \frac{(0.40)^2}{(0.50)(0.20)^3} = \frac{0.16}{(0.50)(0.0080)}$$

$$K_c = \frac{0.16}{0.0040} = 40$$

Option A and Option D result from omitting the exponent on $[\mathrm{H_2}]$.
Option B comes from using $(0.20)^2$ instead of $(0.20)^3$ in the denominator.
