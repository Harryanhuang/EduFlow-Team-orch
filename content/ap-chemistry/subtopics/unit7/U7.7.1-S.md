The following exothermic reaction is at equilibrium:

$$\mathrm{2\,SO_2(g) + O_2(g) \rightleftharpoons 2\,SO_3(g)} \qquad \Delta H < 0$$

Which change will shift the equilibrium to the **right** (toward more $\mathrm{SO_3}$)?

## Options
A. Increasing the temperature.
B. Decreasing the volume of the container.
C. Adding an inert gas at constant volume.
D. Adding a catalyst.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Evaluate each stress with Le Chatelier's principle.

**Decreasing the volume** increases the total pressure. The system shifts toward the side with **fewer moles of gas**. The left side has $2 + 1 = 3$ mol of gas; the right side has $2$ mol. Shifting right reduces the moles of gas and relieves the pressure stress. This is the correct answer.

Option A: Since the reaction is exothermic, heat is a product. Raising the temperature adds "heat," shifting the equilibrium **left**, not right.

Option C: Adding an inert gas at constant volume does not change the partial pressures or concentrations of the reacting species, so there is no shift.

Option D: A catalyst speeds up the forward and reverse reactions equally and does not change the equilibrium position.
