For the reaction $\mathrm{H_2(g) + I_2(g) \rightleftharpoons 2\,HI(g)}$, the equilibrium constant is $K_c = 50.0$ at the reaction temperature. A flask is filled with $[\mathrm{H_2}] = 0.100\ \mathrm{M}$, $[\mathrm{I_2}] = 0.100\ \mathrm{M}$, and $[\mathrm{HI}] = 2.00\ \mathrm{M}$. In which direction will the reaction proceed to reach equilibrium?

## Options
A. To the right (toward products), because $Q < K$.
B. To the left (toward reactants), because $Q > K$.
C. To the right (toward products), because $Q > K$.
D. The system is already at equilibrium, because $Q = K$.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
First calculate the reaction quotient $Q$ using the **current** concentrations and the same form as $K$:

$$Q = \frac{[\mathrm{HI}]^2}{[\mathrm{H_2}][\mathrm{I_2}]} = \frac{(2.00)^2}{(0.100)(0.100)} = \frac{4.00}{0.0100} = 400$$

Now compare $Q$ to $K$:

$$Q = 400 > K = 50.0$$

When $Q > K$, there is too much product relative to equilibrium, so the reaction proceeds in the **reverse** direction (to the left), consuming HI and producing $\mathrm{H_2}$ and $\mathrm{I_2}$ until $Q = K$.

Option A and Option C misidentify the magnitude relationship or the resulting direction.
Option D is incorrect because $Q \neq K$.
