Consider the reversible reaction $\mathrm{N_2O_4(g) \rightleftharpoons 2\,NO_2(g)}$ in a closed container that has reached equilibrium. Which statement correctly describes the system at equilibrium?

## Options
A. The forward and reverse reactions have both stopped.
B. The concentrations of $\mathrm{N_2O_4}$ and $\mathrm{NO_2}$ are equal.
C. The rate of the forward reaction equals the rate of the reverse reaction.
D. All of the $\mathrm{N_2O_4}$ has been converted to $\mathrm{NO_2}$.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Chemical equilibrium is a **dynamic** state. The forward and reverse reactions continue to occur, but they proceed at equal rates, so the macroscopic concentrations of all species remain constant over time.

$$\text{rate}_{\text{forward}} = \text{rate}_{\text{reverse}}$$

Option A is incorrect because the reactions do not stop — molecules continually interconvert at the molecular level.
Option B is incorrect because equilibrium does not require equal concentrations; the concentrations simply stop changing, and their ratio is fixed by $K$.
Option D is incorrect because equilibrium is reached when both species are still present, not when conversion is complete.
