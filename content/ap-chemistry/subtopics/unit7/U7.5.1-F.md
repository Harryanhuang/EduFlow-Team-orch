A flask initially contains $0.50\ \mathrm{M}$ of $\mathrm{N_2O_4}$ and no $\mathrm{NO_2}$. The reaction is:

$$\mathrm{N_2O_4(g) \rightleftharpoons 2\,NO_2(g)}$$

If $x$ mol/L of $\mathrm{N_2O_4}$ reacts, which row correctly gives the equilibrium concentrations of $\mathrm{N_2O_4}$ and $\mathrm{NO_2}$?

## Options
A. $[\mathrm{N_2O_4}] = 0.50 - x$, $\ [\mathrm{NO_2}] = x$
B. $[\mathrm{N_2O_4}] = 0.50 - x$, $\ [\mathrm{NO_2}] = 2x$
C. $[\mathrm{N_2O_4}] = 0.50 - 2x$, $\ [\mathrm{NO_2}] = x$
D. $[\mathrm{N_2O_4}] = 0.50 + x$, $\ [\mathrm{NO_2}] = 2x$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
In an ICE table, the Change row must follow the stoichiometric coefficients of the balanced equation:

$$\mathrm{N_2O_4(g) \rightleftharpoons 2\,NO_2(g)}$$

- $\mathrm{N_2O_4}$ has a coefficient of 1, so if $x$ reacts, its change is $-x$.
- $\mathrm{NO_2}$ has a coefficient of 2, so its change is $+2x$.

Building the Equilibrium row (Initial + Change):

$$[\mathrm{N_2O_4}] = 0.50 - x, \qquad [\mathrm{NO_2}] = 0 + 2x = 2x$$

Option A omits the factor of 2 on $\mathrm{NO_2}$.
Option C misassigns the coefficient to the reactant.
Option D incorrectly increases the reactant concentration.
