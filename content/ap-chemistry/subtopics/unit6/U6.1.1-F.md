When propane burns in a grill, the reaction releases heat and the surrounding air becomes warmer. Which of the following correctly describes this combustion reaction?

## Options
A) Exothermic, with $\Delta H < 0$
B) Exothermic, with $\Delta H > 0$
C) Endothermic, with $\Delta H < 0$
D) Endothermic, with $\Delta H > 0$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Because the reaction releases heat to the surroundings (the air warms up), it is exothermic. By the sign convention for the system, energy leaving the system makes $\Delta H$ negative, so $\Delta H < 0$. Combustion reactions are always exothermic.
