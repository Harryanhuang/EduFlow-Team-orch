How much heat is required to convert $18.0 \text{ g}$ ($1.00 \text{ mol}$) of liquid water at $90.0 \text{ °C}$ into steam at $100.0 \text{ °C}$? Use $c_\text{water} = 4.18 \text{ J/(g·°C)}$ and $\Delta H_\text{vap} = 40.7 \text{ kJ/mol}$.

## Options
A) $40.7 \text{ kJ}$
B) $41.5 \text{ kJ}$
C) $44.7 \text{ kJ}$
D) $75.2 \text{ kJ}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
This requires two segments. First, warm the liquid from $90.0$ to $100.0 \text{ °C}$: $q_1 = mc\Delta T = (18.0)(4.18)(10.0) = 752 \text{ J} = 0.752 \text{ kJ}$. Second, vaporize at $100.0 \text{ °C}$: $q_2 = n\Delta H_\text{vap} = (1.00)(40.7) = 40.7 \text{ kJ}$. The total is $q = 0.752 + 40.7 = 41.5 \text{ kJ}$. The vaporization term dominates because phase changes require far more energy than modest temperature changes.
