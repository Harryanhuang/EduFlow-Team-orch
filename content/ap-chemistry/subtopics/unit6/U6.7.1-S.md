Estimate $\Delta H_\text{rxn}$ for $\text{H}_2(g) + \text{Cl}_2(g) \rightarrow 2\,\text{HCl}(g)$ using these bond enthalpies (kJ/mol): H–H = 436, Cl–Cl = 243, H–Cl = 431.

## Options
A) $-183 \text{ kJ}$
B) $-248 \text{ kJ}$
C) $+183 \text{ kJ}$
D) $+248 \text{ kJ}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Bonds broken (reactants): one H–H ($436$) and one Cl–Cl ($243$), totaling $679 \text{ kJ}$. Bonds formed (products): two H–Cl bonds, $2 \times 431 = 862 \text{ kJ}$. Then $\Delta H_\text{rxn} = \Sigma(\text{broken}) - \Sigma(\text{formed}) = 679 - 862 = -183 \text{ kJ}$. The negative value indicates an exothermic reaction because the bonds formed are stronger (release more energy) than the bonds broken.
