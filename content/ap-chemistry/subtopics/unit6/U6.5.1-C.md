How much total heat is required to convert $36.0 \text{ g}$ ($2.00 \text{ mol}$) of ice at $-10.0 \text{ °C}$ into liquid water at $25.0 \text{ °C}$? Use $c_\text{ice} = 2.09 \text{ J/(g·°C)}$, $c_\text{water} = 4.18 \text{ J/(g·°C)}$, and $\Delta H_\text{fus} = 6.01 \text{ kJ/mol}$.

## Options
A) $12.0 \text{ kJ}$
B) $15.5 \text{ kJ}$
C) $16.5 \text{ kJ}$
D) $20.3 \text{ kJ}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Three segments are needed. (1) Warm ice from $-10.0$ to $0 \text{ °C}$: $q_1 = (36.0)(2.09)(10.0) = 752 \text{ J} = 0.752 \text{ kJ}$. (2) Melt the ice at $0 \text{ °C}$: $q_2 = n\Delta H_\text{fus} = (2.00)(6.01) = 12.02 \text{ kJ}$. (3) Warm the liquid from $0$ to $25.0 \text{ °C}$: $q_3 = (36.0)(4.18)(25.0) = 3{,}762 \text{ J} = 3.76 \text{ kJ}$. The total is $q = 0.752 + 12.02 + 3.76 = 16.5 \text{ kJ}$. Each segment uses the specific heat of the appropriate phase, and the melting term uses moles.
