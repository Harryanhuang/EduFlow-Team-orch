A $50.0 \text{ g}$ sample of water ($c = 4.18 \text{ J/(g·°C)}$) at $80.0 \text{ °C}$ is mixed with a $50.0 \text{ g}$ sample of water at $20.0 \text{ °C}$ in an insulated container. What is the final equilibrium temperature?

## Options
A) $20.0 \text{ °C}$
B) $40.0 \text{ °C}$
C) $50.0 \text{ °C}$
D) $60.0 \text{ °C}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
With equal masses and the same specific heat, the heat lost by the hot water equals the heat gained by the cold water: $m c (80.0 - T_f) = m c (T_f - 20.0)$. Canceling $mc$ gives $80.0 - T_f = T_f - 20.0$, so $2T_f = 100.0$ and $T_f = 50.0 \text{ °C}$. The final temperature is the average because the masses and specific heats are identical.
