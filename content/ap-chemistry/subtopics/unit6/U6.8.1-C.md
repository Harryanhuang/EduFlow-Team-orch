Given the reactions below, determine $\Delta H$ for $\text{C}(s) + \tfrac{1}{2}\text{O}_2(g) \rightarrow \text{CO}(g)$.

Reaction 1: $\text{C}(s) + \text{O}_2(g) \rightarrow \text{CO}_2(g)$, $\Delta H_1 = -393.5 \text{ kJ}$
Reaction 2: $\text{CO}(g) + \tfrac{1}{2}\text{O}_2(g) \rightarrow \text{CO}_2(g)$, $\Delta H_2 = -283.0 \text{ kJ}$

## Options
A) $-676.5 \text{ kJ}$
B) $-110.5 \text{ kJ}$
C) $+110.5 \text{ kJ}$
D) $+676.5 \text{ kJ}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Keep Reaction 1 as written and reverse Reaction 2. Reversing Reaction 2 gives $\text{CO}_2(g) \rightarrow \text{CO}(g) + \tfrac{1}{2}\text{O}_2(g)$ with $\Delta H = +283.0 \text{ kJ}$. Adding: $\text{C}(s) + \text{O}_2(g) + \text{CO}_2(g) \rightarrow \text{CO}_2(g) + \text{CO}(g) + \tfrac{1}{2}\text{O}_2(g)$. The $\text{CO}_2$ cancels and $\text{O}_2$ nets to $\tfrac{1}{2}\text{O}_2$ on the left, yielding $\text{C}(s) + \tfrac{1}{2}\text{O}_2(g) \rightarrow \text{CO}(g)$. The enthalpy is $\Delta H = -393.5 + 283.0 = -110.5 \text{ kJ}$. Reversing Reaction 2 required flipping the sign of $\Delta H_2$.
