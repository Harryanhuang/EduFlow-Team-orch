How much heat is required to raise the temperature of $25.0 \text{ g}$ of water ($c = 4.18 \text{ J/(g·°C)}$) from $20.0 \text{ °C}$ to $50.0 \text{ °C}$?

## Options
A) $627 \text{ J}$
B) $3{,}140 \text{ J}$
C) $5{,}225 \text{ J}$
D) $13{,}060 \text{ J}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The temperature change is $\Delta T = 50.0 - 20.0 = 30.0 \text{ °C}$. Applying $q = mc\Delta T = (25.0 \text{ g})(4.18 \text{ J/(g·°C)})(30.0 \text{ °C}) = 3{,}135 \approx 3{,}140 \text{ J}$. The positive sign indicates heat is absorbed by the water.
