A reaction has a potential energy diagram in which the products sit at a higher potential energy than the reactants. Which statement best explains the energetics of this reaction?

## Options
A) The reaction is exothermic because energy is released when product bonds form.
B) The reaction is endothermic because more energy is absorbed to break reactant bonds than is released forming product bonds.
C) The reaction is exothermic because the activation energy is large.
D) The reaction is endothermic because forming product bonds always absorbs energy.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
When products lie higher in potential energy than reactants, the system has gained energy, so $\Delta H > 0$ and the reaction is endothermic. This occurs because the energy absorbed to break the reactant bonds exceeds the energy released when the new product bonds form. Bond breaking always absorbs energy and bond forming always releases energy, so the net sign depends on the relative magnitudes.
