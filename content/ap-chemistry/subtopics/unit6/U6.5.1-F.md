How much heat is required to melt $2.00 \text{ mol}$ of ice at $0 \text{ °C}$? The molar enthalpy of fusion of water is $\Delta H_\text{fus} = 6.01 \text{ kJ/mol}$.

## Options
A) $3.01 \text{ kJ}$
B) $6.01 \text{ kJ}$
C) $12.0 \text{ kJ}$
D) $24.0 \text{ kJ}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Melting at the melting point is a pure phase change at constant temperature, so $q = n\Delta H_\text{fus} = (2.00 \text{ mol})(6.01 \text{ kJ/mol}) = 12.0 \text{ kJ}$. No $mc\Delta T$ term is needed because the temperature does not change while the ice melts.
