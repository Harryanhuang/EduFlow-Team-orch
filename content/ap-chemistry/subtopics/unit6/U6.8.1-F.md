Which of the following substances has a standard enthalpy of formation, $\Delta H_f^\circ$, equal to zero?

## Options
A) $\text{H}_2\text{O}(l)$
B) $\text{O}_2(g)$
C) $\text{CO}_2(g)$
D) $\text{O}_3(g)$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By definition, an element in its most stable form (standard state) has $\Delta H_f^\circ = 0$. Oxygen's standard state is the diatomic gas $\text{O}_2(g)$, so its $\Delta H_f^\circ = 0$. Water and carbon dioxide are compounds with nonzero formation enthalpies, and ozone $\text{O}_3(g)$ is not the standard state of oxygen, so it also has a nonzero $\Delta H_f^\circ$.
