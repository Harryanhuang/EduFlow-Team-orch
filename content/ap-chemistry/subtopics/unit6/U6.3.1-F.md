A hot metal block at $90 \text{ °C}$ is placed in contact with a cooler metal block at $20 \text{ °C}$, and the two are insulated from their surroundings. What happens as they reach thermal equilibrium?

## Options
A) Heat flows from the cooler block to the hotter block until both reach $90 \text{ °C}$.
B) Heat flows from the hotter block to the cooler block until both reach the same intermediate temperature.
C) No heat flows because the blocks are insulated.
D) Heat flows in both directions equally so neither block changes temperature.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Heat spontaneously flows from the higher-temperature object to the lower-temperature object. The hot block loses thermal energy and cools while the cold block gains thermal energy and warms, until both reach a common intermediate temperature between $20 \text{ °C}$ and $90 \text{ °C}$. This common temperature is thermal equilibrium.
