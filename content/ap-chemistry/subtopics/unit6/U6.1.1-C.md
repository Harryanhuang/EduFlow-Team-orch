The dissolution of ammonium nitrate in water is represented by $\text{NH}_4\text{NO}_3(s) \rightarrow \text{NH}_4^+(aq) + \text{NO}_3^-(aq)$, $\Delta H = +25.7 \text{ kJ/mol}$. A student dissolves the salt and observes the water in the beaker. Which set of conclusions is correct?

## Options
A) The water gets colder, the process is endothermic, and crystallizing the dissolved ions would release $25.7 \text{ kJ/mol}$.
B) The water gets warmer, the process is exothermic, and crystallizing the dissolved ions would absorb $25.7 \text{ kJ/mol}$.
C) The water gets colder, the process is exothermic, and crystallizing the dissolved ions would release $25.7 \text{ kJ/mol}$.
D) The water gets warmer, the process is endothermic, and crystallizing the dissolved ions would absorb $25.7 \text{ kJ/mol}$.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Because $\Delta H = +25.7 \text{ kJ/mol} > 0$, dissolution is endothermic: the system absorbs energy from the surroundings, so the water (the surroundings) gets colder. The reverse process, crystallization of the ions back to solid $\text{NH}_4\text{NO}_3$, has $\Delta H = -25.7 \text{ kJ/mol}$, meaning it releases $25.7 \text{ kJ}$ per mole. Only option A correctly pairs the cold water, endothermic classification, and the sign-reversed reverse process.
