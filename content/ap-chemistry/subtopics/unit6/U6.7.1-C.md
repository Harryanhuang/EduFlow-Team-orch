Estimate $\Delta H_\text{rxn}$ for the combustion $\text{CH}_4(g) + 2\,\text{O}_2(g) \rightarrow \text{CO}_2(g) + 2\,\text{H}_2\text{O}(g)$ using these average bond enthalpies (kJ/mol): C–H = 413, O=O = 498, C=O = 799, O–H = 467.

## Options
A) $-698 \text{ kJ}$
B) $-818 \text{ kJ}$
C) $+818 \text{ kJ}$
D) $-2{,}648 \text{ kJ}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Bonds broken (reactants): four C–H bonds in $\text{CH}_4$ ($4 \times 413 = 1{,}652$) plus two O=O bonds in $2\,\text{O}_2$ ($2 \times 498 = 996$), totaling $2{,}648 \text{ kJ}$. Bonds formed (products): two C=O bonds in $\text{CO}_2$ ($2 \times 799 = 1{,}598$) plus four O–H bonds in $2\,\text{H}_2\text{O}$ ($4 \times 467 = 1{,}868$), totaling $3{,}466 \text{ kJ}$. Then $\Delta H_\text{rxn} = 2{,}648 - 3{,}466 = -818 \text{ kJ}$. The reaction is exothermic, as expected for a combustion, because the strong C=O and O–H bonds formed release more energy than is absorbed breaking the reactant bonds.
