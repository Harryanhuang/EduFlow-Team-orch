Calculate $\Delta H_\text{rxn}^\circ$ for $2\,\text{CO}(g) + \text{O}_2(g) \rightarrow 2\,\text{CO}_2(g)$ using $\Delta H_f^\circ$: CO(g) = $-110.5 \text{ kJ/mol}$, CO2(g) = $-393.5 \text{ kJ/mol}$, O2(g) = $0 \text{ kJ/mol}$.

## Options
A) $-283.0 \text{ kJ}$
B) $-566.0 \text{ kJ}$
C) $-504.0 \text{ kJ}$
D) $+566.0 \text{ kJ}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Using $\Delta H_\text{rxn}^\circ = \Sigma n\Delta H_f^\circ(\text{products}) - \Sigma n\Delta H_f^\circ(\text{reactants})$. Products: $2 \times (-393.5) = -787.0 \text{ kJ}$. Reactants: $2 \times (-110.5) + 1 \times 0 = -221.0 \text{ kJ}$. Then $\Delta H_\text{rxn}^\circ = -787.0 - (-221.0) = -566.0 \text{ kJ}$. The coefficients of 2 on both CO and CO2 must be applied, and O2 contributes zero as an element in its standard state.
