A first-order reaction has a rate constant of $k = 0.0231\ \mathrm{s^{-1}}$. If the initial concentration of reactant is $0.800\ \mathrm{M}$, what is the concentration after $100.\ \mathrm{s}$? (Note: $e^{-2.31} \approx 0.0993$)

## Options
A. $0.0794\ \mathrm{M}$
B. $0.400\ \mathrm{M}$
C. $0.717\ \mathrm{M}$
D. $0.0231\ \mathrm{M}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Use the first-order integrated rate law:

$$\ln[\mathrm{A}] = \ln[\mathrm{A}]_0 - kt$$

Substitute the known values:

$$\ln[\mathrm{A}] = \ln(0.800) - (0.0231)(100.) = -0.223 - 2.31 = -2.53$$

Exponentiate to find $[\mathrm{A}]$:

$$[\mathrm{A}] = e^{-2.53} \approx 0.0794\ \mathrm{M}$$

Equivalently, $[\mathrm{A}] = [\mathrm{A}]_0 \, e^{-kt} = 0.800 \times e^{-2.31} = 0.800 \times 0.0993 \approx 0.0794\ \mathrm{M}$.

Option B incorrectly assumes one half-life has passed.
Option C uses a linear (zero-order) decay instead of exponential.
Option D mistakenly reports the rate constant as the concentration.
