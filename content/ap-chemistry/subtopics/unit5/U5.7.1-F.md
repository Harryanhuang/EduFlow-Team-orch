A reaction proceeds by the following two-step mechanism:

Step 1: $\mathrm{NO_2 + NO_2 \rightarrow NO_3 + NO}$
Step 2: $\mathrm{NO_3 + CO \rightarrow NO_2 + CO_2}$

Which species is an intermediate?

## Options
A. $\mathrm{NO_2}$
B. $\mathrm{CO}$
C. $\mathrm{NO_3}$
D. $\mathrm{CO_2}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
An intermediate is a species that is **formed in one step and consumed in a later step**, so it does not appear in the overall equation.

$\mathrm{NO_3}$ is produced in step 1 and then consumed in step 2, making it an intermediate. Adding the steps and canceling $\mathrm{NO_3}$ (and one $\mathrm{NO_2}$) gives the overall reaction:

$$\mathrm{NO_2 + CO \rightarrow NO + CO_2}$$

Option A, $\mathrm{NO_2}$, is consumed overall (a reactant), not an intermediate.
Option B, $\mathrm{CO}$, is an overall reactant.
Option D, $\mathrm{CO_2}$, is an overall product.
