For the reaction $\mathrm{2N_2O_5(g) \rightarrow 4NO_2(g) + O_2(g)}$, oxygen is being produced at a rate of $0.020\ \mathrm{M/s}$. What is the rate at which $\mathrm{N_2O_5}$ is being consumed?

## Options
A. $0.010\ \mathrm{M/s}$
B. $0.020\ \mathrm{M/s}$
C. $0.040\ \mathrm{M/s}$
D. $0.080\ \mathrm{M/s}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The rate relationships from the balanced equation are:

$$-\frac{1}{2}\frac{\Delta[\mathrm{N_2O_5}]}{\Delta t} = +\frac{1}{1}\frac{\Delta[\mathrm{O_2}]}{\Delta t}$$

The rate of $\mathrm{O_2}$ production is $0.020\ \mathrm{M/s}$. Therefore:

$$-\frac{\Delta[\mathrm{N_2O_5}]}{\Delta t} = 2 \times \frac{\Delta[\mathrm{O_2}]}{\Delta t} = 2 \times 0.020 = 0.040\ \mathrm{M/s}$$

$\mathrm{N_2O_5}$ is consumed at $0.040\ \mathrm{M/s}$ because its coefficient (2) is twice that of $\mathrm{O_2}$ (1).

Option A divides instead of multiplies by 2.
Option B ignores the coefficient ratio entirely.
Option D uses the $\mathrm{NO_2}$ coefficient (4) by mistake.
