For the reaction $\mathrm{2HI(g) \rightarrow H_2(g) + I_2(g)}$, the concentration of $\mathrm{HI}$ drops from $0.500\ \mathrm{M}$ at $t = 0\ \mathrm{s}$ to $0.380\ \mathrm{M}$ at $t = 40.\ \mathrm{s}$. What is the average rate of formation of $\mathrm{H_2}$ over this interval?

## Options
A. $1.5 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{M/s}$
B. $3.0 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{M/s}$
C. $6.0 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{M/s}$
D. $1.2 \times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{M/s}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
First find the average rate of disappearance of $\mathrm{HI}$:

$$-\frac{\Delta[\mathrm{HI}]}{\Delta t} = -\frac{0.380 - 0.500}{40. - 0} = \frac{0.120}{40.} = 3.0 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{M/s}$$

The stoichiometric relationship is:

$$-\frac{1}{2}\frac{\Delta[\mathrm{HI}]}{\Delta t} = +\frac{1}{1}\frac{\Delta[\mathrm{H_2}]}{\Delta t}$$

So the rate of $\mathrm{H_2}$ formation is half the rate of $\mathrm{HI}$ consumption:

$$\frac{\Delta[\mathrm{H_2}]}{\Delta t} = \frac{1}{2}(3.0 \times 10^{-3}) = 1.5 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{M/s}$$

Option B is the rate of $\mathrm{HI}$ disappearance, not $\mathrm{H_2}$ formation.
Option C doubles instead of halves.
Option D forgets to divide by the time interval correctly.
