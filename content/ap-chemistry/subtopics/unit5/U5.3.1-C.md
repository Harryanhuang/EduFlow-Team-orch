For the decomposition of a reactant $\mathrm{A}$, a plot of $1/[\mathrm{A}]$ versus time gives a straight line with a slope of $0.50\ \mathrm{M^{-1}\,s^{-1}}$. If the initial concentration is $0.40\ \mathrm{M}$, what is $[\mathrm{A}]$ after $5.0\ \mathrm{s}$?

## Options
A. $0.20\ \mathrm{M}$
B. $0.30\ \mathrm{M}$
C. $0.15\ \mathrm{M}$
D. $0.40\ \mathrm{M}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
A linear plot of $1/[\mathrm{A}]$ versus time means the reaction is **second order**, and the slope equals the rate constant, $k = 0.50\ \mathrm{M^{-1}\,s^{-1}}$. The integrated rate law is:

$$\frac{1}{[\mathrm{A}]} = \frac{1}{[\mathrm{A}]_0} + kt$$

Substitute the known values:

$$\frac{1}{[\mathrm{A}]} = \frac{1}{0.40} + (0.50)(5.0) = 2.5 + 2.5 = 5.0\ \mathrm{M^{-1}}$$

Invert to find the concentration:

$$[\mathrm{A}] = \frac{1}{5.0} = 0.20\ \mathrm{M}$$

Option B and C come from algebra errors in inverting $1/[\mathrm{A}]$.
Option D ignores any reaction progress over the 5.0 s interval.
