A reaction has the rate law rate $= k[\mathrm{A}]^2[\mathrm{B}]$. In one experiment, when $[\mathrm{A}] = 0.10\ \mathrm{M}$ and $[\mathrm{B}] = 0.20\ \mathrm{M}$, the initial rate is $4.0 \times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{M/s}$. What are the value and units of the rate constant $k$?

## Options
A. $2.0 \times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{M^{-2}\,s^{-1}}$
B. $0.20\ \mathrm{M^{-2}\,s^{-1}}$
C. $2.0\ \mathrm{M^{-1}\,s^{-1}}$
D. $0.20\ \mathrm{M^{-1}\,s^{-1}}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Solve the rate law for $k$:

$$k = \frac{\text{rate}}{[\mathrm{A}]^2[\mathrm{B}]} = \frac{4.0 \times 10^{-4}}{(0.10)^2(0.20)}$$

Compute the denominator: $(0.10)^2 = 0.010$, then $0.010 \times 0.20 = 2.0 \times 10^{-3}$.

$$k = \frac{4.0 \times 10^{-4}}{2.0 \times 10^{-3}} = 0.20$$

**Units:** the overall order is $2 + 1 = 3$, so units are $\mathrm{M}^{1-3}\,\mathrm{s}^{-1} = \mathrm{M^{-2}\,s^{-1}}$.

Therefore $k = 0.20\ \mathrm{M^{-2}\,s^{-1}}$.

Option A has the wrong numerical value (off by a factor of 10).
Option C and D use incorrect units for a third-order reaction.
