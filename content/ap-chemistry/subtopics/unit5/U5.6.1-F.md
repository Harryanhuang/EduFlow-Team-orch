On a reaction energy profile, the reactants sit at $50\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$, the peak of the curve is at $120\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$, and the products sit at $30\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$. What is the activation energy ($E_a$) for the forward reaction?

## Options
A. $20\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$
B. $70\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$
C. $90\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$
D. $120\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The activation energy for the forward reaction is the energy difference between the **transition state (peak)** and the **reactants**:

$$E_a = E_{\text{peak}} - E_{\text{reactants}} = 120 - 50 = 70\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$$

Option A is $\Delta H$ (products minus reactants $= 30 - 50 = -20\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$, magnitude 20).
Option C is the reverse activation energy ($120 - 30 = 90\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$).
Option D is the absolute height of the peak, not measured relative to the reactants.
