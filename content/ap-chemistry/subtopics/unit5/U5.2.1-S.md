For the reaction $\mathrm{A + B \rightarrow products}$, the following initial-rate data were collected:

| Trial | $[\mathrm{A}]$ (M) | $[\mathrm{B}]$ (M) | Initial rate (M/s) |
|-------|------|------|--------------------|
| 1 | 0.10 | 0.10 | $2.0 \times 10^{-3}$ |
| 2 | 0.20 | 0.10 | $8.0 \times 10^{-3}$ |
| 3 | 0.10 | 0.20 | $2.0 \times 10^{-3}$ |

What is the rate law for this reaction?

## Options
A. rate $= k[\mathrm{A}][\mathrm{B}]$
B. rate $= k[\mathrm{A}]^2$
C. rate $= k[\mathrm{A}]^2[\mathrm{B}]$
D. rate $= k[\mathrm{B}]^2$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
**Order in A** (compare trials 1 and 2, where only $[\mathrm{A}]$ changes): $[\mathrm{A}]$ doubles ($0.10 \to 0.20$) and the rate increases by a factor of $\frac{8.0 \times 10^{-3}}{2.0 \times 10^{-3}} = 4$. Since $2^m = 4$, $m = 2$.

**Order in B** (compare trials 1 and 3, where only $[\mathrm{B}]$ changes): $[\mathrm{B}]$ doubles but the rate is unchanged ($2.0 \times 10^{-3}$ in both). Since $2^n = 1$, $n = 0$.

The reaction is second order in $\mathrm{A}$ and zero order in $\mathrm{B}$:

$$\text{rate} = k[\mathrm{A}]^2$$

Option A wrongly makes the reaction first order in each.
Option C includes $[\mathrm{B}]$, but B has no effect on the rate.
Option D assigns the order to the wrong reactant.
