Consider the proposed mechanism for the decomposition of ozone:

Step 1: $\mathrm{Cl + O_3 \rightarrow ClO + O_2}$
Step 2: $\mathrm{ClO + O \rightarrow Cl + O_2}$

Which species acts as a catalyst in this mechanism?

## Options
A. $\mathrm{O_3}$
B. $\mathrm{ClO}$
C. $\mathrm{Cl}$
D. $\mathrm{O_2}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
A catalyst is **consumed in an early step and regenerated in a later step**, so it does not appear in the overall balanced equation.

$\mathrm{Cl}$ is a reactant in step 1 and is reformed as a product in step 2 — it is consumed then regenerated, which is the defining behavior of a catalyst. The overall reaction (adding the steps and canceling $\mathrm{Cl}$ and $\mathrm{ClO}$) is:

$$\mathrm{O_3 + O \rightarrow 2O_2}$$

Option B, $\mathrm{ClO}$, is produced in step 1 and consumed in step 2 — that is an **intermediate**, not a catalyst.
Option A, $\mathrm{O_3}$, is an overall reactant.
Option D, $\mathrm{O_2}$, is an overall product.
