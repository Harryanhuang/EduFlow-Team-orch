A reaction is first order in reactant $\mathrm{A}$. Which statement about its half-life is correct?

## Options
A. The half-life doubles each time the concentration of $\mathrm{A}$ is halved.
B. The half-life is constant and independent of the initial concentration of $\mathrm{A}$.
C. The half-life depends only on the initial concentration of $\mathrm{A}$.
D. The half-life increases as the reaction proceeds.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For a first-order reaction the half-life is given by:

$$t_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{k}$$

This expression contains only the rate constant $k$ and no concentration term. Therefore the half-life is constant throughout the reaction and does not depend on how much $\mathrm{A}$ is present. Each successive half-life takes the same amount of time.

Option A and C incorrectly tie the first-order half-life to concentration (that behavior describes zero- and second-order reactions).
Option D is wrong because the half-life stays the same as the reaction proceeds.
