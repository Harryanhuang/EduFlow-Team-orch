Which statement correctly describes the effect of a catalyst on a chemical reaction?

## Options
A. It increases the rate by lowering the activation energy.
B. It increases the rate by raising the temperature of the system.
C. It shifts the equilibrium to favor more product.
D. It makes the reaction more exothermic by lowering $\Delta H$.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
A catalyst speeds up a reaction by providing an alternative reaction pathway with a **lower activation energy**. With a smaller $E_a$, a larger fraction of collisions have enough energy to react, so both the forward and reverse rates increase.

A catalyst does **not**:
- raise the temperature of the system (option B),
- shift the position of equilibrium — it speeds the forward and reverse rates equally, so the equilibrium constant is unchanged (option C),
- change $\Delta H$, since the energies of the reactants and products are unaffected (option D).

Only the activation energy (the height of the barrier) is lowered, which is why option A is correct.
