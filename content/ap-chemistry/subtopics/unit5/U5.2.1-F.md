The rate law for a reaction is found to be rate $= k[\mathrm{A}]^2[\mathrm{B}]$. What is the overall order of the reaction?

## Options
A. 1
B. 2
C. 3
D. 6

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The overall order of a reaction is the sum of the exponents in the rate law. Here the order with respect to $\mathrm{A}$ is 2 and the order with respect to $\mathrm{B}$ is 1:

$$\text{overall order} = 2 + 1 = 3$$

The reaction is third order overall.

Option A is only the order in $\mathrm{B}$.
Option B is only the order in $\mathrm{A}$.
Option D incorrectly multiplies the exponents instead of adding them.
