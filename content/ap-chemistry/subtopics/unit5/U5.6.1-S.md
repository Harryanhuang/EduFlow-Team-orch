A reaction has a forward activation energy of $45\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$ and a reverse activation energy of $80\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$. What is the enthalpy change ($\Delta H$) for the forward reaction?

## Options
A. $+125\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$
B. $+35\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$
C. $-35\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$
D. $-125\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The enthalpy change of a reaction is the difference between the forward and reverse activation energies:

$$\Delta H = E_{a,\text{forward}} - E_{a,\text{reverse}} = 45 - 80 = -35\ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$$

The negative sign indicates the reaction is **exothermic**: the products are lower in energy than the reactants, which is consistent with a smaller forward activation energy than reverse activation energy.

Option A adds the two activation energies, which has no physical meaning here.
Option B has the correct magnitude but the wrong sign (it would imply endothermic).
Option D reverses subtraction and adds incorrectly.
