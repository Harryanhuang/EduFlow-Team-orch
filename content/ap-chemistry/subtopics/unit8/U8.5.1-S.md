A $0.100 \text{ M}$ weak acid is titrated with $\text{NaOH}$. The equivalence point occurs at $40.0 \text{ mL}$ of added base, and the pH at the half-equivalence point ($20.0 \text{ mL}$) is measured as $4.20$. What is the $K_a$ of the weak acid?

## Options
A) $4.2 \times 10^{-1}$
B) $6.3 \times 10^{-5}$
C) $1.6 \times 10^{-4}$
D) $4.2 \times 10^{-5}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
At the half-equivalence point, half the acid has been converted to its conjugate base, so $[\text{HA}]=[\text{A}^-]$. The Henderson-Hasselbalch equation reduces to $\text{pH}=\text{p}K_a$. Thus $\text{p}K_a = 4.20$, and $K_a = 10^{-4.20} = 6.3 \times 10^{-5}$. The half-equivalence point is the most direct way to read $\text{p}K_a$ off a titration curve.
