What is the approximate pH of a $0.10 \text{ M}$ solution of acetic acid ($\text{CH}_3\text{COOH}$, $K_a = 1.8 \times 10^{-5}$)?

## Options
A) $1.00$
B) $2.87$
C) $4.74$
D) $5.74$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Set up an ICE table for $\text{CH}_3\text{COOH} \rightleftharpoons \text{H}^+ + \text{CH}_3\text{COO}^-$ with $x = [\text{H}^+]$ at equilibrium. Then $K_a = \dfrac{x^2}{0.10 - x} \approx \dfrac{x^2}{0.10}$. Solving: $x = \sqrt{(1.8\times10^{-5})(0.10)} = \sqrt{1.8\times10^{-6}} = 1.34\times10^{-3} \text{ M}$. So $\text{pH} = -\log(1.34\times10^{-3}) = 2.87$. Treating it as a strong acid would wrongly give pH 1.00; pH 4.74 equals $\text{p}K_a$, which is the half-equivalence value, not the pH of the pure acid.
