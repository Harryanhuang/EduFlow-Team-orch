The bicarbonate ion $\text{HCO}_3^-$ participates in two reactions:

Reaction 1: $\text{HCO}_3^- + \text{H}_2\text{O} \rightleftharpoons \text{H}_2\text{CO}_3 + \text{OH}^-$

Reaction 2: $\text{HCO}_3^- + \text{H}_2\text{O} \rightleftharpoons \text{CO}_3^{2-} + \text{H}_3\text{O}^+$

Which statement best describes the behavior of $\text{HCO}_3^-$?

## Options
A) $\text{HCO}_3^-$ acts as a base in Reaction 1 and as an acid in Reaction 2, making it amphoteric.
B) $\text{HCO}_3^-$ acts as an acid in both reactions because it contains a hydrogen atom.
C) $\text{HCO}_3^-$ acts as a base in both reactions because it carries a negative charge.
D) $\text{HCO}_3^-$ cannot be amphoteric because water is the only amphoteric species.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
In Reaction 1, $\text{HCO}_3^-$ accepts a proton from water to form $\text{H}_2\text{CO}_3$, acting as a Brønsted-Lowry base. In Reaction 2, $\text{HCO}_3^-$ donates a proton to water to form $\text{CO}_3^{2-}$, acting as a Brønsted-Lowry acid. A species that can do both is amphoteric (amphiprotic). The negative charge or presence of hydrogen alone does not dictate the role; the reaction partner does.
