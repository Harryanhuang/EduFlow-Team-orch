In the reaction $\text{NH}_3 + \text{H}_2\text{O} \rightleftharpoons \text{NH}_4^+ + \text{OH}^-$, which of the following correctly identifies the conjugate acid-base pairs?

## Options
A) $\text{NH}_3/\text{OH}^-$ and $\text{H}_2\text{O}/\text{NH}_4^+$
B) $\text{NH}_3/\text{NH}_4^+$ and $\text{H}_2\text{O}/\text{OH}^-$
C) $\text{NH}_3/\text{H}_2\text{O}$ and $\text{NH}_4^+/\text{OH}^-$
D) $\text{H}_2\text{O}/\text{NH}_4^+$ and $\text{NH}_3/\text{OH}^-$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Conjugate pairs differ by one proton. $\text{NH}_3$ (base) accepts a proton to become $\text{NH}_4^+$ (conjugate acid), and $\text{H}_2\text{O}$ (acid) donates a proton to become $\text{OH}^-$ (conjugate base). The two conjugate pairs are $\text{NH}_3/\text{NH}_4^+$ and $\text{H}_2\text{O}/\text{OH}^-$.
