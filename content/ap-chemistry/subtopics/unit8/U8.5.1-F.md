What volume of $0.150 \text{ M}$ $\text{NaOH}$ is required to reach the equivalence point when titrating $25.0 \text{ mL}$ of $0.100 \text{ M}$ $\text{HCl}$?

## Options
A) $16.7 \text{ mL}$
B) $25.0 \text{ mL}$
C) $37.5 \text{ mL}$
D) $50.0 \text{ mL}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
At the equivalence point, moles of base = moles of acid for this 1:1 reaction. Moles $\text{HCl} = 0.0250 \text{ L} \times 0.100 \text{ M} = 2.50 \times 10^{-3}$ mol. So $V_{\text{NaOH}} = \dfrac{2.50\times10^{-3} \text{ mol}}{0.150 \text{ M}} = 0.0167 \text{ L} = 16.7 \text{ mL}$. Because the base is more concentrated than the acid, a smaller volume is needed.
