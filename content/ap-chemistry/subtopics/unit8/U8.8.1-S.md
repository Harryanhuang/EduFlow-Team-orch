A buffer contains equal moles of $\text{HCN}$ and $\text{NaCN}$. A small amount of strong base ($\text{NaOH}$) is added. Which statement best describes what happens?

## Options
A) The $\text{OH}^-$ reacts with $\text{HCN}$, decreasing $[\text{HCN}]$ and increasing $[\text{CN}^-]$; pH rises slightly.
B) The $\text{OH}^-$ reacts with $\text{CN}^-$, decreasing $[\text{CN}^-]$; pH falls slightly.
C) The $\text{OH}^-$ does not react because the buffer prevents all changes; pH is unchanged.
D) The $\text{OH}^-$ reacts with water only; pH rises sharply as in pure water.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Added strong base is consumed by the weak acid component: $\text{HCN} + \text{OH}^- \rightarrow \text{CN}^- + \text{H}_2\text{O}$. This lowers $[\text{HCN}]$ and raises $[\text{CN}^-]$. By Henderson-Hasselbalch, the ratio $[\text{CN}^-]/[\text{HCN}]$ increases, so pH rises, but only slightly because the conjugate pair absorbs the added base. A buffer minimizes pH change; it does not prevent it entirely (ruling out C).
