$25.0 \text{ mL}$ of $0.100 \text{ M}$ acetic acid ($K_a = 1.8\times10^{-5}$, $\text{p}K_a = 4.74$) is titrated with $0.100 \text{ M}$ $\text{NaOH}$. What is the pH after $10.0 \text{ mL}$ of $\text{NaOH}$ has been added?

## Options
A) $2.87$
B) $4.57$
C) $4.74$
D) $8.72$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Initial moles acetic acid $= 0.0250 \times 0.100 = 2.50\times10^{-3}$ mol. Added $\text{OH}^- = 0.0100 \times 0.100 = 1.00\times10^{-3}$ mol, which converts that much acid to acetate. Remaining $\text{HA} = 2.50\times10^{-3}-1.00\times10^{-3}=1.50\times10^{-3}$ mol; $\text{A}^- = 1.00\times10^{-3}$ mol. This is the buffer region, so $\text{pH}=\text{p}K_a+\log\dfrac{[\text{A}^-]}{[\text{HA}]}=4.74+\log\dfrac{1.00\times10^{-3}}{1.50\times10^{-3}}=4.74+\log(0.667)=4.74-0.18=4.57$. (Volume cancels in the ratio, so moles may be used directly.) pH equals $\text{p}K_a$ (4.74) only at the half-equivalence point of $12.5$ mL.
