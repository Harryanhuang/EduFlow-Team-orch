Three weak acids have the following $K_a$ values: HF ($6.8 \times 10^{-4}$), $\text{CH}_3\text{COOH}$ ($1.8 \times 10^{-5}$), and HCN ($6.2 \times 10^{-10}$). Which acid is the strongest, and what is the correct $K_a$ expression for the generic weak acid HA?

## Options
A) HCN is strongest; $K_a = \dfrac{[\text{HA}]}{[\text{H}^+][\text{A}^-]}$
B) HF is strongest; $K_a = \dfrac{[\text{H}^+][\text{A}^-]}{[\text{HA}]}$
C) HF is strongest; $K_a = \dfrac{[\text{H}^+][\text{A}^-]}{[\text{HA}][\text{H}_2\text{O}]}$
D) $\text{CH}_3\text{COOH}$ is strongest; $K_a = \dfrac{[\text{H}^+][\text{A}^-]}{[\text{HA}]}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
A larger $K_a$ indicates a stronger acid (more ionization), so HF ($6.8 \times 10^{-4}$) is the strongest of the three. The correct expression is $K_a = \dfrac{[\text{H}^+][\text{A}^-]}{[\text{HA}]}$. Pure liquid water is the solvent and is omitted from the equilibrium expression, ruling out choice C.
