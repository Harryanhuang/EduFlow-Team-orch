A weak acid HA has $K_a = 4.0 \times 10^{-6}$. A student compares a $0.10 \text{ M}$ solution with a $0.0010 \text{ M}$ solution. Which statement correctly describes the percent ionization of the two solutions?

## Options
A) The $0.10 \text{ M}$ solution has higher percent ionization because it is more concentrated.
B) The $0.0010 \text{ M}$ solution has higher percent ionization (about $6.3\%$ vs. $0.63\%$).
C) Both solutions have the same percent ionization because $K_a$ is unchanged.
D) The $0.0010 \text{ M}$ solution has lower percent ionization because $[\text{H}^+]$ is smaller.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Percent ionization $= \dfrac{\sqrt{K_a C_0}}{C_0}\times100\% = \sqrt{\dfrac{K_a}{C_0}}\times100\%$. For $0.10 \text{ M}$: $\sqrt{4.0\times10^{-6}/0.10}=\sqrt{4.0\times10^{-5}}=6.3\times10^{-3}$, i.e. $0.63\%$. For $0.0010 \text{ M}$: $\sqrt{4.0\times10^{-6}/0.0010}=\sqrt{4.0\times10^{-3}}=6.3\times10^{-2}$, i.e. $6.3\%$. Although the dilute solution has a smaller absolute $[\text{H}^+]$, a larger fraction of the acid ionizes — dilution shifts the equilibrium toward more ionization (Le Châtelier). $K_a$ is constant but percent ionization is not.
