$50.0 \text{ mL}$ of $0.100 \text{ M}$ $\text{HCl}$ is mixed with $30.0 \text{ mL}$ of $0.100 \text{ M}$ $\text{NaOH}$. What is the pH of the resulting solution at $25\,^\circ\text{C}$?

## Options
A) $1.60$
B) $1.90$
C) $2.30$
D) $12.40$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Moles $\text{H}^+ = 0.0500 \text{ L} \times 0.100 \text{ M} = 5.00 \times 10^{-3}$ mol. Moles $\text{OH}^- = 0.0300 \text{ L} \times 0.100 \text{ M} = 3.00 \times 10^{-3}$ mol. Neutralization consumes all $\text{OH}^-$, leaving excess $\text{H}^+ = 5.00\times10^{-3} - 3.00\times10^{-3} = 2.00 \times 10^{-3}$ mol. The total volume is $50.0 + 30.0 = 80.0 \text{ mL} = 0.0800 \text{ L}$, so $[\text{H}^+] = (2.00\times10^{-3})/0.0800 = 0.0250 \text{ M}$. Then $\text{pH} = -\log(0.0250) = 1.60$. Choices C and D arise from neglecting the excess-acid calculation or mistakenly treating the base as in excess.
