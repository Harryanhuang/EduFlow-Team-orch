A buffer contains $0.020 \text{ mol}$ $\text{CH}_3\text{COOH}$ and $0.020 \text{ mol}$ $\text{CH}_3\text{COO}^-$ in $1.0 \text{ L}$. If $0.030 \text{ mol}$ of $\text{HCl}$ is added (assume no volume change), what is the approximate pH?

## Options
A) $4.74$
B) $4.56$
C) $2.00$
D) $1.70$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Added $\text{H}^+$ reacts with the conjugate base: $\text{CH}_3\text{COO}^- + \text{H}^+ \rightarrow \text{CH}_3\text{COOH}$. There are only $0.020$ mol of acetate, so all of it is consumed, using $0.020$ mol of the $0.030$ mol $\text{H}^+$. This destroys the buffer and leaves $0.030 - 0.020 = 0.010$ mol of excess strong acid in $1.0 \text{ L}$, giving $[\text{H}^+] = 0.010 \text{ M}$ (the small contribution from acetic acid is negligible). Thus $\text{pH} = -\log(0.010) = 2.00$. Henderson-Hasselbalch no longer applies once the conjugate base is exhausted.
