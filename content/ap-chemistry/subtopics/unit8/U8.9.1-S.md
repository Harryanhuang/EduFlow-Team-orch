A buffer contains $0.30 \text{ M}$ $\text{NH}_3$ and $0.15 \text{ M}$ $\text{NH}_4\text{Cl}$. Given $K_b$ of $\text{NH}_3 = 1.8\times10^{-5}$, what is the pH of the buffer?

## Options
A) $4.75$
B) $8.95$
C) $9.55$
D) $9.85$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
First convert to the conjugate acid: $K_a(\text{NH}_4^+) = \dfrac{1.0\times10^{-14}}{1.8\times10^{-5}} = 5.6\times10^{-10}$, so $\text{p}K_a = 9.25$ (equivalently $\text{p}K_a = 14 - \text{p}K_b = 14 - 4.74 = 9.26$). Apply $\text{pH}=\text{p}K_a+\log\dfrac{[\text{NH}_3]}{[\text{NH}_4^+]}=9.25+\log\dfrac{0.30}{0.15}=9.25+\log(2.0)=9.25+0.30=9.55$. The base ($\text{NH}_3$) goes in the numerator; using $K_b$ directly would wrongly give an acidic pH.
