Which of the following combinations would produce a buffer solution?

## Options
A) $\text{HCl}$ and $\text{NaCl}$
B) $\text{CH}_3\text{COOH}$ and $\text{CH}_3\text{COONa}$
C) $\text{NaOH}$ and $\text{NaCl}$
D) $\text{HCl}$ and $\text{NaOH}$ in equal moles

## Answer
B

## Explanation
A buffer requires a weak acid and its conjugate base in comparable amounts. $\text{CH}_3\text{COOH}$ (weak acid) and $\text{CH}_3\text{COONa}$ (which provides the conjugate base $\text{CH}_3\text{COO}^-$) form a classic buffer. $\text{HCl}/\text{NaCl}$ fails because $\text{HCl}$ is a strong acid and $\text{Cl}^-$ is not a meaningful conjugate base. Choice D produces only water and salt (full neutralization), not a buffer.
