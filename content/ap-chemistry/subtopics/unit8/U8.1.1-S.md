In the reaction $\text{BF}_3 + \text{NH}_3 \rightarrow \text{F}_3\text{B}{-}\text{NH}_3$, which statement correctly classifies the reactants?

## Options
A) $\text{BF}_3$ is a Brønsted-Lowry acid because it donates a proton.
B) $\text{NH}_3$ is a Lewis acid because it accepts an electron pair.
C) $\text{BF}_3$ is a Lewis acid because it accepts an electron pair from $\text{NH}_3$.
D) Both species act as Lewis bases by donating electron pairs.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
$\text{BF}_3$ has an electron-deficient boron atom (only 6 valence electrons) and accepts the lone pair on nitrogen, making it a Lewis acid (electron-pair acceptor). $\text{NH}_3$ donates its lone pair, acting as a Lewis base. No proton is transferred, so Brønsted-Lowry classification does not apply here. This illustrates why the Lewis definition is broader.
