A gas has a density of $1.96 \text{ g/L}$ at $0 \text{ °C}$ and $1.00 \text{ atm}$. What is the molar mass of the gas? (Use $R = 0.0821 \text{ L·atm·mol}^{-1}\text{·K}^{-1}$.)

## Options
A) $22.0 \text{ g/mol}$
B) $44.0 \text{ g/mol}$
C) $28.0 \text{ g/mol}$
D) $88.0 \text{ g/mol}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Use $M = \rho RT/P$ with $T = 0\text{ °C} = 273\text{ K}$: $M = (1.96)(0.0821)(273)/(1.00) = 43.9 \approx 44.0\text{ g/mol}$. This molar mass corresponds to carbon dioxide ($\text{CO}_2$). Forgetting to convert 0 °C to 273 K would make the calculation impossible (division leading to zero) — the kelvin conversion is essential.
