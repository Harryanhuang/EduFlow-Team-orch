According to the Beer-Lambert law ($A = \varepsilon b c$), what happens to the absorbance of a solution if its concentration is doubled while the path length and molar absorptivity stay constant?

## Options
A) The absorbance is halved.
B) The absorbance stays the same.
C) The absorbance is doubled.
D) The absorbance is quadrupled.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The Beer-Lambert law shows absorbance is directly proportional to concentration when path length $b$ and molar absorptivity $\varepsilon$ are held constant. Doubling the concentration therefore doubles the absorbance. This linear relationship is the basis for using a calibration curve to determine an unknown concentration.
