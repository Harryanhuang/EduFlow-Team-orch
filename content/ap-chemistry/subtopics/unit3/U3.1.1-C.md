A student compares the boiling points of three compounds all with molecular formula $\text{C}_5\text{H}_{12}$: n-pentane (unbranched), isopentane (one branch), and neopentane (two branches). Which of the following correctly predicts their order of boiling points from lowest to highest?

## Options
A) n-pentane < isopentane < neopentane
B) neopentane < isopentane < n-pentane
C) isopentane < n-pentane < neopentane
D) All three have identical boiling points because they are isomers.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
All three compounds are nonpolar and interact only through London dispersion forces. Increased branching makes the molecule more spherical, reducing the surface area available for intermolecular contact and thus weakening dispersion forces. Neopentane (most branched, nearly spherical) has the lowest boiling point (9.5 °C), isopentane is intermediate (27.7 °C), and n-pentane (linear, most contact area) has the highest boiling point (36.1 °C). Same molecular formula does not mean same boiling point when geometry differs.
