Which of the following is a postulate of the kinetic molecular theory of ideal gases?

## Options
A) Gas particles experience strong attractive forces toward one another.
B) Collisions between gas particles are elastic, conserving total kinetic energy.
C) All gas particles in a sample move at exactly the same speed.
D) The volume of the individual gas particles is large compared to the container.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
A core postulate of kinetic molecular theory is that collisions between gas particles (and with the walls) are perfectly elastic, so no kinetic energy is lost. KMT also assumes negligible particle volume and negligible intermolecular forces, which eliminates options A and D. Particle speeds follow a distribution (Maxwell-Boltzmann), so they are not all identical, eliminating option C.
