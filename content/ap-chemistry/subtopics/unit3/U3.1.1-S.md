Which of the following correctly ranks the compounds in order of increasing boiling point?

## Options
A) $\text{CH}_4$ < $\text{HCl}$ < $\text{NH}_3$
B) $\text{NH}_3$ < $\text{HCl}$ < $\text{CH}_4$
C) $\text{HCl}$ < $\text{CH}_4$ < $\text{NH}_3$
D) $\text{CH}_4$ < $\text{NH}_3$ < $\text{HCl}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$\text{CH}_4$ (M = 16 g/mol) is nonpolar and has only weak London dispersion forces, giving it the lowest boiling point (−161 °C). $\text{HCl}$ (M = 36.5 g/mol) is polar and has dipole-dipole interactions plus larger London dispersion forces, raising its boiling point to −85 °C. $\text{NH}_3$ (M = 17 g/mol) is lighter than HCl but forms hydrogen bonds (H bonded to N), which are stronger than the dipole-dipole forces in HCl, pushing its boiling point to −33 °C. Therefore the correct order is $\text{CH}_4$ < $\text{HCl}$ < $\text{NH}_3$.
