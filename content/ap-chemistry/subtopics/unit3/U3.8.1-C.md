Equal samples of $\text{O}_2$ and $\text{H}_2$ are held at the same temperature. Which statement correctly compares their average kinetic energies and root-mean-square (rms) speeds?

## Options
A) $\text{O}_2$ has greater average KE and greater rms speed.
B) Both have equal average KE, and $\text{H}_2$ has the greater rms speed.
C) Both have equal average KE and equal rms speed.
D) $\text{H}_2$ has greater average KE and equal rms speed.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Average kinetic energy depends only on absolute temperature ($\overline{KE} = \frac{3}{2}kT$), so at the same temperature both gases have identical average kinetic energies. However, rms speed is $v_{rms} = \sqrt{3RT/M}$, which is inversely related to the square root of molar mass. Since $\text{H}_2$ (M = 2) is much lighter than $\text{O}_2$ (M = 32), hydrogen molecules move faster on average, giving $\text{H}_2$ the greater rms speed.
