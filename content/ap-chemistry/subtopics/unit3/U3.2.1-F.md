A solid substance has a very high melting point, is extremely hard, and does not conduct electricity in any state. What type of solid is it?

## Options
A) Ionic solid
B) Metallic solid
C) Covalent network solid
D) Molecular solid

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Covalent network solids such as diamond ($\text{SiO}_2$, SiC) consist of atoms held together by a continuous lattice of covalent bonds, making them extremely hard and giving them very high melting points. They do not conduct electricity because there are no free electrons or mobile ions. Ionic solids conduct when melted or dissolved; metallic solids conduct in the solid state; molecular solids typically have low melting points.
