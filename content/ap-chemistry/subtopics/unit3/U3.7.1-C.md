A rigid container holds $8.0 \text{ g}$ of $\text{O}_2$ (M = 32 g/mol) and $14.0 \text{ g}$ of $\text{N}_2$ (M = 28 g/mol). If the total pressure in the container is $1.5 \text{ atm}$, what is the partial pressure of $\text{O}_2$?

## Options
A) $0.50 \text{ atm}$
B) $0.55 \text{ atm}$
C) $0.75 \text{ atm}$
D) $1.0 \text{ atm}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Find moles of each gas: $n_{\text{O}_2} = 8.0/32 = 0.25\text{ mol}$ and $n_{\text{N}_2} = 14.0/28 = 0.50\text{ mol}$, for a total of 0.75 mol. The mole fraction of $\text{O}_2$ is $X = 0.25/0.75 = 1/3$. By Dalton's law, $P_{\text{O}_2} = X \cdot P_{total} = (1/3)(1.5) = 0.50\text{ atm}$. Using mass fractions instead of mole fractions would give a wrong answer.
