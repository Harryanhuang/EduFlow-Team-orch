How many times faster does helium (M = 4 g/mol) effuse compared to methane, $\text{CH}_4$ (M = 16 g/mol)?

## Options
A) 1.4 times faster
B) 2.0 times faster
C) 4.0 times faster
D) 16 times faster

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By Graham's law, $\frac{r_{\text{He}}}{r_{\text{CH}_4}} = \sqrt{\frac{M_{\text{CH}_4}}{M_{\text{He}}}} = \sqrt{\frac{16}{4}} = \sqrt{4} = 2.0$. Helium effuses twice as fast as methane. A frequent error is using the ratio of molar masses (16/4 = 4) without taking the square root, which gives the trap answer C.
