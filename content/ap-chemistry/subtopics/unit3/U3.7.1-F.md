What volume does $2.0 \text{ mol}$ of an ideal gas occupy at a pressure of $1.0 \text{ atm}$ and a temperature of $273 \text{ K}$? (Use $R = 0.0821 \text{ L·atm·mol}^{-1}\text{·K}^{-1}$.)

## Options
A) $11.2 \text{ L}$
B) $22.4 \text{ L}$
C) $44.8 \text{ L}$
D) $89.6 \text{ L}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Solve the ideal gas law for volume: $V = nRT/P = (2.0)(0.0821)(273)/(1.0) = 44.8\text{ L}$. One mole at STP occupies 22.4 L, so two moles occupy twice that. The temperature is already in kelvin, so no conversion is needed here.
