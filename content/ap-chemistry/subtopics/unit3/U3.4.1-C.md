A chemist observes that a molecule absorbs infrared radiation but not ultraviolet radiation in the studied range. What does this most likely indicate about the molecule's energy transitions?

## Options
A) The molecule undergoes electronic transitions that require relatively low energy.
B) The energy gap for the molecule's bond vibrations matches IR photon energies, while its electronic transitions require more energy than the UV photons supplied.
C) The molecule has no allowed transitions of any kind.
D) IR photons have higher energy than UV photons for this molecule.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Infrared photons carry the right amount of energy to excite molecular bond vibrations, so absorption in the IR indicates vibrational transitions are occurring. The absence of UV absorption means the molecule's electronic transition energy gaps are larger than the UV photon energies provided in the studied range, so no electronic excitation happens there. IR photons always have lower energy than UV photons (longer wavelength), eliminating option D.
