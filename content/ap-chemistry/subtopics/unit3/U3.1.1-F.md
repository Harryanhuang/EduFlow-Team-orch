Which intermolecular force is responsible for the unusually high boiling point of water ($\text{H}_2\text{O}$) compared to other small molecules of similar molar mass?

## Options
A) London dispersion forces
B) Dipole-dipole interactions
C) Hydrogen bonding
D) Ion-dipole interactions

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Water forms hydrogen bonds because each O–H bond places hydrogen between two electronegative oxygen atoms, creating strong intermolecular attractions. These hydrogen bonds are significantly stronger than the dipole-dipole or London dispersion forces found in comparable molecules such as $\text{H}_2\text{S}$, explaining water's anomalously high boiling point of 100 °C. Ion-dipole forces require an ion and are not relevant here.
