A photon of green light has a wavelength of $500 \text{ nm}$. What is the energy of this photon? (Use $h = 6.63 \times 10^{-34}\text{ J·s}$, $c = 3.00 \times 10^{8}\text{ m/s}$.)

## Options
A) $4.0 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$
B) $4.0 \times 10^{-28} \text{ J}$
C) $3.3 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}$
D) $1.3 \times 10^{-27} \text{ J}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Convert the wavelength to meters: $500\text{ nm} = 5.00\times10^{-7}\text{ m}$. Then $E = hc/\lambda = (6.63\times10^{-34})(3.00\times10^{8})/(5.00\times10^{-7}) = 1.989\times10^{-25}/5.00\times10^{-7} = 3.98\times10^{-19}\text{ J} \approx 4.0\times10^{-19}\text{ J}$. The common error is using $\lambda = 500\text{ m}$ or $500\times10^{-9}$ incorrectly; the nm-to-m conversion is essential.
