What is the net ionic equation for the neutralization reaction between aqueous $\mathrm{HNO_3}$ (a strong acid) and aqueous $\mathrm{KOH}$ (a strong base)?

## Options
A. $\mathrm{H^+(aq) + OH^-(aq) \rightarrow H_2O(l)}$
B. $\mathrm{HNO_3(aq) + KOH(aq) \rightarrow KNO_3(aq) + H_2O(l)}$
C. $\mathrm{K^+(aq) + NO_3^-(aq) \rightarrow KNO_3(s)}$
D. $\mathrm{H^+(aq) + NO_3^-(aq) \rightarrow HNO_3(aq)}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Both $\mathrm{HNO_3}$ and $\mathrm{KOH}$ are strong electrolytes and dissociate fully. The complete ionic equation is:

$$\mathrm{H^+(aq) + NO_3^-(aq) + K^+(aq) + OH^-(aq) \rightarrow K^+(aq) + NO_3^-(aq) + H_2O(l)}$$

The spectator ions $\mathrm{K^+}$ and $\mathrm{NO_3^-}$ cancel, leaving:

$$\mathrm{H^+(aq) + OH^-(aq) \rightarrow H_2O(l)}$$

This is the characteristic net ionic equation for any strong acid–strong base neutralization.

Option B is the molecular equation.
Option C shows soluble $\mathrm{KNO_3}$ incorrectly as a solid.
Option D reverses dissociation of the strong acid.
