A particulate diagram shows the reaction of hydrogen and nitrogen to form ammonia. Before reaction: 3 molecules of $\mathrm{H_2}$ and 1 molecule of $\mathrm{N_2}$. After reaction: 2 molecules of $\mathrm{NH_3}$. Which balanced equation correctly represents this particulate diagram?

## Options
A. $\mathrm{N_2 + H_2 \rightarrow NH_3}$
B. $\mathrm{N_2 + 3H_2 \rightarrow 2NH_3}$
C. $\mathrm{2N_2 + 3H_2 \rightarrow 2NH_3}$
D. $\mathrm{N_2 + 3H_2 \rightarrow 3NH_3}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The diagram shows 1 $\mathrm{N_2}$ molecule (2 N atoms) and 3 $\mathrm{H_2}$ molecules (6 H atoms) forming 2 $\mathrm{NH_3}$ molecules (2 N atoms and 6 H atoms).

Checking conservation of atoms in $\mathrm{N_2 + 3H_2 \rightarrow 2NH_3}$:
- N: 2 on left, 2 on right ✓
- H: 6 on left, 6 on right ✓

This matches the particle counts in the diagram.

Option A is not balanced (atoms do not match).
Option C uses 2 $\mathrm{N_2}$, which does not match the single $\mathrm{N_2}$ shown.
Option D produces 3 $\mathrm{NH_3}$, which would require more nitrogen than available.
