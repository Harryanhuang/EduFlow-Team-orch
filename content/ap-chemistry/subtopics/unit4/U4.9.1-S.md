Consider the reaction: $\mathrm{Zn(s) + Cu^{2+}(aq) \rightarrow Zn^{2+}(aq) + Cu(s)}$. Which statement is correct?

## Options
A. $\mathrm{Zn}$ is oxidized and is the reducing agent
B. $\mathrm{Zn}$ is reduced and is the oxidizing agent
C. $\mathrm{Cu^{2+}}$ is oxidized and is the reducing agent
D. $\mathrm{Cu^{2+}}$ is the reducing agent and $\mathrm{Zn}$ is the oxidizing agent

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Track the oxidation numbers:
- $\mathrm{Zn}$ goes from 0 (free element) to $+2$ in $\mathrm{Zn^{2+}}$: an increase, so $\mathrm{Zn}$ is oxidized (loses electrons).
- $\mathrm{Cu^{2+}}$ goes from $+2$ to 0 in $\mathrm{Cu}$: a decrease, so $\mathrm{Cu^{2+}}$ is reduced (gains electrons).

The species that is oxidized is the reducing agent, so $\mathrm{Zn}$ is the reducing agent. The species reduced is the oxidizing agent, so $\mathrm{Cu^{2+}}$ is the oxidizing agent.

Option A correctly states $\mathrm{Zn}$ is oxidized and is the reducing agent.

Option B reverses the direction of change.
Option C wrongly states $\mathrm{Cu^{2+}}$ is oxidized.
Option D swaps the agent roles.
