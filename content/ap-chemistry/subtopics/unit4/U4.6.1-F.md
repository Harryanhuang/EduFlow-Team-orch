In the titration of hydrochloric acid with sodium hydroxide ($\mathrm{HCl + NaOH \rightarrow NaCl + H_2O}$), which statement best describes the equivalence point?

## Options
A. The moles of added $\mathrm{OH^-}$ equal the moles of $\mathrm{H^+}$ originally present
B. The volume of base added equals the volume of acid
C. All of the indicator has been consumed
D. The base is in large excess

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The equivalence point is defined by stoichiometry: it is reached when the moles of titrant added exactly react with the moles of analyte present. For the 1:1 reaction of $\mathrm{HCl}$ and $\mathrm{NaOH}$, this means:

$$\text{moles } \mathrm{OH^-}\text{ added} = \text{moles } \mathrm{H^+}\text{ originally present}$$

Option B is wrong because equal volumes only coincide with the equivalence point if the concentrations are also equal.
Option C describes an indicator misconception; the indicator signals the endpoint but is not "consumed."
Option D describes a point past equivalence.
