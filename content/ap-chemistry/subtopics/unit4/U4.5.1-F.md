For the reaction $\mathrm{2H_2(g) + O_2(g) \rightarrow 2H_2O(l)}$, how many moles of $\mathrm{H_2O}$ are produced from the complete reaction of 4.0 mol of $\mathrm{H_2}$ (with excess $\mathrm{O_2}$)?

## Options
A. 2.0 mol
B. 4.0 mol
C. 6.0 mol
D. 8.0 mol

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The balanced equation shows a 2:2 (i.e., 1:1) mole ratio between $\mathrm{H_2}$ and $\mathrm{H_2O}$:

$$4.0\ \mathrm{mol\ H_2} \times \frac{2\ \mathrm{mol\ H_2O}}{2\ \mathrm{mol\ H_2}} = 4.0\ \mathrm{mol\ H_2O}$$

Since the ratio of $\mathrm{H_2}$ to $\mathrm{H_2O}$ is 1:1, the moles of water equal the moles of hydrogen reacted.

Option A halves the amount incorrectly (uses a 2:1 ratio).
Option C and D apply incorrect ratios.
