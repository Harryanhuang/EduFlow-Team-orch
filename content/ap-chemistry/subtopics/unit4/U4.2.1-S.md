Aqueous barium chloride, $\mathrm{BaCl_2}$, is mixed with aqueous sodium sulfate, $\mathrm{Na_2SO_4}$. Using solubility rules, which is the correct net ionic equation?

## Options
A. $\mathrm{BaCl_2(aq) + Na_2SO_4(aq) \rightarrow BaSO_4(s) + 2NaCl(aq)}$
B. $\mathrm{Ba^{2+}(aq) + SO_4^{2-}(aq) \rightarrow BaSO_4(s)}$
C. $\mathrm{2Na^+(aq) + 2Cl^-(aq) \rightarrow 2NaCl(s)}$
D. $\mathrm{Ba^{2+}(aq) + 2Cl^-(aq) \rightarrow BaCl_2(s)}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By solubility rules, $\mathrm{BaSO_4}$ is one of the insoluble sulfates (along with $\mathrm{PbSO_4}$ and $\mathrm{CaSO_4}$), while $\mathrm{NaCl}$ is soluble. The complete ionic equation is:

$$\mathrm{Ba^{2+}(aq) + 2Cl^-(aq) + 2Na^+(aq) + SO_4^{2-}(aq) \rightarrow BaSO_4(s) + 2Na^+(aq) + 2Cl^-(aq)}$$

Canceling the spectator ions $\mathrm{Na^+}$ and $\mathrm{Cl^-}$ leaves:

$$\mathrm{Ba^{2+}(aq) + SO_4^{2-}(aq) \rightarrow BaSO_4(s)}$$

Option A is the molecular equation.
Option C incorrectly shows soluble $\mathrm{NaCl}$ as a solid.
Option D reverses the reaction; $\mathrm{BaCl_2}$ is soluble and stays as ions.
