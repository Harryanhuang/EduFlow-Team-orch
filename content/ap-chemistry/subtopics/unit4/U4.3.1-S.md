For the reaction $\mathrm{2CO(g) + O_2(g) \rightarrow 2CO_2(g)}$, a container initially holds 6 molecules of $\mathrm{CO}$ and 4 molecules of $\mathrm{O_2}$. After the reaction goes to completion, what does the particulate mixture contain?

## Options
A. 6 $\mathrm{CO_2}$ and 1 $\mathrm{O_2}$
B. 6 $\mathrm{CO_2}$ and 4 $\mathrm{O_2}$
C. 3 $\mathrm{CO_2}$ and 1 $\mathrm{O_2}$
D. 6 $\mathrm{CO_2}$ only

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The equation requires $\mathrm{CO}$ and $\mathrm{O_2}$ to react in a 2:1 ratio. With 6 $\mathrm{CO}$ molecules, the amount of $\mathrm{O_2}$ needed is:

$$6\ \mathrm{CO} \times \frac{1\ \mathrm{O_2}}{2\ \mathrm{CO}} = 3\ \mathrm{O_2}$$

Only 3 of the 4 $\mathrm{O_2}$ molecules are consumed, so $\mathrm{CO}$ is limiting and 1 $\mathrm{O_2}$ remains in excess.

Product formed: $6\ \mathrm{CO} \times \frac{2\ \mathrm{CO_2}}{2\ \mathrm{CO}} = 6\ \mathrm{CO_2}$.

Final mixture: 6 $\mathrm{CO_2}$ and 1 leftover $\mathrm{O_2}$.

Option B ignores that $\mathrm{O_2}$ is consumed.
Option C undercounts the $\mathrm{CO_2}$ produced.
Option D forgets the leftover $\mathrm{O_2}$.
