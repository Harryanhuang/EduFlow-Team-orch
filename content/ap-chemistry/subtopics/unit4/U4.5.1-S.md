Consider the combustion of methane: $\mathrm{CH_4(g) + 2O_2(g) \rightarrow CO_2(g) + 2H_2O(g)}$. What mass of $\mathrm{CO_2}$ is produced when 8.0 g of $\mathrm{CH_4}$ burns completely in excess oxygen?

(Molar masses: $\mathrm{CH_4}$ = 16.0 g/mol, $\mathrm{CO_2}$ = 44.0 g/mol)

## Options
A. 11 g
B. 22 g
C. 44 g
D. 88 g

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Step 1: Convert mass of $\mathrm{CH_4}$ to moles:

$$n_{\mathrm{CH_4}} = \frac{8.0\ \mathrm{g}}{16.0\ \mathrm{g/mol}} = 0.50\ \mathrm{mol}$$

Step 2: Apply the mole ratio (1:1 between $\mathrm{CH_4}$ and $\mathrm{CO_2}$):

$$0.50\ \mathrm{mol\ CH_4} \times \frac{1\ \mathrm{mol\ CO_2}}{1\ \mathrm{mol\ CH_4}} = 0.50\ \mathrm{mol\ CO_2}$$

Step 3: Convert moles of $\mathrm{CO_2}$ to mass:

$$m_{\mathrm{CO_2}} = 0.50\ \mathrm{mol} \times 44.0\ \mathrm{g/mol} = 22\ \mathrm{g}$$

Option A results from forgetting to apply the molar mass correctly.
Option C assumes 1.0 mol of $\mathrm{CO_2}$.
Option D uses an incorrect doubled ratio.
