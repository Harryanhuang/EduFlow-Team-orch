In acidic solution, the dichromate ion oxidizes $\mathrm{Fe^{2+}}$:

$$\mathrm{Cr_2O_7^{2-} + Fe^{2+} + H^+ \rightarrow Cr^{3+} + Fe^{3+} + H_2O}$$

When this equation is correctly balanced with the smallest whole-number coefficients, how many moles of $\mathrm{Fe^{2+}}$ react per mole of $\mathrm{Cr_2O_7^{2-}}$?

## Options
A. 2
B. 3
C. 6
D. 14

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Write the two half-reactions.

Reduction (each Cr goes from $+6$ to $+3$, gaining 3 e⁻; two Cr atoms gain 6 e⁻):

$$\mathrm{Cr_2O_7^{2-} + 14H^+ + 6e^- \rightarrow 2Cr^{3+} + 7H_2O}$$

Oxidation ($\mathrm{Fe^{2+} \rightarrow Fe^{3+}}$, losing 1 e⁻):

$$\mathrm{Fe^{2+} \rightarrow Fe^{3+} + e^-}$$

To equalize electrons, multiply the iron half-reaction by 6 so that 6 e⁻ are transferred in each half-reaction. Adding them:

$$\mathrm{Cr_2O_7^{2-} + 14H^+ + 6Fe^{2+} \rightarrow 2Cr^{3+} + 7H_2O + 6Fe^{3+}}$$

The balanced equation shows 6 mol $\mathrm{Fe^{2+}}$ per 1 mol $\mathrm{Cr_2O_7^{2-}}$.

Option A and B come from not fully equalizing the 6 electrons gained by dichromate.
Option D is the $\mathrm{H^+}$ coefficient, not the $\mathrm{Fe^{2+}}$ coefficient.
