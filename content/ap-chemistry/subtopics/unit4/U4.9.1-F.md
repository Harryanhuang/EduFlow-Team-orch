What is the oxidation number of sulfur in the sulfate ion, $\mathrm{SO_4^{2-}}$?

## Options
A. $-2$
B. $+2$
C. $+4$
D. $+6$

## Answer
D

## Explanation
Each oxygen is assigned $-2$. With four oxygens, their total contribution is $4 \times (-2) = -8$.

Let the oxidation number of sulfur be $x$. The sum of oxidation numbers must equal the charge of the ion, $-2$:

$$x + (-8) = -2$$

$$x = +6$$

So sulfur has an oxidation number of $+6$ in $\mathrm{SO_4^{2-}}$.

Option A is the charge contribution of a single oxygen, not sulfur.
Option B and C result from miscounting the oxygen total or the ion charge.
