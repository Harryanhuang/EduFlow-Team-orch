What volume of $0.150\ \mathrm{M}$ $\mathrm{NaOH}$ is required to completely neutralize 25.0 mL of $0.200\ \mathrm{M}$ $\mathrm{HCl}$? The reaction is $\mathrm{HCl + NaOH \rightarrow NaCl + H_2O}$.

## Options
A. 18.8 mL
B. 25.0 mL
C. 33.3 mL
D. 50.0 mL

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Because $\mathrm{HCl}$ and $\mathrm{NaOH}$ react in a 1:1 ratio, use $M_a V_a = M_b V_b$:

$$(0.200\ \mathrm{M})(25.0\ \mathrm{mL}) = (0.150\ \mathrm{M})(V_b)$$

Solve for $V_b$:

$$V_b = \frac{(0.200)(25.0)}{0.150} = \frac{5.00}{0.150} = 33.3\ \mathrm{mL}$$

Since the base is more dilute than the acid, more than 25.0 mL is needed, which matches 33.3 mL.

Option A inverts the ratio of concentrations.
Option B incorrectly assumes equal volumes.
Option D doubles the required volume.
