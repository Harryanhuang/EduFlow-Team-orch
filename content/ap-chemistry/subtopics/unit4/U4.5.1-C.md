Aluminum reacts with chlorine gas: $\mathrm{2Al(s) + 3Cl_2(g) \rightarrow 2AlCl_3(s)}$. A mixture of 5.40 g of $\mathrm{Al}$ and 21.3 g of $\mathrm{Cl_2}$ is reacted, and 22.5 g of $\mathrm{AlCl_3}$ is collected. What is the percent yield?

(Molar masses: $\mathrm{Al}$ = 27.0 g/mol, $\mathrm{Cl_2}$ = 71.0 g/mol, $\mathrm{AlCl_3}$ = 133.5 g/mol)

## Options
A. 67.5%
B. 84.3%
C. 75.0%
D. 90.0%

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Step 1: Convert reactants to moles.

$$n_{\mathrm{Al}} = \frac{5.40}{27.0} = 0.200\ \mathrm{mol}, \qquad n_{\mathrm{Cl_2}} = \frac{21.3}{71.0} = 0.300\ \mathrm{mol}$$

Step 2: Identify the limiting reactant by the $\mathrm{AlCl_3}$ each could form.

From $\mathrm{Al}$: $0.200 \times \frac{2}{2} = 0.200\ \mathrm{mol\ AlCl_3}$

From $\mathrm{Cl_2}$: $0.300 \times \frac{2}{3} = 0.200\ \mathrm{mol\ AlCl_3}$

Both give 0.200 mol, so the reactants are present in exact stoichiometric ratio; theoretical yield is 0.200 mol $\mathrm{AlCl_3}$.

Step 3: Theoretical mass:

$$0.200\ \mathrm{mol} \times 133.5\ \mathrm{g/mol} = 26.7\ \mathrm{g}$$

Step 4: Percent yield:

$$\frac{22.5\ \mathrm{g}}{26.7\ \mathrm{g}} \times 100\% = 84.3\%$$

Option A divides by an incorrect theoretical value.
Options C and D result from rounding or ratio errors.
