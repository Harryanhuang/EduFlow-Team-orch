Equal volumes of $0.20\ \mathrm{M}$ $\mathrm{Pb(NO_3)_2}$ and $0.20\ \mathrm{M}$ $\mathrm{KI}$ are mixed, forming the precipitate $\mathrm{PbI_2}$. The net ionic equation is $\mathrm{Pb^{2+}(aq) + 2I^-(aq) \rightarrow PbI_2(s)}$. Which ion is present in the greatest concentration in the final solution?

## Options
A. $\mathrm{Pb^{2+}}$
B. $\mathrm{I^-}$
C. $\mathrm{NO_3^-}$
D. $\mathrm{PbI_2}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Let each solution have volume $V$. Moles before mixing: $\mathrm{Pb^{2+}} = 0.20V$, $\mathrm{I^-} = 0.20V$, $\mathrm{NO_3^-} = 0.40V$ (two per formula unit), $\mathrm{K^+} = 0.20V$.

The reaction requires a 1:2 ratio of $\mathrm{Pb^{2+}}$ to $\mathrm{I^-}$. With only $0.20V$ mol $\mathrm{I^-}$, the iodide is the limiting reactant: it consumes $0.10V$ mol $\mathrm{Pb^{2+}}$, leaving $0.20V - 0.10V = 0.10V$ mol $\mathrm{Pb^{2+}}$ in excess.

Remaining ions in solution: $\mathrm{NO_3^-} = 0.40V$, $\mathrm{Pb^{2+}} = 0.10V$, $\mathrm{K^+} = 0.20V$, $\mathrm{I^-} \approx 0$.

The nitrate ion ($0.40V$ mol) is the most abundant, so $\mathrm{NO_3^-}$ has the greatest concentration.

Option A: $\mathrm{Pb^{2+}}$ is present but at lower amount ($0.10V$).
Option B: $\mathrm{I^-}$ is essentially fully consumed.
Option D: $\mathrm{PbI_2}$ is a solid precipitate, not a dissolved ion.
