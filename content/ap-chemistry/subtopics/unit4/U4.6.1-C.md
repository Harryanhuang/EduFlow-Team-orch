A 20.0 mL sample of sulfuric acid, $\mathrm{H_2SO_4}$, is titrated to the equivalence point with 36.0 mL of $0.250\ \mathrm{M}$ $\mathrm{NaOH}$. The reaction is $\mathrm{H_2SO_4 + 2NaOH \rightarrow Na_2SO_4 + 2H_2O}$. What is the molarity of the $\mathrm{H_2SO_4}$ solution?

## Options
A. 0.113 M
B. 0.225 M
C. 0.450 M
D. 0.900 M

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Step 1: Calculate moles of $\mathrm{NaOH}$:

$$n_{\mathrm{NaOH}} = 0.250\ \mathrm{M} \times 0.0360\ \mathrm{L} = 9.00 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{mol}$$

Step 2: Apply the mole ratio. Each $\mathrm{H_2SO_4}$ reacts with 2 $\mathrm{NaOH}$, so:

$$n_{\mathrm{H_2SO_4}} = 9.00 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{mol\ NaOH} \times \frac{1\ \mathrm{mol\ H_2SO_4}}{2\ \mathrm{mol\ NaOH}} = 4.50 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{mol}$$

Step 3: Divide by the acid volume:

$$M_{\mathrm{H_2SO_4}} = \frac{4.50 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{mol}}{0.0200\ \mathrm{L}} = 0.225\ \mathrm{M}$$

Option A halves the value an extra time.
Option C ignores the 1:2 ratio (treats it as 1:1).
Option D doubles the result incorrectly.
