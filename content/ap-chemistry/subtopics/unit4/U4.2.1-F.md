When aqueous silver nitrate, $\mathrm{AgNO_3}$, is mixed with aqueous sodium chloride, $\mathrm{NaCl}$, a white precipitate of $\mathrm{AgCl}$ forms. What is the correct net ionic equation for this reaction?

## Options
A. $\mathrm{Ag^+(aq) + Cl^-(aq) \rightarrow AgCl(s)}$
B. $\mathrm{AgNO_3(aq) + NaCl(aq) \rightarrow AgCl(s) + NaNO_3(aq)}$
C. $\mathrm{Na^+(aq) + NO_3^-(aq) \rightarrow NaNO_3(aq)}$
D. $\mathrm{Ag^+(aq) + NO_3^-(aq) \rightarrow AgNO_3(s)}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The complete ionic equation is:

$$\mathrm{Ag^+(aq) + NO_3^-(aq) + Na^+(aq) + Cl^-(aq) \rightarrow AgCl(s) + Na^+(aq) + NO_3^-(aq)}$$

The spectator ions $\mathrm{Na^+}$ and $\mathrm{NO_3^-}$ appear unchanged on both sides and are canceled. The net ionic equation includes only the species that combine to form the precipitate:

$$\mathrm{Ag^+(aq) + Cl^-(aq) \rightarrow AgCl(s)}$$

Option B is the molecular (formula) equation, not the net ionic equation.
Option C describes only the spectator ions.
Option D is incorrect because $\mathrm{AgNO_3}$ is soluble and stays dissociated.
