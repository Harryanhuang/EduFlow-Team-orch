For the combustion of methane:

$$\mathrm{CH_4}(g) + 2\,\mathrm{O_2}(g) \rightarrow \mathrm{CO_2}(g) + 2\,\mathrm{H_2O}(g)$$

the standard entropy change is $\Delta S°_{rxn} = -4.9 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$. Using the values below, determine the standard molar entropy of $\mathrm{H_2O}(g)$.

| Substance | $S°$ (J/(mol·K)) |
|-----------|------------------|
| $\mathrm{CH_4}(g)$ | 186.3 |
| $\mathrm{O_2}(g)$  | 205.0 |
| $\mathrm{CO_2}(g)$ | 213.8 |
| $\mathrm{H_2O}(g)$ | ?     |

## Options
A. $188.8 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$
B. $377.6 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$
C. $94.4 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$
D. $193.0 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Start from the entropy summation:

$$\Delta S°_{rxn} = \left[ S°(\mathrm{CO_2}) + 2\,S°(\mathrm{H_2O}) \right] - \left[ S°(\mathrm{CH_4}) + 2\,S°(\mathrm{O_2}) \right]$$

Substitute the known values, letting $x = S°(\mathrm{H_2O})$:

$$-4.9 = \left[ 213.8 + 2x \right] - \left[ 186.3 + 2(205.0) \right]$$

$$-4.9 = 213.8 + 2x - 596.3$$

$$-4.9 = 2x - 382.5$$

Solve for $x$:

$$2x = -4.9 + 382.5 = 377.6$$

$$x = 188.8 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$$

Option B forgets to divide by the coefficient of 2. Option C divides an extra time. Option D drops the coefficient on $\mathrm{O_2}$.
