For a certain reaction at 298 K, $\Delta H = +28.0 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$ and $\Delta S = +110.0 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$. Calculate $\Delta G$ at this temperature.

## Options
A. $-4.78 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$
B. $+60.8 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$
C. $-32{,}752 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$
D. $+28.0 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Apply the Gibbs free energy equation:

$$\Delta G = \Delta H - T\Delta S$$

First convert $\Delta S$ to kJ to match $\Delta H$:

$$\Delta S = 110.0 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)} = 0.1100 \ \mathrm{kJ/(mol \cdot K)}$$

Substitute:

$$\Delta G = 28.0 - (298)(0.1100)$$

$$\Delta G = 28.0 - 32.78 = -4.78 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$$

Since $\Delta G < 0$, the reaction is spontaneous at 298 K. This is an entropy-driven reaction: although it is endothermic ($\Delta H > 0$), the favorable entropy term ($-T\Delta S < 0$) makes it favorable at this temperature.

Option B adds instead of subtracts the $T\Delta S$ term.
Option C fails to convert $\Delta S$ from J to kJ.
Option D ignores the entropy term entirely.
