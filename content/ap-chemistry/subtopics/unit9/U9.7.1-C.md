Molten aluminum oxide is electrolyzed to produce aluminum metal and oxygen gas. The two half-reactions are:

$$\mathrm{Al^{3+}} + 3e^- \rightarrow \mathrm{Al} \qquad 2\,\mathrm{O^{2-}} \rightarrow \mathrm{O_2} + 4e^-$$

Which statement correctly describes this electrolytic cell?

## Options
A. $\mathrm{Al^{3+}}$ is reduced at the cathode; the balanced reaction transfers 12 electrons.
B. $\mathrm{Al^{3+}}$ is oxidized at the anode; the balanced reaction transfers 6 electrons.
C. $\mathrm{O^{2-}}$ is reduced at the cathode; the balanced reaction transfers 4 electrons.
D. $\mathrm{Al^{3+}}$ is reduced at the anode; the balanced reaction transfers 3 electrons.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
In an electrolytic cell, oxidation still occurs at the anode and reduction at the cathode.

- $\mathrm{Al^{3+}} + 3e^- \rightarrow \mathrm{Al}$ is a **reduction** (gains electrons), so it occurs at the **cathode**.
- $2\,\mathrm{O^{2-}} \rightarrow \mathrm{O_2} + 4e^-$ is an **oxidation** (loses electrons), so it occurs at the **anode**.

To balance electrons, find the least common multiple of 3 and 4, which is **12**:

$$4 \times (\mathrm{Al^{3+}} + 3e^- \rightarrow \mathrm{Al}) \quad\Rightarrow\quad 4\,\mathrm{Al^{3+}} + 12e^- \rightarrow 4\,\mathrm{Al}$$

$$3 \times (2\,\mathrm{O^{2-}} \rightarrow \mathrm{O_2} + 4e^-) \quad\Rightarrow\quad 6\,\mathrm{O^{2-}} \rightarrow 3\,\mathrm{O_2} + 12e^-$$

Overall: $4\,\mathrm{Al^{3+}} + 6\,\mathrm{O^{2-}} \rightarrow 4\,\mathrm{Al} + 3\,\mathrm{O_2}$, transferring **12 electrons**. Option A is correct.

Option B wrongly oxidizes $\mathrm{Al^{3+}}$. Option C reverses the oxygen half-reaction. Option D places reduction at the anode and fails to balance electrons.
