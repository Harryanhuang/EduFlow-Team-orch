A galvanic cell has a standard cell potential of $E°_{cell} = +0.78 \ \mathrm{V}$. What can be concluded about the sign of $\Delta G°$ for the cell reaction?

## Options
A. $\Delta G° < 0$, so the reaction is spontaneous.
B. $\Delta G° > 0$, so the reaction is non-spontaneous.
C. $\Delta G° = 0$, so the reaction is at equilibrium.
D. The sign of $\Delta G°$ cannot be determined from $E°_{cell}$ alone.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The relationship between standard free energy and cell potential is:

$$\Delta G° = -nFE°_{cell}$$

Here $n$ (moles of electrons) and $F$ (Faraday's constant, 96{,}485 C/mol) are always positive. So the sign of $\Delta G°$ is the opposite of the sign of $E°_{cell}$.

Since $E°_{cell} = +0.78 \ \mathrm{V} > 0$, the product $-nFE°_{cell}$ is negative, giving $\Delta G° < 0$. A negative $\Delta G°$ means the reaction is spontaneous, consistent with a galvanic cell. Option A is correct.

Option B reverses the sign relationship. Option C would require $E°_{cell} = 0$. Option D is incorrect; the sign of $E°_{cell}$ unambiguously fixes the sign of $\Delta G°$.
