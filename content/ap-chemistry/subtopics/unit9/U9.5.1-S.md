At 298 K, a reaction has an equilibrium constant of $K = 1.0 \times 10^{3}$. Calculate the standard free energy change $\Delta G°$. (Use $R = 8.314 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$.)

## Options
A. $-17.1 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$
B. $-7.4 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$
C. $+17.1 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$
D. $-17{,}100 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Use the relationship between standard free energy and the equilibrium constant:

$$\Delta G° = -RT \ln K$$

Substitute $R = 8.314 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$, $T = 298 \ \mathrm{K}$, and $K = 1.0 \times 10^{3}$:

$$\ln(1.0 \times 10^{3}) = 6.908$$

$$\Delta G° = -(8.314)(298)(6.908)$$

$$\Delta G° = -17{,}114 \ \mathrm{J/mol} = -17.1 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$$

The negative $\Delta G°$ is consistent with $K > 1$ (products favored). Option A is correct.

Option B incorrectly uses $\log_{10}K = 3$ instead of $\ln K$. Option C has the wrong sign (forgetting the negative). Option D fails to convert from J to kJ.
