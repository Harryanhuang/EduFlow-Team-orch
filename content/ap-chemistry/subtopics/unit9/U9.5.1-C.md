At 298 K, a reaction has $\Delta H° = -41.2 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$ and $\Delta S° = -42.0 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$. Calculate the equilibrium constant $K$ at this temperature. (Use $R = 8.314 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$.)

## Options
A. $K \approx 7.5 \times 10^{4}$
B. $K \approx 1.3 \times 10^{-5}$
C. $K \approx 28.7$
D. $K \approx 1.0$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
**Step 1 — Calculate $\Delta G°$** using the Gibbs equation (convert $\Delta S°$ to kJ):

$$\Delta S° = -42.0 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)} = -0.0420 \ \mathrm{kJ/(mol \cdot K)}$$

$$\Delta G° = \Delta H° - T\Delta S° = -41.2 - (298)(-0.0420)$$

$$\Delta G° = -41.2 + 12.52 = -28.68 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$$

**Step 2 — Solve for $K$** from $\Delta G° = -RT \ln K$:

$$\ln K = \frac{-\Delta G°}{RT} = \frac{-(-28{,}680 \ \mathrm{J/mol})}{(8.314)(298)} = \frac{28{,}680}{2477.6} = 11.58$$

$$K = e^{11.58} \approx 7.5 \times 10^{4}$$

The large $K$ is consistent with a negative $\Delta G°$ (products favored).

Option B comes from a sign error giving positive $\Delta G°$. Option C uses only $\Delta H°$ and ignores the entropy contribution. Option D would require $\Delta G° \approx 0$.
