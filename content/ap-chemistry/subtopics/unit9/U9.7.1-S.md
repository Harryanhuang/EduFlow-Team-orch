An electrochemical cell has a standard cell potential of $E°_{cell} = -1.10 \ \mathrm{V}$. Which statement best describes this cell?

## Options
A. It is a galvanic cell that spontaneously produces electrical energy.
B. It is an electrolytic cell that requires an external energy source to drive the reaction.
C. It is a galvanic cell with $\Delta G° < 0$.
D. It is at equilibrium and no current flows.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The sign of the standard cell potential reveals whether the cell reaction is spontaneous:

$$\Delta G° = -nFE°_{cell}$$

When $E°_{cell} < 0$, then $\Delta G° > 0$, so the reaction is **non-spontaneous**. A non-spontaneous redox reaction can only proceed if driven by an external power source — this defines an **electrolytic cell**. Option B is correct.

Galvanic (voltaic) cells have $E°_{cell} > 0$ and $\Delta G° < 0$, spontaneously producing electrical energy. So Options A and C are incorrect for a negative potential.

Option D would require $E°_{cell} = 0$ (equilibrium).
