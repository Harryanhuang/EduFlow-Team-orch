A reaction has a Gibbs free energy change of $\Delta G = -45 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$ at 298 K. Which statement best describes this reaction?

## Options
A. The reaction is thermodynamically favorable (spontaneous) as written.
B. The reaction is at equilibrium.
C. The reaction is thermodynamically unfavorable (non-spontaneous) as written.
D. The reaction cannot occur under any conditions.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The sign of the Gibbs free energy change determines thermodynamic favorability:

- $\Delta G < 0$: reaction is **spontaneous (favorable)** as written
- $\Delta G > 0$: reaction is non-spontaneous (favorable in reverse)
- $\Delta G = 0$: system is at equilibrium

Since $\Delta G = -45 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$ is negative, the reaction is thermodynamically favorable as written, making Option A correct.

Option B would require $\Delta G = 0$.
Option C describes a positive $\Delta G$.
Option D is incorrect; a favorable $\Delta G$ does not forbid the reaction. (Note: $\Delta G$ describes favorability, not rate.)
