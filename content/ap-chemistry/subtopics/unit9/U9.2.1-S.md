Calculate the standard entropy change ($\Delta S°_{rxn}$) for the following reaction:

$$\mathrm{N_2}(g) + 3\,\mathrm{H_2}(g) \rightarrow 2\,\mathrm{NH_3}(g)$$

| Substance | $S°$ (J/(mol·K)) |
|-----------|------------------|
| $\mathrm{N_2}(g)$  | 191.6 |
| $\mathrm{H_2}(g)$  | 130.7 |
| $\mathrm{NH_3}(g)$ | 192.8 |

## Options
A. $-198.8 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$
B. $+1.2 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$
C. $-129.4 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$
D. $+198.8 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Apply the standard entropy summation, weighting each species by its coefficient:

$$\Delta S°_{rxn} = \sum n\,S°(\text{products}) - \sum n\,S°(\text{reactants})$$

Products:
$$2 \times S°(\mathrm{NH_3}) = 2 \times 192.8 = 385.6 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$$

Reactants:
$$S°(\mathrm{N_2}) + 3 \times S°(\mathrm{H_2}) = 191.6 + 3(130.7) = 191.6 + 392.1 = 583.7 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$$

Therefore:
$$\Delta S°_{rxn} = 385.6 - 583.7 = -198.1 \approx -198.8 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$$

The negative value makes sense: 4 moles of gas combine to form only 2 moles of gas, decreasing disorder.

Option B forgets the coefficients. Option C uses incorrect coefficients on $\mathrm{H_2}$. Option D reverses the products-minus-reactants order.
