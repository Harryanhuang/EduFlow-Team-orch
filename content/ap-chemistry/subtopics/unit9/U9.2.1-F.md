Which of the following substances is expected to have the **highest** standard molar entropy ($S°$) at 298 K?

## Options
A. $\mathrm{C}$ (diamond, solid)
B. $\mathrm{H_2O}(l)$
C. $\mathrm{NaCl}(s)$
D. $\mathrm{CO_2}(g)$

## Answer
D

## Explanation
Standard molar entropy generally increases in the order solid < liquid < gas, because gas particles have the greatest freedom of motion and the most accessible energy states.

Option D, $\mathrm{CO_2}(g)$, is a gas, so it has far more positional and motional disorder than any of the condensed phases listed. It has the highest $S°$.

Option A (diamond) is a highly ordered covalent network solid with one of the lowest entropies of any substance.
Option B is a liquid, more disordered than solids but far less than a gas.
Option C is an ordered ionic solid.

The single gas-phase species is the highest-entropy choice.
