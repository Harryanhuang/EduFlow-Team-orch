A reaction has a standard free energy change of $\Delta G° > 0$. Which statement correctly describes the equilibrium constant $K$ for this reaction?

## Options
A. $K > 1$, and products are favored at equilibrium.
B. $K < 1$, and reactants are favored at equilibrium.
C. $K = 1$, and neither side is favored.
D. $K = 0$, and no reaction occurs.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The relationship between standard free energy and the equilibrium constant is:

$$\Delta G° = -RT \ln K$$

When $\Delta G° > 0$, the term $-RT\ln K$ must be positive, so $\ln K < 0$, which means $K < 1$. A value of $K < 1$ means reactants are favored at equilibrium. Option B is correct.

The full pattern:
- $\Delta G° < 0 \Rightarrow K > 1$ (products favored)
- $\Delta G° = 0 \Rightarrow K = 1$
- $\Delta G° > 0 \Rightarrow K < 1$ (reactants favored)

Option A describes a negative $\Delta G°$. Option C describes $\Delta G° = 0$. Option D is incorrect because $K$ can be small but is never exactly zero for a real equilibrium.
