Which of the following processes is expected to have a **negative** entropy change ($\Delta S < 0$)?

## Options
A. $\mathrm{H_2O}(l) \rightarrow \mathrm{H_2O}(g)$
B. $2\,\mathrm{NH_3}(g) \rightarrow \mathrm{N_2}(g) + 3\,\mathrm{H_2}(g)$
C. $\mathrm{CO_2}(g) \rightarrow \mathrm{CO_2}(s)$
D. $\mathrm{NaCl}(s) \rightarrow \mathrm{Na^+}(aq) + \mathrm{Cl^-}(aq)$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Entropy increases when disorder increases. The key indicators are phase changes (solid < liquid < gas) and changes in the number of moles of gas.

Option C, $\mathrm{CO_2}(g) \rightarrow \mathrm{CO_2}(s)$, converts a highly disordered gas into a highly ordered solid (deposition). Disorder decreases, so $\Delta S < 0$.

Option A is vaporization (liquid → gas), which increases disorder, so $\Delta S > 0$.
Option B increases the moles of gas from 2 to 4, increasing disorder, so $\Delta S > 0$.
Option D dissolves an ordered ionic solid into dispersed aqueous ions, increasing disorder, so $\Delta S > 0$.
