In a galvanic (voltaic) cell, which statement correctly describes the anode?

## Options
A. The anode is the electrode where reduction occurs.
B. The anode is the electrode where oxidation occurs.
C. The anode is where electrons are gained from the external circuit.
D. The anode is the positive electrode in a galvanic cell.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
In any electrochemical cell, the electrode definitions are fixed by the type of reaction:

- **Anode**: site of **oxidation** (loss of electrons)
- **Cathode**: site of **reduction** (gain of electrons)

A common mnemonic is "**An Ox, Red Cat**" (Anode = Oxidation, Reduction = Cathode). Therefore Option B is correct.

Option A and C describe the cathode (reduction / gaining electrons).
Option D is incorrect for a galvanic cell: in a galvanic cell, oxidation at the anode releases electrons, making the anode the **negative** electrode. (The sign convention reverses in an electrolytic cell, but the anode is always where oxidation occurs.)
