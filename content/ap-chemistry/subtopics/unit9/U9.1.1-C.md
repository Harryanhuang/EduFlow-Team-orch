An exothermic reaction has $\Delta H_{sys} = -92.0 \ \mathrm{kJ/mol}$ and a system entropy change of $\Delta S_{sys} = -199 \ \mathrm{J/(mol \cdot K)}$. At what approximate temperature does the reaction become **non-spontaneous** (i.e., $\Delta S_{univ}$ changes sign)?

## Options
A. 215 K
B. 350 K
C. 462 K
D. 660 K

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The entropy change of the surroundings depends on the heat released and the temperature:

$$\Delta S_{surr} = \frac{-\Delta H_{sys}}{T} = \frac{-(-92{,}000 \ \mathrm{J/mol})}{T} = \frac{92{,}000}{T}$$

The reaction is spontaneous while $\Delta S_{univ} = \Delta S_{sys} + \Delta S_{surr} > 0$. The crossover (boundary) occurs when $\Delta S_{univ} = 0$:

$$\Delta S_{sys} + \frac{92{,}000}{T} = 0$$

$$-199 + \frac{92{,}000}{T} = 0$$

$$T = \frac{92{,}000}{199} = 462 \ \mathrm{K}$$

Below 462 K, $\Delta S_{surr}$ is large enough to keep $\Delta S_{univ} > 0$ (spontaneous). Above 462 K, the negative $\Delta S_{sys}$ dominates and the reaction becomes non-spontaneous.

Note this is equivalent to the Gibbs criterion: $T = \Delta H / \Delta S = 92{,}000 / 199 = 462 \ \mathrm{K}$.

Option A and B use incorrect unit conversions; Option D divides by an incorrect entropy value.
