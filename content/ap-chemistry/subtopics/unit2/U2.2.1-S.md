Two diatomic molecules X₂ and Y₂ are shown on the same potential energy diagram. Molecule X₂ has a PE minimum of −945 kJ/mol at 0.110 nm; molecule Y₂ has a PE minimum of −432 kJ/mol at 0.154 nm. Which conclusion is best supported?

## Options
A) X₂ has a longer and weaker bond than Y₂.
B) X₂ has a shorter and stronger bond than Y₂.
C) Y₂ has more bond pairs than X₂.
D) Both molecules have the same bond order because they are both diatomic.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
X₂ has a deeper PE well (−945 vs −432 kJ/mol), indicating greater bond dissociation energy (stronger bond), and a shorter equilibrium bond length (0.110 vs 0.154 nm). These data are consistent with X₂ having a higher bond order (e.g., a triple bond like N₂ vs a single bond like F₂). Being diatomic says nothing about bond order.
