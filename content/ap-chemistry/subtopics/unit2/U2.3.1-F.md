In the sodium chloride (NaCl) crystal structure, what is the coordination number of Na⁺?

## Options
A) 4
B) 6
C) 8
D) 12

## Answer
B

## Explanation
In the NaCl (rock salt) structure, each Na⁺ is octahedrally coordinated by 6 Cl⁻ ions (one above, one below, and four around it in a plane). Similarly, each Cl⁻ is surrounded by 6 Na⁺ ions. This 6:6 coordination arises from the approximately 1:1 radius ratio of the ions, which favors an octahedral arrangement over the 8-coordinate CsCl structure.
