Which statement best describes a key difference between the potential energy curves for an ionic bond (Na⁺–Cl⁻) and a covalent bond (H–H)?

## Options
A) The ionic curve has a shallower energy well, indicating a weaker bond.
B) The ionic curve shows significant attractive interaction at much larger internuclear distances than the covalent curve.
C) Both curves become repulsive at the same internuclear distance.
D) The covalent curve has a longer equilibrium bond length than the ionic curve.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Coulombic attraction between Na⁺ and Cl⁻ follows a 1/r dependence, which persists over much longer distances than the quantum-mechanical orbital overlap driving covalent bonding in H₂. As a result, the ionic PE curve begins decreasing at significantly larger separations. The energy well for NaCl (ionic) is also deeper per bond, but the most distinctive feature is the longer range of attraction.
