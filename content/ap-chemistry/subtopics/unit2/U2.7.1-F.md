According to VSEPR theory, what is the molecular geometry of methane (CH₄)?

## Options
A) Linear
B) Trigonal planar
C) Tetrahedral
D) Square planar

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The central carbon in CH₄ has four bonding domains and no lone pairs. Four electron domains arrange to minimize repulsion by pointing toward the corners of a tetrahedron, giving bond angles of about 109.5°. The molecular geometry is therefore tetrahedral.
