The perchlorate ion (ClO₄⁻) has 32 total valence electrons. Which Lewis structure best represents ClO₄⁻?

## Options
A) Cl with four single bonds to O (each O has 3 lone pairs); formal charge on Cl = +3
B) Cl with four double bonds to O (each O has 2 lone pairs); formal charge on Cl = −1
C) Cl with one double bond and three single bonds to O; formal charge on Cl = +2
D) Cl with two double bonds and two single bonds to O (two O with 3 lone pairs, two O with 2 lone pairs); formal charge on Cl = +1

## Answer
B

## Explanation
With all single bonds (choice A), each O has FC = 6 − 6 − 2/2 = −1 and Cl has FC = 7 − 0 − 8/2 = +3, giving large formal charges. In choice B, the four Cl=O double bonds give each O an FC of 6 − 4 − 4/2 = 0, while chlorine has 8 bonding pairs (16 bonding electrons): FC(Cl) = 7 − 0 − 16/2 = −1. This expanded-octet structure minimizes formal charges (0 on each O, −1 on Cl), and the −1 on chlorine matches the overall ion charge. By the formal-charge criterion, choice B is the most plausible structure.
