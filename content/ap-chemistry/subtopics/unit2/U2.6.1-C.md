For the thiocyanate ion (SCN⁻), three resonance structures can be drawn: (I) S≡C–N with the negative charge on N; (II) S=C=N with the negative charge on N; (III) S–C≡N... consider instead these two: structure X has formal charges S(0), C(0), N(−1); structure Y has formal charges S(−1), C(0), N(0). Nitrogen is more electronegative than sulfur. Which structure is the more important resonance contributor, and why?

## Options
A) Structure Y, because sulfur is larger and better stabilizes negative charge.
B) Structure X, because the negative formal charge resides on the more electronegative nitrogen atom.
C) Both contribute equally because they have the same number of formal charges.
D) Structure X, because it has more total formal charges than structure Y.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
When two resonance structures have the same number and magnitude of formal charges, the dominant contributor is the one that places negative formal charge on the more electronegative atom. Nitrogen (EN ≈ 3.0) is more electronegative than sulfur (EN ≈ 2.6), so structure X — with the −1 charge on nitrogen — is the more stable and more important contributor. Choice D misstates that X has more formal charges (both have one −1); choice A wrongly favors sulfur; choice C ignores the electronegativity criterion.
