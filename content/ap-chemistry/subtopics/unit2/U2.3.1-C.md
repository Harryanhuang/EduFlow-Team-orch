Compound Q has a cation-to-anion radius ratio of 0.82. Which crystal structure does compound Q most likely adopt, and what is the expected coordination number of the cation?

## Options
A) ZnS (wurtzite) structure; coordination number 4
B) NaCl (rock salt) structure; coordination number 6
C) CsCl structure; coordination number 8
D) Fluorite structure; coordination number 12

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The radius ratio rule predicts CsCl-type packing (body-centered cubic arrangement of cation inside a cube of anions) when r⁺/r⁻ > 0.73. At a ratio of 0.82, the cation is large enough that 8 anions can pack around it without overlapping. CsCl itself (r⁺/r⁻ ≈ 0.93) exemplifies this. Coordination number 8 (cubic coordination) is therefore the expected result for compound Q.
