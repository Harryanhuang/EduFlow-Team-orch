Which of the following is the best Lewis structure for SF₄?

## Options
A) S with four single bonds to F and no lone pairs (total 8 e around S)
B) S with four single bonds to F and one lone pair on S (total 10 e around S)
C) S with two single bonds and one double bond to F atoms and one lone pair on S
D) S with four double bonds to F atoms and no lone pairs

## Answer
B

## Explanation
SF₄ has 34 total valence electrons (6 from S + 4×7 from F). Four S–F single bonds account for 8 electrons; four F atoms each with 3 lone pairs account for 24 electrons, leaving 2 electrons (1 lone pair) on sulfur. The central sulfur therefore has 5 electron domains (4 bonds + 1 lone pair), totaling 10 electrons around S — an expanded octet allowed because sulfur is in the third period. Answer A is wrong (ignores 1 lone pair on S); C and D are incorrect electron allocations.
