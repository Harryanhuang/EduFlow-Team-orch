What is the hybridization of the central bromine atom in bromine pentafluoride (BrF₅), and what is its molecular geometry?

## Options
A) sp³d, trigonal bipyramidal
B) sp³d², octahedral
C) sp³d², square pyramidal
D) sp³d³, pentagonal bipyramidal

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Bromine in BrF₅ has 5 bonding domains (to the five F atoms) plus 1 lone pair, for a total of 6 electron domains. Six domains require sp³d² hybridization (using two d orbitals). The electron-domain geometry is octahedral, but with one lone pair the molecular geometry — named by atom positions only — is square pyramidal. The lone pair occupies one octahedral position, pushing the five F atoms into a square-pyramidal arrangement.
