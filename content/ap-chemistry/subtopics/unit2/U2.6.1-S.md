The nitrate ion (NO₃⁻) is best described by three equivalent resonance structures. What does experimental data show about its three nitrogen–oxygen bonds?

## Options
A) One bond is shorter (double bond) and two are longer (single bonds).
B) All three N–O bonds are identical, with a length between that of a single and double bond.
C) Two bonds are double bonds and one is a single bond.
D) All three bonds are full double bonds.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Although each individual resonance structure of NO₃⁻ shows one N=O double bond and two N–O single bonds, the actual ion is a resonance hybrid in which the π electrons are delocalized equally over all three oxygens. Experimentally, all three N–O bonds are identical, with a bond order of about 1⅓ and a length intermediate between a single and a double bond.
