On a potential energy versus internuclear distance graph for a diatomic molecule, the equilibrium bond length corresponds to which point?

## Options
A) The internuclear distance where potential energy equals zero
B) The internuclear distance at the minimum of the potential energy curve
C) The internuclear distance where the curve first begins to decrease
D) The internuclear distance that maximizes the attractive force

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The equilibrium bond length is the internuclear separation at the minimum of the PE curve, where the net force between the atoms is zero (attractive and repulsive forces balance). The depth of this minimum below the zero-energy baseline equals the bond dissociation energy required to separate the atoms.
