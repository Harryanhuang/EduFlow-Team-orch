A solid conducts electricity well in both solid and liquid states, is malleable, and has a high melting point. Which type of bonding is most consistent with these properties?

## Options
A) Ionic bonding
B) Polar covalent bonding
C) Nonpolar covalent bonding
D) Metallic bonding

## Answer
D

## Explanation
Metallic solids conduct electricity in both the solid and liquid phase because of the sea of delocalized electrons that are free to move regardless of state. Ionic compounds only conduct when melted or dissolved (ions must be mobile). Covalent solids (molecular or network) are generally poor conductors. Malleability is also a hallmark of metallic bonding, as metal layers can slide without breaking the electron sea.
