How many lone pairs of electrons are on the central oxygen atom in a correct Lewis structure of water (H₂O)?

## Options
A) 0
B) 1
C) 2
D) 3

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Oxygen has 6 valence electrons. In H₂O it forms 2 single bonds to the two hydrogen atoms (using 2 electron pairs). The remaining 4 electrons (2 pairs) stay on oxygen as lone pairs. Thus the central oxygen has 2 lone pairs and 2 bonding pairs, for a total of 4 electron pairs — consistent with an sp³ electron geometry.
