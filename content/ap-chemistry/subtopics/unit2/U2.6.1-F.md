In the carbon dioxide molecule O=C=O, where each oxygen has two lone pairs and one double bond to carbon, what is the formal charge on the central carbon atom?

## Options
A) −2
B) −1
C) 0
D) +1

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Carbon has V = 4 valence electrons. In O=C=O, carbon has no lone pairs (N = 0) and forms two double bonds, giving B = 8 bonding electrons. FC = V − N − B/2 = 4 − 0 − 8/2 = 4 − 4 = 0. The central carbon carries no formal charge, which is consistent with the most stable Lewis structure of CO₂.
