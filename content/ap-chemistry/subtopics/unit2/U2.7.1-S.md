Ammonia (NH₃) has a central nitrogen with three bonding pairs and one lone pair. What is its molecular geometry, and how does the H–N–H bond angle compare to the ideal tetrahedral angle?

## Options
A) Tetrahedral; bond angle exactly 109.5°
B) Trigonal planar; bond angle 120°
C) Trigonal pyramidal; bond angle slightly less than 109.5°
D) Bent; bond angle slightly less than 109.5°

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Nitrogen in NH₃ has four electron domains (3 bonds + 1 lone pair), giving a tetrahedral electron geometry. Because one domain is a lone pair, the molecular geometry — defined by atom positions only — is trigonal pyramidal. The lone pair exerts greater repulsion than bonding pairs, compressing the H–N–H angle to about 107°, slightly less than the ideal 109.5°.
