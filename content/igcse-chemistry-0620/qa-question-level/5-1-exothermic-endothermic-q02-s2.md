# QQL: 5.1 — Q-5.1-02 (F)

**Question ID**: Q-5.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Topic**: 5.1 Exothermic and endothermic reactions
**Question**: What is an endothermic reaction? Give one example.
**Answer**: An endothermic reaction absorbs energy from the surroundings. The temperature of the surroundings decreases. Example: thermal decomposition of calcium carbonate (CaCO3 → CaO + CO2) requires continuous heating.
**Explanation**: Students must state that energy is absorbed and temperature decreases. A common error is confusing endothermic with "needs heating to start" — some exothermic reactions also need activation energy.
