# QQL: 11.1 — Q-11.1-09 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-11.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 11.1 Fuels crude oil alkanes
**Question**: Cracking converts long-chain alkanes into shorter, more useful hydrocarbons. (a) Explain why cracking is economically important. (b) Write a balanced equation for the cracking of dodecane (C12H26) to produce octane (C8H18) and one other product. (c) How could you test that the product contains an alkene?
**Answer**: (a) Crude oil contains a surplus of long-chain fractions (less demand) and a shortage of short-chain fractions (high demand for fuels like petrol). Cracking converts the surplus into the more valuable short-chain products.
(b) C12H26 → C8H18 + C4H8 (butene)
(c) Add bromine water — it decolourises from orange/brown to colourless if an alkene is present.
**Explanation**: Students must explain the economic rationale and write a balanced cracking equation. A common error is producing only alkanes (cracking always produces at least one alkene).
