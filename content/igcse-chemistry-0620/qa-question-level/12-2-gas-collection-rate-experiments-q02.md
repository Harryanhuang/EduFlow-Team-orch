# Question: Question Q-12.2-02
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 12.2
**Question**: In a rate experiment, calcium carbonate reacts with hydrochloric acid and the mass of the reaction vessel decreases over time. Explain why and how the rate can be determined.
**Answer**: Carbon dioxide gas is produced and escapes from the open vessel, causing the mass to decrease. The rate can be determined from the gradient of a mass-vs-time graph — a steeper gradient means a faster rate.
**Explanation**: Mass loss = gas escaping. A common error is saying the mass should stay constant.
