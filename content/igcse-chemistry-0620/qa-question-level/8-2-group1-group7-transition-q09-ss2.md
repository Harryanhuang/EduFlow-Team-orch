# QQL: 8.2 — Q-8.2-09 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-8.2-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 8.2 Group I Group VII transition elements
**Question**: A student adds aqueous silver nitrate followed by dilute nitric acid to three separate samples containing chloride, bromide and iodide ions. Predict the observations for each and write the ionic equation for the chloride test. How would adding aqueous ammonia help confirm the identity?
**Answer**: Chloride: white precipitate of AgCl. Bromide: cream precipitate of AgBr. Iodide: yellow precipitate of AgI.
Ionic equation for chloride: Ag⁺(aq) + Cl⁻(aq) → AgCl(s)
Adding dilute aqueous ammonia: AgCl dissolves, AgBr is partially soluble, AgI is insoluble. This confirms the identity of the halide.
**Explanation**: Students need to know the precipitate colours and solubility tests. A common error is confusing the precipitate colours or not mentioning the acidification step (to remove carbonate interference).
