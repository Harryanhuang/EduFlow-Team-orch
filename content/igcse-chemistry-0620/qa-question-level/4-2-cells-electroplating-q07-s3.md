# Question: Question Q-4.2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 4.2
**Question**: Describe the electrolytic purification of copper.
**Answer**: Impure copper is the anode; pure copper is the cathode; copper(II) sulfate is the electrolyte. At the anode: Cu → Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ (impure copper dissolves, impurities fall as anode sludge). At the cathode: Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu (pure copper deposits). The anode loses mass; the cathode gains mass.
**Explanation**: Impure → anode; pure → cathode. A common error is reversing the electrodes.
