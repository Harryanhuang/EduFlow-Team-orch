# Question: Question Q-1.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 1.2
**Question**: What is the purpose of the baseline in paper chromatography? Why is it drawn in pencil?
**Answer**: The baseline marks the starting position of the samples. It is drawn in pencil because pencil graphite does not dissolve in the solvent, whereas ink would dissolve and interfere with the chromatogram.
**Explanation**: Pencil = inert. A common error is saying pencil "shows up better."
