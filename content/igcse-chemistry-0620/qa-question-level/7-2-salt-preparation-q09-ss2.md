# QQL: 7.2 — Q-7.2-09 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-7.2-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 7.2 Salt preparation
**Question**: Ammonium sulfate is a fertiliser made by reacting ammonia with sulfuric acid. (a) Write the balanced equation. (b) Why is this method suitable for making ammonium sulfate on an industrial scale? (c) How would you test that a white solid is indeed ammonium sulfate?
**Answer**: (a) 2NH3(g) + H2SO4(aq) → (NH4)2SO4(aq)
(b) Both reactants are soluble, the reaction goes to completion, and ammonium sulfate crystallises readily from solution. It's a simple neutralisation that can be scaled.
(c) Test for ammonium: add NaOH and warm — ammonia gas is produced (turns damp red litmus blue). Test for sulfate: add acidified BaCl2 — white precipitate of BaSO4 forms.
**Explanation**: Students need to connect the industrial context with the chemistry. Common errors: wrong equation (forgetting the 2:1 ratio) or not testing both ions.
