# Question: Question Q-11.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 11.2
**Question**: What is the difference between addition and condensation polymerisation?
**Answer**: In addition polymerisation, many small molecules (monomers) with C=C double bonds join together to form a long chain without producing any other products. In condensation polymerisation, monomers join together with the loss of a small molecule (usually water or HCl).
**Explanation**: No byproduct vs. small molecule released. A common error is saying addition polymerisation "adds water."
