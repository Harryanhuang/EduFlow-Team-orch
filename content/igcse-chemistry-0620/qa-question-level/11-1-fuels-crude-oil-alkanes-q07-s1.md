# QQL: 11.1 — Q-11.1-07 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-11.1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 11.1 Fuels crude oil alkanes
**Question**: Explain the process of fractional distillation of crude oil. Why do different fractions separate at different heights in the fractionating column?
**Answer**: Crude oil is heated and vaporised. The vapour enters the bottom of a fractionating column that is hotter at the bottom and cooler at the top. As vapour rises, it cools. Fractions with higher boiling points (longer-chain molecules) condense lower down. Fractions with lower boiling points (shorter-chain molecules) rise higher before condensing. Each fraction is collected at its condensation level.
**Explanation**: Students must link chain length to boiling point to column height. A common error is saying fractions separate by "density" rather than boiling point.
