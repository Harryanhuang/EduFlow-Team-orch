# Question: Question Q-3.2-18
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 3.2
**Question**: 10.0 g of calcium carbonate reacts with 50 cm³ of 2.0 mol/dm³ hydrochloric acid. Determine the limiting reactant. (Ca = 40, C = 12, O = 16)
**Answer**: CaCO₃ moles = 10.0/100 = 0.10 mol. HCl moles = 2.0 × 0.050 = 0.10 mol. Equation: CaCO₃ + 2HCl → CaCl₂ + H₂O + CO₂. Ratio: 1 mol CaCO₃ needs 2 mol HCl. 0.10 mol CaCO₃ needs 0.20 mol HCl but only 0.10 mol is available. HCl is the limiting reactant.
**Explanation**: Compare mole ratios. A common error is comparing moles directly without the stoichiometric ratio.
