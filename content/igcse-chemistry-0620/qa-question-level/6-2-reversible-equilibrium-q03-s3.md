# QQL: 6.2 — Q-6.2-03 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-6.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 6.2 Reversible reactions, equilibrium
**Question**: For the reaction N2(g) + 3H2(g) ⇌ 2NH3(g) ΔH = −92 kJ/mol, predict the effect of: (a) increasing temperature, (b) increasing pressure, (c) adding a catalyst.
**Answer**: (a) Increasing temperature shifts equilibrium to the left (endothermic direction) — less ammonia produced.
(b) Increasing pressure shifts equilibrium to the right (fewer moles of gas: 4 → 2) — more ammonia.
(c) A catalyst has no effect on the position of equilibrium — it speeds up both forward and backward reactions equally. It only helps equilibrium to be reached faster.
**Explanation**: Students must apply Le Chatelier's principle. A common error is saying a catalyst increases yield.
