# QQL: 11.4 — Q-11.4-08 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-11.4-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 11.4 Macromolecules
**Question**: The diagram below shows part of a protein chain with three amino acid residues. (a) Identify and draw a circle around one peptide link. (b) Draw the structures of the two amino acids that could have formed the first peptide link. (c) Name the type of polymerisation involved and identify the small molecule produced.
**Answer**: (a) The peptide link is the −CO−NH− group connecting two amino acid residues.
(b) Each amino acid has the general structure H2N−CH(R)−COOH. The specific R groups depend on the amino acids shown.
(c) Condensation polymerisation. Water (H2O) is the small molecule produced.
**Explanation**: This requires identifying structural features and working backwards from polymer to monomer. A common error is not recognising the peptide link structure or drawing the wrong functional groups.
