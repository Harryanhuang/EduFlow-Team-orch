# QQL: 10.1 — Q-10.1-09 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-10.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 10.1 Air water
**Question**: A mixture of liquid nitrogen (bp −196°C) and liquid oxygen (bp −183°C) is gradually warmed in a fractionating column. (a) Which gas is collected first and from which part of the column? Explain your answer in terms of boiling points. (b) If argon (bp −186°C) is also present in the mixture, explain why it is difficult to separate argon from oxygen using simple fractional distillation. (c) Suggest one method to improve the separation of argon from oxygen.
**Answer**: (a) Nitrogen is collected first from the top of the column because it has the lower boiling point (−196°C), so it vaporises first as the temperature rises. (b) Argon (bp −186°C) and oxygen (bp −183°C) have very similar boiling points (only 3°C apart), so they vaporise at nearly the same temperature, making simple fractional distillation ineffective at separating them. (c) Use a taller fractionating column with more theoretical plates to allow more vaporisation-condensation cycles, improving the separation between argon and oxygen.
**Explanation**: Students must understand the relationship between boiling points and separation. A common error is thinking the gas with the higher boiling point is collected first — the lower bp gas vaporises first. The argon complication requires deeper analysis.
