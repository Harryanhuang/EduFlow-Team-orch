# QQL: 9.3 — Q-9.3-08 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-9.3-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 9.3 Corrosion rusting
**Question**: Explain in terms of electron transfer why zinc protects iron from rusting when the two metals are in contact. Include the relevant half-equations in your answer.
**Answer**: Zinc is more reactive than iron (higher in the reactivity series). When zinc and iron are in contact in the presence of water and oxygen, zinc loses electrons more readily: Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻. These electrons are transferred to the iron, preventing iron from losing electrons (Fe → Fe²⁺ + 2e⁻). The iron is cathodically protected because the zinc acts as the anode (sacrificial electrode).
**Explanation**: Students must explain the electrochemical basis of sacrificial protection. A common error is saying zinc "blocks" rusting rather than "gives electrons to iron".
