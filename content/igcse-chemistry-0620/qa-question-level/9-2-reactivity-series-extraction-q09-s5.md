# Question: Question Q-9.2-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 9.2
**Question**: Explain why gold is found native (as pure metal) in the Earth's crust while iron is not.
**Answer**: Gold is very unreactive (low in the reactivity series) and does not readily combine with oxygen or other elements. Iron is more reactive and readily forms compounds (oxides, carbonates) in nature.
**Explanation**: Unreactive = found native. A common error is saying gold "is rare."
