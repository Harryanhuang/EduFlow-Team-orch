# QQL: 4.1 — Q-4.1-05 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-4.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 4.1 Electrolysis: principles and predictions
**Question**: Explain the difference in products between the electrolysis of molten sodium chloride and aqueous sodium chloride.
**Answer**: Molten NaCl: sodium metal at cathode (Na⁺ + e⁻ → Na), chlorine at anode (2Cl⁻ → Cl2 + 2e⁻).
Aqueous NaCl: hydrogen at cathode (H⁺ from water is preferentially discharged), chlorine at anode (Cl⁻ discharged because concentrated).
The difference is at the cathode: water provides H⁺ which is preferentially discharged over Na⁺ in aqueous solution.
**Explanation**: Students must contrast the two cases. A common error is saying the anode product is different — it's chlorine in both cases for concentrated solutions.
