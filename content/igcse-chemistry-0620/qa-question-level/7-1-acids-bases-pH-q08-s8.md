# QQL: 7.1 — Q-7.1-08 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-7.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 7.1 Properties of acids and bases, pH
**Question**: Explain why the reaction between a strong acid and a strong base always has the same ionic equation regardless of which specific acid and base are used. Write this ionic equation. Why is the enthalpy change of neutralisation approximately the same for all strong acid–strong base reactions?
**Answer**: The ionic equation is: H⁺(aq) + OH⁻(aq) → H2O(l)
Strong acids are fully dissociated into H⁺ and anions. Strong bases are fully dissociated into OH⁻ and cations. The anions and cations are spectator ions.
The enthalpy of neutralisation is approximately −57 kJ/mol for all strong acid–strong base reactions because the same reaction is occurring (H⁺ + OH⁻ → H2O). The spectator ions do not participate in the energy change.
**Explanation**: Students must identify the ionic equation and explain why it is universal. A common error is writing the full molecular equation instead of the ionic equation.
