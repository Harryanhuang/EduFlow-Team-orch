# Question: Question Q-8.2-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Topic**: 8.2
**Question**: How does the reactivity of halogens change down the group?
**Answer**: Reactivity decreases down the group. Fluorine is the most reactive and iodine the least. The outer shell is further from the nucleus in larger atoms, making it harder to attract the electron needed for reaction.
**Explanation**: Decreases down the group. A common error is saying it increases (confusing with Group 1).
