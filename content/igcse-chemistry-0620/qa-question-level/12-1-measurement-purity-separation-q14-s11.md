# Question: Question Q-12.1-14
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 12.1
**Question**: A student performs a flame test on an unknown compound and observes a yellow flame. What metal ion is present? What additional test could confirm the identity?
**Answer**: Sodium ion (Na⁺) is present (yellow flame). To confirm: dissolve in water and add NaOH — a white precipitate that does not dissolve in excess confirms Na⁺ (actually Na⁺ doesn't form a precipitate with NaOH; the flame test is the primary test for sodium).
**Explanation**: Yellow = sodium. A common error is saying no further test is possible.
