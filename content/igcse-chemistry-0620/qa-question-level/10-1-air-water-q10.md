# QQL: 10.1 — Q-10.1-10 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-10.1-10
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 10.1 Air water
**Question**: A student is given a colourless liquid and asked to determine whether it is pure water. (a) Describe TWO chemical tests the student could perform and state the expected results if the liquid is water. (b) Explain why a boiling point measurement would provide stronger evidence than the chemical tests alone. (c) The boiling point of the liquid is measured as 102°C. Is this pure water? Explain what this result indicates.
**Answer**: (a) Chemical test 1: Add a few drops to white anhydrous copper(II) sulfate — it turns blue (CuSO4·5H2O forms). Chemical test 2: Dip blue anhydrous cobalt(II) chloride paper — it turns pink (CoCl2·6H2O forms). (b) The chemical tests only detect the PRESENCE of water, not its purity. Any liquid containing water (e.g. salt solution) would give a positive result. Boiling point is specific: pure water boils at exactly 100°C at standard pressure. (c) 102°C is not pure water. The elevated boiling point indicates dissolved substances are present (boiling point elevation). This is an impure water sample.
**Explanation**: Students must distinguish between testing for presence of water and testing for purity. A common error is thinking positive chemical tests prove purity.
