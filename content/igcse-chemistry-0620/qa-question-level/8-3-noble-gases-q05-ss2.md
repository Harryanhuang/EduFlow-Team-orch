# QQL: 8.3 — Q-8.3-05 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-8.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 8.3 Noble gases and uses
**Question**: Explain why noble gases are unreactive. Include reference to their electronic configuration in your answer.
**Answer**: Noble gases have a full outer shell of electrons (helium: 2, others: 8). This is a very stable arrangement. They do not need to lose, gain, or share electrons to achieve stability, so they do not readily form chemical bonds with other elements.
**Explanation**: Students must link the full outer shell to chemical stability. A common error is saying noble gases have "no electrons to share" rather than "a complete outer shell".
