# QQL: 6.2 — Q-6.2-04 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-6.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 6.2 Reversible reactions, equilibrium
**Question**: Explain why the Haber process operates at 450°C and 200 atm rather than at room temperature or very high pressure.
**Answer**: Room temperature would give a higher yield (exothermic forward reaction) but the rate would be too slow. 450°C is a compromise — reasonable yield with acceptable rate. Very high pressure would increase yield but is expensive and dangerous. 200 atm is a practical and economic compromise. An iron catalyst is used to increase the rate.
**Explanation**: Students must balance yield, rate, and economic factors. A common error is saying "high temperature gives high yield" for an exothermic reaction.
