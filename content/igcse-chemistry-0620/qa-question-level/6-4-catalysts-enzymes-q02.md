# Question: Question Q-6.4-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 6.4
**Question**: Explain how enzymes act as biological catalysts.
**Answer**: Enzymes are protein molecules that act as catalysts in living organisms. They have a specific active site shape that substrate molecules fit into (lock and key model). The enzyme-substrate complex lowers the activation energy for the reaction. After the reaction, the enzyme is unchanged and can catalyse another reaction.
**Explanation**: Lock and key, active site, unchanged. A common error is saying enzymes are "used up."
