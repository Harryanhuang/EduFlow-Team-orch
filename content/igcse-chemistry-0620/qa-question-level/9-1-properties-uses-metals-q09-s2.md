# QQL: 9.1 — Q-9.1-09 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-9.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 9.1 Properties and uses metals
**Question**: Stainless steel is an alloy of iron with chromium and nickel. (a) Explain why stainless steel is harder and stronger than pure iron. (b) How does the chromium content protect against rusting? (c) Why is pure iron not used for aircraft bodies?
**Answer**: (a) Different-sized atoms (Cr, Ni) in the alloy distort the regular layers of iron atoms. This makes it harder for layers to slide over each other, increasing hardness and strength.
(b) Chromium forms a thin, protective oxide layer on the surface that prevents oxygen and water reaching the iron underneath.
(c) Pure iron is too soft for aircraft use. It is also dense and rusts easily. Aircraft need lightweight, strong, corrosion-resistant materials like aluminium alloys.
**Explanation**: Students must explain alloy strengthening at the particle level. A common error is saying alloy atoms "bond more strongly" rather than "disrupt the layers".
