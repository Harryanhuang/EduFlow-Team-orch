# QQL: 5.1 — Q-5.1-07 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-5.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 5.1 Exothermic and endothermic reactions
**Question**: A student dissolves ammonium nitrate in water and observes the temperature drops from 22°C to 15°C. (a) Is this process exothermic or endothermic? (b) Explain the observation in terms of energy changes. (c) Suggest one practical use of this type of process.
**Answer**: (a) Endothermic (temperature decreases — energy absorbed from surroundings).
(b) The dissolution of ammonium nitrate requires more energy to break the ionic lattice apart than is released when the ions are hydrated. The net energy is absorbed from the water, lowering its temperature.
(c) Instant cold packs for sports injuries — the endothermic reaction absorbs heat from the affected area, reducing swelling and pain.
**Explanation**: Students must link observation to the energy process. A common error is saying the salt "is cold" rather than "absorbs heat from the water".
