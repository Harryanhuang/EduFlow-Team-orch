# Question: Question Q-1.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Topic**: 1.1
**Question**: Why does a liquid take the shape of its container but keep the same volume?
**Answer**: Liquid particles are close together but can slide past each other, allowing the liquid to flow and take the container's shape. The particles are still in contact, so the volume remains constant.
**Explanation**: Flow ≠ expansion. A common error is saying particles in a liquid are far apart.
