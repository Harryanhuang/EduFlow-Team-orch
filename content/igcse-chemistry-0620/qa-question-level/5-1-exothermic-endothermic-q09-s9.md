# QQL: 5.1 — Q-5.1-09 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-5.1-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 5.1 Exothermic and endothermic reactions
**Question**: Describe an experiment to measure the energy released when burning a fuel such as ethanol. Include a labelled diagram and explain how to calculate the energy released.
**Answer**: Set up: spirit burner with ethanol, copper calorimeter with known mass of water, thermometer, draught shield.
Procedure: weigh burner, record initial water temperature, burn fuel to heat water, record final temperature, reweigh burner.
Calculation: Q = mcΔT (m = mass of water, c = 4.18 J/g°C, ΔT = temperature change).
Energy per gram = Q / mass of fuel burned.
**Explanation**: Students must describe the full experimental setup. A common error is not including the draught shield or not mentioning weighing before and after.
