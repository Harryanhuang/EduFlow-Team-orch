# Question: Question Q-9.3-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 9.3
**Question**: What is galvanising? Explain why it protects iron from rusting even if the coating is scratched.
**Answer**: Galvanising is coating iron with a layer of zinc. The zinc acts as a physical barrier. If scratched, zinc still protects because it is more reactive than iron — zinc corrodes preferentially (sacrificial protection), losing electrons before iron does.
**Explanation**: Barrier + sacrificial. A common error is saying zinc is "less reactive."
