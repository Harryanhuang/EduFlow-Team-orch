# QQL: 7.1 — Q-7.1-06 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-7.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 7.1 Properties of acids and bases, pH
**Question**: Describe how you could use a titration to find the concentration of a solution of sulfuric acid using a standard solution of sodium hydroxide. Include the indicator used and the end point.
**Answer**: 1. Pipette a known volume of NaOH into a conical flask.
2. Add a few drops of suitable indicator (phenolphthalein or methyl orange).
3. Fill a burette with the sulfuric acid solution.
4. Add acid from the burette to the flask, swirling, until the indicator changes colour.
5. Record the volume of acid used.
6. Repeat for accuracy.
Use the balanced equation (2NaOH + H2SO4 → Na2SO4 + 2H2O) and the volumes to calculate the acid concentration.
**Explanation**: Students must describe the full titration procedure. A common error is forgetting the 2:1 mole ratio between NaOH and H2SO4.
