# QQL: 8.2 — Q-8.2-07 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-8.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 8.2 Group I Group VII transition elements
**Question**: Chlorine gas is bubbled through aqueous potassium bromide. (a) State and explain the observation. (b) Write an ionic equation for the reaction. (c) Why does bromine not react with aqueous potassium chloride?
**Answer**: (a) The solution turns orange/brown as bromine is formed. Chlorine is more reactive than bromine and displaces it from potassium bromide.
(b) Cl2(aq) + 2Br⁻(aq) → 2Cl⁻(aq) + Br2(aq)
(c) Bromine is less reactive than chlorine, so it cannot displace chloride ions. A halogen can only displace a less reactive halogen from its salt.
**Explanation**: This requires knowledge of displacement reactions, ionic equations, and reactivity trends. Common errors: wrong equation (not balanced), or stating the wrong colour for bromine solution.
