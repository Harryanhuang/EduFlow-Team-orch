# Question: Question Q-3.2-16
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 3.2
**Question**: What is a limiting reactant? Explain why it determines the amount of product formed.
**Answer**: The limiting reactant is the substance that is completely used up first in a chemical reaction. Once it is consumed, the reaction stops, so no more product can form. Other reactants are left in excess.
**Explanation**: First-to-run-out controls yield. A common error is saying the reactant with the smallest mass is limiting.
