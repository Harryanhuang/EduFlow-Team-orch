# Question: Question Q-2.3-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 2.3
**Question**: Explain why sodium (a Group 1 metal) has a lower melting point than iron (a transition metal).
**Answer**: Sodium has only one delocalised electron per atom and forms a 1+ ion, giving weaker metallic bonding. Iron has more delocalised electrons (from both 4s and 3d orbitals) and forms stronger metallic bonds, giving a higher melting point.
**Explanation**: More delocalised electrons = stronger bonding. A common error is saying sodium has "fewer atoms."
