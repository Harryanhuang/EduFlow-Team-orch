# Question: Question Q-8.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 8.1
**Question**: Describe two observations when sodium reacts with cold water.
**Answer**: Sodium floats and moves quickly on the surface. It may melt into a small ball and produces hydrogen gas while forming sodium hydroxide solution.
**Explanation**: Sodium is less dense than water and reacts exothermically. Common errors include saying sodium sinks or produces oxygen.
