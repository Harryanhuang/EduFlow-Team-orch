# Question: Question Q-5.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 5.2
**Question**: Explain, in terms of activation energy, how a catalyst increases the rate of reaction.
**Answer**: A catalyst provides an alternative reaction pathway with a lower activation energy. More reactant particles now have enough energy to react, so more collisions per unit time are successful.
**Explanation**: Lower activation energy → more successful collisions. A common error is saying catalysts "increase the energy of particles."
