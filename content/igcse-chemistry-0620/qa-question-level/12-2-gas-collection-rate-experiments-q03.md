# Question: Question Q-12.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 12.2
**Question**: How do you test for the presence of ammonium ions (NH₄⁺) in a solution?
**Answer**: Add sodium hydroxide solution and warm gently. Ammonia gas is produced (NH₄⁺ + OH⁻ → NH₃ + H₂O). Test with damp red litmus paper — it turns blue, confirming ammonia.
**Explanation**: NaOH + warm → NH₃ → blue litmus. A common error is not warming the mixture.
