# QQL: 1.1 — Q-1.1-09 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-1.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 1.1 Kinetic particle states
**Question**: A sealed gas syringe contains 50 cm³ of air at 20°C. The syringe is placed in boiling water and the piston moves freely. (a) Predict the new volume of gas and explain your answer in terms of particles. (b) If the piston were fixed (sealed), what would happen to the pressure instead? Explain.
**Answer**: (a) The volume increases. As temperature rises, gas particles gain kinetic energy and move faster. They collide with the piston more forcefully, pushing it outward. By Charles' law, V1/T1 = V2/T2, so V2 = 50 × (373/293) ≈ 63.6 cm³.
(b) If the piston is fixed, the pressure increases instead. Particles move faster and hit the walls more frequently and with greater force, but the volume cannot change.
**Explanation**: Students must apply particle theory to both constant-pressure and constant-volume scenarios. A common error is saying particles "expand" rather than "move faster and collide harder".
