# Question: Question Q-3.2-19
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 3.2
**Question**: 4.6 g of sodium reacts with 3.2 g of sulfur to form sodium sulfide (Na₂S). Which reactant is limiting? (Na = 23, S = 32)
**Answer**: Na moles = 4.6/23 = 0.20. S moles = 3.2/32 = 0.10. Equation: 2Na + S → Na₂S. Need 2 mol Na per 1 mol S. 0.20 mol Na needs 0.10 mol S — exactly the amount available. Neither is limiting (they are in exact stoichiometric ratio).
**Explanation**: Both consumed completely. A common error is picking one anyway.
