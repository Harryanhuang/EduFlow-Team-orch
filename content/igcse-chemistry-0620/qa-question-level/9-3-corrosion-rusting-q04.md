# Question: Question Q-9.3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 9.3
**Question**: Explain why a ship's hull is protected by attaching blocks of zinc.
**Answer**: Zinc is more reactive than iron (higher in the reactivity series). The zinc blocks lose electrons to the iron, so the zinc corrodes (Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻) while the iron is protected. The zinc must be replaced periodically as it is consumed.
**Explanation**: Sacrificial anode, zinc corrodes instead of iron. A common error is saying zinc "repels water."
