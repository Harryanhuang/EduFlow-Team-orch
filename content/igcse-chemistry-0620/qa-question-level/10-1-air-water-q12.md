# QQL: 10.1 — Q-10.1-12 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-10.1-12
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 10.1 Air water
**Question**: Water treatment involves several stages before it is safe to drink. (a) Describe the stages of water treatment and explain the purpose of each. (b) Why is chlorination essential even after filtration? (c) A town's water supply comes from a river downstream of a sewage works. What additional treatment might be needed?
**Answer**: (a) Stages:
1. Screening — removes large debris (sticks, leaves).
2. Sedimentation — fine particles settle in a tank.
3. Filtration through sand/gravel beds — removes remaining solids and some microorganisms.
4. Chlorination — kills remaining bacteria and microorganisms.
(b) Filtration removes solids but not dissolved bacteria and viruses. Chlorination kills these pathogens and also provides residual protection in the pipes.
(c) Additional treatment may include: activated carbon filtration (removes organic pollutants), UV treatment (kills additional pathogens), or advanced oxidation to remove pharmaceutical residues.
**Explanation**: Students must describe all stages. A common error is thinking filtration alone makes water safe to drink.
