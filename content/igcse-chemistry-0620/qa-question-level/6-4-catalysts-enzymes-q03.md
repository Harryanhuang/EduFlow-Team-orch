# Question: Question Q-6.4-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 6.4
**Question**: Why do enzymes stop working at high temperatures?
**Answer**: At high temperatures, the enzyme molecule vibrates more and the bonds holding its shape break. The active site changes shape (denaturation), so the substrate no longer fits. The reaction rate drops to zero. This is irreversible.
**Explanation**: Denaturation = active site shape lost. A common error is saying the enzyme "dies" (enzymes are not alive).
