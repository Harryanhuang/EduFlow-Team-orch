# Question: Question Q-2.3-10
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 2.3
**Question**: Why can metals be bent without breaking?
**Answer**: The layers of positive metal ions can slide over each other while remaining surrounded by the sea of delocalised electrons. The metallic bonding is maintained throughout the deformation.
**Explanation**: Sliding layers + maintained bonding. A common error is saying the bonds "stretch."
