# Question: Question Q-2.4-10
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 2.4
**Question**: Explain why silica (SiO₂) is used to make glass.
**Answer**: Silica has a giant covalent structure that forms a rigid 3D network. When melted and cooled rapidly, it forms an amorphous (non-crystalline) solid — glass. Its hardness and transparency make it useful.
**Explanation**: Giant covalent → can be melted → amorphous solid. A common error is saying silica is "a simple molecule."
