# QQL: 4.1 — Q-4.1-03 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-4.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 4.1 Electrolysis: principles and predictions
**Question**: Predict the products of the electrolysis of molten lead(II) bromide (PbBr2). Write the half-equations for each electrode.
**Answer**: At the cathode: Pb²⁺(l) + 2e⁻ → Pb(l) — lead metal is produced (reduction).
At the anode: 2Br⁻(l) → Br2(g) + 2e⁻ — bromine gas is produced (oxidation).
Molten electrolysis produces the element at each electrode — no water to compete.
**Explanation**: Students must write correct half-equations with state symbols. A common error is writing Br⁻ → Br + e⁻ (not forming Br2 molecules).
