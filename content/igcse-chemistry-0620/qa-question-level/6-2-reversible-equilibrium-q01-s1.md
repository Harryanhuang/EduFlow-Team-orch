# QQL: 6.2 — Q-6.2-01 (F)

**Question ID**: Q-6.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Topic**: 6.2 Reversible reactions, equilibrium
**Question**: What is meant by a reversible reaction? Give one example.
**Answer**: A reversible reaction can proceed in both the forward and backward directions. It is represented by the symbol ⇌. Example: heating hydrated copper(II) sulfate: CuSO4·5H2O(s) ⇌ CuSO4(s) + 5H2O(g). Heating drives off water (forward); adding water reverses it.
**Explanation**: Students must identify the double direction. A common error is confusing reversible reactions with reactions that can be "undone" by a different chemical process.
