# Question: Question Q-11.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Topic**: 11.1
**Question**: What is crude oil and how is it separated into useful fractions?
**Answer**: Crude oil is a mixture of hydrocarbons. It is separated by fractional distillation, based on the different boiling points of the components. The oil is heated, vapours rise up a fractionating column, and different fractions condense at different heights.
**Explanation**: Fractional distillation by boiling point. A common error is saying it is separated by density.
