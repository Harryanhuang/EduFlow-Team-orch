# Question: Question Q-10.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 10.1
**Question**: Describe an experiment to determine the percentage of oxygen in air using copper.
**Answer**: Pass a known volume of air back and forth over heated copper. Copper reacts with oxygen to form copper(II) oxide: 2Cu + O₂ → 2CuO. Measure the decrease in gas volume. The percentage of oxygen = (volume decrease / original volume) × 100 ≈ 21%.
**Explanation**: Copper removes oxygen, volume drop = oxygen volume. A common error is not heating the copper.
