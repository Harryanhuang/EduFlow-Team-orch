# Question: Question Q-2.4-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 2.4
**Question**: Why is graphite able to conduct electricity?
**Answer**: Each carbon atom in graphite forms only three covalent bonds, leaving one delocalised electron per atom. These delocalised electrons are free to move between the layers, allowing electrical conduction.
**Explanation**: Delocalised electrons between layers. A common error is saying graphite conducts because it is "a metal."
