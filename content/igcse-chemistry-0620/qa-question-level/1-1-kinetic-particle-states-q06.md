# Question: Question Q-1.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 1.1
**Question**: During melting, the temperature of a pure substance remains constant even though heat is still being supplied. Explain why.
**Answer**: The energy supplied is used to overcome the attractive forces between particles (latent heat), allowing them to break free from their fixed positions. This energy goes into changing the state, not raising the temperature.
**Explanation**: Energy breaks bonds, not raises KE. A common error is saying "the substance stops absorbing heat."
