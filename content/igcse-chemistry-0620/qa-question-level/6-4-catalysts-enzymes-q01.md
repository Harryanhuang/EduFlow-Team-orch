# Question: Question Q-6.4-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Topic**: 6.4
**Question**: What is a catalyst? Give one example of a catalyst used in an industrial process.
**Answer**: A catalyst is a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without being used up. It is not consumed and can be reused. Example: iron in the Haber process (making ammonia from nitrogen and hydrogen).
**Explanation**: Increases rate, not consumed. A common error is saying a catalyst "makes the reaction happen" (it only speeds up reactions that would happen anyway).
