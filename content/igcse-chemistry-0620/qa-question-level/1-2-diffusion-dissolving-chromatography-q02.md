# Question: Question Q-1.2-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 1.2
**Question**: In an experiment, ammonia gas (Mᵣ = 17) and hydrogen chloride gas (Mᵣ = 36.5) are released at opposite ends of a glass tube. Where does the white ring of ammonium chloride form, closer to which end? Explain.
**Answer**: Closer to the hydrogen chloride end. Ammonia molecules have a lower relative mass (17) and therefore diffuse faster than the heavier HCl molecules (36.5). They meet closer to the HCl end.
**Explanation**: Lighter = faster diffusion. A common error is saying the ring forms in the middle.
