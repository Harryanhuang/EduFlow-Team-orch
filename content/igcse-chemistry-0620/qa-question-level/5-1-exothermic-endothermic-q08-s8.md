# QQL: 5.1 — Q-5.1-08 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-5.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 5.1 Exothermic and endothermic reactions
**Question**: The complete combustion of 1.00 g of ethanol (C2H5OH) releases 29.7 kJ of energy. (a) Write the balanced equation for the complete combustion of ethanol. (b) Calculate the molar enthalpy of combustion of ethanol. (c) In an experiment, a student measured only 22.1 kJ per gram. Suggest two reasons for this discrepancy.
**Answer**: (a) C2H5OH(l) + 3O2(g) → 2CO2(g) + 3H2O(l)
(b) Mr(C2H5OH) = 46. Moles in 1.00 g = 1.00/46 = 0.0217 mol.
Molar enthalpy = 29.7/0.0217 = −1367 kJ/mol (negative because exothermic).
(c) Heat loss to surroundings (beaker, air); incomplete combustion; evaporation of ethanol; heat absorbed by the thermometer.
**Explanation**: Students must calculate molar enthalpy from mass data. A common error is not converting to per-mole or forgetting the negative sign.
