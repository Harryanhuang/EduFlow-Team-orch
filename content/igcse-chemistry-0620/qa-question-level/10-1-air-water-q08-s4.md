# Question: Question Q-10.1-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 10.1
**Question**: What is the chemical test for pure water (not just the absence of dissolved substances)?
**Answer**: Pure water boils at exactly 100°C at standard pressure and freezes at exactly 0°C. These are the definitive tests. (The anhydrous CuSO₄ test confirms water is present but does not prove purity.)
**Explanation**: Fixed boiling/freezing points = purity. A common error is saying CuSO₄ test proves purity.
