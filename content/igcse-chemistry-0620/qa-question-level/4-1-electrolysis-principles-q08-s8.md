# QQL: 4.1 — Q-4.1-08 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-4.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 4.1 Electrolysis: principles and predictions
**Question**: Explain how the selective discharge of ions during electrolysis depends on: (a) the position of the ion in the reactivity series, (b) the concentration of the solution, and (c) the nature of the electrode. Give one example for each factor.
**Answer**: (a) Reactivity series: less reactive elements are preferentially discharged. In aqueous Na2SO4, H⁺ (from water) discharges before Na⁺ at the cathode.
(b) Concentration: in dilute NaCl(aq), OH⁻ discharges at the anode (producing O2). In concentrated NaCl(aq), Cl⁻ discharges (producing Cl2).
(c) Electrode nature: with inert (carbon) electrodes in CuSO4(aq), oxygen forms at the anode. With copper electrodes, the copper anode dissolves instead.
**Explanation**: Students must address all three factors with examples. A common error is only mentioning one factor.
