# Question: Question Q-7.2-05
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 7.2
**Question**: A student adds excess zinc carbonate to dilute sulfuric acid to make zinc sulfate crystals. Explain why this method may give an impure product, and describe a better preparation route.
**Answer**: Zinc carbonate reacts with sulfuric acid to form zinc sulfate, water and carbon dioxide, but the insoluble excess solid must be filtered carefully; if excess carbonate or side products remain, the crystals may be impure. A better route is to add excess zinc oxide to warm dilute sulfuric acid, filter off the unreacted zinc oxide, then concentrate the filtrate and crystallise zinc sulfate.
**Explanation**: The key is choosing an insoluble base method and removing excess solid before crystallisation. Common errors are evaporating to dryness or forgetting filtration.
