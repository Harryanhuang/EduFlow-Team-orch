# QQL: 8.2 — Q-8.2-08 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-8.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 8.2 Group I Group VII transition elements
**Question**: Iron is a transition element. (a) State three properties of iron or its compounds that are typical of transition elements. (b) Explain why iron is used as a catalyst in the Haber process. (c) Iron forms two different ions, Fe²⁺ and Fe³⁺. How does this relate to the name "transition element"?
**Answer**: (a) Any three: forms coloured compounds (e.g. green Fe²⁺ / brown Fe³⁺ solutions), has variable oxidation states, acts as a catalyst, forms complex ions.
(b) Iron provides an alternative reaction pathway with lower activation energy, increasing the rate without being consumed.
(c) Transition elements can form stable ions with different charges (oxidation states). Fe²⁺ and Fe³⁺ demonstrate this variable oxidation state.
**Explanation**: This tests multiple aspects of transition element chemistry. A common error is confusing transition elements with Group I elements, which only form one stable ion.
