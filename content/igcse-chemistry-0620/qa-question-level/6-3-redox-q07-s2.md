# QQL: 6.3 — Q-6.3-07 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-6.3-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 6.3 Redox
**Question**: Define oxidation and reduction in terms of electron transfer. Use the reaction Zn + CuSO4 → ZnSO4 + Cu to illustrate your answer by writing the two half-equations.
**Answer**: Oxidation is loss of electrons (OIL). Reduction is gain of electrons (RIG).
Oxidation half-equation: Zn(s) → Zn²⁺(aq) + 2e⁻
Reduction half-equation: Cu²⁺(aq) + 2e⁻ → Cu(s)
Zinc loses electrons (oxidised). Copper(II) ions gain electrons (reduced).
**Explanation**: Students must write correct half-equations with state symbols. Common errors: reversing the half-equations or getting the charge wrong (e.g. Zn⁺ instead of Zn²⁺).
