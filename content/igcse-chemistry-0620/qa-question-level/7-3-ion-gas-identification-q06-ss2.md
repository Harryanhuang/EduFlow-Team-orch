# QQL: 7.3 — Q-7.3-06 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-7.3-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 7.3 Ion and gas identification
**Question**: A student is given an unknown white solid. Describe a series of tests that would identify whether it contains carbonate, sulfate, or chloride ions.
**Answer**: Carbonate test: Add dilute acid — effervescence indicates CO2 (turns limewater milky).
Sulfate test: Add acidified BaCl2 solution — white precipitate of BaSO4 confirms sulfate.
Chloride test: Add acidified AgNO3 solution — white precipitate of AgCl confirms chloride.
Note: The acid is added first in the sulfate and chloride tests to remove carbonate interference (which would also give a white precipitate with Ba²⁺).
**Explanation**: Students must describe all three tests and understand the order (carbonate test first, and acidify before sulfate/chloride tests). A common error is not acidifying the BaCl2 or AgNO3.
