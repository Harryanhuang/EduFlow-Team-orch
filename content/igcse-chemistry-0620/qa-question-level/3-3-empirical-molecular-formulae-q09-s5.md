# QQL: 3-3-empirical-molecular-formulae — Q-3.3-09 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-3.3-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 3.3 Empirical and molecular formulae
**Question**: A hydrocarbon has an empirical formula of CH2 and a relative molecular mass of 56. (a) Determine its molecular formula. (b) Calculate the percentage by mass of carbon in this hydrocarbon.
**Answer**: (a) Empirical mass of CH2 = 12 + 2(1) = 14. n = 56/14 = 4. Molecular formula = C4H8.
(b) Mass of C in C4H8 = 4 × 12 = 48. %C = (48/56) × 100 = 85.7%.
**Explanation**: Part (a) tests the molecular/empirical formula relationship. Part (b) requires applying the molecular formula to find % composition. A common error in (b) is using the empirical formula mass instead of the molecular formula mass.
