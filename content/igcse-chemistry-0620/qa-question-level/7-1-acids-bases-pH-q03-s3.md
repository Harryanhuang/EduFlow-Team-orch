# QQL: 7.1 — Q-7.1-03 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-7.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 7.1 Properties of acids and bases, pH
**Question**: Explain the difference between a strong acid and a weak acid in terms of ionisation. Give one example of each.
**Answer**: A strong acid is completely ionised in water (e.g. HCl → H⁺ + Cl⁻). All HCl molecules dissociate into ions.
A weak acid is only partially ionised in water (e.g. ethanoic acid, CH3COOH ⇌ CH3COO⁻ + H⁺). Most molecules remain undissociated — there is an equilibrium.
**Explanation**: Students must link strength to degree of ionisation. A common error is confusing "strong" with "concentrated."
