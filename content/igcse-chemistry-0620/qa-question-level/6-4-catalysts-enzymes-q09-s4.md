# QQL: 6.4 — Q-6.4-09 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-6.4-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 6.4 Catalysts and enzymes
**Question**: Compare the action of a chemical catalyst (e.g. iron in the Haber process) with an enzyme (e.g. amylase). Give two similarities and two differences.
**Answer**: Similarities: (1) Both lower the activation energy of the reaction. (2) Both are not consumed in the reaction and can be reused.
Differences: (1) Enzymes are highly specific — each catalyses only one reaction. Chemical catalysts are less specific. (2) Enzymes only work within a narrow range of temperature and pH. Chemical catalysts often work at high temperatures and are less sensitive to conditions.
**Explanation**: Students must make a structured comparison. Common errors: saying catalysts "provide energy" or that enzymes "only work in living things" (they work in vitro too).
