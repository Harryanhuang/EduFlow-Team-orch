# Question: Question Q-12.1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 12.1
**Question**: How do you test for sulfate ions (SO₄²⁻) in a solution?
**Answer**: Add dilute hydrochloric acid followed by aqueous barium chloride (or barium nitrate). A white precipitate of barium sulfate (BaSO₄) confirms sulfate ions.
**Explanation**: Ba²⁺ + SO₄²⁻ → BaSO₄ (white ppt). A common error is using silver nitrate (that's for halides).
