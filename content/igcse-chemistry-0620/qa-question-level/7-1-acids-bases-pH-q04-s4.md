# QQL: 7.1 — Q-7.1-04 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-7.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 7.1 Properties of acids and bases, pH
**Question**: Write balanced equations for the reactions of: (a) magnesium with dilute hydrochloric acid, (b) calcium carbonate with dilute sulfuric acid, (c) sodium hydroxide with dilute nitric acid. For each, describe one observable result.
**Answer**: (a) Mg(s) + 2HCl(aq) → MgCl2(aq) + H2(g) — effervescence (hydrogen gas), magnesium dissolves.
(b) CaCO3(s) + H2SO4(aq) → CaSO4(aq) + H2O(l) + CO2(g) — effervescence (CO2), solid dissolves.
(c) NaOH(aq) + HNO3(aq) → NaNO3(aq) + H2O(l) — no visible change (neutralisation, temperature increases).
**Explanation**: Students must write balanced equations and describe observations. A common error in (b) is not recognising the reaction may stop due to insoluble CaSO4 coating.
