# QQL: 3-3-empirical-molecular-formulae — Q-3.3-08 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-3.3-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 3.3 Empirical and molecular formulae
**Question**: 0.46 g of sodium reacts with 0.32 g of oxygen to form a sodium oxide. Calculate the empirical formula of the oxide. (Ar: Na = 23, O = 16)
**Answer**: Step 1: Moles of Na = 0.46/23 = 0.020 mol. Moles of O = 0.32/16 = 0.020 mol.
Step 2: Ratio Na:O = 0.020:0.020 = 1:1.
Empirical formula = NaO.
(Note: The actual sodium oxide formed is likely Na2O — this is based on the given experimental data.)
**Explanation**: Students must use mass/Ar to find moles and then the ratio. A common error is using the mass ratio directly instead of converting to moles first.
