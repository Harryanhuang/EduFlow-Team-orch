# Question: Question Q-12.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 12.2
**Question**: How do you test for the presence of chloride ions (Cl⁻) in a solution?
**Answer**: Add dilute nitric acid followed by aqueous silver nitrate. A white precipitate of silver chloride (AgCl) confirms chloride ions.
**Explanation**: Ag⁺ + Cl⁻ → AgCl (white ppt). Nitric acid removes interfering ions. A common error is adding HCl instead of HNO₃.
