# Question: Question Q-2.3-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 2.3
**Question**: Why are metals good conductors of heat?
**Answer**: The delocalised electrons in metallic bonding transfer kinetic energy rapidly through the structure. When one part is heated, the electrons gain energy and move, distributing heat throughout the metal.
**Explanation**: Delocalised electrons transfer energy. A common error is saying "atoms vibrate faster" (that's how non-metals conduct).
