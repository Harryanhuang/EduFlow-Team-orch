# Question: Question Q-10.2-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 10.2
**Question**: Write the equation for the complete combustion of methane.
**Answer**: CH₄(g) + 2O₂(g) → CO₂(g) + 2H₂O(g). One mole of methane reacts with 2 moles of oxygen to produce 1 mole of carbon dioxide and 2 moles of water vapour. The reaction is exothermic.
**Explanation**: Hydrocarbon + oxygen → CO₂ + H₂O. A common error is forgetting to balance the oxygen.
