# Question: Question Q-6.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 6.1
**Question**: Explain why increasing temperature increases the rate of reaction, in terms of collision theory.
**Answer**: Higher temperature means particles have more kinetic energy and move faster. This leads to (1) more frequent collisions per second and (2) more particles having energy ≥ activation energy, so a greater proportion of collisions are successful.
**Explanation**: Both frequency and energy of collisions increase. A common error is only mentioning one factor.
