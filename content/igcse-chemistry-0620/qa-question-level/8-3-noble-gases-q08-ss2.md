# QQL: 8.3 — Q-8.3-08 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-8.3-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 8.3 Noble gases and uses
**Question**: Neon is used in advertising signs. Describe how a neon sign produces light and state the colour it produces.
**Answer**: When a high voltage is applied across a tube containing neon gas at low pressure, electrons in the neon atoms become excited to higher energy levels. When they fall back to lower levels, they emit light. Neon produces a characteristic red-orange glow.
**Explanation**: Students need to describe the excitation and emission process. A common error is saying the gas "burns" or "heats up to glow" rather than electron transitions.
