# QQL: 10.1 — Q-10.1-11 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-10.1-11
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 10.1 Air water
**Question**: Explain how oxides of nitrogen and sulfur dioxide contribute to acid rain. Include the sources of these pollutants and the effects of acid rain on the environment.
**Answer**: Sources: oxides of nitrogen from vehicle engines (high temperature causes N2 + O2 → NOx); sulfur dioxide from burning fossil fuels containing sulfur impurities.
In the atmosphere: SO2 + H2O → H2SO3 (sulfurous acid); further oxidation gives H2SO4. NOx reacts with water to form nitric acid.
Effects: acidifies lakes and rivers (kills fish and aquatic life); damages trees; corrodes limestone buildings and metal structures; leaches toxic metals from soil.
**Explanation**: Students must link the pollutant to the acid formed and the environmental damage. A common error is saying CO2 causes acid rain (it causes ocean acidification, not acid rain).
