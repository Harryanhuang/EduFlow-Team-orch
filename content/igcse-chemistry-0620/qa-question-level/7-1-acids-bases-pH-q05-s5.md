# QQL: 7.1 — Q-7.1-05 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-7.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 7.1 Properties of acids and bases, pH
**Question**: Describe the difference between the terms "dilute" and "concentrated" and "strong" and "weak" when describing acids. Can a strong acid be dilute? Explain.
**Answer**: "Concentrated" and "dilute" refer to the amount of acid dissolved in a given volume of solution (concentration).
"Strong" and "weak" refer to the degree of ionisation in water (how completely the acid dissociates).
Yes, a strong acid can be dilute — it means the acid is fully ionised but there are fewer acid molecules per unit volume. For example, dilute HCl is still fully ionised.
**Explanation**: Students must distinguish the two concepts. A common error is using them interchangeably.
