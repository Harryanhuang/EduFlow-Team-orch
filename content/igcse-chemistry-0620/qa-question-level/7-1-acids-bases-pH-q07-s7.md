# QQL: 7.1 — Q-7.1-07 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-7.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 7.1 Properties of acids and bases, pH
**Question**: A student has two unlabelled solutions: one is 0.1 mol/dm³ hydrochloric acid and the other is 0.1 mol/dm³ ethanoic acid. (a) Predict which has the lower pH and explain why. (b) Both solutions react with magnesium. Predict which reacts faster and explain. (c) If both are titrated with the same NaOH solution, will the volume of NaOH needed be the same or different? Explain.
**Answer**: (a) HCl has the lower pH. It is a strong acid, fully ionised, so [H⁺] = 0.1 mol/dm³. Ethanoic acid is weak, partially ionised, so [H⁺] < 0.1 mol/dm³.
(b) HCl reacts faster. It has a higher [H⁺], so more frequent successful collisions with magnesium.
(c) The volume of NaOH needed will be the SAME. Both acids have the same concentration and are monoprotic. The total number of moles of H⁺ available is the same (the weak acid fully reacts during neutralisation, not just the ionised fraction).
**Explanation**: Part (c) is the most commonly misunderstood — total acid content determines titration volume, not just the ionised fraction.
