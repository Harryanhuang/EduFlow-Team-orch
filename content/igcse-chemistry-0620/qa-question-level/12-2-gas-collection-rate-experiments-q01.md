# Question: Question Q-12.2-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 12.2
**Question**: How would you collect a sample of hydrogen gas produced in a reaction?
**Answer**: By downward displacement of water (collecting over water) using an inverted measuring cylinder or gas syringe. Hydrogen is only slightly soluble in water, so this method works well.
**Explanation**: Collect over water. A common error is using upward delivery (hydrogen is lighter than air and would escape).
