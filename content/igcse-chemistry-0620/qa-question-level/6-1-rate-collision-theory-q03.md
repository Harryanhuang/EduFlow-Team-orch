# Question: Question Q-6.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 6.1
**Question**: A student investigates the effect of concentration on the rate of reaction between sodium thiosulfate and hydrochloric acid. Describe what they would observe and how they would measure the rate.
**Answer**: The solution becomes cloudy as sulfur precipitates. The student places the flask over a marked X on paper and times how long until the X is no longer visible. Higher concentration = faster clouding = shorter time = greater rate.
**Explanation**: Cloudiness blocks visibility. A common error is measuring gas volume (no gas is produced here).
