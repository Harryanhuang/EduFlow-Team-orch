# QQL: 8.2 — Q-8.2-06 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-8.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 8.2 Group I Group VII transition elements
**Question**: Describe the trend in reactivity of the halogens (Group VII) going down the group. Explain this trend in terms of atomic structure.
**Answer**: Reactivity decreases going down Group VII (chlorine > bromine > iodine). As atoms get larger down the group, the outer shell is further from the nucleus and more shielded. The attraction for an incoming electron is weaker, so it is harder to gain an electron, reducing reactivity.
**Explanation**: This is the opposite trend to Group I. A common error is applying the Group I trend to Group VII without recognising the different mechanism (losing vs gaining electrons).
