# Question: Question Q-4.2-11
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Topic**: 4.2
**Question**: Why is aluminium extracted by electrolysis of molten Al₂O₃ and not by reduction with carbon?
**Answer**: Aluminium is above carbon in the reactivity series, so carbon cannot reduce aluminium oxide. Electrolysis is required to force Al³⁺ ions to gain electrons.
**Explanation**: Too reactive for carbon reduction. A common error is saying aluminium "doesn't react with carbon."
