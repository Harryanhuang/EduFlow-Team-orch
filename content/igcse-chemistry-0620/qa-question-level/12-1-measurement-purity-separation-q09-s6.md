# Question: Question Q-12.1-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 12.1
**Question**: How do you test for carbonate ions (CO₃²⁻) in a solid?
**Answer**: Add dilute acid. If carbonates are present, effervescence occurs (bubbles of CO₂ gas). Test the gas with limewater — it turns milky/cloudy, confirming carbon dioxide.
**Explanation**: Acid + carbonate → CO₂. A common error is testing with NaOH instead of acid.
