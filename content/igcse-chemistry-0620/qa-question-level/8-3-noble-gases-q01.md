# Question: Question Q-8.3-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Topic**: 8.3
**Question**: Why are noble gases chemically unreactive?
**Answer**: Noble gases have a full outer shell of electrons (stable electronic configuration). They do not need to gain, lose, or share electrons to achieve stability.
**Explanation**: Full outer shell = stable. A common error is saying they "have no electrons."
**Tags**: noble-gases, unreactive, full-shell
