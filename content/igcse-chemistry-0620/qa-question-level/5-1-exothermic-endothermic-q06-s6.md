# QQL: 5.1 — Q-5.1-06 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-5.1-06
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 5.1 Exothermic and endothermic reactions
**Question**: In the reaction N2(g) + 3H2(g) ⇌ 2NH3(g), ΔH = −92 kJ/mol. (a) Is the forward reaction exothermic or endothermic? (b) What is the ΔH for the reverse reaction? (c) If 0.50 mol of N2 reacts completely with excess H2, how much energy is released? (d) Sketch the reaction profile showing both forward and reverse activation energies.
**Answer**: (a) Exothermic (ΔH is negative).
(b) +92 kJ/mol (the reverse reaction has the same magnitude but opposite sign).
(c) 0.50 mol N2 releases 0.50 × 92 = 46 kJ.
(d) Profile: reactants (N2 + 3H2) at higher energy than products (2NH3). Ea(forward) shown from reactants to peak. Ea(reverse) shown from products to peak — larger than Ea(forward).
**Explanation**: Students must apply the concept of reversible reactions and energy. A common error in (c) is using the stoichiometric ratio wrongly.
