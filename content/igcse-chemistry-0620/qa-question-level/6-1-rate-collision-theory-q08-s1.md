# QQL: 6.1 — Q-6.1-08 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-6.1-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 6.1 Rate collision theory
**Question**: Explain why increasing the concentration of a reactant in solution increases the rate of reaction. Your answer should refer to collision theory.
**Answer**: Increasing concentration means more reactant particles per unit volume. This increases the frequency of collisions between reacting particles per second. More collisions per second means more successful collisions (those with energy ≥ activation energy), so the rate of reaction increases.
**Explanation**: Students must link concentration → particle density → collision frequency → rate. A common error is saying particles "move faster" (concentration doesn't affect speed, only temperature does).
