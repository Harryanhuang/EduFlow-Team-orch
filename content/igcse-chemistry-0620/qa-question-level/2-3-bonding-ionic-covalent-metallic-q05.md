# Question: Question Q-2.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 2.3
**Question**: Why does carbon dioxide (CO₂) have double bonds?
**Answer**: Carbon has 4 valence electrons and each oxygen has 6. Carbon shares two electrons with each oxygen, and each oxygen shares two electrons back, forming two double bonds (C=O). This gives carbon and each oxygen a full outer shell of 8 electrons.
**Explanation**: Double bonds = 2 shared pairs. A common error is showing single bonds only.
