# QQL: 4.1 — Q-4.1-02 (F)

**Question ID**: Q-4.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Topic**: 4.1 Electrolysis: principles and predictions
**Question**: During electrolysis, where do reduction and oxidation occur? State the rule for which ions go to each electrode.
**Answer**: Reduction occurs at the cathode (negative electrode) — positive ions (cations) are attracted here and gain electrons. Oxidation occurs at the anode (positive electrode) — negative ions (anions) are attracted here and lose electrons. Remember: PANIC — Positive Anode, Negative Is Cathode.
**Explanation**: Students must link electrode charge to ion attraction. A common error is confusing anode and cathode charges.
