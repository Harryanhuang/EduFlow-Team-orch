# QQL: 8.2 — Q-8.2-05 (S)

**Question ID**: Q-8.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 8.2 Group I Group VII transition elements
**Question**: Compare the reactions of lithium and potassium with water. Include observations and explain the difference in terms of reactivity.
**Answer**: Potassium reacts much more vigorously than lithium with water — it melts into a ball, moves rapidly across the surface, and ignites with a lilac flame. Lithium fizzes steadily and moves slowly. Potassium is more reactive because its outer electron is further from the nucleus and more shielded, so it is lost more easily.
**Explanation**: Students must link observation to position in the group. A common error is saying the difference is due to "more energy" rather than atomic structure.
