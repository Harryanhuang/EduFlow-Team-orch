# QQL: 7.3 — Q-7.3-08 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-7.3-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 7.3 Ion and gas identification
**Question**: A solution contains either iron(II) or iron(III) ions. (a) Describe how aqueous sodium hydroxide can distinguish between them, including the observations. (b) Write the ionic equations for both reactions. (c) What happens to a precipitate of iron(II) hydroxide if left standing in air?
**Answer**: (a) With NaOH: Fe²⁺ gives a green precipitate of Fe(OH)2. Fe³⁺ gives a brown/rust-coloured precipitate of Fe(OH)3.
(b) Fe²⁺(aq) + 2OH⁻(aq) → Fe(OH)2(s); Fe³⁺(aq) + 3OH⁻(aq) → Fe(OH)3(s)
(c) The green Fe(OH)2 slowly turns brown as it is oxidised by air to Fe(OH)3.
**Explanation**: Students must link the colour to the oxidation state. A common error is confusing the two precipitate colours or not writing balanced ionic equations.
