# QQL: 4.1 — Q-4.1-06 (C)

**Question ID**: Q-4.1-06
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Topic**: 4.1 Electrolysis: principles and predictions
**Question**: During the electrolysis of dilute sulfuric acid (acidified water), (a) Write the half-equations at each electrode. (b) Explain why the volume of gas at the cathode is twice that at the anode. (c) Write the overall equation.
**Answer**: (a) Cathode: 2H⁺(aq) + 2e⁻ → H2(g). Anode: 4OH⁻(aq) → O2(g) + 2H2O(l) + 4e⁻ (or 2H2O(l) → O2(g) + 4H⁺(aq) + 4e⁻).
(b) For every 4 electrons: 2H2 molecules at cathode, 1O2 molecule at anode. Ratio 2:1.
(c) 2H2O(l) → 2H2(g) + O2(g)
**Explanation**: Students must link the mole ratio to the volume ratio (Avogadro's law). A common error is writing the wrong half-equation for the anode in acid.
