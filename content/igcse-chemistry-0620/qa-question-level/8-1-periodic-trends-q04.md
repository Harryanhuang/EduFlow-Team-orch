# Question: Question Q-8.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 8.1
**Question**: Explain why potassium reacts more violently with water than sodium does.
**Answer**: Potassium is below sodium in Group 1. Its outer electron is further from the nucleus and more shielded by inner electron shells, so it is lost more easily. This makes potassium more reactive.
**Explanation**: Further down = easier to lose electron. A common error is saying potassium has "more outer electrons."
