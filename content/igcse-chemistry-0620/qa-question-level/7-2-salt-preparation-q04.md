# Question: Question Q-7.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Topic**: 7.2
**Question**: How would you prepare a pure, dry sample of silver chloride?
**Answer**: Mix aqueous silver nitrate with aqueous sodium chloride. Silver chloride precipitates as a white solid. Filter the precipitate, wash with distilled water (to remove soluble impurities), and dry in an oven or between filter paper.
**Explanation**: Precipitation from two soluble salts. A common error is trying to react silver metal with HCl.
