# Items: 2.1 — Atomic structure, isotopes

### Question Q-2.1-05
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: State the relative mass and charge of a proton, neutron, and electron.
**Answer**: Proton: relative mass = 1, charge = +1. Neutron: relative mass = 1, charge = 0. Electron: relative mass = 1/1840 (negligible), charge = -1.
**Explanation**: Proton +1/1, neutron 0/1, electron -1/negligible mass. A common error is giving the electron a mass of 0 (it's negligible but not zero).
**Tags**: proton, neutron, electron, mass, charge

### Question Q-2.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: An atom has 17 protons, 18 neutrons, and 17 electrons. State its atomic number, mass number, and electronic configuration.
**Answer**: Atomic number = 17 (number of protons). Mass number = 17 + 18 = 35. Electronic configuration = 2,8,7 (or 2.8.7). This is chlorine-35.
**Explanation**: Atomic number = protons; Mass number = protons + neutrons; Config = shells from innermost. A common error is reversing atomic and mass number.
**Tags**: atomic-number, mass-number, configuration, chlorine

### Question Q-2.1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What are isotopes? Explain why isotopes of the same element have the same chemical properties but different physical properties.
**Answer**: Isotopes are atoms of the same element with the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons. They have the same chemical properties because chemical behaviour is determined by the number of electrons (which equals protons). They have different physical properties (e.g., mass, density) because they have different numbers of neutrons.
**Explanation**: Same protons/electrons = same chemistry; different neutrons = different mass. A common error is saying isotopes have different numbers of electrons.
**Tags**: isotopes, chemical-properties, physical-properties

### Question Q-2.1-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Calculate the relative atomic mass of chlorine given that it consists of 75% chlorine-35 and 25% chlorine-37.
**Answer**: Ar = (75 × 35 + 25 × 37) / 100 = (2625 + 925) / 100 = 3550 / 100 = 35.5
**Explanation**: Weighted average of isotope masses by abundance. A common error is taking the simple average (36).
**Tags**: relative-atomic-mass, chlorine, isotope, calculation

### Question Q-2.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: An ion X³⁺ has 10 electrons and 14 neutrons. Identify element X and state its mass number.
**Answer**: X³⁺ has lost 3 electrons, so the neutral atom has 10 + 3 = 13 electrons = 13 protons. Atomic number 13 = aluminium (Al). Mass number = 13 + 14 = 27. This is aluminium-27.
**Explanation**: Ion charge tells electron loss/gain. Protons = electrons in neutral atom. A common error is saying the element has 10 protons.
**Tags**: ion, aluminium, identification, mass-number

### Question Q-2.1-10
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Describe the structure of an atom, including the arrangement of subatomic particles.
**Answer**: An atom has a small, dense nucleus at the centre containing protons and neutrons. Electrons orbit the nucleus in energy levels (shells). The atom is mostly empty space. The nucleus contains almost all the mass of the atom.
**Explanation**: Nucleus (protons + neutrons) at centre, electrons in shells. A common error is saying electrons are "in the nucleus."
**Tags**: atom-structure, nucleus, electron-shells

### Question Q-2.1-11
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why atoms are electrically neutral.
**Answer**: An atom has equal numbers of protons (positive charge) and electrons (negative charge). The charges cancel each other out, so the overall charge is zero. Neutrons have no charge.
**Explanation**: Equal protons and electrons. A common error is saying neutrons "balance" the charge.
**Tags**: neutral, charge, proton, electron
