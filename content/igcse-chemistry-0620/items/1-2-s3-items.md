# Items: 1.5 — Solubility rules

### Question Q-1.2-11
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Which of the following are soluble in water: sodium chloride, silver chloride, lead(II) sulfate, potassium nitrate, calcium carbonate?
**Answer**: Soluble: sodium chloride, potassium nitrate. Insoluble: silver chloride, lead(II) sulfate, calcium carbonate. (All sodium/potassium salts are soluble; most chlorides soluble except AgCl, PbCl₂; most sulfates soluble except PbSO₄; most carbonates insoluble except Group 1.)
**Explanation**: Solubility rules. A common error is thinking all chlorides are soluble.
**Tags**: solubility-rules, soluble, insoluble

### Question Q-1.2-12
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Predict the precipitate formed when barium chloride solution is mixed with sodium sulfate solution. Write the ionic equation.
**Answer**: Barium sulfate (BaSO₄) — a white precipitate. Ionic equation: Ba²⁺(aq) + SO₄²⁻(aq) → BaSO₄(s). Sodium and chloride ions are spectator ions.
**Explanation**: BaSO₄ is insoluble. A common error is saying NaCl precipitates.
**Tags**: precipitation, barium-sulfate, ionic-equation

### Question Q-1.2-13
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: State the solubility rule for nitrates.
**Answer**: All nitrates are soluble in water.
**Explanation**: No exceptions. A common error is thinking some nitrates are insoluble.
**Tags**: nitrates, solubility-rules

### Question Q-1.2-14
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is lead(II) iodide prepared by precipitation rather than by reacting lead with acid?
**Answer**: Lead(II) iodide is insoluble, so it cannot be prepared by the acid method (which produces a soluble salt). Precipitation from two soluble salts (lead nitrate + potassium iodide) produces the insoluble lead(II) iodide directly.
**Explanation**: Insoluble salt → precipitation. A common error is saying lead "doesn't react with acid."
**Tags**: lead-iodide, precipitation, insoluble-salt
