# Items: 11.2 — Alkenes and polymers

### Question Q-11.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the general formula for alkenes?
**Answer**: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂ is wrong — the correct formula is CₙH₂ₙ.
**Explanation**: Unsaturated hydrocarbons with one C=C double bond. A common error is using CₙH₂ₙ₊₂ (alkanes).
**Tags**: alkenes, general-formula, unsaturated

### Question Q-11.2-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: How can you distinguish between an alkane and an alkene using bromine water?
**Answer**: Add bromine water to each. Alkenes decolourise bromine water (orange-brown → colourless) because they undergo an addition reaction with bromine. Alkanes do not react, so the colour stays orange-brown.
**Explanation**: Bromine water test for unsaturation. A common error is saying alkanes decolourise bromine.
**Tags**: bromine-water, alkene-test, unsaturation

### Question Q-11.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is addition polymerisation? Write the equation for the polymerisation of ethene.
**Answer**: Addition polymerisation is the process where many small unsaturated molecules (monomers) join together to form a large molecule (polymer) by opening their double bonds. nC₂H₄ → [-CH₂-CH₂-]ₙ. The monomer is ethene; the polymer is poly(ethene).
**Explanation**: Monomers join, double bonds open. A common error is showing the polymer still having double bonds.
**Tags**: addition-polymerisation, ethene, polyethene, equation

### Question Q-11.2-04
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Why are plastics a problem in the environment?
**Answer**: Most plastics are non-biodegradable (they do not decompose naturally) and persist in the environment for hundreds of years. They accumulate in landfills and oceans, harming wildlife.
**Explanation**: Non-biodegradable + persistent. A common error is saying plastics "dissolve" in water.
**Tags**: plastics, non-biodegradable, environmental

### Question Q-11.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Draw the repeating unit of poly(propene) from the monomer propene (CH₂=CH-CH₃).
**Answer**: -[CH₂-CH(CH₃)]- with single bonds between the carbon atoms and the CH₃ group attached to every other carbon. The double bond of the monomer becomes a single bond in the polymer.
**Explanation**: Double bond → single bond; side group carries through. A common error is keeping the double bond.
**Tags**: polypropene, repeating-unit, polymer-structure
