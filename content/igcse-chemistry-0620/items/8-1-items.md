# Items: 8.1 — The Periodic Table: Group 1 metals

### Question Q-8.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Why are Group 1 metals called "alkali metals"?
**Answer**: They react with water to form alkaline solutions (metal hydroxides) and hydrogen gas. The hydroxides are soluble and make the solution alkaline (pH > 7).
**Explanation**: Alkaline product. A common error is saying they are called "alkali" because they are "metallic."
**Tags**: group-1, alkali-metals, name

### Question Q-8.1-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Write the balanced equation for the reaction of sodium with water.
**Answer**: 2Na(s) + 2H₂O(l) → 2NaOH(aq) + H₂(g)
**Explanation**: Alkali metal + water → metal hydroxide + hydrogen. A common error is writing NaOH as a solid.
**Tags**: sodium, water, equation, alkali-metal

### Question Q-8.1-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Describe the trend in the melting points of Group 1 metals down the group.
**Answer**: Melting points decrease down the group. Lithium has the highest melting point, and it gets lower through sodium, potassium, rubidium, and caesium.
**Explanation**: Decreases down the group. A common error is saying it increases (confusing with reactivity).
**Tags**: group-1, melting-point, trend

### Question Q-8.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why potassium reacts more violently with water than sodium does.
**Answer**: Potassium is below sodium in Group 1. Its outer electron is further from the nucleus and more shielded by inner electron shells, so it is lost more easily. This makes potassium more reactive.
**Explanation**: Further down = easier to lose electron. A common error is saying potassium has "more outer electrons."
**Tags**: potassium, sodium, reactivity-trend, explanation
