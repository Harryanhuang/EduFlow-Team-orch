# Items: 11.6 — Polymers: addition and condensation

### Question Q-11.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the difference between addition and condensation polymerisation?
**Answer**: In addition polymerisation, many small molecules (monomers) with C=C double bonds join together to form a long chain without producing any other products. In condensation polymerisation, monomers join together with the loss of a small molecule (usually water or HCl).
**Explanation**: No byproduct vs. small molecule released. A common error is saying addition polymerisation "adds water."
**Tags**: polymerisation, addition, condensation, difference

### Question Q-11.2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Draw the repeat unit of poly(ethene) formed from ethene monomers.
**Answer**: -[CH₂-CH₂]-ₙ. Each carbon has 2 hydrogens attached, with single bonds between carbons and bonds extending out of each end.
**Explanation**: Remove the C=C double bond, connect with single bonds, and brackets with n. A common error is leaving the double bond in the repeat unit.
**Tags**: polyethene, repeat-unit, addition-polymer

### Question Q-11.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Nylon is a condensation polymer. Name the two types of monomers used to make nylon and the small molecule released.
**Answer**: A dicarboxylic acid and a diamine. The small molecule released is water (H₂O) — one H from the amine group and an OH from the carboxylic acid group combine.
**Explanation**: Two monomers + water released. A common error is saying amino acids (that's proteins, not nylon).
**Tags**: nylon, condensation, monomer, diamine

### Question Q-11.2-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What are the environmental problems associated with disposal of non-biodegradable polymers?
**Answer**: They do not decompose naturally, leading to landfill accumulation. Burning produces toxic gases. They can harm wildlife when they enter ecosystems as microplastics. Recycling is energy-intensive.
**Explanation**: Non-decomposing + toxic combustion + microplastics. A common error is saying they "dissolve in water."
**Tags**: polymer, environment, non-biodegradable, recycling
