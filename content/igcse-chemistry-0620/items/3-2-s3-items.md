# Items: 3.5 — Gas volume calculations (Avogadro's law)

### Question Q-3.2-12
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Calculate the volume of 2 mol of any gas at r.t.p.
**Answer**: 48 dm³
**Explanation**: At r.t.p., 1 mol of any gas occupies 24 dm³. Volume = 2 × 24 = 48 dm³. A common error is using 22.4 dm³ (STP, not r.t.p.).
**Tags**: gas-volume, rtp, Avogadro, calculation

### Question Q-3.2-13
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Calculate the volume of carbon dioxide produced when 10 g of calcium carbonate reacts with excess HCl at r.t.p. (CaCO₃ + 2HCl → CaCl₂ + H₂O + CO₂; molar volume = 24 dm³/mol)
**Answer**: 2.4 dm³
**Explanation**: Mᵣ of CaCO₃ = 100. Moles = 10/100 = 0.1 mol. 1:1 ratio → 0.1 mol CO₂. Volume = 0.1 × 24 = 2.4 dm³. A common error is using the wrong molar mass.
**Tags**: gas-volume, CaCO3, CO2, stoichiometry, calculation

### Question Q-3.2-14
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What volume of oxygen is needed to completely react with 48 dm³ of hydrogen at r.t.p.? (2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O)
**Answer**: 24 dm³
**Explanation**: From the equation: 2 mol H₂ : 1 mol O₂. So 48 dm³ H₂ needs 48/2 = 24 dm³ O₂ (same temperature and pressure → volume ratio = mole ratio). A common error is saying 48 dm³.
**Tags**: gas-volume, hydrogen, oxygen, stoichiometry

### Question Q-3.2-15
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: State Avogadro's Law.
**Answer**: Equal volumes of gases at the same temperature and pressure contain equal numbers of molecules (or moles).
**Explanation**: Same V, T, P → same number of molecules. A common error is saying equal masses.
**Tags**: Avogadro, law, definition
