# Items: 11.4 — Macromolecules: proteins, fats

### Question Q-11.4-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What type of polymer are proteins?
**Answer**: Proteins are natural condensation polymers made from amino acid monomers joined by peptide (amide) links.
**Explanation**: Amino acids → peptide bonds. A common error is saying proteins are made from sugars.
**Tags**: proteins, amino-acids, peptide-link, condensation-polymer

### Question Q-11.4-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What are the products of the hydrolysis of proteins?
**Answer**: Amino acids.
**Explanation**: Proteins break down into their amino acid monomers. A common error is saying glucose.
**Tags**: protein-hydrolysis, amino-acids

### Question Q-11.4-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Compare addition polymerisation and condensation polymerisation.
**Answer**: Addition polymerisation: monomers are unsaturated (have C=C), only one product (the polymer), no small molecule lost. Condensation polymerisation: monomers have two functional groups, two products (polymer + small molecule like H₂O), small molecule eliminated.
**Explanation**: One vs. two products; unsaturated vs. bifunctional monomers. A common error is saying both produce water.
**Tags**: addition-polymerisation, condensation-polymerisation, comparison

### Question Q-11.4-04
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What are fats made from chemically?
**Answer**: Fats (triglycerides) are made from glycerol (an alcohol with three -OH groups) and fatty acids (long-chain carboxylic acids). Three fatty acid molecules react with one glycerol molecule in a condensation reaction.
**Explanation**: Glycerol + 3 fatty acids → fat + 3H₂O. A common error is saying fats are "proteins."
**Tags**: fats, triglycerides, glycerol, fatty-acids
