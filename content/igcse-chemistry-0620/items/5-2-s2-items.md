# Items: 5.3 — Redox reactions, oxidation, reduction

### Question Q-6.3-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Define oxidation and reduction in terms of oxygen transfer.
**Answer**: Oxidation is the gain of oxygen. Reduction is the loss of oxygen. OIL RIG is helpful: Oxidation Is Loss (of electrons), Reduction Is Gain (of electrons).
**Explanation**: Gain/loss of oxygen or electrons. A common error is getting them the wrong way round.
**Tags**: oxidation, reduction, oxygen, definition

### Question Q-6.3-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In the reaction Fe₂O₃ + 3CO → 2Fe + 3CO₂, identify the substance oxidised and the substance reduced.
**Answer**: CO is oxidised to CO₂ (gains oxygen). Fe₂O₃ is reduced to Fe (loses oxygen). Fe₂O₃ is the oxidising agent; CO is the reducing agent.
**Explanation**: Oxygen transfer identifies oxidation and reduction. A common error is saying Fe is oxidised.
**Tags**: redox, oxidised, reduced, blast-furnace

### Question Q-6.3-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In the reaction Zn + Cu²⁺ → Zn²⁺ + Cu, identify the oxidising agent and the reducing agent.
**Answer**: Cu²⁺ is the oxidising agent (it causes Zn to lose electrons and is itself reduced). Zn is the reducing agent (it causes Cu²⁺ to gain electrons and is itself oxidised).
**Explanation**: Oxidising agent = gets reduced. A common error is saying Zn is the oxidising agent because it is reactive.
**Tags**: redox, oxidising-agent, reducing-agent, Zn, Cu

### Question Q-6.3-04
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What does OIL RIG stand for?
**Answer**: Oxidation Is Loss (of electrons), Reduction Is Gain (of electrons).
**Explanation**: Mnemonic for redox. A common error is reversing the letters.
**Tags**: OIL-RIG, oxidation, reduction, mnemonic

### Question Q-6.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Write the ionic half-equation for the reduction of copper(II) ions to copper metal.
**Answer**: Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu
**Explanation**: Copper ions gain 2 electrons to form copper metal. This is reduction (gain of electrons). A common error is writing Cu⁺ instead of Cu²⁺.
**Tags**: half-equation, copper, reduction
