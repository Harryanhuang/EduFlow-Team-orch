# Items: 8.1 — Group 1 metals supplementary questions

### Question Q-8.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe two observations when sodium reacts with cold water.
**Answer**: Sodium floats and moves quickly on the surface. It may melt into a small ball and produces hydrogen gas while forming sodium hydroxide solution.
**Explanation**: Sodium is less dense than water and reacts exothermically. Common errors include saying sodium sinks or produces oxygen.
**Tags**: sodium, water, group-1, observations

### Question Q-8.1-06
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Lithium, sodium and potassium are each added to water. Predict the observations and explain the trend in reactivity using electronic structure.
**Answer**: All three float and move on the surface while producing hydrogen and alkaline hydroxide solutions. Lithium reacts gently, sodium more vigorously and may melt into a ball, and potassium reacts most violently with a lilac flame. Reactivity increases down Group 1 because the outer electron is further from the nucleus and more shielded, so it is lost more easily.
**Explanation**: The answer must link observations to electron loss down the group. A common error is saying potassium is more reactive because it has more outer electrons.
**Tags**: group-1, water, reactivity, electronic-structure

### Question Q-8.1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Write the balanced symbol equation for potassium reacting with water.
**Answer**: 2K(s) + 2H₂O(l) → 2KOH(aq) + H₂(g)
**Explanation**: Group 1 metals form metal hydroxides and hydrogen with water. Common errors include writing K₂O or forgetting to balance hydrogen.
**Tags**: potassium, water, equation, group-1

### Question Q-8.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Element X is in Group 1 below sodium. Predict how X reacts with water compared with sodium, and write a general word equation for the reaction.
**Answer**: X reacts more vigorously than sodium because reactivity increases down Group 1. It forms an alkaline solution of the metal hydroxide and hydrogen gas. Word equation: Group 1 metal + water → metal hydroxide + hydrogen.
**Explanation**: Students must apply the group trend to an unfamiliar element. A common error is predicting lower reactivity because the atom is larger.
**Tags**: group-1, prediction, water, trend

### Question Q-8.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A student claims potassium and sodium have similar reactions with water because both are in Group 1. Evaluate this claim using observations and electronic structure.
**Answer**: The claim is only partly correct. Both react with water to form an alkaline hydroxide and hydrogen, but potassium reacts more vigorously than sodium and may ignite with a lilac flame. Potassium's outer electron is further from the nucleus and more shielded, so it is lost more easily; therefore potassium is more reactive.
**Explanation**: Students must compare similarity within a group with the trend down the group. A common error is saying "same group means identical reactivity."
**Tags**: potassium, sodium, group-1, evaluation
