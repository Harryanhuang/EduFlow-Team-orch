# Items: 11.3 — Alcohols, carboxylic acids, esters

### Question Q-11.3-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the functional group of alcohols?
**Answer**: The hydroxyl group (-OH).
**Explanation**: -OH attached to a carbon chain. A common error is confusing it with the hydroxide ion (OH⁻).
**Tags**: alcohol, functional-group, hydroxyl

### Question Q-11.3-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Ethanol can be produced by two methods: fermentation of glucose and hydration of ethene. Compare the two methods.
**Answer**: Fermentation: renewable resource (sugar/glucose), low temperature, batch process, but slow and produces dilute ethanol. Hydration of ethene: from non-renewable crude oil, continuous process, faster, pure ethanol, but uses fossil fuels.
**Explanation**: Renewable vs. non-renewable; batch vs. continuous. A common error is saying fermentation uses "ethene."
**Tags**: ethanol, fermentation, hydration, comparison

### Question Q-11.3-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the equation for the fermentation of glucose to produce ethanol?
**Answer**: C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2C₂H₅OH + 2CO₂ (catalysed by yeast enzymes at about 30°C, anaerobic conditions).
**Explanation**: Glucose → ethanol + carbon dioxide. A common error is forgetting the CO₂ or getting the ratio wrong.
**Tags**: fermentation, glucose, ethanol, equation

### Question Q-11.3-04
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the functional group of carboxylic acids?
**Answer**: The carboxyl group (-COOH).
**Explanation**: Contains both C=O and -OH on the same carbon. A common error is saying just -OH.
**Tags**: carboxylic-acid, functional-group, carboxyl

### Question Q-11.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is formed when ethanol reacts with ethanoic acid? Name the type of reaction.
**Answer**: Ethyl ethanoate (an ester) and water. This is an esterification (condensation) reaction: CH₃COOH + C₂H₅OH → CH₃COOC₂H₅ + H₂O (catalysed by concentrated sulfuric acid).
**Explanation**: Acid + alcohol → ester + water. A common error is saying a salt is formed.
**Tags**: esterification, ethyl-ethanoate, ester, equation

### Question Q-11.3-06
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: How can you identify ethanoic acid (vinegar) from its smell?
**Answer**: Ethanoic acid has a sharp, vinegar-like smell.
**Explanation**: Characteristic smell. A common error is saying it is odourless.
**Tags**: ethanoic-acid, vinegar, identification
