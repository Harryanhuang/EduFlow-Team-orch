### Question Q-3.1-06
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: 2.4 g of magnesium reacts with excess oxygen to form magnesium oxide. (a) Write the balanced equation. (b) Calculate the maximum mass of magnesium oxide produced. (c) If the actual mass produced was 3.2 g, calculate the percentage yield. (Ar: Mg = 24, O = 16)
**Answer**: (a) 2Mg(s) + O2(g) → 2MgO(s)
(b) Moles of Mg = 2.4/24 = 0.10 mol. From the equation, 2 mol Mg → 2 mol MgO, so 0.10 mol Mg → 0.10 mol MgO.
Mass of MgO = 0.10 × (24+16) = 0.10 × 40 = 4.0 g.
(c) % yield = (3.2/4.0) × 100 = 80%.
**Explanation**: Students must balance the equation, use mole ratios, and calculate yield. Common errors: writing Mg + O → MgO (unbalanced) or using wrong Mr for MgO.
**Tags**: stoichiometry, mole-calculation, percentage-yield, balancing, magnesium-oxide

### Question Q-3.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A student reacts 5.0 g of calcium carbonate with excess hydrochloric acid. (a) Write the balanced equation. (b) Calculate the volume of carbon dioxide produced at r.t.p. (1 mol gas = 24 dm³). (c) Explain why the measured volume might be less than the calculated value.
**Answer**: (a) CaCO3(s) + 2HCl(aq) → CaCl2(aq) + H2O(l) + CO2(g)
(b) Mr(CaCO3) = 40+12+48 = 100. Moles CaCO3 = 5.0/100 = 0.050 mol.
From equation: 1 mol CaCO3 → 1 mol CO2. So 0.050 mol CO2.
Volume = 0.050 × 24 = 1.2 dm³.
(c) Possible reasons: some CO2 dissolves in water; gas escapes before collection; incomplete reaction.
**Explanation**: Students must combine equation writing, mole calculation, and gas volume. A common error is forgetting the 1:1 mole ratio or using 22.4 dm³ (STP) instead of 24 dm³ (r.t.p.).
**Tags**: stoichiometry, gas-volume, mole-calculation, calcium-carbonate

### Question Q-3.1-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Balance the following equations and identify the type of reaction: (a) Fe + H2O → Fe3O4 + H2 (b) Al + CuO → Al2O3 + Cu (c) Na2CO3 + HCl → NaCl + H2O + CO2. For each, explain your classification.
**Answer**: (a) 3Fe + 4H2O → Fe3O4 + 4H2 — displacement/redox (iron displaces hydrogen from water).
(b) 2Al + 3CuO → Al2O3 + 3Cu — displacement/redox (aluminium displaces copper from its oxide).
(c) Na2CO3 + 2HCl → 2NaCl + H2O + CO2 — acid-base/neutralisation (carbonate reacts with acid to produce salt, water, and carbon dioxide).
**Explanation**: Students must balance AND classify. A common error in (a) is writing Fe + H2O → FeO + H2 (wrong iron oxide).
**Tags**: balancing, equation, reaction-type, displacement, neutralisation

### Question Q-3.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A compound of nitrogen and oxygen contains 30.4% nitrogen by mass. Its relative molecular mass is 92. (a) Calculate the empirical formula. (b) Calculate the molecular formula. (c) Write a balanced equation for the formation of this compound from nitrogen and oxygen.
**Answer**: (a) N: 30.4%, O: 69.6%. Moles N = 30.4/14 = 2.17. Moles O = 69.6/16 = 4.35. Ratio: N:O = 2.17:4.35 = 1:2. Empirical formula = NO2.
(b) Empirical mass = 14 + 32 = 46. n = 92/46 = 2. Molecular formula = N2O4.
(c) N2(g) + 2O2(g) → N2O4(g)
**Explanation**: This requires a full percentage composition calculation. A common error is not recognising N2O4 as the dimer of NO2.
**Tags**: empirical-formula, molecular-formula, nitrogen-oxide, calculation
