# Items: 10.1 — The atmosphere, air and water

### Question Q-10.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What are the two main gases in the atmosphere and their approximate percentages?
**Answer**: Nitrogen (~78%) and oxygen (~21%). The remaining ~1% includes argon, carbon dioxide, water vapour, and other trace gases.
**Explanation**: N₂ ≈ 78%, O₂ ≈ 21%. A common error is saying oxygen is the main component.
**Tags**: atmosphere, composition, nitrogen, oxygen

### Question Q-10.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: How is oxygen obtained from the air on an industrial scale?
**Answer**: By fractional distillation of liquefied air. Air is cooled to very low temperatures until it liquefies, then the liquid air is warmed and the components distil off at different boiling points (nitrogen boils off first at -196°C, then argon, then oxygen at -183°C).
**Explanation**: Fractional distillation based on different boiling points. A common error is saying electrolysis of water.
**Tags**: oxygen, fractional-distillation, air-separation

### Question Q-10.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe an experiment to determine the percentage of oxygen in air using copper.
**Answer**: Pass a known volume of air back and forth over heated copper. Copper reacts with oxygen to form copper(II) oxide: 2Cu + O₂ → 2CuO. Measure the decrease in gas volume. The percentage of oxygen = (volume decrease / original volume) × 100 ≈ 21%.
**Explanation**: Copper removes oxygen, volume drop = oxygen volume. A common error is not heating the copper.
**Tags**: oxygen-percentage, copper, experiment

### Question Q-10.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the carbon cycle briefly, explaining how carbon dioxide is removed from and returned to the atmosphere. Name one process that removes CO₂ and two processes that add it.
**Answer**: Carbon dioxide is removed from the atmosphere by photosynthesis in plants: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C6H12O6 + 6O₂. It is returned by: (1) respiration in living organisms (glucose + oxygen → CO₂ + water + energy), and (2) combustion of fossil fuels and biomass (hydrocarbons + oxygen → CO₂ + water). Additionally, decomposition of dead organisms releases CO₂.
**Explanation**: Balance of photosynthesis vs respiration/combustion. A common error is thinking only combustion produces CO₂, forgetting respiration.
**Tags**: carbon-cycle, photosynthesis, respiration, combustion, CO2

### Question Q-10.1-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the chemical test for pure water (not just the absence of dissolved substances)?
**Answer**: Pure water boils at exactly 100°C at standard pressure and freezes at exactly 0°C. These are the definitive tests. (The anhydrous CuSO₄ test confirms water is present but does not prove purity.)
**Explanation**: Fixed boiling/freezing points = purity. A common error is saying CuSO₄ test proves purity.
**Tags**: pure-water, boiling-point, test
