### Question Q-6.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why a catalyst does not change the position of equilibrium but does increase the rate at which equilibrium is reached.
**Answer**: A catalyst lowers the activation energy for both the forward and backward reactions by the same amount. This means both reactions speed up equally. The equilibrium position (the ratio of products to reactants at equilibrium) depends only on thermodynamics (temperature), not on the pathway. The catalyst simply allows the system to reach equilibrium faster.
**Explanation**: Students must explain that both directions are affected equally. A common error is saying the catalyst "favours" one direction.
**Tags**: catalyst, equilibrium, activation-energy, rate, explanation

### Question Q-6.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In the reaction Fe³⁺(aq) + SCN⁻(aq) ⇌ FeSCN²⁺(aq), the product FeSCN²⁺ is blood-red. Describe how you would use this equilibrium to demonstrate Le Chatelier's principle experimentally.
**Answer**: Set up: mix dilute FeCl3 and KSCN solutions to establish equilibrium (pale red colour).
Test 1: Add more Fe³⁺ — colour deepens (equilibrium shifts right).
Test 2: Add more SCN⁻ — colour deepens (shifts right).
Test 3: Add Ag⁺ (precipitates SCN⁻) — colour fades (shifts left).
Each change demonstrates the system responding to counteract the disturbance.
**Explanation**: Students must describe a practical experiment with predictions. A common error is not identifying what would be observed.
**Tags**: le-chatelier, experiment, colour-change, iron, demonstration

### Question Q-6.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: For the reaction 2SO2(g) + O2(g) ⇌ 2SO3(g) ΔH = −196 kJ/mol, the equilibrium mixture contains 0.40 mol SO2, 0.20 mol O2, and 0.60 mol SO3 in a 1 dm³ container. (a) Write the expression for Kc. (b) Calculate Kc and state its units. (c) What would happen to Kc if the temperature were increased?
**Answer**: (a) Kc = [SO3]² / ([SO2]² × [O2])
(b) Kc = (0.60)² / ((0.40)² × 0.20) = 0.36 / 0.032 = 11.25. Units: dm³/mol.
(c) The reaction is exothermic. Increasing temperature shifts equilibrium left. Kc decreases.
**Explanation**: Students must calculate Kc correctly and understand that Kc changes with temperature. A common error is saying Kc doesn't change.
**Tags**: equilibrium-constant, Kc, calculation, temperature-effect, contact-process

### Question Q-6.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Describe the Contact process for manufacturing sulfuric acid. Include the conditions used for the key step (2SO2 + O2 ⇌ 2SO3) and explain how Le Chatelier's principle justifies these conditions.
**Answer**: Key step: V2O5 catalyst at 450°C and 1-2 atm.
Le Chatelier: Low temperature favours SO3 (exothermic), but 450°C is a compromise for rate. High pressure favours SO3 (3→2 mol gas), but 1-2 atm gives sufficient yield at lower cost. Catalyst increases rate but not yield.
Other steps: burn S → SO2; dissolve SO3 in H2SO4 → oleum → dilute with water.
**Explanation**: Students must describe all steps and apply Le Chatelier. A common error is saying SO3 is dissolved directly in water.
**Tags**: contact-process, sulfuric-acid, le-chatelier, catalyst, conditions

### Question Q-6.2-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: The equilibrium N2O4(g) ⇌ 2NO2(g) is endothermic forward. N2O4 is colourless, NO2 is brown. A sealed tube at equilibrium is placed in (a) hot water and (b) ice water. Predict and explain the colour change in each case.
**Answer**: (a) Hot water: turns darker brown. Forward is endothermic, so increasing temperature shifts equilibrium right, producing more brown NO2.
(b) Ice water: turns paler. Decreasing temperature shifts equilibrium left (exothermic direction), producing more colourless N2O4.
**Explanation**: Students must link temperature to equilibrium shift and observable colour. A common error is predicting the wrong direction.
**Tags**: equilibrium, temperature, colour-change, le-chatelier, observation
