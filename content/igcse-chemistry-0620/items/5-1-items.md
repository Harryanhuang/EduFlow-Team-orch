# Items: 5.1 — Exothermic and endothermic reactions


### Question Q-5.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is an exothermic reaction? Give one example and describe the energy transfer.
**Answer**: An exothermic reaction releases energy to the surroundings (usually as heat). The temperature of the surroundings increases. Example: combustion of methane (CH4 + 2O2 → CO2 + 2H2O). The products have less energy than the reactants.
**Explanation**: Students must state that energy is released and temperature increases. A common error is saying "energy is created" — energy is transferred, not created.
**Tags**: exothermic, definition, energy-transfer, combustion

### Question Q-5.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is an endothermic reaction? Give one example.
**Answer**: An endothermic reaction absorbs energy from the surroundings. The temperature of the surroundings decreases. Example: thermal decomposition of calcium carbonate (CaCO3 → CaO + CO2) requires continuous heating.
**Explanation**: Students must state that energy is absorbed and temperature decreases. A common error is confusing endothermic with "needs heating to start" — some exothermic reactions also need activation energy.
**Tags**: endothermic, definition, energy-transfer, thermal-decomposition

### Question Q-5.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain, in terms of bond breaking and bond making, why some reactions are exothermic and some are endothermic.
**Answer**: Breaking bonds requires energy (endothermic). Making bonds releases energy (exothermic). If more energy is released from bond making than is absorbed in bond breaking, the overall reaction is exothermic. If more energy is absorbed in bond breaking than is released in bond making, the overall reaction is endothermic.
**Explanation**: Students must address both bond breaking AND bond making. A common error is saying "breaking bonds releases energy" — it requires energy.
**Tags**: bond-energy, bond-breaking, bond-making, exothermic, endothermic

### Question Q-5.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Use the following bond energies to calculate the enthalpy change for the reaction: H2 + Cl2 → 2HCl. Bond energies: H−H = 436 kJ/mol, Cl−Cl = 242 kJ/mol, H−Cl = 431 kJ/mol. State whether the reaction is exothermic or endothermic.
**Answer**: Bonds broken: H−H (436) + Cl−Cl (242) = 678 kJ/mol.
Bonds made: 2 × H−Cl (2 × 431) = 862 kJ/mol.
ΔH = 678 − 862 = −184 kJ/mol.
The reaction is exothermic (ΔH is negative, more energy released than absorbed).
**Explanation**: Students must use ΔH = bonds broken − bonds made. A common error is reversing the formula or not multiplying H−Cl by 2.
**Tags**: bond-energy, calculation, enthalpy, exothermic
