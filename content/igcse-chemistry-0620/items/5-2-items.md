# Items: 5.2 — Rate of reaction, collision theory, catalysts

### Question Q-5.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is collision theory?
**Answer**: Collision theory states that for a chemical reaction to occur, reactant particles must collide with sufficient energy (activation energy) and the correct orientation.
**Explanation**: Collisions + energy + orientation. A common error is saying "any collision causes a reaction."
**Tags**: collision-theory, activation-energy, definition

### Question Q-5.2-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why increasing the concentration of a reactant increases the rate of reaction.
**Answer**: Higher concentration means more reactant particles per unit volume. This leads to more frequent collisions per unit time, increasing the rate of reaction.
**Explanation**: More particles → more collisions → faster rate. A common error is saying particles move "faster."
**Tags**: concentration, rate, collision-theory

### Question Q-5.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why increasing the temperature increases the rate of reaction (give two reasons).
**Answer**: (1) Particles have more kinetic energy, so more collisions have energy greater than the activation energy. (2) Particles move faster, so collisions are more frequent. The first reason has the greater effect.
**Explanation**: Energy per collision + frequency. A common error is only mentioning collision frequency.
**Tags**: temperature, rate, activation-energy, collision-theory

### Question Q-5.2-04
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is a catalyst?
**Answer**: A catalyst is a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without being used up itself. It provides an alternative reaction pathway with a lower activation energy.
**Explanation**: Not consumed; lowers activation energy. A common error is saying catalysts "increase the yield."
**Tags**: catalyst, definition, activation-energy

### Question Q-5.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain, in terms of activation energy, how a catalyst increases the rate of reaction.
**Answer**: A catalyst provides an alternative reaction pathway with a lower activation energy. More reactant particles now have enough energy to react, so more collisions per unit time are successful.
**Explanation**: Lower activation energy → more successful collisions. A common error is saying catalysts "increase the energy of particles."
**Tags**: catalyst, activation-energy, pathway, rate

### Question Q-5.2-06
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Why are catalysts important in industrial processes?
**Answer**: Catalysts lower the activation energy, allowing reactions to occur at lower temperatures. This reduces energy costs and makes the process more economical and environmentally friendly.
**Explanation**: Lower temperature = lower cost. A common error is saying catalysts "produce more product."
**Tags**: catalyst, industry, economics
