# Items: 4.2 — Electrochemical cells, electroplating

### Question Q-4.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is a simple electrochemical cell?
**Answer**: A device that converts chemical energy into electrical energy using two different metals (electrodes) immersed in an electrolyte. The more reactive metal acts as the negative electrode (anode) and the less reactive as the positive electrode (cathode).
**Explanation**: Chemical → electrical energy. A common error is saying it "stores" electricity.
**Tags**: electrochemical-cell, definition, energy-conversion

### Question Q-4.2-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In a simple cell with zinc and copper electrodes, which is the negative electrode and why?
**Answer**: Zinc is the negative electrode because it is more reactive than copper. Zinc loses electrons more readily (Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻), and the electrons flow through the external circuit to the copper electrode.
**Explanation**: More reactive = negative electrode. A common error is saying the less reactive metal is negative.
**Tags**: reactivity-series, zinc, copper, cell

### Question Q-4.2-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is electroplating? Give one reason for electroplating.
**Answer**: Electroplating is the process of coating an object with a thin layer of metal using electrolysis. Reasons: improve appearance (e.g., silver plating), prevent corrosion (e.g., tin plating), reduce friction.
**Explanation**: Uses electrolysis to deposit metal. A common error is saying it "makes the object stronger."
**Tags**: electroplating, definition, applications

### Question Q-4.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how you would electroplate a steel fork with silver.
**Answer**: The fork (object to be plated) is the cathode. A piece of silver is the anode. The electrolyte is aqueous silver nitrate. At the anode: Ag → Ag⁺ + e⁻ (silver dissolves). At the cathode: Ag⁺ + e⁻ → Ag (silver deposits on the fork).
**Explanation**: Object = cathode; plating metal = anode; matching electrolyte. A common error is making the fork the anode.
**Tags**: electroplating, silver, setup, half-equations

### Question Q-4.2-05
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Why is electroplating with chromium useful for car parts?
**Answer**: Chromium is very hard, resistant to corrosion, and has a shiny appearance. Electroplating car parts with chromium protects them from rusting and gives an attractive finish.
**Explanation**: Hard + shiny + corrosion-resistant. A common error is saying chromium makes the part lighter.
**Tags**: chromium, electroplating, applications, corrosion
