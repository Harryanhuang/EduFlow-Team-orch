# Items: 4.4 — Uses of electrolysis

### Question Q-4.2-06
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Give two uses of electrolysis.
**Answer**: (1) Extraction of reactive metals (e.g., aluminium from aluminium oxide). (2) Electroplating (coating objects with a thin layer of metal). Also: purification of copper, production of chlorine and sodium hydroxide.
**Explanation**: Extraction + plating + purification. A common error is saying electrolysis is used for distillation.
**Tags**: electrolysis, applications, uses

### Question Q-4.2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the electrolytic purification of copper.
**Answer**: Impure copper is the anode; pure copper is the cathode; copper(II) sulfate is the electrolyte. At the anode: Cu → Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ (impure copper dissolves, impurities fall as anode sludge). At the cathode: Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu (pure copper deposits). The anode loses mass; the cathode gains mass.
**Explanation**: Impure → anode; pure → cathode. A common error is reversing the electrodes.
**Tags**: copper-purification, electrolysis, anode, cathode

### Question Q-4.2-08
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Why is aluminium used for overhead power cables?
**Answer**: Aluminium is a good conductor of electricity and has a low density (lightweight), making it suitable for overhead cables where weight is important. It is also resistant to corrosion due to its oxide layer.
**Explanation**: Conductor + lightweight. A common error is saying it is the "best" conductor (copper is better but heavier).
**Tags**: aluminium, conductivity, cables, properties
