# Items: 8.2 — The Periodic Table: Group 7 (halogens)

### Question Q-8.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: List the halogens in order and describe the trend in colour and physical state.
**Answer**: Fluorine (pale yellow gas), chlorine (green gas), bromine (red-brown liquid), iodine (grey-black solid). As you go down the group, they become darker in colour and change from gas to liquid to solid.
**Explanation**: Darker + denser down the group. A common error is saying bromine is a gas.
**Tags**: halogens, colour, physical-state, trend

### Question Q-8.2-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is a displacement reaction? Write the equation for chlorine displacing bromide from potassium bromide solution.
**Answer**: A more reactive halogen displaces a less reactive halogen from its salt solution. Cl₂(aq) + 2KBr(aq) → 2KCl(aq) + Br₂(aq). The solution turns orange-brown due to bromine.
**Explanation**: More reactive displaces less reactive. A common error is not balancing the equation.
**Tags**: displacement, halogens, chlorine, bromide, equation

### Question Q-8.2-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: How does the reactivity of halogens change down the group?
**Answer**: Reactivity decreases down the group. Fluorine is the most reactive and iodine the least. The outer shell is further from the nucleus in larger atoms, making it harder to attract the electron needed for reaction.
**Explanation**: Decreases down the group. A common error is saying it increases (confusing with Group 1).
**Tags**: halogens, reactivity-trend, group-7

### Question Q-8.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Iodine does not displace chlorine from potassium chloride solution. Explain why.
**Answer**: Iodine is less reactive than chlorine (it is below chlorine in Group 7). A less reactive halogen cannot displace a more reactive halogen from its salt solution.
**Explanation**: Reactivity order: Cl > Br > I. A common error is saying iodine "isn't soluble enough."
**Tags**: displacement, iodine, chlorine, reactivity
