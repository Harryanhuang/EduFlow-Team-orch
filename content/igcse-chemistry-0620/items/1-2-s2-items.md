# Items: 1.4 — Solutions, solubility, crystallisation

### Question Q-1.2-07
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Define the terms "solute," "solvent," and "solution."
**Answer**: Solute: the substance that dissolves. Solvent: the liquid that does the dissolving. Solution: the mixture of solute dissolved in solvent.
**Explanation**: Solute + solvent = solution. A common error is mixing up solute and solvent.
**Tags**: solute, solvent, solution, definition

### Question Q-1.2-08
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is meant by the solubility of a substance?
**Answer**: Solubility is the maximum mass of a substance that can dissolve in 100 g of water (or solvent) at a given temperature to form a saturated solution.
**Explanation**: Maximum mass per 100 g solvent at a temperature. A common error is saying "any amount" can dissolve.
**Tags**: solubility, saturated, definition

### Question Q-1.2-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how you would obtain pure salt crystals from a salt solution.
**Answer**: (1) Heat the solution to evaporate some water (concentrate). (2) Stop heating when crystals begin to form at the edges (crystallisation point). (3) Leave to cool slowly so large, pure crystals form. (4) Filter, wash with a little cold water, and dry.
**Explanation**: Crystallisation, not evaporation to dryness. A common error is evaporating to complete dryness.
**Tags**: crystallisation, pure-crystals, method

### Question Q-1.2-10
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does temperature affect the solubility of most solids in water?
**Answer**: Increasing temperature generally increases the solubility of solids in water. More solid can dissolve at higher temperatures.
**Explanation**: Hotter = more soluble (for most solids). A common error is saying temperature has no effect.
**Tags**: solubility, temperature, trend
