# Items: 9.3 — Corrosion, rusting, sacrificial protection

### Question Q-9.3-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What are the two essential conditions for iron to rust?
**Answer**: Iron rusts only when both oxygen (from air) and water are present. The chemical reaction is: 4Fe + 3O₂ + 2xH₂O → 2Fe₂O₃·xH₂O (hydrated iron(III) oxide).
**Explanation**: Both air AND water needed. A common error is saying only water is needed.
**Tags**: rusting, conditions, iron, oxygen, water

### Question Q-9.3-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe two methods to prevent rusting and explain how each works.
**Answer**: (1) Barrier methods: paint, oil, grease, or plastic coating — these physically block oxygen and water from reaching the iron surface. (2) Sacrificial protection: attach a more reactive metal (zinc, magnesium) to the iron — the more reactive metal corrodes preferentially, protecting the iron.
**Explanation**: Block contact or sacrifice. A common error is saying galvanising is just a barrier (it's also sacrificial).
**Tags**: rust-prevention, barrier, sacrificial, galvanising

### Question Q-9.3-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is galvanising? Explain why it protects iron from rusting even if the coating is scratched.
**Answer**: Galvanising is coating iron with a layer of zinc. The zinc acts as a physical barrier. If scratched, zinc still protects because it is more reactive than iron — zinc corrodes preferentially (sacrificial protection), losing electrons before iron does.
**Explanation**: Barrier + sacrificial. A common error is saying zinc is "less reactive."
**Tags**: galvanising, zinc, sacrificial-protection, rust

### Question Q-9.3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why a ship's hull is protected by attaching blocks of zinc.
**Answer**: Zinc is more reactive than iron (higher in the reactivity series). The zinc blocks lose electrons to the iron, so the zinc corrodes (Zn → Zn²⁺ + 2e⁻) while the iron is protected. The zinc must be replaced periodically as it is consumed.
**Explanation**: Sacrificial anode, zinc corrodes instead of iron. A common error is saying zinc "repels water."
**Tags**: sacrificial-protection, ship, zinc, hull

### Question Q-9.3-05
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is rust chemically? Give its formula.
**Answer**: Rust is hydrated iron(III) oxide, Fe₂O₃·xH₂O. It is a reddish-brown, flaky substance that forms on the surface of iron and steel when exposed to air and water.
**Explanation**: Hydrated iron(III) oxide. A common error is saying iron(II) oxide.
**Tags**: rust, formula, iron-oxide, hydrated
