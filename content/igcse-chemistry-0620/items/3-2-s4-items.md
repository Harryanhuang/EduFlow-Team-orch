# Items: 3.6 — Limiting reactants

### Question Q-3.2-16
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is a limiting reactant? Explain why it determines the amount of product formed.
**Answer**: The limiting reactant is the substance that is completely used up first in a chemical reaction. Once it is consumed, the reaction stops, so no more product can form. Other reactants are left in excess.
**Explanation**: First-to-run-out controls yield. A common error is saying the reactant with the smallest mass is limiting.
**Tags**: limiting-reactant, definition, yield

### Question Q-3.2-17
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: 2.0 g of magnesium reacts with excess oxygen. Calculate the maximum mass of magnesium oxide produced. (Mg = 24, O = 16)
**Answer**: 3.33 g
**Explanation**: Mg moles = 2.0/24 = 0.0833. From 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO: 2 mol Mg → 2 mol MgO, so 0.0833 mol MgO. Mass = 0.0833 × 40 = 3.33 g. A common error is using MgO mass as 24 instead of 40.
**Tags**: limiting-reactant, MgO, calculation, moles

### Question Q-3.2-18
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: 10.0 g of calcium carbonate reacts with 50 cm³ of 2.0 mol/dm³ hydrochloric acid. Determine the limiting reactant. (Ca = 40, C = 12, O = 16)
**Answer**: CaCO₃ moles = 10.0/100 = 0.10 mol. HCl moles = 2.0 × 0.050 = 0.10 mol. Equation: CaCO₃ + 2HCl → CaCl₂ + H₂O + CO₂. Ratio: 1 mol CaCO₃ needs 2 mol HCl. 0.10 mol CaCO₃ needs 0.20 mol HCl but only 0.10 mol is available. HCl is the limiting reactant.
**Explanation**: Compare mole ratios. A common error is comparing moles directly without the stoichiometric ratio.
**Tags**: limiting-reactant, HCl, CaCO3, calculation

### Question Q-3.2-19
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: 4.6 g of sodium reacts with 3.2 g of sulfur to form sodium sulfide (Na₂S). Which reactant is limiting? (Na = 23, S = 32)
**Answer**: Na moles = 4.6/23 = 0.20. S moles = 3.2/32 = 0.10. Equation: 2Na + S → Na₂S. Need 2 mol Na per 1 mol S. 0.20 mol Na needs 0.10 mol S — exactly the amount available. Neither is limiting (they are in exact stoichiometric ratio).
**Explanation**: Both consumed completely. A common error is picking one anyway.
**Tags**: limiting-reactant, Na2S, stoichiometric
