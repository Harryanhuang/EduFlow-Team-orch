# Items: 6.5 — Extraction and recycling of metals

### Question Q-9.2-10
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Give two advantages of recycling metals.
**Answer**: (1) Saves energy — extracting metal from recycled material requires much less energy than from ore. (2) Conserves natural resources — reduces the need to mine new ore. Also: reduces landfill waste and pollution from mining.
**Explanation**: Energy + resource conservation. A common error is saying recycling makes the metal "purer."
**Tags**: recycling, metals, advantages

### Question Q-9.2-11
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why recycling aluminium saves 95% of the energy compared to extracting it from bauxite.
**Answer**: Extracting aluminium from bauxite requires electrolysis of molten Al₂O₃ at 900°C, which needs enormous amounts of electricity. Recycling only requires melting the already-extracted aluminium, which uses far less energy.
**Explanation**: Electrolysis vs. simple melting. A common error is saying recycling "requires no energy."
**Tags**: aluminium, recycling, energy, electrolysis

### Question Q-9.2-12
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is an ore?
**Answer**: An ore is a rock or mineral from which a metal (or other valuable substance) can be extracted profitably.
**Explanation**: Economically viable source. A common error is saying "any rock containing a metal."
**Tags**: ore, definition, extraction
