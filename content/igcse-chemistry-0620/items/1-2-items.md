# Items: 1.2 — Diffusion, dissolving, chromatography

### Question Q-1.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is diffusion?
**Answer**: Diffusion is the net movement of particles from a region of higher concentration to a region of lower concentration, caused by their random movement.
**Explanation**: Net movement down a concentration gradient. A common error is saying particles move "only from high to low" — they move both ways, but net is high→low.
**Tags**: diffusion, definition, concentration-gradient

### Question Q-1.2-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In an experiment, ammonia gas (Mᵣ = 17) and hydrogen chloride gas (Mᵣ = 36.5) are released at opposite ends of a glass tube. Where does the white ring of ammonium chloride form, closer to which end? Explain.
**Answer**: Closer to the hydrogen chloride end. Ammonia molecules have a lower relative mass (17) and therefore diffuse faster than the heavier HCl molecules (36.5). They meet closer to the HCl end.
**Explanation**: Lighter = faster diffusion. A common error is saying the ring forms in the middle.
**Tags**: diffusion, molecular-mass, rate

### Question Q-1.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why does sugar dissolve faster in hot water than in cold water?
**Answer**: Water particles in hot water have more kinetic energy and move faster. They collide with sugar particles more frequently and with greater energy, breaking them apart more quickly.
**Explanation**: Temperature increases particle speed. A common error is saying hot water "is thinner."
**Tags**: dissolving, temperature, rate

### Question Q-1.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the purpose of the baseline in paper chromatography? Why is it drawn in pencil?
**Answer**: The baseline marks the starting position of the samples. It is drawn in pencil because pencil graphite does not dissolve in the solvent, whereas ink would dissolve and interfere with the chromatogram.
**Explanation**: Pencil = inert. A common error is saying pencil "shows up better."
**Tags**: chromatography, baseline, pencil

### Question Q-1.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A chromatogram shows two spots for substance A and three spots for substance B. What can you conclude about A and B?
**Answer**: Substance A is a mixture of at least two components. Substance B is a mixture of at least three components. Pure substances produce a single spot on a chromatogram.
**Explanation**: Number of spots = number of components. A common error is saying "more spots = purer."
**Tags**: chromatography, purity, mixture

### Question Q-1.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the Rf value in chromatography? Calculate it for a substance that moves 4 cm from the baseline when the solvent front travels 8 cm.
**Answer**: Rf = distance moved by substance / distance moved by solvent front. Rf = 4/8 = 0.5. Rf values are always between 0 and 1.
**Explanation**: Dimensionless ratio. A common error is swapping numerator and denominator.
**Tags**: Rf-value, chromatography, calculation
