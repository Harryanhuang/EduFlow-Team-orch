# Items: 4.1 — Electrolysis: principles and predictions


### Question Q-4.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is electrolysis? What type of compounds can undergo electrolysis?
**Answer**: Electrolysis is the breakdown of an ionic compound using electricity. The compound must be molten or dissolved in water so that the ions are free to move. Direct current (DC) is used to pass electricity through the compound.
**Explanation**: Students must identify that the compound must be ionic AND molten/dissolved. A common error is saying any liquid can be electrolysed.
**Tags**: electrolysis, definition, ionic-compound, molten, aqueous

### Question Q-4.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: During electrolysis, where do reduction and oxidation occur? State the rule for which ions go to each electrode.
**Answer**: Reduction occurs at the cathode (negative electrode) — positive ions (cations) are attracted here and gain electrons. Oxidation occurs at the anode (positive electrode) — negative ions (anions) are attracted here and lose electrons. Remember: PANIC — Positive Anode, Negative Is Cathode.
**Explanation**: Students must link electrode charge to ion attraction. A common error is confusing anode and cathode charges.
**Tags**: electrolysis, anode, cathode, reduction, oxidation, PANIC

### Question Q-4.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Predict the products of the electrolysis of molten lead(II) bromide (PbBr2). Write the half-equations for each electrode.
**Answer**: At the cathode: Pb²⁺(l) + 2e⁻ → Pb(l) — lead metal is produced (reduction).
At the anode: 2Br⁻(l) → Br2(g) + 2e⁻ — bromine gas is produced (oxidation).
Molten electrolysis produces the element at each electrode — no water to compete.
**Explanation**: Students must write correct half-equations with state symbols. A common error is writing Br⁻ → Br + e⁻ (not forming Br2 molecules).
**Tags**: electrolysis, lead-bromide, half-equation, molten, prediction

### Question Q-4.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Predict the products of the electrolysis of concentrated aqueous sodium chloride (brine). Explain why these products form instead of sodium metal.
**Answer**: At the cathode: hydrogen gas (H2) is produced. H⁺ ions from water are preferentially discharged over Na⁺ because hydrogen is lower in the reactivity series.
At the anode: chlorine gas (Cl2) is produced. Cl⁻ ions are discharged because the solution is concentrated.
Water is present, so H⁺ is preferentially discharged at the cathode rather than Na⁺.
**Explanation**: Students must explain preferential discharge. A common error is predicting sodium metal (which would only form from molten NaCl).
**Tags**: electrolysis, brine, aqueous, preferential-discharge, chlorine, hydrogen
