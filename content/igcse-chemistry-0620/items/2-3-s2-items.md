# Items: 2.6 — Metallic bonding and properties

### Question Q-2.3-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why are metals good conductors of heat?
**Answer**: The delocalised electrons in metallic bonding transfer kinetic energy rapidly through the structure. When one part is heated, the electrons gain energy and move, distributing heat throughout the metal.
**Explanation**: Delocalised electrons transfer energy. A common error is saying "atoms vibrate faster" (that's how non-metals conduct).
**Tags**: metals, thermal-conductivity, delocalised-electrons

### Question Q-2.3-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why sodium (a Group 1 metal) has a lower melting point than iron (a transition metal).
**Answer**: Sodium has only one delocalised electron per atom and forms a 1+ ion, giving weaker metallic bonding. Iron has more delocalised electrons (from both 4s and 3d orbitals) and forms stronger metallic bonds, giving a higher melting point.
**Explanation**: More delocalised electrons = stronger bonding. A common error is saying sodium has "fewer atoms."
**Tags**: sodium, iron, melting-point, metallic-bonding

### Question Q-2.3-10
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Why can metals be bent without breaking?
**Answer**: The layers of positive metal ions can slide over each other while remaining surrounded by the sea of delocalised electrons. The metallic bonding is maintained throughout the deformation.
**Explanation**: Sliding layers + maintained bonding. A common error is saying the bonds "stretch."
**Tags**: metals, malleable, ductile, metallic-bonding
