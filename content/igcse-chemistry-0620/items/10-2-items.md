# Items: 10.2 — Pollution: greenhouse effect, acid rain

### Question Q-10.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What causes acid rain?
**Answer**: Acid rain is caused by sulfur dioxide (SO₂) and nitrogen oxides (NOₓ) being released into the atmosphere (from burning fossil fuels). These gases dissolve in rainwater to form sulfuric acid and nitric acid.
**Explanation**: SO₂ + NOₓ → acids in rain. A common error is saying CO₂ causes acid rain.
**Tags**: acid-rain, SO2, NOx, cause

### Question Q-10.2-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Write the equation for the formation of sulfuric acid from sulfur dioxide in the atmosphere.
**Answer**: 2SO₂ + O₂ → 2SO₃ (in the atmosphere), then SO₃ + H₂O → H₂SO₄. Alternatively: SO₂ + H₂O → H₂SO₃ (sulfurous acid), then oxidised to H₂SO₄.
**Explanation**: Two-step process: oxidation then hydration. A common error is writing SO₂ + H₂O → H₂SO₄ directly.
**Tags**: acid-rain, sulfuric-acid, equation

### Question Q-10.2-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: State two harmful effects of acid rain.
**Answer**: (1) It damages buildings and statues made of limestone/marble (calcium carbonate reacts with acid). (2) It kills fish and aquatic life by acidifying lakes and rivers. Also: damages trees, corrodes metal structures.
**Explanation**: Chemical + ecological damage. A common error is saying it "causes global warming."
**Tags**: acid-rain, effects, environmental

### Question Q-10.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does carbon dioxide contribute to climate change?
**Answer**: CO₂ is a greenhouse gas that absorbs and re-emits infrared radiation, trapping heat in the atmosphere. Increased CO₂ from burning fossil fuels and deforestation enhances the natural greenhouse effect, leading to global warming and climate change.
**Explanation**: Traps infrared radiation. A common error is saying CO₂ "reflects sunlight."
**Tags**: CO2, greenhouse-gas, climate-change, mechanism

### Question Q-10.2-05
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is one way to reduce the amount of sulfur dioxide released from power stations?
**Answer**: Flue gas desulfurisation — passing the waste gases through a scrubber containing calcium oxide (lime) or calcium carbonate, which reacts with SO₂ to form calcium sulfate (gypsum), preventing it from entering the atmosphere.
**Explanation**: Remove SO₂ before it escapes. A common error is saying "burn less coal" (that's reduction of use, not removal).
**Tags**: SO2, flue-gas-desulfurisation, pollution-control
