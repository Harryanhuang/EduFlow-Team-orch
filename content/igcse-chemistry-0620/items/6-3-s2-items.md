# Items: 6.3 — Redox (supplementary)


### Question Q-6.3-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Identify which substance is oxidised and which is reduced in each reaction: (a) 2Fe2O3 + 3C → 4Fe + 3CO2 (b) CuO + H2 → Cu + H2O. Explain your answers in terms of oxygen transfer.
**Answer**: (a) Carbon is oxidised (gains oxygen → CO2). Iron(III) oxide is reduced (loses oxygen → Fe).
(b) Hydrogen is oxidised (gains oxygen → H2O). Copper(II) oxide is reduced (loses oxygen → Cu).
Oxidation is gain of oxygen; reduction is loss of oxygen.
**Explanation**: Students must identify both processes in each reaction. A common error is only identifying oxidation or only reduction.
**Tags**: redox, oxidation, reduction, oxygen-transfer

### Question Q-6.3-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Define oxidation and reduction in terms of electron transfer. Use the reaction Zn + CuSO4 → ZnSO4 + Cu to illustrate your answer by writing the two half-equations.
**Answer**: Oxidation is loss of electrons (OIL). Reduction is gain of electrons (RIG).
Oxidation half-equation: Zn(s) → Zn²⁺(aq) + 2e⁻
Reduction half-equation: Cu²⁺(aq) + 2e⁻ → Cu(s)
Zinc loses electrons (oxidised). Copper(II) ions gain electrons (reduced).
**Explanation**: Students must write correct half-equations with state symbols. Common errors: reversing the half-equations or getting the charge wrong (e.g. Zn⁺ instead of Zn²⁺).
**Tags**: redox, electron-transfer, half-equation, OIL-RIG

### Question Q-6.3-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: In the reaction 2FeCl2 + Cl2 → 2FeCl3: (a) Identify the oxidising agent. (b) Explain your answer in terms of electron transfer. (c) Write the ionic equation and identify which species is oxidised.
**Answer**: (a) Chlorine (Cl2) is the oxidising agent.
(b) Cl2 causes Fe²⁺ to lose an electron (become Fe³⁺) and is itself reduced to Cl⁻. An oxidising agent accepts electrons and is reduced.
(c) Ionic equation: 2Fe²⁺(aq) + Cl2(aq) → 2Fe³⁺(aq) + 2Cl⁻(aq). Fe²⁺ is oxidised (loses an electron, oxidation state increases from +2 to +3).
**Explanation**: Students must identify the oxidising agent by what it does (accepts electrons), not by what it is. A common error is naming FeCl2 as the oxidising agent.
**Tags**: redox, oxidising-agent, electron-transfer, oxidation-state, ionic-equation

### Question Q-6.3-09
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Potassium iodide solution reacts with acidified potassium manganate(VII). The solution changes colour from purple to brown. (a) What causes the brown colour? (b) Is the iodide ion oxidised or reduced? Explain in terms of electrons. (c) What would you observe if you added starch solution to the product?
**Answer**: (a) The brown colour is due to iodine (I2) being formed.
(b) The iodide ion (I⁻) is oxidised — it loses electrons: 2I⁻ → I2 + 2e⁻. Oxidation is loss of electrons.
(c) Starch solution would turn blue-black, confirming the presence of iodine.
**Explanation**: Students must link the colour change to the redox process. Common errors: saying iodide is reduced (confusing the direction) or not knowing the starch-iodine test.
**Tags**: redox, iodide, manganate, starch-test, colour-change
