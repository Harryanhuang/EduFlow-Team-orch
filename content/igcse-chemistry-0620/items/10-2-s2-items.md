# Items: 10.4 — Carbon cycle, photosynthesis, respiration

### Question Q-10.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain how photosynthesis and respiration are linked in the carbon cycle.
**Answer**: Photosynthesis removes CO₂ from the atmosphere: plants use CO₂ + H₂O + sunlight → glucose + O₂. Respiration returns CO₂: organisms break down glucose + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O + energy. These two processes balance atmospheric CO₂ levels.
**Explanation**: CO₂ out (photosynthesis) vs CO₂ in (respiration). A common error is saying they are the reverse reaction (they are not exact reverses).
**Tags**: carbon-cycle, photosynthesis, respiration, CO2

### Question Q-10.2-07
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Name three processes that release carbon dioxide into the atmosphere.
**Answer**: (1) Respiration of living organisms. (2) Combustion of fossil fuels and wood. (3) Decomposition of dead organic matter by microorganisms.
**Explanation**: Living, burning, rotting. A common error is including photosynthesis (it absorbs CO₂).
**Tags**: CO2, sources, carbon-cycle

### Question Q-10.2-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does deforestation affect the carbon cycle?
**Answer**: Fewer trees means less photosynthesis, so less CO₂ is removed from the atmosphere. Burning cleared trees also releases stored carbon as CO₂. This increases atmospheric CO₂ concentration, contributing to the greenhouse effect.
**Explanation**: Less uptake + more release. A common error is saying it increases oxygen.
**Tags**: deforestation, carbon-cycle, greenhouse

### Question Q-10.2-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Write the equation for the complete combustion of methane.
**Answer**: CH₄(g) + 2O₂(g) → CO₂(g) + 2H₂O(g). One mole of methane reacts with 2 moles of oxygen to produce 1 mole of carbon dioxide and 2 moles of water vapour. The reaction is exothermic.
**Explanation**: Hydrocarbon + oxygen → CO₂ + H₂O. A common error is forgetting to balance the oxygen.
**Tags**: methane, combustion, equation, carbon-cycle
