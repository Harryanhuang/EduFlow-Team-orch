# Items: 11.1 — Fuels, crude oil, alkanes

### Question Q-11.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is crude oil and how is it separated into useful fractions?
**Answer**: Crude oil is a mixture of hydrocarbons. It is separated by fractional distillation, based on the different boiling points of the components. The oil is heated, vapours rise up a fractionating column, and different fractions condense at different heights.
**Explanation**: Fractional distillation by boiling point. A common error is saying it is separated by density.
**Tags**: crude-oil, fractional-distillation, hydrocarbons

### Question Q-11.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Name three fractions obtained from crude oil and give one use for each.
**Answer**: (1) Refinery gas — used for heating and cooking. (2) Gasoline/petrol — used as fuel for cars. (3) Diesel — used as fuel for lorries and trains. Also: kerosene (aircraft fuel), bitumen (road surfacing).
**Explanation**: Different fractions = different uses. A common error is confusing kerosene with diesel.
**Tags**: fractions, uses, crude-oil

### Question Q-11.1-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the general formula for alkanes?
**Answer**: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂, where n is the number of carbon atoms.
**Explanation**: Saturated hydrocarbons. A common error is CₙH₂ₙ (that's alkenes).
**Tags**: alkanes, general-formula, saturated

### Question Q-11.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Write the balanced equation for the complete combustion of propane (C₃H₈).
**Answer**: C₃H₈ + 5O₂ → 3CO₂ + 4H₂O
**Explanation**: Carbon: 3 on each side. Hydrogen: 8 on each side. Oxygen: 10 on each side (5×2 = 3×2 + 4). A common error is using 4O₂ instead of 5O₂.
**Tags**: combustion, propane, equation

### Question Q-11.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is cracking? Why is it useful?
**Answer**: Cracking is the process of breaking down long-chain hydrocarbons into shorter, more useful hydrocarbons (including alkenes). It is useful because there is higher demand for short-chain hydrocarbons (e.g., petrol) than are naturally present in crude oil.
**Explanation**: Long → short + alkene. A common error is saying cracking "combines" molecules.
**Tags**: cracking, long-chain, short-chain, alkenes

### Question Q-11.1-06
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Name the homologous series to which methane, ethane, and propane belong.
**Answer**: Alkanes.
**Explanation**: Saturated hydrocarbons. A common error is saying "hydrocarbons" (too broad — not a homologous series name).
**Tags**: alkanes, homologous-series, methane
