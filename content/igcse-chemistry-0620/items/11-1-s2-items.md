# Items: 11.1 — Fuels crude oil alkanes (supplementary)


### Question Q-11.1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain the process of fractional distillation of crude oil. Why do different fractions separate at different heights in the fractionating column?
**Answer**: Crude oil is heated and vaporised. The vapour enters the bottom of a fractionating column that is hotter at the bottom and cooler at the top. As vapour rises, it cools. Fractions with higher boiling points (longer-chain molecules) condense lower down. Fractions with lower boiling points (shorter-chain molecules) rise higher before condensing. Each fraction is collected at its condensation level.
**Explanation**: Students must link chain length to boiling point to column height. A common error is saying fractions separate by "density" rather than boiling point.
**Tags**: fractional-distillation, crude-oil, boiling-point, chain-length

### Question Q-11.1-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the trend in properties of alkanes as the chain length increases. Include viscosity, volatility, boiling point, and flammability.
**Answer**: As alkane chain length increases: viscosity increases (longer chains tangle more, flow less easily); volatility decreases (harder to evaporate); boiling point increases (stronger intermolecular forces between longer chains); flammability decreases (harder to ignite, burns with a more smoky flame).
**Explanation**: Students must describe all four trends. A common error is saying "longer chains are more flammable" — shorter chains are actually more flammable.
**Tags**: alkane, trend, viscosity, volatility, boiling-point, flammability

### Question Q-11.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Cracking converts long-chain alkanes into shorter, more useful hydrocarbons. (a) Explain why cracking is economically important. (b) Write a balanced equation for the cracking of dodecane (C12H26) to produce octane (C8H18) and one other product. (c) How could you test that the product contains an alkene?
**Answer**: (a) Crude oil contains a surplus of long-chain fractions (less demand) and a shortage of short-chain fractions (high demand for fuels like petrol). Cracking converts the surplus into the more valuable short-chain products.
(b) C12H26 → C8H18 + C4H8 (butene)
(c) Add bromine water — it decolourises from orange/brown to colourless if an alkene is present.
**Explanation**: Students must explain the economic rationale and write a balanced cracking equation. A common error is producing only alkanes (cracking always produces at least one alkene).
**Tags**: cracking, alkane, alkene, bromine-test, economic
