# Items: 3.3 — Empirical and molecular formulae (supplementary batch)


### Question Q-3.3-05
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the empirical formula of a compound?
**Answer**: The empirical formula shows the simplest whole-number ratio of atoms of each element present in a compound.
**Explanation**: Empirical = simplest ratio. A common error is confusing it with the molecular formula, which gives the actual number of atoms.
**Tags**: empirical-formula, definition

### Question Q-3.3-06
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Glucose has the molecular formula C6H12O6. What is its empirical formula?
**Answer**: CH2O. Divide all subscripts (6, 12, 6) by their highest common factor, which is 6, to get the simplest ratio 1:2:1.
**Explanation**: A common error is giving the molecular formula as the answer. Always simplify to the smallest whole-number ratio.
**Tags**: empirical-formula, calculation

### Question Q-3.3-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A compound contains 40.0% carbon, 6.7% hydrogen and 53.3% oxygen by mass. Its relative molecular mass is 180. Determine the molecular formula of the compound.
**Answer**: Step 1: Assume 100 g sample → C: 40.0 g, H: 6.7 g, O: 53.3 g.
Step 2: Moles → C: 40.0/12.0 = 3.33, H: 6.7/1.0 = 6.7, O: 53.3/16.0 = 3.33.
Step 3: Divide by smallest (3.33) → C:1, H:2, O:1. Empirical formula = CH2O.
Step 4: Empirical mass = 12+2+16 = 30. n = 180/30 = 6.
Molecular formula = C6H12O6.
**Explanation**: This requires a full 4-step calculation. Common errors: using atomic number instead of Ar, rounding too early, or forgetting to multiply the empirical formula by n.
**Tags**: empirical-formula, molecular-formula, percentage-composition, calculation

### Question Q-3.3-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: 0.46 g of sodium reacts with 0.32 g of oxygen to form a sodium oxide. Calculate the empirical formula of the oxide. (Ar: Na = 23, O = 16)
**Answer**: Step 1: Moles of Na = 0.46/23 = 0.020 mol. Moles of O = 0.32/16 = 0.020 mol.
Step 2: Ratio Na:O = 0.020:0.020 = 1:1.
Empirical formula = NaO.
(Note: The actual sodium oxide formed is likely Na2O — this is based on the given experimental data.)
**Explanation**: Students must use mass/Ar to find moles and then the ratio. A common error is using the mass ratio directly instead of converting to moles first.
**Tags**: empirical-formula, mole-ratio, calculation

### Question Q-3.3-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A hydrocarbon has an empirical formula of CH2 and a relative molecular mass of 56. (a) Determine its molecular formula. (b) Calculate the percentage by mass of carbon in this hydrocarbon.
**Answer**: (a) Empirical mass of CH2 = 12 + 2(1) = 14. n = 56/14 = 4. Molecular formula = C4H8.
(b) Mass of C in C4H8 = 4 × 12 = 48. %C = (48/56) × 100 = 85.7%.
**Explanation**: Part (a) tests the molecular/empirical formula relationship. Part (b) requires applying the molecular formula to find % composition. A common error in (b) is using the empirical formula mass instead of the molecular formula mass.
**Tags**: molecular-formula, empirical-formula, percentage-composition, hydrocarbon, calculation
