# Items: 12.4 — Identification of gases

### Question Q-12.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: How do you test for hydrogen gas?
**Answer**: Hold a lighted splint near the gas. Hydrogen burns with a squeaky pop sound. The pop confirms hydrogen.
**Explanation**: Squeaky pop test. A common error is saying it relights a glowing splint (that's oxygen).
**Tags**: hydrogen-test, pop-test, gas

### Question Q-12.2-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How do you test for oxygen gas?
**Answer**: Insert a glowing splint into the gas. Oxygen relights the glowing splint.
**Explanation**: Relights glowing splint. A common error is saying it extinguishes it.
**Tags**: oxygen-test, glowing-splint, gas

### Question Q-12.2-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How do you test for ammonia gas?
**Answer**: Hold damp red litmus paper near the gas. Ammonia turns red litmus paper blue (ammonia is alkaline). It also has a characteristic pungent smell.
**Explanation**: Red → blue litmus. A common error is using blue litmus.
**Tags**: ammonia-test, litmus, gas

### Question Q-12.2-10
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How do you test for chlorine gas?
**Answer**: Hold damp blue litmus paper in the gas. Chlorine turns it red briefly (acidic) then bleaches it white.
**Explanation**: Red → white (bleached). A common error is saying it stays red.
**Tags**: chlorine-test, litmus, bleaching, gas

### Question Q-12.2-11
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A gas extinguishes a lighted splint and turns limewater milky. Identify the gas.
**Answer**: Carbon dioxide (CO₂). It does not support combustion (extinguishes the splint) and turns limewater milky due to formation of calcium carbonate.
**Explanation**: Does not burn + limewater milky = CO₂. A common error is saying nitrogen (also extinguishes but doesn't affect limewater).
**Tags**: CO2-test, limewater, gas
