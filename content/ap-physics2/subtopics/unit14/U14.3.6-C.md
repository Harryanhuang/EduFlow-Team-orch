The pupil of a human eye has diameter $3.0 \text{ mm}$. For $\lambda = 550 \text{ nm}$, what is the theoretical minimum angular resolution?

## Options
A) $1.1 \times 10^{-4} \text{ rad}$
B) $2.2 \times 10^{-4} \text{ rad}$
C) $3.4 \times 10^{-4} \text{ rad}$
D) $6.7 \times 10^{-4} \text{ rad}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$\theta_{\text{min}} = 1.22\lambda/D = 1.22 \times 550 \times 10^{-9} / (3.0 \times 10^{-3}) = 6.71 \times 10^{-7} / 3.0 \times 10^{-3} = 2.24 \times 10^{-4} \text{ rad}$. This is the diffraction limit of the eye — about 0.013° or roughly half an arcminute. Real eye resolution is slightly worse due to additional factors (retina density, optical imperfections).
