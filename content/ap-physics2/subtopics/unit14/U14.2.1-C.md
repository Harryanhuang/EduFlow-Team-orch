Sound travels at approximately $343 \text{ m/s}$ in air, $1{,}480 \text{ m/s}$ in water, and $5{,}120 \text{ m/s}$ in steel. This is because:

## Options
A) Air is less dense than water and steel
B) Steel and water have higher elastic moduli, which more than compensate for their higher densities
C) Sound does not actually travel faster in steel
D) The frequency of sound changes in different media

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Wave speed $v = \sqrt{E/\rho}$. Although steel and water are denser than air, their elastic moduli are much larger, and the increase in $E$ more than compensates for the increase in $\rho$, resulting in higher wave speeds.
