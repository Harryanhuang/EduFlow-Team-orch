A soap film ($n = 1.33$) in air is illuminated with white light. At the point where the film is very thin (nearly zero thickness), it appears:

## Options
A) Bright
B) Dark
C) Colored
D) Transparent

## Answer
B

## Explanation
At the top surface (air→soap, $n$ increases), there is a $\pi$ phase shift. At the bottom surface (soap→air, $n$ decreases), there is no phase shift. For $t \approx 0$: path difference $= 2nt \approx 0$, but the $\pi$ phase shift from the top reflection means the two reflected waves are out of phase → destructive interference → dark.
