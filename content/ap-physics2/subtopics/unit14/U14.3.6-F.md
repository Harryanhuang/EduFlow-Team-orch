A circular aperture of diameter $2.0 \text{ mm}$ is illuminated with $\lambda = 550 \text{ nm}$ light. What is the angular radius of the first dark ring?

## Options
A) $0.016°$
B) $0.033°$
C) $0.050°$
D) $0.16°$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$\sin\theta = 1.22\lambda/D = 1.22 \times 550 \times 10^{-9} / (2.0 \times 10^{-3}) = 6.71 \times 10^{-7}/2.0 \times 10^{-3} = 3.36 \times 10^{-4}$. $\theta = \arcsin(3.36 \times 10^{-4}) \approx 3.36 \times 10^{-4} \text{ rad} = 1.93 \times 10^{-2}° \approx 0.019°$. Closest to A ($0.016°$). The angular radius of the first dark ring scales inversely with the aperture diameter — a smaller aperture produces a broader diffraction pattern.
