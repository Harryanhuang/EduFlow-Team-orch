In a double-slit experiment, the intensity at the central maximum is $I_0$. At the first-order maximum ($m = 1$), the intensity from the double-slit factor alone is:

## Options
A) $I_0$
B) $I_0/2$
C) $I_0/4$
D) $0$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
At any bright fringe (constructive interference), the double-slit intensity factor $\cos^2(\phi/2) = \cos^2(m\pi) = 1$. So the double-slit factor gives full intensity $I_0$ at every bright fringe. The actual intensity decreases for higher orders due to the single-slit diffraction envelope, but the double-slit factor itself is $1$ at all constructive maxima.
