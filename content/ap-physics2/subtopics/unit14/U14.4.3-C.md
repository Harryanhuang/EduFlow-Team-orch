A diamond ($n = 2.42$) sparkles brilliantly because:

## Options
A) It absorbs most light
B) It has a very low critical angle, causing many internal reflections before light escapes
C) It fluoresces under visible light
D) It has a very high dispersion only

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$\sin\theta_c = 1/2.42 = 0.413$, $\theta_c = 24.4°$. This very small critical angle means light entering the diamond is likely to undergo total internal reflection many times before exiting, creating the characteristic sparkle. Combined with dispersion (different colors bend differently), this produces the fire and brilliance of a cut diamond.
