Using Huygens' principle, light bends toward the normal when entering a medium with higher refractive index because:

## Options
A) The light slows down, and the wavefronts bunch up
B) The light speeds up
C) The wavelength increases
D) The frequency changes

## Answer
A

## Explanation
In a denser medium ($n_2 > n_1$), light travels slower ($v = c/n$). The wavelets in the denser medium travel shorter distances in the same time, causing the wavefront to bend toward the normal. The wavelength decreases ($\lambda_n = \lambda/n$) but frequency stays constant.
