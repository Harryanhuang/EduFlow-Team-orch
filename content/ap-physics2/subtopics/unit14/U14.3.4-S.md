A thin oil film ($n = 1.50$) on water ($n = 1.33$) is illuminated with green light ($\lambda = 550 \text{ nm}$). What is the minimum film thickness for constructive interference in the reflected light?

## Options
A) $92 \text{ nm}$
B) $183 \text{ nm}$
C) $275 \text{ nm}$
D) $367 \text{ nm}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Phase shifts: air ($n = 1$) → oil ($n = 1.50$): $n$ increases, so $\pi$ phase shift. Oil ($n = 1.50$) → water ($n = 1.33$): $n$ decreases, so no phase shift. One net $\pi$ phase shift. For constructive interference in reflection: $2nt = (m + \frac{1}{2})\lambda$, $m = 0$ (minimum): $t = \lambda/(4n) = 550/(4 \times 1.50) = 91.7 \text{ nm} \approx 92 \text{ nm}$. This is the minimum film thickness for constructive reflection.
