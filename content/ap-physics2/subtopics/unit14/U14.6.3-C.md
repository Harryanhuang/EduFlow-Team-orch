A simple convex lens produces a blurry image with colored fringes. The colored fringes are caused by:

## Options
A) Spherical aberration
B) Chromatic aberration
C) Polarization effects
D) Diffraction

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Chromatic aberration: the lens refracts different wavelengths by different amounts (because $n$ depends on $\lambda$), so each color focuses at a different distance. This causes colored fringes around images. Achromatic doublets (combining converging and diverging lenses of different glass) can correct this.
