In single-slit diffraction, the intensity of the first secondary maximum is approximately what fraction of the central maximum intensity?

## Options
A) $1/2$
B) $1/4$
C) $1/21$
D) $1/100$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The first secondary maximum occurs at $\beta \approx 3\pi/2$, giving $I/I_0 = [\sin(3\pi/2)/(3\pi/2)]^2 = [(-1)/(3\pi/2)]^2 = 4/(9\pi^2) \approx 0.045 \approx 1/22$. The closest answer is C ($1/21$). Secondary maxima are much weaker than the central maximum.
