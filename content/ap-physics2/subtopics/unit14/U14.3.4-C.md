A glass lens ($n = 1.52$) is coated with a thin film of $n = 1.38$ to reduce reflection of yellow light ($\lambda = 580 \text{ nm}$). What is the minimum coating thickness?

## Options
A) $105 \text{ nm}$
B) $210 \text{ nm}$
C) $290 \text{ nm}$
D) $420 \text{ nm}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Air→coating ($1.0 \to 1.38$, $n$ increases): phase shift. Coating→glass ($1.38 \to 1.52$, $n$ increases): phase shift. Two phase shifts = net zero phase shift (both reflections have the same shift). Destructive (anti-reflection): $2nt = (m + \frac{1}{2})\lambda$. Minimum ($m = 0$): $t = \lambda/(4n) = 580/(4 \times 1.38) = 580/5.52 = 105 \text{ nm}$.
