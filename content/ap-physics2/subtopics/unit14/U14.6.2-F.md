A concave lens:

## Options
A) Brings parallel light to a real focus
B) Spreads parallel light as if from a virtual focus
C) Always forms real images
D) Has a positive focal length

## Answer
B

## Explanation
A concave (diverging) lens causes parallel rays to spread out as if they came from a virtual focal point on the same side as the incoming light. By convention, its focal length is negative. Concave lenses always form virtual, upright, diminished images.
