Huygens' principle states that:

## Options
A) Light travels in straight lines only
B) Every point on a wavefront acts as a source of secondary wavelets
C) Waves always interfere constructively
D) The speed of light is constant in all media

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Huygens' principle: each point on a wavefront is a source of secondary spherical wavelets. The wavefront at a later time is the envelope (tangent surface) of all these wavelets. This explains how waves bend around obstacles (diffraction) and change direction at boundaries (refraction).
