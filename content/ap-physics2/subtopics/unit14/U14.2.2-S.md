Which wave can be polarized?

## Options
A) Sound wave in air
B) Light wave
C) Compression wave on a spring
D) Pressure wave in a fluid

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Only transverse waves can be polarized because polarization involves restricting the oscillation direction perpendicular to propagation. Light is a transverse EM wave and can be polarized. Sound and compression waves are longitudinal and cannot be polarized.
