A wave on a string has speed $v_1$. The tension is quadrupled while keeping the linear density constant. What is the new speed?

## Options
A) $v_1$
B) $2v_1$
C) $4v_1$
D) $16v_1$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$v = \sqrt{T/\mu}$. If $T$ is quadrupled: $v_2 = \sqrt{4T/\mu} = 2\sqrt{T/\mu} = 2v_1$.
