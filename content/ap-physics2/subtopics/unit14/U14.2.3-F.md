If the amplitude of a wave is tripled, by what factor does the energy change?

## Options
A) $3$
B) $6$
C) $9$
D) $27$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
$E \propto A^2$. If $A$ is tripled: $E' \propto (3A)^2 = 9A^2$. Energy increases by a factor of 9.
