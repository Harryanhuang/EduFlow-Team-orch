A single slit is made narrower. How does the diffraction pattern on a distant screen change?

## Options
A) The pattern becomes narrower
B) The pattern becomes wider
C) The pattern stays the same
D) The pattern disappears

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The angular width of the central maximum is $\propto \lambda/a$. Decreasing $a$ (narrower slit) increases the angular width, making the pattern wider. This is a direct consequence of the uncertainty principle: confining the light more tightly in position (narrower slit) spreads it more in momentum (wider angle).
