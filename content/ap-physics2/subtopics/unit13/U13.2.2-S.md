A铁 sample is heated above its Curie temperature ($770°\text{C}$). What happens to its magnetic properties?

## Options
A) It becomes a stronger permanent magnet.
B) It loses all magnetic properties.
C) It becomes paramagnetic.
D) It becomes diamagnetic.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Above the Curie temperature, thermal energy overcomes the alignment of magnetic domains, and the material transitions from ferromagnetic to paramagnetic. It still has unpaired electrons (paramagnetic) but loses its permanent magnetism.
