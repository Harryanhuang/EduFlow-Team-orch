A north pole of a magnet is pushed toward a coil. What is the direction of the induced current (viewed from the magnet side)?

## Options
A) Clockwise
B) Counterclockwise
C) No current is induced
D) Depends on the speed of the magnet

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By Lenz's law, the induced current opposes the increasing flux. The induced field must point away from the approaching north pole (to repel it). By the right-hand rule, a counterclockwise current (viewed from the magnet) produces a field pointing toward the magnet (north pole facing the magnet), which repels the incoming north pole.
