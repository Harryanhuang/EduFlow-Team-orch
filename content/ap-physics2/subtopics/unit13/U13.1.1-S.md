Two parallel wires $0.05 \text{ m}$ apart carry currents of $3.0 \text{ A}$ and $5.0 \text{ A}$ in the same direction. What is the force per unit length between them?

## Options
A) $6.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N/m}$, attractive
B) $6.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N/m}$, repulsive
C) $3.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N/m}$, attractive
D) $1.2 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N/m}$, attractive

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$F/L = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi d} = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(3.0)(5.0)}{2\pi(0.05)} = \frac{6.0 \times 10^{-6}}{0.10} = 6.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N/m}$. Same direction currents → attractive.
