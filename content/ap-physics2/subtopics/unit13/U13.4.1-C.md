A bar magnet is completely enclosed within a Gaussian surface. What is the total magnetic flux through the surface?

## Options
A) Positive (net north pole inside)
B) Negative (net south pole inside)
C) Zero — field lines entering equal field lines leaving
D) Depends on the orientation of the magnet

## Answer
C

## Explanation
By Gauss's law for magnetism, the total magnetic flux through any closed surface is zero. Every field line that enters the surface (through the south pole) must also exit (through the north pole), because field lines form closed loops. There are no isolated magnetic charges (monopoles).
