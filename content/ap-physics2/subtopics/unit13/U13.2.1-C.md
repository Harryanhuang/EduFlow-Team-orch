A magnetic dipole with $\mu = 0.40 \text{ A·m}^2$ is in a $0.30 \text{ T}$ field, initially aligned with the field ($\theta = 0°$). How much work is needed to rotate it to $\theta = 180°$?

## Options
A) $0$
B) $0.060 \text{ J}$
C) $0.12 \text{ J}$
D) $0.24 \text{ J}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
$W = \Delta U = U_f - U_i = (-\mu B \cos 180°) - (-\mu B \cos 0°) = \mu B - (-\mu B) = 2\mu B = 2(0.40)(0.30) = 0.24 \text{ J}$. Wait — that gives D. Let me recalculate: $U_i = -\mu B \cos 0° = -(0.40)(0.30)(1) = -0.12 \text{ J}$. $U_f = -\mu B \cos 180° = -(0.40)(0.30)(-1) = +0.12 \text{ J}$. $\Delta U = 0.12 - (-0.12) = 0.24 \text{ J}$. The answer is D. But option C ($0.12 \text{ J}$) would be the work to rotate from $0°$ to $90°$: $W = -\mu B(\cos 90° - \cos 0°) = -\mu B(0 - 1) = \mu B = 0.12 \text{ J}$. The answer to the question as asked is D ($0.24 \text{ J}$).
