A magnetic dipole with $\mu = 0.40 \text{ A·m}^2$ is in a $0.30 \text{ T}$ field, initially aligned with the field ($\theta = 0°$). How much work is needed to rotate it to $\theta = 180°$?

## Options
A) $0$
B) $0.060 \text{ J}$
C) $0.12 \text{ J}$
D) $0.24 \text{ J}$

## Answer
D

## Explanation
$U_i = -\mu B \cos 0° = -(0.40)(0.30)(1) = -0.12 \text{ J}$. $U_f = -\mu B \cos 180° = -(0.40)(0.30)(-1) = +0.12 \text{ J}$. $\Delta U = 0.12 - (-0.12) = 0.24 \text{ J}$. This is the work an external agent must do to rotate the dipole from the stable equilibrium ($\theta = 0°$) to the unstable equilibrium ($\theta = 180°$).
