A $0.50 \text{ m}^2$ loop is in a uniform magnetic field of $0.20 \text{ T}$. The field is turned off in $0.10 \text{ s}$. What is the average induced EMF?

## Options
A) $0.05 \text{ V}$
B) $0.10 \text{ V}$
C) $0.50 \text{ V}$
D) $1.0 \text{ V}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$\Delta\Phi = BA - 0 = (0.20)(0.50) = 0.10 \text{ Wb}$. $|\varepsilon| = |\Delta\Phi/\Delta t| = 0.10/0.10 = 1.0 \text{ V}$. Wait — that gives D. Let me recalculate: $\Phi_i = BA = 0.20 \times 0.50 = 0.10 \text{ Wb}$. $\Phi_f = 0$. $\Delta\Phi = 0.10 \text{ Wb}$. $\varepsilon = 0.10/0.10 = 1.0 \text{ V}$. The answer is D.
