A DC motor has a $12 \text{ V}$ supply and a coil resistance of $2.0 \text{ Ω}$. When running at full speed, it generates a back-EMF of $10 \text{ V}$. What current does it draw?

## Options
A) $1.0 \text{ A}$
B) $6.0 \text{ A}$
C) $10 \text{ A}$
D) $12 \text{ A}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Net voltage across coil resistance: $V_{\text{net}} = V_{\text{supply}} - \text{back-EMF} = 12 - 10 = 2 \text{ V}$. Current: $I = V_{\text{net}}/R = 2/2.0 = 1.0 \text{ A}$. The back-EMF opposes the supply, reducing the current.
