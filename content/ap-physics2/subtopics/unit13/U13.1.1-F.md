A long straight wire carries a current of $5.0 \text{ A}$. What is the magnetic field at a distance of $0.10 \text{ m}$ from the wire? ($\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T·m/A}$)

## Options
A) $1.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}$
B) $1.0 \times 10^{-6} \text{ T}$
C) $6.3 \times 10^{-6} \text{ T}$
D) $6.3 \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r} = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(5.0)}{2\pi(0.10)} = \frac{2.0 \times 10^{-6}}{0.20} = 1.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}$.
