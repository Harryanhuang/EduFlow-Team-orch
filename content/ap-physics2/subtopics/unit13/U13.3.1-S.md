On a contour map of electric potential, what does the spacing between contour lines indicate?

## Options
A) The direction of the electric field
B) The magnitude of the electric field
C) The charge distribution
D) The current density

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Close contour lines indicate a steep potential gradient, which means a strong electric field ($E = -dV/dx$). Far apart contours indicate a weak field. The direction of the field is perpendicular to the contour lines, pointing from high to low potential.
