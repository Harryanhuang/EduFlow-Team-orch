At a distance $r$ from a magnetic dipole, the field strength is proportional to:

## Options
A) $1/r$
B) $1/r^2$
C) $1/r^3$
D) $1/r^4$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
A magnetic dipole field falls off as $1/r^3$ at large distances, which is faster than the $1/r^2$ falloff of a point charge (electric monopole) field. This is because the two "poles" of the dipole partially cancel each other at large distances.
