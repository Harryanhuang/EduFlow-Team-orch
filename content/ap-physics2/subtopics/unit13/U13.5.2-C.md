A solid metal plate is dropped through a magnetic field and experiences a braking force even though it carries no net current. This is due to:

## Options
A) Static electricity
B) Eddy currents induced in the plate by the changing flux
C) The weight of the magnetic field
D) Friction with air molecules

## Answer
B

## Explanation
As the plate moves through the field, different parts of it experience changing flux, inducing circulating currents (eddy currents). By Lenz's law, these currents create fields that oppose the motion, producing a braking force. This is the principle behind magnetic braking in trains and eddy current separators.
