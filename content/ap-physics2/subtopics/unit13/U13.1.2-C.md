In a mass spectrometer, two ions with the same charge $+e$ but different masses ($m_1$ and $m_2 = 2m_1$) are accelerated through the same potential $V$ and enter the same $B$ field. What is the ratio of their radii?

## Options
A) $r_1/r_2 = 1$
B) $r_1/r_2 = 1/\sqrt{2}$
C) $r_1/r_2 = \sqrt{2}$
D) $r_1/r_2 = 2$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
$r \propto \sqrt{m}$ (when $q$, $V$, $B$ are constant). $r_1/r_2 = \sqrt{m_1/m_2} = \sqrt{1/2} = 1/\sqrt{2}$. So $r_2 = r_1 \sqrt{2}$. The heavier ion has a larger radius. The ratio $r_1/r_2 = 1/\sqrt{2}$, which means option B is correct.
