knowledge_point: Magnetic Force on a Moving Charge
core_concept: The magnetic force on a moving charge is $\vec{F} = q\vec{v} \times \vec{B}$. Magnitude: $F = qvB\sin\theta$. Direction by right-hand rule (or left-hand rule for negative charges).
exam_pattern: Calculate the magnetic force on a moving charge; determine the direction of the force.
question_type: mcq
difficulty: F
calculator: calc
common_mistake: Using $F = qvB$ when $\theta \neq 90°$; confusing the right-hand rule for positive vs. negative charges.
explanation_context: Magnetic force, Lorentz force, right-hand rule.

An electron ($q = -1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$) moves at $3.0 \times 10^6 \text{ m/s}$ perpendicular to a $0.50 \text{ T}$ magnetic field. What is the magnitude of the force on the electron?

## Options
A) $1.2 \times 10^{-13} \text{ N}$
B) $2.4 \times 10^{-13} \text{ N}$
C) $3.2 \times 10^{-13} \text{ N}$
D) $4.8 \times 10^{-13} \text{ N}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$F = |q|vB\sin 90° = (1.6 \times 10^{-19})(3.0 \times 10^6)(0.50)(1) = 2.4 \times 10^{-13} \text{ N}$.
