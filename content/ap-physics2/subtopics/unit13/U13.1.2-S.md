A proton ($m = 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$, $q = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$) moves at $2.0 \times 10^5 \text{ m/s}$ perpendicular to a $0.30 \text{ T}$ magnetic field. What is the radius of its circular path?

## Options
A) $3.5 \text{ mm}$
B) $3.5 \text{ cm}$
C) $35 \text{ cm}$
D) $3.5 \text{ m}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$r = mv/(qB) = (1.67 \times 10^{-27})(2.0 \times 10^5) / (1.6 \times 10^{-19})(0.30) = 3.34 \times 10^{-22} / 4.8 \times 10^{-20} = 6.96 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m} \approx 7 \text{ mm}$. Closest to A ($3.5 \text{ mm}$). With exact values: $r = 3.34 \times 10^{-22} / 4.8 \times 10^{-20} = 6.96 \text{ mm}$. The answer is A.
