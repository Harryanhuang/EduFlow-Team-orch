A 2D electric potential map shows circular equipotential lines centered on a positive point charge. At a point to the right of the charge, in which direction does the electric field point?

## Options
A) Toward the charge (left)
B) Away from the charge (right)
C) Tangent to the equipotential (upward)
D) Into the page

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The electric field points from high potential to low potential. For a positive point charge, potential decreases with distance, so the field points radially outward — to the right at a point to the right of the charge. This is consistent with $\vec{E} = -\nabla V$: the gradient points inward (toward higher potential), so the field points outward.
