A long solenoid produces a uniform field $B_0$ at its center. At the very end of the solenoid (just inside), the field is approximately:

## Options
A) $B_0$
B) $B_0/2$
C) $2B_0$
D) $0$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For a finite solenoid, the field at the end is approximately half the field at the center: $B_{\text{end}} \approx B_0/2$. This is because at the end, only half the turns contribute to the field in the same direction. An infinitely long solenoid would have $B = \mu_0 n I$ everywhere inside, but real solenoids have end effects.
