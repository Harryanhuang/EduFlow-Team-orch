The magnetic field lines around a bar magnet:

## Options
A) Start at the north pole and end at the south pole
B) Form closed loops from north to south (outside) and south to north (inside)
C) Start at both poles and go to infinity
D) Are straight lines from north to south

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Magnetic field lines always form closed loops — they emerge from the north pole, curve around to the south pole, and continue through the interior of the magnet from south to north. This is because magnetic monopoles do not exist (Gauss's law for magnetism: $\nabla \cdot \vec{B} = 0$).
