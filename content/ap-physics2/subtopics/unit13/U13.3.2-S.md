A circular loop of radius $0.10 \text{ m}$ carries $5.0 \text{ A}$. What is the magnetic field at the center of the loop?

## Options
A) $1.6 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}$
B) $3.1 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}$
C) $6.3 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}$
D) $1.3 \times 10^{-4} \text{ T}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2R} = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7})(5.0)}{2(0.10)} = \frac{6.28 \times 10^{-6}}{0.20} = 3.14 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T} \approx 3.1 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}$.
