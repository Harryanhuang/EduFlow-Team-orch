A hollow metal sphere is given a net positive charge. Where does the excess charge reside?

## Options
A) Uniformly throughout the volume
B) Only on the outer surface
C) Only on the inner surface
D) Both on the inner and outer surfaces

## Answer
B

## Explanation
In a conductor at electrostatic equilibrium, all excess charge resides on the outer surface. This is because any charge inside would create an electric field that would move free charges until the field inside is zero. The charges repel each other and migrate to the outer surface to minimize their potential energy.
