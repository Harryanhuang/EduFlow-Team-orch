What is the electric field at a point $0.10 \text{ m}$ from a point charge of $-5 \text{ nC}$?

## Options
A) $4.5 \times 10^{3} \text{ N/C}$, directed toward the charge
B) $4.5 \times 10^{3} \text{ N/C}$, directed away from the charge
C) $4.5 \times 10^{6} \text{ N/C}$, directed toward the charge
D) $4.5 \times 10^{6} \text{ N/C}$, directed away from the charge

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$E = k|q|/r^2 = (8.99 \times 10^9)(5 \times 10^{-9})/(0.10)^2 = 44.95 / 0.01 = 4{,}495 \text{ N/C} \approx 4.5 \times 10^3 \text{ N/C}$. Since the charge is negative, the field points toward the charge (inward).
