A charged metal box has a cavity. A charge $+q$ is placed inside the cavity (not touching the walls). Which statement is correct?

## Options
A) The field outside the box is zero.
B) The field outside the box depends on $+q$.
C) The field inside the metal is zero.
D) The field inside the cavity is zero.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Inside the metal (the conductor itself), $E = 0$ always in equilibrium. The charge $+q$ inside the cavity induces $-q$ on the inner surface and $+q$ on the outer surface of the box. The field outside depends on the outer surface charge, which is $+q$. The field inside the cavity is NOT zero (there is a field from $+q$ to the induced $-q$ on the inner wall). The key fact: $E = 0$ inside the conductor material.
