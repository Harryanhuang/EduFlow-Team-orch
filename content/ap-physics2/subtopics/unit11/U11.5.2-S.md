A proton ($q = +1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$, $m = 1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$) is accelerated from rest through a potential difference of $1{,}000 \text{ V}$. What is its final speed?

## Options
A) $4.4 \times 10^5 \text{ m/s}$
B) $3.1 \times 10^5 \text{ m/s}$
C) $6.2 \times 10^5 \text{ m/s}$
D) $1.4 \times 10^6 \text{ m/s}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$qV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$, so $v = \sqrt{2qV/m} = \sqrt{2 \times 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \times 1000 / (1.67 \times 10^{-27})} = \sqrt{1.92 \times 10^{11}} = 4.38 \times 10^5 \text{ m/s} \approx 4.4 \times 10^5 \text{ m/s}$.
