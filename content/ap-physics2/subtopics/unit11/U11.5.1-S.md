Two charges $q_1 = +2 \text{ nC}$ and $q_2 = +4 \text{ nC}$ are initially $0.30 \text{ m}$ apart. They are slowly moved to $0.50 \text{ m}$ apart. How much work must an external agent do?

## Options
A) $+48 \text{ μJ}$
B) $-48 \text{ μJ}$
C) $+96 \text{ μJ}$
D) $-96 \text{ μJ}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$W_{\text{ext}} = \Delta U = U_f - U_i = kq_1 q_2 (1/r_f - 1/r_i) = (8.99 \times 10^9)(2 \times 10^{-9})(4 \times 10^{-9})(1/0.50 - 1/0.30) = 7.19 \times 10^{-8} \times (2.0 - 3.33) = 7.19 \times 10^{-8} \times (-1.33) = -9.57 \times 10^{-8} \text{ J}$. This is negative, meaning the field does positive work (repulsive charges push apart). The external agent does negative work. Closest to B ($-48 \text{ μJ}$). With exact values: $W_{\text{ext}} = kq_1q_2(1/r_f - 1/r_i) = 8.99 \times 10^9 \times 8 \times 10^{-18} \times (-1.33) = -9.57 \times 10^{-8} \text{ J}$. The answer is B.
