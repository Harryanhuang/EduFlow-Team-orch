Two charges $q_1 = +2 \text{ μC}$ and $q_2 = +4 \text{ μC}$ are initially $0.30 \text{ m}$ apart. They are slowly moved to $0.50 \text{ m}$ apart. How much work must an external agent do?

## Options
A) $+48 \text{ J}$
B) $-48 \text{ J}$
C) $+96 \text{ J}$
D) $-96 \text{ J}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$W_{\text{ext}} = \Delta U = U_f - U_i = kq_1 q_2 (1/r_f - 1/r_i)$. $kq_1 q_2 = (8.99 \times 10^9)(2 \times 10^{-6})(4 \times 10^{-6}) = 0.0719 \text{ J·m}$. $1/r_f - 1/r_i = 1/0.50 - 1/0.30 = 2.0 - 3.33 = -1.33 \text{ m}^{-1}$. $W_{\text{ext}} = 0.0719 \times (-1.33) = -0.0956 \text{ J} \approx -0.096 \text{ J}$. The external agent does negative work (the field pushes the charges apart, doing positive work; the external agent must restrain them to move slowly).
