The electric potential decreases linearly from $100 \text{ V}$ at $x = 0$ to $20 \text{ V}$ at $x = 0.40 \text{ m}$. What is the electric field in this region?

## Options
A) $-200 \text{ N/C}$
B) $+200 \text{ N/C}$
C) $-50 \text{ N/C}$
D) $+50 \text{ N/C}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$E = -\Delta V / \Delta x = -(20 - 100)/(0.40 - 0) = -(-80)/0.40 = +200 \text{ N/C}$. The field points in the $+x$ direction because the potential decreases in that direction.
