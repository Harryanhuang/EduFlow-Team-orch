Charge $q_1 = +9 \text{ nC}$ is at $x = 0$ and charge $q_2 = +1 \text{ nC}$ is at $x = 0.40 \text{ m}$. Where on the $x$-axis is the total potential zero?

## Options
A) Between the charges at $x = 0.36 \text{ m}$
B) To the left of $q_1$ at $x = -0.20 \text{ m}$
C) To the right of $q_2$ at $x = 0.60 \text{ m}$
D) The potential is never zero (both charges are positive).

## Answer
D

## Explanation
Both charges are positive, so $V = kq_1/r_1 + kq_2/r_2 > 0$ everywhere (both terms are always positive). The potential can never be zero. For two like charges, the potential is always the same sign as the charges. The zero-potential point only exists between unlike charges.
