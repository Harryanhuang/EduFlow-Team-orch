The electric field along the $x$-axis is $E(x) = 3x^2 \text{ N/C}$ (pointing in $+x$). What is the potential difference $V(2) - V(0)$?

## Options
A) $-8 \text{ V}$
B) $-4 \text{ V}$
C) $+4 \text{ V}$
D) $+8 \text{ V}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$\Delta V = -\int_0^2 E \, dx = -\int_0^2 3x^2 \, dx = -[x^3]_0^2 = -(8 - 0) = -8 \text{ V}$. The potential decreases in the direction of the field, consistent with $E = -dV/dx > 0$.
