An electron ($q = -1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$, $m = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$) starts from rest and passes through three sequential regions with potential differences $+200 \text{ V}$, $-50 \text{ V}$, and $+300 \text{ V}$. What is its final speed?

## Options
A) $1.2 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s}$
B) $1.3 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s}$
C) $1.5 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s}$
D) $2.1 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Net potential difference experienced by the electron: $\Delta V_{\text{total}} = 200 + (-50) + 300 = 450 \text{ V}$. The electron gains kinetic energy: $\frac{1}{2}mv^2 = |q|\Delta V_{\text{total}} = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \times 450 = 7.2 \times 10^{-17} \text{ J}$. $v = \sqrt{2 \times 7.2 \times 10^{-17} / 9.11 \times 10^{-31}} = \sqrt{1.58 \times 10^{14}} = 1.26 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s} \approx 1.3 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s}$.
