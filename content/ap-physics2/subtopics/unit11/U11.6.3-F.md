In a parallel-plate capacitor with plate separation $d = 0.01 \text{ m}$ and voltage $V_0 = 100 \text{ V}$ (positive plate at $x = 0$, negative at $x = d$), what is the potential at $x = 0.004 \text{ m}$?

## Options
A) $40 \text{ V}$
B) $60 \text{ V}$
C) $100 \text{ V}$
D) $0 \text{ V}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The potential varies linearly: $V(x) = V_0 - (V_0/d)x = 100 - (100/0.01)(0.004) = 100 - 40 = 60 \text{ V}$.
