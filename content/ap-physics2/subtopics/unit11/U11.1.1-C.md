Charge $q_1 = +1 \text{ nC}$ is at the origin and charge $q_2 = +4 \text{ nC}$ is at $x = 0.30 \text{ m}$. Where should a third charge $q_3$ be placed so that the net force on it is zero?

## Options
A) At $x = 0.10 \text{ m}$
B) At $x = 0.15 \text{ m}$
C) At $x = 0.20 \text{ m}$
D) At $x = 0.12 \text{ m}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
For zero net force: $kq_1 q_3 / x^2 = kq_2 q_3 / (0.30 - x)^2$. Canceling $kq_3$: $q_1/x^2 = q_2/(0.30-x)^2$. Taking square roots: $\sqrt{q_1}/x = \sqrt{q_2}/(0.30-x)$. $1/x = 2/(0.30-x)$. $0.30 - x = 2x$. $0.30 = 3x$. $x = 0.10 \text{ m}$.
