A $+8 \text{ nC}$ rod touches a neutral metal sphere, then is removed. The sphere now has $+3 \text{ nC}$. Where is the remaining charge?

## Options
A) It was destroyed.
B) It is on the rod, which now has $+5 \text{ nC}$.
C) It is on the rod, which now has $-5 \text{ nC}$.
D) It is evenly split between the rod and sphere.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By charge conservation: total charge before $= +8 + 0 = +8 \text{ nC}$. After: sphere $= +3 \text{ nC}$, so rod $= +8 - 3 = +5 \text{ nC}$. Charge is neither created nor destroyed — it is simply redistributed between the two objects.
