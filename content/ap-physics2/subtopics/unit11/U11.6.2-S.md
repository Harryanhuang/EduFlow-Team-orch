A charge is moved from point A to point B along two different paths. Path 1 goes directly from A to B. Path 2 goes from A to C to B. Which statement about the potential difference is correct?

## Options
A) $\Delta V_1 > \Delta V_2$ because Path 1 is shorter.
B) $\Delta V_1 < \Delta V_2$ because Path 2 passes through a higher potential at C.
C) $\Delta V_1 = \Delta V_2$ because the electric field is conservative.
D) Cannot be determined without knowing the exact field.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The electric field is conservative, meaning the work done (and hence the potential difference) depends only on the endpoints, not the path. $\Delta V = V_B - V_A$ regardless of the path taken. This is a fundamental property of all conservative fields.
