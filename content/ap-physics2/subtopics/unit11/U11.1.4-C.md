An oil drop in a Millikan experiment is measured to have a charge of $-4.8 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$. How many excess electrons does the drop carry? ($e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$)

## Options
A) $1$
B) $2$
C) $3$
D) $4$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Number of excess electrons: $n = |q|/e = 4.8 \times 10^{-19} / 1.6 \times 10^{-19} = 3$. The drop carries 3 excess electrons, confirming that charge comes in discrete multiples of $e$.
