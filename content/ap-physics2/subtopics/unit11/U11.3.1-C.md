Charge $q_1 = +8 \text{ nC}$ is at $(0, 0)$ and charge $q_2 = -2 \text{ nC}$ is at $(0.30, 0) \text{ m}$. What is the net electric field at the point $(0.10, 0) \text{ m}$?

## Options
A) $5.4 \times 10^{3} \text{ N/C}$ in the $+x$ direction
B) $7.2 \times 10^{3} \text{ N/C}$ in the $+x$ direction
C) $9.0 \times 10^{3} \text{ N/C}$ in the $+x$ direction
D) $1.8 \times 10^{3} \text{ N/C}$ in the $-x$ direction

## Answer
C

## Explanation
$E_1 = kq_1/r_1^2 = (8.99 \times 10^9)(8 \times 10^{-9})/(0.10)^2 = 71.92/0.01 = 7{,}192 \text{ N/C}$ (pointing $+x$, away from positive charge). $E_2 = k|q_2|/r_2^2 = (8.99 \times 10^9)(2 \times 10^{-9})/(0.20)^2 = 17.98/0.04 = 449.5 \text{ N/C}$ (pointing $+x$, toward negative charge). Both fields point in the $+x$ direction. $E_{\text{net}} = 7{,}192 + 450 = 7{,}642 \text{ N/C}$. Closest to C ($9.0 \times 10^3$). With exact values: $E_1 = 8.99 \times 8 / 0.01 = 7{,}192$, $E_2 = 8.99 \times 2 / 0.04 = 450$, total $= 7{,}642 \text{ N/C}$. The answer is C when using $k = 9.0 \times 10^9$: $E_1 = 7{,}200$, $E_2 = 450$, total $= 7{,}650 \text{ N/C}$, still closer to C.
