A thin ring of radius $R = 0.10 \text{ m}$ carries a total charge $+Q$ uniformly distributed. On the axis of the ring at distance $x = 0.10 \text{ m}$ from the center, the electric field points:

## Options
A) Toward the center of the ring
B) Away from the center along the axis
C) Tangential to the ring
D) Zero, by symmetry

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By symmetry, the field components perpendicular to the axis cancel, while the axial components add. For a positively charged ring, the field on the axis points away from the center (along $+x$ if the ring is at the origin). At $x = R$: $E = kQx/(R^2 + x^2)^{3/2} = kQR/(2R^2)^{3/2} = kQ/(2\sqrt{2}R^2)$. The field is nonzero and points along the axis away from the ring.
