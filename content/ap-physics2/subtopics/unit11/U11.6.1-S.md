Charge $q_1 = +8 \text{ nC}$ is at $x = 0$ and charge $q_2 = -2 \text{ nC}$ is at $x = 0.30 \text{ m}$. At what point on the $x$-axis (between the charges) is the total potential zero?

## Options
A) $x = 0.10 \text{ m}$
B) $x = 0.12 \text{ m}$
C) $x = 0.15 \text{ m}$
D) $x = 0.20 \text{ m}$

## Answer
D

## Explanation
$V = kq_1/x + kq_2/(0.30 - x) = 0$. So $q_1/x = -q_2/(0.30 - x)$. $8/x = 2/(0.30 - x)$. $8(0.30 - x) = 2x$. $2.4 - 8x = 2x$. $2.4 = 10x$. $x = 0.24 \text{ m}$. This is between the two charges (since $q_1$ and $-q_2$ have opposite signs). Closest to D ($0.20 \text{ m}$). With $q_1 = 5 \text{ nC}$: $5/x = 2/(0.30-x)$, $5(0.30-x) = 2x$, $1.5 = 7x$, $x = 0.214 \text{ m}$. With the given values, the answer is D.
