A solid metal sphere carries a net charge $+Q$. Which statement about the electric field inside the sphere (not the cavity) is correct?

## Options
A) $E = 0$ everywhere inside, because all excess charge is on the surface.
B) $E$ is nonzero near the center, pointing outward.
C) $E$ is nonzero near the surface, pointing inward.
D) $E$ varies as $1/r^2$ inside, just like outside.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
In electrostatic equilibrium, the electric field inside a conductor is zero everywhere. If there were any field, it would exert a force on the free electrons, causing them to move — contradicting equilibrium. All excess charge resides on the surface, and the field lines terminate on or originate from surface charges. Inside the conductor, $E = 0$ regardless of the shape or charge distribution.
