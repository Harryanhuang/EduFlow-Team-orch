A charge is moved along an equipotential surface from point A to point B. How much work is done by the electric field?

## Options
A) $q(V_A - V_B)$
B) $qV_A$
C) Zero
D) $qV_B$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
By definition, an equipotential surface has the same potential everywhere: $V_A = V_B$. Work done by the field: $W = q(V_A - V_B) = q(0) = 0$.
