A hollow conducting sphere has inner radius $R_1$ and outer radius $R_2$. A point charge $+Q$ is placed at the center of the cavity. What are the charges on the inner and outer surfaces of the conductor?

## Options
A) Inner: $0$; Outer: $+Q$
B) Inner: $-Q$; Outer: $+Q$
C) Inner: $+Q$; Outer: $-Q$
D) Inner: $-Q$; Outer: $0$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By Gauss's law, a Gaussian surface inside the conductor (between $R_1$ and $R_2$) must enclose zero net charge (since $E = 0$ inside the conductor). The enclosed charge is $Q_{\text{center}} + Q_{\text{inner surface}} = 0$, so $Q_{\text{inner}} = -Q$. By charge conservation, the outer surface must have $+Q$ (total conductor charge = 0 if neutral, so $-Q + Q_{\text{outer}} = 0$, giving $Q_{\text{outer}} = +Q$).
