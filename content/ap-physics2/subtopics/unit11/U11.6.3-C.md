A Van de Graaff generator has a spherical dome of radius $0.15 \text{ m}$ charged to $+50 \text{ μC}$. What is the potential at the surface, and what is the potential at the center of the dome?

## Options
A) Surface: $3.0 \times 10^6 \text{ V}$; Center: $0 \text{ V}$
B) Surface: $3.0 \times 10^6 \text{ V}$; Center: $3.0 \times 10^6 \text{ V}$
C) Surface: $3.0 \times 10^6 \text{ V}$; Center: $1.5 \times 10^6 \text{ V}$
D) Surface: $1.5 \times 10^6 \text{ V}$; Center: $1.5 \times 10^6 \text{ V}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Surface potential: $V = kQ/R = (8.99 \times 10^9)(50 \times 10^{-6})/0.15 = 449{,}500/0.15 = 2.997 \times 10^6 \text{ V} \approx 3.0 \times 10^6 \text{ V}$. Inside a conducting sphere, $E = 0$, so the potential is constant and equal to the surface value. The potential at the center is also $3.0 \times 10^6 \text{ V}$.
