A graph of electric potential vs. position shows: from $x = 0$ to $x = 2 \text{ m}$, $V$ decreases linearly from $10 \text{ V}$ to $4 \text{ V}$; from $x = 2$ to $x = 4 \text{ m}$, $V$ is constant at $4 \text{ V}$; from $x = 4$ to $x = 6 \text{ m}$, $V$ decreases linearly from $4 \text{ V}$ to $0 \text{ V}$. What is the electric field in each region?

## Options
A) $E_1 = 3 \text{ N/C}$, $E_2 = 0$, $E_3 = 2 \text{ N/C}$ (all in $+x$)
B) $E_1 = -3 \text{ N/C}$, $E_2 = 0$, $E_3 = -2 \text{ N/C}$ (all in $-x$)
C) $E_1 = 3 \text{ N/C}$, $E_2 = 4 \text{ V}$, $E_3 = 2 \text{ N/C}$
D) $E_1 = -3 \text{ N/C}$, $E_2 = 0$, $E_3 = 2 \text{ N/C}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Region 1 ($0$ to $2 \text{ m}$): $E = -\Delta V/\Delta x = -(4-10)/(2-0) = -(-6)/2 = +3 \text{ N/C}$. Region 2 ($2$ to $4 \text{ m}$): $V$ is constant, slope $= 0$, so $E = 0$. Region 3 ($4$ to $6 \text{ m}$): $E = -(0-4)/(6-4) = -(-4)/2 = +2 \text{ N/C}$. All fields point in $+x$ direction (potential decreases in $+x$).
