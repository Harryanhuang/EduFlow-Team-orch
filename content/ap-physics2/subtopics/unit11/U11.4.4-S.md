Which statement about equipotential lines and electric field lines is correct?

## Options
A) Equipotential lines are parallel to field lines.
B) The electric field is strongest where equipotential lines are farthest apart.
C) Moving a charge along an equipotential line requires zero work.
D) Equipotential lines for a point charge are straight lines.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
By definition, an equipotential is a surface (or line in 2D) where $V$ is constant. Work done moving a charge along it: $W = q\Delta V = q \times 0 = 0$. Equipotentials are perpendicular to field lines (A is wrong), the field is strongest where equipotentials are closest together (B is wrong), and for a point charge they are concentric circles (D is wrong).
