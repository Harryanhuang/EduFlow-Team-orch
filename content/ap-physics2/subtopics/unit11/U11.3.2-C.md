An electric dipole is aligned with (parallel to) a non-uniform electric field that increases in magnitude to the right. Which statement about the net force on the dipole is correct?

## Options
A) No net force, because the dipole is neutral.
B) Net force to the right (toward stronger field).
C) Net force to the left (toward weaker field).
D) The force depends on the dipole moment magnitude, not the field gradient.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
In a uniform field, a dipole experiences torque but no net force. In a non-uniform field, the force on the positive charge (in the stronger field region) is larger than the force on the negative charge (in the weaker region). When the dipole is aligned with the field, both forces point in the same direction — the positive charge is pushed in the field direction and the negative charge is pulled opposite, but the positive charge experiences a larger force. The net force is toward the stronger field (right).
