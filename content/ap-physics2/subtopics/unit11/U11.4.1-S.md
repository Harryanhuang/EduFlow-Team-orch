Charge $q_1 = +6 \text{ nC}$ is at $(0, 0)$ and $q_2 = +6 \text{ nC}$ is at $(0.40, 0) \text{ m}$. What is the electric field at $(0.20, 0.20) \text{ m}$?

## Options
A) $6.4 \times 10^{2} \text{ N/C}$ in the $+y$ direction
B) $9.5 \times 10^{2} \text{ N/C}$ in the $+y$ direction
C) $1.3 \times 10^{3} \text{ N/C}$ in the $+y$ direction
D) $0 \text{ N/C}$ (fields cancel)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Both charges are equidistant from the point. $r = \sqrt{0.20^2 + 0.20^2} = 0.283 \text{ m}$. $E = kq/r^2 = (8.99 \times 10^9)(6 \times 10^{-9})/(0.283)^2 = 53.94/0.08 = 674 \text{ N/C}$ from each charge. By symmetry, $x$-components cancel and $y$-components add. Each field makes $45°$ with $x$-axis: $E_y = 2 \times 674 \times \sin 45° = 2 \times 674 \times 0.707 = 953 \text{ N/C} \approx 9.5 \times 10^2 \text{ N/C}$ in the $+y$ direction.
