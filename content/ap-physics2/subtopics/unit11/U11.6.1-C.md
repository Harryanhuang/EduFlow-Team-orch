A uniformly charged ring of radius $R = 0.10 \text{ m}$ carries total charge $+10 \text{ nC}$. What is the potential at the center of the ring?

## Options
A) $0 \text{ V}$ (by symmetry)
B) $900 \text{ V}$
C) $450 \text{ V}$
D) $1{,}800 \text{ V}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Every element of charge $dq$ is at the same distance $R$ from the center. $V = \int k \, dq / R = (k/R) \int dq = kQ/R = (8.99 \times 10^9)(10 \times 10^{-9})/0.10 = 89.9/0.10 = 899 \text{ V} \approx 900 \text{ V}$. Unlike the field (which is zero at the center by symmetry), the potential is a scalar and all contributions add constructively.
