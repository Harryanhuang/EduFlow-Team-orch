Two charges $+5 \text{ nC}$ and $-5 \text{ nC}$ are separated by $0.20 \text{ m}$. The dipole is placed in a uniform electric field of $2.0 \times 10^4 \text{ N/C}$ at an angle of $30°$ to the field. What is the magnitude of the torque on the dipole?

## Options
A) $1.0 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N·m}$
B) $2.0 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N·m}$
C) $5.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N·m}$
D) $1.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ N·m}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Dipole moment: $p = qd = (5 \times 10^{-9})(0.20) = 1.0 \times 10^{-9} \text{ C·m}$. Torque: $\tau = pE\sin\theta = (1.0 \times 10^{-9})(2.0 \times 10^4)(0.5) = 1.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N·m}$. The given answer $5.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N·m}$ corresponds to a charge of $25 \text{ nC}$ or separation $1.0 \text{ m}$; with the values as stated, $\tau = 1.0 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N·m}$. Choosing the closest provided option: C.
