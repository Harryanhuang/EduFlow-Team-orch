An electron ($m = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$, $q = -1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$) enters a uniform electric field of $2.0 \times 10^4 \text{ N/C}$ pointing downward, with an initial horizontal velocity of $3.0 \times 10^6 \text{ m/s}$. What is the acceleration of the electron?

## Options
A) $3.5 \times 10^{15} \text{ m/s}^2$ upward
B) $3.5 \times 10^{15} \text{ m/s}^2$ downward
C) $2.2 \times 10^{14} \text{ m/s}^2$ upward
D) $5.5 \times 10^{-27} \text{ m/s}^2$ upward

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$a = F/m = qE/m = (1.6 \times 10^{-19})(2.0 \times 10^4) / (9.11 \times 10^{-31}) = 3.2 \times 10^{-15} / 9.11 \times 10^{-31} = 3.5 \times 10^{15} \text{ m/s}^2$. Since the electron is negative, the force is opposite to the field (which points downward), so the acceleration is upward.
