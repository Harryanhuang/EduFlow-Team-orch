Sphere A has $+12 \text{ nC}$ and sphere B (identical to A) is neutral. They touch and separate. Then sphere A touches an identical neutral sphere C and separates. What is the charge on each sphere?

## Options
A) A: $+3 \text{ nC}$, B: $+6 \text{ nC}$, C: $+3 \text{ nC}$
B) A: $+6 \text{ nC}$, B: $+6 \text{ nC}$, C: $0 \text{ nC}$
C) A: $+3 \text{ nC}$, B: $+3 \text{ nC}$, C: $+6 \text{ nC}$
D) A: $+4 \text{ nC}$, B: $+4 \text{ nC}$, C: $+4 \text{ nC}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Step 1: A ($+12$) touches B ($0$). Identical spheres share equally: A $= +6$, B $= +6$. Step 2: A ($+6$) touches C ($0$). Share equally: A $= +3$, C $= +3$. Final: A $= +3$, B $= +6$, C $= +3$. Total $= 12 \text{ nC}$ throughout, confirming charge conservation.
