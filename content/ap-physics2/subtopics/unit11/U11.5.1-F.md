Two charges $q_1 = +3 \text{ nC}$ and $q_2 = -5 \text{ nC}$ are $0.10 \text{ m}$ apart. What is the electric potential energy of this pair?

## Options
A) $-1.35 \times 10^{-6} \text{ J}$
B) $+1.35 \times 10^{-6} \text{ J}$
C) $-1.35 \times 10^{-3} \text{ J}$
D) $+1.35 \times 10^{-3} \text{ J}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$U = kq_1 q_2 / r = (8.99 \times 10^9)(3 \times 10^{-9})(-5 \times 10^{-9}) / 0.10 = (8.99 \times -15 \times 10^{-9}) / 0.10 = -1.35 \times 10^{-6} \text{ J}$. The energy is negative because the charges have opposite signs (attractive interaction).
