Which property of electric field lines is correct?

## Options
A) Field lines can cross at points of zero field.
B) Field lines are more closely spaced where the field is stronger.
C) Field lines point from negative to positive charges.
D) Field lines form closed loops in electrostatic situations.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The density (closeness) of field lines represents the magnitude of the electric field — closer lines mean a stronger field. Field lines never cross (each point has a unique field direction), they point from positive to negative, and they do not form closed loops in electrostatics (they start on positive charges and end on negative charges).
