A positive charge is released from rest at a point with electric potential of $100 \text{ V}$. It moves to a point with potential $60 \text{ V}$. What happens?

## Options
A) It accelerates; its kinetic energy increases by $40 \text{ eV}$ per unit charge.
B) It decelerates; it loses kinetic energy.
C) It moves at constant velocity.
D) It cannot move without an external force.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
A positive charge spontaneously moves from high to low potential (in the direction of the electric field). Its potential energy decreases: $\Delta U = q\Delta V = q(60 - 100) = -40q$. By energy conservation, kinetic energy increases by the same amount: $\Delta K = +40q$. Per unit charge, this is $40 \text{ eV}$ (or $40 \text{ J/C}$).
