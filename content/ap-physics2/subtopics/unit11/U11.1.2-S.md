A neutral metal sphere on an insulating stand is brought near a positively charged rod. A ground wire is briefly connected to the far side of the sphere and then removed. The rod is then removed. What is the charge on the sphere?

## Options
A) Positive
B) Negative
C) Neutral
D) Depends on the material of the rod

## Answer
B

## Explanation
When the positive rod is near, it attracts electrons to the near side and repels positive charge (pushes electrons away). Connecting the ground allows electrons to flow from the ground into the sphere (attracted by the positive rod). Removing the ground, then the rod, leaves the sphere with a net negative charge. This is charging by induction.
