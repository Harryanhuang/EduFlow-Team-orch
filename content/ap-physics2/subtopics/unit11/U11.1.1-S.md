Two charges $q_1 = +4 \text{ nC}$ and $q_2 = -4 \text{ nC}$ are placed $0.30 \text{ m}$ apart on the $x$-axis ($q_1$ at origin, $q_2$ at $x = 0.30$). A third charge $q_3 = +2 \text{ nC}$ is placed at $x = 0.15 \text{ m}$ (midpoint). What is the net force on $q_3$?

## Options
A) $0 \text{ N}$
B) $3.2 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N}$, toward $q_1$
C) $3.2 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N}$, toward $q_2$
D) $6.4 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N}$, toward $q_2$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Force from $q_1$ on $q_3$: $F_{13} = kq_1 q_3/r^2 = (8.99 \times 10^9)(4 \times 10^{-9})(2 \times 10^{-9})/(0.15)^2 = 3.20 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N}$ (repulsive, pointing away from $q_1$, i.e., $+x$). Force from $q_2$ on $q_3$: $F_{23} = k|q_2|q_3/r^2 = (8.99 \times 10^9)(4 \times 10^{-9})(2 \times 10^{-9})/(0.15)^2 = 3.20 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N}$ (attractive, pointing toward $q_2$, i.e., $+x$). Both forces point in the $+x$ direction (toward $q_2$), so $F_{\text{net}} = 3.20 \times 10^{-5} + 3.20 \times 10^{-5} = 6.4 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N}$ toward $q_2$. Wait — $F_{13}$ is repulsive (both positive), so it pushes $q_3$ away from $q_1$, i.e., toward $q_2$. $F_{23}$ is attractive ($q_2$ negative), so it pulls $q_3$ toward $q_2$. Both forces point in the same direction. Net force $= 6.4 \times 10^{-5} \text{ N}$ toward $q_2$. The answer is D.
