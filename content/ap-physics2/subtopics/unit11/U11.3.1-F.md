A $+2 \text{ nC}$ test charge experiences a force of $6 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N}$ to the right. What is the electric field at that point?

## Options
A) $3 \times 10^{-4} \text{ N/C}$ to the right
B) $3 \times 10^{5} \text{ N/C}$ to the right
C) $1.2 \times 10^{-12} \text{ N/C}$ to the right
D) $3 \times 10^{5} \text{ N/C}$ to the left

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$E = F/q_0 = (6 \times 10^{-4}) / (2 \times 10^{-9}) = 3 \times 10^{5} \text{ N/C}$. The field points in the same direction as the force on a positive test charge — to the right.
