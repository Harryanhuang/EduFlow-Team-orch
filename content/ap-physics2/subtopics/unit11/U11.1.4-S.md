A $+6 \text{ nC}$ charged sphere is brought into contact with an identical uncharged sphere. The spheres are then separated. What is the charge on each sphere?

## Options
A) $+6 \text{ nC}$ and $0 \text{ nC}$
B) $+3 \text{ nC}$ and $+3 \text{ nC}$
C) $+6 \text{ nC}$ and $-6 \text{ nC}$
D) $+4 \text{ nC}$ and $+2 \text{ nC}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
When two identical conductors touch, charge redistributes equally. Total charge $= +6 \text{ nC} + 0 = +6 \text{ nC}$. After contact: each sphere has $+6/2 = +3 \text{ nC}$. Charge is conserved (total remains $+6 \text{ nC}$) and distributes equally because the spheres are identical.
