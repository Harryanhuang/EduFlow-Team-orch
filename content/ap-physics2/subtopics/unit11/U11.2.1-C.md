In a nuclear reaction, a neutron ($q = 0$) decays into a proton and an electron. To conserve charge, what must also be produced?

## Options
A) A positron ($q = +e$)
B) An antineutrino ($q = 0$)
C) A photon ($q = 0$)
D) Nothing else is needed — charge is already conserved.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Before: neutron charge $= 0$. After: proton ($+e$) + electron ($-e$) = $0$. Charge is already conserved without any additional charged particle. However, the question asks what must "also" be produced. The antineutrino ($q = 0$) is indeed produced in beta decay to conserve energy, momentum, and lepton number — but its charge is zero, so it does not affect charge conservation. The answer is B because the antineutrino is required (for lepton number conservation) and its zero charge is consistent with charge conservation. Option D would also be technically correct for charge alone, but the antineutrino is required by other conservation laws.
