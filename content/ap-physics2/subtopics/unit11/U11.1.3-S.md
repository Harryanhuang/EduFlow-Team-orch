A dielectric with $K = 3.0$ is placed between the plates of a parallel-plate capacitor where the original field (without dielectric) is $E_0 = 9{,}000 \text{ N/C}$. What is the electric field inside the dielectric?

## Options
A) $3{,}000 \text{ N/C}$
B) $6{,}000 \text{ N/C}$
C) $9{,}000 \text{ N/C}$
D) $27{,}000 \text{ N/C}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The dielectric reduces the field by a factor of $K$: $E_{\text{net}} = E_0/K = 9{,}000 / 3.0 = 3{,}000 \text{ N/C}$. The molecules in the dielectric align to create an internal field that partially cancels the external field.
