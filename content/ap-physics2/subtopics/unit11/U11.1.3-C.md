A positively charged rod is brought near (but does not touch) a neutral conducting sphere on an insulating stand. Which statement about the force on the sphere is correct?

## Options
A) There is no force because the sphere is neutral.
B) The sphere is repelled because both the rod and induced positive charges are on the same side.
C) The sphere is attracted because the induced negative charges are closer to the rod than the induced positive charges.
D) The sphere is attracted because the rod induces a net positive charge on the sphere.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The positive rod induces a negative charge on the near side of the sphere and a positive charge on the far side. The negative charges are closer to the rod, so the attractive force on them is stronger than the repulsive force on the farther positive charges. The net force is attractive. This is a general result: a charged object always attracts a neutral conductor via polarization.
