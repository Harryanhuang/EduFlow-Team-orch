A solid metal sphere carries a net charge. Inside the metal (not the cavity), the electric field is:

## Options
A) Zero
B) Nonzero, pointing outward
C) Nonzero, pointing inward
D) Depends on the amount of charge

## Answer
A

## Explanation
In electrostatic equilibrium, $E = 0$ inside a conductor. Free charges redistribute on the surface until the internal field is exactly zero. This is a defining property of conductors in equilibrium.
