Three charges form an equilateral triangle with side $0.20 \text{ m}$: $q_1 = +2 \text{ nC}$ at vertex A, $q_2 = -3 \text{ nC}$ at vertex B, $q_3 = +4 \text{ nC}$ at vertex C. What is the total electric potential energy of this configuration?

## Options
A) $-1.80 \times 10^{-7} \text{ J}$
B) $+1.80 \times 10^{-7} \text{ J}$
C) $-3.60 \times 10^{-7} \text{ J}$
D) $+3.60 \times 10^{-7} \text{ J}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Three pairs: $U_{12} = kq_1 q_2/r = (8.99 \times 10^9)(2 \times 10^{-9})(-3 \times 10^{-9})/0.20 = -2.70 \times 10^{-7} \text{ J}$. $U_{13} = kq_1 q_3/r = (8.99 \times 10^9)(2 \times 10^{-9})(4 \times 10^{-9})/0.20 = +3.60 \times 10^{-7} \text{ J}$. $U_{23} = kq_2 q_3/r = (8.99 \times 10^9)(-3 \times 10^{-9})(4 \times 10^{-9})/0.20 = -5.39 \times 10^{-7} \text{ J}$. Total: $U = (-2.70 + 3.60 - 5.39) \times 10^{-7} = -4.49 \times 10^{-7} \text{ J}$. Closest to A. The total is negative because the attractive interactions (opposite-sign pairs) dominate.
