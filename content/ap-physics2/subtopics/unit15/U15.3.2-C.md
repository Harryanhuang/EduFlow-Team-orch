Classical wave theory failed to explain the photoelectric effect because:

## Options
A) The effect was too weak to detect
B) Classical theory predicted no dependence on frequency, but $K_{\text{max}}$ depends on $f$
C) The effect occurs only with visible light
D) Classical theory predicted too many electrons

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Classical theory predicted that any color of light could eject electrons if the intensity were high enough, and that $K_{\text{max}}$ would increase with intensity. Experiments showed that $K_{\text{max}}$ depends only on frequency, and below $f_0$ no electrons are emitted no matter how bright the light. This required Einstein's photon model: each photon's energy $hf$ must exceed the work function to eject one electron.
