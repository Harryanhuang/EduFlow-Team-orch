A metal has a work function of $2.0 \text{ eV}$. Light with frequency $8.0 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$ shines on it. What is the maximum kinetic energy of ejected electrons? ($h = 4.14 \times 10^{-15} \text{ eV·s}$)

## Options
A) $1.31 \text{ eV}$
B) $2.0 \text{ eV}$
C) $3.0 \text{ eV}$
D) $5.0 \text{ eV}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$hf = (4.14 \times 10^{-15})(8.0 \times 10^{14}) = 3.31 \text{ eV}$. $K_{\text{max}} = hf - W = 3.31 - 2.0 = 1.31 \text{ eV}$.
