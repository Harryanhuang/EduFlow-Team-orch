An electron absorbs a photon and moves to a higher energy level. What happens next?

## Options
A) It stays in the higher level forever
B) It eventually falls back to a lower level, emitting a photon
C) It splits into two electrons
D) It leaves the atom

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Excited states are typically short-lived. The electron eventually drops to a lower (often ground) state, emitting a photon with energy equal to the energy difference of the levels: $E_{\text{photon}} = E_{\text{initial}} - E_{\text{final}}$.
