The discovery of the neutrino was motivated by the observation that in beta decay:

## Options
A) The electron always had the same kinetic energy
B) The electron had a continuous energy spectrum, not a single value
C) Energy was not conserved
D) Momentum was not conserved

## Answer
B

## Explanation
If only the electron and recoil nucleus were produced, conservation of energy and momentum would require the electron to have a single fixed energy. Instead, electrons showed a continuous spectrum up to a maximum. Pauli proposed (1930) that an invisible third particle (the neutrino) carries away the missing energy and momentum, preserving all conservation laws.
