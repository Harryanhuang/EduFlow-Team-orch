What is the de Broglie wavelength of an electron ($m = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$, $v = 1.0 \times 10^6 \text{ m/s}$)? ($h = 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J·s}$)

## Options
A) $7.3 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m}$
B) $7.3 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$
C) $6.6 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m}$
D) $1.5 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$\lambda = h/(mv) = (6.63 \times 10^{-34})/((9.11 \times 10^{-31})(1.0 \times 10^6)) = 6.63 \times 10^{-34} / 9.11 \times 10^{-25} = 7.28 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m} \approx 7.3 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m}$.
