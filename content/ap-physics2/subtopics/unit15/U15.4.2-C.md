The double-slit experiment with single electrons shows:

## Options
A) Electrons always act as particles
B) Electrons always act as waves
C) The build-up of a wave interference pattern from individual particle impacts
D) Classical wave-particle dualism

## Answer
C

## Explanation
When fired one at a time, each electron hits the detector as a localized point (particle-like). But the accumulated pattern over many electrons shows the bright and dark fringes of interference (wave-like). This demonstrates the wave-particle duality of matter and the role of measurement in quantum phenomena.
