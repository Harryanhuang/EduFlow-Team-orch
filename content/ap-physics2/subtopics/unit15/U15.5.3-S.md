A hydrogen atom in its ground state ($n = 1$) is illuminated with a photon of energy $10.0 \text{ eV}$. Which transition occurs?

## Options
A) $n = 1 \to n = 2$ (energy needed: $10.2 \text{ eV}$)
B) The photon has insufficient energy; no transition occurs
C) $n = 1 \to n = 3$ (energy needed: $12.09 \text{ eV}$)
D) The atom is ionized

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The energy needed to go from $n = 1$ to $n = 2$ is $10.2 \text{ eV}$; for $n = 3$, it's $12.09 \text{ eV}$. A $10.0 \text{ eV}$ photon has insufficient energy and is not absorbed. The atom remains in the ground state.
