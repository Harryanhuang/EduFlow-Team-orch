An X-ray photon scatters at $60°$ and its wavelength increases from $0.050 \text{ nm}$ to $0.052 \text{ nm}$. What is the kinetic energy of the recoil electron? ($h = 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J·s}$, $c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$)

## Options
A) $0 \text{ eV}$
B) $2.4 \times 10^{-17} \text{ J}$
C) $3.2 \times 10^{-17} \text{ J}$
D) $5.6 \times 10^{-17} \text{ J}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$\Delta E = hc(1/\lambda_i - 1/\lambda_f) = (6.63 \times 10^{-34})(3.0 \times 10^8)(1/5.0 \times 10^{-11} - 1/5.2 \times 10^{-11}) = (1.989 \times 10^{-25})(0.4 \times 10^{10}) = 1.99 \times 10^{-15} \times 0.04 = 7.95 \times 10^{-17} \text{ J}$. With exact numbers: $1/0.050 = 20$, $1/0.052 = 19.23$, diff $= 0.77$. $hc \times 0.77 / 10^{9} = 1.99 \times 10^{-25} \times 0.77 \times 10^{9} = 1.53 \times 10^{-16} \text{ J}$. Closest to B.
