After a positron is emitted in beta-plus decay, it quickly encounters an electron. What happens?

## Options
A) They orbit each other forming positronium
B) They annihilate, producing two gamma-ray photons
C) The positron is absorbed into the nucleus
D) Nothing — they pass through each other

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The positron (antimatter electron) annihilates with a nearby electron (matter), converting all mass to energy: $e^+ + e^- \to 2\gamma$. Each gamma photon has energy $0.511 \text{ MeV}$ (= rest mass energy of electron/positron). This is the basis of PET (Positron Emission Tomography) scanning.
