If a hydrogen atom emits light at $656 \text{ nm}$ (Balmer-alpha), what can we say about its absorption spectrum?

## Options
A) It absorbs all wavelengths uniformly
B) It absorbs strongly at $656 \text{ nm}$
C) It absorbs strongly at the Lyman series
D) It absorbs only UV light

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By Kirchhoff's principle of thermal radiation, a cool gas absorbs the same wavelengths it would emit. Hydrogen atoms that emit at $656 \text{ nm}$ (transition $n = 3 \to n = 2$) also absorb at $656 \text{ nm}$ when cool gas sits in front of a continuous light source.
