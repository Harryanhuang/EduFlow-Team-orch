After alpha or beta decay, the daughter nucleus is often in an excited state. It releases the excess energy by:

## Options
A) Emitting another particle
B) Emitting a gamma-ray photon
C) Undergoing beta decay again
D) Splitting into two smaller nuclei

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The excited nucleus transitions to its ground state by emitting a gamma-ray photon. This is gamma decay — no change in $A$ or $Z$, only in the nuclear energy state.
