According to the binding energy curve, which process releases energy for uranium-235?

## Options
A) Fusion with hydrogen
B) Fission (splitting into lighter nuclei)
C) Alpha decay
D) Gamma emission

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Uranium-235 is well past the iron peak on the binding energy curve. Splitting it into lighter fragments moves the products to higher binding energy per nucleon, releasing the difference as energy. This is nuclear fission — the basis of nuclear power plants and atomic bombs.
