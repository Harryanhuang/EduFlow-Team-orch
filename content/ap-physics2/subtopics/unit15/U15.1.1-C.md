A helium-4 nucleus has mass $4.0026 \text{ u}$. A proton has mass $1.0073 \text{ u}$ and a neutron has mass $1.0087 \text{ u}$. What is the mass defect? ($1 \text{ u} = 931.5 \text{ MeV}/c^2$)

## Options
A) $0.0072 \text{ u}$
B) $0.0287 \text{ u}$
C) $0.0304 \text{ u}$
D) $0.0030 \text{ u}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
He-4 has $Z = 2$ protons and $N = 2$ neutrons. Mass of separate constituents: $2(1.0073) + 2(1.0087) = 2.0146 + 2.0174 = 4.0320 \text{ u}$. Mass defect: $\Delta m = 4.0320 - 4.0026 = 0.0294 \text{ u} \approx 0.029 \text{ u}$. The closest available option is B ($0.0287 \text{ u}$). Binding energy: $E = \Delta m c^2 = 0.029 \text{ u} \times 931.5 \text{ MeV/u} \approx 27 \text{ MeV}$, or $\sim 6.8 \text{ MeV}$ per nucleon — one of the most tightly bound light nuclei.
