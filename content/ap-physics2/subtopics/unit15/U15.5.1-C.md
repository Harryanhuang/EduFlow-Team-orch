A hydrogen transition produces a photon at $656 \text{ nm}$. Which series does this belong to?

## Options
A) Lyman
B) Balmer
C) Paschen
D) Brackett

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$656 \text{ nm}$ corresponds to the H-alpha line of the Balmer series (transition from $n = 3$ to $n = 2$). The Balmer series lies in the visible region of the EM spectrum, while Lyman is UV and Paschen/Brackett/Pfund are IR.
