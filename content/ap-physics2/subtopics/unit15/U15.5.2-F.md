What is the source of the bright lines in an atomic emission spectrum?

## Options
A) Blackbody radiation from the hot gas
B) Electrons transitioning from higher to lower energy levels
C) Nuclei vibrating in the gas
D) Reflection of light from the gas

## Answer
B

## Explanation
In a hot, low-density gas, atoms are excited by collisions. When excited electrons drop to lower levels, they emit photons with energies equal to the level differences. The resulting discrete wavelengths are the bright spectral lines unique to each element.
