A $1.0 \text{ kg}$ ball moves at $1.0 \text{ m/s}$. What is its de Broglie wavelength?

## Options
A) $6.6 \times 10^{-34} \text{ m}$
B) $6.6 \times 10^{-32} \text{ m}$
C) $6.6 \times 10^{-36} \text{ m}$
D) $1.0 \text{ m}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$\lambda = h/(mv) = (6.63 \times 10^{-34})/(1.0 \times 1.0) = 6.63 \times 10^{-34} \text{ m}$. This is orders of magnitude smaller than atomic scales, making wave behavior undetectable for macroscopic objects.
