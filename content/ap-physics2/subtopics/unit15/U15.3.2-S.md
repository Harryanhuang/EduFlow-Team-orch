A photoelectric experiment uses light at $6.0 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$. The stopping potential is $0.50 \text{ V}$. What is the work function of the metal?

## Options
A) $0.50 \text{ eV}$
B) $1.98 \text{ eV}$
C) $2.48 \text{ eV}$
D) $0.50 \text{ J}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$hf = (4.14 \times 10^{-15})(6.0 \times 10^{14}) = 2.48 \text{ eV}$. $W = hf - eV_s = 2.48 - 0.50 = 1.98 \text{ eV}$.
