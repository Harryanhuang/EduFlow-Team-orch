A hydrogen atom transitions from $n = 3$ to $n = 1$. What is the wavelength of the emitted photon? (Hydrogen energy levels: $E_n = -13.6/n^2 \text{ eV}$)

## Options
A) $102 \text{ nm}$
B) $121 \text{ nm}$
C) $656 \text{ nm}$
D) $1875 \text{ nm}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$E_3 = -13.6/9 = -1.51 \text{ eV}$. $E_1 = -13.6/1 = -13.6 \text{ eV}$. $\Delta E = -1.51 - (-13.6) = 12.09 \text{ eV}$. $\lambda = hc/\Delta E = 1240/12.09 = 102.6 \text{ nm} \approx 102 \text{ nm}$ (in UV).
