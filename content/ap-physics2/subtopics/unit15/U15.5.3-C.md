The Bohr model accurately predicts energy levels for:

## Options
A) All atoms
B) Only hydrogen and hydrogen-like (single-electron) ions
C) Only multi-electron atoms
D) Only solids

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The Bohr model assumes a single electron orbiting a nucleus with no electron-electron interactions. It works for hydrogen (1 electron) and ions like He+, Li2+, but breaks down for multi-electron atoms where electron repulsion and shielding modify the energy levels significantly.
