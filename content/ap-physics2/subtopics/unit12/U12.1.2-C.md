A wire has a resistivity of $\rho = 1.7 \times 10^{-8} \text{ Ω·m}$. If the electric field inside the wire is $0.50 \text{ V/m}$, what is the current density?

## Options
A) $8.5 \times 10^{-9} \text{ A/m}^2$
B) $2.9 \times 10^{7} \text{ A/m}^2$
C) $3.4 \times 10^{-8} \text{ A/m}^2$
D) $1.7 \times 10^{-8} \text{ A/m}^2$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$\sigma = 1/\rho = 1/(1.7 \times 10^{-8}) = 5.88 \times 10^{7} \text{ S/m}$. $J = \sigma E = 5.88 \times 10^{7} \times 0.50 = 2.94 \times 10^{7} \text{ A/m}^2 \approx 2.9 \times 10^{7} \text{ A/m}^2$.
