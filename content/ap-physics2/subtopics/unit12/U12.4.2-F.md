At time $t = 0$, a $12 \text{ V}$ battery is connected in series with a $10 \text{ kΩ}$ resistor and an uncharged $100 \text{ μF}$ capacitor. At the instant the switch closes, what is the voltage across the resistor?

## Options
A) $0 \text{ V}$
B) $6 \text{ V}$
C) $12 \text{ V}$
D) $24 \text{ V}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
At $t = 0$, the uncharged capacitor has $q = 0$, so $V_C = q/C = 0$. By the loop rule: $\varepsilon - V_R - V_C = 0$, so $V_R = \varepsilon - V_C = 12 - 0 = 12 \text{ V}$. All the battery voltage drops across the resistor initially.
