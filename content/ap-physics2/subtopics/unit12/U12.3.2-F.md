A $10 \text{ μF}$ capacitor is charged to $200 \text{ V}$. How much energy is stored?

## Options
A) $0.10 \text{ J}$
B) $0.20 \text{ J}$
C) $0.40 \text{ J}$
D) $2.0 \text{ J}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 = \frac{1}{2}(10 \times 10^{-6})(200)^2 = \frac{1}{2}(10 \times 10^{-6})(4 \times 10^{4}) = \frac{1}{2}(0.40) = 0.20 \text{ J}$.
