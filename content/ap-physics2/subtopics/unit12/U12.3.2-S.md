A $20 \text{ μF}$ capacitor is charged to $50 \text{ V}$, then disconnected. A dielectric with $K = 4$ is inserted. What is the new stored energy?

## Options
A) $0.025 \text{ J}$
B) $0.10 \text{ J}$
C) $0.00625 \text{ J}$
D) $0.20 \text{ J}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Initial: $Q = CV = 20 \times 10^{-6} \times 50 = 1.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ C}$. After dielectric: $C' = 4 \times 20 = 80 \text{ μF}$. Charge conserved: $Q = 1.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ C}$. New voltage: $V' = Q/C' = 1.0 \times 10^{-3} / (80 \times 10^{-6}) = 12.5 \text{ V}$. Energy: $U = \frac{1}{2}QV' = \frac{1}{2}(1.0 \times 10^{-3})(12.5) = 6.25 \times 10^{-3} \text{ J} = 0.00625 \text{ J}$.
