A resistor has a resistance of $100 \text{ Ω}$. If a current of $0.50 \text{ A}$ flows through it, what is the voltage across it?

## Options
A) $50 \text{ V}$
B) $200 \text{ V}$
C) $0.005 \text{ V}$
D) $5.0 \text{ V}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
By Ohm's law: $V = IR = 0.50 \times 100 = 50 \text{ V}$.
