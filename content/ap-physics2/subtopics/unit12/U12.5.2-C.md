A circuit contains a $12 \text{ V}$ battery, a $4 \text{ Ω}$ resistor, and a light bulb in series. The bulb's resistance varies with current: at $1.0 \text{ A}$ it is $2 \text{ Ω}$, at $1.5 \text{ A}$ it is $4 \text{ Ω}$, at $2.0 \text{ A}$ it is $6 \text{ Ω}$. What is the current in the circuit?

## Options
A) $1.0 \text{ A}$
B) $1.5 \text{ A}$
C) $2.0 \text{ A}$
D) $3.0 \text{ A}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By Kirchhoff's loop rule: $12 = I(4 + R_{\text{bulb}})$. Try $I = 1.5 \text{ A}$: $R_{\text{bulb}} = 4 \text{ Ω}$ at this current. $I(4 + 4) = 1.5 \times 8 = 12 \text{ V}$ ✓. The circuit settles at $I = 1.5 \text{ A}$ where the total resistance equals $8 \text{ Ω}$.
