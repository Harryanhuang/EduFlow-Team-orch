A $5.0 \text{ μF}$ capacitor charged to $100 \text{ V}$ is discharged through a $200 \text{ kΩ}$ resistor. What voltage remains after $2.0 \text{ s}$?

## Options
A) $14 \text{ V}$
B) $37 \text{ V}$
C) $61 \text{ V}$
D) $82 \text{ V}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$\tau = RC = (200 \times 10^3)(5.0 \times 10^{-6}) = 1.0 \text{ s}$. $V(t) = V_0 e^{-t/\tau} = 100 \times e^{-2.0/1.0} = 100 \times e^{-2} = 100 \times 0.135 = 13.5 \text{ V} \approx 14 \text{ V}$.
