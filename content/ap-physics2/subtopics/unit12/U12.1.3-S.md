Two resistors $R_1 = 4 \text{ Ω}$ and $R_2 = 12 \text{ Ω}$ are connected in parallel across a $24 \text{ V}$ source. Which statement about power dissipation is correct?

## Options
A) $R_1$ dissipates more power.
B) $R_2$ dissipates more power.
C) Both dissipate the same power.
D) Cannot determine without knowing the current.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
In parallel, both resistors have the same voltage: $P_1 = V^2/R_1 = 576/4 = 144 \text{ W}$; $P_2 = V^2/R_2 = 576/12 = 48 \text{ W}$. The smaller resistor dissipates more power in parallel (inversely proportional to $R$).
