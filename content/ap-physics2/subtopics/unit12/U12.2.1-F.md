A wire has resistivity $\rho = 1.7 \times 10^{-8} \text{ Ω·m}$, length $2.0 \text{ m}$, and cross-sectional area $0.50 \text{ mm}^2$. What is its resistance?

## Options
A) $1.7 \times 10^{-2} \text{ Ω}$
B) $6.8 \times 10^{-2} \text{ Ω}$
C) $0.17 \text{ Ω}$
D) $6.8 \times 10^{-5} \text{ Ω}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
$R = \rho L/A = (1.7 \times 10^{-8})(2.0) / (0.50 \times 10^{-6}) = 3.4 \times 10^{-8} / 5.0 \times 10^{-7} = 0.068 \text{ Ω} = 6.8 \times 10^{-2} \text{ Ω}$.
