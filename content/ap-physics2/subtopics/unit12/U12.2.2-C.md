Two resistors $R_1 = 3 \text{ Ω}$ and $R_2 = 9 \text{ Ω}$ are in series with a $12 \text{ V}$ source. Which ratio correctly describes the power dissipated by $R_2$ to $R_1$?

## Options
A) $1:1$
B) $1:3$
C) $3:1$
D) $9:1$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
In series, $I$ is the same. $P_2/P_1 = I^2R_2 / (I^2R_1) = R_2/R_1 = 9/3 = 3:1$. The larger resistor dissipates three times more power.
