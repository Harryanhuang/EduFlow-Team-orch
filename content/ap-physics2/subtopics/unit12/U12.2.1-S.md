A copper wire ($\alpha = 3.9 \times 10^{-3} \text{ K}^{-1}$) has resistance $5.0 \text{ Ω}$ at $20°\text{C}$. What is its resistance at $120°\text{C}$?

## Options
A) $5.0 \text{ Ω}$
B) $6.0 \text{ Ω}$
C) $7.0 \text{ Ω}$
D) $10.0 \text{ Ω}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
$R = R_0[1 + \alpha\Delta T] = 5.0[1 + (3.9 \times 10^{-3})(100)] = 5.0[1 + 0.39] = 5.0 \times 1.39 = 6.95 \text{ Ω} \approx 7.0 \text{ Ω}$.
