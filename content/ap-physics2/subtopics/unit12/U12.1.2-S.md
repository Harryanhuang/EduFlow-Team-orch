A device has the following $V$-$I$ data: $(0.2 \text{ A}, 1.0 \text{ V})$, $(0.4 \text{ A}, 3.0 \text{ V})$, $(0.6 \text{ A}, 7.0 \text{ V})$. Is this device ohmic?

## Options
A) Yes, with $R = 5 \text{ Ω}$
B) Yes, with $R = 10 \text{ Ω}$
C) No, because the resistance increases with current
D) No, because the resistance decreases with current

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Calculate resistance at each point: $R_1 = 1.0/0.2 = 5 \text{ Ω}$, $R_2 = 3.0/0.4 = 7.5 \text{ Ω}$, $R_3 = 7.0/0.6 = 11.7 \text{ Ω}$. The resistance increases with current, so the $V$-$I$ graph is not a straight line through the origin. The device is non-ohmic.
