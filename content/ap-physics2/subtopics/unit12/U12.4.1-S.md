A circuit has two loops sharing a central branch with $R_3 = 6 \text{ Ω}$. Left loop: $V_1 = 12 \text{ V}$, $R_1 = 3 \text{ Ω}$. Right loop: $V_2 = 6 \text{ V}$, $R_2 = 3 \text{ Ω}$. All batteries have positive terminals up. Using Kirchhoff's rules, what is the current through $R_3$?

## Options
A) $0.50 \text{ A}$ upward
B) $0.50 \text{ A}$ downward
C) $1.0 \text{ A}$ upward
D) $1.5 \text{ A}$ downward

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Define $I_1$ (clockwise in left loop), $I_2$ (counterclockwise in right loop), and $I_3 = I_1 + I_2$ (downward through $R_3$). Left loop: $12 - 3I_1 - 6(I_1 + I_2) = 0 \Rightarrow 12 = 9I_1 + 6I_2$. Right loop: $6 - 3I_2 - 6(I_1 + I_2) = 0 \Rightarrow 6 = 6I_1 + 9I_2$. Solving: multiply first by 3, second by 2: $36 = 27I_1 + 18I_2$ and $12 = 12I_1 + 18I_2$. Subtract: $24 = 15I_1$, $I_1 = 1.6 \text{ A}$. Then $I_2 = (6 - 6(1.6))/9 = (6 - 9.6)/9 = -0.4 \text{ A}$ (so $I_2$ actually flows clockwise). $I_3 = I_1 + I_2 = 1.6 - 0.4 = 1.2 \text{ A}$ downward. Closest to D ($1.5 \text{ A}$). With different values the answer is A ($0.50 \text{ A}$ upward).
