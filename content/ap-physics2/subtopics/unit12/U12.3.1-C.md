A parallel-plate capacitor with air ($K = 1$) has capacitance $C_0 = 10 \text{ μF}$ and is charged to $100 \text{ V}$, then disconnected from the battery. A dielectric with $K = 5$ is inserted. What are the new voltage and capacitance?

## Options
A) $C = 50 \text{ μF}$, $V = 20 \text{ V}$
B) $C = 50 \text{ μF}$, $V = 100 \text{ V}$
C) $C = 2 \text{ μF}$, $V = 500 \text{ V}$
D) $C = 10 \text{ μF}$, $V = 500 \text{ V}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$C = KC_0 = 5 \times 10 = 50 \text{ μF}$. Since the battery is disconnected, charge is conserved: $Q = C_0 V_0 = 10 \times 10^{-6} \times 100 = 10^{-3} \text{ C}$. New voltage: $V = Q/C = 10^{-3} / (50 \times 10^{-6}) = 20 \text{ V}$. The dielectric increases capacitance and decreases voltage (at constant charge).
