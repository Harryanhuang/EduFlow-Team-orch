In a circuit with two junctions and three branches, two independent junction equations are written. If the circuit has 3 unknown branch currents, how many independent loop equations are needed?

## Options
A) $0$
B) $1$
C) $2$
D) $3$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For a circuit with $B$ branches and $J$ junctions: the number of independent junction equations is $J - 1$, and the number of independent loop equations is $B - (J - 1)$. Here: $B = 3$, $J = 2$. Independent junction equations: $J - 1 = 1$. Independent loop equations: $B - (J - 1) = 3 - 1 = 2$. But the question states two junction equations are written (one is dependent), so only $2 - 1 = 1$ independent junction equation exists. Independent loop equations needed: $3 - 1 = 2$. However, with only 3 unknowns and 1 independent junction equation, we need 2 loop equations. The answer is B if considering that the junction rule already provides 1 independent equation and we need 2 more from loops — but with 3 unknowns and 1 junction equation, 2 loop equations are needed. The answer is C.
