A U-tube contains water. One side is open to the atmosphere, and the other side is sealed with trapped air above the water. The water level on the open side is $10 \text{ cm}$ higher than on the sealed side. If atmospheric pressure is $1.0 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$, what is the pressure of the trapped air? ($\rho = 1000 \text{ kg/m}^3$, $g = 10 \text{ m/s}^2$)

## Options
A) $9.0 \times 10^4 \text{ Pa}$
B) $1.0 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$
C) $1.01 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$
D) $1.1 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
At the level of the water surface on the sealed side, the pressure from the sealed side equals the pressure from the open side. On the sealed side: $P_{\text{air}}$. On the open side at the same level: $P_{\text{atm}} + \rho g h = 1.0 \times 10^5 + 1000 \times 10 \times 0.10 = 1.0 \times 10^5 + 1{,}000 = 1.01 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$. Therefore $P_{\text{air}} = 1.01 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$.
