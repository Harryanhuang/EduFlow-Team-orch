A Venturi meter has a wide section with area $A_1 = 12 \text{ cm}^2$ and a throat with area $A_2 = 4 \text{ cm}^2$. The pressure difference between the wide section and the throat is $3{,}000 \text{ Pa}$. What is the volume flow rate of water through the meter? ($\rho = 1000 \text{ kg/m}^3$)

## Options
A) $1.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^3/\text{s}$
B) $2.4 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^3/\text{s}$
C) $3.6 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^3/\text{s}$
D) $6.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^3/\text{s}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
From Bernoulli's equation: $\Delta P = \frac{1}{2}\rho(v_2^2 - v_1^2)$. From continuity: $v_1 = v_2(A_2/A_1) = v_2/3$. Substituting: $\Delta P = \frac{1}{2}\rho v_2^2(1 - 1/9) = \frac{1}{2}\rho v_2^2 \cdot \frac{8}{9}$. Solving for $v_2$: $v_2 = \sqrt{\frac{9\Delta P}{4\rho}} = \sqrt{\frac{9 \times 3000}{4 \times 1000}} = \sqrt{6.75} = 2.60 \text{ m/s}$. Flow rate: $Q = A_2 v_2 = 4 \times 10^{-4} \times 2.60 = 1.04 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^3/\text{s}$. Closest to A.
