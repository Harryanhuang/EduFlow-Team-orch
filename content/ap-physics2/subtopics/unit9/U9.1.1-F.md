A hydraulic press has two pistons. The smaller piston has an area of $2 \text{ cm}^2$ and the larger piston has an area of $100 \text{ cm}^2$. If a force of $10 \text{ N}$ is applied to the smaller piston, what is the force exerted by the larger piston?

## Options
A) $10 \text{ N}$
B) $200 \text{ N}$
C) $500 \text{ N}$
D) $1000 \text{ N}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
By Pascal's law, the pressure applied to the smaller piston is transmitted undiminished throughout the fluid. Pressure $P = F_1 / A_1 = 10 / (2 \times 10^{-4}) = 50{,}000 \text{ Pa}$. The force on the larger piston is $F_2 = P \times A_2 = 50{,}000 \times (100 \times 10^{-4}) = 500 \text{ N}$. Alternatively, $F_2 = F_1 \times (A_2 / A_1) = 10 \times (100/2) = 500 \text{ N}$. The common mistake is to assume the force is the same on both sides, ignoring the area ratio.
