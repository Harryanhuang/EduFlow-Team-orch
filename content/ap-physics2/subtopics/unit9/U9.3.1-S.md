Water flows horizontally through a pipe. At point A (area $20 \text{ cm}^2$), the pressure is $2.0 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$ and the speed is $1.5 \text{ m/s}$. At point B (area $10 \text{ cm}^2$), what is the pressure? ($\rho = 1000 \text{ kg/m}^3$)

## Options
A) $1.97 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$
B) $2.00 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$
C) $2.03 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$
D) $2.25 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
By continuity: $v_B = v_A \times (A_A / A_B) = 1.5 \times (20/10) = 3.0 \text{ m/s}$. By Bernoulli's equation (horizontal, so $h_A = h_B$): $P_A + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_A^2 = P_B + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_B^2$. $P_B = P_A + \frac{1}{2}\rho(v_A^2 - v_B^2) = 2.0 \times 10^5 + 0.5 \times 1000 \times (2.25 - 9.0) = 2.0 \times 10^5 - 3{,}375 = 1.966 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa} \approx 1.97 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$. The pressure decreases where the fluid speeds up.
