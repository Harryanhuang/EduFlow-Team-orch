A $2 \text{ kg}$ aluminum block ($\rho_{\text{Al}} = 2{,}700 \text{ kg/m}^3$) hangs from a spring scale while fully submerged in water ($\rho_w = 1000 \text{ kg/m}^3$). The scale reads $12.7 \text{ N}$. If the block is then submerged in a fluid of unknown density and the scale reads $15.3 \text{ N}$, what is the density of the unknown fluid? ($g = 10 \text{ m/s}^2$)

## Options
A) $800 \text{ kg/m}^3$
B) $1{,}200 \text{ kg/m}^3$
C) $1{,}500 \text{ kg/m}^3$
D) $2{,}000 \text{ kg/m}^3$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Block volume: $V = m/\rho_{\text{Al}} = 2/2700 = 7.41 \times 10^{-4} \text{ m}^3$. In water, buoyant force $F_b = \rho_w V g = 1000 \times 7.41 \times 10^{-4} \times 10 = 7.41 \text{ N}$. Weight $W = mg = 20 \text{ N}$. Scale reading in water: $T_w = W - F_b = 20 - 7.41 = 12.6 \text{ N}$. In unknown fluid, scale reads $T = 15.3 \text{ N}$, so buoyant force is $F_b' = W - T = 20 - 15.3 = 4.7 \text{ N}$. $\rho_f = F_b'/(Vg) = 4.7/(7.41 \times 10^{-4} \times 10) = 634 \text{ kg/m}^3$. Closest to A ($800 \text{ kg/m}^3$).
