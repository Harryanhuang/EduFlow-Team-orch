An airplane wing is designed so that air travels $20\%$ faster over the top surface than beneath the bottom surface. If the air speed beneath the wing is $80 \text{ m/s}$, which statement best explains the origin of lift?

## Options
A) The faster air on top pushes the wing downward more strongly.
B) The faster air on top creates lower pressure above the wing than below, producing a net upward force.
C) The slower air beneath the wing has higher pressure, pushing the wing sideways.
D) Lift is caused entirely by the wing deflecting air downward, with no role for pressure differences.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By Bernoulli's equation, where air speed is higher, pressure is lower. Air on top moves at $v_{\text{top}} = 1.2 \times 80 = 96 \text{ m/s}$, and air below moves at $v_{\text{bottom}} = 80 \text{ m/s}$. The higher speed on top creates lower pressure above: $P_{\text{top}} < P_{\text{bottom}}$. This pressure difference produces a net upward force (lift). While Newton's third law (deflection of air) also contributes to lift, the question specifically asks about the Bernoulli explanation, which is the pressure difference due to speed difference.
