A small hole is made at the bottom of a water tank where the water surface is $1.8 \text{ m}$ above the hole. What is the speed of the water emerging from the hole? ($g = 10 \text{ m/s}^2$)

## Options
A) $3 \text{ m/s}$
B) $6 \text{ m/s}$
C) $18 \text{ m/s}$
D) $36 \text{ m/s}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By Torricelli's theorem (a special case of Bernoulli's equation): $v = \sqrt{2gh} = \sqrt{2 \times 10 \times 1.8} = \sqrt{36} = 6 \text{ m/s}$. The efflux speed equals the speed a freely falling object would reach after falling from the same height.
