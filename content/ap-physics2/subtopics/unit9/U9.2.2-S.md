A hydrometer floats in two different fluids. In fluid A, $40\%$ of its volume is submerged. In fluid B, $50\%$ of its volume is submerged. What is the ratio $\rho_A / \rho_B$?

## Options
A) $0.8$
B) $1.0$
C) $1.25$
D) $1.5$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The hydrometer floats in both fluids, so $F_b = mg$ in each. In fluid A: $\rho_A V_{\text{sub,A}} g = mg$, so $V_{\text{sub,A}} = m / (\rho_A V)$. In fluid B: $V_{\text{sub,B}} = m / (\rho_B V)$. Taking the ratio: $V_{\text{sub,A}} / V_{\text{sub,B}} = \rho_B / \rho_A$, so $\rho_A / \rho_B = V_{\text{sub,B}} / V_{\text{sub,A}} = 0.50 / 0.40 = 1.25$. The fluid in which the hydrometer sinks less (A) is denser.
