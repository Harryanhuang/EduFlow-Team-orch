A hydraulic system has three pistons with areas $A_1 = 10 \text{ cm}^2$, $A_2 = 30 \text{ cm}^2$, and $A_3 = 60 \text{ cm}^2$. A force of $200 \text{ N}$ is applied to piston 1. Pistons 2 and 3 are free to move and support loads. If the system is in equilibrium, what are the forces on pistons 2 and 3 respectively?

## Options
A) $F_2 = 200 \text{ N}$, $F_3 = 200 \text{ N}$
B) $F_2 = 600 \text{ N}$, $F_3 = 1200 \text{ N}$
C) $F_2 = 100 \text{ N}$, $F_3 = 50 \text{ N}$
D) $F_2 = 400 \text{ N}$, $F_3 = 800 \text{ N}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By Pascal's law, the pressure from piston 1 is $P = F_1 / A_1 = 200 / (10 \times 10^{-4}) = 200{,}000 \text{ Pa}$. This pressure is transmitted equally to pistons 2 and 3. Therefore $F_2 = P \times A_2 = 200{,}000 \times (30 \times 10^{-4}) = 600 \text{ N}$, and $F_3 = P \times A_3 = 200{,}000 \times (60 \times 10^{-4}) = 1200 \text{ N}$. The key insight is that pressure, not force, is conserved in a hydraulic system. Force scales with piston area.
