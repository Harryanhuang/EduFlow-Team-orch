A cube of side $10 \text{ cm}$ and density $800 \text{ kg/m}^3$ floats at the interface between oil ($\rho_{\text{oil}} = 700 \text{ kg/m}^3$) on top and water ($\rho_w = 1000 \text{ kg/m}^3$) below. How much of the cube's volume is submerged in the water? ($g = 10 \text{ m/s}^2$)

## Options
A) $333 \text{ cm}^3$
B) $500 \text{ cm}^3$
C) $667 \text{ cm}^3$
D) $800 \text{ cm}^3$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Let $V_w$ be the volume in water and $V_o$ the volume in oil. Total volume: $V = (0.10)^3 = 1.0 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}^3$. Weight: $mg = 800 \times 1.0 \times 10^{-3} \times 10 = 8.0 \text{ N}$. Buoyant force: $F_b = \rho_w V_w g + \rho_o V_o g = 1000 V_w (10) + 700 (1.0 \times 10^{-3} - V_w)(10) = 10{,}000 V_w + 7.0 - 7{,}000 V_w = 3{,}000 V_w + 7.0$. Setting $F_b = mg$: $3{,}000 V_w + 7.0 = 8.0$, so $V_w = 1.0/3{,}000 = 3.33 \times 10^{-4} \text{ m}^3 = 333 \text{ cm}^3$. The answer is A.
