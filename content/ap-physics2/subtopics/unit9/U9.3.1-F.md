Water flows through a horizontal pipe. At point A, the pipe has a cross-sectional area of $10 \text{ cm}^2$ and the water speed is $2 \text{ m/s}$. At point B, the pipe narrows to $5 \text{ cm}^2$. What is the water speed at point B?

## Options
A) $1 \text{ m/s}$
B) $2 \text{ m/s}$
C) $4 \text{ m/s}$
D) $8 \text{ m/s}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
By the continuity equation for incompressible fluids: $A_1 v_1 = A_2 v_2$, so $v_2 = v_1 \times (A_1 / A_2) = 2 \times (10/5) = 4 \text{ m/s}$. When the pipe narrows, the fluid speeds up.
