A $0.5 \text{ kg}$ block floats at the surface of water with $60\%$ of its volume submerged. What is the buoyant force on the block? ($g = 10 \text{ m/s}^2$)

## Options
A) $3 \text{ N}$
B) $5 \text{ N}$
C) $8 \text{ N}$
D) $12 \text{ N}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For a floating object in equilibrium, the buoyant force equals the object's weight: $F_b = mg = 0.5 \times 10 = 5 \text{ N}$. The fact that $60\%$ is submerged tells us the object's density is $60\%$ of water's density, but the buoyant force is simply equal to the weight.
