A solid block of volume $0.05 \text{ m}^3$ is fully submerged in water ($\rho = 1000 \text{ kg/m}^3$). What is the magnitude of the buoyant force on the block? ($g = 10 \text{ m/s}^2$)

## Options
A) $50 \text{ N}$
B) $500 \text{ N}$
C) $5{,}000 \text{ N}$
D) $50{,}000 \text{ N}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By Archimedes' principle, $F_b = \rho_{\text{fluid}} V_{\text{displaced}} g = 1000 \times 0.05 \times 10 = 500 \text{ N}$. The buoyant force depends on the fluid density and the volume of fluid displaced, not on the object's density or mass.
