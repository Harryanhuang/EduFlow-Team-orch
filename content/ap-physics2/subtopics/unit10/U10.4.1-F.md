A wall has an area of $3.0 \text{ m}^2$, a thickness of $0.20 \text{ m}$, and thermal conductivity $k = 0.04 \text{ W/(m·K)}$. The inside temperature is $25°\text{C}$ and the outside is $5°\text{C}$. What is the rate of heat transfer through the wall?

## Options
A) $0.012 \text{ W}$
B) $1.2 \text{ W}$
C) $12 \text{ W}$
D) $120 \text{ W}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
By Fourier's law of heat conduction: $\frac{Q}{t} = \frac{kA\Delta T}{d} = \frac{0.04 \times 3.0 \times (25 - 5)}{0.20} = \frac{0.04 \times 3.0 \times 20}{0.20} = \frac{2.4}{0.20} = 12 \text{ W}$.
