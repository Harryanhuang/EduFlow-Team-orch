A $0.10 \text{ kg}$ ice cube at $-10°\text{C}$ is dropped into $0.50 \text{ kg}$ of water at $20°\text{C}$ in an insulated container. What is the final state? ($c_{\text{ice}} = 2{,}100 \text{ J/(kg·K)}$, $L_f = 3.34 \times 10^5 \text{ J/kg}$, $c_{\text{water}} = 4{,}186 \text{ J/(kg·K)}$)

## Options
A) All ice melts; final temperature $= 2.5°\text{C}$
B) All ice melts; final temperature $= 7.3°\text{C}$
C) Some ice remains; final temperature $= 0°\text{C}$
D) All ice melts; final temperature $= 0°\text{C}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Step 1: Warm ice from $-10°\text{C}$ to $0°\text{C}$: $Q_1 = 0.10 \times 2100 \times 10 = 2100 \text{ J}$. Step 2: Melt all ice: $Q_2 = 0.10 \times 3.34 \times 10^5 = 33{,}400 \text{ J}$. Total heat needed $= 35{,}500 \text{ J}$. Step 3: Available heat from water cooling to $0°\text{C}$: $Q_{\text{water}} = 0.50 \times 4186 \times 20 = 41{,}860 \text{ J}$. Since $41{,}860 > 35{,}500$, all ice melts with surplus $41{,}860 - 35{,}500 = 6{,}360 \text{ J}$. Step 4: Final temperature rise: $\Delta T = 6360/[(0.10 + 0.50) \times 4186] = 6360/2512 = 2.5°\text{C}$. Final state: all ice melted, system at $2.5°\text{C}$.
