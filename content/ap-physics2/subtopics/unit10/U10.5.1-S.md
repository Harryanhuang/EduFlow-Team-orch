$1{,}000 \text{ J}$ of heat flows spontaneously from a reservoir at $500 \text{ K}$ to a reservoir at $250 \text{ K}$. What is the total entropy change of the universe?

## Options
A) $-2.0 \text{ J/K}$
B) $0 \text{ J/K}$
C) $+2.0 \text{ J/K}$
D) $+4.0 \text{ J/K}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Entropy change of the hot reservoir: $\Delta S_{\text{hot}} = -Q/T_{\text{hot}} = -1000/500 = -2.0 \text{ J/K}$. Entropy change of the cold reservoir: $\Delta S_{\text{cold}} = +Q/T_{\text{cold}} = +1000/250 = +4.0 \text{ J/K}$. Total: $\Delta S_{\text{universe}} = -2.0 + 4.0 = +2.0 \text{ J/K}$. The total entropy increases, consistent with the second law for an irreversible process.
