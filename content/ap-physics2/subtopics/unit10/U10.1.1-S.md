A system absorbs $500 \text{ J}$ of heat and does $200 \text{ J}$ of work on its surroundings. What is the change in internal energy of the system?

## Options
A) $-700 \text{ J}$
B) $-300 \text{ J}$
C) $+300 \text{ J}$
D) $+700 \text{ J}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
By the first law: $\Delta U = Q - W = 500 - 200 = +300 \text{ J}$. The system gains $500 \text{ J}$ from heat but loses $200 \text{ J}$ through work, so the net internal energy increases by $300 \text{ J}$.
