A gas is compressed in a piston-cylinder device. The gas is the system. Which of the following describes the energy transfer to the system?

## Options
A) Work is done on the system by the surroundings.
B) Heat is added to the system by the surroundings.
C) Both heat and work are done on the system.
D) Neither heat nor work is transferred.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
When a piston compresses a gas, the piston (surroundings) exerts a force over a distance on the gas (system). This is work done on the system: $W > 0$ (by convention where work done on the system is positive). No heat transfer is implied by compression alone — it could be adiabatic. The key distinction is that compression is a mechanical process (work), not a thermal one (heat).
