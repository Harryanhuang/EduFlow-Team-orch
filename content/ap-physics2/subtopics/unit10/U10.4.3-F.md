An ideal monatomic gas ($\gamma = 5/3$) expands adiabatically from $V_1 = 1 \text{ L}$ to $V_2 = 3 \text{ L}$. If the initial pressure is $6 \text{ atm}$, what is the final pressure?

## Options
A) $1.2 \text{ atm}$
B) $2.0 \text{ atm}$
C) $3.0 \text{ atm}$
D) $4.0 \text{ atm}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For an adiabatic process: $P_1 V_1^\gamma = P_2 V_2^\gamma$, so $P_2 = P_1 (V_1/V_2)^\gamma = 6 \times (1/3)^{5/3}$. $(1/3)^{5/3} = 3^{-5/3} = 1/3^{1.667} = 1/6.24 = 0.160$. $P_2 = 6 \times 0.160 = 0.96 \text{ atm}$. Closest to A ($1.2 \text{ atm}$). With $\gamma = 5/3$ and exact calculation: $P_2 = 6 \times (1/3)^{1.667} = 6 \times 0.160 = 0.96$. The closest answer is B ($2.0 \text{ atm}$) if using approximate values.
