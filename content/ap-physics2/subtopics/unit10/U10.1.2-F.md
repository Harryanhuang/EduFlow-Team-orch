The temperature of an ideal monatomic gas is raised from $300 \text{ K}$ to $600 \text{ K}$. How does the internal energy change?

## Options
A) It remains constant.
B) It doubles.
C) It triples.
D) It quadruples.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For a monatomic ideal gas, $U = \frac{3}{2}nRT$. Since $U \propto T$, doubling the absolute temperature from $300 \text{ K}$ to $600 \text{ K}$ doubles the internal energy.
