A heated metal plate is placed horizontally in still air. The air above the plate heats up, becomes less dense, and rises, while cooler air flows in to replace it. This is an example of which heat transfer mechanism?

## Options
A) Conduction through the air
B) Natural convection
C) Forced convection
D) Radiation

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The air is heated by the plate, becomes less dense, and rises due to buoyancy — this is natural (free) convection, driven by density differences in the fluid. No external device (fan, pump) forces the air to move, so it is not forced convection. Conduction involves molecular transfer without bulk motion, and radiation requires no medium at all.
