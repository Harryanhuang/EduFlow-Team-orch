Equal numbers of moles of a monatomic gas (A) and a diatomic gas (B) are both heated from $300 \text{ K}$ to $600 \text{ K}$ at constant volume. What is the ratio of the internal energy increase of B to that of A?

## Options
A) $1:1$
B) $5:3$
C) $3:5$
D) $7:5$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For a monatomic gas: $\Delta U_A = \frac{3}{2}nR\Delta T$. For a diatomic gas (at moderate temperatures where rotational modes are active): $\Delta U_B = \frac{5}{2}nR\Delta T$. The ratio $\Delta U_B / \Delta U_A = (5/2)/(3/2) = 5/3$. The diatomic gas has more degrees of freedom (3 translational + 2 rotational), so more energy is stored for the same temperature increase.
