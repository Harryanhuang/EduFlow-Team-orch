An ideal monatomic gas ($\gamma = 5/3$) at $T_1 = 600 \text{ K}$ expands adiabatically to twice its initial volume. What is the final temperature?

## Options
A) $300 \text{ K}$
B) $379 \text{ K}$
C) $424 \text{ K}$
D) $456 \text{ K}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For an adiabatic process: $TV^{\gamma-1} = \text{constant}$, so $T_2 = T_1 (V_1/V_2)^{\gamma-1} = 600 \times (1/2)^{2/3} = 600 \times 0.630 = 378 \text{ K} \approx 379 \text{ K}$. The gas cools from $600 \text{ K}$ to $379 \text{ K}$ during the adiabatic expansion.
