A $0.5 \text{ kg}$ block of aluminum ($c = 900 \text{ J/(kg·K)}$) at $200°\text{C}$ is placed in a $2.0 \text{ kg}$ pool of water ($c = 4{,}186 \text{ J/(kg·K)}$) at $20°\text{C}$. What is the final equilibrium temperature? (Ignore heat loss to the container.)

## Options
A) $29°\text{C}$
B) $38°\text{C}$
C) $56°\text{C}$
D) $110°\text{C}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Heat lost by aluminum = heat gained by water: $m_{\text{Al}} c_{\text{Al}} (T_{\text{Al}} - T_f) = m_w c_w (T_f - T_w)$. $0.5 \times 900 \times (200 - T_f) = 2.0 \times 4186 \times (T_f - 20)$. $450(200 - T_f) = 8372(T_f - 20)$. $90{,}000 - 450 T_f = 8372 T_f - 167{,}440$. $257{,}440 = 8822 T_f$. $T_f = 29.2°\text{C}$. Closest to A.
