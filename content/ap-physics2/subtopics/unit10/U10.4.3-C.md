An ideal monatomic gas ($\gamma = 5/3$) at $(P_0, V_0, T_0)$ expands to $2V_0$. Compare the final temperatures for (1) isothermal expansion and (2) adiabatic expansion.

## Options
A) Both give $T_f = T_0$
B) Isothermal: $T_f = T_0$; Adiabatic: $T_f = 0.63 T_0$
C) Isothermal: $T_f = T_0$; Adiabatic: $T_f = 0.79 T_0$
D) Isothermal: $T_f = 0.5 T_0$; Adiabatic: $T_f = 0.63 T_0$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For isothermal expansion, $\Delta T = 0$ by definition, so $T_f = T_0$. For adiabatic expansion: $T_f = T_0(V_0/2V_0)^{\gamma-1} = T_0(1/2)^{2/3} = T_0 \times 0.630 = 0.63 T_0$. The adiabatic final temperature is lower because the gas does work at the expense of its internal energy. On a $P$-$V$ diagram, the adiabatic curve is steeper than the isothermal curve, meaning the gas reaches a lower pressure and temperature at the same final volume.
