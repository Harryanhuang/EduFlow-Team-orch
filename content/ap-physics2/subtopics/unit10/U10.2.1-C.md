A frictionless piston of mass $5.0 \text{ kg}$ and area $10 \text{ cm}^2$ sits on top of a cylinder containing an ideal gas. The external atmospheric pressure is $1.0 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$. The gas is heated until the piston rises slowly (quasi-statically). What is the gas pressure when the piston is in equilibrium? ($g = 10 \text{ m/s}^2$)

## Options
A) $1.0 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$
B) $1.5 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$
C) $2.0 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$
D) $2.5 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The piston is in equilibrium when the upward gas force equals the sum of the downward atmospheric force and the piston's weight: $P_{\text{gas}} A = P_{\text{atm}} A + mg$. Therefore $P_{\text{gas}} = P_{\text{atm}} + mg/A = 1.0 \times 10^5 + (5.0 \times 10) / (10 \times 10^{-4}) = 1.0 \times 10^5 + 5.0 \times 10^4 = 1.5 \times 10^5 \text{ Pa}$. The gas pressure must support both the atmosphere and the piston.
