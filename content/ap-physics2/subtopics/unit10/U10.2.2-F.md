A $2 \text{ kg}$ block of copper at $100°\text{C}$ is placed in contact with a $2 \text{ kg}$ block of copper at $0°\text{C}$ in an insulated container. What is the final equilibrium temperature?

## Options
A) $25°\text{C}$
B) $50°\text{C}$
C) $75°\text{C}$
D) $100°\text{C}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Both blocks are the same material (copper) and same mass, so they have the same heat capacity. Heat lost by the hot block = heat gained by the cold block: $mc(T_{\text{hot}} - T_f) = mc(T_f - T_{\text{cold}})$. Since $m$ and $c$ cancel: $100 - T_f = T_f - 0$, giving $T_f = 50°\text{C}$.
