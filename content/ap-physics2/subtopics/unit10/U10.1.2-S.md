An ideal gas expands isothermally at $T = 400 \text{ K}$ and does $600 \text{ J}$ of work on its surroundings. How much heat does the gas absorb?

## Options
A) $0 \text{ J}$
B) $300 \text{ J}$
C) $600 \text{ J}$
D) $1200 \text{ J}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
In an isothermal process for an ideal gas, $\Delta T = 0$, so $\Delta U = 0$. By the first law: $\Delta U = Q - W = 0$, so $Q = W = 600 \text{ J}$. The gas absorbs exactly as much heat as it expends as work, keeping the internal energy (and temperature) constant.
