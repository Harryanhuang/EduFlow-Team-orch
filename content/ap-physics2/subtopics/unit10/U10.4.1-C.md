Two blackbody objects have surface temperatures $T_1 = 300 \text{ K}$ and $T_2 = 600 \text{ K}$ and the same surface area. What is the ratio of the power radiated by object 2 to that radiated by object 1?

## Options
A) $2$
B) $4$
C) $8$
D) $16$

## Answer
D

## Explanation
By the Stefan-Boltzmann law, $P = \sigma A T^4$ for a blackbody ($\epsilon = 1$). The ratio $P_2/P_1 = (T_2/T_1)^4 = (600/300)^4 = 2^4 = 16$. The $T^4$ dependence means doubling the absolute temperature increases the radiated power by a factor of 16.
