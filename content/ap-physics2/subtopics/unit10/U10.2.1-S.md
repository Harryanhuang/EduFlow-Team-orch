A gas occupies $3.0 \text{ L}$ at $27°\text{C}$ and $2.0 \text{ atm}$. It is heated at constant pressure to $127°\text{C}$. What is the new volume?

## Options
A) $3.0 \text{ L}$
B) $3.6 \text{ L}$
C) $4.0 \text{ L}$
D) $6.0 \text{ L}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
At constant pressure (isobaric): $V_1/T_1 = V_2/T_2$. Convert temperatures: $T_1 = 27 + 273 = 300 \text{ K}$, $T_2 = 127 + 273 = 400 \text{ K}$. $V_2 = V_1 \times (T_2/T_1) = 3.0 \times (400/300) = 4.0 \text{ L}$.
