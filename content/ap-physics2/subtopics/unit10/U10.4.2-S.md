An ideal gas undergoes a cyclic process on a $P$-$V$ diagram that forms a clockwise triangle with vertices at $(V_1, P_1)$, $(V_2, P_1)$, and $(V_2, P_2)$, where $V_1 = 1 \text{ L}$, $V_2 = 3 \text{ L}$, $P_1 = 1 \text{ atm}$, and $P_2 = 3 \text{ atm}$. What is the net work done by the gas per cycle? ($1 \text{ L·atm} = 101.3 \text{ J}$)

## Options
A) $101 \text{ J}$
B) $203 \text{ J}$
C) $304 \text{ J}$
D) $608 \text{ J}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The net work in a cyclic process equals the area enclosed by the path on the $P$-$V$ diagram. Area of the triangle $= \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height} = \frac{1}{2} \times (V_2 - V_1) \times (P_2 - P_1) = \frac{1}{2} \times 2 \text{ L} \times 2 \text{ atm} = 2 \text{ L·atm}$. Converting: $2 \times 101.3 = 202.6 \text{ J} \approx 203 \text{ J}$. Clockwise means the gas does net positive work.
