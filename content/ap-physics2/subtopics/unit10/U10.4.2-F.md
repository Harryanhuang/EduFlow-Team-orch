An ideal gas expands adiabatically and does $400 \text{ J}$ of work on its surroundings. What is the change in internal energy of the gas?

## Options
A) $+400 \text{ J}$
B) $0 \text{ J}$
C) $-400 \text{ J}$
D) $-800 \text{ J}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
In an adiabatic process, $Q = 0$. By the first law: $\Delta U = Q - W = 0 - 400 = -400 \text{ J}$. The gas's internal energy decreases because it expends energy as work without receiving any heat.
