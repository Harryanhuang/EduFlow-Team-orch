An ideal gas undergoes four different processes, each starting from the same state $(P_0, V_0, T_0)$. In which process does the gas do the LEAST work?

## Options
A) Isobaric expansion to $2V_0$
B) Isothermal expansion to $2V_0$
C) Isochoric heating to $2T_0$
D) Adiabatic expansion to $2V_0$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
In an isochoric (constant volume) process, $\Delta V = 0$, so $W = \int P \, dV = 0$. No work is done. In all other processes (isobaric, isothermal, adiabatic), the gas expands and does positive work. Isochoric heating increases temperature and pressure but does no mechanical work.
