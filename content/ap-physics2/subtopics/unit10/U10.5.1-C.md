A heat engine operates between a hot reservoir at $500 \text{ K}$ and a cold reservoir at $300 \text{ K}$. What is the maximum possible efficiency, and what does this imply?

## Options
A) $40\%$; any real engine can achieve this.
B) $40\%$; real engines always have efficiency below this.
C) $60\%$; any real engine can achieve this.
D) $60\%$; real engines always have efficiency below this.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Carnot efficiency: $\eta_{\text{max}} = 1 - T_C/T_H = 1 - 300/500 = 0.40 = 40\%$. This is the theoretical maximum. No real engine can achieve it because real processes involve irreversibilities (friction, non-quasi-static processes, heat losses). All real engines have $\eta < 40\%$. The Carnot efficiency is an upper bound that can only be approached with perfectly reversible processes.
