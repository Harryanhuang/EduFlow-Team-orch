# Unit 1 Outline — International Course Template (Minimal)

## 1.1 Overview
- **Subject**: Academic Writing & Research Skills (跨学科通用)
- **Curriculum alignment**: AP English Language / A-Level EGP / IELTS Academic / TOEFL Writing / IGCSE ESL / IB TOK / SAT Essay
- **Duration**: 4 weeks (12 contact hours)
- **Level**: B2–C1 CEFR

## 1.2 Learning Objectives
By the end of Unit 1, students will be able to:
1. Identify the components of an effective academic paragraph (topic sentence, evidence, analysis, link)
2. Construct a thesis-driven essay outline using the PEEL/TEEL structure
3. Paraphrase and summarize source material with proper citation (APA 7th)
4. Distinguish between descriptive, analytical, and critical writing tones

## 1.3 Lesson Breakdown

| Week | Topic | Key Skills | Assessment |
|------|-------|-----------|------------|
| 1 | Anatomy of an Academic Paragraph | PEEL structure, topic sentences, linking | Draft 1 paragraph |
| 2 | Thesis & Outline | Thesis statements, essay mapping, outlining | Submit outline |
| 3 | Source Use & Citation | Paraphrasing, quoting, APA in-text citations | Citation exercise |
| 4 | Tone & Register | Descriptive vs. analytical vs. critical; critical rewrite techniques | Rewrite 3 passages (incl. 1 critical upgrade) |

## 1.4 Core Materials
- Sample annotated essay (model text)
- PEEL paragraph worksheet
- APA 7th quick-reference sheet
- Source text bank (3 short academic excerpts)
- Academic integrity mini-lesson: plagiarism types (direct, mosaic, paraphrase without attribution), Turnitin-style similarity report walkthrough

## 1.5 QA Notes
- Paragraph rubric (5 criteria, 0–2 each): coherence, evidence quality, citation accuracy, thesis alignment, tone appropriateness. Max 10; pass = 7/10 (70%). Remediation worksheet provided below threshold.
- Academic integrity check: students must correctly attribute ≥3 sources in Week 3 citation exercise; zero-tolerance for uncited verbatim lifts (>7 consecutive words).
- Cross-curriculum mapping: IGCSE ESL Writing → PEEL; IB TOK Essay → thesis + citation + academic integrity; SAT Essay → analytical/critical tone; AP Lang → full rubric + plagiarism awareness.
