A student measures the period T for various masses m on a spring and plots T² vs m. The graph is a straight line with slope 4.0 s²/kg. What is the spring constant k?

## Options
A) π² N/m ≈ 9.87 N/m
B) 4π² N/m ≈ 39.5 N/m
C) 4.0 N/m
D) 16π² N/m ≈ 158 N/m

## Answer
A

## Explanation
From T = 2π√(m/k), squaring both sides: T² = 4π²m/k. This is a linear equation T² = (4π²/k) × m, where the slope of T² vs m is 4π²/k. Given slope = 4.0 s²/kg: 4π²/k = 4.0. Solving: k = 4π²/4.0 = π² ≈ 9.87 N/m. Option B (4π²) forgets to divide by the slope — it equals 4π²/1, not 4π²/4. Option C (4.0) confuses the slope with k directly. Option D (16π²) multiplies instead of divides. The key technique: always linearize SHM equations before extracting parameters from graph slopes.
