A mass m hangs from a vertical spring (constant k) and oscillates up and down. Compared to the same mass-spring system on a horizontal frictionless surface, the vertical system has:

## Options
A) A higher frequency because gravity adds to the restoring force.
B) The same frequency — gravity shifts the equilibrium position but does not change the oscillation frequency.
C) A lower frequency because gravity opposes the spring force.
D) No oscillation — gravity prevents SHM in the vertical direction.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
In the vertical system, gravity shifts the equilibrium position downward by Δy = mg/k (the static stretch). However, once we measure displacement from THIS new equilibrium, the net restoring force is still F = -kx' (where x' is displacement from the new equilibrium). Gravity is a constant force that only changes where equilibrium is — it doesn't change the spring's restoring force constant k. Since ω = √(k/m) and neither k nor m changed, the frequency is the same. Option A is wrong — gravity doesn't add to the oscillating restoring force; it's constant. Option C is wrong for similar reasons. Option D is wrong — vertical SHM works fine; it just has a shifted equilibrium.
