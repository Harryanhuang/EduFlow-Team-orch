A mass-spring system oscillates with period T. The mass is doubled AND the spring constant is doubled. What is the new period?

## Options
A) T/2
B) T/√2
C) T
D) T√2

## Answer
C

## Explanation
T_new = 2π√(m_new/k_new) = 2π√(2m/(2k)) = 2π√(m/k) × √(2/2) = 2π√(m/k) × 1 = T. When both mass and spring constant change by the same factor, the effects cancel. Doubling m alone would increase T by √2, and doubling k alone would decrease T by √2 — together they cancel. Option A (T/2) uses the ratio without square roots. Option B (T/√2) only accounts for k change. Option D (T√2) only accounts for m change.
