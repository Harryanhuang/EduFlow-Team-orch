Which of the following is a necessary condition for simple harmonic motion?

## Options
A) The object must move in a circle.
B) The restoring force must be proportional to the displacement and directed toward equilibrium.
C) The acceleration must be constant.
D) The velocity must be constant.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Simple harmonic motion requires a restoring force that is proportional to the displacement from equilibrium and directed toward the equilibrium position: F = -kx. This produces an acceleration a = -(k/m)x, which means the acceleration is also proportional to displacement and directed toward equilibrium. Option A is wrong — circular motion is not SHM (though uniform circular motion's projection is SHM). Option C is wrong — acceleration in SHM varies with position (maximum at extremes, zero at equilibrium). Option D is wrong — velocity changes continuously in SHM.
