Which of the following best describes the shape of a velocity vs. position (v-x) graph for an object in simple harmonic motion?

## Options
A) A straight line through the origin.
B) A sine or cosine curve.
C) An ellipse (or circle if axes are scaled appropriately).
D) A parabola opening downward.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
From energy conservation: v² = ω²(A² - x²). Rearranging: v²/(ωA)² + x²/A² = 1. This is the equation of an ellipse in the v-x plane, with semi-axes ωA (along v) and A (along x). If we scale the v-axis by 1/ω, it becomes a circle. The v-x graph is NOT the same as the x-t or v-t graphs (which are sinusoidal). Option A is wrong — v-x is not linear (that would mean v ∝ x, which is not SHM). Option B describes x-t or v-t graphs, not v-x. Option D (parabola) would be v² ∝ x, which is not the correct relationship. The elliptical v-x graph reflects that speed is maximum at equilibrium and zero at the extremes.
