A mass on a spring (k = 100 N/m) oscillates with amplitude A = 0.10 m. What is the kinetic energy when the mass is at x = 0.06 m?

## Options
A) 0.18 J
B) 0.32 J
C) 0.50 J
D) 0.64 J

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Total energy: E = ½kA² = ½(100)(0.10)² = ½(100)(0.01) = 0.50 J.
Potential energy at x = 0.06 m: U = ½kx² = ½(100)(0.06)² = ½(100)(0.0036) = 0.18 J.
Kinetic energy: K = E - U = 0.50 - 0.18 = 0.32 J.

Option A (0.18 J) is the potential energy at that position, not the kinetic energy. Option C (0.50 J) is the total energy. Option D (0.64 J) exceeds total energy — impossible. The energy splits between kinetic and potential, with their sum always equal to the total.
