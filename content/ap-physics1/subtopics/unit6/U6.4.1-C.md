In SHM with amplitude A, at what displacement x is the kinetic energy equal to the potential energy?

## Options
A) x = A/2
B) x = A/√2
C) x = A/4
D) x = A

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Set K = U: ½k(A² - x²) = ½kx². Simplifying: A² - x² = x² → 2x² = A² → x² = A²/2 → x = A/√2 ≈ 0.707A. At this position, each form of energy equals half the total: K = U = E/2 = ¼kA². Option A (A/2) is a common trap — at x = A/2: U = ½k(A/2)² = ⅛kA² = E/4, K = ¾E, not equal. Option C (A/4) gives even less potential energy. Option D (A) gives U = E, K = 0. The √2 factor comes from the quadratic nature of the energy equations.
