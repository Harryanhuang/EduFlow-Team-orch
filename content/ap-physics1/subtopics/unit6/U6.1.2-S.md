A mass on a spring oscillates in SHM. The position-time graph shows the mass moves from +8 cm to -8 cm and back to +8 cm in 0.4 s. What are the amplitude and period?

## Options
A) Amplitude = 8 cm, Period = 0.2 s
B) Amplitude = 8 cm, Period = 0.4 s
C) Amplitude = 16 cm, Period = 0.4 s
D) Amplitude = 16 cm, Period = 0.8 s

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The amplitude is the maximum displacement from equilibrium: A = |+8 cm| = 8 cm (NOT 16 cm — that would be the peak-to-peak distance). The period is the time for one COMPLETE oscillation — from +8 cm to -8 cm and BACK to +8 cm constitutes one full cycle, so T = 0.4 s. Option A (T = 0.2 s) confuses half a cycle with a full cycle (+8 to -8 is only half). Option C confuses peak-to-peak with amplitude. Option D doubles the period and uses peak-to-peak. The motion +8 → -8 → +8 is exactly one full period.
