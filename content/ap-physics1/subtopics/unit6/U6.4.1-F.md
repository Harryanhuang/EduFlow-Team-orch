In simple harmonic motion, at which position is the kinetic energy of the oscillating object maximum?

## Options
A) At maximum displacement (amplitude).
B) At the equilibrium position.
C) At the point halfway between equilibrium and maximum displacement.
D) Kinetic energy is constant throughout the motion.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
In SHM, total energy E = ½kA² is constant. Kinetic energy K = E - U = ½kA² - ½kx². At equilibrium (x = 0): K = ½kA² - 0 = ½kA² = maximum. At maximum displacement (x = ±A): K = ½kA² - ½kA² = 0 (minimum). The object moves fastest at equilibrium, so kinetic energy is maximum there. Potential energy U = ½kx² is maximum at the extremes (x = ±A) and zero at equilibrium. Option A is wrong — that's where KE is minimum (zero). Option C is an intermediate value. Option D is wrong — KE varies continuously.
