A pendulum clock keeps perfect time on Earth (g = 9.8 m/s²). It is taken to the Moon, where g_moon ≈ g/6. Compared to its Earth period, the pendulum's period on the Moon will be:

## Options
A) √6 times shorter
B) 6 times shorter
C) √6 times longer
D) 6 times longer

## Answer
C

## Explanation
T = 2π√(L/g). On the Moon: T_moon = 2π√(L/g_moon) = 2π√(L/(g/6)) = 2π√(6L/g) = √6 × 2π√(L/g) = √6 × T_earth. Since g_moon = g/6 (g decreases by factor of 6), the period increases by factor √6 ≈ 2.45. The pendulum swings slower on the Moon because the restoring force (gravity) is weaker. A pendulum clock on the Moon would run slow — each "tick" takes √6 times longer. Option A is the inverse. Option B forgets the square root. Option D uses 6 instead of √6. The key: T ∝ 1/√g, so when g decreases by 6, T increases by √6.
