A pendulum is pulled to a large angle (60°) and released. Compared to the period predicted by the small-angle formula T = 2π√(L/g), the actual period will be:

## Options
A) Exactly the same — the small-angle formula is always exact.
B) Slightly longer — the restoring force is weaker than the linear approximation predicts at large angles.
C) Slightly shorter — the pendulum swings faster at large amplitudes.
D) Exactly half — the amplitude doubles the frequency.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
At 60°, the small-angle approximation sin θ ≈ θ breaks down significantly. At θ = 60° = π/3 rad: sin(60°) = 0.866, while θ = 1.047 rad. Since sin θ < θ for θ > 0, the actual restoring force (mg sin θ) is WEAKER than the linear approximation (mgθ). A weaker restoring force means slower acceleration back to equilibrium, which means the pendulum takes slightly longer to complete each swing. The exact period includes correction terms: T = 2π√(L/g)[1 + (1/16)θ₀² + ...], where θ₀ is the amplitude in radians. At 60° (θ₀ ≈ 1.047 rad), the correction is about 7%. Option A is wrong — the formula is only an approximation. Option C is wrong — weaker force means slower, not faster. Option D is wrong — period does not halve with amplitude.
