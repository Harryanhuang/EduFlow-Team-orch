An object in SHM has its position described by x(t) = (0.05 m)cos(4πt + π/3). What is the object's position at t = 0?

## Options
A) 0.025 m
B) 0.05 m
C) -0.025 m
D) 0.05√3/2 m

## Answer
A

## Explanation
At t = 0: x(0) = 0.05 × cos(π/3) = 0.05 × 0.5 = 0.025 m. The amplitude is A = 0.05 m, the angular frequency is ω = 4π rad/s (so f = 2 Hz, T = 0.5 s), and the phase constant is φ = π/3. Option B (0.05 m) would be the answer if φ = 0 (starting at maximum displacement). Option C (-0.025) uses cos(2π/3) instead of cos(π/3). Option D (0.05√3/2) would come from cos(π/6) — wrong angle. The initial position depends on both the amplitude and the phase constant.
