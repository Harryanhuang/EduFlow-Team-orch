A simple pendulum of length L has a bob of mass m. For small oscillations, what is the effective "spring constant" k_eff that makes this system analogous to a mass-spring system (F = -k_eff × x)?

## Options
A) mg
B) mg/L
C) mL/g
D) g/L

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For small angles, the restoring force is F ≈ -mg sin θ ≈ -mgθ. Since the arc displacement is s = Lθ, we have θ = s/L. Substituting: F ≈ -mg(s/L) = -(mg/L)s. Comparing with F = -k_eff × s, we get k_eff = mg/L. Option A (mg) is the weight, not the effective spring constant. Option C (mL/g) has incorrect units. Option D (g/L) is missing the mass — it equals k_eff/m = ω², not k_eff itself. This analogy shows why the pendulum period T = 2π√(m/k_eff) = 2π√(m/(mg/L)) = 2π√(L/g).
