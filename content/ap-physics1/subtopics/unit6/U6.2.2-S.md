Pendulum A has length L and period T. Pendulum B has length 4L. What is the ratio of their periods T_B/T_A?

## Options
A) 1/2
B) 1
C) 2
D) 4

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Since T = 2π√(L/g), we have T ∝ √L. For Pendulum B with length 4L: T_B = 2π√(4L/g) = 2 × 2π√(L/g) = 2T_A. Therefore T_B/T_A = 2. Quadrupling the length doubles the period (because of the square root relationship). Option A (1/2) would be for L/4. Option B (1) assumes length doesn't matter. Option D (4) forgets the square root and just uses the length ratio directly.
