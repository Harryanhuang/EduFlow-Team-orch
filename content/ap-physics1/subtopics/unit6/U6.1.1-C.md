A particle experiences a restoring force given by F = -kx³, where k is a positive constant and x is the displacement from equilibrium. Which statement is correct?

## Options
A) The motion is simple harmonic because there is a restoring force.
B) The motion is not simple harmonic because the restoring force is not proportional to x.
C) The motion is simple harmonic but with a non-constant period.
D) The motion is simple harmonic because the force always points toward equilibrium.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For simple harmonic motion, the restoring force must be F = -kx (linear in displacement). Here F = -kx³ is a cubic (nonlinear) restoring force. While the force does point toward equilibrium (negative sign) and increases with displacement, it is NOT proportional to x — it's proportional to x³. This means the motion is periodic but NOT simple harmonic. The period will depend on amplitude (unlike SHM where period is amplitude-independent). Option A is wrong — having a restoring force is necessary but not sufficient for SHM. Option C is wrong — non-constant period contradicts SHM (SHM period is independent of amplitude). Option D is wrong — pointing toward equilibrium is necessary but not sufficient; the force must be linear in x.
