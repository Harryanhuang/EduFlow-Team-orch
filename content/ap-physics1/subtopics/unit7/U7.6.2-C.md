A hollow sphere (I = ⅔mR²) and a solid sphere (I = ⅖mR²) of the same mass and radius roll without slipping down the same incline from the same height. What is the ratio of their speeds at the bottom v_hollow/v_solid?

## Options
A) √(21/25) ≈ 0.92
B) √(25/21) ≈ 1.09
C) 5/7 ≈ 0.71
D) 1

## Answer
A

## Explanation
For rolling without slipping: v = √(2gh/(1 + c)), where c = I/(mR²).
- Hollow sphere: c_h = ⅔ → v_h = √(2gh/(1 + ⅔)) = √(2gh/(5/3)) = √(6gh/5)
- Solid sphere: c_s = ⅖ → v_s = √(2gh/(1 + ⅖)) = √(2gh/(7/5)) = √(10gh/7)

Ratio: v_h/v_s = √((6/5)/(10/7)) = √((6×7)/(5×10)) = √(42/50) = √(21/25).

The hollow sphere is slower because it has a larger fraction of its mass far from the center (larger c = ⅔ vs ⅖), so more energy goes into rotation and less into translation. The mass and radius cancel out — only the shape matters. Option B has the ratio inverted. Option C (5/7) would be the ratio of c_h/c_s, not the velocity ratio. Option D is wrong — different shapes roll at different speeds.
