A disk of radius R is rotating and speeding up with angular acceleration α. At a certain instant, its angular velocity is ω. What is the magnitude of the total acceleration of a point on the rim?

## Options
A) Rα
B) Rω²
C) R√(α² + ω⁴)
D) R(α + ω²)

## Answer
C

## Explanation
A point on the rim has two acceleration components: tangential acceleration a_t = Rα (changes the speed) and centripetal acceleration a_c = Rω² (changes the direction). These are perpendicular to each other (tangential is along the rim's tangent; centripetal points toward the center). The total acceleration is the vector sum: |a_total| = √(a_t² + a_c²) = √((Rα)² + (Rω²)²) = √(R²α² + R²ω⁴) = R√(α² + ω⁴). Option A (Rα) is only the tangential component. Option B (Rω²) is only the centripetal component. Option D adds them directly instead of using the Pythagorean theorem.
