A wheel accelerates uniformly from 2 rad/s to 10 rad/s in 4 seconds. What is its angular displacement?

## Options
A) 8 rad
B) 16 rad
C) 24 rad
D) 48 rad

## Answer
C

## Explanation
First find α: α = (ω - ω₀)/t = (10 - 2)/4 = 2 rad/s². Then use Δθ = ω₀t + ½αt² = (2)(4) + ½(2)(4²) = 8 + ½(2)(16) = 8 + 16 = 24 rad. Alternatively, use the average angular velocity: Δθ = ((ω₀ + ω)/2) × t = ((2 + 10)/2) × 4 = 6 × 4 = 24 rad. Option A (8) is just ω₀t. Option B (16) is just ½αt². Option D (48) uses Δθ = ω₀t + αt² (missing the ½).
