A disk (I = 2 kg·m²) spins at 10 rad/s. A second disk (I = 3 kg·m²) at rest is dropped on top. They stick together and rotate as one. What is their final angular velocity?

## Options
A) 2 rad/s
B) 4 rad/s
C) 6 rad/s
D) 10 rad/s

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Angular momentum is conserved (no external torque during the collision): I₁ω₁ = (I₁ + I₂)ω_f. (2)(10) = (2 + 3)ω_f. 20 = 5ω_f. ω_f = 4 rad/s. This is a completely inelastic rotational collision — the two disks stick together. Kinetic energy is NOT conserved: KE_i = ½(2)(10²) = 100 J; KE_f = ½(5)(4²) = 40 J. 60 J is lost to friction/heat during the sticking process. Option A (2) uses I₂ω₁/I₁ or similar. Option C (6) uses (I₁ω₁ - I₂ω₁)/(I₁). Option D (10) means no change — ignores the second disk.
