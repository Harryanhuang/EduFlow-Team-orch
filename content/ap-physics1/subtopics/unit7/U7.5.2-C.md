A satellite orbits Earth in an elliptical path. At perigee (closest approach), its distance from Earth's center is r₁ and speed is v₁. At apogee (farthest point), its distance is r₂ = 3r₁. What is its speed at apogee?

## Options
A) v₁/9
B) v₁/3
C) 3v₁
D) 9v₁

## Answer
B

## Explanation
At perigee and apogee, the velocity is perpendicular to the radius, so angular momentum L = mvr. Since gravity provides no torque about Earth's center (force is radial), angular momentum is conserved: mv₁r₁ = mv₂r₂. v₂ = v₁r₁/r₂ = v₁r₁/(3r₁) = v₁/3. The satellite moves 3× slower at apogee because it's 3× farther away. This is a consequence of Kepler's second law (equal areas in equal times). Option A (v₁/9) uses r² dependence. Option C (3v₁) has the inverse relationship backwards. Option D (9v₁) uses r² and wrong direction.
