Two objects have the same mass M and radius R: a solid disk and a thin hoop. Which has greater moment of inertia about its central axis?

## Options
A) The solid disk (I = ½MR²)
B) The thin hoop (I = MR²)
C) They have the same moment of inertia.
D) Cannot be determined without knowing the actual mass and radius values.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For a solid disk: I = ½MR². For a thin hoop (all mass at distance R): I = MR². The hoop has twice the moment of inertia because all its mass is concentrated at the maximum distance R from the axis. The disk has mass distributed from r = 0 to r = R, with much of the mass closer to the axis, giving a smaller I. Moment of inertia depends on how mass is distributed relative to the axis — mass farther from the axis contributes more (r² dependence). Option A is wrong — ½MR² < MR². Option C is wrong — different mass distributions give different I. Option D is wrong — the comparison is determined by the shape.
