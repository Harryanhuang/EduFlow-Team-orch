A ball is thrown horizontally on a rough surface with initial speed v₀ but zero initial angular velocity. Initially, what is the velocity of the contact point, and in which direction does friction act?

## Options
A) Contact point velocity = 0; friction acts backward.
B) Contact point velocity = v₀ forward; friction acts backward.
C) Contact point velocity = v₀ forward; friction acts forward.
D) Contact point velocity = 0; no friction.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The ball is translating at v₀ (forward) but not rotating (ω = 0). The contact point velocity = v_cm - Rω = v₀ - 0 = v₀ forward (since the contact point moves with the center of mass when there's no rotation). Since the contact point slides forward relative to the ground, friction opposes this sliding and acts backward. This backward friction both decelerates the translation and provides torque to start rotation, until v_cm = Rω (rolling without slipping is achieved). Option A is wrong — the contact point is NOT stationary initially. Option C is wrong — friction opposes the sliding direction. Option D is wrong — there IS relative motion, so friction exists.
