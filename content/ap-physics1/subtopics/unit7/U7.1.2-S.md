A wheel rotates counterclockwise (positive ω) and is slowing down. What is the direction of the angular acceleration?

## Options
A) Counterclockwise (positive α)
B) Clockwise (negative α)
C) Zero — the wheel is decelerating, not accelerating.
D) It alternates between positive and negative.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
When an object slows down, its acceleration opposes its velocity. The wheel has positive ω (counterclockwise) and is slowing down, so α must be negative (clockwise). This is directly analogous to linear motion: a car moving forward (positive v) and braking has negative acceleration. Option A is wrong — positive α would speed up counterclockwise rotation. Option C is wrong — deceleration IS acceleration (just in the opposite direction of motion). Option D is wrong — α is constant (or at least has a definite sign) during deceleration.
