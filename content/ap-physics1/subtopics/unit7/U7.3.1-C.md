A uniform rod is pivoted at its center. Four forces of equal magnitude F are applied as follows: (1) perpendicular at the right end, (2) along the rod toward the pivot at the right end, (3) perpendicular at half the distance from the pivot, (4) parallel to the rod at the left end. Which forces produce zero torque?

## Options
A) Force 1 only
B) Forces 2 and 4 only
C) Forces 1 and 3 only
D) All four forces produce nonzero torque

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Torque = rF sin θ where θ is the angle between r and F.
- Force 1: perpendicular at end → θ = 90°, τ = (L/2)F sin 90° = LF/2 ≠ 0. ✓
- Force 2: along the rod toward pivot → θ = 180°, τ = (L/2)F sin 180° = 0. ✓ (force points along r)
- Force 3: perpendicular at L/4 → θ = 90°, τ = (L/4)F sin 90° = LF/4 ≠ 0. ✓
- Force 4: parallel to rod at left end → θ = 0° or 180° → τ = (L/2)F sin 0° = 0. ✓ (force is radial)

Forces 2 and 4 both act along the rod's direction (parallel to r or anti-parallel), so sin θ = 0 and they produce zero torque. A force along the rod's length either pushes toward or pulls away from the pivot — neither causes rotation. Option A only catches force 2. Option C catches the forces that DO produce torque (wrong way). Option D is wrong — two forces clearly have zero torque.
