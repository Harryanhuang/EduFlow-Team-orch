A solid sphere (I = ⅖MR²), a solid disk (I = ½MR²), and a thin hoop (I = MR²), all with the same mass M and radius R, roll without slipping down the same incline. Which reaches the bottom first?

## Options
A) The hoop (greatest moment of inertia)
B) The disk (intermediate moment of inertia)
C) The sphere (smallest moment of inertia relative to MR²)
D) They all reach at the same time.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
For rolling without slipping down an incline, the acceleration is a = g sin θ / (1 + I/(MR²)). The factor I/(MR²) represents how much of the energy goes into rotation vs. translation:
- Sphere: I/(MR²) = ⅖ = 0.4 → a = g sin θ / 1.4 ≈ 0.71 g sin θ
- Disk: I/(MR²) = ½ = 0.5 → a = g sin θ / 1.5 ≈ 0.67 g sin θ
- Hoop: I/(MR²) = 1.0 → a = g sin θ / 2.0 = 0.50 g sin θ

The sphere has the smallest I/(MR²), meaning least energy goes to rotation and most to translation → fastest acceleration → reaches bottom first. Note: mass M and radius R cancel — only the shape matters. Option A is backwards (greatest I = slowest). Option B is second, not first. Option D is wrong — different shapes accelerate differently.
