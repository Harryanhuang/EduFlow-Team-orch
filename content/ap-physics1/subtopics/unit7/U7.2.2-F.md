A bicycle wheel of radius R rolls without slipping at center-of-mass speed v. What is the angular velocity of the wheel?

## Options
A) v/R
B) vR
C) v²/R
D) R/v

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Rolling without slipping requires v_cm = Rω, so ω = v_cm/R = v/R. The bottom of the wheel (contact point) is instantaneously at rest: its translational velocity v (forward) and rotational velocity Rω (backward at the bottom) cancel. Option B (vR) is v × R — wrong units. Option C (v²/R) is centripetal acceleration, not angular velocity. Option D (R/v) is the inverse.
