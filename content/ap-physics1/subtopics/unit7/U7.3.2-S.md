A disk is pivoted at its center. Three tangential forces act on it: F₁ = 10 N at radius R (counterclockwise), F₂ = 6 N at radius R (clockwise), and F₃ = 8 N at radius R/2 (clockwise). What is the net torque and resulting behavior?

## Options
A) 0 — the disk is in rotational equilibrium (no angular acceleration).
B) +4R N·m counterclockwise — disk accelerates CCW.
C) -4R N·m clockwise — disk accelerates CW.
D) +24R N·m counterclockwise — disk accelerates CCW.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Taking counterclockwise as positive:
τ₁ = +10R, τ₂ = -6R, τ₃ = -8(R/2) = -4R.
Net τ = 10R - 6R - 4R = 0. The counterclockwise torque from F₁ is exactly balanced by the combined clockwise torques from F₂ and F₃. The disk either remains at rest or continues rotating at constant angular velocity (no angular acceleration). Option B only counts F₁ minus F₂, ignoring F₃. Option C is the negative of the same error. Option D adds all magnitudes: (10+6+8)R = 24R, ignoring directions.
