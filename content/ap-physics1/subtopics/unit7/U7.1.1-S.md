A wheel of radius 0.3 m rotates at 10 rad/s. What is the linear speed of a point on the rim?

## Options
A) 0.03 m/s
B) 0.3 m/s
C) 3.0 m/s
D) 33 m/s

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The linear speed of a point at distance r from the axis: v = rω = (0.3)(10) = 3.0 m/s. Option A (0.03) divides instead of multiplies. Option B (0.3) uses only r or ignores ω. Option D (33) uses ω/r instead of rω. The relationship v = rω shows that for a given angular velocity, points farther from the axis move faster.
