A child stands at the edge of a freely rotating merry-go-round. The system has angular momentum L. If the child walks to the center, what happens to the total angular momentum?

## Options
A) It increases because the child moves faster.
B) It decreases because the moment of inertia decreases.
C) It stays the same — angular momentum is conserved (no external torque).
D) It becomes zero because the child is at the center.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The merry-go-round + child system has no external torque acting on it (the axle is frictionless). Therefore, total angular momentum is conserved regardless of how the child moves. When the child walks to the center, the moment of inertia decreases, so the angular velocity increases (L = Iω stays constant). But L itself doesn't change. Option A is wrong — L doesn't increase without external torque. Option B confuses L conservation with I change. Option D is wrong — L ≠ 0 just because I changed.
