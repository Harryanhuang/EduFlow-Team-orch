A wheel (I = 5 kg·m²) is driven by a motor providing a constant torque of 50 N·m. A friction torque of 10 N·m opposes the motion. Starting from rest, what is the angular velocity after 8 seconds?

## Options
A) 40 rad/s
B) 64 rad/s
C) 80 rad/s
D) 100 rad/s

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Net torque: τ_net = 50 - 10 = 40 N·m (motor minus friction). Angular acceleration: α = τ_net/I = 40/5 = 8 rad/s². Starting from rest: ω = ω₀ + αt = 0 + (8)(8) = 64 rad/s. Option A (40) uses only the friction torque to compute α. Option C (80) uses only motor torque: α = 50/5 = 10, ω = 10 × 8 = 80 — forgot to subtract friction. Option D (100) uses the wrong time value.
