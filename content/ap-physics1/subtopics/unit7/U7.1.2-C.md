A rotating object has angular position θ(t) = 2t³ - 6t² + 4 (in radians, t in seconds). What is the angular acceleration at t = 2 s?

## Options
A) 0 rad/s²
B) 6 rad/s²
C) 12 rad/s²
D) 24 rad/s²

## Answer
C

## Explanation
ω(t) = dθ/dt = 6t² - 12t. α(t) = dω/dt = 12t - 12. At t = 2 s: α(2) = 12(2) - 12 = 24 - 12 = 12 rad/s². Option A (0) is ω(2) = 6(4) - 12(2) = 24 - 24 = 0 rad/s — the angular velocity is zero at t=2, but angular acceleration is not. Option B (6) might use just the coefficient of t³. Option D (24) is 12t evaluated at t=2 without subtracting 12.
