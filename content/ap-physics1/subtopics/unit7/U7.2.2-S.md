A wheel rolls without slipping to the right. At a given instant, what is the velocity of the top of the wheel relative to the ground?

## Options
A) 0 (the top is the furthest from the ground)
B) v_cm to the right (same as the center)
C) 2v_cm to the right
D) v_cm to the left

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Each point on the wheel has two velocity components: translational (v_cm to the right, same for all points) and rotational (tangent to the circle, magnitude Rω = v_cm). At the top: translational v_cm (right) + rotational v_cm (right, since rotation carries the top forward) = 2v_cm to the right. At the bottom (contact point): v_cm (right) + Rω (left) = 0 — this is the rolling condition. At the center: just v_cm (rotation contributes zero at the center). Option A confuses "furthest from ground" with zero velocity. Option B is the center's velocity. Option D would be the velocity relative to the center at the front or back.
