A mass m hangs from a string wrapped around a solid disk pulley of mass M and radius R. The string doesn't slip. What is the acceleration of the hanging mass? (Disk: I = ½MR²)

## Options
A) g
B) mg/(m + M)
C) mg/(m + M/2)
D) mg/(m + 2M)

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Two equations:
1. Hanging mass: mg - T = ma (Newton's 2nd law)
2. Pulley rotation: TR = Iα = I(a/R) = (½MR²)(a/R) = ½MRa → T = ½Ma

Substitute T into equation 1: mg - ½Ma = ma → mg = ma + ½Ma = a(m + M/2).
Therefore: a = mg/(m + M/2).

The effective inertia includes both the hanging mass m and half the pulley mass M/2 (because for a disk, I = ½MR², so the rotational inertia contributes M/2 to the effective mass). Option A (g) assumes the mass falls freely (no pulley inertia). Option B (m + M) would be the answer for a massless pulley with M on the table, or if I = MR². Option D (m + 2M) uses I = 2MR².
