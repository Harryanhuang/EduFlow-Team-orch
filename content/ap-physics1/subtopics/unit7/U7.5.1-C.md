A wheel (I = 2 kg·m²) is initially at rest. A constant torque of 6 N·m acts on it for 5 seconds. What is its final angular momentum and angular velocity?

## Options
A) L = 30 kg·m²/s, ω = 15 rad/s
B) L = 12 kg·m²/s, ω = 6 rad/s
C) L = 30 kg·m²/s, ω = 7.5 rad/s
D) L = 11 kg·m²/s, ω = 5.5 rad/s

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Angular impulse = torque × time: ΔL = τΔt = (6)(5) = 30 kg·m²/s. Since starting from rest, L_f = 30 kg·m²/s. Angular velocity: ω = L/I = 30/2 = 15 rad/s. Option B (L=12) uses I × τ/something or τ × t/2.5. Option C (ω=7.5) uses L/(2I) = 30/4 = 7.5. Option D (L=11, ω=5.5) adds I and τ. The angular impulse-momentum theorem: τΔt = ΔL = Iω_f - Iω_i = Iω_f (since ω_i = 0).
