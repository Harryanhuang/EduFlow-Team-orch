Two forces act on a pivoted rod: 5 N at 2 m to the left of the pivot (pushing down) and 10 N at 1 m to the right of the pivot (pushing down). What is the net torque?

## Options
A) 0 N·m (balanced)
B) 10 N·m counterclockwise
C) 10 N·m clockwise
D) 20 N·m clockwise

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Taking counterclockwise as positive:
- Left force (5 N at 2 m left, pushing down): creates counterclockwise torque → τ₁ = +5 × 2 = +10 N·m
- Right force (10 N at 1 m right, pushing down): creates clockwise torque → τ₂ = -10 × 1 = -10 N·m

Net torque = +10 + (-10) = 0 N·m. The system is in rotational equilibrium. Option B (10 CCW) only counts one force. Option C (10 CW) same error with wrong sign. Option D (20 CW) adds magnitudes without considering direction.
