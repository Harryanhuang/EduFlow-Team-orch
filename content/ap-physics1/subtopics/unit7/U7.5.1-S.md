A figure skater spins with arms extended (I = 3 kg·m², ω = 2 rad/s). She pulls her arms in, reducing I to 1.5 kg·m². What is her new angular velocity? (Assume no external torques.)

## Options
A) 1 rad/s
B) 2 rad/s
C) 3 rad/s
D) 4 rad/s

## Answer
D

## Explanation
No external torque → angular momentum is conserved: L₁ = L₂. I₁ω₁ = I₂ω₂. (3)(2) = (1.5)(ω₂). ω₂ = 6/1.5 = 4 rad/s. Halving the moment of inertia doubles the angular velocity. This is why skaters spin faster when they pull their arms in. Option A (1) halves the speed — wrong direction. Option B (2) means no change — ignores conservation. Option C (3) uses I₁/I₂ = 3/1.5 = 2 but adds it to ω instead of multiplying.
