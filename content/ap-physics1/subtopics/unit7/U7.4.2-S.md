A uniform rod of mass M and length L has I_cm = (1/12)ML² about its center. What is its moment of inertia about an axis through one end (perpendicular to the rod)?

## Options
A) (1/12)ML²
B) (1/6)ML²
C) (1/4)ML²
D) (1/3)ML²

## Answer
D

## Explanation
Using the parallel axis theorem: I_end = I_cm + Md², where d = L/2 (distance from center to end). I_end = (1/12)ML² + M(L/2)² = (1/12)ML² + M(L²/4) = (1/12)ML² + (3/12)ML² = (4/12)ML² = (1/3)ML². Option A is I_cm (forgot the parallel axis correction). Option B (1/6) = 2/12 — incorrect addition. Option C (1/4) = 3/12 — also incorrect. The parallel axis theorem always increases I when moving away from the center of mass.
