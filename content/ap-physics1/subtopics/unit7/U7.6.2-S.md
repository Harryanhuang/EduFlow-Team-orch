A 1 kg mass hangs from a string wrapped around a pulley (I = 1 kg·m², R = 1 m) that can rotate freely about its fixed horizontal axis. The mass falls 10 m from rest. What is its speed at the bottom? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 5 m/s
B) 10 m/s
C) 10√2 m/s
D) 20 m/s

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Energy conservation: mgh = ½mv² + ½Iω². Since the string unwinds without slipping, v = Rω, so ω = v/R = v/1 = v.
mgh = ½mv² + ½Iv² = ½(1)v² + ½(1)v² = v².
(1)(10)(10) = v² → v² = 100 → v = 10 m/s.

The potential energy splits equally between the mass's translational KE (50 J) and the pulley's rotational KE (50 J). Option A (5) would be if energy were divided differently. Option C (10√2 ≈ 14.1) is the free-fall speed √(2gh) — ignores the pulley's rotational inertia. Option D (20) doubles the answer. The pulley's rotational inertia acts like extra mass that must be accelerated, reducing the falling speed compared to free fall.
