A firework at rest explodes into three pieces. Piece A (0.3 kg) flies east at 20 m/s. Piece B (0.4 kg) flies north at 15 m/s. What is the momentum of Piece C (0.5 kg)?

## Options
A) 6.0 kg·m/s east
B) 6.0 kg·m/s west
C) √(36+36) = 6√2 ≈ 8.5 kg·m/s, southwest
D) 12 kg·m/s, southwest

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Initial momentum = 0 (at rest). So p_A + p_B + p_C = 0 → p_C = -(p_A + p_B). p_A = (0.3)(20) = 6.0 kg·m/s east = (6, 0). p_B = (0.4)(15) = 6.0 kg·m/s north = (0, 6). p_A + p_B = (6, 6). p_C = -(6, 6) = (-6, -6). |p_C| = √(36+36) = 6√2 ≈ 8.5 kg·m/s, directed southwest. Piece C has momentum 8.5 kg·m/s southwest, and its speed is v_C = 8.5/0.5 = 17 m/s. Option A and B give only one component. Option D (12) adds magnitudes without vector addition. The key: when total initial momentum is zero, the fragments' momenta must form a closed triangle.
