A 0.50 kg puck slides east at 4.0 m/s, then is deflected 90° to travel north at 3.0 m/s. What is the magnitude of its momentum change?

## Options
A) 0.50 kg·m/s
B) 1.0 kg·m/s
C) 2.5 kg·m/s
D) 3.5 kg·m/s

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Initial momentum: p_i = (0.50)(4.0) = 2.0 kg·m/s east = (2.0, 0). Final momentum: p_f = (0.50)(3.0) = 1.5 kg·m/s north = (0, 1.5). Δp = p_f - p_i = (-2.0, 1.5). |Δp| = √(4.0 + 2.25) = √6.25 = 2.5 kg·m/s. The momentum change has components in both directions — the eastward component reverses (from +2.0 to 0) and the northward component increases (from 0 to +1.5). Option A (0.50) = |Δv|. Option B (1.0) uses the difference of magnitudes. Option D (3.5) adds magnitudes.
