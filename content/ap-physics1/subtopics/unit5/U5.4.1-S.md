A ball falls toward Earth. A student wants to apply momentum conservation to analyze this situation. Which system choice makes momentum conservation valid?

## Options
A) The ball alone.
B) The Earth alone.
C) The ball + Earth system.
D) Neither — momentum is never conserved when gravity acts.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
If we choose the ball alone (System A), gravity from Earth is an external force → momentum changes. If we choose Earth alone (B), the ball's gravitational pull on Earth is an external force → Earth's momentum changes. But if we choose ball + Earth together (C), the gravitational forces between them are internal forces — they cancel by Newton's Third Law. With no external forces (ignoring other celestial bodies), the ball+Earth system's total momentum is conserved. The ball gains downward momentum while Earth gains equal upward momentum. Option D is wrong — momentum conservation works when net external force is zero, which depends on system choice.
