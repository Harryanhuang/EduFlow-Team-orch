A 2.0 kg object initially at rest experiences a force: F = +10 N for 0 to 3 s, then F = -5 N for 3 to 5 s. What is the object's final speed?

## Options
A) 5.0 m/s
B) 7.5 m/s
C) 10 m/s
D) 15 m/s

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Impulse from the F-t graph: Phase 1 (0-3 s): J₁ = (+10)(3) = +30 N·s. Phase 2 (3-5 s): J₂ = (-5)(2) = -10 N·s. Total impulse: J = 30 - 10 = 20 N·s. By the impulse-momentum theorem: J = Δp = mv_f - 0 = mv_f. So v_f = J/m = 20/2.0 = 10 m/s. The net impulse is positive (the forward force acts longer and stronger), so the object moves in the positive direction at 10 m/s. Option A (5.0 m/s) uses only J₁ = 30 and divides by m = 2 but forgets the time structure. Option B (7.5 m/s) uses J = 15 (incorrectly computing the areas).
