A 0.40 kg ball moves right at 5.0 m/s, hits a wall, and bounces back left at 3.0 m/s. Taking right as positive, what is the ball's change in momentum?

## Options
A) -0.80 kg·m/s
B) +0.80 kg·m/s
C) -3.2 kg·m/s
D) +3.2 kg·m/s

## Answer
C

## Explanation
v_i = +5.0 m/s (right), v_f = -3.0 m/s (left, negative). Δp = m(v_f - v_i) = 0.40(-3.0 - 5.0) = 0.40(-8.0) = -3.2 kg·m/s. The negative sign means the momentum change is directed to the left — consistent with the wall pushing the ball to the left. Option A (-0.80) = m(v_f - v_i) with wrong signs (uses 3 - 5 = -2 instead of -3 - 5 = -8). Option B (+0.80) uses |Δp| with wrong arithmetic. Option D (+3.2) has wrong sign.
