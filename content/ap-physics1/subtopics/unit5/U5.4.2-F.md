A net force of 10 N acts on a 2 kg object for 3 seconds. What is the change in the object's momentum?

## Options
A) 6.67 kg·m/s
B) 15 kg·m/s
C) 30 kg·m/s
D) 60 kg·m/s

## Answer
C

## Explanation
By the impulse-momentum theorem: Δp = F_net × Δt = 10 N × 3 s = 30 kg·m/s. The change in momentum equals the impulse applied. Option A (6.67) incorrectly divides. Option B (15) uses half the force or time. Option D (60) uses F × Δt × mass = 10 × 3 × 2 = 60, which is dimensionally wrong. The correct impulse-momentum relation is simply Δp = F_net × Δt.
