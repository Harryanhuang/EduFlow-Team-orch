A bullet of mass m is fired at speed v₀ into a wooden block of mass M at rest on a frictionless surface. The bullet embeds in the block. What fraction of the initial kinetic energy is converted to thermal energy and deformation?

## Options
A) m/(m + M)
B) M/(m + M)
C) m·M/(m + M)²
D) 100% (all kinetic energy is lost)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Perfectly inelastic collision. Final velocity: v_f = mv₀/(m + M) (momentum conservation).

K_i = ½mv₀².
K_f = ½(m + M)v_f² = ½(m + M)[mv₀/(m + M)]² = ½m²v₀²/(m + M).

Energy lost = K_i - K_f = ½mv₀² - ½m²v₀²/(m + M) = ½mv₀²[1 - m/(m + M)] = ½mv₀²[M/(m + M)].

Fraction lost = [½mv₀² · M/(m+M)] / [½mv₀²] = M/(m + M).

Physical insight: the larger M is relative to m, the more kinetic energy is lost. If M >> m, nearly 100% is lost. If m >> M, very little is lost. Option A is the complement (fraction remaining). Option C is a common incorrect formula. Option D is wrong — some kinetic energy always remains (the combined mass moves).
