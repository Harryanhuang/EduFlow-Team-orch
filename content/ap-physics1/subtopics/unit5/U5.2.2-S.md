A ball of mass m bounces off a wall. It approaches at speed v at angle θ to the wall surface and rebounds at the same speed and angle. What is the direction of the ball's momentum change?

## Options
A) Parallel to the wall (along the surface)
B) Perpendicular to the wall (into the wall)
C) Perpendicular to the wall (away from the wall)
D) Along the ball's incoming direction

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Setting up coordinates: x perpendicular to wall (toward wall is positive), y parallel to wall. Before: p_ix = mv cosθ (toward wall), p_iy = -mv sinθ. After: p_fx = -mv cosθ (away from wall), p_fy = -mv sinθ (parallel component unchanged). Δp_x = -mv cosθ - mv cosθ = -2mv cosθ (away from wall). Δp_y = 0 (parallel component unchanged). The momentum change is entirely perpendicular to the wall, directed away from it. The wall pushes the ball away (normal force) without affecting the parallel component (frictionless wall). This is consistent with the wall exerting only a normal force perpendicular to its surface.
