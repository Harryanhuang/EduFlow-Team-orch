A 2 kg cart moving at 3 m/s collides with a 1 kg cart at rest. After the collision, both carts stick together and move at 2 m/s. What fraction of the initial kinetic energy is lost?

## Options
A) 0% (elastic collision)
B) 33%
C) 50%
D) 67%

## Answer
B

## Explanation
First verify momentum: p_i = (2)(3) + (1)(0) = 6 kg·m/s. p_f = (2+1)(2) = 6 kg·m/s. ✓ Momentum is conserved.

Now check kinetic energy:
K_i = ½(2)(3²) + ½(1)(0²) = 9 + 0 = 9 J.
K_f = ½(3)(2²) = ½(3)(4) = 6 J.

Kinetic energy lost = K_i - K_f = 9 - 6 = 3 J.
Fraction lost = 3/9 = 1/3 ≈ 33%.

The carts stick together (perfectly inelastic collision), so maximum kinetic energy is lost for these masses. Option A (0%) would be elastic — but K_f ≠ K_i. Option C (50%) would require K_f = 4.5 J. Option D (67%) would require K_f = 3 J. The correct answer: 33% of kinetic energy is converted to other forms (thermal energy, deformation).
