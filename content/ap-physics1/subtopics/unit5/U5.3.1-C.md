A ball is thrown horizontally and caught by a person standing on a frictionless skateboard. During the catch, which momentum components are conserved?

## Options
A) Neither horizontal nor vertical momentum is conserved.
B) Only horizontal momentum is conserved.
C) Both horizontal and vertical momentum are conserved.
D) Only vertical momentum is conserved.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
During the catch, the forces on the person+skateboard+ball system are: gravity (down), normal force from ground (up), and no horizontal forces (frictionless). Vertically: gravity and normal force are external forces, and the normal force changes during the catch (as the person absorbs the ball's slight downward motion). So vertical momentum is NOT conserved. Horizontally: no external forces act, so horizontal momentum IS conserved. The ball's horizontal momentum transfers entirely to the person+ball system. This illustrates that momentum conservation can apply in one direction but not others, depending on the external forces in each direction.
