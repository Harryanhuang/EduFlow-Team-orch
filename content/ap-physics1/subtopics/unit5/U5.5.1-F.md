Which of the following is always true for an elastic collision but NOT necessarily true for an inelastic collision?

## Options
A) Total momentum is conserved.
B) Total kinetic energy is conserved.
C) The objects bounce apart.
D) Newton's Third Law applies.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
In an elastic collision, both momentum AND kinetic energy are conserved. In an inelastic collision, momentum is still conserved (if no external forces), but kinetic energy is NOT conserved — some is converted to thermal energy, sound, or deformation. Option A is true for BOTH types (momentum is conserved in all collisions without external forces). Option C — objects can bounce apart in inelastic collisions too (only perfectly inelastic means they stick). Option D is always true for all interactions. The defining feature of elastic vs inelastic is whether kinetic energy is conserved.
