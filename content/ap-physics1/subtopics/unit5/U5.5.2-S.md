A 2 kg clay ball moving east at 3 m/s collides with a 2 kg clay ball moving north at 4 m/s. They stick together. What is the magnitude of their final velocity?

## Options
A) 2.5 m/s
B) 3.5 m/s
C) 5.0 m/s
D) 7.0 m/s

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Perfectly inelastic collision in 2D. Total mass after = 4 kg.

x-direction (east): p_x = (2)(3) + (2)(0) = 6 kg·m/s. v_fx = 6/4 = 1.5 m/s.
y-direction (north): p_y = (2)(0) + (2)(4) = 8 kg·m/s. v_fy = 8/4 = 2.0 m/s.

Speed = √(v_fx² + v_fy²) = √(1.5² + 2.0²) = √(2.25 + 4.0) = √6.25 = 2.5 m/s.

Option B (3.5) might average the speeds. Option C (5.0) uses √(3²+4²) = 5 but forgets to divide by total mass / account for both masses. Option D (7.0) adds speeds directly.
