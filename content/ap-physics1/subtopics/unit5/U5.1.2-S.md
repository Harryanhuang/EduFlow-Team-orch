A passenger in a car crash experiences the same momentum change whether or not she wears a seatbelt. The seatbelt increases the stopping time from 0.05 s to 0.50 s. By what factor does the seatbelt reduce the average force on the passenger?

## Options
A) 2
B) 5
C) 10
D) 100

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Since Δp is the same: F₁Δt₁ = F₂Δt₂. So F₂/F₁ = Δt₁/Δt₂ = 0.05/0.50 = 1/10. The seatbelt reduces the force by a factor of 10. This is the impulse-momentum theorem in action: same momentum change spread over 10× the time means 10× less force. This is why airbags, crumple zones, and bent knees when landing all work — they increase the collision duration to reduce peak forces.
