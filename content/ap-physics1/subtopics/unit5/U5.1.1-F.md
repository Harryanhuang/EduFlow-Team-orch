A 0.50 kg ball moves east at 6.0 m/s. A 2.0 kg ball moves west at 1.0 m/s. What is the total momentum of the two-ball system? (Take east as positive.)

## Options
A) 1.0 kg·m/s east
B) 3.0 kg·m/s east
C) 5.0 kg·m/s east
D) 0 kg·m/s

## Answer
A

## Explanation
p₁ = (0.50)(+6.0) = +3.0 kg·m/s (east). p₂ = (2.0)(-1.0) = -2.0 kg·m/s (west). Total: p = +3.0 + (-2.0) = +1.0 kg·m/s = 1.0 kg·m/s east. Option B (3.0) ignores ball 2's momentum. Option C (5.0) adds magnitudes without accounting for direction (treating momentum as scalar). Option D assumes they cancel (only true if momenta are equal and opposite). Momentum is a vector — direction matters in addition.
