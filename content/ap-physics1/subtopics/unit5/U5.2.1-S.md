A 20 g bullet at 300 m/s embeds in a 980 g block at rest. What is the magnitude of the bullet's change in momentum?

## Options
A) 6.0 kg·m/s
B) 5.4 kg·m/s
C) 0.06 kg·m/s
D) 0

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Momentum conservation: m_bullet × v_i = (m_bullet + m_block) × v_f. (0.020)(300) = (1.0)v_f → v_f = 6.0 m/s. Bullet's momentum change: Δp = m(v_f - v_i) = 0.020(6.0 - 300) = 0.020(-294) = -5.88 ≈ -6.0 kg·m/s. Magnitude: |Δp| ≈ 6.0 kg·m/s. The bullet loses almost all of its initial momentum (6.0 kg·m/s), transferring most of it to the block. Option B (5.4) would correspond to a different mass ratio. Option C (0.06) uses only the final momentum of the bullet. Option D assumes no change.
