Car A (1000 kg) and Car B (2000 kg) collide head-on. During the collision, which statement about momentum change is correct?

## Options
A) Car B has twice the momentum change of Car A because it's heavier.
B) Car A has twice the momentum change of Car B because it experiences greater acceleration.
C) Both cars have the same magnitude of momentum change.
D) The momentum changes depend on the collision speed.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
By Newton's Third Law, the force on Car A equals the force on Car B (opposite direction) during every instant of the collision. Since the forces are equal and opposite, and the collision duration is the same for both: Impulse on A = -Impulse on B. Therefore Δp_A = -Δp_B. Both cars experience the same magnitude of momentum change. This is true regardless of the mass ratio or collision speed. The difference is in the acceleration (a = F/m): Car A (lighter) experiences greater acceleration and greater velocity change, but the momentum change magnitudes are identical. Option A incorrectly assumes heavier = more momentum change. Option B incorrectly assumes lighter = more momentum change.
