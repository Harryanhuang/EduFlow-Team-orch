Which of the following best defines a closed system in the context of momentum?

## Options
A) A system where no forces act on any object.
B) A system where no matter enters or leaves, and the total momentum changes only due to external net force.
C) A system where all objects are at rest.
D) A system where the net force is always zero.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
A closed system is one where no matter crosses the system boundary. The total momentum of a closed system can still change if a net external force acts on it (via impulse: Δp = F_net × Δt). However, if the net external force is zero, then momentum is conserved. Option A is wrong — forces can act; what matters is whether they sum to zero. Option C is wrong — objects can be moving. Option D is wrong — a closed system can have a nonzero net external force.
