A 0.15 kg baseball is hit by a bat. The contact time is 0.010 s and the ball's velocity changes from -30 m/s (toward bat) to +40 m/s (away from bat). What is the average force on the ball?

## Options
A) 150 N
B) 450 N
C) 1050 N
D) 700 N

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Δp = m(v_f - v_i) = 0.15(40 - (-30)) = 0.15 × 70 = 10.5 kg·m/s. Average force: F_avg = Δp/Δt = 10.5/0.010 = 1050 N. Option A (150 N) uses only the final velocity (ignores direction reversal). Option B (450 N) = m × v_f (ignores initial velocity). Option D (700 N) uses Δv = 40 + 30 = 70 but forgets the mass or uses wrong formula. The ball's momentum changes by 10.5 kg·m/s because the velocity must reverse direction — this is why bouncing produces large forces.
