Two skaters on frictionless ice push off each other. Which statement is correct?

## Options
A) Only the lighter skater's momentum is conserved.
B) The total momentum of the two-skater system is conserved.
C) Each skater's momentum is individually conserved.
D) Momentum is not conserved because there is a force between them.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The two skaters form a closed system (no external horizontal forces on frictionless ice). The push force between them is an internal force — it doesn't change the total momentum. Initial total momentum = 0 (both at rest). Final total momentum = 0 (they move in opposite directions with equal momentum magnitudes). Option A is wrong — individual momentum is NOT conserved (each skater experiences a force). Option C is wrong — each skater's momentum changes due to the push. Option D is wrong — internal forces don't violate momentum conservation. The key: conservation applies to the SYSTEM, not to individual objects.
