A 0.5 kg ball drops vertically and hits the floor at 6 m/s, bouncing back up at 4 m/s. The contact time is 0.02 s. Taking upward as positive, what is the average normal force exerted by the floor on the ball during contact? (Use g = 10 m/s².)

## Options
A) 250 N
B) 255 N
C) 500 N
D) 5 N

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Change in momentum: Δp = m·v_f - m·v_i = (0.5)(+4) - (0.5)(-6) = 2 + 3 = 5 kg·m/s upward. The NET average force: F_net = Δp/Δt = 5/0.02 = 250 N upward. But the net force is F_net = N - mg (normal force up, gravity down). So N = F_net + mg = 250 + (0.5)(10) = 250 + 5 = 255 N. The question asks for the normal force from the floor, not the net force. Option A (250 N) is the net force — a common trap if you forget to add gravity back. Option C (500) forgets the initial velocity direction or doubles. Option D (5 N) is just gravity. The normal force must both reverse the ball's momentum AND support its weight during contact.
