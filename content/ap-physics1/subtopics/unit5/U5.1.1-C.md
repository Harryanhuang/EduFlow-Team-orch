A 0.20 kg ball hits a wall at 5.0 m/s. Compare the magnitude of momentum change in three cases: (I) ball stops, (II) ball bounces back at 3.0 m/s, (III) ball bounces back at 5.0 m/s (elastic).

## Options
A) I = II = III
B) I < II < III
C) III < II < I
D) II < I < III

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Taking initial direction as positive: (I) stops: Δp = 0 - (0.20)(5.0) = -1.0 kg·m/s, |Δp| = 1.0. (II) bounces at 3 m/s: Δp = (0.20)(-3.0) - (0.20)(5.0) = -0.6 - 1.0 = -1.6, |Δp| = 1.6. (III) bounces at 5 m/s: Δp = (0.20)(-5.0) - (0.20)(5.0) = -1.0 - 1.0 = -2.0, |Δp| = 2.0. So |Δp_I| < |Δp_II| < |Δp_III|. The key insight: bouncing always produces a larger momentum change (and therefore larger impulse/force) than simply stopping. This is why bouncing balls exert more force on walls than clay that sticks.
