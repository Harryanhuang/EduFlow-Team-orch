A 1 kg ball moving at 4 m/s collides head-on with a 1 kg ball at rest. After the collision, the first ball is at rest and the second ball moves at 4 m/s. What type of collision is this?

## Options
A) Elastic — kinetic energy is conserved.
B) Inelastic — kinetic energy is lost.
C) Perfectly inelastic — the balls stick together.
D) Cannot be determined without knowing the forces.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Check momentum: p_i = (1)(4) + (1)(0) = 4 kg·m/s. p_f = (1)(0) + (1)(4) = 4 kg·m/s. ✓ Momentum conserved. Check kinetic energy: K_i = ½(1)(4²) + ½(1)(0²) = 8 J. K_f = ½(1)(0²) + ½(1)(4²) = 8 J. K_i = K_f = 8 J. Kinetic energy is conserved, so this is an elastic collision. This is a classic result for equal-mass elastic collisions — the objects exchange velocities. Option B is wrong — no kinetic energy is lost. Option C is wrong — the balls don't stick together. Option D is wrong — we have all the information needed.
