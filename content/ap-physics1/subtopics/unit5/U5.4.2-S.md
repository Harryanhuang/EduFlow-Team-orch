Two carts (m₁ = 3 kg, v₁ = 4 m/s right; m₂ = 1 kg, v₂ = -2 m/s left) collide on a frictionless track. The collision lasts 0.5 s. What is the change in total momentum of the two-cart system during the collision?

## Options
A) 0 kg·m/s
B) 5 kg·m/s
C) 10 kg·m/s
D) 14 kg·m/s

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The two-cart system on a frictionless track has no net external force acting on it (gravity and normal force cancel vertically, no horizontal external forces). By the impulse-momentum theorem for the system: Δp_system = F_net,external × Δt = 0 × 0.5 = 0 kg·m/s. The total momentum of the system does not change. The internal collision forces between the carts are equal and opposite (Newton's Third Law), so their impulses cancel. Total initial momentum = (3)(4) + (1)(-2) = 12 - 2 = 10 kg·m/s. Total final momentum = 10 kg·m/s (unchanged). Option B/C/D incorrectly include internal forces or miscalculate.
