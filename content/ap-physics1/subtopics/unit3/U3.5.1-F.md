Planet X has the same mass as Earth but twice Earth's radius. How does Planet X's escape velocity compare to Earth's? (Earth v_esc ≈ 11.2 km/s)

## Options
A) 22.4 km/s
B) 11.2 km/s
C) 7.9 km/s
D) 5.6 km/s

## Answer
C

## Explanation
v_esc = √(2GM/R). For Planet X: v_esc,X = √(2GM/(2R_E)) = √(2GM/R_E)/√2 = v_esc,Earth/√2 = 11.2/1.414 ≈ 7.9 km/s. Since the planet has twice the radius but the same mass, the surface gravity is weaker and the escape velocity is lower by a factor of √2. Option A (22.4) doubles instead of dividing. Option B (11.2) assumes no change. Option D (5.6) divides by 2 instead of √2.
