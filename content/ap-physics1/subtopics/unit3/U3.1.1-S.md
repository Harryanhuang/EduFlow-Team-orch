Two coins are placed on a rotating turntable: Coin A at radius r and Coin B at radius 2r. Which statement is correct?

## Options
A) Coin B has twice the angular velocity of Coin A.
B) Coin B has twice the linear speed of Coin A.
C) Coin B has half the period of Coin A.
D) Coin B has four times the linear speed of Coin A.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Both coins are on the same turntable, so they share the same angular velocity ω and period T. Therefore options A and C are wrong. Linear speed v = ωr. For Coin A: v_A = ωr. For Coin B: v_B = ω(2r) = 2ωr = 2v_A. So Coin B has twice the linear speed (B is correct). Option D would be correct if it were about centripetal acceleration (a_c = ω²r), not linear speed. Key: on a rigid rotating body, ω is uniform but v scales with r.
