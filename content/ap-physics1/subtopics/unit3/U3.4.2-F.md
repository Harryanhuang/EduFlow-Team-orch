Earth orbits the Sun at 1 AU with period 1 year. Jupiter orbits at 5.2 AU. What is Jupiter's orbital period?

## Options
A) 5.2 years
B) 11.9 years
C) 27 years
D) 140 years

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By Kepler's Third Law: T² ∝ r³. For Earth: T_E = 1 year, r_E = 1 AU. For Jupiter: T_J²/T_E² = r_J³/r_E³. So T_J² = (5.2)³ = 140.6. T_J = √140.6 ≈ 11.9 years. Option A (5.2 years) incorrectly assumes T ∝ r. Option C (27 years) uses T = r³/2 or similar wrong formula. Option D (140 years) gives r³ without taking the square root. The correct relationship is T² ∝ r³, not T ∝ r.
