A satellite of mass m is in circular orbit of radius r around Earth (mass M). What is the satellite's total mechanical energy?

## Options
A) GMm/(2r)
B) -GMm/(2r)
C) -GMm/r
D) GMm/r

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Kinetic energy: KE = ½mv² = ½m(GM/r) = GMm/(2r) (using v² = GM/r for circular orbit). Potential energy: PE = -GMm/r. Total energy: E = KE + PE = GMm/(2r) - GMm/r = GMm/(2r) - 2GMm/(2r) = -GMm/(2r). The total energy is negative, indicating a bound state — the satellite cannot escape without additional energy. Option A is just the kinetic energy. Option C is the potential energy. Option D is the magnitude of the potential energy.
