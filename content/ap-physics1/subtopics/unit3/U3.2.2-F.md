A car rounds a flat (unbanked) curve at constant speed. Which force provides the centripetal force?

## Options
A) Gravity
B) The normal force
C) Static friction between the tires and the road
D) Kinetic friction between the tires and the road

## Answer
C

## Explanation
On a flat curve, the only horizontal force available to point toward the center is friction between the tires and the road. Since the tires are rolling (not sliding sideways), this is static friction — the tire contact patch grips the road and prevents the car from sliding outward. Gravity (A) acts vertically. The normal force (B) also acts vertically. Kinetic friction (D) would mean the tires are sliding, which is not normal turning. In a properly executed turn, the tires grip the road via static friction, which provides the centripetal force needed for circular motion.
