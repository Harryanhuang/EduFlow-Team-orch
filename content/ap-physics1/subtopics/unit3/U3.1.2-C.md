A point on a rotating disk (r = 0.20 m) has angular velocity ω = 5 rad/s and angular acceleration α = 10 rad/s². What is the magnitude of the total acceleration of this point?

## Options
A) 2.0 m/s²
B) 5.0 m/s²
C) 5.4 m/s²
D) 7.0 m/s²

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The total acceleration has two components: tangential a_t = rα = 0.20 × 10 = 2.0 m/s² (tangent to the circle), and centripetal a_c = ω²r = 25 × 0.20 = 5.0 m/s² (toward the center). These are perpendicular, so: a_total = √(a_t² + a_c²) = √(4 + 25) = √29 ≈ 5.4 m/s². Option A (2.0) is only the tangential component. Option B (5.0) is only the centripetal component. Option D adds the two components (2+5=7) instead of combining them as perpendicular vectors. Key: in non-uniform circular motion, always identify both acceleration components and combine them vectorially.
