Satellite A orbits Earth with period T and orbital radius r. Satellite B orbits the Moon with the same orbital radius r. Given that Earth's mass is about 81 times the Moon's mass, what is T_B/T_A?

## Options
A) 1/9
B) 1/81
C) 9
D) 81

## Answer
C

## Explanation
From Kepler's Third Law: T² = 4π²r³/(GM). For the same r: T² ∝ 1/M. So T_B²/T_A² = M_Earth/M_Moon = 81. Therefore T_B/T_A = √81 = 9. The satellite orbiting the less massive Moon takes 9 times longer because the weaker gravitational pull provides less centripetal force, resulting in a slower orbital speed and longer period. Option A (1/9) inverts the ratio. Option B (1/81) uses T ∝ 1/M (missing the square root). Option D (81) uses T² ∝ M (wrong direction of proportionality).
