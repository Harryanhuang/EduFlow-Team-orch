A ball on a string of length 0.80 m swings in a vertical circle. What is the minimum speed at the top of the circle for the ball to maintain circular motion? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 0 m/s
B) 2.0 m/s
C) 2.8 m/s
D) 4.0 m/s

## Answer
C

## Explanation
At the top, both tension and gravity point toward the center: T + mg = mv²/r. For minimum speed, T = 0 (string just barely taut): mg = mv²_min/r. So v_min = √(gr) = √(10 × 0.80) = √8 = 2.8 m/s. Option A applies to the rod model (which can support the ball). Option B uses v = √(gr/2). Option D uses v = √(2gr) (which would be the minimum speed at the BOTTOM for the ball to just reach the top with v = 0). The key distinction: string model needs v > 0 at the top to maintain tension.
