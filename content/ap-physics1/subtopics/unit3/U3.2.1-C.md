A ball on a string moves in a vertical circle of radius r. At the top, the ball has speed v_top. At the bottom, the ball has speed v_bot = √(5gr). What is the ratio of centripetal acceleration at the bottom to that at the top? (Assume the ball barely makes it over the top, so v_top = √(gr).)

## Options
A) 1
B) 3
C) 5
D) 7

## Answer
C

## Explanation
At the top: v_top = √(gr) (minimum speed for circular motion with a string). a_c,top = v_top²/r = gr/r = g. At the bottom: v_bot = √(5gr). a_c,bot = v_bot²/r = 5gr/r = 5g. Ratio: a_c,bot/a_c,top = 5g/g = 5. The speed at the bottom is greater due to gravitational acceleration as the ball falls from top to bottom (energy conservation: ½mv_bot² = ½mv_top² + mg(2r) = mgr + 2mgr = 5mgr, so v_bot² = 5gr ✓). The centripetal acceleration at the bottom is 5 times that at the top because of both the higher speed and the energy gained from falling.
