A 0.50 kg ball on a string moves in a horizontal circle of radius 1.0 m at 6.0 m/s. The string can withstand a maximum tension of 15 N. What happens?

## Options
A) The ball continues in its circular path safely.
B) The string breaks and the ball flies radially outward.
C) The string breaks and the ball flies off tangent to the circle.
D) The ball slows down gradually.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Required centripetal force: F_c = mv²/r = 0.50 × 36/1.0 = 18 N. Since 18 N > 15 N (maximum tension), the string breaks. After breaking, there is no longer any radial force on the ball. By Newton's First Law, the ball continues in a straight line tangent to the circle at the point where the string broke (its instantaneous velocity direction at that moment). It does NOT fly radially outward — there is no "centrifugal force" pushing it outward. The outward appearance is an illusion from a rotating reference frame.
