A satellite orbits very close to the surface of a spherical planet (orbit radius ≈ planet radius R). The orbital period is T. What is the planet's average density ρ?

## Options
A) ρ = 3π/(GT²)
B) ρ = 4π/(3GT²)
C) ρ = 3πR³/(GT²)
D) ρ = GM/(4πR³/3)

## Answer
A

## Explanation
For a near-surface orbit: GMm/R² = m(2π/T)²R → GM = 4π²R³/T². The planet mass: M = ρV = ρ(4πR³/3). Substituting: G·ρ(4πR³/3) = 4π²R³/T². Simplifying: ρ·4πG/3 = 4π²/T². So ρ = 3π/(GT²). The planet radius cancels out — the density depends only on the orbital period near the surface. This is a remarkable result: by measuring how long it takes a satellite to orbit close to a planet, we can determine its density without knowing its size. Option B (4π/3GT²) has the coefficient inverted. Option C incorrectly retains R³. Option D is just the definition of density, not the result of the orbital analysis.
