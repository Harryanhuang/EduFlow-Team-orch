A coin is placed 0.20 m from the center of a turntable. The coefficient of static friction between the coin and the turntable is 0.50. What is the maximum angular velocity before the coin slides off? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 2.5 rad/s
B) 5.0 rad/s
C) 7.1 rad/s
D) 25 rad/s

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Static friction provides the centripetal force: f_s = mω²r ≤ μ_s·N = μ_s·mg. At the critical condition: mω²_max·r = μ_s·mg. Solving: ω_max = √(μ_s·g/r) = √(0.50 × 10/0.20) = √25 = 5.0 rad/s. The coin's mass cancels out — the result depends only on μ_s, g, and r. Option A (2.5 rad/s) uses ω = μ_s·g/r (without square root). Option C (7.1) uses g = 9.8 instead of 10, or computes √(μ_s·g) without dividing by r. Option D (25) forgets the square root entirely.
