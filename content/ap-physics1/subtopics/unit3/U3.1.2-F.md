A wheel of radius 0.30 m rotates with an angular velocity of 10 rad/s. What is the linear speed of a point on the rim?

## Options
A) 0.03 m/s
B) 3.0 m/s
C) 33 m/s
D) 30 m/s

## Answer
B

## Explanation
v = rω = 0.30 m × 10 rad/s = 3.0 m/s. Option A divides instead of multiplies. Option C uses v = ω/r. Option D uses v = rω but forgets to include the radius unit correctly. The relationship is straightforward: linear speed equals radius times angular velocity.
