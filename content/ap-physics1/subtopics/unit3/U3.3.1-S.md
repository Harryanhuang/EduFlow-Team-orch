A highway curve of radius 100 m is banked at angle θ for a design speed of 20 m/s. If a car travels the curve at exactly 20 m/s on a frictionless surface, what provides the centripetal force?

## Options
A) Friction
B) The horizontal component of the normal force
C) Gravity
D) The vertical component of the normal force

## Answer
B

## Explanation
On a frictionless banked curve, the only forces are gravity (down) and the normal force (perpendicular to the surface). The normal force has a horizontal component N sinθ pointing toward the center of the curve. This horizontal component provides the centripetal force. At the design speed, no friction is needed: N sinθ = mv²/r. The vertical component N cosθ balances gravity: N cosθ = mg. Dividing gives tanθ = v²/(rg), which is the design condition. Option A is wrong because the surface is frictionless. Option C (gravity) is vertical, not horizontal. Option D (vertical component) balances gravity, not centripetal force.
