A ball on a rigid rod (not a string) swings in a vertical circle of radius r. What is the minimum speed at the top for circular motion?

## Options
A) 0
B) √(gr)
C) √(2gr)
D) √(gr/2)

## Answer
A

## Explanation
A rigid rod can push (provide compressive force) as well as pull (provide tension). At the top of the circle, if the ball moves very slowly, the rod can push upward to support the ball. The minimum speed is therefore v = 0 — the rod holds the ball in place even when it momentarily stops at the top. This is fundamentally different from the string model, where the string can only pull (tension ≥ 0), requiring v ≥ √(gr) at the top to maintain circular motion. The rod model allows the ball to complete the circle at any speed, including zero.
