A conical pendulum consists of a mass on a string of length L swinging in a horizontal circle. The string makes angle θ with the vertical. If L = 1.0 m and θ = 30°, what is the speed of the mass? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 1.2 m/s
B) 1.7 m/s
C) 2.4 m/s
D) 5.0 m/s

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The radius of the circular path: r = L sinθ = 1.0 × sin30° = 0.50 m. From the force analysis: T cosθ = mg and T sinθ = mv²/r. Dividing: tanθ = v²/(rg). So v² = rg·tanθ = 0.50 × 10 × tan30° = 5.0 × 0.577 = 2.89. v = √2.89 ≈ 1.7 m/s. Option A uses v² = Lg·tanθ without the sinθ for the radius. Option C uses g·tanθ directly. Option D uses v = √(Lg). The key is correctly identifying r = L sinθ and applying tanθ = v²/(rg).
