A ball on a string moves in a vertical circle. At the bottom, the tension is T_bot. At the top, the tension is T_top. What is T_bot − T_top? (Assume the ball just barely makes it over the top.)

## Options
A) 2mg
B) 4mg
C) 6mg
D) 8mg

## Answer
C

## Explanation
At the top (barely making it): v_top = √(gr). T_top + mg = mv_top²/r = mg, so T_top = 0. Energy conservation from bottom to top: ½mv_bot² = ½mv_top² + mg(2r) = mgr + 2mgr = 5mgr. So v_bot² = 5gr. At the bottom: T_bot - mg = mv_bot²/r = 5mg. So T_bot = 6mg. Therefore T_bot - T_top = 6mg - 0 = 6mg. The difference of 6mg comes from both the gravitational contribution and the energy conversion: the ball loses kinetic energy climbing, so the centripetal acceleration (and thus tension) is much greater at the bottom than at the top.
