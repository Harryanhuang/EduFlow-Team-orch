Two gears are meshed together. Gear A has radius 5 cm and Gear B has radius 15 cm. If Gear A rotates at 60 rpm, what is the angular velocity of Gear B?

## Options
A) 20 rpm
B) 60 rpm
C) 180 rpm
D) 10 rpm

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Meshed gears have the same linear speed at their contact point: v_A = v_B, so ω_A × r_A = ω_B × r_B. Therefore ω_B = ω_A × r_A/r_B = 60 × 5/15 = 20 rpm. The larger gear rotates slower. Also, meshed gears rotate in opposite directions. Option B assumes same angular velocity (co-axial, not meshed). Option C inverts the ratio. Option D uses 60/6 = 10 for no clear reason. Key: meshed gears → equal linear speed at teeth → ω inversely proportional to r.
