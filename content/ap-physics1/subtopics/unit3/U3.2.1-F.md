A car travels at constant speed 20 m/s around a circular track of radius 50 m. What is the car's centripetal acceleration?

## Options
A) 0.4 m/s²
B) 2.5 m/s²
C) 8.0 m/s²
D) 400 m/s²

## Answer
C

## Explanation
a_c = v²/r = (20)²/50 = 400/50 = 8.0 m/s², directed toward the center of the track. Option A uses a = v/r (missing the square). Option B uses a = r/v² (inverted). Option D gives v² without dividing by r. The centripetal acceleration is always directed toward the center of the circular path, even though the car's speed is constant — the velocity direction is continuously changing.
