A person stands at latitude 60° on Earth (Earth radius R = 6400 km). What is the person's linear speed due to Earth's rotation?

## Options
A) 230 m/s
B) 460 m/s
C) 330 m/s
D) 0 m/s

## Answer
A

## Explanation
At latitude φ = 60°, the person rotates in a circle of radius r = R cosφ = 6400 × cos60° = 6400 × 0.5 = 3200 km = 3.2 × 10⁶ m. Earth's rotation period T = 24 h = 86400 s. Linear speed: v = 2πr/T = 2π × 3.2 × 10⁶ / 86400 ≈ 233 m/s ≈ 230 m/s. Option B (460 m/s) is the speed at the equator (φ = 0°, r = R). Option C (330 m/s) might come from using sin60° instead of cos60° or other errors. Option D ignores Earth's rotation entirely. At latitude 60°, the rotation radius is half the equatorial radius, so the speed is half the equatorial speed.
