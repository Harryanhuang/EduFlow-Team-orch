A satellite in circular orbit at radius r has speed v₀. What additional speed Δv (applied in the direction of motion) is needed for the satellite to escape from this orbit?

## Options
A) v₀
B) (√2 − 1)v₀
C) √2 · v₀
D) 2v₀

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Current orbital speed: v₀ = √(GM/r). Escape speed from this radius: v_esc = √(2GM/r) = √2 · v₀. The additional speed needed: Δv = v_esc - v₀ = √2·v₀ - v₀ = (√2 - 1)v₀ ≈ 0.414v₀. Option A (v₀) is the additional speed needed to double the kinetic energy, not to escape. Option C (√2·v₀) is the total escape speed, not the additional speed. Option D (2v₀) is the speed needed to quadruple kinetic energy.
