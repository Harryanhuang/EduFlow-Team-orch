Two satellites orbit Earth: Satellite A at radius r and Satellite B at radius 4r. What is the ratio v_A/v_B of their orbital speeds?

## Options
A) 1/2
B) 1/4
C) 2
D) 4

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Orbital speed: v = √(GM/r). So v_A = √(GM/r) and v_B = √(GM/4r) = √(GM/r)/2 = v_A/2. Therefore v_A/v_B = 2. Closer orbits are faster — this is why low-Earth-orbit satellites move faster than geostationary satellites. The ratio follows the inverse-square-root law: v ∝ 1/√r. Quadrupling the radius halves the speed.
