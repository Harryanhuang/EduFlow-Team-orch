A satellite is moved from a circular orbit of radius r to a circular orbit of radius 2r. What is the change in total energy ΔE = E_final - E_initial?

## Options
A) -GMm/(4r)
B) GMm/(4r)
C) -GMm/(2r)
D) GMm/(2r)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
E_initial = -GMm/(2r). E_final = -GMm/(2·2r) = -GMm/(4r). ΔE = E_final - E_initial = -GMm/(4r) - (-GMm/(2r)) = -GMm/(4r) + GMm/(2r) = -GMm/(4r) + 2GMm/(4r) = GMm/(4r). The total energy increases (becomes less negative) — this is positive work done to raise the orbit. Interestingly, as the satellite moves to a higher orbit: KE decreases (it moves slower), PE increases (becomes less negative), and the increase in PE is greater than the decrease in KE, so total energy increases.
