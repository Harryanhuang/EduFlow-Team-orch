Car A and Car B travel on concentric circular tracks. Car A is on the inner track (radius r) and Car B on the outer track (radius 2r). Both complete one lap in the same time T. What is the ratio of their centripetal accelerations a_B/a_A?

## Options
A) 1/2
B) 1
C) 2
D) 4

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Since both cars have the same period T, they share the same angular velocity: ω = 2π/T. Using a_c = ω²r: a_A = ω²r, a_B = ω²(2r) = 2ω²r. Therefore a_B/a_A = 2. The key constraint is "same period" → same ω. With ω constant, a_c ∝ r. Doubling the radius doubles the centripetal acceleration. If instead the problem stated "same speed," then a_c = v²/r and a_B/a_A = r_A/r_B = 1/2.
