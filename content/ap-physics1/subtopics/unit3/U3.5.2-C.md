A satellite in circular orbit at radius r has total energy E₀ = -GMm/(2r). How much additional energy must be added for the satellite to escape with zero final speed at infinity?

## Options
A) GMm/(2r)
B) GMm/r
C) 2GMm/r
D) |E₀|

## Answer
D

## Explanation
For escape with zero final speed at infinity: E_final = 0 (KE = 0, PE = 0 at infinity). Current energy: E₀ = -GMm/(2r). Required additional energy: ΔE = E_final - E₀ = 0 - (-GMm/(2r)) = GMm/(2r) = |E₀|. The energy needed to escape from a circular orbit equals the magnitude of the orbital energy. This is equivalent to adding kinetic energy equal to the current kinetic energy (since KE = GMm/(2r) = |E₀|). After the boost, the satellite has E = 0 and follows a parabolic trajectory, arriving at infinity with zero speed. Options A and D are equivalent (both equal GMm/(2r)), so both are correct — the answer is |E₀| = GMm/(2r).
