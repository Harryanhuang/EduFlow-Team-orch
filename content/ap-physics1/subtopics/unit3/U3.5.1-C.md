Two planets have the same density but different radii: R_A = R and R_B = 2R. What is the ratio of their escape velocities v_esc,B/v_esc,A?

## Options
A) 1
B) √2
C) 2
D) 4

## Answer
C

## Explanation
M = ρV = ρ(4πR³/3). So v_esc = √(2GM/R) = √(2G·ρ(4πR³/3)/R) = √(8πGρR²/3) = R√(8πGρ/3). Since both planets have the same density ρ: v_esc ∝ R. Therefore v_esc,B/v_esc,A = R_B/R_A = 2R/R = 2. When density is the same, the escape velocity scales linearly with radius. A planet twice as large (same density) has twice the escape velocity. This makes sense: doubling the radius increases the mass by 8× (volume scales as R³), but the distance from center also doubles, and √(M/R) = √(8/2) = √4 = 2.
