A student collects data for an object sliding down a frictionless incline. The object starts from rest, and its velocity $v$ is measured at various positions $x$ from the starting point. The relationship is $v^2 = 2ax$. If the student plots $v^2$ vs $x$ and obtains a straight line with slope $4.0 \text{ m/s}^2$, what is the acceleration of the object?

## Options
A) $1.0 \text{ m/s}^2$
B) $2.0 \text{ m/s}^2$
C) $4.0 \text{ m/s}^2$
D) $8.0 \text{ m/s}^2$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
由 $v^2 = 2ax$，plot $v^2$ vs $x$ 得直线，斜率 = $2a$。已知斜率 = $4.0 \text{ m/s}^2$，因此 $2a = 4.0$，$a = 2.0 \text{ m/s}^2$。选项 B 正确。常见错误是直接将斜率当作加速度（$a = 4.0$），或混淆斜率与截距的物理意义。注意线性化后斜率与物理参数的关系需要通过原公式推导。
