Two objects A and B move along the same straight line. Object A starts at $x_A = 10 \text{ m}$ and moves with a constant velocity of $+4 \text{ m/s}$. Object B starts at $x_B = 30 \text{ m}$ and moves with a constant velocity of $-2 \text{ m/s}$. At what time do the two objects meet?

## Options
A) $2.5 \text{ s}$
B) $3.3 \text{ s}$
C) $5.0 \text{ s}$
D) $10.0 \text{ s}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
物体 A 的位置函数：$x_A(t) = 10 + 4t$。物体 B 的位置函数：$x_B(t) = 30 - 2t$。相遇时 $x_A = x_B$，即 $10 + 4t = 30 - 2t$。解得 $6t = 20$，$t = 20/6 = 10/3 \approx 3.3 \text{ s}$。选项 B 正确。常见错误是直接用初始距离除以速度之和 $20 / (4+2) = 3.3$（虽然数值碰巧正确，但逻辑不严谨），或忽略初始位置直接用 $30/6 = 5$ s。
