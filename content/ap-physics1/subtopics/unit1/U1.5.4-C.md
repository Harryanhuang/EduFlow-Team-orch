A student measures the period $T$ of a simple pendulum for various lengths $L$. The theoretical relationship for small oscillations is $T = 2\pi\sqrt{L/g}$. Which plot will produce a straight line, and what is the physical meaning of the slope?

## Options
A) $T$ vs $L$; slope = $2\pi/g$
B) $T^2$ vs $L$; slope = $4\pi^2/g$
C) $T$ vs $\sqrt{L}$; slope = $2\pi/\sqrt{g}$
D) $1/T$ vs $L$; slope = $\sqrt{g}/(2\pi)$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
由 $T = 2\pi\sqrt{L/g}$，两边平方得 $T^2 = (4\pi^2/g)L$。因此 plot $T^2$ vs $L$ 得直线，斜率 = $4\pi^2/g$。选项 B 正确。选项 C 虽然也能得到直线（$T = (2\pi/\sqrt{g})\sqrt{L}$），但题目问的是标准的线性化方法。常见错误是选择 $T$ vs $L$（得到抛物线），或错误推导斜率表达式。本题需要理解如何通过变量替换将非线性关系线性化，并正确识别斜率的物理意义。
