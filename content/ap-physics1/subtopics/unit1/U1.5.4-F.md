A student wants to verify that an object starting from rest undergoes uniform acceleration. The theoretical relationship is $x = \frac{1}{2}at^2$. Which pair of variables should the student plot to obtain a straight line?

## Options
A) $x$ vs $t$
B) $x$ vs $t^2$
C) $x$ vs $1/t$
D) $x^2$ vs $t$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
要将非线性关系 $x = \frac{1}{2}at^2$ 线性化，应令 $y = x$，$x' = t^2$。则 $y = \frac{1}{2}a x'$，即 $x$ vs $t^2$ 为直线，斜率为 $\frac{1}{2}a$。选项 B 正确。常见错误是直接 plot $x$ vs $t$ 得到抛物线，或选择错误的变量组合。
