A spring-loaded launcher shoots a ball vertically upward. The ball reaches maximum height h. Which sequence correctly describes the energy transformations from launch to maximum height?

## Options
A) KE → PE_gravity throughout
B) PE_spring → KE → PE_gravity
C) PE_spring → PE_gravity directly (no KE intermediate)
D) PE_spring → KE → PE_gravity + thermal energy (always)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Stage 1 (spring releases): PE_spring converts to KE of the ball. Stage 2 (ball rises): KE converts to PE_gravity as the ball climbs. At maximum height: KE = 0, PE_gravity = PE_spring_initial (if no friction). The full sequence is PE_spring → KE → PE_gravity. Option A misses the initial spring energy. Option C incorrectly skips the KE stage — the ball has KE as it leaves the spring. Option D adds thermal energy which only applies if friction is present (the problem implies an ideal case).
