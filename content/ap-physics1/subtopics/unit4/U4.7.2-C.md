Ball A (mass m) moving at speed v collides elastically with Ball B (mass m) at rest. After the collision, what are their speeds?

## Options
A) A: v, B: 0 (A passes through B)
B) A: 0, B: v (A stops, B takes over)
C) A: v/2, B: v/2 (equal split)
D) A: -v, B: 2v (A bounces back)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Equal masses, elastic collision: the balls exchange velocities. Ball A stops (v_A = 0) and Ball B moves at speed v. This can be derived from the two conservation equations: Momentum: mv = mv_Af + mv_Bf → v = v_Af + v_Bf. Relative velocity: v - 0 = -(v_Af - v_Bf) → v = v_Bf - v_Af. Adding: 2v = 2v_Bf → v_Bf = v. Then v_Af = 0. This is the classic billiard ball result: when a cue ball hits another ball head-on, the cue ball stops and the target ball takes off at the original speed. Option A violates momentum (A keeps going AND B moves). Option C is a perfectly inelastic result (equal speeds). Option D violates momentum (m(-v) + m(2v) = mv ✓, but KE: ½mv² + ½m(4v²) ≠ ½mv² ✗).
