A block starts from rest at the top of a 30° incline of length 4.0 m and reaches the bottom at 4.0 m/s. What is μ_k? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 0.15
B) 0.25
C) 0.35
D) 0.50

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Energy method: mgh + W_fric = ½mv². Height: h = L sin30° = 4.0 × 0.5 = 2.0 m. Friction work: W_fric = -μ_k mg cos30° × L = -μ_k × m × 10 × 0.866 × 4.0 = -34.6μ_k × m. Final KE: ½mv² = ½m(16) = 8m. So: m(10)(2.0) - 34.6μ_k m = 8m. Dividing by m: 20 - 34.6μ_k = 8. So μ_k = 12/34.6 ≈ 0.35. The friction coefficient must be 0.35 to slow the block to 4.0 m/s at the bottom. Without friction, the block would reach v = √(2gh) = √40 ≈ 6.3 m/s.
