A hydroelectric dam converts gravitational PE of water to electricity. Water flows at 200 kg/s from a height of 50 m. The plant produces 80 kW of electrical power. What fraction of the input power is lost to inefficiency? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 20%
B) 36%
C) 60%
D) 80%

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Input power from gravitational PE: P_in = ṁgh = 200 × 10 × 50 = 100,000 W = 100 kW. Useful output: P_out = 80 kW. Efficiency: η = 80/100 = 80%. Fraction lost: 1 - η = 20%. The 20 kW loss appears as heat (friction in turbines), sound, and turbulence in the water. Option D (80%) is the efficiency, not the loss fraction. Option C (60%) and B (36%) are incorrect calculations. This dam converts 80% of the water's gravitational PE into useful electricity — a reasonably efficient system.
