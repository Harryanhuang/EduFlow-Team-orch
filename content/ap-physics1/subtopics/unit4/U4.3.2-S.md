How much energy is required to move a satellite of mass m from the surface of Earth (radius R) to an orbit at radius 2R? (M = Earth mass)

## Options
A) GMm/(2R)
B) GMm/R
C) 2GMm/R
D) GMm/(4R)

## Answer
A

## Explanation
PE at surface: PE_i = -GMm/R. PE at orbit 2R: PE_f = -GMm/(2R). ΔPE = PE_f - PE_i = -GMm/(2R) - (-GMm/R) = -GMm/(2R) + GMm/R = GMm/R - GMm/(2R) = GMm/(2R). The energy required is GMm/(2R) > 0, confirming we need to add energy to lift the satellite. Option B (GMm/R) is the magnitude of the surface PE (not the change). Option C (2GMm/R) is double the surface PE magnitude. Option D is the PE at 2R without the subtraction.
