A car braking from 30 km/h requires a stopping distance of 10 m (with constant braking force). What is the stopping distance from 90 km/h with the same braking force?

## Options
A) 30 m
B) 60 m
C) 90 m
D) 10 m

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Work-energy theorem: Fd = ½mv². With same F and m: d ∝ v². Tripling the speed (30 → 90 km/h, factor of 3) increases the stopping distance by 3² = 9 times. d = 9 × 10 = 90 m. Option A (30 m) uses d ∝ v (linear, not squared). Option B (60 m) adds rather than multiplies. This quadratic relationship explains why high-speed driving is disproportionately dangerous: doubling highway speed quadruples the braking distance.
