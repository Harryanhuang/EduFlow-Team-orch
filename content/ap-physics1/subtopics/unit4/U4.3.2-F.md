A satellite moves from orbit at radius r to orbit at radius 3r. How does the gravitational potential energy change?

## Options
A) PE becomes 3 times more negative.
B) PE becomes 3 times less negative (increases toward zero).
C) PE becomes 1/3 as negative (increases toward zero).
D) PE doesn't change.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
PE = -GMm/r. Initial: PE_i = -GMm/r. Final: PE_f = -GMm/(3r) = (1/3)(-GMm/r) = PE_i/3. Since PE_i is negative, dividing by 3 makes it less negative — PE increases toward zero. Moving the satellite farther from Earth requires energy input, which raises the potential energy. Option A would make PE more negative (moving closer). Option B (3× less negative) misstates the factor — PE becomes 1/3 of its original value, not 1/3 as negative in the sense of option B. Option C correctly states PE becomes 1/3 as negative.
