A 60 kg person climbs a flight of stairs 3.0 m high in 5.0 s. What is the average power output? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 36 W
B) 180 W
C) 360 W
D) 900 W

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Work done against gravity: W = mgh = 60 × 10 × 3.0 = 1800 J. Average power: P = W/t = 1800/5.0 = 360 W. Option A (36 W) = mg (weight, not power). Option B (180 W) = W/h (work per height, not work per time). Option D (900 W) = mgh × t (multiplying instead of dividing). Power measures how fast energy is transferred — the same work done in less time requires more power.
