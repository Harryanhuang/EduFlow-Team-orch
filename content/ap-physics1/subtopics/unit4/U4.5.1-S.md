A 3.0 kg block slides 5.0 m down a frictionless 30° incline. What is the total work done on the block by all forces? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 37 J
B) 75 J
C) 150 J
D) 0 J

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Forces on the block: gravity (mg down) and normal force (perpendicular to incline). Normal force does zero work because it is perpendicular to the displacement. The only force doing work is gravity. Height descended: h = L sin30° = 5.0 × 0.5 = 2.5 m. Work by gravity: W = mgh = 3.0 × 10 × 2.5 = 75 J. Total work = work by gravity = 75 J (normal force contributes 0). This equals the change in KE: the block gains 75 J of kinetic energy. Option A uses g = 9.8 instead of the specified g = 10. Option C uses W = mgL without the sin30° factor. Option D incorrectly claims no work is done.
