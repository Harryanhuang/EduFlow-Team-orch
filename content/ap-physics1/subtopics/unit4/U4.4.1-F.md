A ball is thrown straight upward (ignoring air resistance). At which points during the flight is mechanical energy conserved?

## Options
A) Only at the highest point.
B) Only during the upward journey.
C) Only during the downward journey.
D) Throughout the entire flight.

## Answer
D

## Explanation
With no air resistance, the only force is gravity (a conservative force). Mechanical energy is conserved throughout the entire flight — during ascent, at the top, and during descent. At all points: KE + PE = constant. During ascent: KE decreases, PE increases. At the top: KE = 0, PE = max. During descent: KE increases, PE decreases. The sum remains the same at every instant. Option A, B, C incorrectly restrict conservation to specific portions of the flight.
