Two identical balls collide head-on. Before collision: ball A moves right at 3 m/s, ball B moves left at 3 m/s. After collision: ball A moves left at 3 m/s, ball B moves right at 3 m/s. What type of collision is this?

## Options
A) Perfectly inelastic
B) Inelastic (but not perfectly inelastic)
C) Elastic
D) Cannot be determined

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Check momentum: p_before = m(3) + m(-3) = 0. p_after = m(-3) + m(3) = 0. Momentum conserved ✓. Check KE: KE_before = ½m(9) + ½m(9) = 9m. KE_after = ½m(9) + ½m(9) = 9m. KE conserved ✓. Since both momentum and kinetic energy are conserved, this is an elastic collision. The balls simply exchanged velocities — a hallmark of elastic collisions between identical masses. Option A (perfectly inelastic) would mean they stick together. Option B means KE is lost.
