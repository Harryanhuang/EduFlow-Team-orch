A block slides down a rough incline at constant speed. Which energy bar chart correctly represents this process?

## Options
A) KE stays the same, PE decreases, no thermal energy.
B) KE stays the same, PE decreases, thermal energy increases by the same amount.
C) KE decreases, PE decreases, thermal energy increases.
D) KE increases, PE decreases, total energy increases.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
At constant speed: KE is constant (v doesn't change). As the block descends: PE decreases (height drops). The lost PE doesn't become KE (speed is constant) — it becomes thermal energy due to friction. So the bar chart shows: KE same, PE decreases, thermal energy (E_th) increases by the same amount as the PE decrease. Total energy is conserved (initial PE = final PE + E_th). Option A misses the thermal energy. Option C says KE decreases, contradicting "constant speed." Option D violates energy conservation.
