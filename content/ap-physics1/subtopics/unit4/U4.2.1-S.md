A 1.0 kg ball sits on the floor of a train moving at 20 m/s. What is the ball's kinetic energy as measured by (1) a passenger on the train and (2) a person standing on the ground?

## Options
A) (1) 200 J, (2) 200 J
B) (1) 0 J, (2) 200 J
C) (1) 0 J, (2) 0 J
D) (1) 200 J, (2) 0 J

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For the passenger on the train: the ball is at rest relative to the train, so v = 0 and KE = ½(1.0)(0)² = 0 J. For the person on the ground: the ball moves at 20 m/s with the train, so KE = ½(1.0)(20)² = 200 J. The same object has different kinetic energies in different reference frames — this is because KE depends on velocity, and velocity is frame-dependent. Neither measurement is "wrong"; they simply describe the ball's energy in different frames.
