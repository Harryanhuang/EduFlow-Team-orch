A 1.0 kg ball moving at 4.0 m/s collides with a stationary 3.0 kg ball. They stick together. What fraction of the initial kinetic energy is lost?

## Options
A) 1/4
B) 1/2
C) 3/4
D) All of it

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Momentum conservation: (1.0)(4.0) + (3.0)(0) = (1.0+3.0)v_f. So v_f = 4.0/4.0 = 1.0 m/s. Initial KE: ½(1.0)(16) = 8.0 J. Final KE: ½(4.0)(1.0)² = 2.0 J. Energy lost: 8.0 - 2.0 = 6.0 J. Fraction lost: 6.0/8.0 = 3/4. In a perfectly inelastic collision, the maximum possible KE is lost consistent with momentum conservation. The fraction lost is m_target/(m_projectile + m_target) = 3/(1+3) = 3/4. This general result shows heavy targets cause more energy loss.
