A power plant generates electricity with 40% efficiency. The transmission lines deliver power with 90% efficiency. An electric motor converts electricity to mechanical work with 75% efficiency. What is the overall efficiency from fuel to mechanical work?

## Options
A) 68%
B) 30%
C) 27%
D) 205%

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Overall efficiency: η = η₁ × η₂ × η₃ = 0.40 × 0.90 × 0.75 = 0.27 = 27%. Each stage loses energy: 60% at the plant, 10% in transmission, 25% in the motor. The cumulative effect means only 27% of the original fuel energy reaches the final mechanical output. Option A (68%) adds the efficiencies. Option B (30%) uses only two stages. Option D (205%) adds them incorrectly. This multiplicative cascade explains why energy efficiency improvements at each stage are so important.
