A 1.0 kg block slides at 4.0 m/s on a frictionless surface, then hits a rough section (μ_k = 0.20, length 5.0 m). What is its speed after the rough section? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 0 m/s (the block stops within the rough section)
B) 2.0 m/s
C) √6 ≈ 2.4 m/s
D) 3.0 m/s

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Initial KE: ½mv² = ½(1.0)(4.0)² = 8.0 J. Friction force: f_k = μ_k mg = 0.20 × 1.0 × 10 = 2.0 N. Maximum energy friction can remove over the full 5.0 m: W_max = f_k × 5.0 = 10 J. Since 10 J > 8.0 J (the available KE), the block stops before traversing the entire rough section. Stopping distance: d = KE/f_k = 8.0/2.0 = 4.0 m, which is less than 5.0 m. The block stops at 4.0 m into the rough section.
