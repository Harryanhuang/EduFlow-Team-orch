A 2.0 kg block is launched at 5.0 m/s across a rough horizontal surface (μ_k = 0.25). Using energy methods, how far does the block slide before stopping? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 2.5 m
B) 5.0 m
C) 10 m
D) 1.25 m

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The system is block + surface (open system with friction). Initial energy: KE_i = ½mv² = ½ × 2.0 × 25 = 25 J. Final energy: KE_f = 0 (stopped). Energy lost to friction: W_f = f_k × d = μ_k mg × d = 0.25 × 2.0 × 10 × d = 5d. Setting KE_i - W_f = KE_f: 25 - 5d = 0. So d = 5.0 m. Option A forgets the mass in the friction calculation. Option C forgets the μ_k. Option D uses v²/(2μ_k g) but divides incorrectly. The energy approach cleanly avoids needing to find acceleration first.
