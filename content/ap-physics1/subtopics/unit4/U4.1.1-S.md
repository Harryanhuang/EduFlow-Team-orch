A block slides down a frictionless incline. If the system is defined as "block only" (not including Earth), which statement about energy is correct?

## Options
A) Mechanical energy is conserved because there is no friction.
B) Gravitational potential energy is part of the system's energy.
C) Gravity does work on the block, increasing its kinetic energy.
D) The normal force does positive work on the block.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
With "block only" as the system, gravity is an external force. There is no gravitational PE in this system (PE belongs to the block-Earth system, not the block alone). Gravity does positive work on the block as it descends, converting to kinetic energy. Option A is wrong — without PE in the system, "mechanical energy" isn't well-defined for the block alone; we should say work-energy theorem applies. Option B is wrong — PE doesn't belong to the block-only system. Option D is wrong — the normal force is perpendicular to motion, doing zero work.
