Two ice skaters at rest push off each other on frictionless ice. Skater A (50 kg) moves left at 2 m/s. Skater B (75 kg) moves right. What is the ratio of their kinetic energies KE_A/KE_B?

## Options
A) 2/3
B) 3/2
C) 1
D) 4/9

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Momentum conservation (initial p = 0): m_A v_A = m_B v_B (magnitudes). 50 × 2 = 75 × v_B, so v_B = 100/75 = 4/3 m/s. KE_A = ½(50)(4) = 100 J. KE_B = ½(75)(16/9) = ½(75)(4/3)² = ½ × 75 × 16/9 = 600/9 ≈ 66.7 J. Ratio: KE_A/KE_B = 100/(200/3) = 300/200 = 3/2. The lighter skater has more kinetic energy because, with equal momentum magnitudes, KE = p²/(2m) is inversely proportional to mass. Option A (2/3) inverts the ratio. Option C (1) assumes equal KE. Option D (4/9) uses velocity ratio instead of energy.
