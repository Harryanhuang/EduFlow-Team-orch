A ball falls freely in a vacuum (no air resistance). If the system is defined as "ball + Earth," which statement is correct?

## Options
A) The total mechanical energy of the system increases as the ball falls.
B) The total mechanical energy of the system remains constant.
C) The gravitational potential energy of the ball alone is conserved.
D) The kinetic energy of the Earth changes significantly.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
With "ball + Earth" as the system, gravity is an internal force. No external forces do work, so the system is effectively closed and total mechanical energy is conserved: as the ball falls, gravitational PE converts to KE of the ball, but the sum KE + PE stays constant. Option A is wrong — energy doesn't increase. Option C is wrong — PE belongs to the ball-Earth system, not the ball alone. Option D is wrong — Earth's KE change is negligible due to its enormous mass (momentum is conserved, but Earth's velocity change is essentially zero).
