A spring (k = 200 N/m) is compressed 0.20 m and launches a 1.0 kg block across a frictionless surface. The block then enters a rough section (μ_k = 0.30). How far does the block travel in the rough section before stopping? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 0.67 m
B) 1.3 m
C) 2.0 m
D) 4.0 m

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Stage 1 (spring launch): PE_spring = ½kx² = ½(200)(0.04) = 4.0 J. This becomes KE on the frictionless surface: KE = 4.0 J. Stage 2 (rough section): friction does negative work until the block stops. W_fric = -μ_k mgd = -(0.30)(1.0)(10)d = -3d. Setting KE + W_fric = 0: 4.0 - 3d = 0. So d = 4.0/3 ≈ 1.3 m. The spring's stored energy is entirely dissipated by friction. Option A (0.67 m) forgets to square the compression (uses kx instead of ½kx²). Option C (2.0 m) uses the wrong friction force. Option D (4.0 m) uses W_fric = μ_k mg without the mass or uses incorrect values.
