A particle moves in a potential energy field given by PE(x) = ax² (a > 0). At x = 0, what is the nature of equilibrium and the force direction for x > 0?

## Options
A) Unstable equilibrium; force points in +x direction
B) Stable equilibrium; force points in -x direction (toward x = 0)
C) Neutral equilibrium; no force anywhere
D) Stable equilibrium; force points in +x direction (away from x = 0)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
PE(x) = ax² is a parabola opening upward with minimum at x = 0. At the minimum, dPE/dx = 0, so F = -dPE/dx = 0 — this is equilibrium. Since PE has a minimum here, it's a stable equilibrium (displacing the particle raises PE, and the force pushes it back). For x > 0: F = -dPE/dx = -2ax < 0, pointing in the -x direction (toward x = 0, the equilibrium). This is analogous to a mass on a spring (PE = ½kx²). Option A describes a maximum (unstable). Option D has the force direction wrong (force pushes TOWARD equilibrium, not away).
