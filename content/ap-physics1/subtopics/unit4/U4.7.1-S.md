A compressed spring between two carts (m₁ = 1 kg, m₂ = 3 kg) on a frictionless track is released. The spring has 24 J of potential energy. What is the kinetic energy of the lighter cart?

## Options
A) 6 J
B) 8 J
C) 18 J
D) 24 J

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Momentum conservation (initial p = 0): m₁v₁ = m₂v₂ → v₂ = v₁/3 (since m₂ = 3m₁). Energy conservation: ½kx² = ½m₁v₁² + ½m₂v₂² = ½(1)v₁² + ½(3)(v₁/3)² = ½v₁² + ½(3)(v₁²/9) = ½v₁² + v₁²/6 = ½v₁²(1 + 1/3) = ½v₁²(4/3). So 24 = (2/3)v₁² → v₁² = 36 → v₁ = 6 m/s. KE₁ = ½(1)(36) = 18 J. KE₂ = 24 - 18 = 6 J. The lighter cart gets 75% of the energy because, with equal momentum, KE = p²/(2m) is inversely proportional to mass. Ratio: KE₁/KE₂ = m₂/m₁ = 3/1, so KE₁ = ¾ × 24 = 18 J.
