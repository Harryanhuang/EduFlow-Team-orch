A crane lifts a heavy load at constant power P. As the load accelerates from rest, how do the tension T in the cable and the acceleration a change over time?

## Options
A) T stays constant; a stays constant.
B) T decreases; a decreases.
C) T increases; a increases.
D) T stays constant; a decreases.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
At constant power: P = Tv (tension × velocity). As the load accelerates from rest, v increases. Since P is constant and v increases, T = P/v must decrease. By Newton's Second Law: T - mg = ma, so a = (T - mg)/m. As T decreases (approaching mg from above), the net force T - mg decreases, and therefore a decreases. The load's acceleration approaches zero as the speed approaches the terminal speed v_max = P/(mg). This is why vehicles at constant power don't accelerate uniformly — the acceleration decreases as speed builds up.
