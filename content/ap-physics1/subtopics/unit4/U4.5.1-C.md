A satellite orbits Earth in a circular orbit. Over one complete orbit, what is the total work done by Earth's gravitational force on the satellite?

## Options
A) Positive work (gravity pulls the satellite inward)
B) Negative work (gravity opposes the motion)
C) Zero work
D) Cannot be determined without knowing the orbit radius

## Answer
C

## Explanation
In a circular orbit, gravity always points toward Earth's center, which is perpendicular to the satellite's velocity (tangent to the orbit). Since the force is perpendicular to displacement at every instant: dW = F·ds = F ds cos90° = 0. Over any portion of a circular orbit, gravity does zero work. This is consistent with constant speed (KE doesn't change, so W_net = ΔKE = 0). In an elliptical orbit, gravity does positive work as the satellite approaches (speeds up) and negative work as it recedes (slows down), with zero net work over a complete orbit.
