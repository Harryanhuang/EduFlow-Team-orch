Car A has mass m and speed v. Car B has mass 2m and speed v/2. Which car has greater kinetic energy?

## Options
A) Car A
B) Car B
C) They have the same kinetic energy.
D) Cannot be determined without numerical values.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
KE_A = ½mv². KE_B = ½(2m)(v/2)² = ½(2m)(v²/4) = ½mv²/2 = ¼mv². So KE_A/KE_B = (½mv²)/(¼mv²) = 2. Car A has twice the kinetic energy of Car B. Doubling the mass increases KE by 2×, but halving the speed decreases KE by 4× (since KE ∝ v²). The net effect is KE_B = 2/4 × KE_A = ½ KE_A. Car A wins.
