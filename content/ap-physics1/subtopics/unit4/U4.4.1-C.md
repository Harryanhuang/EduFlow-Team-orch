A vertical spring (k = 200 N/m) supports a 2.0 kg mass at equilibrium. The mass is pulled down 0.10 m from equilibrium and released. What is the maximum speed of the mass? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 0.5 m/s
B) 1.0 m/s
C) 1.4 m/s
D) 2.0 m/s

## Answer
B

## Explanation
At equilibrium, the spring is already stretched by x₀ = mg/k = 20/200 = 0.10 m. When pulled down an additional 0.10 m and released, the mass oscillates about the equilibrium. Using energy conservation about the equilibrium position: at maximum displacement A = 0.10 m, all energy is "spring PE relative to equilibrium" = ½kA². At equilibrium, all energy is KE: ½mv²_max = ½kA². So v_max = A√(k/m) = 0.10 × √(200/2.0) = 0.10 × √100 = 0.10 × 10 = 1.0 m/s. Note: gravity doesn't affect the oscillation about equilibrium — it just shifts the equilibrium point. The maximum speed depends only on k, m, and the amplitude measured from equilibrium.
