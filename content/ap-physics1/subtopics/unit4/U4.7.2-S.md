A 10 g bullet is fired into a 990 g wooden block at rest. The bullet embeds in the block, and they swing up to a height of 0.20 m. What was the bullet's initial speed? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 20 m/s
B) 100 m/s
C) 200 m/s
D) 2000 m/s

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Stage 1 (collision — momentum only): m_bullet × v₀ = (m_bullet + m_block) × V. 0.01v₀ = 1.0V → v₀ = 100V. Stage 2 (swing — energy conservation): ½(M)V² = (M)gh → V = √(2gh) = √(2 × 10 × 0.20) = √4 = 2.0 m/s. So v₀ = 100 × 2.0 = 200 m/s. The key: you CANNOT use energy conservation across the collision (it's inelastic — KE is lost). Energy conservation only applies during the swing (after the collision). Option A uses v₀ = V (ignores mass ratio). Option B (100 m/s) uses V = 1 m/s. Option D (2000 m/s) forgets to convert grams to kg.
