A person of mass m stands at one end of a boat of mass M on still water (no water resistance). The person walks to the other end. When both the person and boat are moving, what fraction of the total kinetic energy belongs to the boat?

## Options
A) m/(m+M)
B) M/(m+M)
C) m/M
D) M/m

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Momentum conservation (initial p = 0): mv_person = Mv_boat (magnitudes). So v_boat = mv_person/M. KE_person = ½mv_person². KE_boat = ½Mv_boat² = ½M(mv_person/M)² = ½m²v_person²/M. Total KE = ½mv_person² + ½m²v_person²/M = ½mv_person²(1 + m/M) = ½mv_person²(M+m)/M. Fraction belonging to boat: KE_boat/KE_total = [½m²v_person²/M]/[½mv_person²(M+m)/M] = m/(M+m). The lighter object always gets the larger fraction of KE (since KE = p²/(2m) for equal momenta). If the person is lighter than the boat (m < M), the person gets more KE.
