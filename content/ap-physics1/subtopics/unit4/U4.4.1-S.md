A block slides from rest down a frictionless curved ramp from height h. What is its speed at the bottom?

## Options
A) v = √(gh)
B) v = √(2gh)
C) v = 2gh
D) v = gh

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Mechanical energy conservation (frictionless; normal force does no work): KE_i + PE_i = KE_f + PE_f. Starting from rest: 0 + mgh = ½mv² + 0. Solving: v² = 2gh, v = √(2gh). The shape of the ramp doesn't matter — only the height difference determines the final speed. This is because gravity is conservative: the work done by gravity depends only on the vertical displacement, not the path. Option A (√gh) misses the factor of 2. Option C (2gh) has wrong units. Option D (gh) also has wrong units.
