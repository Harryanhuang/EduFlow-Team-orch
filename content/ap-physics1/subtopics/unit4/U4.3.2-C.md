A comet has total mechanical energy E = +5 × 10¹⁰ J relative to the Sun. What can be concluded about the comet's orbit?

## Options
A) The comet is in a circular orbit around the Sun.
B) The comet is in an elliptical orbit around the Sun.
C) The comet will escape the Sun's gravity and never return.
D) The comet is at rest relative to the Sun.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Total energy E = KE + PE. For gravitational systems: E < 0 → bound (elliptical/circular orbit). E = 0 → parabolic (just escapes). E > 0 → hyperbolic (escapes with leftover KE). Since E = +5 × 10¹⁰ J > 0, the comet has more than enough energy to escape. It follows a hyperbolic trajectory and will never return to the inner solar system. Option A (circular) requires E < 0. Option B (elliptical) also requires E < 0. Option D would require KE = 0, but with E > 0 and PE < 0, KE = E - PE = E + |PE| > 0, so the comet is definitely moving.
