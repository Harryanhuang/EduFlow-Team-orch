Two identical spheres of mass m are separated by a center-to-center distance d. The gravitational force between them is F. If the distance is doubled to 2d, what is the new gravitational force?

## Options
A) F/4
B) F/2
C) 2F
D) 4F

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation: F = Gm₁m₂/r² = Gm²/d². When distance becomes 2d: F' = Gm²/(2d)² = Gm²/(4d²) = F/4. The gravitational force follows an inverse-square law — doubling the distance reduces the force by a factor of 4. The force is always attractive, directed along the line connecting the centers of the two masses.
