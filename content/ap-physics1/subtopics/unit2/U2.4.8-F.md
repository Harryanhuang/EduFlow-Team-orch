A spring has a spring constant k = 200 N/m. The spring is stretched 0.15 m from its equilibrium position. What is the magnitude of the restoring force exerted by the spring?

## Options
A) 3.0 N
B) 15 N
C) 30 N
D) 1333 N

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Hooke's Law: F_s = kx (magnitude only, ignoring the sign). F = 200 N/m × 0.15 m = 30 N. The restoring force is directed toward the equilibrium position (opposite to the stretch). Option A comes from 200 × 0.15/10 = 3 (incorrect arithmetic). Option B uses 0.15 × 100 = 15 (wrong k). Option D divides instead of multiplies: 200/0.15 ≈ 1333. Key: Hooke's Law gives a force proportional to displacement, and the spring constant k tells you how "stiff" the spring is.
