Two identical springs, each with spring constant k, are connected in parallel. A force F is applied to stretch the combination. What is the effective spring constant of the system?

## Options
A) k/2
B) k
C) 2k
D) k²

## Answer
C

## Explanation
In parallel, both springs stretch by the same amount x. Each spring exerts force F₁ = kx and F₂ = kx. Total force: F = F₁ + F₂ = 2kx. By definition F = k_eq × x, so k_eq = 2k. Parallel springs are stiffer because both contribute to the restoring force. For comparison: in series, the same force stretches each spring, and total stretch is doubled, giving k_eq = k/2. The parallel combination is always stiffer than individual springs.
