Planet A has mass M and radius R. Planet B has mass 4M and radius 2R. What is the ratio of surface gravity g_B/g_A?

## Options
A) 1/2
B) 1
C) 2
D) 4

## Answer
B

## Explanation
g = GM/R². For Planet A: g_A = GM/R². For Planet B: g_B = G(4M)/(2R)² = 4GM/(4R²) = GM/R² = g_A. So g_B/g_A = 1. Although Planet B is more massive, its larger radius exactly compensates due to the inverse-square dependence. This is a key insight in proportional reasoning with the inverse-square law: doubling both mass (×4 effect) and radius (÷4 effect) gives the same surface gravity.
