Three horizontal forces act on a box on a frictionless surface: F₁ = 30 N to the right, F₂ = 40 N upward (perpendicular to F₁), and F₃ = 30 N to the left. What is the magnitude of the net force on the box?

## Options
A) 30 N
B) 40 N
C) 70 N
D) 100 N

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Resolving into components: F_x = 30 N (right) + (-30 N) (left) = 0 N. F_y = 40 N (up). Since F₁ and F₃ cancel exactly, the net force equals F₂ = 40 N upward. If someone incorrectly adds all forces as scalars: 30 + 40 + 30 = 100 N (option D — wrong). If they subtract magnitudes: |30 - 40 + 30| = 20 N (not an option). If they compute √(30² + 40²) = 50 N for two forces but forget the third cancels... The key is to always decompose forces into components before summing, not add magnitudes directly.
