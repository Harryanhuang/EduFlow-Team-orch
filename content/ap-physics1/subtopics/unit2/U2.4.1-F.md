Two ice skaters stand on frictionless ice and push against each other. If the system is defined as both skaters together, which of the following is an external force on the system?

## Options
A) The force skater A exerts on skater B
B) The force skater B exerts on skater A
C) The gravitational force on each skater
D) The force the skaters exert on each other

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The gravitational force on each skater is exerted by the Earth, which is outside the defined system (both skaters). This makes gravity an external force. The forces skaters A and B exert on each other (options A, B, D) are internal forces — they are action-reaction pairs within the system. Internal forces cancel in pairs (Newton's Third Law) and cannot change the total momentum of the system. Only external forces can alter the system's center-of-mass motion.
