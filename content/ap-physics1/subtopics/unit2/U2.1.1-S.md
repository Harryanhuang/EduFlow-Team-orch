A skydiver falls at terminal velocity. Which statement best describes the forces involved?

## Options
A) Gravity is stronger than air resistance, producing downward acceleration.
B) Air resistance exceeds gravity, producing upward deceleration.
C) Gravity and air resistance are equal in magnitude, producing zero acceleration — consistent with Newton's First Law.
D) There is no net force, but the skydiver still accelerates due to gravity.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Terminal velocity means constant velocity (zero acceleration). By Newton's First Law, the net force must be zero. Gravity (downward) and air resistance (upward) are equal in magnitude. Option C is correct. Students often think terminal velocity still involves acceleration — it does not; acceleration is zero once terminal velocity is reached.
