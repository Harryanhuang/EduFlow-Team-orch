A car accelerates forward on a flat road. Which pair of forces is NOT a Newton's Third Law pair?

## Options
A) The tire pushes backward on the road; the road pushes forward on the tire.
B) The car's weight (Earth pulls car down) and the car pulls Earth up.
C) The tire pushes backward on the road; the road pushes forward on the car.
D) The normal force on the car (road pushes up) and the car pushes down on the road.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Third Law pairs always involve the same two objects in a single interaction. In which scenario are the two mentioned forces NOT a Newton's Third Law pair?

## Options
A) A bat hits a ball: the force of the bat on the ball and the force of the ball on the bat.
B) A book sits on a table: the gravitational force on the book and the normal force on the book.
C) A rocket fires: the force of the exhaust on the rocket and the force of the rocket on the exhaust.
D) Two magnets attract: the force of magnet A on magnet B and the force of magnet B on magnet A.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Option B: gravity on the book and normal force on the book — both act on the same object and are different types of forces (gravitational vs. contact). They are NOT a Third Law pair. Third Law pairs must act on different objects and arise from the same interaction. Options A, C, D all describe equal-and-opposite forces from a single interaction acting on the two different objects involved.
