An astronaut measures the weight of a 2.0 kg object at two positions on a planet. On the surface, the weight is 58.8 N. At height h above the surface, the weight is 14.7 N. What is h in terms of the planet's radius R?

## Options
A) h = R
B) h = 2R
C) h = 3R
D) h = 4R

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Surface gravity: g₀ = W₀/m = 58.8/2.0 = 29.4 m/s². At height h: g(h) = W(h)/m = 14.7/2.0 = 7.35 m/s². Using the inverse-square law: g(h)/g₀ = R²/(R+h)². Substituting: 7.35/29.4 = R²/(R+h)². Simplifying: 1/4 = R²/(R+h)². Taking the square root: 1/2 = R/(R+h). Solving: R+h = 2R, so h = R. The gravitational field (and weight) drops to one-quarter when the distance from the planet's center doubles. Option B gives (R/(3R))² = 1/9 (too much reduction). Option C gives 1/16. Option D gives 1/25.
