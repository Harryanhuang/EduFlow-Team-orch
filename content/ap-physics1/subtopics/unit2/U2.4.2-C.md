Planet X has twice the mass of Earth and three times the radius of Earth. A 10-kg object weighs 98 N on Earth. What does this object weigh on Planet X?

## Options
A) 98 N
B) 21.8 N
C) 196 N
D) 9.7 N

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Surface gravity g = GM/R². For Planet X: g_X = G(2M_E)/(3R_E)² = 2GM_E/(9R_E²) = (2/9)g_E. Since g_E ≈ 9.8 m/s², g_X = (2/9)(9.8) ≈ 2.18 m/s². Weight on Planet X = mg_X = 10 kg × 2.18 m/s² ≈ 21.8 N. The mass of the object (10 kg) is the same everywhere. Option A assumes g is unchanged. Option C only accounts for the mass doubling. Option D divides by 10 instead of computing 2/9 correctly. Key insight: both mass and radius of the planet affect surface g.
