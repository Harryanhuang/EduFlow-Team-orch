A point mass M creates a gravitational field in the space around it. At a distance r from M, the gravitational field strength is g₀. What is the gravitational field strength at a distance 3r from M?

## Options
A) g₀/9
B) g₀/3
C) 3g₀
D) 9g₀

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Gravitational field strength g = GM/r². At distance r: g₀ = GM/r². At distance 3r: g' = GM/(3r)² = GM/(9r²) = g₀/9. The gravitational field follows an inverse-square law, just like the gravitational force. The field strength is independent of any test mass placed in it — it is a property of the space around the source mass M. Tripling the distance reduces the field strength by a factor of 9.
