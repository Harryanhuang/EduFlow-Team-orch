A 4 kg block sits on a frictionless 30° incline. It is connected by a light string over a frictionless pulley to a hanging mass m. What value of m will keep the system in equilibrium?

## Options
A) 2.0 kg
B) 3.5 kg
C) 4.0 kg
D) 3.46 kg

## Answer
A

## Explanation
For equilibrium (a = 0), the tension must balance both sides. For the block on the incline: T = M g sinθ = 4 × 10 × sin30° = 4 × 10 × 0.5 = 20 N (component of gravity along the incline, since no friction). For the hanging mass: T = mg. Setting equal: mg = 20, so m = 20/10 = 2.0 kg. The hanging mass needs to be 2.0 kg to exactly balance the incline component of the 4 kg block's weight. Option B (3.5 kg) would accelerate the block up the incline. Option C (4.0 kg) ignores the sin30° decomposition. Option D (3.46 kg) uses cos30° instead of sin30° (incorrect component). Key: on a frictionless incline, only the parallel component of gravity (mg sinθ) matters for the string tension balance.
