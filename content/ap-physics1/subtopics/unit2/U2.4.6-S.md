A 10 kg box is on a horizontal floor. A force F = 50 N is applied at 30° below the horizontal (pushing down and forward). The box slides horizontally. What is the normal force on the box? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 100 N
B) 75 N
C) 125 N
D) 150 N

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The applied force has a vertical component pointing downward: F_y = F sin30° = 50 × 0.5 = 25 N (downward). The weight is mg = 100 N (downward). In the vertical direction, the box doesn't accelerate: N - mg - F sin30° = 0, so N = mg + F sin30° = 100 + 25 = 125 N. The downward component of the applied force presses the box harder into the floor, increasing the normal force beyond just the weight. Option A ignores the applied force's vertical component. Option B subtracts instead of adds. Option D incorrectly uses F directly instead of its vertical component.
