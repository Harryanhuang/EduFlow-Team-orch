An astronaut takes both a beam balance (天平) and a spring scale (弹簧秤) to the Moon. On Earth, the balance reads 5.0 kg and the spring scale reads 49 N for a rock. On the Moon (g = 1.6 m/s²), what will each instrument read?

## Options
A) Balance: 5.0 kg, Spring scale: 49 N
B) Balance: 5.0 kg, Spring scale: 8.0 N
C) Balance: 0.82 kg, Spring scale: 8.0 N
D) Balance: 0.82 kg, Spring scale: 49 N

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The beam balance compares masses using torque equilibrium. Since both the standard masses and the rock are affected equally by the reduced gravity, the balance reading remains 5.0 kg. The spring scale measures the gravitational force (weight): W = mg = 5.0 kg × 1.6 m/s² = 8.0 N. On Earth it read 49 N (5.0 × 9.8). The spring scale reading changes because the weight changes, but the mass (measured by the balance) stays the same.
