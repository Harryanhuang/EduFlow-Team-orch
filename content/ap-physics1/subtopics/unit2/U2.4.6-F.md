A 5.0 kg box rests on a frictionless incline angled at 30° above the horizontal. What is the magnitude of the normal force on the box? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 50 N
B) 43 N
C) 25 N
D) 10 N

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The normal force is perpendicular to the incline surface. Resolving gravity into components: perpendicular to the incline, mg cosθ acts into the surface. Since the box doesn't accelerate perpendicular to the surface: N = mg cosθ = 5.0 × 10 × cos30° = 50 × 0.866 ≈ 43 N. Option A (50 N) would be correct only on a horizontal surface (θ = 0°). Option C (25 N) = mg sin30° is the component parallel to the incline, not the normal force. The key is that on an incline, N < mg because only the perpendicular component of gravity needs to be balanced.
