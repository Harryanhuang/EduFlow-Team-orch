A 50 kg skater pushes a 70 kg skater and they move apart. During the push, which statement is correct?

## Options
A) The 70 kg skater pushes back with less force because of greater inertia.
B) Both skaters experience the same magnitude of force.
C) The 50 kg skater accelerates more because she exerts a larger force.
D) The force on the 50 kg skater is greater because she initiates the push.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By Newton's Third Law, the forces the two skaters exert on each other are equal in magnitude and opposite in direction. Both experience the same |F|. The 50 kg skater accelerates more because a = F/m and her mass is smaller. The push initiator does not exert more force — Third Law guarantees equality regardless. Option B is correct.
