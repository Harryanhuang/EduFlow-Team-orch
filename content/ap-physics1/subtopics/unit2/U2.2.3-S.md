A 4 kg block slides down a frictionless 37° incline. What are the acceleration and the normal force? (sin 37° ≈ 0.6, cos 37° ≈ 0.8, g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) a = 6 m/s², N = 32 N
B) a = 8 m/s², N = 32 N
C) a = 6 m/s², N = 40 N
D) a = 10 m/s², N = 0 N

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Acceleration: a = g sin 37° = 10 × 0.6 = 6 m/s². Normal force: N = mg cos 37° = 4 × 10 × 0.8 = 32 N. Option A is correct. Note: N ≠ mg on an incline — students often make this mistake.
