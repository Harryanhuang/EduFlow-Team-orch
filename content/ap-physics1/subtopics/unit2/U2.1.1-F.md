A ball rolls at constant velocity across a frictionless horizontal surface. What can be concluded about the net force on the ball?

## Options
A) There must be a forward force keeping the ball moving.
B) The net force is zero, consistent with Newton's First Law.
C) The net force equals the ball's weight.
D) Gravity is balanced by a small forward force.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Newton's First Law states that an object in motion maintains constant velocity when the net force is zero. On a frictionless surface with no applied force, the ball coasts at constant velocity because no net force acts on it. Option B is correct. A common misconception is that a force is needed to "keep it moving" — that is the Aristotelian view, not Newtonian physics.
