A book sits on a table. Which forces form a Newton's Third Law pair?

## Options
A) Gravity on the book and the normal force on the book.
B) Gravity on the book and gravity on the Earth.
C) The normal force on the book and the weight of the table.
D) Gravity on the book and the normal force on the table.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Newton's Third Law pairs act on different objects. The book pulls the Earth upward (gravity pair): "book on Earth" and "Earth on book." Option A describes two forces on the same book — not a Third Law pair. Option B correctly identifies the action-reaction pair: gravity of book on Earth and gravity of Earth on book. Option D also describes forces on different objects, but they are not the same type of interaction.
