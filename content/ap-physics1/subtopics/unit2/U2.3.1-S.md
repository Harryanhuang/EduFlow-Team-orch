A 10 kg block is on a 25° incline (μ_s = 0.5). What is the minimum horizontal force F (pushing into the incline) needed to prevent the block from sliding down? (sin 25° ≈ 0.42, cos 25° ≈ 0.91, g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 0 N (friction alone holds it)
B) About 2.2 N
C) About 8.8 N
D) About 42 N

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Component down the slope: mg sin 25° = 10(10)(0.42) = 42 N. Maximum static friction: μ_s mg cos 25° = 0.5(10)(10)(0.91) = 45.5 N. Since f_s_max = 45.5 N > 42 N = mg sin θ, static friction alone can hold the block without any applied force. Option A is correct.
