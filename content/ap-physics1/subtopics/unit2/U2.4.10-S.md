In an Atwood machine with m₁ = 2 kg and m₂ = 6 kg, what is the tension in the string? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 15 N
B) 20 N
C) 30 N
D) 40 N

## Answer
C

## Explanation
First find acceleration: a = (m₂-m₁)g/(m₁+m₂) = (6-2)×10/(2+6) = 40/8 = 5 m/s². For m₁ (accelerating upward): T - m₁g = m₁a → T = m₁(g+a) = 2×(10+5) = 30 N. Verification using m₂: m₂g - T = m₂a → T = m₂(g-a) = 6×(10-5) = 30 N. Both equations give T = 30 N ✓. The tension is between the two weights: m₁g = 20 N < T = 30 N < m₂g = 60 N, which makes sense — the tension must be greater than the lighter weight (to accelerate it up) and less than the heavier weight (to allow it to accelerate down).
