A box slides to the right across a rough horizontal surface and gradually slows down. Which of the following forces does NOT act on the box?

## Options
A) A friction force directed to the left
B) A gravitational force directed downward
C) A normal force directed upward
D) A force of motion directed to the right

## Answer
D

## Explanation
The forces on the sliding box are: gravity (downward), normal force (upward), and kinetic friction (opposing motion, so to the left since the box moves right). There is no "force of motion" — motion does not require a force to maintain it (Newton's First Law). A common misconception is that a moving object must have a force pushing it in the direction of motion. In reality, the box slows down precisely because friction is the only horizontal force and it acts opposite to the motion. The net force is to the left.
