A satellite orbits Earth at a height h above the surface. If Earth has radius R and surface gravity g₀, what is the gravitational acceleration at the satellite's position?

## Options
A) g₀(R/(R+h))²
B) g₀(R+h)/R
C) g₀ · R/(R+h)
D) g₀((R+h)/R)²

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Surface gravity: g₀ = GM/R². At distance (R+h) from Earth's center: g' = GM/(R+h)². Taking the ratio: g'/g₀ = [GM/(R+h)²]/[GM/R²] = R²/(R+h)² = (R/(R+h))². Therefore g' = g₀(R/(R+h))². Since R/(R+h) < 1, we confirm g' < g₀ (gravity weakens with distance). Option B and C give values > g₀ (wrong). Option D inverts the ratio. Key insight: always use center-to-center distance, not surface distance.
