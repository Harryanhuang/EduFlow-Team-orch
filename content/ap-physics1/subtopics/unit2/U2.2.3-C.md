Two frictionless inclines have angles 30° and 60°. What is the ratio of the acceleration of a block sliding down the 60° incline to that on the 30° incline?

## Options
A) 1 : 1
B) √3 : 1
C) 2 : 1
D) 3 : 1

## Answer
B

## Explanation
a₁ = g sin 30° = 0.5g. a₂ = g sin 60° = (√3/2)g ≈ 0.866g. Ratio: a₂/a₁ = (√3/2)/(1/2) = √3 ≈ 1.732. Option B is correct. The ratio of angles is 60:30 = 2:1, but acceleration is not proportional to θ — it is proportional to sin θ.
