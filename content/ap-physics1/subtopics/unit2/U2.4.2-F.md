An astronaut has a mass of 80 kg on Earth. The gravitational acceleration on the Moon is approximately 1.6 m/s². What is the astronaut's weight on the Moon?

## Options
A) 80 kg
B) 128 N
C) 784 N
D) 48 N

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Weight = mg. The astronaut's mass is 80 kg everywhere (mass is an intrinsic property). On the Moon: W = 80 kg × 1.6 m/s² = 128 N. Option A incorrectly gives mass in kg as the weight. Option C (784 N) is the weight on Earth (80 × 9.8). Option D incorrectly uses g = 0.6 m/s² instead of 1.6 m/s². The key distinction: mass is constant (80 kg everywhere), but weight changes with gravitational acceleration.
