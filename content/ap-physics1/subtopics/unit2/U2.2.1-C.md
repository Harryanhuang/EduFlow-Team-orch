A book is pressed against a vertical wall by a horizontal force. Which forces act on the book?

## Options
A) Gravity (down), normal force (toward wall), applied force (away from wall).
B) Gravity (down), normal force (away from wall), applied force (into wall), static friction (up).
C) Gravity (down), normal force (away from wall), static friction (up).
D) Gravity (down), applied force (into wall), kinetic friction (up).

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Four forces: (1) gravity mg downward, (2) applied force pushing the book into the wall (horizontal, toward wall), (3) normal force from the wall pushing outward (horizontal, away from wall), (4) static friction acting upward to prevent the book from sliding down (opposes intended motion). On a vertical surface, the normal force is horizontal, not vertical — it does NOT equal mg. Option B is correct.
