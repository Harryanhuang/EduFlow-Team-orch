Three blocks of masses m₁ = 2 kg, m₂ = 3 kg, and m₃ = 5 kg are stacked vertically on the floor (m₃ on bottom, m₂ in middle, m₁ on top). The system is at rest. What is the normal force that the floor exerts on m₃? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 50 N
B) 80 N
C) 100 N
D) 150 N

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The floor must support all three blocks. Total weight = (m₁ + m₂ + m₃)g = (2 + 3 + 5) × 10 = 100 N. Using the system approach: treating all three blocks as one system, the only forces are gravity (100 N down) and the floor's normal force (N up). Since the system is at rest: N = 100 N. If asked for the normal force between m₃ and m₂ (the middle block), we would isolate m₁ and m₂ together: N_floor_on_m3 supports everything above, so between m₃ and m₂: N = (m₁ + m₂)g = 50 N. Between m₂ and m₁: N = m₁g = 20 N. The floor bears the full weight of the stack.
