A block of mass m rests on a frictionless incline of angle θ. A horizontal force F is applied to the block. The block remains at rest. What is the magnitude of the normal force N on the block?

## Options
A) N = mg cos θ
B) N = mg cos θ + F sin θ
C) N = mg + F
D) N = mg sin θ + F cos θ

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Setting up axes perpendicular and parallel to the incline. Perpendicular to the incline: N - mg cos θ - F sin θ = 0 (the block doesn't accelerate perpendicular to the surface). The component of gravity perpendicular to the incline is mg cos θ. The component of the horizontal force F perpendicular to the incline is F sin θ (since F is horizontal, its angle with the perpendicular-to-incline direction is θ). Therefore N = mg cos θ + F sin θ. Option A forgets the contribution of F. Option C incorrectly adds F directly without decomposing. Option D uses incorrect decomposition angles.
