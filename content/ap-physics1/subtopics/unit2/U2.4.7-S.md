A 60 kg person stands on a scale in an elevator. The elevator is moving upward but decelerating at 2.0 m/s². What does the scale read? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 600 N
B) 720 N
C) 480 N
D) 0 N

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The elevator moves upward but decelerates, meaning acceleration is downward: a = -2.0 m/s². Using Newton's Second Law (taking upward as positive): N - mg = ma = m(-2.0). So N = m(g - 2.0) = 60 × (10 - 2.0) = 60 × 8.0 = 480 N. The scale reads less than the actual weight (600 N) because the deceleration creates a partial "weightless" effect. Option A (600 N) would be the reading at constant velocity (a = 0). Option B (720 N) would correspond to accelerating upward at 2.0 m/s² (N = 60 × 12 = 720). Option D (0 N) would be free fall (a = -g). Key: decelerating upward = accelerating downward = apparent weight decreases.
