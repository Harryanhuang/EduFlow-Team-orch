A block sits on top of a larger block. A horizontal force F is applied to the lower block. Both blocks accelerate together without sliding. If the system is redefined from "both blocks" to "the top block only," which statement is correct?

## Options
A) The friction force between the blocks changes from an internal force to an external force on the top block.
B) The applied force F becomes an external force on the top block.
C) The normal force on the top block becomes an internal force.
D) The gravitational force on the top block is no longer an external force.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
When the system is "both blocks," the friction between them is internal — it acts between two parts of the system. When the system is redefined as "the top block only," that same friction force is now external — it is exerted by the bottom block (outside the system) on the top block (inside the system). The applied force F (B) acts on the bottom block, not the top block, so it was always external to the top-block system. The normal force (C) and gravity (D) remain external in both system definitions.
