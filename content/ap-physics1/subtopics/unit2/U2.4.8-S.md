A spring hangs vertically from the ceiling. A 0.50 kg mass is attached and the system reaches equilibrium with the spring stretched 0.10 m from its natural length. What is the spring constant k? (g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) 5.0 N/m
B) 20 N/m
C) 50 N/m
D) 100 N/m

## Answer
C

## Explanation
At equilibrium, the spring force balances gravity: kx₀ = mg. So k = mg/x₀ = (0.50 × 10)/0.10 = 5.0/0.10 = 50 N/m. The new equilibrium position is 0.10 m below the natural length. If the mass is displaced further from this equilibrium and released, it will oscillate. Option A (5.0 N/m) = mg (forgot to divide by x₀). Option B (20 N/m) = x₀/mg (inverted the ratio). Option D (100 N/m) = mg × x₀ (multiplied instead of dividing). Key: at equilibrium, the spring's upward force equals the weight downward.
