A compressed spring is placed between two carts of masses m and 2m on a frictionless track. The spring is released, pushing the carts apart. Which statement about the system of both carts is correct?

## Options
A) The center of mass of the system accelerates in the direction of the lighter cart.
B) The total kinetic energy of the system equals the elastic potential energy initially stored in the spring.
C) The final momentum of the system is nonzero because the spring exerts a net force.
D) The lighter cart has greater momentum than the heavier cart.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The spring force is internal to the system (both carts + spring). Since no external horizontal force acts on the system, the center of mass does not accelerate (A is wrong) and total momentum remains zero (C is wrong). The elastic potential energy converts entirely to kinetic energy of the two carts (B is correct). Since total momentum is zero, both carts have equal and opposite momentum magnitudes (D is wrong — the lighter cart has greater speed, not greater momentum). The key insight: internal forces redistribute kinetic energy among system components but cannot change total momentum.
