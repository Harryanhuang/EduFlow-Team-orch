A tunnel is drilled through the center of a uniform-density Earth (radius R, mass M). An object of mass m is at a distance r from the center (r < R). What is the gravitational force on the object?

## Options
A) GMm/R² (constant)
B) GMmr/R³ (proportional to r)
C) GMm/r² (inverse square)
D) Zero (gravity cancels inside Earth)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Inside a uniform sphere at radius r < R: by the shell theorem, only the mass within radius r contributes to the gravitational field (the outer shell exerts zero net field). The enclosed mass is M(r) = M(r/R)³. The field at r is g(r) = GM(r)/r² = G[M(r/R)³]/r² = GMr³/(R³r²) = GMr/R³. So F = mg(r) = GMmr/R³, proportional to r. The force is zero at the center and maximum at the surface. Option A gives the surface value (wrong inside). Option C applies the inverse-square law without accounting for reduced enclosed mass. Option D is only true inside a hollow shell, not inside a solid sphere.
