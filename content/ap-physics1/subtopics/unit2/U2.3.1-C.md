A 20 kg box sits on a rough horizontal surface (μ_s = 0.4). You pull with a 100 N force at 30° above the horizontal. Does the box move? (cos 30° ≈ 0.87, sin 30° = 0.5, g = 10 m/s²)

## Options
A) Yes, because the horizontal component (87 N) exceeds f_s_max = 80 N.
B) No, because the horizontal component (87 N) is less than the maximum static friction.
C) Yes, because pulling upward reduces the normal force, lowering f_s_max to 60 N < 87 N.
D) No, because the vertical component reduces the normal force and thus friction is still 80 N.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Pulling at 30° above horizontal: horizontal component = 100 cos 30° ≈ 87 N. Vertical component (upward) = 100 sin 30° = 50 N. Normal force: N = mg − F_y = 200 − 50 = 150 N. f_s_max = μ_s N = 0.4 × 150 = 60 N. Since 87 N > 60 N, the box moves. Pulling upward reduces N, which reduces f_s_max. Option C is correct.
