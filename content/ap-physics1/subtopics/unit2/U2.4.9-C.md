A uniform spring of spring constant k₀ is cut into two equal halves. One half is used vertically to support a mass m at rest. What is the extension of this half-spring compared to the original spring's extension under the same mass?

## Options
A) The extension is the same as the original spring.
B) The extension is half of the original spring's extension.
C) The extension is twice the original spring's extension.
D) The extension is one-quarter of the original spring's extension.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Cutting a spring in half doubles its spring constant: k_half = 2k₀ (a shorter spring is stiffer). Under the same mass m: extension of original = mg/k₀, extension of half-spring = mg/(2k₀) = (1/2)(mg/k₀). The half-spring stretches half as much because it's twice as stiff. This is a fundamental property: spring constant is inversely proportional to length for a uniform spring. Physically, each half must support the same force, but with half the coils, each coil deforms the same amount, giving half the total extension. This principle is key for understanding series springs: n identical springs in series behave like one spring of length nL with k = k₀/n.
