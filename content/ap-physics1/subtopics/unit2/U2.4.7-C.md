A roller coaster car passes over the top of a circular loop of radius R. At what minimum speed v will the passengers experience weightlessness (apparent weight = 0) at the top of the loop?

## Options
A) v = √(gR)
B) v = √(2gR)
C) v = gR
D) v = 2gR

## Answer
A

## Explanation
At the top of the loop, forces on a passenger are: mg (downward) and N (downward, from the seat). The net force provides centripetal acceleration toward the center (downward at the top): mg + N = mv²/R. For weightlessness, N = 0: mg = mv²/R, so v = √(gR). At this speed, the passengers feel completely weightless — gravity alone provides the centripetal acceleration, and no normal force is needed. If v < √(gR), the passengers would need to be restrained (the seat would need to pull them down, but seats typically can't pull). If v > √(gR), N > 0 and passengers feel pressed into the seat. This is a classic application of apparent weight in circular motion.
