## Item 1
**Tags**: chromosomes | genes | DNA | diploid
**Difficulty**: S
**Question**: How many chromosomes are in a human body cell?
**Answer**: A human body cell contains 46 chromosomes, arranged in 23 pairs. One chromosome in each pair comes from each parent. This is called diploid (2n) cells.

## Item 2
**Tags**: chromosomes | genes | DNA | diploid
**Difficulty**: F
**Question**: What is the difference between a gene and a chromosome?
**Answer**: A chromosome is a long DNA molecule carrying many genes. A gene is a short section of DNA that codes for a specific protein or characteristic. Each chromosome contains hundreds to thousands of genes.
**Explanation**: Recall that chromosomes are thread-like structures made of DNA that carry genetic information.

## Item 3
**Tags**: chromosomes | genes | DNA | diploid
**Difficulty**: S
**Question**: Describe the structure of DNA.
**Answer**: DNA has a double helix structure. Two strands of nucleotides run in opposite directions (antiparallel). Each strand has a sugar-phosphate backbone. Nitrogenous bases (A, T, G, C) pair up: A pairs with T (2 hydrogen bonds), G pairs with C (3 hydrogen bonds). The sequence of bases carries the genetic code.
**Explanation**: Standard difficulty: requires applying the core definition to a specific biological context.

**Question**: Explain how mutations in DNA can affect protein function.
**Answer**: Mutations change the DNA base sequence. This can alter the amino acid sequence in the protein. If the amino acid change affects the protein's active site (in enzymes) or structure, the protein may not function properly. This can lead to genetic disorders or changes in phenotype.

## Item 6
**Tags**: chromosomes | genes | DNA | diploid
**Difficulty**: C
**Question**: Compare the structure of DNA and RNA.
**Answer**: DNA is double-stranded with deoxyribose sugar, thymine base, and stable. RNA is single-stranded with ribose sugar, uracil base, and less stable. DNA stores genetic information long-term. RNA is involved in protein synthesis (mRNA carries code from DNA, tRNA brings amino acids, rRNA forms ribosome structure).

## Item 7
**Tags**: chromosomes | genes | DNA | diploid
**Difficulty**: C
**Question**: Explain the role of mRNA in protein synthesis.
**Answer**: mRNA is transcribed from DNA in the nucleus. It carries the genetic code from DNA to the ribosome in the cytoplasm. Each group of three bases (codon) on mRNA specifies a particular amino acid. The ribosome reads the mRNA codons and tRNA molecules bring the matching amino acids to build the protein chain.
**Explanation**: Challenge level: requires connecting multiple genetic concepts to explain the biological mechanism.

## Item 8
**Tags**: chromosomes | genes | DNA | diploid
**Difficulty**: C
**Question**: What is a codon and how does it relate to the genetic code?
**Answer**: A codon is a sequence of three bases on mRNA that codes for one amino acid. There are 64 possible codons (4^3) coding for 20 amino acids. The genetic code is degenerate (redundant) — most amino acids have more than one codon. Three codons (UAA, UAG, UGA) are stop codons that signal the end of protein synthesis.
**Explanation**: Challenge level: requires connecting multiple genetic concepts to explain the biological mechanism.
