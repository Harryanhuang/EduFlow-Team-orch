### Question Q-16.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is selective breeding (artificial selection)? Give an example.
**Answer**: Selective breeding is the process of breeding organisms with desirable characteristics to produce offspring with those traits. Steps: (1) Choose parents with desired traits. (2) Breed them. (3) Select offspring with the desired trait. (4) Breed these offspring over many generations. Example: breeding cows that produce the most milk, or wheat with the highest yield.
**Explanation**: Select, breed, select again, repeat. A common error is saying selective breeding is "genetic engineering."
**Tags**: selective-breeding, artificial-selection, examples, generations

### Question Q-16.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In humans, the ability to taste PTC is dominant (T) and non-tasting is recessive (t). Two tasters have a non-taster child. What are the parents' genotypes?
**Answer**: Both parents must be heterozygous (Tt). A non-taster child has genotype tt, meaning it received a t allele from each parent. Since both parents are tasters but carry the t allele, they must be Tt. The probability of a non-taster child from Tt × Tt is 25%.
**Explanation**: Both Tt, 25% non-taster. A common error is saying one parent must be tt.
**Tags**: PTC, taster, non-taster, Punnett, heterozygous

### Question Q-18.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What conditions are needed for fermentation?
**Answer**: Fermentation by yeast requires: (1) Sugar (glucose) as the food source. (2) Warm temperature (25-35°C — optimum for yeast enzymes). (3) Anaerobic conditions (no oxygen — otherwise yeast respires aerobically). (4) Suitable pH (slightly acidic, pH 4-6). If oxygen is present, yeast produces CO₂ and water instead of ethanol.
**Explanation**: Sugar, warmth, no oxygen, pH. A common error is saying fermentation needs oxygen.
**Tags**: fermentation, conditions, yeast, anaerobic
