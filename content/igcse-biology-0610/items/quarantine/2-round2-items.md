### Question Q-2.1-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the function of the cell membrane?
**Answer**: The cell membrane controls what enters and leaves the cell. It is selectively (partially) permeable, allowing some substances to pass through while blocking others. It is made of a phospholipid bilayer with embedded proteins.
**Explanation**: Selectively permeable, controls entry/exit. A common error is saying the cell membrane is "fully permeable."
**Tags**: cell-membrane, selectively-permeable, phospholipid

### Question Q-2.1-09
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is cytoplasm and what happens there?
**Answer**: Cytoplasm is the jelly-like substance that fills the cell between the nucleus and the cell membrane. Most chemical reactions in the cell occur in the cytoplasm. It contains organelles and is mostly water with dissolved substances.
**Explanation**: Jelly-like, chemical reactions, organelles. A common error is saying cytoplasm is "just water."
**Tags**: cytoplasm, organelles, chemical-reactions

### Question Q-2.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How is a palisade mesophyll cell in a leaf adapted for photosynthesis?
**Answer**: Palisade cells are located near the top of the leaf where they receive maximum light. They contain many chloroplasts (more than other plant cells) to absorb light efficiently. They are tall and column-shaped, packed closely together to maximise light absorption.
**Explanation**: Many chloroplasts, top of leaf, column shape. A common error is saying palisade cells are at the bottom of the leaf.
**Tags**: palisade-cell, photosynthesis, adaptation, leaf

### Question Q-2.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Give an example of an organ system in the human body and name the organs involved.
**Answer**: The digestive system: mouth, oesophagus, stomach, small intestine, large intestine, pancreas, liver, gall bladder. These organs work together to break down food, absorb nutrients, and remove waste.
**Explanation**: All organs listed. A common error is forgetting the liver or pancreas.
**Tags**: digestive-system, organs, organ-system

### Question Q-3.1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does the surface area of a membrane affect the rate of diffusion?
**Answer**: A larger surface area allows more particles to diffuse across the membrane at the same time, increasing the rate of diffusion. This is why exchange surfaces (alveoli, villi) have folds or projections to increase surface area.
**Explanation**: Larger surface area = faster rate. A common error is confusing surface area with thickness.
**Tags**: diffusion, surface-area, membrane, rate

### Question Q-3.2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is osmosis described as a special type of diffusion?
**Answer**: Osmosis is diffusion of water molecules only (not solute particles) through a partially permeable membrane. Like diffusion, it moves from high to low concentration (water potential). It is "special" because it only involves water and requires a partially permeable membrane.
**Explanation**: Water only, partially permeable membrane. A common error is saying osmosis is "completely different" from diffusion.
**Tags**: osmosis, diffusion, water, special
