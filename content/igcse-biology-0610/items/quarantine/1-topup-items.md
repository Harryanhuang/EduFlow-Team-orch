# Items: 1-topup — Classification top-up

### Question Q-1.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What features are used to classify animals into vertebrate groups?
**Answer**: Five vertebrate groups: Fish — scales, gills, fins, lay eggs in water. Amphibians — moist skin, metamorphosis, lay eggs in water. Reptiles — dry scaly skin, lay eggs on land with shells. Birds — feathers, beak, lay hard-shelled eggs, warm-blooded. Mammals — fur/hair, mammary glands, give birth to live young (mostly), warm-blooded.
**Explanation**: Five groups with distinguishing features. A common error is confusing amphibians with reptiles.
**Tags**: vertebrates, fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, mammals

### Question Q-1.2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What features are used to classify organisms as arthropods?
**Answer**: Arthropods have: jointed legs, exoskeleton (external skeleton made of chitin), segmented body. Four main groups: Insects (3 body parts, 6 legs, antennae), Arachnids (2 body parts, 8 legs), Crustaceans (hard shell, gills), Myriapods (many segments, many legs). All arthropods are invertebrates.
**Explanation**: Jointed legs, exoskeleton, segmented. A common error is saying arthropods have "internal skeletons."
**Tags**: arthropods, insects, arachnids, crustaceans, myriapods
