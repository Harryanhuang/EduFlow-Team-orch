### Question Q-8.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the function of root hair cells in water absorption?
**Answer**: Root hair cells are specialised epidermal cells with long extensions that increase the surface area for water and mineral ion absorption. They have thin cell walls for short diffusion distance and many mitochondria to provide energy for active transport of mineral ions. Water enters by osmosis from the soil.
**Explanation**: Large surface area, thin wall, mitochondria. A common error is saying root hairs "pump" water.
**Tags**: root-hair-cell, water-absorption, osmosis, surface-area

### Question Q-10.1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How can the spread of cholera be prevented?
**Answer**: Cholera is spread through contaminated water. Prevention: (1) Clean water supply (treatment, boiling). (2) Proper sewage disposal. (3) Hand washing with soap. (4) Proper food hygiene (cooking food thoroughly). (5) Oral rehydration therapy for those infected. (6) Vaccination in high-risk areas.
**Explanation**: Clean water, sewage, hygiene, ORT. A common error is saying cholera is spread through air.
**Tags**: cholera, water, prevention, hygiene

### Question Q-10.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why do organ transplant patients need to take immunosuppressant drugs?
**Answer**: The immune system recognises the transplanted organ as "foreign" because it has different antigens on its cells. Without immunosuppressant drugs, the body would mount an immune response and reject the organ. Immunosuppressants reduce the immune response, preventing rejection. However, they also make the patient more vulnerable to infections.
**Explanation**: Foreign antigens → rejection → immunosuppressants. A common error is saying immunosuppressants "strengthen" the immune system.
**Tags**: transplant, immunosuppressant, rejection, antigens
