### Question Q-18.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What are the risks of genetic engineering?
**Answer**: Risks include: (1) Unintended effects — inserting a gene may disrupt other genes or cause unexpected traits. (2) Gene transfer — engineered genes may spread to wild populations (e.g., herbicide resistance in weeds). (3) Reduced biodiversity — GM crops may replace traditional varieties. (4) Ethical concerns — "playing God," animal welfare. (5) Allergic reactions — new proteins may cause allergies.
**Explanation**: Unintended effects, gene spread, biodiversity, ethics, allergies. A common error is saying GM foods are "proven safe" or "proven unsafe."
**Tags**: genetic-engineering, risks, unintended-effects, gene-transfer

### Question Q-19.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does deforestation affect the water cycle?
**Answer**: Deforestation reduces transpiration (fewer trees releasing water vapour), which reduces atmospheric moisture and rainfall. It increases surface runoff (no roots to absorb water), leading to flooding and soil erosion. It reduces infiltration (water soaking into the ground), lowering groundwater levels. Overall, it disrupts the local and regional water cycle.
**Explanation**: Less transpiration, more runoff, less infiltration. A common error is saying deforestation "increases rainfall."
**Tags**: deforestation, water-cycle, transpiration, runoff, erosion
