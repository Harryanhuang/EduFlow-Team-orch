# Items: 7-topup — Human nutrition top-up

### Question Q-7.1-10
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why adolescents need more protein than adults.
**Answer**: Adolescents are growing rapidly and need protein for: building new tissues (muscle, bone, organs), producing hormones for growth and development, and replacing worn-out cells. Adults have finished growing, so their protein needs are lower (mainly for repair and maintenance).
**Explanation**: Growth requires new tissue building. A common error is saying adolescents need more protein "for energy."
**Tags**: protein, adolescents, growth, nutrition

### Question Q-7.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the role of the large intestine?
**Answer**: The large intestine absorbs water from undigested food, concentrating the waste material into faeces. It also absorbs some vitamins produced by gut bacteria. The remaining waste is stored in the rectum and eliminated through the anus (egestion). No digestion occurs in the large intestine.
**Explanation**: Water absorption, faeces formation. A common error is saying the large intestine "digests" food.
**Tags**: large-intestine, water-absorption, faeces, egestion

### Question Q-7.2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is peristalsis and where does it occur?
**Answer**: Peristalsis is the wave-like contraction and relaxation of muscles in the walls of the alimentary canal. It pushes food along the digestive tract. It occurs from the oesophagus through the stomach and intestines. Circular and longitudinal muscles work together to create the wave.
**Explanation**: Wave-like muscle contractions, pushes food. A common error is saying peristalsis "digests" food.
**Tags**: peristalsis, alimentary-canal, muscle, movement
