# Items: 15.2 — Human reproductive system and development

### Question Q-15.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Name the main parts of the female reproductive system and state one function of each.
**Answer**: Ovaries — produce eggs (ova) and female sex hormones (oestrogen, progesterone). Oviduct (Fallopian tube) — carries the egg from the ovary to the uterus; site of fertilisation. Uterus (womb) — where the embryo implants and develops. Cervix — ring of muscle at the neck of the uterus that keeps the baby in place during pregnancy. Vagina — birth canal; receives sperm during copulation.
**Explanation**: Functions of ovaries, oviduct, uterus, cervix and vagina. A common error is saying fertilisation occurs in the uterus.
**Tags**: female-reproductive, ovaries, oviduct, uterus, cervix, vagina

### Question Q-15.2-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Name the main parts of the male reproductive system and state one function of each.
**Answer**: Testes — produce sperm and testosterone. Scrotum — holds the testes outside the body to keep them cooler than body temperature for sperm production. Sperm duct (vas deferens) — carries sperm from the testes to the urethra. Urethra — tube through which semen and urine pass (not at the same time). Penis — places sperm into the vagina during copulation.
**Explanation**: Functions of testes, scrotum, sperm duct, urethra and penis. A common error is saying the testes store sperm.
**Tags**: male-reproductive, testes, scrotum, sperm-duct, urethra, penis

### Question Q-15.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the process of fertilisation in humans.
**Answer**: Fertilisation occurs when a sperm cell fuses with an egg cell in the oviduct (Fallopian tube). The acrosome of the sperm releases enzymes to digest the jelly coat/zona pellucida. The sperm nucleus enters the egg and fuses with the egg nucleus to form a zygote with a full set of chromosomes (46 in humans). The egg membrane changes to prevent other sperm from entering. The zygote divides by mitosis as it travels to the uterus.
**Explanation**: Sperm + egg → zygote in oviduct; membrane changes; mitosis begins. A common error is saying fertilisation happens in the uterus.
**Tags**: fertilisation, zygote, oviduct, acrosome, membrane-change

### Question Q-15.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain the role of the placenta during pregnancy.
**Answer**: The placenta: (1) allows exchange of substances between maternal and foetal blood — oxygen and nutrients diffuse from mother to foetus; carbon dioxide and urea diffuse from foetus to mother. (2) Produces hormones (progesterone, oestrogen) that maintain pregnancy. (3) Acts as a barrier to some (but not all) harmful substances. Maternal and foetal blood do NOT mix.
**Explanation**: Exchange, hormones, barrier; blood does not mix. A common error is saying maternal and foetal blood mix.
**Tags**: placenta, pregnancy, exchange, hormones, barrier

### Question Q-15.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the main events of the menstrual cycle and the hormones involved.
**Answer**: The menstrual cycle is about 28 days. (1) Follicular phase (days 1–14): FSH from the pituitary gland causes a follicle in the ovary to mature; the follicle secretes oestrogen, which repairs and thickens the uterus lining. (2) Ovulation (around day 14): LH surge causes the mature egg to be released from the ovary. (3) Luteal phase (days 15–28): the empty follicle forms a corpus luteum that secretes progesterone, which maintains the thickened uterus lining ready for implantation. If fertilisation does not occur, the corpus luteum breaks down, progesterone and oestrogen levels fall, and the lining is shed as menstruation.
**Explanation**: FSH → follicle/oestrogen; LH surge → ovulation; corpus luteum → progesterone; no fertilisation → menstruation. A common error is confusing oestrogen and progesterone.
**Tags**: menstrual-cycle, FSH, LH, oestrogen, progesterone, ovulation

### Question Q-15.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe two adaptations of sperm and two adaptations of the egg for their roles in reproduction.
**Answer**: Sperm adaptations: small and motile with a tail/flagellum for swimming; many mitochondria in the middle piece to provide energy; an acrosome containing enzymes to penetrate the egg membrane. Egg adaptations: large with a bulky cytoplasm containing nutrients (yolk) for early development; non-motile; surrounded by a jelly coat/zona pellucida that only allows one sperm to enter.
**Explanation**: Sperm = motile, energy, enzymes; egg = nutrients, non-motile, protective coat. A common error is saying the egg is motile.
**Tags**: gametes, sperm, egg, adaptations, fertilisation

### Question Q-15.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain how hormones control the menstrual cycle using the principle of negative feedback.
**Answer**: At the start of the cycle, low oestrogen and progesterone allow the pituitary to release FSH. FSH stimulates follicle growth in the ovary; the follicle secretes oestrogen. Rising oestrogen initially inhibits FSH (negative feedback) to prevent more follicles developing, then at high levels triggers a surge in LH (positive feedback). The LH surge causes ovulation. After ovulation, the corpus luteum secretes progesterone and oestrogen. These hormones thicken the uterus lining and inhibit FSH and LH (negative feedback), preventing further ovulation. If no fertilisation occurs, the corpus luteum degenerates, hormone levels fall, the lining breaks down (menstruation), and the fall in hormones removes the inhibition on FSH so the next cycle begins.
**Explanation**: Negative feedback controls FSH/LH; high progesterone/oestrogen inhibit FSH and LH; fall in hormones restarts cycle. A common error is thinking all feedback is positive.
**Tags**: menstrual-cycle, negative-feedback, FSH, LH, oestrogen, progesterone, hormones

### Question Q-15.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare internal fertilisation with external fertilisation in animals. Include advantages and disadvantages of each.
**Answer**: Internal fertilisation: sperm deposited inside the female's body; fertilisation occurs inside the female reproductive tract. Advantages: fewer gametes needed because sperm are protected and directed towards the egg; embryos are protected inside the female; development can be controlled. Disadvantages: requires complex reproductive behaviour/copulation; only one parent carries the young. External fertilisation: eggs and sperm released into the water outside the body; fertilisation occurs in the environment. Advantages: large numbers of eggs can be produced; no need for physical contact between parents. Disadvantages: huge numbers of gametes needed because many eggs/sperm are lost; embryos are unprotected from predators and environmental change. Humans use internal fertilisation; most fish and amphibians use external fertilisation.
**Explanation**: Internal = protected, fewer gametes, parental care; External = many gametes, unprotected. A common error is saying external fertilisation occurs inside the body.
**Tags**: internal-fertilisation, external-fertilisation, comparison, advantages, animals

### Question Q-15.2-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Describe four different methods of birth control and explain how each prevents pregnancy.
**Answer**: (1) Barrier methods (e.g. condom): physically block sperm from reaching the egg; condoms also reduce STI transmission. (2) Hormonal methods (e.g. contraceptive pill): contain oestrogen/progesterone that inhibit FSH release, preventing follicle development and ovulation. (3) Intra-uterine device (IUD): a small device placed in the uterus that prevents implantation of a fertilised egg. (4) Surgical methods: vasectomy (cuts/blocks sperm ducts in males) or sterilisation/tubal ligation (blocks oviducts in females) prevent sperm and egg meeting. Natural methods include abstinence and monitoring the menstrual cycle to avoid intercourse during fertile days.
**Explanation**: Barrier, hormonal, IUD, surgical/natural; each mechanism. A common error is saying the pill kills sperm.
**Tags**: birth-control, contraception, condom, pill, IUD, sterilisation
