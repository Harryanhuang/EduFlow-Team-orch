### Question Q-4.1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the test for lipids (fats) in a food sample.
**Answer**: Emulsion test: crush the food sample, add ethanol, and shake vigorously. Then add water and shake again. If lipids are present, a cloudy white emulsion forms. A negative result remains clear.
**Explanation**: Ethanol + water → cloudy emulsion. A common error is using the wrong solvent.
**Tags**: lipid-test, emulsion-test, ethanol, food-test

### Question Q-4.1-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is water described as the "universal solvent" in biology?
**Answer**: Water dissolves many substances (salts, sugars, amino acids, gases) because of its polar molecule structure. This allows dissolved substances to be transported in blood and sap, and chemical reactions to occur in aqueous solutions. Most cellular reactions happen in water.
**Explanation**: Polar molecule, dissolves many substances, transport. A common error is saying water dissolves "everything" (it does not dissolve lipids).
**Tags**: water, solvent, polar, transport

### Question Q-5.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Name two enzymes and the reactions they catalyse.
**Answer**: Amylase — breaks down starch into maltose (digestion). Protease (e.g., pepsin) — breaks down proteins into amino acids (digestion). Lipase — breaks down lipids into fatty acids and glycerol. Catalase — breaks down hydrogen peroxide into water and oxygen. DNA polymerase — builds DNA from nucleotides.
**Explanation**: Named enzyme + specific reaction. A common error is confusing amylase with protease.
**Tags**: enzymes, amylase, protease, lipase, catalase

### Question Q-5.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why does an enzyme stop working if the pH is too far from its optimum?
**Answer**: Extreme pH changes the charges on the amino acids in the enzyme's active site. This alters the shape of the active site (denaturation), so the substrate no longer fits. The enzyme-substrate complex cannot form, and the reaction cannot be catalysed.
**Explanation**: Active site shape changes, substrate no longer fits. A common error is saying the enzyme is "killed."
**Tags**: enzyme, pH, denaturation, active-site
