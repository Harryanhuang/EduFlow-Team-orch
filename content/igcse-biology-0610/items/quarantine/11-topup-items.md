# Items: 11-topup — Gas exchange top-up

### Question Q-11.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What adaptations does the trachea have for its function?
**Answer**: The trachea (windpipe) has C-shaped rings of cartilage that keep it open and prevent collapse during breathing. The open part of the C faces the oesophagus, allowing food to pass. The inner surface is lined with ciliated epithelium and mucus-producing cells — mucus traps dust and pathogens, and cilia sweep them upwards towards the throat.
**Explanation**: Cartilage rings, ciliated epithelium, mucus. A common error is saying cartilage is "complete rings."
**Tags**: trachea, cartilage, cilia, mucus

### Question Q-11.1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is tobacco smoke harmful to the gas exchange system?
**Answer**: Tobacco smoke: (1) Damages cilia — mucus and pathogens not cleared, causing smoker's cough. (2) Tar damages alveoli walls, reducing surface area (emphysema). (3) Irritates bronchi, causing excess mucus (bronchitis). (4) Contains carcinogens that cause lung cancer. (5) Carbon monoxide reduces oxygen-carrying capacity of blood.
**Explanation**: Cilia damage, tar, carcinogens, CO. A common error is saying smoking "only causes cancer."
**Tags**: tobacco-smoke, lungs, cilia, tar, emphysema, cancer
