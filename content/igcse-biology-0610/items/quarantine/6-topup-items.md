# Items: 6-topup — Photosynthesis top-up

### Question Q-6.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Describe an experiment to show that light is needed for photosynthesis.
**Answer**: Destarch a potted plant (keep in darkness for 48 hours). Cover part of a leaf with aluminium foil (blocks light). Expose the plant to light for 6 hours. Remove the leaf, test for starch (boil in water, then ethanol, then add iodine). The exposed areas turn blue-black (starch present). The foil-covered area stays orange/brown (no starch). Conclusion: light is needed for photosynthesis.
**Explanation**: Destarch → cover → light → starch test → covered area no starch. A common error is not destarching first.
**Tags**: photosynthesis, light, experiment, starch-test, destarch

### Question Q-6.1-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe an experiment to show that CO₂ is needed for photosynthesis.
**Answer**: Destarch a plant. Place a potted plant in a sealed bell jar with soda lime (absorbs CO₂). Place another plant in an identical jar without soda lime (control). Expose both to light for 6 hours. Test leaves for starch. The plant with soda lime has no starch (no CO₂). The control has starch. Conclusion: CO₂ is needed.
**Explanation**: Soda lime removes CO₂, control has CO₂, starch test. A common error is not controlling light.
**Tags**: photosynthesis, CO2, experiment, soda-lime

### Question Q-6.2-10
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why does increasing CO₂ concentration in a greenhouse increase crop yield?
**Answer**: CO₂ is a raw material for photosynthesis. Increasing its concentration (from 0.04% atmospheric to about 0.1%) increases the rate of photosynthesis, producing more glucose. More glucose means more growth and higher yield. However, the increase only works up to a point — eventually another factor becomes limiting.
**Explanation**: CO₂ is raw material, rate increases to a limit. A common error is saying CO₂ provides "energy."
**Tags**: CO2, greenhouse, photosynthesis, yield
