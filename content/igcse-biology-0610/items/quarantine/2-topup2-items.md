### Question Q-2.1-10
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the function of mitochondria?
**Answer**: Mitochondria are the site of aerobic respiration in cells. They release energy from glucose, which is used to make ATP (adenosine triphosphate). ATP provides energy for cell activities. Cells with high energy demands (muscle cells, sperm cells, secretory cells) contain many mitochondria.
**Explanation**: Aerobic respiration, energy release, ATP. A common error is saying mitochondria "store" energy.
**Tags**: mitochondria, respiration, ATP, energy

### Question Q-2.3-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How do tissues differ from organs?
**Answer**: A tissue is a group of similar cells performing a specific function (e.g., muscle tissue, epithelial tissue, xylem tissue). An organ is made of different tissues working together to perform a function (e.g., the stomach contains muscle tissue, epithelial tissue, blood tissue, and nervous tissue). Organs are more complex than tissues.
**Explanation**: Tissue = similar cells; Organ = different tissues. A common error is calling any body part a "tissue."
**Tags**: tissue, organ, difference, complexity

### Question Q-3.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A student places red blood cells in solutions of different concentrations. In solution A, cells burst. In solution B, cells shrink and become wrinkled. In solution C, cells remain normal. Explain these results.
**Answer**: Solution A is hypotonic (more dilute than cell cytoplasm) — water enters by osmosis, cells burst (no cell wall). Solution B is hypertonic (more concentrated) — water leaves by osmosis, cells shrink (crenation). Solution C is isotonic (same concentration) — no net water movement, cells remain normal.
**Explanation**: Hypotonic = burst, hypertonic = shrink, isotonic = normal. A common error is reversing hypertonic and hypotonic.
**Tags**: osmosis, RBC, hypotonic, hypertonic, isotonic
