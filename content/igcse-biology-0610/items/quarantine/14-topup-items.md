### Question Q-14.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the structure of a motor neurone.
**Answer**: A motor neurone has: cell body (contains nucleus, at one end), dendrites (short branches from cell body receiving signals), long axon (carries impulse away from cell body), myelin sheath (insulating layer from Schwann cells, speeds up impulse), and nerve endings (connect to muscle fibres at the neuromuscular junction).
**Explanation**: Cell body, dendrites, axon, myelin, nerve endings. A common error is confusing motor with sensory neurone structure.
**Tags**: motor-neurone, structure, axon, myelin

### Question Q-14.3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe an experiment to investigate phototropism in plant shoots.
**Answer**: Grow oat seedlings in three conditions: (1) normal light — shoots grow straight up. (2) Unilateral (one-sided) light — shoots bend towards the light. (3) Darkness — shoots grow straight but are pale (etiolated). Cover the tip of some shoots with foil — they do not bend, showing the tip detects light and produces auxin. Conclusion: shoots respond to unilateral light via auxin.
**Explanation**: Three conditions, tip covered, auxin role. A common error is not including the tip-covered condition.
**Tags**: phototropism, experiment, auxin, unilateral-light
