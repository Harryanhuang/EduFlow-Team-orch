# Items: 13-round2 — Excretion + Coordination round 2

### Question Q-13.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is carbon dioxide considered a waste product?
**Answer**: Carbon dioxide is produced by aerobic respiration in cells. If it accumulates, it dissolves in body fluids to form carbonic acid, which lowers pH and disrupts enzyme activity and cellular processes. It must be removed via the blood to the lungs and exhaled.
**Explanation**: Forms acid, lowers pH, disrupts enzymes. A common error is saying CO₂ is "not toxic."
**Tags**: CO2, waste, pH, respiration

### Question Q-13.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the function of the nephron in the kidney?
**Answer**: The nephron is the functional unit of the kidney. It filters blood (ultrafiltration in the glomerulus), reabsorbs useful substances (all glucose, most water, some salts) back into the blood, and concentrates urea and excess substances into urine. Each kidney contains about one million nephrons.
**Explanation**: Ultrafiltration, selective reabsorption, urine concentration. A common error is saying the nephron "makes urea."
**Tags**: nephron, kidney, ultrafiltration, reabsorption

### Question Q-14.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is an effector? Give two examples.
**Answer**: An effector is a structure that carries out a response to a stimulus. Examples: muscles (contract to produce movement, e.g., pulling hand away from heat) and glands (secrete hormones or other substances, e.g., adrenaline from adrenal gland, insulin from pancreas). Effectors receive signals from motor neurones.
**Explanation**: Muscles and glands. A common error is saying effectors "detect" stimuli (that's receptors).
**Tags**: effector, muscle, gland, response

### Question Q-14.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is negative feedback? Give an example.
**Answer**: Negative feedback is a mechanism that reverses a change and maintains a steady state (homeostasis). When a level deviates from the set point, the body detects this and triggers a response to return to normal. Example: blood glucose control — high glucose → insulin → glucose decreases → insulin stops. Low glucose → glucagon → glucose increases → glucagon stops.
**Explanation**: Reverses change, returns to normal. A common error is saying negative feedback "amplifies" changes.
**Tags**: negative-feedback, homeostasis, blood-glucose, insulin
