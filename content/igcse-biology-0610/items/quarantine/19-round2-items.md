### Question Q-19.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does pollution affect aquatic ecosystems?
**Answer**: Pollution affects aquatic ecosystems in several ways: (1) Sewage/fertiliser runoff causes eutrophication — algae bloom, die, decomposers use oxygen, fish die from lack of oxygen. (2) Oil spills coat birds and marine animals, reducing insulation and buoyancy. (3) Heavy metals accumulate in food chains (biomagnification). (4) Plastics harm animals through ingestion and entanglement.
**Explanation**: Eutrophication, oil, heavy metals, plastics. A common error is saying pollution "only affects fish."
**Tags**: pollution, aquatic, eutrophication, biomagnification

### Question Q-19.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is eutrophication? Describe the process.
**Answer**: Eutrophication is the enrichment of water with nutrients (nitrates, phosphates) causing excessive algal growth. Process: (1) Fertilisers/sewage enter water. (2) Algae bloom rapidly, blocking light. (3) Plants below die (no photosynthesis). (4) Decomposers multiply, using up oxygen. (5) Fish and other organisms die from lack of oxygen. The water becomes dead and smelly.
**Explanation**: Nutrients → algae bloom → light blocked → decomposers → oxygen depleted → death. A common error is saying algae "directly kill" fish.
**Tags**: eutrophication, algae-bloom, oxygen, nutrients

### Question Q-19.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is sustainable fishing and how can it be practised?
**Answer**: Sustainable fishing maintains fish populations at levels that can reproduce and replenish. Methods: (1) Quotas — limits on catch numbers. (2) Net size regulations — mesh large enough to let juvenile fish escape. (3) Closed seasons — no fishing during breeding periods. (4) Protected areas — no-fishing zones where populations can recover. (5) Aquaculture — farming fish instead of wild capture.
**Explanation**: Quotas, net size, seasons, protected areas, aquaculture. A common error is saying sustainable fishing means "no fishing."
**Tags**: sustainable-fishing, quotas, aquaculture, conservation

### Question Q-1.1-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How can you test whether something is a living organism?
**Answer**: Check for the seven characteristics of life (MRS GREN): Movement, Respiration, Sensitivity, Growth, Reproduction, Excretion, Nutrition. All living organisms must show all seven characteristics at some point. Non-living things may show some (e.g., fire moves and grows) but not all seven.
**Explanation**: All seven characteristics needed. A common error is checking only one or two characteristics.
**Tags**: living-organisms, MRS-GREN, test, characteristics

### Question Q-3.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Compare diffusion, osmosis, and active transport in terms of energy, direction, and membrane requirement.
**Answer**: Diffusion: no energy, high to low concentration, no membrane needed (but occurs across membranes). Osmosis: no energy, high to low water potential, requires partially permeable membrane. Active transport: needs energy (respiration), low to high concentration (against gradient), requires a membrane with carrier proteins.
**Explanation**: Energy (none/none/yes), direction (down/down/against), membrane (optional/required/required). A common error is saying osmosis needs energy.
**Tags**: diffusion, osmosis, active-transport, comparison
