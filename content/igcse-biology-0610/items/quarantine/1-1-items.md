# Items: 1.1 — Characteristics of living organisms

### Question Q-1.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: List the seven characteristics of living organisms.
**Answer**: Movement, respiration, sensitivity, growth, reproduction, excretion, nutrition (MRS GREN). All living organisms show all seven characteristics at some point in their life cycle.
**Explanation**: MRS GREN mnemonic. A common error is including "breathing" instead of "respiration" (respiration is cellular, not breathing).
**Tags**: MRS-GREN, characteristics, living-organisms

### Question Q-1.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is excretion in living organisms?
**Answer**: Excretion is the removal of waste products of metabolism (such as carbon dioxide, urea, and excess water) from the body. It is different from egestion (removal of undigested food).
**Explanation**: Waste from metabolism, not undigested food. A common error is confusing excretion with egestion.
**Tags**: excretion, metabolism, waste

### Question Q-1.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the difference between nutrition in plants and nutrition in animals?
**Answer**: Plants are autotrophs — they make their own food by photosynthesis using CO₂, water, and sunlight. Animals are heterotrophs — they must consume other organisms for nutrition.
**Explanation**: Autotroph vs heterotroph. A common error is saying plants "eat" soil nutrients.
**Tags**: nutrition, autotroph, heterotroph, photosynthesis

### Question Q-1.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is a virus not considered a living organism by some scientists?
**Answer**: Viruses cannot carry out all seven life processes independently. They cannot reproduce without a host cell, cannot grow, and do not have cellular structure. They only show characteristics of life when inside a host cell.
**Explanation**: Dependent on host cell. A common error is saying viruses are "dead" — they are acellular particles.
**Tags**: virus, living, non-living, characteristics

### Question Q-1.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why respiration is a characteristic of all living organisms.
**Answer**: All living organisms need energy to carry out life processes (growth, movement, reproduction, etc.). Respiration releases energy from glucose (or other food molecules) in cells. Without respiration, organisms cannot survive.
**Explanation**: Energy for life processes. A common error is confusing respiration with breathing.
**Tags**: respiration, energy, life-processes

### Question Q-1.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how sensitivity is demonstrated in plants and animals.
**Answer**: In plants: phototropism (growth towards light), roots growing towards gravity (geotropism), Mimosa leaves folding when touched. In animals: pupils constricting in bright light, withdrawing hand from a hot surface, animals moving towards food.
**Explanation**: Response to stimuli. A common error is saying plants "don't respond."
**Tags**: sensitivity, response, stimuli, tropism

### Question Q-1.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A crystal of salt grows in a saturated solution. Is this growth a characteristic of living organisms? Explain your answer.
**Answer**: No. Crystal growth is the addition of material from the outside (more salt molecules depositing on the surface). Living organisms grow from within — by cell division and cell enlargement, not just adding external material.
**Explanation**: Internal growth (cells) vs external deposition. A common error is saying crystals are alive.
**Tags**: growth, crystal, living, distinction

### Question Q-1.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A robot can move, respond to touch, and use electricity for energy. Explain why it is still not classified as a living organism, referring to at least three characteristics of life.
**Answer**: The robot cannot reproduce (make copies of itself from genetic material), cannot grow by cell division, cannot excrete metabolic waste, and cannot carry out metabolism (chemical reactions in cells). Movement and sensitivity alone are not sufficient — non-living things can also show these. All seven characteristics must be present.
**Explanation**: Multiple characteristics needed. A common error is focusing on just one characteristic like movement.
**Tags**: robot, non-living, characteristics, evaluation

### Question Q-1.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Mules (offspring of a horse and donkey) are sterile and cannot reproduce. Explain whether this means mules are not living organisms.
**Answer**: Mules are still living organisms because they show all other characteristics of life (movement, respiration, sensitivity, growth, excretion, nutrition). Reproduction is a characteristic of the species as a whole, not a requirement for every individual. Many individual organisms (sterile workers, post-menopausal humans) cannot reproduce but are clearly alive.
**Explanation**: Species-level vs individual characteristic. A common error is saying reproduction is required for every individual to be "alive."
**Tags**: mule, sterile, reproduction, individual-vs-species
