# Items: 15-topup — Reproduction top-up

### Question Q-15.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how bacteria reproduce asexually.
**Answer**: Bacteria reproduce by binary fission. The circular DNA molecule replicates (copies itself). The cell elongates and the two DNA copies move to opposite ends. The cell membrane pinches inwards and a new cell wall forms, dividing the cell into two identical daughter cells. Under ideal conditions, some bacteria divide every 20 minutes.
**Explanation**: DNA replicates, cell divides, two identical. A common error is saying bacteria reproduce by mitosis (mitosis is for eukaryotic cells).
**Tags**: bacteria, binary-fission, asexual-reproduction, DNA

### Question Q-15.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What happens during the menstrual cycle?
**Answer**: Day 1-5: Menstruation — uterus lining breaks down and is shed (period). Day 5-14: Uterus lining rebuilds (thickens, blood vessels develop). Day 14: Ovulation — an egg is released from the ovary. Day 14-28: Uterus lining maintained (ready for implantation). If no fertilisation, the cycle repeats. The cycle is controlled by hormones (oestrogen, progesterone).
**Explanation**: Menstruation, rebuilding, ovulation, maintenance. A common error is saying ovulation happens on day 1.
**Tags**: menstrual-cycle, menstruation, ovulation, hormones
