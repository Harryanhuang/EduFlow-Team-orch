# Items: 5-topup2 — Enzymes + Digestion top-up

### Question Q-5.1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why do enzymes in washing powders work best at 30-40°C?
**Answer**: Washing powders contain protease and lipase enzymes that break down protein and fat stains. These enzymes have an optimum temperature of 30-40°C. Below this, the rate is too slow (not enough kinetic energy). Above this, the enzymes denature (lose their active site shape) and stop working. Using too hot a temperature wastes energy and damages the enzymes.
**Explanation**: Optimum temperature, denaturation above. A common error is saying "hotter is always better."
**Tags**: enzymes, washing-powder, temperature, optimum

### Question Q-7.1-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What are the consequences of an unbalanced diet (obesity and malnutrition)?
**Answer**: Obesity (too much energy intake): risk of Type 2 diabetes, heart disease, high blood pressure, joint problems. Malnutrition (deficiency of specific nutrients): scurvy (vitamin C), rickets (vitamin D), anaemia (iron), kwashiorkor (protein). Both are forms of malnutrition — not just "not eating enough."
**Explanation**: Obesity consequences + deficiency diseases. A common error is saying malnutrition only means "starvation."
**Tags**: obesity, malnutrition, Type-2-diabetes, deficiency

### Question Q-7.2-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the role of the pancreas in digestion?
**Answer**: The pancreas produces digestive enzymes: pancreatic amylase (breaks down starch), protease/trypsin (breaks down proteins), and lipase (breaks down fats). These enzymes are released into the small intestine via the pancreatic duct. The pancreas also produces sodium hydrogen carbonate to neutralise stomach acid, providing alkaline conditions for intestinal enzymes.
**Explanation**: Enzymes + neutralising acid. A common error is confusing the pancreas with insulin production only.
**Tags**: pancreas, enzymes, amylase, protease, lipase
