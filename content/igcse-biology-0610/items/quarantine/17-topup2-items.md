### Question Q-17.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is energy lost at each trophic level in a food chain?
**Answer**: Energy is lost: (1) As heat from respiration (the biggest loss). (2) In uneaten parts (bones, roots, fur). (3) In waste (egestion in faeces, excretion in urine). (4) In movement and other life processes. Only about 10% of energy is transferred to the next level. This limits the number of trophic levels.
**Explanation**: Heat, uneaten parts, waste, movement. A common error is saying energy is "destroyed" (it's transferred as heat).
**Tags**: energy-loss, trophic-levels, 10-percent, food-chain

### Question Q-17.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does the greenhouse effect differ from global warming?
**Answer**: The greenhouse effect is a natural process — greenhouse gases trap heat, keeping Earth warm enough for life. Without it, Earth would be too cold. Global warming is the enhanced greenhouse effect caused by human activities (burning fossil fuels, deforestation) that increase greenhouse gas concentrations, causing Earth to warm beyond the natural level.
**Explanation**: Natural vs human-enhanced. A common error is saying the greenhouse effect is "bad."
**Tags**: greenhouse-effect, global-warming, natural, enhanced

### Question Q-17.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is biodiversity and how can it be measured?
**Answer**: Biodiversity is the variety of species in an area. It can be measured by: (1) Species richness — the number of different species present. (2) Species evenness — how evenly individuals are distributed among species. High biodiversity indicates a healthy, stable ecosystem. Low biodiversity makes ecosystems vulnerable.
**Explanation**: Richness + evenness, healthy ecosystem. A common error is counting only the number of organisms.
**Tags**: biodiversity, species-richness, evenness, ecosystem
