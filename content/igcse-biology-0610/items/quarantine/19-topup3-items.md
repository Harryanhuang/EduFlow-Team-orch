### Question Q-19.1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is acid rain and how is it formed?
**Answer**: Acid rain is rain with a pH below 5.6, formed when sulfur dioxide (from burning fossil fuels with sulfur impurities) and nitrogen oxides (from vehicle exhausts) dissolve in rainwater to form sulfuric and nitric acid. Effects: damages trees, acidifies lakes (killing fish), erodes limestone buildings, and damages soil nutrients.
**Explanation**: SO₂ + NOx → sulfuric + nitric acid. A common error is saying CO₂ causes acid rain (it causes ocean acidification, not acid rain).
**Tags**: acid-rain, sulfur-dioxide, nitrogen-oxides, effects

### Question Q-19.2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How can individuals help to conserve ecosystems?
**Answer**: Individuals can: (1) Reduce, reuse, recycle — reduce waste and resource use. (2) Save energy — use public transport, switch off appliances. (3) Use sustainable products — certified wood, sustainable fish. (4) Support conservation organisations. (5) Plant trees. (6) Reduce water usage. (7) Avoid products made from endangered species.
**Explanation**: Practical individual actions. A common error is saying individuals "can't make a difference."
**Tags**: conservation, individual-actions, reduce, recycle

### Question Q-1.1-10
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What are the characteristics of a virus that distinguish it from a bacterium?
**Answer**: Viruses: non-cellular (no cell structure), much smaller than bacteria, contain DNA or RNA (not both), cannot reproduce independently (need a host cell), do not respire, do not grow. Bacteria: cellular (prokaryotic), larger, contain both DNA and RNA, reproduce by binary fission, respire, have cell walls.
**Explanation**: Non-cellular, smaller, host-dependent. A common error is saying viruses are "small bacteria."
**Tags**: virus, bacteria, characteristics, comparison

### Question Q-8.2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why do some plants have adaptations to reduce transpiration?
**Answer**: Plants in dry environments (xerophytes) need to conserve water. Adaptations: small leaves or spines (reduced surface area), thick waxy cuticle (reduces evaporation), sunken stomata (traps humid air), rolled leaves (reduces air movement over stomata), hairy leaves (traps moisture). These reduce water loss by transpiration.
**Explanation**: Xerophyte adaptations, reduce water loss. A common error is saying these plants "don't transpire."
**Tags**: xerophyte, transpiration, adaptation, water-conservation
