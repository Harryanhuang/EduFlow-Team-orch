### Question Q-8.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How are phloem sieve tubes adapted for translocation?
**Answer**: Phloem sieve tubes are made of living cells with sieve plates (perforated end walls) that allow sugars to flow through. They have few organelles to allow easy flow of substances. Companion cells provide energy (ATP from mitochondria) for active loading of sucrose into the sieve tubes.
**Explanation**: Sieve plates, living, companion cells provide energy. A common error is saying phloem cells are dead (xylem is dead).
**Tags**: phloem, sieve-tubes, translocation, companion-cells

### Question Q-8.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why do plants wilt when they lose more water by transpiration than they absorb?
**Answer**: When water loss exceeds absorption, cells lose water by osmosis and become flaccid. Turgor pressure drops, so cells can no longer support the plant structure. Leaves and stems droop. If the water deficit continues, cells may die and the plant cannot recover.
**Explanation**: Water loss → flaccid cells → no turgor → wilting. A common error is saying wilting is caused by "too much sun."
**Tags**: wilting, turgor, transpiration, flaccid

### Question Q-9.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the double circulatory system and why is it advantageous?
**Answer**: In humans, blood passes through the heart twice for each complete circuit: right side → lungs → left side → body → right side. Advantages: (1) Blood can be pumped to the body at high pressure (left ventricle). (2) Blood pressure is reduced going to the lungs (protects delicate lung capillaries). (3) Oxygenated and deoxygenated blood are kept separate.
**Explanation**: Two circuits, high pressure to body, separate blood. A common error is saying blood goes through the heart once per circuit.
**Tags**: double-circulation, heart, pressure, lungs, body

### Question Q-9.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is plasma important in the blood?
**Answer**: Plasma is the liquid component of blood (about 55%). It transports: dissolved nutrients (glucose, amino acids), waste products (CO₂, urea), hormones, and heat. It also contains proteins for clotting and defence. Plasma is mostly water.
**Explanation**: Transport medium, dissolved substances. A common error is saying plasma carries oxygen.
**Tags**: plasma, transport, nutrients, waste
