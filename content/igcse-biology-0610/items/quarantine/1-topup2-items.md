### Question Q-1.2-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What are the key features used to identify a vertebrate as a mammal?
**Answer**: Mammals have: hair or fur, mammary glands (produce milk for young), give birth to live young (except monotremes), warm-blooded (endothermic), have a diaphragm, four-chambered heart, and different types of teeth (incisors, canines, molars). These features distinguish mammals from other vertebrate groups.
**Explanation**: Hair, mammary glands, live birth, warm-blooded. A common error is saying "all mammals give birth to live young" (monotremes lay eggs).
**Tags**: mammals, vertebrates, identification, features

### Question Q-2.3-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Give an example of a plant tissue and explain its function.
**Answer**: Xylem tissue transports water and mineral ions from roots to leaves. It is made of dead, hollow cells with lignin-thickened walls for strength. Phloem tissue transports sucrose and amino acids from sources to sinks (translocation). It is made of living sieve tube cells with companion cells that provide energy.
**Explanation**: Xylem = water transport, phloem = food transport. A common error is confusing xylem with phloem.
**Tags**: plant-tissue, xylem, phloem, transport

### Question Q-4.2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is DNA described as a "double helix"?
**Answer**: DNA consists of two strands of nucleotides twisted around each other in a spiral shape (helix). Each strand has a sugar-phosphate backbone with nitrogenous bases pointing inwards. The two strands are held together by hydrogen bonds between complementary base pairs (A-T, C-G). The twisted ladder shape is the double helix.
**Explanation**: Two strands, twisted, base pairs. A common error is saying the strands are "parallel" (they are antiparallel).
**Tags**: DNA, double-helix, nucleotides, base-pairs
