### Question Q-9.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What are coronary arteries and why are they important?
**Answer**: Coronary arteries are blood vessels that supply the heart muscle itself with oxygenated blood. They branch off the aorta just after it leaves the heart. If a coronary artery becomes blocked (by a blood clot or fatty deposit), the heart muscle downstream is deprived of oxygen, causing a heart attack (myocardial infarction).
**Explanation**: Supply heart muscle, blockage = heart attack. A common error is thinking the heart gets oxygen from the blood flowing through its chambers.
**Tags**: coronary-arteries, heart-attack, oxygen, blockage

### Question Q-9.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How do platelets contribute to blood clotting?
**Answer**: When a blood vessel is damaged, platelets gather at the wound site. They release chemicals (clotting factors) that trigger a cascade of reactions. The final step converts soluble fibrinogen (in plasma) into insoluble fibrin threads. These form a mesh that traps red blood cells and platelets, forming a clot that seals the wound.
**Explanation**: Platelets trigger → fibrinogen → fibrin → mesh → clot. A common error is saying platelets "are" the clot.
**Tags**: platelets, blood-clotting, fibrin, wound
