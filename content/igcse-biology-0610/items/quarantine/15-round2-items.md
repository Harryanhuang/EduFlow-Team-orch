# Items: 15-round2 — Reproduction + Inheritance round 2

### Question Q-15.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how fungi reproduce asexually.
**Answer**: Fungi reproduce asexually by producing spores. Spores are formed by mitosis in sporangia and are released into the air. They can travel long distances and germinate when they land on a suitable substrate (warm, moist, with food). This allows rapid colonisation of new areas.
**Explanation**: Spores, sporangia, mitosis, colonisation. A common error is saying fungi reproduce "by seeds."
**Tags**: fungi, asexual-reproduction, spores, sporangia

### Question Q-15.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the function of the amniotic sac and amniotic fluid during pregnancy?
**Answer**: The amniotic sac encloses the developing foetus and contains amniotic fluid. The fluid: (1) cushions and protects the foetus from mechanical shock. (2) Allows the foetus to move freely for muscle development. (3) Maintains a constant temperature. (4) Prevents the foetus from drying out.
**Explanation**: Protection, movement, temperature, prevents drying. A common error is saying amniotic fluid "nourishes" the foetus.
**Tags**: amniotic-sac, amniotic-fluid, pregnancy, foetus

### Question Q-16.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is meiosis necessary for sexual reproduction?
**Answer**: Meiosis reduces the chromosome number by half (from diploid 46 to haploid 23) so that when two gametes fuse at fertilisation, the normal diploid number is restored (23 + 23 = 46). Without meiosis, each generation would double the chromosome number. Meiosis also creates genetic variation.
**Explanation**: Halves chromosomes, restores at fertilisation, variation. A common error is saying meiosis "doubles" chromosomes.
**Tags**: meiosis, sexual-reproduction, chromosomes, haploid

### Question Q-16.2-05
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: In a species of flower, red (R) and white (r) alleles show incomplete dominance, producing pink flowers in heterozygotes. What would be the offspring of a cross between two pink flowers?
**Answer**: Parents: Rr × Rr. Offspring genotypes: RR, Rr, Rr, rr. Phenotypes: 1 red (RR) : 2 pink (Rr) : 1 white (rr). The ratio is 1:2:1 (phenotypic = genotypic). This differs from complete dominance where the phenotypic ratio is 3:1.
**Explanation**: 1:2:1 ratio, incomplete dominance. A common error is giving a 3:1 ratio.
**Tags**: incomplete-dominance, pink-flowers, Punnett, ratio
