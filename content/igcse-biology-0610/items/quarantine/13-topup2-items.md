### Question Q-13.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the difference between the renal artery and the renal vein?
**Answer**: The renal artery carries blood containing urea, excess water, and salts TO the kidney for filtration. The renal vein carries blood WITH LESS urea and excess substances removed, leaving the kidney. The renal artery has higher pressure (branch of aorta). The renal vein has lower pressure. Both carry oxygenated/deoxygenated blood respectively.
**Explanation**: Artery = to kidney (with urea), vein = from kidney (less urea). A common error is saying the renal vein carries "clean" blood (it still has waste, just less).
**Tags**: renal-artery, renal-vein, kidney, urea

### Question Q-15.3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is self-pollination and cross-pollination? Which is better for variation?
**Answer**: Self-pollination: pollen from an anther is transferred to the stigma of the same flower or another flower on the same plant. Cross-pollination: pollen is transferred to a different plant of the same species. Cross-pollination is better for genetic variation because it combines genes from two different plants, increasing diversity.
**Explanation**: Same plant vs different plant, cross = more variation. A common error is saying self-pollination produces "better" offspring.
**Tags**: self-pollination, cross-pollination, variation

### Question Q-19.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the role of international agreements in conservation?
**Answer**: International agreements coordinate conservation efforts across borders. Examples: CITES (Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species) — bans trade in endangered species and their products. The Rio Convention on Biological Diversity — commits countries to conserving biodiversity. The Kyoto/Paris agreements — reduce greenhouse gas emissions to combat climate change.
**Explanation**: CITES, Rio, Paris, cross-border cooperation. A common error is saying agreements "enforce" conservation (they rely on voluntary compliance).
**Tags**: conservation, international, CITES, agreements
