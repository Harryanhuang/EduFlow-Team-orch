# Items: 12-topup — Respiration top-up

### Question Q-12.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe an experiment to show that living organisms release energy during respiration.
**Answer**: Place germinating peas in a vacuum flask with a thermometer. Seal the flask. Record the temperature over several days. The temperature rises because respiration in the germinating seeds releases energy, some of which is lost as heat. A control flask with dead (boiled) seeds shows no temperature increase.
**Explanation**: Vacuum flask, thermometer, temperature rise, dead seed control. A common error is not insulating the flask.
**Tags**: respiration, experiment, heat, energy, germinating-seeds

### Question Q-12.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe an experiment to show that living organisms produce CO₂ during respiration.
**Answer**: Place living organisms (e.g., woodlice or germinating seeds) in a sealed container with a tube leading to limewater. As the organisms respire, CO₂ is produced and bubbles through the limewater. The limewater turns cloudy, confirming CO₂ production. A control with dead organisms shows no change.
**Explanation**: Limewater turns cloudy, CO₂ produced, dead control. A common error is not providing enough time for CO₂ to accumulate.
**Tags**: respiration, CO2, limewater, experiment
