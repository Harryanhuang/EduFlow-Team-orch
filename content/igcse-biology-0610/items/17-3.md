## Item 1
**Tags**: inheritance | genetics | alleles | Mendel
**Difficulty**: S
**Question**: What happens to chromosome number during mitosis?
**Answer**: In mitosis, the chromosome number is maintained. The chromosome number in the daughter cells is the same as in the parent cell. Before division, chromosomes replicate (each becomes two chromatids). After division, each daughter cell receives a complete set of chromosomes identical to the parent cell.

**Question**: How many chromosomes does a human body cell contain?
**Answer**: A human body cell (diploid cell) contains 46 chromosomes, or 23 pairs. One set of 23 chromosomes comes from the mother and one set of 23 comes from the father.

## Item 3
**Tags**: inheritance | genetics | alleles | Mendel
**Difficulty**: S
**Question**: Describe the main stages of mitosis in the correct order.
**Answer**: 1. Interphase: DNA replicates, chromosomes become visible as two chromatids. 2. Prophase: chromosomes condense, nuclear envelope breaks down. 3. Metaphase: chromosomes line up at the equator, spindle fibres attach. 4. Anaphase: sister chromatids separate to opposite poles. 5. Telophase: nuclear envelopes reform, cytoplasm divides (cytokinesis). Two genetically identical daughter cells result.
**Explanation**: Standard difficulty: requires applying the core definition to a specific biological context.

**Difficulty**: S
**Question**: Why is mitosis important in growth and repair?
**Answer**: Mitosis produces genetically identical cells. In growth, it increases cell numbers so organisms increase in size. In repair, it replaces damaged or worn-out cells with identical copies. Since no variation is introduced, the new cells function exactly like the cells they replace.

## Item 5
**Tags**: inheritance | genetics | alleles | Mendel
**Difficulty**: S
**Question**: What is the difference between asexual and sexual reproduction in terms of genetic variation?
**Answer**: Asexual reproduction uses mitosis only. Offspring are genetically identical clones of the parent (no variation). Sexual reproduction uses meiosis to produce gametes with half the chromosome number, then fertilisation restores the full number. Offspring are genetically unique with variation from both parents.

## Item 6
**Tags**: inheritance | genetics | alleles | Mendel
**Difficulty**: C
**Question**: Compare mitosis and meiosis in terms of chromosome number, number of divisions, and genetic variation.
**Answer**: Mitosis: one division, chromosome number maintained (diploid to diploid), no genetic variation in daughter cells. Meiosis: two divisions, chromosome number halved (diploid to haploid), genetic variation introduced through crossing over in prophase I and independent assortment. Mitosis produces 2 identical cells; meiosis produces 4 genetically different cells.
**Explanation**: Challenge level: requires connecting multiple genetic concepts to explain the biological mechanism.

## Item 7
**Tags**: inheritance | genetics | alleles | Mendel
**Difficulty**: C
**Question**: Explain how crossing over in prophase I of meiosis contributes to genetic variation.
**Answer**: During prophase I, homologous chromosomes pair up and form bivalents. Non-sister chromatids can twist around each other and exchange segments of DNA at points called chiasmata. This crossing over means genes from one chromosome are swapped onto the other. Each resulting chromatid is a mixture of both parental chromosomes, creating new combinations of alleles. This produces gametes that are genetically unique.
**Explanation**: Challenge level: requires connecting multiple genetic concepts to explain the biological mechanism.

## Item 8
**Tags**: inheritance | genetics | alleles | Mendel
**Difficulty**: C
**Question**: Why is meiosis necessary for sexual reproduction?
**Answer**: Meiosis reduces the chromosome number by half (from diploid to haploid), producing gametes. If gametes were diploid, fertilisation would double the chromosome number each generation. Meiosis ensures that when two haploid gametes fuse, the zygote has the correct diploid number. Meiosis also introduces genetic variation through crossing over and independent assortment, which is essential for evolution and adaptation.
**Explanation**: Challenge level: requires connecting multiple genetic concepts to explain the biological mechanism.
