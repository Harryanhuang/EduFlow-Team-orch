**Question**: What is monohybrid inheritance?
**Answer**: Monohybrid inheritance is the inheritance of a single characteristic controlled by one gene. One allele is dominant (shown in the phenotype) and one is recessive (only shown when homozygous). For example, tall (T) is dominant over short (t). A cross between TT and tt parents produces all Tt offspring (all tall).
**Explanation**: Recall that chromosomes are thread-like structures made of DNA that carry genetic information.

**Question**: Explain why recessive遗传 traits can skip generations.
**Answer**: Recessive alleles can be carried (heterozygous Tt) without showing in the phenotype. A recessive phenotype only appears when an individual inherits two recessive alleles (tt). This means a carrier (Tt) can pass the recessive allele to a child who then pairs it with another recessive from the other parent, showing the trait in the next generation.

**Question**: A woman with normal colour vision (carrier of red-green colour blindness) marries a colour-blind man. Show the Punnett square and state the possible genotypes and phenotypes of their children.
**Answer**: Father: XcY (colour-blind). Mother: XCXc (carrier). Cross: XCXc x XcY. Offspring: XXC (normal female 25%), XcXc (carrier female 25%), XCY (normal male 25%), XcY (colour-blind male 25%). Probability of colour-blind child = 50%. Sons have 50% chance of being colour-blind; daughters cannot be colour-blind but 50% are carriers.

**Question**: Define co-dominance and give an example involving human blood groups.
**Answer**: Co-dominance: both alleles are expressed equally in the heterozygote, producing a phenotype that shows both characteristics. Human blood group AB is an example: IAIB individuals have both A and B antigens on their red blood cells. Neither IA nor IB is dominant over the other.
