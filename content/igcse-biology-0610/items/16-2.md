**Question**: What is monohybrid inheritance?
**Answer**: Monohybrid inheritance involves the inheritance of a single characteristic controlled by one gene. One allele is dominant (expressed in the phenotype) and one is recessive (only expressed when homozygous).

**Tags**: monohybrid | alleles | dominant | recessive
**Difficulty**: S
**Question**: What is a test cross and why is it useful?
**Answer**: A test cross crosses an individual of unknown genotype with a homozygous recessive individual. If all offspring show the dominant phenotype, the unknown parent is homozygous dominant. If half show the recessive phenotype, the unknown parent is heterozygous. It reveals the unknown genotype.
**Explanation**: Standard difficulty: requires applying the core definition to a specific biological context.

**Tags**: monohybrid | alleles | dominant | recessive
**Difficulty**: S
**Question**: Explain the terms dominant and recessive.
**Answer**: A dominant allele is expressed even if only one copy is present (heterozygous). A recessive allele is only expressed when two copies are present (homozygous recessive). Dominant alleles mask recessive alleles in heterozygous individuals.

**Difficulty**: C
**Question**: A woman who is a carrier for cystic fibrosis (Ff) has children with a man who does not have cystic fibrosis (ff). Draw a Punnett square and state the probability of having an affected child.
**Answer**: Cross: Ff x ff. Offspring: Ff (carrier, unaffected) 50%, ff (affected) 50%. Probability of affected child = 1/2 = 50%.

## Item 7
**Tags**: monohybrid | alleles | dominant | recessive
**Difficulty**: C
**Question**: In humans, blood group is determined by multiple alleles. Explain how blood groups ABO demonstrate multiple allelism.
**Answer**: The ABO system has three alleles: IA, IB (both dominant over IO) and IO (recessive). IA codes for A antigen, IB codes for B antigen, IO codes for neither. Combinations: IAIA or IAIO = Group A; IBIB or IBIO = Group B; IAIB = Group AB; IOIO = Group O. Both IA and IB are fully expressed in heterozygotes (co-dominance).
**Explanation**: Challenge level: requires connecting multiple genetic concepts to explain the biological mechanism.
