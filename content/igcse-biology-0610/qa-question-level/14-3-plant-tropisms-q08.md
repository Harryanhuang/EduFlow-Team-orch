# Question: Question Q-14.3-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain the experimental evidence that the shoot tip is the site of auxin production and light detection.
**Answer**: (1) Darwin (1880): opaque cap on coleoptile tip → no bending; tip removed → no bending; transparent cap → normal bending. Tip detects light. (2) Boysen-Jensen (1913): gelatin block between tip and stump → bending normal (signal passes through gelatin); mica sheet → no bending (impermeable barrier); mica on lit side → still bends (signal travels shaded side). Chemical signal travels down shaded side. (3) Went (1928): cut tips placed on agar blocks; agar block asymmetrically on decapitated shoot → bending away from agar side even in darkness. Auxin isolated as the signal. Together: tip produces auxin, light redistributes auxin to shaded side, asymmetric auxin causes bending.
**Explanation**: Darwin→tip detects; Boysen-Jensen→chemical signal shaded path; Went→auxin isolated. A common error is attributing all findings to one experiment.
