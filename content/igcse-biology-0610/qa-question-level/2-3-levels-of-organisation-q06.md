# Question: Question Q-2.3-06
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A student claims that "stomata are tissues because they are made of similar cells." Evaluate this claim using knowledge of plant organisation.
**Answer**: Stomata are NOT tissues. Each stoma consists of TWO guard cells (a pair of specialised epidermal cells that open and close the pore). Two cells do not constitute a tissue — a tissue requires a group of similar cells. The stomatal pore itself is an opening, not a cell. The correct classification: guard cells are specialised cells of the epidermal tissue; the leaf (containing guard cells, epidermis, palisade, spongy mesophyll, xylem, and phloem tissues) is the organ. The student has confused a structure (stoma) with a tissue.
**Explanation**: Guard cells = cells, stomatal pore = opening, leaf = organ. A common error is calling stomata "tissues."
