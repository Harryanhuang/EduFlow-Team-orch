# Question: Question Q-10.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is a pathogen? Name four types of pathogen and give an example of a disease each causes.
**Answer**: A pathogen is a disease-causing organism. Types: (1) Bacteria — e.g., Salmonella (food poisoning), Mycobacterium tuberculosis (TB). (2) Viruses — e.g., HIV (AIDS), influenza virus (flu). (3) Fungi — e.g., Trichophyton (athlete's foot), Diplocarpon rosae (rose black spot). (4) Protoctista — e.g., Plasmodium (malaria).
**Explanation**: Disease-causing organism, four types with named diseases. A common error is lumping bacteria and viruses as "germs."
