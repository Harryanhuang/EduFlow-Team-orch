# Question: Question Q-10.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why do organ transplant patients need immunosuppressant drugs, and what is the risk?
**Answer**: The immune system recognises the transplanted organ as "foreign" because cells carry different surface antigens (HLA proteins) from the donor. Without immunosuppressants, T-lymphocytes would attack the transplant, causing organ rejection. Immunosuppressants reduce this immune response, enabling the organ to survive. Risk: reduced immune function makes the patient more vulnerable to opportunistic infections (e.g., fungal pneumonia, CMV) because the immune system cannot respond effectively to real pathogens. Lifelong monitoring is required.
**Explanation**: Foreign antigens → immune attack → immunosuppressant needed; trade-off: infection risk. A common error is saying immunosuppressants "strengthen" the immune system.
