# Question: Question Q-19.2-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Describe the carbon footprint of different food production systems and explain how dietary choices can contribute to sustainable development.
**Answer**: Food production contributes significantly to carbon emissions: beef and lamb have the highest carbon footprints (high methane from cattle, large land use for grazing/feed). Pork and chicken have moderate footprints. Plant-based foods (grains, legumes, vegetables) have the lowest footprints. Factors contributing to food carbon footprint: land use change (deforestation for agriculture), enteric fermentation (methane from ruminants), fertiliser use (N₂O emissions), transportation (food miles), processing and packaging, refrigeration. Sustainable dietary choices: reducing meat consumption (especially beef and lamb), eating locally produced seasonal food (reducing food miles), choosing organic food (often lower inputs, but may require more land), reducing food waste (approximately 30% of food is wasted). These choices reduce individual carbon footprints while maintaining food security. Combining dietary change with sustainable farming practices (agroforestry, regenerative agriculture) can significantly reduce food system emissions.
**Explanation**: Meat vs plant foods, food miles, waste reduction. A common error is saying "going vegetarian" alone solves the problem.
