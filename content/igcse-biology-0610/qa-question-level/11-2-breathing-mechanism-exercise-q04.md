# Question: Question Q-11.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What happens to the breathing rate and depth during recovery after exercise? Explain why it stays elevated.
**Answer**: After exercise stops, breathing rate and depth remain elevated for several minutes. Reasons: (1) Oxygen debt — O₂ is needed to convert lactic acid back to glucose in the liver and to replenish myoglobin O₂ stores. (2) Residual CO₂ must be exhaled. (3) Elevated body temperature increases metabolic rate. The fitter the person, the faster they recover. Breathing gradually returns to resting levels as these processes complete.
**Explanation**: Oxygen debt + residual CO₂ + temperature. A common error is saying breathing "returns to normal immediately."
