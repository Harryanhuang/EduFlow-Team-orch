# Question: Question Q-15.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Compare the structure and function of the placenta with the amniotic sac during pregnancy.
**Answer**: Placenta: (1) Exchange organ — oxygen, nutrients, antibodies diffuse from maternal to foetal blood; CO₂, urea, waste products diffuse from foetal to maternal blood. (2) Hormone production — secretes progesterone and oestrogen to maintain pregnancy. (3) Partial barrier — blocks some pathogens and harmful substances but allows many through (e.g., alcohol, nicotine). Maternal and foetal blood do NOT mix; exchange occurs across the placental membrane. Amniotic sac: (1) A membrane filled with amniotic fluid that surrounds and cushions the developing foetus. (2) Protects the foetus from physical shock, maintains a constant temperature, and allows freedom of movement for muscle development. (3) Acts as a barrier against infection.
**Explanation**: Placenta = exchange + hormones; Amniotic sac = cushioning + temperature + movement. A common error is confusing their functions.
