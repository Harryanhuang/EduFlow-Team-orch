# Question: Question Q-14.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare the structure and function of sensory and motor neurones.
**Answer**: Sensory: cell body mid-axon (dorsal root ganglion) — protects soma; short axon from receptor → CNS, long central axon → spinal cord; dendrites connect to receptor; axon terminal synapses with relay neurone; direction = receptor → CNS. Motor: cell body at one end (in CNS); very long axon (up to 1 m) to effector; dendrites connect to relay neurones; terminal innervates muscle/gland. Both myelinated for fast conduction. Sensory = gatherer; motor = commander.
**Explanation**: Structural comparison table + functional roles. A common error is drawing both identically.
