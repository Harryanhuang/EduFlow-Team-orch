# Question: Question Q-4.2-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is DNA described as a "double helix"?
**Answer**: DNA consists of two strands of nucleotides twisted around each other in a spiral shape (helix). Each strand has a sugar-phosphate backbone with nitrogenous bases pointing inwards. The two strands are held together by hydrogen bonds between complementary base pairs (A-T, C-G). The twisted ladder shape is the double helix.
**Explanation**: Two strands, twisted, base pairs. A common error is saying the strands are "parallel" (they are antiparallel).
