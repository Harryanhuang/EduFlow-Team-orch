# Question: Q-15.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A species of fungus can reproduce both asexually and sexually. Describe the conditions under which each method might be favoured and explain the biological advantage of each in those conditions.
**Answer**: Asexual reproduction (spores by mitosis) is favoured in stable, favourable environments where the parent is well-adapted. Advantage: rapid colonisation, no need for a mate, all offspring preserve successful traits. Sexual reproduction is favoured in changing or stressful environments (e.g., new disease, temperature shift, nutrient shortage). Advantage: genetic recombination and variation through meiosis creates new allele combinations; some offspring may be better adapted to the new conditions, increasing the chance of species survival.
**Explanation**: Stable = asexual (fast, clones); Changing = sexual (variation, adaptation). A common error is saying one method is always better than the other.
