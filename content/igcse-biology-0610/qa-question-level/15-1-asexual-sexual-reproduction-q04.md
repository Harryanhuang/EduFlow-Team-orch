# Question: Q-15.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Give two advantages of asexual reproduction.
**Answer**: (1) Rapid population increase — only one parent is needed, so no time is spent finding a mate. (2) All offspring inherit favourable traits from the parent (if the parent is well-adapted to a stable environment, all offspring are too). (3) Faster than sexual reproduction in stable environments where conditions do not change.
**Explanation**: Fast, no mate needed, favourable traits inherited. A common error is saying asexual reproduction produces "better" offspring.
