# Question: Question Q-4.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the difference between saturated and unsaturated fats?
**Answer**: Saturated fats have no double bonds between carbon atoms in the fatty acid chains (all carbons are saturated with hydrogen). They are usually solid at room temperature (e.g., butter, animal fat). Unsaturated fats have one or more double bonds, usually liquid at room temperature (e.g., olive oil).
**Explanation**: Double bonds present or absent. A common error is saying unsaturated fats are "healthier" without explaining the structure.
