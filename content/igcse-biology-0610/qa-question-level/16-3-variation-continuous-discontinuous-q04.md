# Question: Question Q-16.3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain how both genetic and environmental factors can affect the height of a plant.
**Answer**: Genetic factors: the plant's genes determine its potential maximum height (e.g., dwarf vs tall varieties). Environmental factors: light intensity (photosynthesis), water availability, mineral ions (nitrates for growth), temperature, and competition. A plant with tall genes will not reach its full height in poor conditions.
**Explanation**: Genes = potential, environment = realisation. A common error is saying only genes determine height.
