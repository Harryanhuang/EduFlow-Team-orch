# Question: Question Q-6.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain how temperature affects the rate of photosynthesis.
**Answer**: Photosynthesis is controlled by enzymes. At low temperatures, enzymes have less kinetic energy and the rate is slow. As temperature rises towards the optimum (25-30°C for most plants), the rate increases. Above the optimum, enzymes involved in photosynthesis begin to denature, and the rate drops sharply. At very high temperatures (>45°C), photosynthesis stops as all relevant enzymes are denatured.
**Explanation**: Enzyme-controlled: low → optimum → denature. A common error is forgetting that photosynthesis involves enzymes.
**Tags**: temperature, enzymes, optimum, denaturation
