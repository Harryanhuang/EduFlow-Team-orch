# Question: Question Q-13.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain the significance of excretion for homeostasis, giving examples of how three different excretory products are linked to homeostatic balance.
**Answer**: Three links: (1) CO₂ — excretion by lungs maintains blood pH 7.35-7.45. CO₂ accumulation → ↓pH → enzyme denaturation. Increased breathing during exercise removes excess CO₂ → pH restored. (2) Urea — excretion by kidneys maintains nitrogen balance. Without excretion, urea accumulates (uraemia) → toxic to brain, nausea, death. (3) Water — excretion by kidneys (urine volume varies with ADH) + skin (sweat) maintains osmotic balance. Water excess → dilute urine; deficit → concentrated urine + thirst. Each product is tied to a homeostatic variable; excretion is the regulatory pathway that keeps it within limits.
**Explanation**: CO₂→pH, Urea→nitrogen, H₂O→osmoregulation. A common error is listing products without linking to homeostatic variable.
