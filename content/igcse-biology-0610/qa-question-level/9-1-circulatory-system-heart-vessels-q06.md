# Question: Question Q-9.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why do veins have valves but arteries do not?
**Answer**: Veins have valves because blood pressure in veins is low (blood has passed through capillaries and lost pressure). Valves prevent backflow, ensuring blood flows in one direction towards the heart. Skeletal muscle contraction helps squeeze veins, pushing blood past the valves. Arteries do not need valves because blood pressure from the heart is high enough to keep blood flowing forward. The only valves in arteries are the semilunar valves at the exits of the heart.
**Explanation**: Low pressure in veins → valves needed; high pressure in arteries → no valves. A common error is saying arteries have valves.
