# Question: Question Q-13.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is excretion? Why is it necessary?
**Answer**: Excretion is the removal of waste products of metabolism (toxic by-products of chemical reactions in cells) from the body. It is necessary because metabolic wastes — CO₂ (from respiration), urea (from deamination), and excess water/salts — are toxic if allowed to accumulate. Accumulation disrupts enzyme activity, pH balance, and osmotic balance, eventually causing cell death. Excretory products and routes: CO₂ → lungs (exhaled); urea + excess water/ions → kidneys (urine); water + salts + trace urea → skin (sweat).
**Explanation**: Removal of metabolic waste, prevents toxicity. A common error is confusing excretion with egestion.
