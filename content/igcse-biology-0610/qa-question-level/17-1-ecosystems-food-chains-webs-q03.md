# Question: Question Q-17.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What do the arrows in a food chain represent?
**Answer**: Arrows represent the direction of energy flow — from the organism being eaten to the organism that eats it. They show where energy is transferred, not "who eats who." For example: grass → grasshopper → frog → snake → hawk means energy flows from grass to the hawk through each step.
**Explanation**: Energy flow direction. A common error is saying arrows point to "what gets eaten."
