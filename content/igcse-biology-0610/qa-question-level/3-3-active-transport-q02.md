# Question: Question Q-3.3-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is active transport? How does it differ from diffusion?
**Answer**: Active transport is the movement of particles against a concentration gradient (from low to high concentration), using energy from respiration. Diffusion moves particles down a concentration gradient (high to low) without energy input.
**Explanation**: Against gradient, needs energy. A common error is saying active transport "does not need energy."
