# Question: Question Q-15.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the main events of the menstrual cycle and the hormones involved.
**Answer**: The menstrual cycle is about 28 days. (1) Follicular phase (days 1–14): FSH from the pituitary gland causes a follicle in the ovary to mature; the follicle secretes oestrogen, which repairs and thickens the uterus lining. (2) Ovulation (around day 14): LH surge causes the mature egg to be released from the ovary. (3) Luteal phase (days 15–28): the empty follicle forms a corpus luteum that secretes progesterone, which maintains the thickened uterus lining ready for implantation. If fertilisation does not occur, the corpus luteum breaks down, progesterone and oestrogen levels fall, and the lining is shed as menstruation.
**Explanation**: FSH → follicle/oestrogen; LH surge → ovulation; corpus luteum → progesterone; no fertilisation → menstruation. A common error is confusing oestrogen and progesterone.
