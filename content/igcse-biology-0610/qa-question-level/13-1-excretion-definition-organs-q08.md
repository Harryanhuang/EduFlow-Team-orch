# Question: Question Q-13.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A person eats a very high-protein diet for a month. Explain how the excretory system responds.
**Answer**: High protein → more amino acids than needed for synthesis. Liver: increased deamination rate → more NH₃ → more urea via ornithine cycle. Carbon skeletons used for respiration or converted to glycogen/fat. Kidneys: increased urea in blood → increased filtration load → more urea in urine. Urea is an osmotic diuretic → more water in urine → increased urine volume → thirst. Long-term: potential kidney strain. Urine: higher urea concentration, possibly lower pH (sulphur-containing amino acids → sulphate → acidic urine). This demonstrates the liver-kidney axis responding to dietary variation to maintain homeostasis.
**Explanation**: Dietary excess → liver↑ deamination → kidney↑ filtration → urine↑ volume. A common error is describing only one organ.
