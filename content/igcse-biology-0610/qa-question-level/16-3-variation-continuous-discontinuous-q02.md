# Question: Question Q-16.3-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Give two examples of continuous variation and two examples of discontinuous variation in humans.
**Answer**: Continuous: height (varies from shortest to tallest with all values in between), weight, skin colour, hand span. Discontinuous: blood group (A, B, AB, O only), ability to roll tongue (yes/no), earlobe attachment (attached/free), gender (male/female).
**Explanation**: Continuous = measurable range; Discontinuous = distinct categories. A common error is putting height in discontinuous.
**Tags**: variation, examples, height, blood-group, tongue-rolling
