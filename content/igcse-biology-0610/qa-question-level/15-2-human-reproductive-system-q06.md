# Question: Question Q-15.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe two adaptations of sperm and two adaptations of the egg for their roles in reproduction.
**Answer**: Sperm adaptations: small and motile with a tail/flagellum for swimming; many mitochondria in the middle piece to provide energy; an acrosome containing enzymes to penetrate the egg membrane. Egg adaptations: large with a bulky cytoplasm containing nutrients (yolk) for early development; non-motile; surrounded by a jelly coat/zona pellucida that only allows one sperm to enter.
**Explanation**: Sperm = motile, energy, enzymes; egg = nutrients, non-motile, protective coat. A common error is saying the egg is motile.
