# Question: Question Q-19.2-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: How does deforestation affect the water cycle?
**Answer**: Deforestation reduces transpiration (fewer trees releasing water vapour), which reduces atmospheric moisture and rainfall. It increases surface runoff (no roots to absorb water), leading to flooding and soil erosion. It reduces infiltration (water soaking into the ground), lowering groundwater levels. Overall, it disrupts the local and regional water cycle.
**Explanation**: Less transpiration, more runoff, less infiltration. A common error is saying deforestation "increases rainfall."
