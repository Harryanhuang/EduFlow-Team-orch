# Question: Question Q-17.3-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is competition in an ecosystem? Give an example.
**Answer**: Competition occurs when organisms vie for the same limited resource. Intraspecific competition: between members of the same species (e.g., two oak trees competing for light). Interspecific competition: between different species (e.g., foxes and owls competing for mice). Competition limits population sizes and can drive natural selection.
**Explanation**: Same resource, intra vs inter-specific. A common error is saying competition is "always physical fighting."
**Tags**: competition, intraspecific, interspecific, resources
