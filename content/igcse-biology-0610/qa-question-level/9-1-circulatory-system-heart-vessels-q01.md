# Question: Question Q-9.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What are the three types of blood vessels and their functions?
**Answer**: Arteries carry blood away from the heart (usually oxygenated, thick muscular walls, high pressure). Veins carry blood towards the heart (usually deoxygenated, thin walls, valves to prevent backflow, low pressure). Capillaries connect arteries and veins (one cell thick for exchange, very narrow).
**Explanation**: Arteries = away, veins = towards, capillaries = exchange. A common error is saying all arteries carry oxygenated blood (pulmonary artery does not).
