# Question: Question Q-18.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how yeast is used in bread making.
**Answer**: Yeast respires anaerobically (fermentation) on sugars in the flour, producing carbon dioxide and ethanol. The CO₂ gas forms bubbles in the dough, causing it to rise. During baking, the yeast is killed and the ethanol evaporates, leaving the bread with a light, airy texture.
**Explanation**: Fermentation → CO₂ → rises. A common error is saying yeast "makes the bread taste better" (that's not the main purpose).
