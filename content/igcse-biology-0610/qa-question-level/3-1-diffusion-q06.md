# Question: Question Q-3.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does the concentration gradient affect the rate of diffusion?
**Answer**: A steeper concentration gradient (greater difference between the two regions) results in a faster rate of diffusion. As the concentration equalises, the gradient becomes less steep and the rate of diffusion decreases until equilibrium is reached, where particles continue to move but there is no net movement.
**Explanation**: Steeper gradient = faster rate. A common error is saying diffusion stops at equilibrium (it continues but with no net change).
