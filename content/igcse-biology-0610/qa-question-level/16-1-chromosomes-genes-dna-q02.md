# Question: Question Q-16.1-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the relationship between DNA, genes, and chromosomes?
**Answer**: DNA is the molecule that carries genetic information. A gene is a section of DNA that codes for a specific protein. A chromosome is a long DNA molecule wrapped around proteins, found in the nucleus. Humans have 46 chromosomes (23 pairs) in body cells. Each chromosome contains many genes.
**Explanation**: DNA → gene (section) → chromosome (packaged DNA). A common error is saying a gene "is" a chromosome.
