# Question: Question Q-13.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Describe the gross structure of the kidney including the main blood vessels.
**Answer**: The kidney has: (1) renal artery — carries blood with urea + excess water/ions TO the kidney at high pressure. (2) renal vein — carries filtered blood FROM the kidney (less urea, balanced). (3) ureter — tube carrying urine from kidney to bladder. (4) capsule — outer protective layer. (5) cortex — outer region, contains glomeruli and Bowman's capsules. (6) medulla — inner region, contains loops of Henle and collecting ducts. (7) pelvis — central cavity collecting urine.
**Explanation**: Renal artery (in), renal vein (out), ureter (urine out), cortex, medulla, pelvis. A common error is confusing artery/vein direction.
