# Question: Question Q-16.2-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Define the terms: allele, genotype, phenotype, dominant, recessive, homozygous, heterozygous.
**Answer**: Allele — alternative form of a gene. Genotype — genetic makeup (e.g., Bb). Phenotype — observable characteristic (e.g., brown eyes). Dominant — allele expressed even if only one copy is present (B). Recessive — allele expressed only if two copies are present (b). Homozygous — two identical alleles (BB or bb). Heterozygous — two different alleles (Bb).
**Explanation**: All seven terms defined. A common error is confusing genotype with phenotype.
