# Question: Question Q-13.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Distinguish between excretion, egestion, and secretion, giving clear examples. Explain why "the liver excretes bile" is partially incorrect.
**Answer**: Excretion — removal of metabolic waste (CO₂, urea). The substance was inside cells, toxic if accumulated. Egestion — removal of undigested, unabsorbed material (faeces). The material never entered cells. Secretion — release of useful substances produced by cells for a function (bile, enzymes, hormones). "Liver excretes bile" is partially incorrect: (1) bile is primarily a secretion — it has a function (emulsifying fats). (2) However, bile also contains bilirubin (Hb breakdown product from old RBCs) — this component IS excretory. (3) Bilirubin leaves in faeces — so faeces contain both egested (undigested food) and excreted (bile pigments) material. Bile straddles secretion + excretion.
**Explanation**: Three terms with examples; bile = secretion + excretion component. A common error is treating all three as synonyms.
