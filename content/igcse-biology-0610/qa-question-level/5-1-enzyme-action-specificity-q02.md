# Question: Question Q-5.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is an enzyme?
**Answer**: An enzyme is a protein that acts as a biological catalyst. It speeds up chemical reactions in living organisms without being used up. Enzymes are specific — each enzyme catalyses only one type of reaction.
**Explanation**: Protein catalyst, not used up, specific. A common error is saying enzymes are "carbohydrates."
**Tags**: enzyme, definition, catalyst, protein
