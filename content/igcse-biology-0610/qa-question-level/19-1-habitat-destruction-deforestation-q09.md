# Question: Question Q-19.1-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Evaluate the effectiveness of different approaches to reducing carbon emissions and assess their potential impact on slowing climate change.
**Answer**: Approaches to reducing carbon emissions include: (1) Transition to renewable energy (solar, wind, hydroelectric) — highly effective, currently expanding rapidly. Challenges: intermittency (sun/wind not always available), storage issues, initial infrastructure costs. (2) Nuclear power — very low CO₂ emissions, reliable baseload power. Challenges: radioactive waste disposal, safety concerns after Chernobyl/Fukushima. (3) Carbon capture and storage (CCS) — captures CO₂ from power plants. Challenges: expensive, energy-intensive, limited deployment. (4) Reforestation — removes CO₂ through photosynthesis. Challenges: takes decades to mature, vulnerable to fires and disease. (5) International agreements (Paris Agreement) — sets targets but relies on voluntary compliance. Effectiveness varies — renewable energy is currently the most scalable solution. No single approach is sufficient; a combination is needed. Even with reductions, some climate change is locked in due to existing atmospheric CO₂.
**Explanation**: Multiple approaches, each with pros and cons. A common error is saying one solution will "solve" climate change alone.
