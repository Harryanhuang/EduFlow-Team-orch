# Question: Question Q-3.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe what happens to a plant cell placed in pure water.
**Answer**: Water enters the cell by osmosis (water potential is higher outside). The cell becomes turgid — the vacuole swells and pushes the cytoplasm against the cell wall. The cell wall prevents the cell from bursting. Turgor pressure supports the plant.
**Explanation**: Turgid, cell wall prevents bursting. A common error is saying the cell bursts.
