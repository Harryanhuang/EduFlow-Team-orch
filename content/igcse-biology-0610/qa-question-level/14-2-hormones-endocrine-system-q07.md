# Question: Question Q-14.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes in terms of cause, insulin status, typical onset, and treatment.
**Answer**: Type 1: autoimmune β-cell destruction → little/no insulin; onset usually young; lean; ~10% of diabetes; requires insulin injections; not preventable. Type 2: insulin resistance + β-cell exhaustion; insulin may be normal/low/high but cells don't respond; onset usually older (increasingly young with obesity); often overweight; ~90% of diabetes; treated with diet, exercise, metformin, sometimes insulin later; largely preventable through healthy weight. Key: Type 1 = deficiency; Type 2 = resistance.
**Explanation**: Type 1 = no production (autoimmune); Type 2 = resistance (lifestyle-linked). A common error is treating them as interchangeable.
