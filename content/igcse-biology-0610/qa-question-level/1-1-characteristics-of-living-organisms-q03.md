# Question: Question Q-1.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the difference between nutrition in plants and nutrition in animals?
**Answer**: Plants are autotrophs — they make their own food by photosynthesis using CO₂, water, and sunlight. Animals are heterotrophs — they must consume other organisms for nutrition.
**Explanation**: Autotroph vs heterotroph. A common error is saying plants "eat" soil nutrients.
**Tags**: nutrition, autotroph, heterotroph, photosynthesis
