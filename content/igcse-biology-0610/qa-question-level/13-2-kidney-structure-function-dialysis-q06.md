# Question: Question Q-13.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is dialysis, when is it needed, and how does it work?
**Answer**: Dialysis = artificial filtration for kidney failure (GFR <15 mL/min). Blood passed through dialyser with partially permeable membrane fibres bathed in dialysis fluid. Dialysis fluid: NO urea (gradient → urea diffuses out), normal ion levels (excess ions diffuse out), same glucose as blood (glucose not lost). Blood cells + proteins retained. Heparin prevents clotting. Sessions: 3-4 hours, 3×/week. Dialysis manages but does NOT cure kidney failure.
**Explanation**: Artificial kidney — membrane + dialysis fluid with correct gradients. A common error is saying dialysis "cures."
