# Question: Question Q-10.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why antibiotics do not work against viral infections.
**Answer**: Antibiotics target specific bacterial structures or metabolic processes: (1) bacterial cell wall synthesis (e.g., penicillin blocks peptidoglycan cross-linking), (2) bacterial protein synthesis (target 70S ribosomes, different from human 80S), (3) bacterial DNA replication enzymes. Viruses have none of these structures — they are non-cellular and use the host cell's machinery to replicate. Antibiotics cannot target viruses without damaging the host's own cells. Using antibiotics for viral infections contributes to antibiotic resistance without any benefit. Antiviral drugs target virus-specific processes.
**Explanation**: Antibiotics target bacterial-specific structures, viruses lack them. A common error is saying antibiotics "kill viruses too."
