# Question: Question Q-14.3-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A potted plant is placed on its side in a dark room for 5 days. Explain what you would observe and the role of auxin.
**Answer**: Observation: shoot bends and grows upward (negative geotropism); root bends and grows downward (positive geotropism). Mechanism: gravity sensed by statoliths in root cap and shoot endodermis. Auxin transported to lower side of both organs. SHOOT: higher auxin stimulates cell elongation → lower side grows faster → curves UPWARD. ROOT: higher auxin inhibits cell elongation (more sensitive) → upper side grows faster → curves DOWNWARD. Once vertical, auxin redistributes symmetrically and growth continues straight. Dark room eliminates phototropism as a confounder.
**Explanation**: Shoot up (auxin stimulates), root down (auxin inhibits), statoliths detect gravity, dark control. A common error is forgetting to explain opposite responses.
