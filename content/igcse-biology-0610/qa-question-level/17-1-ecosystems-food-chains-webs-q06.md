# Question: Question Q-17.1-06
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Why is energy lost at each trophic level in a food chain?
**Answer**: Energy is lost: (1) As heat from respiration (the biggest loss). (2) In uneaten parts (bones, roots, fur). (3) In waste (egestion in faeces, excretion in urine). (4) In movement and other life processes. Only about 10% of energy is transferred to the next level. This limits the number of trophic levels.
**Explanation**: Heat, uneaten parts, waste, movement. A common error is saying energy is "destroyed" (it's transferred as heat).
