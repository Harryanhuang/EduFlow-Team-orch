# Question: Question Q-6.2-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is a "limiting factor" in photosynthesis?
**Answer**: A limiting factor is the factor that is in shortest supply and therefore limits (restricts) the rate of photosynthesis. Even if all other factors are abundant, the rate cannot increase until the limiting factor is also increased. The concept follows the Law of Limiting Factors.
**Explanation**: Shortest supply restricts the rate. A common error is saying a limiting factor "stops" photosynthesis completely.
