# Question: Question Q-5.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What happens to an enzyme after it has catalysed a reaction?
**Answer**: The enzyme is unchanged and can catalyse the same reaction again. The substrate binds to the active site, the reaction occurs, products are released, and the enzyme is free to bind another substrate molecule. Enzymes are not used up in reactions.
**Explanation**: Unchanged, reusable. A common error is saying enzymes are "used up" like reactants.
**Tags**: enzyme, unchanged, reusable, catalyst
