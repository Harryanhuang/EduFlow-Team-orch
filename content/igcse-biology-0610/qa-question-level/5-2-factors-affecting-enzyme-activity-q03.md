# Question: Question Q-5.2-03
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: How does temperature affect enzyme activity? Explain the shape of the rate vs temperature graph.
**Answer**: As temperature increases, the rate of enzyme activity increases (more kinetic energy, more collisions). The rate peaks at the optimum temperature (usually 37°C for human enzymes). Above the optimum, the rate drops sharply because the enzyme denatures — the active site shape changes and the substrate no longer fits.
**Explanation**: Increases to optimum, then denatures. A common error is saying the enzyme "dies" at high temperature.
**Tags**: enzyme, temperature, optimum, denaturation
