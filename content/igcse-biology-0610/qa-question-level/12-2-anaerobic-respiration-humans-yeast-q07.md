# Question: Question Q-12.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain the role of NAD⁺ regeneration in anaerobic respiration. Why is this essential for cell survival during oxygen shortage?
**Answer**: Glycolysis (glucose → 2 pyruvate) requires NAD⁺ as an electron acceptor. In aerobic respiration, NADH is re-oxidised to NAD⁺ at the ETC, passing electrons to O₂. When O₂ is absent → ETC stops → NADH accumulates → NAD⁺ depleted → glycolysis halts → ATP production stops → cell dies within minutes. Anaerobic respiration solves this: in humans, LDH transfers electrons from NADH to pyruvate → lactate, regenerating NAD⁺. In yeast, alcohol DH transfers electrons from NADH to acetaldehyde → ethanol, regenerating NAD⁺. The key purpose is NOT to produce lactate/ethanol — it is to regenerate NAD⁺ so glycolysis can continue producing 2 ATP to keep the cell alive.
**Explanation**: NAD⁺ regeneration is the purpose; lactate/ethanol are waste. Without it, glycolysis stops → no ATP → cell death. A common error is saying the purpose is "to produce lactic acid."
