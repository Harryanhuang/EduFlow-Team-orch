# Question: Question Q-9.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What substances are transported in blood plasma?
**Answer**: Plasma transports: (1) Glucose and amino acids from the small intestine to cells. (2) Carbon dioxide from respiring cells to the lungs (mostly as hydrogen carbonate ions). (3) Urea from the liver to the kidneys. (4) Hormones from endocrine glands to target organs. (5) Heat from metabolically active organs (liver, muscles) to cooler areas, distributing body temperature. (6) Mineral ions and vitamins to where they are needed. (7) Plasma proteins (fibrinogen, albumin, globulins).
**Explanation**: Transport medium for all dissolved substances. A common error is saying plasma carries oxygen (O₂ carried by haemoglobin in RBCs).
