# Question: Question Q-1.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Mules (offspring of a horse and donkey) are sterile and cannot reproduce. Explain whether this means mules are not living organisms.
**Answer**: Mules are still living organisms because they show all other characteristics of life (movement, respiration, sensitivity, growth, excretion, nutrition). Reproduction is a characteristic of the species as a whole, not a requirement for every individual. Many individual organisms (sterile workers, post-menopausal humans) cannot reproduce but are clearly alive.
**Explanation**: Species-level vs individual characteristic. A common error is saying reproduction is required for every individual to be "alive."
