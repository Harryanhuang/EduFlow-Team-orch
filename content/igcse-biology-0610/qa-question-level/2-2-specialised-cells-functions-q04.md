# Question: Question Q-2.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How is a sperm cell adapted for its function?
**Answer**: Sperm cells fertilise egg cells. Adaptations: tail (flagellum) for swimming towards the egg; many mitochondria in the middle section to provide energy for movement; acrosome (enzyme-filled cap) in the head to break through the egg's outer layers; haploid nucleus with half the normal chromosome number.
**Explanation**: Tail, mitochondria, acrosome, haploid. A common error is saying sperm has a diploid nucleus.
