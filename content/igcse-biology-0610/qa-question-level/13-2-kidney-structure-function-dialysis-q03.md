# Question: Question Q-13.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the process of ultrafiltration in the glomerulus and Bowman's capsule.
**Answer**: Blood enters glomerulus via afferent arteriole at high pressure. Efferent arteriole is narrower → creates high hydrostatic pressure. This forces water, glucose, amino acids, urea, ions through capillary wall → basement membrane → podocyte filtration slits → Bowman's capsule. Large molecules (proteins, blood cells) are too large and remain in blood. Glomerular filtrate: ~125 cm³/min; >99% reabsorbed later. Ultrafiltration is passive (no energy).
**Explanation**: High pressure from afferent > efferent → forces small molecules through filter. Proteins/cells retained. A common error is saying proteins pass into filtrate.
