# Question: Question Q-15.3-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Describe two adaptations of sperm for swimming to the egg, and two adaptations of the egg for receiving the sperm.
**Answer**: Sperm adaptations: (1) Tail/flagellum — whips to propel the sperm through the female reproductive tract. (2) Many mitochondria in the middle piece — provide energy for swimming. (3) Acrosome at the tip — contains enzymes to digest through the egg's jelly coat. Egg adaptations: (1) Jelly coat (zona pellucida) — contains receptors that recognise the species-specific sperm and only allows one sperm to enter. (2) Large cytoplasm containing yolk — provides nutrients for the early embryo before implantation.
**Explanation**: Sperm = motile (tail, mitochondria, acrosome enzymes). Egg = non-motile (coat, yolk nutrients). A common error is saying the egg has a tail.
**Tags**: sperm, egg, adaptations, acrosome, zona-pellucida, yolk
