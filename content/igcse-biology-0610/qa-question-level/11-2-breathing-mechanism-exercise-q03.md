# Question: Question Q-11.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does exercise affect breathing rate and depth? Explain the physiological trigger.
**Answer**: During exercise, both breathing rate (breaths per minute) and depth (tidal volume) increase. The primary trigger is rising CO₂ concentration in the blood (not falling O₂). Muscle cells respire more → produce more CO₂ → CO₂ dissolves forming carbonic acid → lowers blood pH → detected by chemoreceptors in the medulla oblongata → the medulla sends impulses to diaphragm and intercostal muscles to contract more frequently and forcefully. Adrenaline also stimulates faster breathing.
**Explanation**: CO₂↑ → pH↓ → chemoreceptors → medulla → rate↑ + depth↑. A common error is saying the trigger is "need for oxygen."
