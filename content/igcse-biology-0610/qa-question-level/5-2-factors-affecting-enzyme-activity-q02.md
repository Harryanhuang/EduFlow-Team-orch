# Question: Question Q-5.2-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What happens to an enzyme if the temperature is raised too high above its optimum?
**Answer**: The enzyme denatures. The high temperature breaks the hydrogen and ionic bonds that maintain the enzyme's specific three-dimensional shape. The active site changes shape permanently, so the substrate can no longer fit. The enzyme loses its catalytic ability and cannot be restored — denaturation is usually irreversible.
**Explanation**: Shape changes, active site destroyed, irreversible. A common error is saying denaturation is "reversible on cooling."
