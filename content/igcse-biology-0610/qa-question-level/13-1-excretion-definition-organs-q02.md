# Question: Question Q-13.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the difference between excretion and egestion?
**Answer**: Excretion — removal of metabolic waste products (CO₂, urea) produced by chemical reactions inside cells. These were once part of the body's metabolism. Egestion — removal of undigested food material (faeces) from the alimentary canal. Faeces were never inside body cells; they were never absorbed. Key: excreted substances were inside cells/metabolic pathways; egested substances never entered cells.
**Explanation**: Metabolic waste (inside cells) vs undigested food (never absorbed). A common error is calling faeces "excreted."
