# Question: Question Q-12.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe an experiment to demonstrate anaerobic respiration in yeast. Include the method, expected results, and control.
**Answer**: Apparatus: conical flask with glucose solution + yeast suspension, sealed with delivery tube to limewater. A layer of oil on top of yeast-glucose mixture prevents O₂ entry (maintaining anaerobic conditions). Control: identical setup with boiled (dead) yeast. Leave both at 25-30°C for 1 hour. Results: live yeast — limewater turns milky (CO₂ produced). Control — limewater stays clear. Yeast smells of ethanol. Conclusion: respiring yeast produce CO₂ and ethanol anaerobically. Variation: collect gas in inverted measuring cylinder over water.
**Explanation**: Oil layer excludes O₂; limewater proves CO₂; dead yeast control. A common error is forgetting the oil layer.
