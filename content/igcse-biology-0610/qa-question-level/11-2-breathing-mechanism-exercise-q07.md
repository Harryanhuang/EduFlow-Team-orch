# Question: Question Q-11.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A student measures their breathing rate at rest and after running 400 metres. Explain the shape of the spirometer trace over a 15-minute period and the physiological reasons for each phase.
**Answer**: Phase 1 (rest, 0-2 min): regular low-amplitude trace — tidal volume ~0.5 dm³, rate ~12-15 breaths/min. Phase 2 (exercise, 2-4 min): dramatic increase in rate (~30-40 breaths/min) and depth (~2-3 dm³ tidal volume). CO₂ drive from medulla triggers this. Phase 3 (early recovery, 4-8 min): rate and depth gradually decrease but stay above resting — repaying oxygen debt (oxidising lactic acid, replenishing myoglobin O₂). Phase 4 (late recovery, 8-15 min): returns to resting. Total extra O₂ consumed during recovery equals the oxygen debt. Fitter person = faster recovery.
**Explanation**: Four-phase trace linked to CO₂ drive, O₂ debt, fitness. A common error is describing only rate without spirometer trace interpretation.
