# Question: Question Q-6.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how to test a leaf for the presence of starch.
**Answer**: Boil the leaf in water (to kill cells), then boil in ethanol (to remove chlorophyll — leaf turns white). Wash in warm water (to soften). Add iodine solution. If starch is present, the leaf turns blue-black. A control (leaf kept in darkness) stays orange/brown.
**Explanation**: Boil water → boil ethanol → iodine → blue-black. A common error is adding iodine directly to a green leaf.
