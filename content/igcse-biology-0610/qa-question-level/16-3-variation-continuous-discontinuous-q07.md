# Question: Question Q-16.3-07
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Explain the difference between gene mutations and chromosomal mutations. Give an example of each.
**Answer**: Gene mutations are changes in the DNA base sequence of a single gene. Point mutations (substitution of one base for another) are the most common, e.g., sickle cell anaemia (GAG → GTG in the haemoglobin gene, causing glutamic acid to be replaced by valine). Chromosomal mutations are changes in the structure or number of whole chromosomes. Examples: deletion (loss of part of a chromosome), duplication (extra copy of a segment), inversion (segment reversed), translocation (segment moved to another chromosome). Down syndrome is caused by an extra copy of chromosome 21 (trisomy 21).
**Explanation**: Gene = base sequence change; Chromosomal = whole chromosome structure/number change. A common error is calling chromosomal mutations "gene mutations."
