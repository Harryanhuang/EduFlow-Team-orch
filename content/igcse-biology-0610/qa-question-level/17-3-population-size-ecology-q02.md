# Question: Question Q-17.3-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What factors affect population size in an ecosystem?
**Answer**: Biotic factors: food availability (more food = larger population), predation (more predators = smaller population), competition (more competition = smaller population), disease. Abiotic factors: temperature, water availability, light, space. These factors determine the carrying capacity — the maximum population an environment can support.
**Explanation**: Biotic + abiotic factors, carrying capacity. A common error is listing only one type of factor.
**Tags**: population, biotic, abiotic, carrying-capacity
