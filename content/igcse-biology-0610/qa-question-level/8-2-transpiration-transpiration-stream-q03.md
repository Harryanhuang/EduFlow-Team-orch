# Question: Question Q-8.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How do temperature, humidity, wind speed, and light intensity affect the rate of transpiration?
**Answer**: Temperature ↑ → transpiration ↑ (more kinetic energy, faster evaporation). Humidity ↑ → transpiration ↓ (smaller water vapour concentration gradient between leaf and air). Wind speed ↑ → transpiration ↑ (water vapour removed from leaf surface, maintaining steep gradient). Light intensity ↑ → transpiration ↑ (stomata open wider for photosynthesis, increasing water vapour exit pathway).
**Explanation**: Four factors with direction and mechanism. A common error is saying humidity increases transpiration.
