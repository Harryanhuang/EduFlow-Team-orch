# Question: Q-15.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Name two examples of asexual reproduction in organisms and explain how each works.
**Answer**: (1) Bacteria — binary fission: the circular DNA replicates and the cell divides into two identical daughter cells. (2) Strawberry plants — runners: horizontal stems grow along the ground and produce new plants at nodes. (3) Potato — tubers: underground swollen stems with buds (eyes) that grow into new plants. (4) Yeast — budding: a small outgrowth develops and detaches to form a new cell.
**Explanation**: Bacteria, runners, tubers, budding are all valid. A common error is saying seed production is asexual.
