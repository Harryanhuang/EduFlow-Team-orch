# Question: Question Q-6.1-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the role of chlorophyll in photosynthesis?
**Answer**: Chlorophyll absorbs light energy (mainly red and blue wavelengths) and converts it to chemical energy. This energy is used to split water molecules and drive the reactions that produce glucose from carbon dioxide and water. Chlorophyll is found in chloroplasts.
**Explanation**: Absorbs light energy, converts to chemical. A common error is saying chlorophyll "makes" glucose.
