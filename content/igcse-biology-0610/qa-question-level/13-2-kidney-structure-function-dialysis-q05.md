# Question: Question Q-13.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does the composition of urine differ from blood plasma? Explain why each difference exists.
**Answer**: Urine vs plasma: urea high (~2%) vs low (~0.03%) — urea concentrated, water reabsorbed. Glucose 0% vs 0.1% — all actively reabsorbed in PCT. Protein 0% vs 7% — too large for glomerular filter. Blood cells 0% — too large for filter. Ions ~0.4% vs 0.7% — most reabsorbed, excess excreted. Water ~95% — variable depending on ADH. Urine = plasma minus proteins, blood cells, glucose, most water; urea concentrated ~70×. Glucose/protein in urine = diagnostic indicator.
**Explanation**: Comparative composition with reasons. A common error is saying urine normally contains glucose.
