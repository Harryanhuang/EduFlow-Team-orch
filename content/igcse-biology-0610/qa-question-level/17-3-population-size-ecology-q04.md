# Question: Question Q-17.3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What happens to a predator-prey population over time?
**Answer**: Predator and prey populations fluctuate in cycles. When prey is abundant, predator numbers increase. More predators eat more prey, so prey numbers decrease. Fewer prey means less food for predators, so predator numbers decrease. Fewer predators allows prey to recover, and the cycle repeats.
**Explanation**: Cyclic fluctuation, predator follows prey. A common error is saying both populations increase together.
