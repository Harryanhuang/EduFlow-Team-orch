# Question: Question Q-16.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: In snapdragons, flower colour is controlled by a single gene with two alleles showing incomplete dominance: CRCR = red, CRCW = pink, CWCW = white. A pink snapdragon is crossed with a white snapdragon. Draw a Punnett square and state all possible genotypes and phenotypes of the offspring.
**Answer**: Parents: CRCW (pink) × CWCW (white). Punnett square: CRCW, CRCW, CWCW, CWCW. Offspring genotypes: 50% CRCW, 50% CWCW. Offspring phenotypes: 50% pink flowers (CRCW), 50% white flowers (CWCW). There will be no red flowers because neither parent carries the CRCR genotype. The phenotypic ratio is 1 pink : 1 white (1:1).
**Explanation**: 1:1 ratio, incomplete dominance. A common error is producing a 3:1 ratio (which occurs with CRCW × CRCW).
