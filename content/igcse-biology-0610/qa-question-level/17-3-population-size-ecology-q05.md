# Question: Question Q-17.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is biodiversity and how can it be measured?
**Answer**: Biodiversity is the variety of species in an area. It can be measured by: (1) Species richness — the number of different species present. (2) Species evenness — how evenly individuals are distributed among species. High biodiversity indicates a healthy, stable ecosystem. Low biodiversity makes ecosystems vulnerable.
**Explanation**: Richness + evenness, healthy ecosystem. A common error is counting only the number of organisms.
**Tags**: biodiversity, species-richness, evenness, ecosystem
