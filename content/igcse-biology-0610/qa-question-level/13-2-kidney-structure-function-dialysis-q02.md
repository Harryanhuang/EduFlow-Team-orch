# Question: Question Q-13.2-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the function of the nephron in the kidney?
**Answer**: The nephron is the functional unit (~1 million per kidney). Three steps: (1) Ultrafiltration — blood filtered in glomerulus/Bowman's capsule under pressure → filtrate (water, glucose, ions, urea; NO proteins/cells). (2) Selective reabsorption — all glucose, most water, needed ions reabsorbed in PCT. (3) Urine concentration — urea, excess water, excess ions become urine in collecting duct.
**Explanation**: Nephron = filter + reabsorb + concentrate. A common error is saying nephron "makes urea."
