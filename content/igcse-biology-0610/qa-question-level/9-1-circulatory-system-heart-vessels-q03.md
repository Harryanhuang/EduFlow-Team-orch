# Question: Question Q-9.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How are arteries adapted for their function?
**Answer**: Arteries have thick, muscular walls to withstand high blood pressure from the heart. They contain elastic fibres that stretch and recoil to maintain blood pressure between heartbeats. The lumen is relatively narrow to maintain pressure. They do not have valves (except semilunar valves at the heart exit).
**Explanation**: Thick walls, muscle, elastic. A common error is saying arteries have valves.
