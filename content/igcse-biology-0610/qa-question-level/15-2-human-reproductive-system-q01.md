# Question: Question Q-15.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Name the main parts of the female reproductive system and state one function of each.
**Answer**: Ovaries — produce eggs (ova) and female sex hormones (oestrogen, progesterone). Oviduct (Fallopian tube) — carries the egg from the ovary to the uterus; site of fertilisation. Uterus (womb) — where the embryo implants and develops. Cervix — ring of muscle at the neck of the uterus that keeps the baby in place during pregnancy. Vagina — birth canal; receives sperm during copulation.
**Explanation**: Functions of ovaries, oviduct, uterus, cervix and vagina. A common error is saying fertilisation occurs in the uterus.
