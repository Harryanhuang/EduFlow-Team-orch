# Question: Question Q-11.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain how anaerobic respiration relates to breathing changes during intense exercise.
**Answer**: During intense exercise, O₂ demand exceeds O₂ supply. Muscles switch to anaerobic respiration: glucose → lactic acid (+ small ATP). This produces energy without O₂ but is inefficient (2 ATP per glucose vs 38 ATP aerobically) and produces lactic acid as a toxic by-product causing muscle fatigue. Breathing cannot supply enough O₂ fast enough during the activity itself. After exercise, the oxygen debt is repaid — extra O₂ is used to oxidise lactic acid back to pyruvate → CO₂ + H₂O (in liver) or convert it to glucose. This is why breathing stays elevated during recovery.
**Explanation**: Anaerobic→lactic acid→O₂ debt→elevated post-exercise breathing. A common error is saying anaerobic replaces aerobic — they occur simultaneously.
