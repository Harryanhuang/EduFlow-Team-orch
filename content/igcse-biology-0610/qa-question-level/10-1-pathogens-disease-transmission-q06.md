# Question: Question Q-10.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how Salmonella bacteria cause disease and how infection can be prevented.
**Answer**: Salmonella causes food poisoning (salmonellosis). Transmission: contaminated food (especially raw/undercooked eggs, poultry, unpasteurised milk) or contaminated water. Mechanism: bacteria multiply in the intestines and secrete toxins that damage the gut lining, causing inflammation. Symptoms: vomiting, diarrhoea, abdominal cramps, fever (onset 12-72 hours after ingestion). Prevention: thorough cooking (>70°C kills bacteria), proper refrigeration, separate raw and cooked food, hand washing before food preparation, avoiding raw eggs.
**Explanation**: Bacteria multiply in gut → toxins → symptoms. Prevention: cook, chill, separate, clean. A common error is saying Salmonella is a virus.
