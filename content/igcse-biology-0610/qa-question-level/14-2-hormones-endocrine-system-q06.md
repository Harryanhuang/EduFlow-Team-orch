# Question: Question Q-14.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is Type 1 diabetes, what causes it, and how is it treated?
**Answer**: Type 1 diabetes: autoimmune destruction of insulin-producing β-cells in pancreatic islets → little/no insulin. Blood glucose rises dangerously after eating. Symptoms: thirst (polydipsia), frequent urination (polyuria — glucose in urine draws water), tiredness (cells starved of glucose), weight loss. Treatment: regular insulin injections (not tablet — protein would be digested), blood glucose monitoring, carbohydrate counting, healthy lifestyle. NOT caused by diet — it's autoimmune. Lifelong, not curable.
**Explanation**: Autoimmune β-cell destruction → hyperglycaemia → insulin injections.A common error is saying Type 1 is caused by eating sugar.
