# Question: Question Q-15.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the process of fertilisation in humans.
**Answer**: Fertilisation occurs when a sperm cell fuses with an egg cell in the oviduct (Fallopian tube). The acrosome of the sperm releases enzymes to digest the jelly coat/zona pellucida. The sperm nucleus enters the egg and fuses with the egg nucleus to form a zygote with a full set of chromosomes (46 in humans). The egg membrane changes to prevent other sperm from entering. The zygote divides by mitosis as it travels to the uterus.
**Explanation**: Sperm + egg → zygote in oviduct; membrane changes; mitosis begins. A common error is saying fertilisation happens in the uterus.
