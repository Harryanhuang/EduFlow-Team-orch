# Question: Question Q-16.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare the processes of DNA replication and protein synthesis. Include where each occurs in the cell and what molecules are involved.
**Answer**: DNA replication occurs in the nucleus during S phase of the cell cycle. The double helix unwinds, and DNA polymerase adds complementary nucleotides along each strand, producing two identical DNA molecules. Protein synthesis has two stages: (1) Transcription — in the nucleus, DNA unzips and mRNA is built using complementary base pairing (A→U, T→A, C→G, G→C). (2) Translation — mRNA moves to the ribosome in the cytoplasm. tRNA molecules bring amino acids, pairing anticodons with mRNA codons to build the polypeptide chain. Replication = DNA → DNA; Protein synthesis = DNA → mRNA → protein.
**Explanation**: Replication in nucleus, transcription + translation for protein synthesis. A common error is confusing the roles of mRNA and tRNA.
