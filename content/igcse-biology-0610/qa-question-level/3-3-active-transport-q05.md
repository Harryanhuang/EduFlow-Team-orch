# Question: Question Q-3.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why a plant placed in a solution containing a respiratory poison cannot absorb mineral ions from the soil.
**Answer**: The respiratory poison stops respiration in root cells. Active transport of mineral ions requires energy from respiration. Without respiration, there is no energy for active transport. Mineral ions cannot be absorbed against their concentration gradient. Water absorption by osmosis is unaffected.
**Explanation**: No respiration = no energy = no active transport. A common error is saying osmosis is also affected.
