# Question: Question Q-10.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how HIV is transmitted and why it cannot be transmitted by casual contact.
**Answer**: HIV is transmitted through direct exchange of body fluids: (1) unprotected sexual contact, (2) sharing contaminated needles, (3) from mother to baby during pregnancy, birth, or breastfeeding, (4) contaminated blood transfusions. HIV cannot survive long outside the body — it cannot be transmitted by casual contact (touching, sharing food, hugging, kissing, toilet seats) because saliva, tears, sweat, and urine contain negligible virus, and intact skin blocks entry.
**Explanation**: Body fluid exchange only; casual contact ≠ transmission. A common error is including sharing utensils.
