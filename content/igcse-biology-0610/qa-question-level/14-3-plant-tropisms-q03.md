# Question: Question Q-14.3-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What role does auxin play in phototropism? Explain the mechanism.
**Answer**: Auxin (IAA) is produced in the shoot tip. In unilateral light: auxin redistributes from the lit side to the shaded side, travels down the shaded side, and stimulates cell elongation by loosening cell walls (expansins). Cells on the shaded side elongate more than cells on the lit side → shoot bends toward light. Tip detects light; response zone bends.
**Explanation**: Auxin redistributes to shaded side → more elongation → bend toward light. A common error is saying auxin is destroyed by light.
