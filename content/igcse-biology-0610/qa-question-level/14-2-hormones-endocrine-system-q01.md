# Question: Question Q-14.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is a hormone? Name three glands and the hormone each produces.
**Answer**: A hormone is a chemical messenger produced by an endocrine gland, secreted directly into the bloodstream, and carried to target organs where it produces a specific effect. Examples: Pancreas — insulin + glucagon; Adrenal glands — adrenaline; Pituitary — ADH, growth hormone; Thyroid — thyroxine; Ovaries/testes — oestrogen/testosterone. Hormones regulate long-term processes: growth, metabolism, reproduction, homeostasis.
**Explanation**: Chemical messenger, gland → blood → target, specific effect. A common error is saying hormones travel in nerves.
