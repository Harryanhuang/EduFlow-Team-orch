# Question: Question Q-3.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe what happens to an animal cell (e.g., red blood cell) placed in pure water.
**Answer**: Water enters the cell by osmosis. The cell swells and eventually bursts (lyses) because it has no cell wall to resist the pressure. Pure water has a higher water potential than the cell cytoplasm.
**Explanation**: Swells and bursts, no cell wall. A common error is saying it becomes "turgid" (turgid applies to plant cells only).
