# Question: Question Q-14.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the role of insulin and glucagon in controlling blood glucose using negative feedback.
**Answer**: Dual-hormone negative feedback: High glucose (after meal): β-cells in pancreatic islets → secrete insulin → liver: glucose→glycogen (glycogenesis), muscle: GLUT4 transporters → glucose uptake. Blood glucose ↓ to set point. Low glucose (between meals): α-cells → secrete glucagon → liver: glycogen→glucose (glycogenolysis) + amino acids→glucose (gluconeogenesis). Blood glucose ↑ to set point. Both stop at set point. Insulin and glucagon are antagonistic.
**Explanation**: Insulin (β-cells) ↓glucose; Glucagon (α-cells) ↑glucose. Dual antagonistic negative feedback. A common error is reversing which hormone does what.
