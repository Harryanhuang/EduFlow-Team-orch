# Question: Question Q-2.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain how the circulatory system and respiratory system work together as organ systems.
**Answer**: The respiratory system brings oxygen into the lungs (alveoli) and removes carbon dioxide. The circulatory system transports oxygen from the lungs to all body cells (via red blood cells) and carries carbon dioxide from cells back to the lungs. The two systems work together — gas exchange in the lungs connects them: oxygen diffuses from alveoli into blood capillaries; CO₂ diffuses from blood into alveoli.
**Explanation**: Two systems linked at lungs. A common error is saying one system "does everything."
