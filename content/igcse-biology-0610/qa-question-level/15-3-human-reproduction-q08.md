# Question: Question Q-15.3-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A woman discovers she is pregnant. Describe the sequence of development from fertilisation to the end of the first trimester (12 weeks), including the roles of the placenta and hormones.
**Answer**: Weeks 1–2: Fertilisation in oviduct → zygote → morula → blastocyst → implantation in endometrium. The placenta begins to form from chorionic villi. hCG (human chorionic gonadotropin) is secreted by the developing placenta — this maintains the corpus luteum, which continues secreting progesterone to prevent menstruation and maintain the endometrium. Weeks 3–8 (embryonic period): Major organs begin to form — neural tube (brain and spinal cord), heart, circulatory system. The embryo is most vulnerable to teratogens (alcohol, rubella, nicotine) during this period. The placenta develops fully and takes over progesterone/oestrogen production from the corpus luteum by week 10. Weeks 9–12 (foetal period): All major organs are formed; the foetus grows rapidly. Limbs, fingers, toes develop. By week 12: the foetus is about 5 cm long, can move, and the heartbeat is detectable. The amniotic sac continues to protect and cushion the foetus throughout.
**Explanation**: Fertilisation → implantation → organogenesis (weeks 3–8) → placental takeover → foetal growth. hCG, progesterone, oestrogen roles. A common error is saying the placenta forms immediately at fertilisation.
