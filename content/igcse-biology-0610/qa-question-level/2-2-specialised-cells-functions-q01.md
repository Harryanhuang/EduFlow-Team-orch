# Question: Question Q-2.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is a specialised cell? Give two examples.
**Answer**: A specialised cell has a specific structure adapted for a particular function. Examples: red blood cells (carrying oxygen — biconcave shape for large surface area, no nucleus for more haemoglobin), root hair cells (absorbing water — long projection for large surface area), nerve cells (transmitting impulses — long axon), sperm cells (fertilisation — tail for swimming).
**Explanation**: Structure adapted for function. A common error is describing a general cell instead of a specialised one.
