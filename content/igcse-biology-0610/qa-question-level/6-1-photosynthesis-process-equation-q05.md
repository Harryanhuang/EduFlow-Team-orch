# Question: Question Q-6.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What happens to the glucose produced by photosynthesis?
**Answer**: Glucose is used for: (1) respiration — to release energy. (2) Making starch for storage. (3) Making cellulose for cell walls. (4) Making proteins (combined with nitrate ions). (5) Making fats/oils for storage in seeds. (6) Transported as sucrose to other parts of the plant.
**Explanation**: Respiration, starch, cellulose, proteins, fats, transport. A common error is saying glucose is "stored as glucose."
