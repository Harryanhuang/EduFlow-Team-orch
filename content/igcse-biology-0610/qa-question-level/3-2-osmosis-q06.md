# Question: Question Q-3.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What happens to a plant cell placed in a concentrated sugar solution?
**Answer**: Water leaves the cell by osmosis (water potential is higher inside the cell). The vacuole shrinks, the cytoplasm pulls away from the cell wall, and the cell becomes flaccid (plasmolysed). The plant wilts.
**Explanation**: Plasmolysis, water leaves. A common error is saying water enters.
**Tags**: osmosis, plant-cell, plasmolysis, flaccid
