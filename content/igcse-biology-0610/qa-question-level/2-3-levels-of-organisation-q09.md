# Question: Question Q-2.3-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: The stomach contains muscular tissue, epithelial tissue, glandular tissue, and connective tissue. Explain the function of each tissue and how their combined action allows the stomach to carry out its role in digestion.
**Answer**: Muscular tissue: contracts (peristalsis) to churn food and mix it with gastric juices. Epithelial tissue: lines the stomach and protects it from acid corrosion. Glandular tissue: secretes hydrochloric acid (kills bacteria, provides acidic pH) and pepsin enzyme (begins protein digestion). Connective tissue: holds the stomach together and provides structure. Their combined action: muscles churn food while glands secrete enzymes and acid onto it; epithelial tissue protects the organ from self-digestion; connective tissue maintains structure. The coordination of all four tissues allows the stomach to mechanically and chemically digest food simultaneously.
**Explanation**: Four tissues, coordinated function. A common error is listing the tissues without explaining how they work together.
