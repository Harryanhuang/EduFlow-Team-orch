# Question: Question Q-16.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is mitosis and why is it important?
**Answer**: Mitosis is cell division that produces two genetically identical daughter cells, each with the same number of chromosomes as the parent cell. It is important for: growth (increasing the number of cells), repair (replacing damaged cells), and asexual reproduction (producing identical offspring).
**Explanation**: Two identical cells, growth, repair, asexual. A common error is saying mitosis produces "different" cells.
