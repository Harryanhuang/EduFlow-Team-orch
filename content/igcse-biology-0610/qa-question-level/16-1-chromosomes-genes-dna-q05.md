# Question: Question Q-16.1-05
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: What is meiosis and how does it differ from mitosis?
**Answer**: Meiosis is cell division that produces four genetically different haploid cells (gametes), each with half the number of chromosomes of the parent cell. Unlike mitosis (2 identical diploid cells), meiosis creates variation through the random mixing of chromosomes. Meiosis occurs only in the testes and ovaries.
**Explanation**: Four different haploid cells, gametes, variation. A common error is saying meiosis produces identical cells.
**Tags**: meiosis, gametes, variation, haploid
