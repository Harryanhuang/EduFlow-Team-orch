# Question: Question Q-6.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how increasing light intensity affects the rate of photosynthesis, referring to the shape of the graph.
**Answer**: At low light intensity, the rate of photosynthesis increases proportionally with light (straight line on graph). The rate then levels off to a plateau — at this point, CO₂ concentration or temperature has become the limiting factor. Increasing light beyond this point has no effect on the rate. If CO₂ or temperature were then increased, the rate could rise further until a new limiting factor takes over.
**Explanation**: Linear rise → plateau; another factor limits. A common error is saying the rate keeps increasing indefinitely.
