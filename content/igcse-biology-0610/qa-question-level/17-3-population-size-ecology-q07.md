# Question: Question Q-17.3-07
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Describe how a pyramid of biomass differs from a pyramid of numbers and explain when pyramids of numbers might appear inverted.
**Answer**: A pyramid of biomass shows the dry mass of living material at each trophic level (grams per square metre). It is always pyramid-shaped because energy is lost at each level. A pyramid of numbers shows the number of individual organisms at each level, which may not form a regular pyramid. Pyramids of numbers can appear inverted (narrow base, wide top) when: producers are large but few (e.g., a single tree supporting many insects), or when parasites outnumber their hosts. In such cases, the pyramid of numbers is misleading about energy transfer. The pyramid of biomass is more reliable because it reflects the amount of energy available at each level, not just the count of organisms.
**Explanation**: Biomass = always pyramid; Numbers = can be inverted. A common error is assuming pyramids of numbers always match energy transfer.
