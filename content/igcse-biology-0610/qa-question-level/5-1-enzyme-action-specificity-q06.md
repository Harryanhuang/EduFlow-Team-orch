# Question: Question Q-5.1-06
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A student adds the enzyme amylase to a starch solution. After 10 minutes, no starch is detected. Explain what happened and why adding more starch would restart the reaction.
**Answer**: Amylase catalysed the breakdown of starch into maltose. All starch was converted. Adding more starch restarts the reaction because the amylase enzymes are unchanged and still available — they were not used up. More substrate molecules can bind to the active sites.
**Explanation**: Enzymes reusable, substrate depleted. A common error is saying the enzyme was "used up."
