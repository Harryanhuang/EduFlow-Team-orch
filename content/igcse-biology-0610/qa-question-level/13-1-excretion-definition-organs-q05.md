# Question: Question Q-13.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is carbon dioxide considered a waste product that must be excreted?
**Answer**: CO₂ is a waste product of aerobic respiration. If it accumulates: CO₂ dissolves in blood plasma → forms carbonic acid (H₂CO₃) → dissociates to H⁺ + HCO₃⁻ → lowers blood pH (respiratory acidosis, normal pH 7.35-7.45) → enzymes denature → cellular metabolism disrupted. CO₂ is transported to lungs (as HCO₃⁻, carbamino-Hb, dissolved) and excreted by exhalation. The breathing rate is primarily controlled by blood CO₂/pH (not O₂) — demonstrating the toxicity priority of CO₂ removal.
**Explanation**: CO₂ → H₂CO₃ → H⁺ → pH↓ → enzyme disruption. CO₂ drives breathing rate. A common error is saying CO₂ is "not harmful."
