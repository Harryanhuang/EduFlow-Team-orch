# Question: Question Q-8.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why do plants wilt when they lose more water by transpiration than they absorb?
**Answer**: When water loss exceeds absorption, cells lose water by osmosis and become flaccid. Turgor pressure drops, so cells can no longer support the plant structure. Leaves and stems droop (wilt). This is a temporary protection — wilting reduces leaf surface area exposed to sun, reducing further transpiration. If the water deficit continues, cells may die and the plant cannot recover.
**Explanation**: Water loss → flaccid cells → no turgor → wilting. A common error is saying wilting is caused by "too much sun."
