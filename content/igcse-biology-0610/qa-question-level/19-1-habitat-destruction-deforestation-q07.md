# Question: Question Q-19.1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is acid rain and how is it formed?
**Answer**: Acid rain is rain with a pH below 5.6, formed when sulfur dioxide (from burning fossil fuels with sulfur impurities) and nitrogen oxides (from vehicle exhausts) dissolve in rainwater to form sulfuric and nitric acid. Effects: damages trees, acidifies lakes (killing fish), erodes limestone buildings, and damages soil nutrients.
**Explanation**: SO₂ + NOx → sulfuric + nitric acid. A common error is saying CO₂ causes acid rain (it causes ocean acidification, not acid rain).
