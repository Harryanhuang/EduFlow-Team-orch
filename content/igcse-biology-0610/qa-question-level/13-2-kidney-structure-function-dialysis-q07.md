# Question: Question Q-13.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare kidney transplant and dialysis as treatments for kidney failure.
**Answer**: Transplant: ADV — no dialysis sessions, fewer dietary restrictions, better quality of life, lower long-term cost. DIS — lifelong immunosuppressants (infection risk), risk of rejection, donor shortage, major surgery. Dialysis: ADV — no immunosuppressants, no surgery, immediately available. DIS — 12+ hr/week on machine, strict diet (low protein/salt/fluid), fatigue, vascular access complications, does not fully replace kidney function (anaemia, bone disease). Transplant = better quality of life + cost-effective long-term; dialysis = bridge until transplant available.
**Explanation**: Balanced pros/cons. A common error is saying transplant "requires no drugs."
