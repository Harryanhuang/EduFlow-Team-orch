# Question: Question Q-3.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does temperature affect the rate of diffusion? Explain your answer.
**Answer**: Higher temperature increases the rate of diffusion. Particles have more kinetic energy at higher temperatures, so they move faster and diffuse more quickly. The concentration gradient is steeper at the start.
**Explanation**: More kinetic energy = faster movement. A common error is saying temperature "widens" the concentration gradient.
