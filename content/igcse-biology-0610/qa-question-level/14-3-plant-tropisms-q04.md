# Question: Question Q-14.3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What role does auxin play in geotropism?
**Answer**: In a horizontal root or shoot, auxin accumulates on the lower side (guided by statoliths). In SHOOTS: higher auxin stimulates cell elongation → lower side grows faster → shoot bends upward (negative geotropism). In ROOTS: higher auxin inhibits cell elongation (roots are more sensitive) → upper side grows faster → root bends downward (positive geotropism). Same auxin, opposite effects due to different sensitivity.
**Explanation**: Auxin to lower side → stimulates shoot, inhibits root. Sensitivity difference. A common error is saying auxin has the same effect in shoots and roots.
