# Question: Question Q-16.3-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the difference between continuous and discontinuous variation?
**Answer**: Continuous variation shows a range of values with no distinct categories (e.g., height, weight, hand span). It is influenced by both genes and environment, and results are shown as a bell curve. Discontinuous variation has distinct categories with no intermediates (e.g., blood group, eye colour, ability to roll tongue). It is controlled by genes only.
**Explanation**: Range vs categories, genes+environment vs genes only. A common error is saying continuous variation is "only genetic."
**Tags**: continuous-variation, discontinuous-variation, genes, environment
