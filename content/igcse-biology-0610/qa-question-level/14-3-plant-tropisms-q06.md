# Question: Question Q-14.3-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe an experiment to demonstrate geotropism in roots and shoots.
**Answer**: Use a clinostat: germinating bean seeds on a vertical disc rotated slowly (1 rev/hour) so gravity acts equally from all directions. Control: identical seeds on a stationary disc. Both in darkness (eliminates phototropism), watered, 2-3 days. Result: clinostat — roots and shoots grow straight out; control — roots grow downward (+ve geotropism), shoots grow upward (-ve geotropism). Conclusion: gravity detected by statoliths is required for directional growth.
**Explanation**: Clinostat cancels gravity → straight growth. A common error is forgetting to control for light.
