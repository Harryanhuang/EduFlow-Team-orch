# Question: Question Q-12.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain the economic importance of anaerobic respiration in yeast, giving specific examples of how different fermentation conditions produce different products.
**Answer**: (1) Bread-making: yeast + flour starch → CO₂ + ethanol. CO₂ trapped in gluten → dough rises. Ethanol evaporates during baking. ~27°C optimal. (2) Beer brewing: yeast + malted barley → ethanol + CO₂. Hops for flavour. Anaerobic sealed vat. 3-8% ethanol. (3) Wine-making: yeast + grape juice → ethanol + CO₂. 9-15% ethanol. Fermentation stops when ethanol reaches ~15% (yeast enzymes denatured). (4) Biofuel: yeast + sugarcane/corn → ethanol, distilled >95% for fuel. (5) Soy sauce/yoghurt: different microorganisms (Lactobacillus, Aspergillus) with different pathways. Same principle — anaerobic respiration by microorganisms — underpins all, with substrate, temperature, strain, and duration determining the product.
**Explanation**: Five applications with process conditions. A common error is listing products without explaining how conditions affect outcome.
