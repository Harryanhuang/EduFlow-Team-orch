# Question: Question Q-17.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain why energy cannot be recycled in an ecosystem, unlike nutrients. What are the implications for the length of food chains?
**Answer**: Energy flows through ecosystems in a one-way path — it enters as sunlight, is converted to chemical energy by photosynthesis, and is ultimately lost as heat at each trophic level through respiration. This heat energy dissipates into the atmosphere and cannot be recaptured by living organisms. Unlike nutrients (carbon, nitrogen), energy cannot be recycled. Only about 10% of energy at each trophic level is passed to the next level; 90% is lost as heat, in waste, and in uneaten parts. Because energy is continuously lost and cannot be replenished from within the system, there is insufficient energy to support many trophic levels. Most food chains have 4-5 levels at most.
**Explanation**: Energy flows one way, lost as heat; nutrients cycle. A common error is saying energy is "recycled like nutrients."
