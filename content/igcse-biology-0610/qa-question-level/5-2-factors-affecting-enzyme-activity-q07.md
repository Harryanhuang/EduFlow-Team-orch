# Question: Question Q-5.2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why do enzymes have an optimum temperature rather than a single temperature?
**Answer**: The "optimum" is a range (not a single point) because enzyme activity gradually increases with temperature (more kinetic energy) up to a peak, then gradually decreases as denaturation begins. The exact optimum varies between enzymes — human enzymes ~37°C, thermophilic bacteria enzymes ~70°C.
**Explanation**: Range, gradual increase/decrease, varies by enzyme. A common error is saying optimum is one exact temperature.
