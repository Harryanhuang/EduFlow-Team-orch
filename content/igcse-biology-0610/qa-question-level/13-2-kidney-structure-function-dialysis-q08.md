# Question: Question Q-13.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain how the nephron's structure is adapted for its function, referring to specific regions.
**Answer**: (1) Glomerulus — coiled capillary, afferent > efferent diameter → high pressure for ultrafiltration. Fenestrated capillary walls allow passage. (2) Bowman's capsule — podocytes with filtration slits; basement membrane as molecular filter. (3) PCT — microvilli (surface area), many mitochondria (ATP for active transport), tight junctions (prevent back-leak). (4) Loop of Henle — descending limb water-permeable (osmosis out); ascending limb water-impermeable, actively transports Na⁺ out → medullary concentration gradient. (5) Collecting duct — passes through salty medulla; ADH controls water permeability → fine-tunes urine concentration. Each region's structure directly enables its specific filtration→reabsorption→concentration step.
**Explanation**: Five regions with adaptation-function pairs. A common error is listing parts without linking to function.
