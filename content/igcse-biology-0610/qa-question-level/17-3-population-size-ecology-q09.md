# Question: Question Q-17.3-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the mark-release-recapture method for estimating population size. What assumptions must be made and how do violations of these assumptions affect the accuracy of the estimate?
**Answer**: The mark-release-recapture method estimates population size of mobile organisms: (1) Capture a sample of organisms and mark them without harming them (e.g., tags, paint). (2) Release them back into the environment. (3) After sufficient time for mixing, capture another sample. (4) Count marked (recaptured) and unmarked individuals. The formula: Population = (N₁ × N₂) / M, where N₁ = first capture, N₂ = second capture, M = marked recaptures. Assumptions: (1) Marked individuals mix randomly with the population — if they stay near release point, estimate is inaccurate. (2) No birth, death, immigration, or emigration during the study — if individuals enter or leave, estimate changes. (3) Marks are not lost or altered — if marks fade, M is underestimated, inflating population estimate. (4) Sampling is random — if traps preferentially catch certain individuals, estimate is biased.
**Explanation**: Lincoln-Petersen formula, assumptions about mixing and stability. A common error is saying the method gives an exact population count.
