# Question: Question Q-15.3-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the process of fertilisation in humans, from copulation to the formation of the zygote.
**Answer**: During copulation, semen containing millions of sperm is deposited in the vagina. Sperm swim through the cervix and uterus into the oviduct. At the oviduct, many sperm surround the egg. The acrosome of each sperm releases enzymes to digest the jelly coat (zona pellucida) of the egg. One sperm penetrates through to the egg cell membrane. The sperm nucleus enters the egg and fuses with the egg nucleus to form a diploid zygote (46 chromosomes). The egg membrane immediately changes to prevent any other sperm from entering (block to polyspermy). The zygote then divides by mitosis (cleavage) as it travels down the oviduct towards the uterus.
**Explanation**: Sperm travel → acrosome reaction → one sperm enters → nuclear fusion → zygote. A common error is saying fertilisation occurs in the uterus.
