# Question: Question Q-13.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How is urea produced in the body? Explain the role of the liver.
**Answer**: Urea is produced in the liver via deamination. Excess amino acids (from protein digestion exceeding need for protein synthesis) cannot be stored. In liver hepatocytes, the amino group (–NH₂) is removed (deamination) → converted to ammonia (NH₃, highly toxic) → rapidly converted to urea (CO(NH₂)₂, less toxic) via the ornithine cycle. The remaining carbon skeleton (keto acid) is used in respiration or converted to glycogen/fat. Urea enters blood → transported to kidneys → filtered out in urine. This is a detoxification pathway.
**Explanation**: Deamination: amino acid → NH₃ → urea (ornithine cycle). Keto acid → energy. A common error is saying urea comes from "digestion."
