# Question: Question Q-7.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A child in a developing country has a swollen belly, thinning hair, and is underweight. The child eats mainly rice. Identify the likely deficiency condition and explain the cause and treatment.
**Answer**: The condition is kwashiorkor — severe protein deficiency. The child gets enough calories from rice (carbohydrates) but insufficient protein. Protein is needed to make blood plasma proteins (albumin), which maintain osmotic balance. Without enough albumin, fluid leaks from blood into tissues, causing the swollen belly (oedema). Hair thins because keratin (a protein) cannot be made. Treatment: gradually introduce protein-rich foods (eggs, milk, legumes, fish) and ensure a balanced diet. Complications (infections) should be treated simultaneously.
**Explanation**: Kwashiorkor = protein deficiency despite adequate calories → oedema, hair loss. A common error is confusing kwashiorkor with marasmus (total calorie + protein deficiency).
