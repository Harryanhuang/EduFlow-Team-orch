# Question: Question Q-13.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Compare the roles of the liver and kidneys in excretion.
**Answer**: Liver — metabolic processing: deaminates excess amino acids → urea; breaks down Hb from old RBCs → bilirubin (excreted via bile into faeces); detoxifies alcohol, drugs, hormones → products excreted by kidneys. Kidneys — filtration and excretion: filter urea, excess water, excess ions from blood; form urine; regulate water potential (osmoregulation). The liver converts toxic substances into excretable forms; the kidneys physically filter and remove them. Complementary — neither can function without the other.
**Explanation**: Liver = metabolic processing/detox; Kidney = filtration/excretion. Complementary. A common error is treating them as independent.
