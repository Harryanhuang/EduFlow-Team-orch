# Question: Question Q-8.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How do guard cells control the opening and closing of stomata?
**Answer**: In bright light, guard cells actively transport K⁺ ions in, lowering water potential. Water enters by osmosis and guard cells become turgid. Their unevenly thickened walls cause them to bend outward, opening the stoma. At night or in water shortage, K⁺ ions are pumped out, water follows by osmosis, guard cells become flaccid and the stoma closes. This regulates CO₂ entry for photosynthesis and water loss by transpiration.
**Explanation**: Turgid = open, flaccid = close, K⁺ ion pump mechanism. A common error is saying stomata open at night.
