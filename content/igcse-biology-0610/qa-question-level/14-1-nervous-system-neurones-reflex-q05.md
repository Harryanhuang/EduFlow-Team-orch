# Question: Question Q-14.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the structure of a motor neurone and explain how each part relates to function.
**Answer**: (1) Cell body — nucleus + organelles; in CNS. (2) Dendrites — short branched; receive impulses from relay neurones. (3) Axon — very long (up to 1 m); carries impulse away from cell body to effector. (4) Myelin sheath — Schwann cell layers; insulates → saltatory conduction → speed 1→100 m/s. (5) Nodes of Ranvier — gaps; concentrated Na⁺ channels → action potential regenerated. (6) Axon terminals — mitochondria + neurotransmitter vesicles; transmit signal to effector across synapse.
**Explanation**: Six parts mapped to function. A common error is confusing motor (cell body at end) with sensory (mid-axon ganglion).
