# Question: Question Q-8.2-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the transpiration stream?
**Answer**: The transpiration stream is the continuous flow of water from the roots, through the xylem vessels, up the stem to the leaves. It is driven by transpiration at the leaves (evaporation creates suction/tension). Water molecules are cohesive (stick together via hydrogen bonds) so the pull from the top draws the entire water column upwards. It delivers water for photosynthesis and dissolved mineral ions to all plant cells.
**Explanation**: Continuous water column from root to leaf, driven by evaporation. A common error is saying water is "pumped" from the roots.
