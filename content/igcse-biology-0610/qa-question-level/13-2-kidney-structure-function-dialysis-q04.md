# Question: Question Q-13.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain what happens during selective reabsorption in the proximal convoluted tubule.
**Answer**: In the PCT: all glucose reabsorbed by active transport + facilitated diffusion (ATP). Most amino acids, vitamins, hormones reabsorbed by active transport. ~80% water reabsorbed by osmosis (follows solutes). Most Na⁺, Cl⁻ reabsorbed by active transport. PCT cells adapted: microvilli (surface area), many mitochondria (ATP), numerous carrier proteins. After PCT: filtrate still has urea, some water/ions. Glucose in urine → diabetes mellitus (exceeds kidney threshold).
**Explanation**: All glucose + most water/ions reabsorbed. PCT cells adapted with microvilli + mitochondria. A common error is saying urea is reabsorbed.
