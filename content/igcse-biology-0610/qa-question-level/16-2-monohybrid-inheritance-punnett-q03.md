# Question: Question Q-16.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In pea plants, tall (T) is dominant over short (t). Draw a Punnett square for a cross between two heterozygous tall plants. State the phenotypic ratio.
**Answer**: Parent genotypes: Tt × Tt. Punnett square: TT, Tt, Tt, tt. Phenotypic ratio: 3 tall : 1 short. Genotypic ratio: 1 TT : 2 Tt : 1 tt. The recessive phenotype (short) only appears in the homozygous recessive genotype (tt).
**Explanation**: 3:1 phenotypic ratio. A common error is saying the ratio is 1:1.
