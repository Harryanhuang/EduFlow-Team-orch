# Question: Question Q-18.2-06
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What are the risks of genetic engineering?
**Answer**: Risks include: (1) Unintended effects — inserting a gene may disrupt other genes or cause unexpected traits. (2) Gene transfer — engineered genes may spread to wild populations (e.g., herbicide resistance in weeds). (3) Reduced biodiversity — GM crops may replace traditional varieties. (4) Ethical concerns — "playing God," animal welfare. (5) Allergic reactions — new proteins may cause allergies.
**Explanation**: Unintended effects, gene spread, biodiversity, ethics, allergies. A common error is saying GM foods are "proven safe" or "proven unsafe."
