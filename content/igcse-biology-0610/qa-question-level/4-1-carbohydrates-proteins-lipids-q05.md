# Question: Question Q-4.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why is glycogen an important storage molecule in animals?
**Answer**: Glycogen is a polysaccharide made of many glucose molecules joined together. It is stored in the liver and muscles. When blood glucose levels drop, glycogen is broken down to release glucose for respiration. It is compact and can be quickly mobilised.
**Explanation**: Glucose storage, liver/muscles, energy release. A common error is confusing glycogen with starch (starch is plant storage).
