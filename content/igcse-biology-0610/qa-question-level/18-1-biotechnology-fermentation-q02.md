# Question: Question Q-18.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What conditions are needed for fermentation?
**Answer**: Fermentation by yeast requires: (1) Sugar (glucose) as the food source. (2) Warm temperature (25-35°C — optimum for yeast enzymes). (3) Anaerobic conditions (no oxygen — otherwise yeast respires aerobically). (4) Suitable pH (slightly acidic, pH 4-6). If oxygen is present, yeast produces CO₂ and water instead of ethanol.
**Explanation**: Sugar, warmth, no oxygen, pH. A common error is saying fermentation needs oxygen.
