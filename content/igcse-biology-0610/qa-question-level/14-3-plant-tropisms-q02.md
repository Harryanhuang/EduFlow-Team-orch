# Question: Question Q-14.3-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is geotropism (gravitropism)? How does it differ from phototropism?
**Answer**: Geotropism/gravitropism is directional growth in response to gravity. Roots grow towards gravity (positive geotropism); shoots grow away from gravity (negative geotropism). Unlike phototropism, geotropism operates even in darkness. Both tropisms together orient the plant correctly regardless of seed position.
**Explanation**: Roots with gravity (+ve), shoots against (-ve). A common error is saying shoots grow with gravity.
