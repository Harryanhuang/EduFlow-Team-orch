# Question: Question Q-2.2-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain how a red blood cell is adapted for its function.
**Answer**: Red blood cells carry oxygen. Adaptations: biconcave disc shape increases surface area for oxygen absorption; no nucleus leaves more space for haemoglobin (oxygen-carrying protein); thin cell membrane allows oxygen to diffuse through easily; small size allows them to fit through narrow capillaries.
**Explanation**: Shape, no nucleus, haemoglobin, thin membrane. A common error is saying RBCs have a nucleus.
