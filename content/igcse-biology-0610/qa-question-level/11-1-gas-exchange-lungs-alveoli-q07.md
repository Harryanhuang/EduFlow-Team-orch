# Question: Question Q-11.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Why is tobacco smoke harmful to the gas exchange system? Include specific effects on cilia, alveoli, and gas transport.
**Answer**: Tobacco smoke contains over 4000 chemicals. Harmful effects: (1) Tar — deposits on airway lining, paralyses and destroys cilia → mucus accumulates → smoker's cough ("chronic bronchitis"). Tar also damages alveolar walls → walls break down and alveoli merge → reduced surface area → emphysema (severe breathlessness). (2) Carcinogens (e.g., benzopyrene) — cause mutations in epithelial cells → lung cancer. (3) Carbon monoxide — binds to haemoglobin 250× more strongly than O₂, forming carboxyhaemoglobin → reduces O₂-carrying capacity → tissues receive less oxygen → fatigue, poor exercise tolerance. (4) Nicotine — addictive, constricts blood vessels. (5) Irritants — stimulate excess mucus production. The combined effect is progressive, irreversible destruction.
**Explanation**: Tar → cilia death + emphysema; carcinogens → cancer; CO → O₂ transport reduced. A common error is saying smoking "only causes cancer."
