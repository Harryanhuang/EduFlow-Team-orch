# Question: Question Q-12.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe an experiment to show that living organisms release energy (heat) during respiration. Include the control.
**Answer**: Apparatus: two vacuum flasks, each with a thermometer sealed through a bung. Flask A: living germinating peas (respiring). Flask B: boiled/dead peas (control, not respiring). Both sterilised to eliminate microbial respiration. Record temperature every 24 hours for 3 days. Result: Flask A temperature rises (+2-3°C); Flask B stays constant. Conclusion: heat from respiring cells. The vacuum flask insulates; dead pea control proves heat is biological.
**Explanation**: Vacuum flasks + thermometers, germinating vs dead peas. A common error is omitting the dead pea control.
