# Question: Question Q-12.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Where does aerobic respiration take place in cells? Explain which cells have the most mitochondria.
**Answer**: Aerobic respiration takes place mainly in the mitochondria. The first stage (glycolysis) occurs in the cytoplasm; subsequent stages (Krebs cycle, electron transport chain) occur on and within the inner mitochondrial membrane (cristae). Cells with high energy demands have more mitochondria: muscle cells (contraction), sperm cells (swimming), companion cells in phloem (active loading of sucrose), neurones (ion pumping).
**Explanation**: Mitochondria (cristae for ETC), cytoplasm (glycolysis). High energy demand → many mitochondria. A common error is saying respiration only in cytoplasm.
