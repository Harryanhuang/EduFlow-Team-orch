# Question: Question Q-18.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain the role of vectors in genetic engineering and compare the advantages and disadvantages of using plasmids versus viruses as vectors for gene delivery.
**Answer**: Vectors are vehicles that transfer foreign DNA into host cells. They must: replicate independently in the host cell, contain recognition sites for restriction enzymes, and carry a selectable marker gene (e.g., antibiotic resistance). Plasmids: circular DNA molecules found in bacteria. Advantages — easy to manipulate, can carry moderate-sized genes, low immune response risk, self-replicate in bacteria for insulin production. Disadvantages — limited capacity for large genes, inefficient delivery into eukaryotic cells, may not integrate into host genome (transient expression). Viruses: modified to deliver genes without causing disease. Advantages — highly efficient at entering cells, can integrate into host genome (long-term expression). Disadvantages — risk of immune reactions, possible reversion to pathogenic form, size constraints on inserted DNA, ethical concerns about using viruses. For insulin production, plasmids in bacteria are used; for gene therapy in humans, modified viruses are more common despite risks.
**Explanation**: Plasmids for bacteria, viruses for eukaryotic cells. A common error is saying all vectors are "plasmids."
