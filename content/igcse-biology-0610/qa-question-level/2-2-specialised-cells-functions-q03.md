# Question: Question Q-2.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How is a root hair cell adapted for absorbing water and mineral ions?
**Answer**: Root hair cells have a long, thin projection (root hair) that greatly increases the surface area for absorption. They have a large permanent vacuole that helps draw water in by osmosis. They contain many mitochondria to provide energy for active transport of mineral ions.
**Explanation**: Large surface area, vacuole, mitochondria. A common error is saying they absorb by diffusion (mineral ions use active transport).
