# Question: Question Q-4.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe the structure of a DNA molecule.
**Answer**: DNA is a double helix made of two polynucleotide strands twisted around each other. Each strand has a sugar-phosphate backbone with nitrogenous bases attached. The two strands are held together by hydrogen bonds between complementary base pairs: A pairs with T, C pairs with G.
**Explanation**: Double helix, sugar-phosphate backbone, base pairs A-T, C-G. A common error is saying A pairs with C.
