# Question: Question Q-3.3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why do root hair cells contain many mitochondria?
**Answer**: Root hair cells use active transport to absorb mineral ions from the soil against a concentration gradient. Active transport requires energy from respiration. Mitochondria are the site of aerobic respiration, so having many mitochondria provides the energy needed.
**Explanation**: Active transport needs energy, mitochondria provide it. A common error is saying mitochondria are "for water absorption" (that's osmosis, doesn't need energy).
