# Question: Question Q-11.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare inhalation and exhalation in terms of muscle action, pressure changes, and whether each is an active or passive process.
**Answer**: Inhalation (inspiration) — active process requiring energy: External intercostals CONTRACT → ribs up and out. Diaphragm CONTRACTS → flattens down. Thoracic volume INCREASES → pressure DECREASES below atmospheric → air flows IN. Exhalation (expiration) — at rest, passive process (no energy): External intercostals RELAX → ribs down and in. Diaphragm RELAXES → domes up. Thoracic volume DECREASES → pressure INCREASES above atmospheric → air flows OUT. Elastic recoil of stretched lung tissue contributes. Forced exhalation (e.g., during exercise, coughing) — active: Internal intercostals CONTRACT → ribs pulled down and in. Abdominal muscles CONTRACT → push diaphragm up further.
**Explanation**: Inhale=active (contraction), exhale at rest=passive (relaxation + elastic recoil), forced exhale=active. A common error is saying all exhalation is active.
