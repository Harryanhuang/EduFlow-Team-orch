# Question: Q-15.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the difference between asexual and sexual reproduction?
**Answer**: Asexual reproduction involves one parent and produces genetically identical offspring (clones). No fusion of gametes occurs. Sexual reproduction involves two parents, fusion of male and female gametes, and produces genetically different offspring. Asexual reproduction involves mitosis; sexual reproduction involves meiosis.
**Explanation**: One parent/identical vs two parents/different. A common error is saying asexual reproduction involves "no cell division."
