# Question: Question Q-5.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why are enzymes specific to particular substrates?
**Answer**: Each enzyme has a unique three-dimensional shape of its active site. Only a substrate with a complementary shape can fit into the active site, like a key fitting a lock. If the substrate does not match the active site shape, the enzyme cannot catalyse the reaction.
**Explanation**: Active site shape determines specificity. A common error is saying enzymes "choose" their substrate.
