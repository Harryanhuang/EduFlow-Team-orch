# Question: Question Q-12.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain why increasing temperature affects the rate of respiration up to a point, and what happens above the optimum.
**Answer**: Respiration is enzyme-controlled (e.g., dehydrogenase, ATP synthase). As T rises toward optimum (~37°C humans, ~25-30°C many plants): molecules have more KE → more frequent enzyme-substrate collisions → rate increases. At optimum, enzymes work at Vmax. Above optimum: hydrogen bonds in enzyme tertiary structure break → active site denatures → substrate no longer fits → rate drops sharply. At extreme T (>60°C), almost all respiratory enzymes denatured → respiration stops → cell death. Different organisms have different optima (thermophilic bacteria >80°C). Q₁₀ ≈ 2 (rate doubles per 10°C rise up to optimum).
**Explanation**: Enzyme kinetics: T↑ → faster → optimum → denature → stop. A common error is describing temperature without enzyme mechanism.
