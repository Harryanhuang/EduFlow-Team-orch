# Question: Question Q-16.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: In humans, the ability to taste PTC is dominant (T) and non-tasting is recessive (t). Two tasters have a non-taster child. What are the parents' genotypes?
**Answer**: Both parents must be heterozygous (Tt). A non-taster child has genotype tt, meaning it received a t allele from each parent. Since both parents are tasters but carry the t allele, they must be Tt. The probability of a non-taster child from Tt × Tt is 25%.
**Explanation**: Both Tt, 25% non-taster. A common error is saying one parent must be tt.
