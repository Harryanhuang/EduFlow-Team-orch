# Question: Question Q-8.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain what would happen to a plant if its xylem vessels were blocked and how this differs from the effect of blocking phloem sieve tubes.
**Answer**: Xylem blockage: water and mineral ion transport from roots to leaves stops. Leaves wilt because cells lose turgor pressure (water cannot reach mesophyll cells). Photosynthesis stops as stomata close to conserve water. The plant eventually dies from desiccation. Phloem blockage: sucrose and amino acid transport from source to sink stops. Growing regions (root tips, shoot tips) are starved of sugars for respiration and amino acids for protein synthesis — growth halts. Stored starch in sink organs (e.g., developing fruits) is not replenished. Mature leaves may accumulate excess sugars. The plant survives longer than with xylem blockage because water can still reach leaves, but growth and reproduction are severely impaired.
**Explanation**: Xylem→wilting→death vs Phloem→growth halt. A common error is not distinguishing the downstream consequences.
