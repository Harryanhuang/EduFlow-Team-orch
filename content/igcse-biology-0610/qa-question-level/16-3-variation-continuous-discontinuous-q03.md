# Question: Question Q-16.3-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does mutation contribute to variation?
**Answer**: Mutation is a random change in the DNA sequence (gene or chromosome). It creates new alleles, which can lead to new characteristics. Most mutations are harmful or neutral, but occasionally a mutation gives an advantage (e.g., antibiotic resistance in bacteria). Mutation is the ultimate source of genetic variation.
**Explanation**: Random DNA change, new alleles, source of variation. A common error is saying mutations are "always harmful."
**Tags**: mutation, variation, DNA, alleles
