# Question: Question Q-4.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how to test for the presence of reducing sugars (e.g., glucose) in a food sample.
**Answer**: Add Benedict's solution to the sample and heat in a water bath at 80-90°C for 5 minutes. A positive result shows a colour change from blue → green → yellow → orange → brick-red precipitate (depending on sugar concentration). Blue = no reducing sugar.
**Explanation**: Benedict's + heat → brick-red. A common error is forgetting to heat the solution.
