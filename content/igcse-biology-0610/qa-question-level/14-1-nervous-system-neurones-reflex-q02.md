# Question: Question Q-14.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the difference between the CNS and the PNS?
**Answer**: CNS = brain + spinal cord — processes and integrates information, coordinates responses. PNS = all nerves outside CNS — carries sensory impulses from receptors TO the CNS, and motor impulses FROM the CNS to effectors. CNS = "processor"; PNS = "wiring."
**Explanation**: CNS = processor; PNS = wiring. A common error is saying PNS "processes" information.
