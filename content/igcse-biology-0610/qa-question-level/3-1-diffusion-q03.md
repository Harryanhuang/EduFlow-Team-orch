# Question: Question Q-3.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Give two examples of diffusion in living organisms.
**Answer**: (1) Oxygen diffuses from alveoli into blood capillaries in the lungs (oxygen concentration is higher in alveoli). (2) Carbon dioxide diffuses from blood into alveoli. (3) Glucose diffuses from the small intestine into blood. (4) Oxygen diffuses into cells for respiration.
**Explanation**: Gas exchange or nutrient absorption. A common error is using active transport examples.
