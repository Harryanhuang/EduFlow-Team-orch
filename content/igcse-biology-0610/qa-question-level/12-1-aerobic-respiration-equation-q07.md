# Question: Question Q-12.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare aerobic and anaerobic respiration in terms of: site in cell, O₂ requirement, ATP yield per glucose, products, and organisms that use each.
**Answer**: Aerobic: O₂ required ✓, site = cytoplasm + mitochondria, ATP = ~38 per glucose, products = CO₂ + H₂O, complete breakdown, sustainable indefinitely with fuel. Used by most eukaryotes all the time. Anaerobic: O₂ not required, site = cytoplasm only, ATP = ~2 per glucose, products = lactic acid (animals) or ethanol + CO₂ (yeast/plants), incomplete breakdown. Used during O₂ shortage: intense exercise (muscles), waterlogged roots, yeast fermentation. Aerobic is 19× more efficient. Anaerobic is an emergency backup — allows glycolysis to continue when ETC cannot run. The waste products (lactate/ethanol) are toxic and must be oxidised later.
**Explanation**: 38 vs 2 ATP, complete vs incomplete, site, use case. A common error is saying anaerobic produces "no energy."
