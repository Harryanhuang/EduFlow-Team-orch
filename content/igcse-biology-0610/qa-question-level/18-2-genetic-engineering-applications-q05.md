# Question: Question Q-18.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why restriction enzymes and DNA ligase are both essential in genetic engineering.
**Answer**: Restriction enzymes cut DNA at specific recognition sequences, creating "sticky ends" that can pair with complementary sticky ends from another DNA fragment. DNA ligase joins the sugar-phosphate backbone, forming permanent bonds between the gene and the vector. Both are needed — cutting without joining is useless, and joining requires compatible cuts.
**Explanation**: Restriction = cut, ligase = join, both needed. A common error is saying one enzyme does both jobs.
