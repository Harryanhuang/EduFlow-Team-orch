# Question: Question Q-16.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the difference between a diploid and a haploid cell?
**Answer**: Diploid cells have two sets of chromosomes (2n, 46 in humans) — one from each parent. Body cells (somatic cells) are diploid. Haploid cells have one set of chromosomes (n, 23 in humans). Gametes (sperm and egg) are haploid. When gametes fuse at fertilisation, the diploid number is restored.
**Explanation**: 2n = diploid (body cells), n = haploid (gametes). A common error is saying gametes are diploid.
