# Question: Question Q-5.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why increasing substrate concentration increases the rate of an enzyme-controlled reaction up to a point, then the rate levels off.
**Answer**: At low substrate concentration, there are many empty active sites, so adding more substrate increases the rate. At high substrate concentration, all active sites are occupied (saturated), so adding more substrate cannot increase the rate further. The enzyme concentration becomes the limiting factor.
**Explanation**: Saturation, all active sites occupied. A common error is saying the enzyme "runs out."
