# Question: Question Q-18.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Describe the advantages and disadvantages of using microorganisms in industrial biotechnology compared to traditional chemical synthesis of products.
**Answer**: Advantages: (1) Specificity — microorganisms produce complex molecules (e.g., insulin, antibiotics) that are difficult to synthesise chemically. (2) Efficiency — enzymes catalyse reactions at moderate temperatures and pressures, reducing energy costs. (3) Scalability — fermenters can produce large quantities efficiently. (4) Renewable substrates — microorganisms can grow on agricultural waste, reducing reliance on petrochemicals. (5) Self-replicating — once a productive strain is isolated, it reproduces indefinitely. Disadvantages: (1) Contamination risk — unwanted microorganisms can outcompete the desired strain. (2) Product purification — separating the desired product from the culture medium can be complex. (3) Sensitivity — microorganisms require precise conditions (temperature, pH, nutrients); deviations kill or reduce productivity. (4) Regulatory hurdles — biological products require extensive safety testing. (5) Genetic instability — engineered strains may lose or transfer modified genes.
**Explanation**: Advantages of specificity and efficiency; Disadvantages of contamination and complexity. A common error is saying microorganisms are "always better" than chemical synthesis.
