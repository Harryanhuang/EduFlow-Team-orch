# Question: Question Q-2.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Ciliated cells line the trachea. Explain how their structure relates to their function, and predict what would happen if cilia stopped beating.
**Answer**: Ciliated cells have hair-like projections (cilia) on their surface that beat rhythmically to sweep mucus (containing trapped dust and pathogens) up towards the throat, where it is swallowed. If cilia stopped beating, mucus would accumulate in the airways, blocking airflow (breathing difficulty) and trapping pathogens against the airway lining (increased infection risk). This happens in smokers where tar paralyses cilia.
**Explanation**: Cilia beat → move mucus; cilia stop → accumulation + infection. A common error is saying mucus "dissolves."
