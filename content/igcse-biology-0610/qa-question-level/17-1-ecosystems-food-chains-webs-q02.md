# Question: Question Q-17.1-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is the difference between a food chain and a food web?
**Answer**: A food chain shows a single pathway of energy transfer from one organism to another (e.g., grass → rabbit → fox). A food web shows all the interconnected feeding relationships in an ecosystem, with many overlapping food chains. Food webs give a more complete picture of energy flow.
**Explanation**: Single pathway vs multiple interconnected pathways. A common error is saying food webs are "more accurate" without explaining why.
