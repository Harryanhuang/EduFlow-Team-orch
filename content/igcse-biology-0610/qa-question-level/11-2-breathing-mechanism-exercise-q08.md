# Question: Question Q-11.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain how the respiratory system and circulatory system work together to meet increased oxygen demand during exercise.
**Answer**: Coordination by the medulla oblongata: (1) Medulla detects ↑CO₂/↓pH → signals respiratory muscles to increase ventilation rate and depth. (2) Also signals SAN to increase heart rate → cardiac output ↑. (3) Increased ventilation brings more O₂ into alveoli. (4) Faster blood flow maintains steep O₂/CO₂ gradients across alveolar walls. (5) Arterioles in muscles vasodilate, gut arterioles vasoconstrict → blood diverted to active muscles. (6) Bohr effect — haemoglobin releases O₂ more readily at higher temperature and lower pH in active muscles. The two systems are co-regulated, not independent.
**Explanation**: Respiratory + circulatory co-regulation by medulla; Bohr effect; vasodilation. A common error is describing systems separately without coordination.
