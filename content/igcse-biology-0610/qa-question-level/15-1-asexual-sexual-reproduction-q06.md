# Question: Q-15.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why genetic variation is important for the survival of a species.
**Answer**: Genetic variation means that not all individuals in a population are identical. If the environment changes (e.g., new disease, climate change, new predator), some individuals may have traits that help them survive. These individuals reproduce and pass on their advantageous alleles. Without variation, all individuals would be equally vulnerable, and the species could become extinct.
**Explanation**: Variation allows some individuals to survive environmental change. A common error is saying variation is "always good" without explaining the survival mechanism.
