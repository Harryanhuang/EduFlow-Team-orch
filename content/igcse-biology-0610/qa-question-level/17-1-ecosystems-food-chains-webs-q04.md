# Question: Question Q-17.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why do food chains rarely have more than four or five trophic levels?
**Answer**: At each trophic level, about 90% of energy is lost as heat (respiration), in waste (egestion, excretion), and in uneaten parts. Only about 10% is transferred to the next level. After 4-5 levels, too little energy remains to sustain another population. This limits the length of food chains.
**Explanation**: 10% energy transfer, 90% lost. A common error is saying organisms "run out of food."
