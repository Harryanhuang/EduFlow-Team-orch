# Question: Question Q-18.2-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Describe the process of producing a clone of a mammal through somatic cell nuclear transfer. Evaluate the potential applications and ethical issues of this technology.
**Answer**: Somatic cell nuclear transfer (SCNT) clones a mammal: (1) A somatic (body) cell is taken from the donor animal. (2) Its nucleus (containing the full genetic complement) is removed. (3) An egg cell has its nucleus removed, leaving its cytoplasm with its own mitochondrial DNA. (4) The donor nucleus is inserted into the enucleated egg. (5) The cell is stimulated to divide (electrical shock or chemicals). (6) The embryo is implanted into a surrogate mother and carried to term. Dolly the sheep (1996) was produced this way. Applications: preserving endangered species, producing clones of genetically valuable livestock, therapeutic cloning (producing stem cells for medicine). Ethical issues: the "yuk" factor — discomfort with cloning; efficiency is very low (many failed attempts); health concerns (Dolly aged prematurely); questions about animal welfare; concerns about human reproductive cloning; religious objections to "playing God."
**Explanation**: Nucleus transfer, surrogate mother, applications vs ethics. A common error is saying clones are "identical in every way" (epigenetic differences exist).
