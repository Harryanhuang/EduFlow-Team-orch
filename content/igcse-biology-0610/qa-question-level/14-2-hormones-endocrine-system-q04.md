# Question: Question Q-14.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Compare nervous and hormonal control in terms of speed, duration, pathway, and type of signal.
**Answer**: Nervous: electrical + chemical (neurotransmitter) signal; specific neurone pathway (wired); very fast (ms); short-lived (ms); localised effect; e.g., withdrawal reflex. Hormonal: chemical only (hormone); bloodstream (broadcast); slower (seconds-hours); long-lasting (minutes-days); widespread effect; e.g., blood glucose regulation. Nervous = rapid precise; Hormonal = sustained body-wide. They interact — hypothalamus links both.
**Explanation**: Fast/electrical/wired vs slow/chemical/broadcast. A common error is saying hormones are "faster" than nerves.
