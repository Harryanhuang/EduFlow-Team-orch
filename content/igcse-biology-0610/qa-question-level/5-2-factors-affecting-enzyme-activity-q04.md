# Question: Question Q-5.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does pH affect enzyme activity?
**Answer**: Each enzyme has an optimum pH at which it works fastest. Most human enzymes work best at pH 7. Pepsin (in the stomach) works best at pH 2. At pH values far from the optimum, the enzyme's active site changes shape (denaturation), and the substrate no longer fits, reducing the rate.
**Explanation**: Optimum pH, denaturation at extremes. A common error is saying all enzymes work at pH 7.
