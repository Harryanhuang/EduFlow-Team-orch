# Question: Question Q-10.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Describe the body's first line of defence against pathogens.
**Answer**: Physical barriers: intact skin (keratinised epidermis blocks pathogen entry), mucous membranes (sticky mucus traps pathogens in respiratory/digestive tracts). Chemical barriers: stomach acid (HCl at pH 1-2 kills most ingested pathogens), lysozyme enzyme in tears and saliva (breaks down bacterial cell walls). Mechanical barriers: cilia in airways (sweep trapped mucus + pathogens upward to throat to be swallowed).
**Explanation**: Skin, mucus, acid, cilia, lysozyme — all non-specific pre-infection barriers. A common error is calling these "immune system" (they are non-specific barriers, immunity is specific).
