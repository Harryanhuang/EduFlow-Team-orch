# Question: Question Q-11.2-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What are tidal volume and vital capacity?
**Answer**: Tidal volume is the volume of air breathed in and out during normal, resting breathing (~0.5 dm³ per breath). Vital capacity is the maximum volume of air that can be forcibly exhaled after the deepest possible inhalation (~3-5 dm³ in adults). Tidal volume is always smaller than vital capacity. Spirometer traces show tidal volume as the regular wave amplitude; vital capacity as the maximum peak-to-trough height.
**Explanation**: Tidal = normal breath; vital capacity = max forced breath. A common error is confusing vital capacity with total lung capacity.
