# Question: Question Q-7.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why do pregnant women need more iron, calcium, and protein in their diet?
**Answer**: Iron: needed to produce extra haemoglobin for increased blood volume and to supply oxygen to the developing foetus. Calcium: needed for foetal bone and tooth development. Protein: needed for growth of foetal tissues and the placenta. Deficiency in any of these can harm both mother and baby — e.g., iron-deficiency anaemia increases the risk of premature birth.
**Explanation**: Iron→haemoglobin for foetus; Calcium→foetal bones; Protein→tissue growth. A common error is saying "more energy" without specifying nutrients.
