# Question: Question Q-17.1-05
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the role of decomposers in an ecosystem?
**Answer**: Decomposers (bacteria and fungi) break down dead organisms and waste products, releasing nutrients (minerals, nitrates) back into the soil. These nutrients are then absorbed by plants, completing the nutrient cycle. Without decomposers, nutrients would remain locked in dead matter.
**Explanation**: Break down dead matter, recycle nutrients. A common error is saying decomposers "produce energy."
**Tags**: decomposers, bacteria, fungi, nutrient-cycle
