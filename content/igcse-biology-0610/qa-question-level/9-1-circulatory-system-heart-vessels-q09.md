# Question: Question Q-9.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare the structure and function of veins and arteries, and explain why the differences are essential for a working circulatory system.
**Answer**: Arteries — carry blood away from heart at high pressure (~120/80 mmHg). Thick tunica media (smooth muscle + elastic fibres) withstands pressure. Elastic recoil maintains diastolic pressure. Narrow lumen maintains velocity. No valves (except semilunar). Veins — carry blood toward heart at low pressure (~5-10 mmHg). Thin tunica media, wide lumen — low resistance. Valves present to prevent backflow. Skeletal muscle pump aids venous return. Differences are essential because: (1) If arteries had thin walls like veins, they would burst under systolic pressure. (2) If veins had thick walls like arteries, they would be too stiff for skeletal muscle compression to work. (3) If veins lacked valves, blood would pool in the legs under gravity when standing. (4) If capillaries had thick walls, diffusion exchange would be too slow to sustain tissue respiration. The entire circulatory system is a pressure cascade — each vessel type's structure is optimised for its pressure regime.
**Explanation**: Full structural comparison with functional rationale. A common error is listing structures without explaining WHY each difference matters.
