# Question: Question Q-3.3-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain why a plant placed in a solution containing a respiratory poison cannot absorb mineral ions from the soil. Would water absorption by osmosis also be affected? Justify your answer.
**Answer**: The respiratory poison stops respiration in root cells, so no ATP is produced. Active transport of mineral ions requires ATP, so mineral absorption stops. Water absorption by osmosis is NOT directly affected because osmosis is a passive process that does not require energy — water moves down its water potential gradient without ATP. However, long-term water absorption may decrease because the plant needs mineral ions to maintain the osmotic gradient that drives water uptake.
**Explanation**: No respiration → no ATP → no active transport. Osmosis = passive, not directly affected. A common error is saying osmosis also stops.
