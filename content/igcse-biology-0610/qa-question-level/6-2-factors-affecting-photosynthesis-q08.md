# Question: Question Q-6.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A student investigates light intensity using pondweed but notices that bubbles stop appearing at very high light intensities. Another student claims "photosynthesis has stopped." Evaluate this claim.
**Answer**: The claim is incorrect. At very high light intensities, photosynthesis is still occurring — but oxygen is being produced at a constant maximum rate (plateau reached). The rate does NOT decrease at high light — it simply does not increase further. Bubbles still appear, just not more frequently. If bubbles stop completely, one of these is likely: (1) the lamp is too close and the water has heated up, denaturing the enzymes; (2) CO₂ (from NaHCO₃) has been depleted; or (3) the plant is damaged. True cessation of photosynthesis would require enzyme denaturation (extreme heat), not high light alone. The experiment should control temperature with a heat shield or water bath.
**Explanation**: High light → plateau (not stop). If bubbles stop → overheating or CO₂ depletion. A common error is confusing plateau with stopping.
