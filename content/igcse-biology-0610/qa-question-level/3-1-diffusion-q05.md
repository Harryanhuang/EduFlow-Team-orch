# Question: Question Q-3.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why do small organisms rely on diffusion for gas exchange, but large organisms need specialised exchange surfaces?
**Answer**: Small organisms have a large surface area to volume ratio, so diffusion alone is sufficient for gas exchange. Large organisms have a small surface area to volume ratio — diffusion over large distances is too slow, so they need specialised surfaces (lungs, gills) with large surface areas.
**Explanation**: Surface area to volume ratio. A common error is saying diffusion "stops" at a certain size.
