# Question: Question Q-17.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does deforestation affect the carbon cycle and the water cycle?
**Answer**: Carbon cycle: fewer trees = less photosynthesis = less CO₂ removed from atmosphere. Burning cleared trees releases stored carbon. Both increase atmospheric CO₂. Water cycle: fewer trees = less transpiration = less water vapour in atmosphere = less rainfall. Reduced root absorption leads to increased runoff and flooding.
**Explanation**: Both cycles affected — less uptake, more release. A common error is only mentioning one cycle.
