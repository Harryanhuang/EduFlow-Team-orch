# Question: Question Q-12.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe an experiment to show that living organisms produce CO₂ during respiration. Include the control.
**Answer**: Two sealed containers: A — living organisms (woodlice/germinating seeds); B — dead organisms (control). Each connected by delivery tube to limewater. Air inlet passes through KOH/soda lime to remove ambient CO₂ before entering containers. Result: limewater in A turns milky (CaCO₃ precipitate); B stays clear. Conclusion: CO₂ comes from respiring organisms, not ambient air. CO₂ + Ca(OH)₂ → CaCO₃↓ + H₂O.
**Explanation**: CO₂ + limewater → milky; KOH removes inlet CO₂; dead control. A common error is not scrubbing inlet CO₂.
