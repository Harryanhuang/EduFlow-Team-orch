# Question: Question Q-15.3-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Evaluate two natural methods and two artificial methods of contraception, considering how each prevents fertilisation or implantation.
**Answer**: Natural methods: (1) Abstinence (periodic abstinence / fertility awareness): tracking the menstrual cycle to avoid intercourse during ovulation (when the egg is fertile). Prevents fertilisation by keeping sperm and egg apart. Advantage: no side effects; Disadvantage: high failure rate because ovulation timing is hard to predict. (2) Withdrawal: the male withdraws before ejaculation. Prevents sperm from entering the vagina. Advantage: no equipment needed; Disadvantage: pre-ejaculate fluid may contain sperm; high failure rate. Artificial methods: (3) Condom (barrier): a latex sheath placed over the penis (or female condom in the vagina) that physically blocks sperm from entering the cervix. Advantage: also reduces STI transmission; Disadvantage: can break or slip. (4) Contraceptive pill (hormonal): contains oestrogen and progesterone (or progesterone only) that inhibit FSH release, preventing follicle development and ovulation. No egg is released, so fertilisation cannot occur. Advantage: very effective if taken correctly; Disadvantage: side effects (mood changes, blood clots), requires daily compliance, does not protect against STIs.
**Explanation**: Natural = keeping gametes apart; Artificial = barrier or hormonal suppression of ovulation. A common error is confusing how each method works.
