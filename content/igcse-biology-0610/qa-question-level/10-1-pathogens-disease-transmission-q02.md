# Question: Question Q-10.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the difference between a communicable and a non-communicable disease?
**Answer**: A communicable (infectious) disease can be transmitted from person to person or from organism to organism (e.g., influenza, TB, malaria, cholera). A non-communicable disease cannot be spread between people; it arises from genetic, lifestyle, or environmental factors (e.g., cancer, heart disease, diabetes, scurvy).
**Explanation**: Transmissible vs not transmissible. A common error is saying non-communicable means "not serious."
