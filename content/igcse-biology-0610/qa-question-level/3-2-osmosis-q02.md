# Question: Question Q-3.2-02
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A student places red blood cells in solutions of different concentrations. In solution A, cells burst. In solution B, cells shrink and become wrinkled. In solution C, cells remain normal. Explain these results.
**Answer**: Solution A is hypotonic (more dilute than cell cytoplasm) — water enters by osmosis, cells burst (no cell wall). Solution B is hypertonic (more concentrated) — water leaves by osmosis, cells shrink (crenation). Solution C is isotonic (same concentration) — no net water movement, cells remain normal.
**Explanation**: Hypotonic = burst, hypertonic = shrink, isotonic = normal. A common error is reversing hypertonic and hypotonic.
**Tags**: osmosis, RBC, hypotonic, hypertonic, isotonic
