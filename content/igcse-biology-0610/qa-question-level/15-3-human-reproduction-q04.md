# Question: Question Q-15.3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe what happens from the zygote to implantation in the uterus wall.
**Answer**: After fertilisation in the oviduct: (1) The zygote divides by mitosis (cleavage) — first into 2 cells, then 4, then 8 — forming a ball of cells called a morula. (2) The morula continues dividing and becomes a hollow ball of cells called a blastocyst (about 5 days after fertilisation). (3) The blastocyst floats in the uterus for 1–2 days. (4) The blastocyst then implants (embeds) into the thickened endometrium (uterus lining) that was prepared by progesterone. Implantation marks the beginning of pregnancy.
**Explanation**: Zygote → mitosis → morula → blastocyst → implantation in endometrium. A common error is saying implantation happens immediately after fertilisation.
