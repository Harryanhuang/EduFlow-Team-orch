# Question: Question Q-5.1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why do enzymes in washing powders work best at 30-40°C?
**Answer**: Washing powders contain protease and lipase enzymes that break down protein and fat stains. These enzymes have an optimum temperature of 30-40°C. Below this, the rate is too slow (not enough kinetic energy). Above this, the enzymes denature (lose their active site shape) and stop working. Using too hot a temperature wastes energy and damages the enzymes.
**Explanation**: Optimum temperature, denaturation above. A common error is saying "hotter is always better."
