# Question: Question Q-3.3-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A scientist discovers a new drug that blocks carrier proteins in cell membranes. Predict the effects of this drug on: (a) the absorption of glucose from the small intestine, (b) the absorption of oxygen in the lungs, (c) the uptake of mineral ions by plant roots. Justify each prediction.
**Answer**: (a) Glucose absorption would STOP — absorption occurs by active transport which requires carrier proteins, and co-transport mechanisms also need carriers. (b) Oxygen absorption would CONTINUE — oxygen diffuses directly through the phospholipid bilayer down its concentration gradient; it does not need carrier proteins. (c) Mineral ion uptake would STOP — ions are absorbed by active transport requiring carrier proteins. The root cells would eventually become mineral-deficient.
**Explanation**: Carrier proteins needed for active transport, not for simple diffusion. A common error is saying "all absorption stops."
