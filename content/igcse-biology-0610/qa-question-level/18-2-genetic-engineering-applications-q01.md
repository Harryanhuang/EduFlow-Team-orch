# Question: Question Q-18.2-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How is genetic engineering used to produce herbicide-resistant crops?
**Answer**: A gene for herbicide resistance is identified and cut from a resistant organism using restriction enzymes. It is inserted into a plasmid vector and introduced into crop plant cells. The modified plants grow normally when herbicide is sprayed — the herbicide kills weeds but not the crop. This increases yield and reduces the need for manual weeding.
**Explanation**: Resistance gene → plasmid → crop plants → herbicide kills weeds only. A common error is saying the crop "produces" herbicide.
