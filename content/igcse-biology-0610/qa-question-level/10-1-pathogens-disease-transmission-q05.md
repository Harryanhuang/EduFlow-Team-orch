# Question: Question Q-10.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How can the spread of infectious diseases be controlled?
**Answer**: Control methods: (1) Hygiene — washing hands, sterilising medical equipment. (2) Vaccination — immunising populations to achieve herd immunity. (3) Isolation/quarantine — separating infected individuals. (4) Vector control — destroying mosquitoes, rats. (5) Safe water supply and sewage systems. (6) Antibiotics — for bacterial infections only (not viruses). (7) Safe food handling — thorough cooking, refrigeration, hand washing. (8) Public health education.
**Explanation**: Multiple control layers. A common error is saying antibiotics work on viruses.
