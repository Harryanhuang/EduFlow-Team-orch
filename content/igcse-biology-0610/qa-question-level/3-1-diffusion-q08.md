# Question: Question Q-3.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A patient with emphysema has damaged alveolar walls, reducing the surface area of their lungs. Explain how this affects the diffusion of oxygen and carbon dioxide, and predict the symptoms the patient would experience.
**Answer**: Reduced alveolar surface area directly reduces the area available for gas exchange by diffusion. Since rate of diffusion is proportional to surface area (Fick's law: rate ∝ surface area × concentration gradient ÷ thickness), less oxygen diffuses into the blood and less CO₂ diffuses out. Predicted symptoms: shortness of breath (hypoxia — low blood oxygen), especially during exercise when oxygen demand increases; cyanosis (blue tinge to skin); fatigue; rapid breathing to compensate. The patient cannot get enough oxygen to meet metabolic demands.
**Explanation**: Fick's law applied, reduced diffusion → hypoxia → breathlessness. A common error is saying "diffusion becomes faster" with less surface area.
