# Question: Question Q-12.2-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why anaerobic respiration cannot be sustained for long periods in humans.
**Answer**: Three reasons: (1) Low ATP yield — only ~2 ATP per glucose vs ~38 ATP aerobically (5% efficiency). Cells cannot meet energy demands — glycogen stores deplete too fast. (2) Lactic acid accumulation — lowers muscle cell pH from ~7.2 to ~6.5, inhibiting enzyme activity → muscle fatigue, pain, cramp. (3) Lactic acid removal rate is limited — liver can process ~0.3 g/min. If production exceeds removal, pH drops dangerously low. Anaerobic is emergency backup, not primary.
**Explanation**: Low ATP + lactic acid inhibition + limited clearance rate. A common error is saying anaerobic "produces no energy."
