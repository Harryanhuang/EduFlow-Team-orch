# Question: Question Q-3.2-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A student places potato cylinders in solutions of different concentrations. The cylinder in 0.0 mol/dm³ (pure water) increased in mass by 12%. The cylinder in 0.4 mol/dm³ decreased in mass by 8%. Explain both results in terms of osmosis.
**Answer**: In pure water: water potential is much higher outside the potato cells, so water enters by osmosis, increasing mass. In 0.4 mol/dm³: the sugar solution has a lower water potential than the potato cell sap, so water leaves the potato cells by osmosis, decreasing mass. The solution is hypertonic relative to the cells. If the mass had not changed, the solution would be isotonic.
**Explanation**: Water potential differences drive water movement. A common error is saying "sugar enters" the potato.
