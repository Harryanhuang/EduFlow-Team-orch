# Question: Question Q-10.1-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How is malaria transmitted and how can it be prevented?
**Answer**: Malaria is caused by Plasmodium (a protoctist) transmitted by the bite of an infected female Anopheles mosquito. The mosquito injects Plasmodium into the bloodstream when feeding. Prevention: (1) Insecticide-treated bed nets while sleeping. (2) Mosquito repellent on skin. (3) Eliminating standing water (mosquito breeding sites). (4) Indoor residual spraying with insecticide. (5) Antimalarial prophylactic drugs for travellers. (6) Window screens to exclude mosquitoes.
**Explanation**: Mosquito vector, Plasmodium, multiple prevention layers. A common error is saying malaria is caused by a virus.
