# Question: Question Q-5.1-03
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Explain the "lock and key" model of enzyme action.
**Answer**: The enzyme has a specifically shaped active site (the lock). Only the correct substrate molecule (the key) fits into the active site. When the substrate binds, an enzyme-substrate complex forms, and the reaction occurs. The products are released and the enzyme is unchanged, ready to catalyse another reaction.
**Explanation**: Specific fit, active site, unchanged. A common error is saying the enzyme changes shape permanently.
