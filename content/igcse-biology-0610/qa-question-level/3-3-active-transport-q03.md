# Question: Question Q-3.3-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Give two examples of active transport in living organisms.
**Answer**: (1) Root hair cells absorbing mineral ions from soil (ion concentration is higher in the root than in soil). (2) Absorption of glucose from the small intestine into blood (against the concentration gradient). Both require energy from respiration.
**Explanation**: Mineral ions and glucose. A common error is using oxygen/CO₂ exchange (that's diffusion).
