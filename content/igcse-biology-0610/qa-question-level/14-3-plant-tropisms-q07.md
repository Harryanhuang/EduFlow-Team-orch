# Question: Question Q-14.3-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare how auxin controls phototropism and geotropism. Why does the same hormone produce different responses?
**Answer**: Both involve auxin redistribution + differential growth. Phototropism (shoot): light → auxin moves to shaded side → more elongation → bend toward light. Geotropism (shoot): gravity → auxin accumulates on lower side → more elongation → upward bend. Geotropism (root): gravity → auxin on lower side → INHIBITS elongation (roots more sensitive) → root bends downward. Key: differential sensitivity — shoots are stimulated by low auxin; roots are inhibited by lower auxin concentration. One hormone, multiple responses via tissue-specific dose-response curves.
**Explanation**: Shoot = auxin stimulates; Root = auxin inhibits. A common error is saying different hormones control shoot vs root tropisms.
