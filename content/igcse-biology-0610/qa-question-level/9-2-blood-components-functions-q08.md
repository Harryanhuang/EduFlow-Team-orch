# Question: Question Q-9.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Compare the two main types of white blood cells (phagocytes and lymphocytes) in terms of their appearance, mode of action, and specificity.
**Answer**: Phagocytes — appearance: irregular shape, lobed nucleus, can change shape (amoeboid movement). Mode of action: non-specific engulfing and digestion (phagocytosis) of any foreign particle or pathogen. Do not distinguish between different pathogens. Lymphocytes — appearance: large round nucleus, small cytoplasm. Mode of action: highly specific — produce antibodies that match only one specific antigen on one specific pathogen. Memory lymphocytes remain after infection, providing long-term immunity (rapid response on re-exposure). Phagocytes provide immediate, broad defence; lymphocytes provide specific, memory-based defence.
**Explanation**: Non-specific (phagocytes) vs specific (lymphocytes). A common error is saying phagocytes "recognise" specific pathogens like lymphocytes do.
