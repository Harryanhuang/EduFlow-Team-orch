# Question: Question Q-18.1-01
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How is biotechnology used in sewage treatment?
**Answer**: Sewage treatment uses bacteria to break down organic waste. In the aeration tank, bacteria digest organic matter (proteins, carbohydrates, fats) using oxygen supplied by bubbling air. The bacteria convert waste into CO₂, water, and mineral salts. Solid sludge is separated and the treated water is released. This prevents pollution of waterways.
**Explanation**: Bacteria digest organic waste, aeration, treated water. A common error is saying chemicals treat sewage.
