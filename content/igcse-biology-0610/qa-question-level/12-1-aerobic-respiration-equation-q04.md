# Question: Question Q-12.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why plants must respire even though they photosynthesise.
**Answer**: Photosynthesis makes glucose using light energy (anabolic). Respiration releases energy from glucose (catabolic). Plants respire continuously (24 hr/day) in ALL living cells because every cell needs energy for active transport, protein synthesis, growth, and cell division. Photosynthesis occurs only in cells with chloroplasts and only during daylight. At night, plants rely entirely on respiration using stored starch. The gases exchanged during respiration (O₂ in, CO₂ out) are the reverse of photosynthesis.
**Explanation**: Photosynthesis makes food; respiration uses food for energy. All cells, all the time. A common error is saying plants "only photosynthesise."
