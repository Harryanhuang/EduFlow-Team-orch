# Question: Question Q-8.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how a potometer is used to measure the rate of transpiration.
**Answer**: A potometer measures water uptake by a plant shoot (≈ water loss by transpiration). Steps: cut a leafy shoot underwater (prevents air entering xylem), fit to potometer underwater, introduce air bubble into capillary tube, record bubble position, measure distance moved in set time. Rate = distance/time (mm/min). Repeat under different conditions (light, wind, humidity) to compare. Note: water used in photosynthesis is very small and ignored.
**Explanation**: Water uptake ≈ transpiration, bubble movement, rate. A common error is saying the potometer measures transpiration directly.
