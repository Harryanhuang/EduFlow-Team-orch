# Question: Question Q-19.1-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the greenhouse effect and how do human activities increase it?
**Answer**: The greenhouse effect is the trapping of heat in the atmosphere by greenhouse gases (CO₂, methane, water vapour). These gases allow sunlight in but absorb and re-radiate heat. Human activities increase greenhouse gases: burning fossil fuels (CO₂), deforestation (less CO₂ absorption), agriculture (methane from cattle, rice paddies), and industry.
**Explanation**: Greenhouse gases trap heat, human activities increase gases. A common error is confusing the greenhouse effect with ozone depletion.
