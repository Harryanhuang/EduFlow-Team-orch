# Question: Question Q-12.2-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is "oxygen debt" and how is it repaid?
**Answer**: Oxygen debt is the extra oxygen needed after intense exercise to reverse the effects of anaerobic respiration. It is repaid by continuing to breathe deeply and rapidly after exercise stops. The extra O₂ is used to: (1) oxidise lactic acid back to pyruvate → CO₂ + H₂O in the liver. (2) Convert some lactic acid to glucose (gluconeogenesis) in the liver. (3) Replenish myoglobin O₂ stores in muscles. (4) Restore ATP and phosphocreatine levels.
**Explanation**: O₂ debt = post-exercise O₂ for lactic acid oxidation + O₂ store replenishment. A common error is saying O₂ debt is "O₂ needed during exercise."
