# Question: Question Q-6.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why a variegated leaf is used to show that chlorophyll is needed for photosynthesis.
**Answer**: A variegated leaf has green areas (with chlorophyll) and white areas (without chlorophyll). After testing for starch, only the green areas turn blue-black with iodine, showing that photosynthesis only occurs where chlorophyll is present. The white areas have no starch.
**Explanation**: Green = starch, white = no starch. A common error is saying the white areas "have no water."
