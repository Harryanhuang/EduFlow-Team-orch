# Question: Question Q-16.2-04
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A homozygous brown-eyed person (BB) has children with a blue-eyed person (bb). What are the possible genotypes and phenotypes of their children?
**Answer**: All children will be Bb (heterozygous). The phenotype will be brown eyes (B is dominant). The recessive blue eye allele (b) is present but not expressed. All offspring are carriers of the blue eye allele.
**Explanation**: All Bb, brown eyes. A common error is saying half will have blue eyes.
