# Question: Question Q-5.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe an experiment to investigate the effect of temperature on the enzyme amylase.
**Answer**: Set up water baths at different temperatures (e.g., 10, 20, 30, 40, 50, 60°C). Add amylase to starch solution at each temperature. At regular intervals, remove a sample and test with iodine. Record the time taken for the starch to be completely broken down (iodine stays orange). Plot rate (1/time) against temperature. The graph should show a peak at the optimum temperature.
**Explanation**: Water baths, iodine test, time measurement, graph. A common error is not controlling enzyme concentration.
