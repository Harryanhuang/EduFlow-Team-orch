# Question: Question Q-18.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Describe how yoghurt is produced using bacteria.
**Answer**: Milk is heated to kill unwanted bacteria, then cooled. Lactobacillus bacteria are added. The bacteria ferment lactose (milk sugar) into lactic acid. The acid lowers the pH, causing milk proteins to coagulate (thicken) and giving yoghurt its characteristic sour taste. The process is carried out at a warm temperature (about 40°C).
**Explanation**: Lactobacillus, lactose → lactic acid, coagulation. A common error is saying yeast makes yoghurt.
