# Question: Question Q-6.2-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: On a hot, bright summer day, a plant's stomata close to conserve water. Predict the effect on the rate of photosynthesis and explain why. If CO₂ is the limiting factor, what could a farmer do to maintain photosynthesis?
**Answer**: When stomata close, CO₂ cannot diffuse into the leaf. CO₂ becomes the limiting factor — photosynthesis rate drops sharply, even though light intensity is high. This is because CO₂ is a raw material for the Calvin cycle. The plant cannot make glucose without CO₂. A farmer could: (1) in a greenhouse, artificially increase CO₂ concentration so that even when stomata partially close, enough CO₂ enters; (2) use shade cloth to reduce light and temperature, reducing water loss so stomata can stay more open; (3) ensure adequate watering so the plant does not need to close stomata to conserve water.
**Explanation**: Closed stomata → no CO₂ entry → CO₂ limits → rate drops. Solutions: add CO₂, shade, water. A common error is saying light limits on a bright day.
