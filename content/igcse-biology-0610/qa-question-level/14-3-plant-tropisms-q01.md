# Question: Question Q-14.3-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is phototropism? Explain how it benefits a plant.
**Answer**: Phototropism is a directional growth response to light. Shoots grow towards light (positive phototropism) for maximum light capture for photosynthesis. Roots grow away from light (negative phototropism) to grow deeper into soil for water/minerals. Controlled by auxin.
**Explanation**: Shoots towards, roots away. A common error is saying roots grow towards light.
**Tags**: phototropism, light, shoots, roots, auxin
