# Question: Question Q-16.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is selective breeding (artificial selection)? Give an example.
**Answer**: Selective breeding is the process of breeding organisms with desirable characteristics to produce offspring with those traits. Steps: (1) Choose parents with desired traits. (2) Breed them. (3) Select offspring with the desired trait. (4) Breed these offspring over many generations. Example: breeding cows that produce the most milk, or wheat with the highest yield.
**Explanation**: Select, breed, select again, repeat. A common error is saying selective breeding is "genetic engineering."
