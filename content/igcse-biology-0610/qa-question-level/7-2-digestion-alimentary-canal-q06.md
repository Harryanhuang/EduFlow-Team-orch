# Question: Question Q-7.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What enzymes are involved in digestion and what do they break down?
**Answer**: Amylase breaks down starch into maltose (produced by salivary glands and pancreas). Protease (e.g., pepsin, trypsin) breaks down proteins into amino acids (stomach and pancreas). Lipase breaks down fats (lipids) into fatty acids and glycerol (pancreas). Maltase breaks maltose into glucose.
**Explanation**: Amylase → starch, protease → protein, lipase → fats. A common error is confusing which enzyme breaks down which substrate.
