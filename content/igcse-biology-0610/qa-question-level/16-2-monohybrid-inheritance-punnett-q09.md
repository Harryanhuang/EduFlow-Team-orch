# Question: Question Q-16.2-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A farmer wants to produce only tall pea plants. She has a plant that appears tall but has an unknown genotype. Explain with a genetic cross how she could determine whether the plant is homozygous tall (TT) or heterozygous tall (Tt). State all possible outcomes.
**Answer**: She should perform a test cross — cross the tall plant with a short plant (tt). Cross 1: If the tall plant is TT × tt → all offspring are Tt (all tall). Cross 2: If the tall plant is Tt × tt → offspring are 50% Tt (tall) and 50% tt (short). By observing the offspring, she can determine the parent's genotype. If all offspring are tall, the parent was TT (homozygous). If approximately half are short, the parent was Tt (heterozygous). To produce only tall offspring, she should use a TT parent.
**Explanation**: Test cross with homozygous recessive, observe phenotype ratio. A common error is crossing with another tall plant.
