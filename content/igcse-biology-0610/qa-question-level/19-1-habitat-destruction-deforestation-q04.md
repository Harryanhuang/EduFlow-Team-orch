# Question: Question Q-19.1-04
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: What is biodiversity and why is it important?
**Answer**: Biodiversity is the variety of different species in an area. High biodiversity means a healthy, stable ecosystem that can resist changes and recover from disturbances. It provides food, medicines, and ecosystem services (pollination, water purification, soil fertility). Low biodiversity makes ecosystems vulnerable to collapse.
**Explanation**: Species variety, ecosystem stability, services. A common error is saying biodiversity is "just the number of animals."
**Tags**: biodiversity, ecosystem, stability, importance
