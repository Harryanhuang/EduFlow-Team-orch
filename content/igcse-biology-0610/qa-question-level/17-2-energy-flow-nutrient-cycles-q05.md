# Question: Question Q-17.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: How does the greenhouse effect differ from global warming?
**Answer**: The greenhouse effect is a natural process — greenhouse gases trap heat, keeping Earth warm enough for life. Without it, Earth would be too cold. Global warming is the enhanced greenhouse effect caused by human activities (burning fossil fuels, deforestation) that increase greenhouse gas concentrations, causing Earth to warm beyond the natural level.
**Explanation**: Natural vs human-enhanced. A common error is saying the greenhouse effect is "bad."
