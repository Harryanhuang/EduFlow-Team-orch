# Question: Question Q-17.3-03
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Describe the typical growth curve of a population.
**Answer**: Phase 1 (lag phase): slow growth as population establishes. Phase 2 (log/exponential phase): rapid growth as resources are abundant. Phase 3 (stationary phase): population levels off as resources become limited — birth rate equals death rate. The maximum population at this point is the carrying capacity.
**Explanation**: Lag → exponential → stationary, carrying capacity. A common error is saying the population "keeps growing."
