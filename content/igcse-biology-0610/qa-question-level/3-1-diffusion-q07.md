# Question: Question Q-3.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: In an experiment, potassium permanganate crystals are placed in water. Describe what happens and explain why, in terms of diffusion.
**Answer**: The purple colour spreads from the crystals throughout the water until the solution is evenly coloured. Potassium permanganate particles diffuse from the region of high concentration (near the crystals) to regions of lower concentration (the surrounding water) due to random particle movement. Eventually, dynamic equilibrium is reached — particles continue to move in all directions but there is no net change in concentration anywhere in the solution.
**Explanation**: Spread of colour, high to low, random movement, equilibrium. A common error is saying the colour "dissolves" (it also diffuses).
