### Question Q-3.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: List four types of errors that a trial balance CANNOT detect and explain why each does not affect the agreement of debits and credits.
**Answer**: 1) Error of omission — a complete transaction is not recorded at all, so neither debit nor credit is entered; both sides remain equal. 2) Error of commission — a transaction is posted to the wrong account but the correct side (e.g., crediting supplier A instead of supplier B); debits and credits are still equal. 3) Error of principle — a transaction is posted to the wrong type of account but with correct debit/credit sides (e.g., capital expenditure recorded as expense); the amounts still balance. 4) Compensating errors — two or more errors cancel each other out mathematically; the totals happen to agree.
**Explanation**: All these errors maintain debit = credit equality despite being wrong. A common error is listing "error of original entry" without explaining — this is where the wrong amount is used in both debit and credit entries, so they still balance.
