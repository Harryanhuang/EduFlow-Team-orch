### Question Q-3.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A trial balance shows: Purchases $12,000 (Dr), Sales $45,000 (Cr), Rent $3,600 (Dr), Capital $20,000 (Cr), Bank $8,000 (Dr), Trade payables $6,400 (Cr), Trade receivables $7,200 (Dr), Drawings $4,000 (Dr), Inventory $5,600 (Dr), Bank loan $10,000 (Cr). Verify whether the trial balance agrees.
**Answer**: Debit column total = $12,000 + $3,600 + $8,000 + $7,200 + $4,000 + $5,600 = $40,400. Credit column total = $45,000 + $20,000 + $6,400 + $10,000 = $81,400. The trial balance does NOT agree — credits exceed debits by $41,000. There must be an error in the bookkeeping (an imbalance in double entry, or an omitted debit).
**Explanation**: When the trial balance does not agree, look for missing entries. Here, the $45,000 sales (credit) has no corresponding debit entry listed — the missing debit would be cash/receivables. A common error is only adding one column and assuming it balances.
