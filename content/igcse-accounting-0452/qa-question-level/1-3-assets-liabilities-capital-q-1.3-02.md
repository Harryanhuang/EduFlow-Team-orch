### Question Q-1.3-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is a current asset? Give three examples.
**Answer**: A current asset is an asset that is expected to be converted into cash, sold, or consumed within one year (or within the normal operating cycle of the business). Examples: cash, bank balance, inventory (stock of goods), trade receivables (debtors), prepaid expenses.
**Explanation**: The one-year time frame is the key distinguishing feature. A common error is including non-current assets like equipment in the current assets list. Inventory is a current asset even though it may take months to sell.
