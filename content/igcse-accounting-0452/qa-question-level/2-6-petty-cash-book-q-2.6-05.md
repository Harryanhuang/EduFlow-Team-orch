### Question Q-2.6-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What are analysis columns in a petty cash book? Why are they useful?
**Answer**: Analysis columns are separate columns in the petty cash book that categorise payments by expense type (e.g., postage, stationery, travel, cleaning). Each payment is recorded in the total column and also in the relevant analysis column. They are useful because they automatically total each category, making it easy to post the correct amount to each expense account in the ledger at the end of the period.
**Explanation**: Analysis columns save time at period end — instead of adding up each category manually, the column total can be posted directly. A common error is recording payments only in the total column and not in the analysis column — this defeats the purpose of having analysis columns.
