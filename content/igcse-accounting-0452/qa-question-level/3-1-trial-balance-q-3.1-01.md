### Question Q-3.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is a trial balance?
**Answer**: A trial balance is a list of all the ledger accounts and their balances at a specific date. It has two columns: debit balances in the left column and credit balances in the right column. The total of the debit column should equal the total of the credit column, confirming that the double entry has been maintained.
**Explanation**: A trial balance is not a financial statement — it is a checking tool used to verify arithmetical accuracy. A common error is confusing a trial balance with the statement of financial position — a trial balance lists all account balances (including revenue and expenses), while a statement of financial position only shows assets, liabilities and capital.
