# Question Q-7.3-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: What disclosures are required when a business changes an accounting policy? Why are they important?

**Answer**: Required disclosures: 1) **Nature of the change** — what was changed and the new policy. 2) **Reason** — why the new policy provides more relevant and reliable information, or reference to the new standard. 3) **Financial effect** — the amount of the adjustment for current and prior periods, including effect on revenue, profit, assets, liabilities, and equity. 4) **Restated comparatives** — if practicable, prior periods restated under the new policy. These are important because: 1) Users need to understand whether figures changed due to genuine performance or method changes. 2) Trend analysis depends on consistent measurement. 3) Transparency prevents management from hiding poor performance. 4) Users can assess whether the change is genuinely an improvement.

**Explanation**: Disclosure is required under accounting standards. A common error is stating the change and reason without quantifying the financial effect.
