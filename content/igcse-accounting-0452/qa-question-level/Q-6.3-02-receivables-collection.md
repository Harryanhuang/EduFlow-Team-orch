# Question Q-6.3-02

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is the trade receivables collection period? How is it calculated and what does it indicate?

**Answer**: The trade receivables collection period (also called receivables days or debtors days) measures the average number of days it takes to collect payment from credit customers. In 'times' format: Receivables turnover = Revenue / Trade receivables. In 'days' format: Receivables collection period = (Trade receivables / Revenue) × 365. A shorter period indicates efficient credit control. A period longer than credit terms offered suggests poor credit control or customer payment difficulties. A very short period may indicate overly strict credit terms that could lose sales.

**Explanation**: Use revenue (or credit sales) in the denominator. A common error is using cost of sales — receivables relate to sales at selling price, not cost.
