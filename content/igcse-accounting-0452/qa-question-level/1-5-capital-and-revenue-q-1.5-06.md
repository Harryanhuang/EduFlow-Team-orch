### Question Q-1.5-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What happens to profit and asset values if revenue expenditure is incorrectly classified as capital expenditure?
**Answer**: Profit will be overstated (too high) because the expenditure is recorded as an asset rather than an expense in the income statement. Non-current assets will be overstated in the statement of financial position because the asset figure includes spending that should have been expensed. Additionally, depreciation will be overstated in future years because depreciation is calculated on the inflated asset value.
**Explanation**: The opposite effect of Q-1.5-05. A common error is confusing the direction — recording an expense as an asset reduces expenses in the current year, inflating profit.
