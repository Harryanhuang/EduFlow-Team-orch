# Question Q-7.1-03

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: What is the accruals (matching) concept? How does it apply to accruals and prepayments?

**Answer**: The accruals concept states that revenue and expenses should be recorded in the period they are earned or incurred, regardless of when cash is received or paid. Applications: 1) **Accruals (outstanding expenses)** — expenses incurred but not yet paid are recorded as a liability and charged to the current period (e.g. electricity used in December but paid in January). 2) **Prepayments** — expenses paid in advance are recorded as an asset; only the current period portion is charged (e.g. insurance paid for 12 months — only one month charged this year). 3) **Accrued revenue** — revenue earned but not yet received is recorded as an asset and recognized in the current period. 4) **Revenue received in advance** — cash received before service is delivered is a liability. This ensures each period's profit reflects all revenues earned and expenses incurred.

**Explanation**: Accruals refers to timing of recognition; matching refers to linking expenses to the revenues they helped generate. A common error is thinking accruals only means recording unpaid bills — it also means deferring expenses paid in advance.
