# Question Q-6.3-01

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is the inventory turnover ratio? How is it calculated in both 'times' and 'days' formats?

**Answer**: The inventory turnover ratio measures how many times a business sells and replaces its inventory during a period. In 'times' format: Inventory turnover = Cost of sales / Average inventory (or closing inventory if average not available). In 'days' format: Inventory turnover days = (Inventory / Cost of sales) × 365. A higher turnover (more times) or lower days indicates more efficient inventory management — selling stock quickly and not tying up capital. A low turnover or high days may indicate obsolete stock, over-ordering, or declining demand.

**Explanation**: A common error is using revenue instead of cost of sales — inventory is valued at cost, so cost of sales is the matching figure. Using revenue gives an artificially high turnover.
