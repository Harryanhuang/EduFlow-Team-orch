# Question Q-6.3-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A business has: Revenue $800,000, cost of sales $480,000, inventory $72,000, trade receivables $90,000, trade payables $65,000, purchases $500,000, non-current assets $300,000, current assets (excl. inventory & receivables) $38,000, current liabilities (excl. payables) $25,000. Calculate all efficiency ratios and the working capital cycle. If inventory could be reduced by 20% and receivables collection improved by 10 days, what would the new working capital cycle be?

**Answer**:

**Current position:**
- Inventory turnover = $480,000 / $72,000 = 6.67 times
- Inventory days = ($72,000 / $480,000) × 365 = 54.8 days
- Receivables days = ($90,000 / $800,000) × 365 = 41.1 days
- Payables days = ($65,000 / $500,000) × 365 = 47.5 days
- Asset turnover = $800,000 / ($300,000 + $72,000 + $90,000 + $38,000 - $65,000 - $25,000) = $800,000 / $410,000 = 1.95 times
- Working capital cycle = 54.8 + 41.1 - 47.5 = 48.4 days

**After improvements:**
- New inventory = $72,000 × 0.8 = $57,600
- New inventory days = ($57,600 / $480,000) × 365 = 43.8 days
- New receivables days = 41.1 - 10 = 31.1 days
- Payables unchanged at 47.5 days
- New working capital cycle = 43.8 + 31.1 - 47.5 = 27.4 days

**Impact:** Cycle improved from 48.4 to 27.4 days — a 21.0 day reduction. The business needs 21 fewer days of financing. Inventory reduction contributed 11.0 days, receivables improvement contributed 10.0 days. This would significantly improve cash flow and reduce borrowing needs.

**Explanation**: Always recalculate inventory days from the new inventory value. A common error is simply reducing days by 20% instead of recalculating from the new inventory figure.
