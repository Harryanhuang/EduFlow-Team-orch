# Question Q-6.2-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Compare the profitability of two businesses: Business A has revenue of $500,000, gross profit $200,000, net profit $75,000, capital employed $400,000. Business B has revenue of $300,000, gross profit $150,000, net profit $60,000, capital employed $200,000. Which business is more profitable and why?

**Answer**:
**Business A:**
- Gross profit margin = ($200,000 / $500,000) × 100% = 40.0%
- Net profit margin = ($75,000 / $500,000) × 100% = 15.0%
- ROCE = ($75,000 / $400,000) × 100% = 18.75%

**Business B:**
- Gross profit margin = ($150,000 / $300,000) × 100% = 50.0%
- Net profit margin = ($60,000 / $300,000) × 100% = 20.0%
- ROCE = ($60,000 / $200,000) × 100% = 30.0%

Business B is more profitable on all three measures despite lower revenue. Its gross margin (50% vs 40%) shows better pricing or lower costs. Its net margin (20% vs 15%) shows better expense control. Its ROCE (30% vs 18.75%) shows much more efficient capital use — $0.30 profit per $1 of capital vs $0.19. Business B uses half the capital to generate 80% of A's profit.

**Explanation**: Always compare all three ratios. A common error is concluding A is more profitable because of higher revenue or absolute profit — profitability ratios reveal efficiency, not scale.
