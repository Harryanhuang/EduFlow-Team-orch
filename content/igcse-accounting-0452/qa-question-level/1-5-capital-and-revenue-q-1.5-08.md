### Question Q-1.5-08
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Is the cost of buying inventory capital expenditure or revenue expenditure? Explain your answer.
**Answer**: Buying inventory is revenue expenditure. Inventory is a current asset held for resale in the ordinary course of business — it is not a non-current asset. The cost of inventory is treated as an expense (cost of sales) when the goods are sold, matched against the sales revenue in the income statement.
**Explanation**: A common error is classifying inventory purchases as capital expenditure because inventory is an "asset" — but inventory is a current asset, and the purchase of items for resale is a revenue (operating) activity, not a capital investment.
