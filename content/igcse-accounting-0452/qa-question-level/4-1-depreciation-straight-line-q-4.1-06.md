### Question Q-4.1-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A business buys a machine for $15,000 on 1 April 20X3. Residual value $3,000. Useful life 4 years. Depreciation is charged using the straight-line method. The financial year ends 31 December. Calculate the depreciation charge for the year ended 31 December 20X3 and the carrying amount at that date.
**Answer**: Annual depreciation = ($15,000 - $3,000) / 4 = $12,000 / 4 = $3,000 per year. The machine was owned for 9 months in the year ended 31 December 20X3 (April to December). Depreciation for 9 months = $3,000 × 9/12 = $2,250. Carrying amount at 31 December 20X3 = $15,000 - $2,250 = $12,750.
**Explanation**: A common error is charging a full year's depreciation when the asset was not owned for the full year. Depreciation must be time-apportioned for the period the asset was actually owned. Always check the purchase date against the financial year end to determine how many months to charge.
