# Question Q-5.6-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: What are the key features of published financial statements for a limited company? How do they differ from those of a sole trader or partnership?

**Answer**: Published company financial statements include: 1) **Statement of comprehensive income** (income statement) — structured format showing revenue, cost of sales, gross profit, expenses, finance costs, profit before tax, tax, profit after tax. 2) **Statement of financial position** (balance sheet) — non-current assets, current assets, equity (share capital, share premium, reserves, retained earnings), non-current liabilities, current liabilities. 3) **Statement of changes in equity** — movements in share capital, share premium, reserves, and retained earnings. 4) **Statement of cash flows** — operating, investing, and financing activities. 5) **Notes** — accounting policies, detailed breakdowns, directors' remuneration, contingencies, related party transactions. Differences from sole trader/partnership: a) Equity shows share capital and reserves (not a single capital account). b) Additional statements required (cash flows, changes in equity). c) Extensive notes and legal disclosures. d) Must comply with accounting standards (IFRS/Companies Act). e) Format is prescribed by law, not by choice.

**Explanation**: The format is mandatory. A common error is presenting company equity like a sole trader's capital account — companies have multiple equity components that must be shown separately.
