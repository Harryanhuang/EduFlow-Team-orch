# Question Q-7.5-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Two businesses in the same industry report: Business X: Current ratio 2.5:1, ROCE 18%, Gross profit margin 40%, Net profit margin 12%, Inventory turnover 8 times. Business Y: Current ratio 1.2:1, ROCE 22%, Gross profit margin 35%, Net profit margin 15%, Inventory turnover 12 times. Compare performance and recommend which business a bank should lend to.

**Answer**: **Liquidity**: X (2.5:1) is stronger than Y (1.2:1). Y may struggle to meet short-term obligations. However, X may have too much idle capital. **Profitability**: Y outperforms X on ROCE (22% vs 18%) and net margin (15% vs 12%), despite lower gross margin (35% vs 40%). This means Y controls operating expenses better. **Efficiency**: Y has much higher inventory turnover (12 vs 8), suggesting better inventory management and possibly faster sales. **Overall**: Y is more profitable and efficient but has weaker liquidity. X is more liquid but less profitable. **Recommendation**: Lend to Y with conditions. Y's superior profitability and efficiency indicate strong operational performance. The low current ratio is a risk but may reflect efficient use of working capital. Mitigation: require collateral, shorter repayment terms, or a covenant to maintain minimum current ratio. Y's ROCE of 22% suggests ability to generate returns above borrowing cost.

**Explanation**: No single ratio tells the full story. A common error is recommending X based on liquidity alone, ignoring Y's superior profitability and efficiency.
