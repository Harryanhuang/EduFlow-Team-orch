# Question Q-5.5-01

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is a partnership agreement (Articles of Partnership)? Why is it important in the context of partnership accounting?

**Answer**: A partnership agreement (also called Articles of Partnership) is a written legal document that sets out the terms under which partners will operate the business together. It typically covers: profit-sharing ratios, capital contributions by each partner, partners' salaries, interest on capital, interest on drawings, procedures for admitting new partners or handling retirement/death, and dispute resolution mechanisms. It is important because: 1) Without a written agreement, the Partnership Act 1890 default provisions apply (equal profit sharing regardless of capital contribution, no interest on capital, no partners' salaries). 2) It prevents disputes by clearly defining each partner's rights and obligations. 3) It provides the basis for preparing the appropriation account correctly.

**Explanation**: The Partnership Act 1890 default rules rarely suit partners whose contributions differ. A common error is thinking a partnership agreement is optional — while a verbal agreement is legally valid, exam answers should stress the risks of relying on default provisions and how the agreement directly determines appropriation account items.
