### Question Q-5.4-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What are drawings? How do drawings differ from expenses? The owner takes $3,000 cash for personal use and takes goods costing $500 from inventory for personal use. Show the journal entries and the effect on financial statements.
**Answer**: Drawings are withdrawals of assets (cash or goods) by the owner for personal use. They reduce the owner's capital but are NOT business expenses — they do not appear in the income statement.
Journal entries:
(1) Cash drawings: Debit Drawings $3,000, Credit Cash $3,000.
(2) Goods drawings: Debit Drawings $500, Credit Purchases (or inventory) $500.
Total drawings = $3,500.
Effect: In the SOFP, drawings are deducted from capital (opening capital + net profit - drawings = closing capital). Goods drawings reduce purchases in the income statement (they are not an additional expense — they reduce the cost of goods available for sale).
**Explanation**: A common error is recording goods drawings as an expense — they reduce the purchases figure in the income statement, not add an expense. The goods are no longer available for sale, so the purchases figure must be reduced. Drawings are NOT deducted from profit to calculate net profit — they are deducted AFTER net profit in the capital section.
