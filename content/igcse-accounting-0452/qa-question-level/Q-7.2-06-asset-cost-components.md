# Question Q-7.2-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A business spent $50,000 on a new machine, $5,000 on delivery and installation, $3,000 on staff training, and $2,000 on annual maintenance. How should each be classified and treated?

**Answer**: 1) **Machine $50,000** — Capital. Recorded as non-current asset at cost, depreciated over useful life. 2) **Delivery and installation $5,000** — Capital. Directly attributable costs necessary to bring the asset to usable location and condition. Added to asset cost (total = $55,000). 3) **Staff training $3,000** — Revenue expenditure. Under IGCSE Accounting, training is treated as revenue because the benefit (trained staff) cannot be controlled by the business (staff can leave). 4) **Annual maintenance $2,000** — Revenue. Maintenance cost charged as expense in the current period. Total capital = $55,000. Total revenue = $5,000.

**Explanation**: Directly attributable costs (delivery, installation, legal fees) are part of the asset's cost. A common error is treating only the purchase price as capital — all costs necessary to bring the asset to usable condition are capitalized.
