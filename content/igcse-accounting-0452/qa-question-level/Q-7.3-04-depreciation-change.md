# Question Q-7.3-04

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A business changes depreciation from reducing balance (25%) to straight-line (10%) on a machine costing $80,000, depreciated for 3 years. Calculate the Year 4 depreciation charge under both methods and the difference.

**Answer**: 
**Reducing balance (25%):**
- Year 1: $20,000 | NBV $60,000
- Year 2: $15,000 | NBV $45,000
- Year 3: $11,250 | NBV $33,750
- Year 4: 25% × $33,750 = $8,438

**Straight-line (10% of cost):**
- Year 4: 10% × $80,000 = $8,000

**Difference:** $8,438 - $8,000 = $438 lower under straight-line.

Cumulative effect: Reducing balance has charged $46,250 vs $32,000 under straight-line — a difference of $14,250. The change reduces Year 4 charge by $438, increasing profit by that amount.

**Explanation**: The direction of change matters. Switching from reducing balance to straight-line typically reduces the charge in later years. A common error is only comparing the current year without considering cumulative effect.
