# Question Q-7.3-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A business changed three policies in one year: (1) FIFO to AVCO (-$8,000 profit), (2) reducing balance to straight-line (+$5,000), (3) receivables allowance 3% to 5% (-$4,000). Revenue $500,000, profit before changes $60,000. Calculate adjusted profit and evaluate how users would view the combination.

**Answer**:

**Adjusted profit:**
- Profit before: $60,000
- AVCO: -$8,000 | Depreciation: +$5,000 | Allowance: -$4,000
- Net effect: -$7,000
- Adjusted profit: $53,000

**Evaluation:** Three changes in one year is unusual and raises concerns. (1) FIFO to AVCO could be justified if items are interchangeable. (2) Reducing balance to straight-line could be justified if usage is even. (3) Increasing allowance from 3% to 5% is a **change in estimate**, not a policy change — justified by evidence of deteriorating customer credit. Combined effect: profit reduced 11.7%. Users may view negatively if: (a) changes appear to "take a bath" — reducing profit in a poor year to make future years look better. (b) No clear justification. (c) Timing coincides with management change (new management blaming predecessors). Users want detailed disclosures for each change. If genuinely improvements, they should be viewed positively long-term despite lower current profit.

**Explanation:** Multiple policy changes in one year are a red flag. A common error is treating an allowance percentage change as a policy change — it is a change in estimate, handled prospectively.
