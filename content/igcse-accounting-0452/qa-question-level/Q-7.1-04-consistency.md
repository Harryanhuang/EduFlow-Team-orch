# Question Q-7.1-04

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is the consistency concept? Why is it important and what happens when a business changes its accounting policy?

**Answer**: The consistency concept requires that once a business adopts an accounting method, it should continue to use it from period to period. Important because: 1) Allows meaningful comparison of results over time. 2) Prevents profit manipulation by switching methods. 3) Ensures changes in ratios reflect genuine performance changes, not accounting treatment changes. When a policy changes: 1) Disclose the change in the notes. 2) Explain the reason. 3) Show the financial effect (what profit would have been under the old method). The change should be justified by improved relevance or reliability, not by a desire to manipulate results. Consistency does not mean a business can never change — it means changes must be justified and transparent.

**Explanation**: A common error is thinking that changing an accounting policy is always wrong — it is acceptable if it provides more relevant or reliable information, but must be disclosed.
