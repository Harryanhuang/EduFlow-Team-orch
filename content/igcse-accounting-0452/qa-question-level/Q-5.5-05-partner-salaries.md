# Question Q-5.5-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: What are partners' salaries in partnership accounts? How are they treated in the financial statements?

**Answer**: Partners' salaries are amounts paid to partners for their work in the business, as specified in the partnership agreement. They are NOT expenses of the business — they are appropriations of profit. In the appropriation account, partners' salaries are deducted from net profit BEFORE the residual profit is shared. This recognizes that some partners may contribute more time or effort and should be compensated before profit is shared in the agreed ratio. If the partnership makes a loss, salaries are still shown in the appropriation account (unless the agreement states otherwise), resulting in a larger residual loss allocation. Partners' salaries do not appear in the income statement.

**Explanation**: A partner's salary is not an employment salary — partners are not employees. A common error is treating partner salaries as operating expenses. They belong only in the appropriation account. Always check whether the question states salaries are payable "whether or not profit is made."
