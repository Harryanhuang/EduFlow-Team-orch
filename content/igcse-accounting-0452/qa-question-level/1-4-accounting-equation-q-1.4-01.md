### Question Q-1.4-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: State the accounting equation.
**Answer**: Assets = Liabilities + Capital (A = L + C). This equation shows that a business's resources (assets) are financed either by borrowing (liabilities) or by the owner's investment (capital).
**Explanation**: The equation must always balance. A common error is writing it as A = L - C or C = A + L — both are wrong. The correct form shows that assets equal the total claims against the business (external claims by creditors plus the owner's claim).
