### Question Q-4.4-03
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A business wrote off a debt of $2,000 as irrecoverable in 20X3. In 20X4, the customer unexpectedly pays $1,200 in full settlement. Show the journal entries to record the recovery of a previously written-off debt.
**Answer**: When a previously written-off debt is partially recovered, two methods are acceptable:

**Full reinstatement method** (three entries, clearly separating SPL from balance sheet):
Step 1 — Reinstate the FULL original debt: Debit Receivables $2,000, Credit Irrecoverable debts recovered (SPL income) $2,000. (This is a pure reversal — the receivable returns to the books.)
Step 2 — Record the cash actually received: Debit Cash $1,200, Credit Receivables $1,200. (Balance sheet only: cash increases, receivable decreases.)
Step 3 — Write off the remaining unrecovered balance: Debit Irrecoverable debts expense (SPL expense) $800, Credit Receivables $800. (The remaining $2,000 - $1,200 = $800 will not be collected, so it is re-written off.)
SPL impact: Irrecoverable debts recovered $2,000 (income) - Irrecoverable debts expense $800 (expense) = Net income $1,200.

**Simplified method** (one entry):
Debit Cash $1,200, Credit Irrecoverable debts recovered (SPL income) $1,200.
SPL impact: Irrecoverable debts recovered $1,200 (income). Same net result as the full reinstatement method.

Both methods give the same SPL net income of $1,200 (= cash received). The full reinstatement method is preferred in exams because it shows the full picture: the debt that was reversed, the cash collected, and the balance that remains uncollectible.
**Explanation**: A common error is crediting Sales when a bad debt is recovered — this is not a sale, it is a recovery of a previously written-off amount. The correct credit is to Irrecoverable debts recovered (income), which appears in the statement of profit or loss as other income.
