# Question Q-6.1-03

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: A business has current assets of $180,000 (inventory $65,000, receivables $75,000, cash $40,000) and current liabilities of $90,000. Calculate the current ratio and acid test ratio. Comment on the results.

**Answer**:
Current ratio = $180,000 / $90,000 = 2.0:1
Acid test ratio = ($180,000 - $65,000) / $90,000 = $115,000 / $90,000 = 1.28:1

Comment: The current ratio of 2.0:1 is at the upper end of the acceptable range, suggesting sufficient current assets to cover short-term debts. However, it may indicate idle resources — inventory of $65,000 is 36% of current assets. The acid test of 1.28:1 is above the 1:1 benchmark, confirming the business can meet immediate obligations without selling inventory. The gap between the two ratios (0.72) reveals inventory is a significant portion of current assets, which may be a concern if inventory is slow-moving.

**Explanation**: Always comment on both ratios and what the gap reveals about inventory levels. A common error is calculating only one ratio or not linking the gap to inventory analysis.
