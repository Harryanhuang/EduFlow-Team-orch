# Question Q-7.1-06

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: What is the substance over form concept? Give two examples of how it applies in practice.

**Answer**: Substance over form requires that transactions are recorded based on their economic reality rather than their legal form. This ensures financial statements present a true and fair view. Examples: 1) **Hire purchase (finance lease) assets** — legally the asset belongs to the finance company until the final payment. In substance, the business controls and benefits from the asset, so it is recorded as a non-current asset with a corresponding liability and depreciated. 2) **Sale with agreement to repurchase** — "selling" inventory with a buyback agreement at a fixed price. Legally this looks like a sale, but in substance it is a loan secured against inventory. The inventory stays on the seller's balance sheet and cash received is a liability. 3) **Revenue recognition** — goods delivered but with right of return. Legally delivered, but in substance risks have not transferred — revenue should not be recognized.

**Explanation**: Substance over form prevents using legal structures to misrepresent financial position. A common error is recording transactions based solely on legal documentation without considering economic reality.
