# Question Q-7.5-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: An investor has $200,000 to invest and is considering two opportunities. Opportunity A (buy existing business): Purchase price $200,000. Last year's financials: Sales $300,000; Gross profit $120,000; Net profit $45,000. Assets at fair value: Equipment $80,000; Inventory $35,000; Receivables $25,000. Goodwill valued at $60,000. Opportunity B (start new business): Initial investment $200,000 (equipment $100,000; working capital $100,000). Projected Year 1: Sales $180,000; Gross profit margin 35%; Operating expenses $50,000. Projected Year 2: Sales $250,000; Gross profit margin 38%; Operating expenses $65,000. Projected Year 3: Sales $320,000; Gross profit margin 40%; Operating expenses $80,000. Calculate for each: (a) Year 1 profit, (b) payback period (assume equal annual cash flows for A based on last year), (c) ROCE, (d) qualitative factors. Recommend which to choose.

**Answer**: Opportunity A: (a) Year 1 profit: $45,000 (based on last year, assuming maintained). (b) Payback: $200,000 / $45,000 = 4.4 years. (c) ROCE: Net profit / Capital employed = $45,000 / $200,000 = 22.5%. (d) Qualitative: Established customer base, proven track record, existing staff. Risk: why is owner selling? May be declining. Opportunity B: (a) Year 1 profit: $180,000 × 35% = $63,000 GP - $50,000 = $13,000 NP. Year 2: $250,000 × 38% = $95,000 - $65,000 = $30,000. Year 3: $320,000 × 40% = $128,000 - $80,000 = $48,000. (b) Cumulative: Y1 $13,000; Y2 $43,000; Y3 $91,000. Payback: not within 3 years. Average annual $30,667; payback ≈ 6.5 years. (c) Average ROCE: Average profit $30,667 / $200,000 = 15.3%. (d) Qualitative: Growth potential, no legacy issues. Risk: unproven, no guaranteed customers. Recommendation: A has better returns (22.5% vs 15.3% ROCE), faster payback (4.4 vs 6.5 years), and lower risk (established). However, B has growth trajectory — Year 3 profit ($48,000) exceeds A's ($45,000). If investor is patient and confident in projections, B may be worthwhile. For most investors: A is the safer, more profitable choice.

**Explanation**: Investment appraisal requires both quantitative and qualitative analysis. A common error is looking only at first-year profit without considering payback, growth trajectory, and risk.
