### Question Q-3.1-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Which types of errors can a trial balance detect? Give two examples.
**Answer**: A trial balance can detect errors that cause debits not to equal credits. Examples: 1) Single entry error — recording only the debit side of a transaction without the corresponding credit (or vice versa). 2) Transposition error — posting different amounts to the debit and credit sides (e.g., debit $540, credit $450). 3) Calculation error — incorrect balancing of an account leading to a wrong balance being extracted.
**Explanation**: The trial balance only catches mathematical imbalances. A common error is thinking it can detect all errors — it cannot detect errors where debits still equal credits despite being wrong.
