# Question Q-7.3-01

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is an accounting policy? Give three examples of accounting policies a business might choose.

**Answer**: An accounting policy is the specific principle, basis, convention, rule or practice adopted by a business in preparing financial statements — the method chosen from available alternatives under accounting standards. Examples: 1) **Inventory valuation** — FIFO vs AVCO. 2) **Depreciation method** — straight-line vs reducing balance. 3) **Receivables allowance** — percentage of receivables, specific review of individual debts, or historical loss rates. Each policy choice affects reported profit and asset values, so consistency in applying the chosen policy is essential.

**Explanation**: A common error is confusing policies with concepts. Concepts (going concern, prudence) are fundamental principles that must always be followed. Policies are specific methods chosen within those concepts.
