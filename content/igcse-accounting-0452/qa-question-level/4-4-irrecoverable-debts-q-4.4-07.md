### Question Q-4.4-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A business has an existing allowance for receivables of $3,000 at 1 January 20X4. At 31 December 20X4, after writing off $1,000 of irrecoverable debts, the remaining receivables are $40,000. The business decides to reduce the general allowance from 5% to 4% of receivables. Show: (a) the irrecoverable debts expense, (b) the change in allowance, (c) the total charge to the statement of profit or loss.
**Answer**: (a) Irrecoverable debts written off = $1,000. This is charged to the statement of profit or loss as irrecoverable debts expense.
(b) New allowance = 4% × $40,000 = $1,600. Existing allowance = $3,000. Decrease in allowance = $3,000 - $1,600 = $1,400. A decrease in allowance is credited to the statement of profit or loss (it reduces the expense / is income).
(c) Total charge to statement of profit or loss: Irrecoverable debts expense $1,000 - Decrease in allowance $1,400 = net credit of $400 (net income of $400).
Journal entries: (1) Dr Irrecoverable debts expense $1,000, Cr Receivables $1,000. (2) Dr Allowance for receivables $1,400, Cr Decrease in allowance (SPL) $1,400.
**Explanation**: A common error is treating the decrease in allowance as an additional expense — it is the opposite. A decrease means the business now expects to collect MORE than previously estimated, so the reduction is income (a credit to SPL). Always check: increase in allowance = expense; decrease in allowance = income.
