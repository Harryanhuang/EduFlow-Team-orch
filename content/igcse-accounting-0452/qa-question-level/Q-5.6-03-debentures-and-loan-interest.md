# Question Q-5.6-03

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What are debentures? How does debenture (loan) interest differ from dividends on shares?

**Answer**: Debentures are long-term loans secured against the company's assets. Debenture holders are lenders, not owners, and receive a fixed rate of interest. Differences from share dividends: 1) **Legal obligation**: Debenture interest must be paid even if the company makes a loss. Dividends are only paid from distributable profits. 2) **Accounting treatment**: Debenture interest is a finance cost in the income statement (an expense that reduces profit). Dividends are an appropriation of profit shown after profit after tax. 3) **Tax treatment**: Debenture interest is tax-deductible — it reduces the company's taxable profit. Dividends are paid from after-tax profit and are not tax-deductible. 4) **Security**: Debentures are secured against specific company assets. Shares represent ownership with no security. 5) **Repayment**: Debentures have a fixed repayment date. Share capital does not need to be repaid.

**Explanation**: The critical distinction is debt vs equity. A common error is treating debenture interest as an appropriation — it is an expense and appears in the income statement, not the appropriation account.
