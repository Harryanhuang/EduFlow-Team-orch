### Question Q-2.6-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: The petty cash imprest is $300. At month end, cash in hand is $72 but the vouchers total only $218. There is a shortage of $10. How should this be recorded and what might be the cause?
**Answer**: Expected cash = $300 - $218 = $82. Actual cash = $72. Shortage = $10. The shortage should be recorded as an additional petty cash payment (debit an expense account such as "petty cash shortage" or "cash losses" $10, credit petty cash $10). This increases total payments to $228. Reimbursement = $228. Possible causes: a payment was made without a voucher, cash was stolen, or a voucher was lost. The shortage should be investigated.
**Explanation**: Cash shortages must be recorded to keep the imprest system in balance. A common error is ignoring the shortage and reimbursing only $218 — this would leave the fund at $290 instead of $300 next period.
