# Question Q-7.4-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Compare two approaches: Approach A emphasizes relevance (fair values, forward-looking estimates, management judgment). Approach B emphasizes faithful representation (historical cost, verifiable transactions, conservative estimates). Evaluate strengths and weaknesses of each.

**Answer**: **Approach A (relevance-focused)**: Strengths: Current, decision-useful information. Fair values reflect market conditions. Forward-looking estimates help assess future prospects. Weaknesses: Fair value estimates may not be verifiable. Forward-looking estimates may be unreliable. Management judgment introduces bias risk. Market volatility creates reportable volatility not reflecting underlying performance. Best serves: relevance, timeliness. May compromise: faithful representation, verifiability. **Approach B (faithful representation-focused)**: Strengths: Historical cost is objectively verifiable. Conservative estimates reduce overstatement risk. Consistent and reliable. Less susceptible to manipulation. Weaknesses: Historical cost may become irrelevant over time (e.g. a building bought 30 years ago at $100,000 may now be worth $2 million). Conservative estimates may understate assets and profits. May not reflect economic reality. Best serves: faithful representation, verifiability, comparability. May compromise: relevance. **Conclusion**: Neither is perfect. The ideal is balanced — fair value where reliable market data exists, historical cost where it does not. Accounting standards reflect this balance (fair value for financial instruments, historical cost for property, plant and equipment).

**Explanation**: The relevance vs reliability trade-off is the central tension in accounting. A common error is declaring one approach "better" — the correct answer recognizes both have value and the best approach depends on the item being measured.
