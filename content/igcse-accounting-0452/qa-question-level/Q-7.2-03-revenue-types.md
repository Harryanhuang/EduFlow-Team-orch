# Question Q-7.2-03

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: What types of expenditure are classified as revenue expenditure? Distinguish between repairs, maintenance, and improvements.

**Answer**: Revenue expenditure includes: 1) **Repairs** — restoring an asset to its original working condition after damage. Example: repairing a broken machine part. 2) **Maintenance** — routine work to keep an asset in good order. Example: servicing a vehicle, painting a building. 3) **Operating costs** — day-to-day running. Example: wages, rent, utilities, insurance, raw materials. 4) **Finance costs** — interest on loans, bank charges. 5) **Selling and distribution costs** — advertising, delivery costs, commissions. Key distinction: repairs and maintenance restore or maintain existing condition (revenue); improvements increase capacity, quality, or useful life beyond original condition (capital). Repainting in original colour = maintenance (revenue); specialized coating extending building life by 10 years = enhancement (capital).

**Explanation**: The test is whether expenditure brings the asset back to original condition (revenue) or takes it beyond (capital). A common error is classifying all expenditure on non-current assets as capital — the nature determines classification.
