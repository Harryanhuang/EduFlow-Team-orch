### Question Q-4.4-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: At 31 December 20X4, receivables total $60,000. The business makes a specific allowance of $2,000 against customer B (who is in financial difficulty) and a general allowance of 3% against the remaining receivables. Calculate the total allowance and show the net receivables in the statement of financial position.
**Answer**: First write off any irrecoverable debts (none given, so receivables = $60,000).
Specific allowance = $2,000 (against customer B).
Remaining receivables after specific allowance = $60,000 - $2,000 = $58,000.
General allowance = 3% × $58,000 = $1,740.
Total allowance = $2,000 + $1,740 = $3,740.
Net receivables in statement of financial position = $60,000 - $3,740 = $56,260.
Alternatively shown as: Receivables $60,000 less allowance for receivables $3,740 = $56,260.
**Explanation**: A common error is calculating the general allowance on the full $60,000 instead of the remaining balance after the specific allowance. The specific allowance is deducted first because it represents a known reduction; the general allowance applies to the remainder where the risk is unknown. Always apply the general allowance to receivables net of specific allowances.
