# Question Q-7.5-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A business changed its inventory valuation method from FIFO to AVCO on 1 January 20X5. Under FIFO, inventory at 31 Dec 20X4 was $85,000; under AVCO it would have been $78,000. 20X5 purchases $320,000; sales $500,000. Closing inventory 31 Dec 20X5: FIFO $92,000; AVCO $88,000. Operating expenses $120,000 (both years). Prepare: (a) income statements for 20X5 under both methods, (b) comparative analysis showing the effect of the change, (c) disclose the change in accounting policy with justification.

**Answer**: (a) FIFO 20X5: Opening inventory $85,000 + Purchases $320,000 - Closing $92,000 = Cost of sales $313,000. Gross profit: $500,000 - $313,000 = $187,000. Net profit: $187,000 - $120,000 = $67,000. AVCO 20X5: Opening inventory $78,000 + Purchases $320,000 - Closing $88,000 = Cost of sales $310,000. Gross profit: $500,000 - $310,000 = $190,000. Net profit: $190,000 - $120,000 = $70,000. (b) Effect of change: Net profit under AVCO is $3,000 higher. This is because the opening inventory adjustment ($85,000 - $78,000 = $7,000 reduction) partially offsets the closing inventory difference ($92,000 - $88,000 = $4,000 reduction), net effect $7,000 - $4,000 = $3,000 increase in profit. (c) Disclosure note: "The business changed its inventory valuation method from FIFO to AVCO with effect from 1 January 20X5. The change provides a more consistent measure of inventory cost in a period of volatile prices. The effect on net profit for the year is an increase of $3,000. Comparative figures for 20X4 have been restated."

**Explanation**: Policy changes require disclosure and restatement. A common error is not adjusting the opening inventory to the new basis when comparing.
