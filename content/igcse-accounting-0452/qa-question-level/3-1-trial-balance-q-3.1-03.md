### Question Q-3.1-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Which of the following account types normally have debit balances and which have credit balances? (a) Assets, (b) Liabilities, (c) Capital, (d) Expenses, (e) Revenue, (f) Drawings.
**Answer**: Debit balances: (a) Assets, (d) Expenses, (f) Drawings. Credit balances: (b) Liabilities, (c) Capital, (e) Revenue. In the trial balance, each account is placed in the column matching its normal balance type.
**Explanation**: This follows the DEALER framework. A common error is putting revenue in the debit column — revenue increases capital and capital increases are credits, so revenue has a credit balance.
