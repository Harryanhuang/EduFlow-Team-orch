### Question Q-1.2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why managers need more detailed accounting information than owners.
**Answer**: Managers need detailed, frequent (often weekly or monthly) reports broken down by department, product, or region to make operational decisions such as pricing, cost control, resource allocation, and performance evaluation. Owners typically need summary-level information (annual reports) to assess overall investment returns. Managers run the business day-to-day and need granular data; owners evaluate the business as a whole.
**Explanation**: The level of detail reflects the different roles — managers operate, owners invest. A common error is assuming owners and managers need the same information — in large businesses they are different people with different needs.
