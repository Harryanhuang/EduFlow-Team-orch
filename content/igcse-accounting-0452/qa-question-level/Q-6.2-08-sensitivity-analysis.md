# Question Q-6.2-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A business has: Revenue $600,000, cost of sales $360,000, operating expenses $144,000, non-current assets $350,000, current assets $180,000, current liabilities $80,000. Calculate all profitability ratios. If cost of sales could be reduced by 5% without changing revenue, how would each ratio change?

**Answer**:

**Current position:**
- Gross profit = $240,000 | Gross margin = ($240,000 / $600,000) × 100% = 40.0%
- Net profit = $96,000 | Net margin = ($96,000 / $600,000) × 100% = 16.0%
- Capital employed = $350,000 + ($180,000 - $80,000) = $450,000
- ROCE = ($96,000 / $450,000) × 100% = 21.3%

**After 5% cost reduction:**
- New cost of sales = $342,000
- New gross profit = $258,000 | New gross margin = 43.0%
- New net profit = $114,000 | New net margin = 19.0%
- New ROCE = ($114,000 / $450,000) × 100% = 25.3%

**Impact:**
- Gross margin improved by 3.0 percentage points
- Net margin improved by 3.0 percentage points (same improvement because expenses are fixed)
- ROCE improved by 4.0 percentage points
- The full $18,000 saving flows through to net profit since operating expenses are unchanged.

**Explanation**: A cost of sales reduction flows through to all three ratios. A common error is not recognizing that net profit improvement equals gross profit improvement when expenses are fixed.
