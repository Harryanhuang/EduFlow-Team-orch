# Question Q-5.6-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: What is a company's appropriation account? How does it differ from a partnership's appropriation account?

**Answer**: A company's appropriation account shows how profit after tax is distributed. It starts with profit after tax, then shows dividends paid or proposed (preference and ordinary) and transfers to reserves (e.g. general reserve), arriving at retained profit for the year. Differences from a partnership: 1) **Starting point**: Company uses profit after tax; partnership uses net profit (no corporation tax). 2) **Items**: Company shows dividends and reserve transfers; partnership shows interest on capital, partners' salaries, interest on drawings, and residual profit share. 3) **Directors' salaries**: Company treats them as an income statement expense; partnership treats partners' salaries as appropriations. 4) **Tax**: Company deducts corporation tax in the income statement; partnerships do not pay corporation tax. 5) **Reserves**: Companies may transfer profit to reserves; partnerships distribute all profit to partners.

**Explanation**: The structure reflects different ownership models. A common error is including directors' salaries in the company appropriation account — they are an expense, not an appropriation. Only dividends and reserve transfers appear.
