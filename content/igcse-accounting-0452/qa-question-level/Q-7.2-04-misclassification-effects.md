# Question Q-7.2-04

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: How does misclassifying capital expenditure as revenue expenditure (or vice versa) affect the financial statements?

**Answer**: **Capital classified as revenue** (expensing a capital item): 1) Current period expenses overstated → profit understated. 2) Non-current assets understated (asset not on balance sheet). 3) Future periods' profit overstated (no depreciation charge). 4) Total equity understated. **Revenue classified as capital** (capitalizing an expense): 1) Current period expenses understated → profit overstated. 2) Non-current assets overstated (expense recorded as asset). 3) Future periods' profit understated (depreciation on inflated value). 4) Total equity overstated. Both errors distort true financial performance and position. Materiality determines whether the error is significant enough to require correction.

**Explanation**: The direction determines whether profit is overstated or understated. A common error is thinking total profit over the asset's life is unaffected — it is, but timing is wrong, misleading users.
