### Question Q-1.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A supplier is considering offering trade credit to a new business customer. What accounting information would the supplier want to see and why?
**Answer**: The supplier would want to see the business's statement of financial position (to assess assets vs liabilities and net worth), recent income statement (to check profitability), and liquidity position (current ratio). This information shows whether the customer can pay their debts on time. The supplier may also check payment history with other suppliers through credit reference agencies.
**Explanation**: Suppliers are concerned about trade receivables becoming irrecoverable. A common error is focusing only on the customer's sales volume — a high-sales business with poor cash management may still default.
