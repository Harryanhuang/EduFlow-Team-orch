### Question Q-2.4-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A trade receivables account shows: Debit side total $12,000, Credit side total $8,000. What is the balance and what type of balance is it?
**Answer**: The balance is $4,000 debit balance ($12,000 - $8,000). A debit balance in a trade receivables account means customers owe the business $4,000. This is normal — receivables are assets and should have debit balances. The balance c/f of $4,000 would be entered on the credit side to balance the account, and brought forward as a debit balance b/f.
**Explanation**: The side with the larger total determines the balance type. A common error is reversing the calculation ($8,000 - $12,000) or misidentifying the balance type.
