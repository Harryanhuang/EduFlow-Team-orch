### Question Q-4.4-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: At 1 January 20X4, a business had receivables of $52,000 and an allowance for receivables of $2,600 (5% of receivables). During the year: credit sales $180,000; cash received from receivables $170,000; irrecoverable debts written off $3,000. At 31 December 20X4, the business increases the general allowance to 6% of remaining receivables. Calculate: (a) closing receivables before allowance, (b) the new allowance, (c) the total charge to the statement of profit or loss for irrecoverable debts and change in allowance.
**Answer**: (a) Closing receivables before allowance: Opening $52,000 + Credit sales $180,000 - Cash received $170,000 - Written off $3,000 = $59,000.
(b) New allowance = 6% × $59,000 = $3,540. Existing allowance = $2,600. Increase = $3,540 - $2,600 = $940.
(c) Total charge to statement of profit or loss: Irrecoverable debts expense $3,000 + Increase in allowance $940 = $3,940 total expense.
Statement of financial position at 31 Dec 20X4: Receivables $59,000 less allowance $3,540 = net receivables $55,460.
**Explanation**: A common error is not reconstructing the receivables T-account — closing receivables must be calculated from opening + sales - cash - write-offs. Another error is calculating the new allowance on the opening receivables ($52,000) instead of the closing balance ($59,000). Always calculate the receivables balance at the reporting date first, then apply the allowance percentage.
