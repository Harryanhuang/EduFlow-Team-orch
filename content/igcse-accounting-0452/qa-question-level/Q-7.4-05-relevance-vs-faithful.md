# Question Q-7.4-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: How do relevance and faithful representation sometimes conflict? Give an example and explain resolution.

**Answer**: They conflict when the most relevant information is difficult to measure reliably. Example: **Fair value of property** — market value may be highly relevant (users want current value), but without an active market, valuation requires judgment and assumptions, making faithful representation questionable. **Contingent assets** — a possible future gain from a lawsuit may be relevant, but if outcome is uncertain, recognizing it would not be faithfully represented. Resolution: 1) Include information if relevant AND can be faithfully represented (even with estimation — acceptable if method is disclosed). 2) Disclose in notes rather than recognize if relevant but cannot be reliably measured (e.g. contingent assets disclosed but not recognized). 3) Fully disclose methods and assumptions so users can assess reliability.

**Explanation**: Conflict is common. A common error is thinking one always trumps the other — the correct approach is to include with appropriate disclosure of uncertainty.
