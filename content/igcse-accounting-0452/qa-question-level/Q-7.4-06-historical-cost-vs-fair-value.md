# Question Q-7.4-06

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Evaluate how changing from historical cost to fair value for non-current assets affects each of the six qualitative characteristics.

**Answer**: **Relevance**: Increases — fair value provides current information useful for decision-making. **Faithful representation**: May decrease — fair value estimates involve judgment and assumptions that may not be verifiable or free from error, especially without active markets. **Comparability**: Mixed — improves comparability with other fair value users, but reduces comparability with own prior periods (unless restated). **Verifiability**: Decreases — historical cost is verified from purchase documents; fair value requires valuation techniques different valuers may estimate differently. **Timeliness**: May decrease — fair value assessments take more time, potentially delaying publication. **Understandability**: May decrease — fair value measurements and assumptions are more complex than historical cost. Overall: The change trades reliability (faithful representation, verifiability) for relevance. Whether worthwhile depends on asset nature and market data availability.

**Explanation**: The six characteristics can point in different directions. A common error is evaluating only relevance and ignoring the impact on faithful representation and verifiability.
