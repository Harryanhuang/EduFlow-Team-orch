# Question Q-7.3-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A business changed from straight-line to reducing balance depreciation in Year 3, claiming better reflection of asset usage. Profit before depreciation was $100,000 each year. Asset cost $60,000, no residual value, 5-year life. Calculate profit under both methods for all 5 years and evaluate the change.

**Answer**:

**Straight-line ($12,000/year):** Y1-Y5 profit = $88,000 each. Total $440,000.

**Reducing balance (40%):**
- Y1: Dep $24,000 | Profit $76,000
- Y2: Dep $14,400 | Profit $85,600
- Y3: Dep $8,640 | Profit $91,360
- Y4: Dep $5,184 | Profit $94,816
- Y5: Dep $7,776 | Profit $92,224
- Total $440,000

**Evaluation:** The change in Year 3 increased profit from $88,000 to $91,360 (by $3,360). In Year 4, profit increased to $94,816. The change front-loaded depreciation (higher early, lower later). If the asset genuinely loses value more quickly early (e.g. technology), reducing balance is appropriate. If made specifically to boost declining profits, it is manipulation. Total profit over 5 years is the same — only timing differs.

**Explanation:** Total is the same under both methods — only timing differs. A common error is concluding the change "improved" performance without recognizing total profit is unchanged.
