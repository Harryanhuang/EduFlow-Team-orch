### Question Q-5.2-09
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Prepare the income statement for the year ended 31 December 20X4 from the following trial balance extract and adjustments. Trial balance: Sales $350,000; Opening inventory $25,000; Purchases $190,000; Carriage inwards $7,000; Wages $55,000; Rent $20,000; Electricity $10,000; Motor vehicles at cost $80,000; Accumulated depreciation - vehicles $32,000; Receivables $40,000; Allowance for receivables $2,000. Adjustments: (i) Closing inventory $28,000. (ii) Wages accrued $4,000. (iii) Rent prepaid $3,000. (iv) Vehicles depreciated 25% reducing balance. (v) $2,000 irrecoverable debt written off. (vi) Allowance adjusted to 5% of remaining receivables.
**Answer**:
Income statement for the year ended 31 December 20X4:

Sales: $350,000
Less COGS: Opening $25,000 + Purchases $190,000 + Carriage inwards $7,000 - Closing $28,000 = $194,000
Gross profit: $350,000 - $194,000 = $156,000

Operating expenses:
  Wages ($55,000 + $4,000): $59,000
  Rent ($20,000 - $3,000): $17,000
  Electricity: $10,000
  Vehicle depreciation (($80,000 - $32,000) × 25%): $12,000
  Irrecoverable debts: $2,000
  Change in allowance: new = 5% × ($40,000 - $2,000) = $1,900; existing $2,000; decrease $100 (credit)
  Total expenses: $59,000 + $17,000 + $10,000 + $12,000 + $2,000 - $100 = $99,900

Net profit: $156,000 - $99,900 = $56,100

**Explanation**: Critical order of operations: (1) COGS includes carriage inwards in purchases. (2) Vehicle depreciation uses reducing balance on carrying amount ($80,000 - $32,000 = $48,000 × 25% = $12,000). (3) Irrecoverable debt written off FIRST ($40,000 - $2,000 = $38,000), then allowance on remaining (5% × $38,000 = $1,900). (4) Existing allowance $2,000 → new $1,900 → decrease of $100, which is a CREDIT to SPL (reduces expenses). (5) The decrease in allowance partially offsets the irrecoverable debts expense.
