### Question Q-1.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the difference between bookkeeping and accounting?
**Answer**: Bookkeeping is the routine process of recording financial transactions in the books of account. Accounting goes further — it includes classifying, summarising, interpreting and communicating financial information to help users make decisions. Bookkeeping is a subset of accounting.
**Explanation**: A common error is treating bookkeeping and accounting as the same thing. Bookkeeping is the mechanical recording stage; accounting adds analysis, interpretation and reporting. Exam answers that only say "bookkeeping records and accounting analyses" are incomplete — accounting also includes classification, summarisation and communication.
