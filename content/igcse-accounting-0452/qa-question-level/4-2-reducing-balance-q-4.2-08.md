### Question Q-4.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A business buys a vehicle for $45,000 on 1 January 20X1. Depreciation is charged at 40% per annum on a reducing balance basis. On 30 September 20X3 the vehicle is sold for $10,000. Calculate the profit or loss on disposal and show the relevant entries in the statement of profit or loss and statement of financial position for the year ended 31 December 20X3.
**Answer**: Year 1 (20X1): Depreciation = $45,000 × 40% = $18,000. Carrying amount = $45,000 - $18,000 = $27,000. Year 2 (20X2): Depreciation = $27,000 × 40% = $10,800. Carrying amount = $27,000 - $10,800 = $16,200. Year 3 (20X3, to 30 September = 9 months): Full year depreciation on carrying amount = $16,200 × 40% = $6,480. Time-apportioned for 9 months = $6,480 × 9/12 = $4,860. Carrying amount at disposal = $16,200 - $4,860 = $11,340. Sale proceeds = $10,000. Loss on disposal = $11,340 - $10,000 = $1,340 loss.
Statement of profit or loss for year ended 31 Dec 20X3: Depreciation expense $4,860 (9 months) + Loss on disposal $1,340 = total charge $6,200. Statement of financial position at 31 Dec 20X3: No vehicle (disposed). The accumulated depreciation at disposal = $18,000 + $10,800 + $4,860 = $33,660. Check: Cost $45,000 - Accumulated $33,660 = Carrying $11,340.
**Explanation**: A common error is charging a full year's depreciation in the year of disposal — depreciation is only charged up to the date of disposal (9 months here). Another common error is comparing sale proceeds with original cost instead of carrying amount at the disposal date. Profit/loss = Sale proceeds minus carrying amount at disposal.
