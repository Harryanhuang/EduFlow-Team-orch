### Question Q-1.5-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the difference between capital receipt and revenue receipt? Give one example of each.
**Answer**: Capital receipt is income from selling non-current assets or from issuing shares/capital introduction. Example: proceeds from selling an old machine. Revenue receipt is income earned from the normal trading activities of the business. Example: sales revenue, rent received, interest received on a bank deposit.
**Explanation**: Capital receipts come from disposing of long-term assets or raising capital; revenue receipts come from regular business operations. A common error is treating the sale of a non-current asset as sales revenue.
