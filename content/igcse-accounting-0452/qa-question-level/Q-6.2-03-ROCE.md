# Question Q-6.2-03

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is ROCE (return on capital employed)? How is it calculated and why is it important?

**Answer**: ROCE measures how efficiently a business uses its capital to generate profit. It is calculated as: ROCE = (Operating profit / Capital employed) × 100%. Where capital employed = total assets minus current liabilities (or equity plus non-current liabilities). ROCE is important because: 1) It shows the return generated on invested capital. 2) It can be compared to alternative investments (e.g. bank rates) to assess effectiveness. 3) A ROCE higher than borrowing costs indicates value creation; lower indicates value destruction. 4) Trends reveal whether the business is becoming more or less efficient.

**Explanation**: ROCE is one of the most important profitability ratios. A common error is using net profit after interest and tax instead of operating profit in the numerator. ROCE measures return on ALL capital, so profit should be before interest and tax.
