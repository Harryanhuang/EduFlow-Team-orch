### Question Q-4.2-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A machine is depreciated at 30% per annum using the reducing balance method. After 2 years, the accumulated depreciation is $13,020. What was the original cost of the machine? What is the carrying amount at the end of year 3?
**Answer**: Let the cost = C. Carrying amount after 1 year = C × (1 - 0.30) = 0.70C. Carrying amount after 2 years = 0.70C × 0.70 = 0.49C. Accumulated depreciation after 2 years = Cost - Carrying amount = C - 0.49C = 0.51C. We are told 0.51C = $13,020. So C = $13,020 / 0.51 = $25,529 (rounded to nearest dollar). Verification: Year 1 dep = $25,529 × 30% = $7,659. Carrying = $25,529 - $7,659 = $17,870. Year 2 dep = $17,870 × 30% = $5,361. Accumulated = $7,659 + $5,361 = $13,020. Carrying amount after 3 years = $25,529 × 0.70^3 = $25,529 × 0.343 = $8,756. Alternatively: Year 3 dep = $17,870 - $5,361 = $12,509 × 30% = $3,753 (wait — recalculate from carrying at end of year 2: $12,509 × 30% = $3,753). Carrying = $12,509 - $3,753 = $8,756.
**Explanation**: A common error is setting up the equation as C × rate × years = accumulated depreciation — that is the straight-line approach. Under reducing balance, accumulated depreciation = C × (1 - (1-rate)^n). Always verify by working forward year by year to confirm the accumulated depreciation matches the given figure.
