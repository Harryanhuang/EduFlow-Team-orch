# Question Q-7.4-01

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What are the two fundamental qualitative characteristics of useful financial information?

**Answer**: The two fundamental characteristics are **relevance** and **faithful representation**. **Relevance** means information can make a difference in users' decisions — it has predictive value (helps forecast future outcomes), confirmatory value (confirms or corrects past assessments), or both. **Faithful representation** means information accurately reflects the economic phenomena it represents. It must be complete (all necessary information included), neutral (free from bias), and free from error. Both must be present — relevant information that is not faithfully represented is misleading, and faithfully represented information that is not relevant is useless.

**Explanation**: A common error is listing all characteristics equally. Relevance and faithful representation are fundamental; comparability, verifiability, timeliness, and understandability are enhancing.
