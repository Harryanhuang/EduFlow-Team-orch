### Question Q-2.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is a debit note? How does it differ from a credit note?
**Answer**: A debit note is a document sent by a buyer to a seller requesting a reduction in the amount owed, usually because goods have been returned or were incorrectly supplied. It differs from a credit note in who issues it: a credit note is issued by the seller, while a debit note is issued by the buyer. Both relate to the same underlying event (e.g., returned goods) but from opposite perspectives.
**Explanation**: A common error is confusing the issuer — credit notes come from the seller, debit notes from the buyer. Some businesses use debit notes internally to authorise a credit note request.
