# Question Q-6.3-03

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is the trade payables payment period? How is it calculated and what does it indicate about the business's relationship with suppliers?

**Answer**: The trade payables payment period (also called payables days or creditors days) measures the average number of days it takes to pay suppliers. In 'times' format: Payables turnover = Credit purchases / Trade payables. In 'days' format: Payables payment period = (Trade payables / Credit purchases) × 365. When purchases are not given, cost of sales may be used as a proxy. A longer period may indicate using suppliers as free finance, potentially damaging relationships and losing discounts. A very short period may indicate paying too quickly unless early payment discounts are available. The period should be compared with agreed credit terms.

**Explanation**: Use purchases (or cost of sales as proxy) in the denominator. A common error is using revenue — payables relate to purchases at cost, not sales.
