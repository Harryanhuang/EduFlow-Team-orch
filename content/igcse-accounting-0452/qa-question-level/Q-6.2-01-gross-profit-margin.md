# Question Q-6.2-01

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is the gross profit margin? How is it calculated and what does it measure?

**Answer**: The gross profit margin measures the percentage of revenue that remains as gross profit after deducting the cost of sales. It is calculated as: Gross profit margin = (Gross profit / Revenue) × 100%. A higher margin indicates the business retains more from each dollar of revenue to cover expenses and generate net profit. An increase could result from higher selling prices, lower cost of sales, or a shift to higher-margin products. A decrease could result from price cuts, rising costs, or increased competition.

**Explanation**: Expressed as a percentage. A common error is using net profit instead of gross profit, or reversing the formula. Always use gross profit and revenue from the income statement.
