# Question Q-7.2-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Classify and explain: (a) $500 legal fees for purchasing a building. (b) $3,000 repainting a warehouse. (c) $15,000 installing new air conditioning that increases factory output. (d) $200 annual van insurance. (e) $8,000 replacing a truck engine, extending useful life by 3 years.

**Answer**:
(a) **Capital** — legal fees directly attributable to asset acquisition are part of the asset's cost, necessary to bring it to usable condition.

(b) **Revenue** — repainting is maintenance that restores the warehouse to original condition. Does not increase capacity, quality, or useful life.

(c) **Capital** — the air conditioning increases factory output capacity (enhancement). Provides benefits beyond the current period.

(d) **Revenue** — insurance is an operating cost that protects the asset but does not enhance it. Benefits consumed within the policy period.

(e) **Capital** — replacing the engine extends the truck's useful life by 3 years (enhancement). Provides long-term benefits beyond the original expected life.

**Explanation**: The key test is whether expenditure provides benefits beyond the current period. Legal fees for purchase are capital. Repairs/maintenance are revenue. Enhancements are capital.
