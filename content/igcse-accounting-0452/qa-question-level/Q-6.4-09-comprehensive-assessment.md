# Question Q-6.4-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A business's ratios over three years: Gross margin: 38%, 36%, 34%. Net margin: 14%, 12%, 11%. Current ratio: 1.8:1, 1.5:1, 1.3:1. ROCE: 22%, 18%, 14%. Inventory turnover: 7, 6, 5 times. Receivables days: 38, 42, 48. Provide a comprehensive assessment and recommend actions.

**Answer**:

**Assessment:** All ratios deteriorate consistently — a business in decline across all dimensions:

1) **Profitability**: Gross margin fell 4pp (38% to 34%) — cost of sales growing faster than revenue (rising input costs, price pressure, or lower-margin product mix). Net margin fell 3pp (14% to 11%) — operating expenses also outpacing revenue.

2) **Liquidity**: Current ratio fell from 1.8:1 to 1.3:1 — becoming less able to meet short-term obligations.

3) **Efficiency**: Inventory turnover fell from 7 to 5 times (52 to 73 days) — stock moving much slower. Receivables days increased from 38 to 48 — credit control weakening. Both tie up cash.

4) **Returns**: ROCE fell from 22% to 14% — significantly less return on capital. Still may exceed borrowing costs, but trajectory is concerning.

**Diagnosis**: Combination of pricing pressure, operational inefficiency, and deteriorating financial health — not a single-issue problem.

**Recommended actions**: 1) **Pricing review** — assess price increases or product mix shift to higher-margin items. 2) **Cost of sales analysis** — negotiate with suppliers, seek alternatives, improve production efficiency. 3) **Operating expense audit** — eliminate unnecessary costs. 4) **Inventory management** — review stock levels, discontinue slow-movers, implement JIT. 5) **Credit control** — tighten terms, improve collections, consider factoring. 6) **Cash flow management** — prepare forecasts and identify contingency funding. 7) **Strategic review** — consider whether the business model needs fundamental change.

**Explanation**: When all ratios deteriorate together, the problem is systemic. A common error is focusing on one ratio when the business needs a comprehensive turnaround plan.
