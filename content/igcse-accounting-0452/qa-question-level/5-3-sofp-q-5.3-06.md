### Question Q-5.3-06
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: How is equity (capital) calculated in the statement of financial position? A sole trader has: opening capital $50,000; net profit for the year $28,000; drawings $12,000. Show the capital section and verify that net assets equal capital.
**Answer**: Capital (owner's equity) for a sole trader:
Opening capital: $50,000
Add net profit for the year: $28,000
Less drawings: ($12,000)
Closing capital: $66,000

The net profit from the income statement is added to capital because it belongs to the owner. Drawings are deducted because the owner has withdrawn funds for personal use.
The SOFP must balance: total net assets = closing capital. If net assets are calculated as $66,000, this equals the closing capital of $66,000 — the SOFP balances.
**Explanation**: A common error is deducting drawings before adding net profit — the order is: opening capital + profit - drawings = closing capital. Another error is treating drawings as an expense in the income statement — drawings are NOT an expense; they are a reduction in capital shown only in the SOFP.
