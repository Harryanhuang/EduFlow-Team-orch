### Question Q-5.2-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A business's trial balance (extract) at 31 December 20X4 shows: Sales $150,000; Purchases $75,000; Opening inventory $12,000; Rent $6,000; Wages $25,000; Allowance for receivables at 1 Jan 20X4 $1,000; Receivables $20,000. Adjustments: (a) Closing inventory valued at $14,000. (b) Rent accrued $800. (c) Wages prepaid $1,500. (d) Allowance for receivables to be increased to 6% of receivables. Prepare the income statement for the year.
**Answer**:
Income statement for the year ended 31 December 20X4:

Sales: $150,000
Less COGS: Opening inventory $12,000 + Purchases $75,000 - Closing inventory $14,000 = $73,000
Gross profit: $150,000 - $73,000 = $77,000

Operating expenses:
  Rent ($6,000 + $800 accrual): $6,800
  Wages ($25,000 - $1,500 prepayment): $23,500
  Increase in allowance (6% × $20,000 = $1,200; existing $1,000; increase $200): $200
  Total operating expenses: $30,500

Net profit: $77,000 - $30,500 = $46,500

**Explanation**: A common error is not making all adjustments before calculating expenses. Each adjustment must be applied: accruals add to the trial balance figure, prepayments subtract, and the change in allowance is the difference between new and old amounts. The allowance calculation is on receivables ($20,000) — the trial balance receivables figure is before any write-off (none given here).
