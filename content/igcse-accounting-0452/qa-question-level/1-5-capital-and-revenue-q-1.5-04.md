### Question Q-1.5-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Classify each of the following as capital or revenue expenditure: (a) $5,000 spent on repairing a delivery van, (b) $25,000 spent on a new delivery van, (c) $3,000 spent on installing a new engine that increases the van's capacity, (d) $200 spent on annual van insurance.
**Answer**: (a) Revenue expenditure — repair maintains the van in its current condition. (b) Capital expenditure — purchase of a non-current asset. (c) Capital expenditure — the new engine improves the asset's capacity, adding value beyond its original state. (d) Revenue expenditure — insurance is a recurring operating cost.
**Explanation**: The key distinction is whether spending adds value/improves the asset (capital) or simply maintains it (revenue). A common error is classifying the new engine as revenue expenditure because it is a "part" — but it enhances performance, which makes it capital.
