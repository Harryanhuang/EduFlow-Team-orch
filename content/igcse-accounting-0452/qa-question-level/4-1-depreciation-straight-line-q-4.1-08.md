### Question Q-4.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A company has a policy of charging depreciation on a straight-line basis. During the year ended 31 December 20X4, the following information relates to machinery: Cost at 1 January 20X4: $80,000. Accumulated depreciation at 1 January 20X4: $14,400. On 1 July 20X4, additional machinery was purchased for $20,000 (residual value $4,000, useful life 8 years). The original machinery (cost $80,000) was purchased on 1 January 20X2 with residual value $8,000 and useful life 10 years. Calculate the total depreciation charge for the year ended 31 December 20X4.
**Answer**: Two assets to consider: Original machinery: Annual depreciation = ($80,000 - $8,000) / 10 = $7,200 per year. This asset was owned for the full year 20X4, so charge = $7,200. Verification: accumulated depreciation at 1 Jan 20X4 = $7,200 × 2 years (20X2 and 20X3) = $14,400 — matches the given figure. New machinery: Annual depreciation = ($20,000 - $4,000) / 8 = $16,000 / 8 = $2,000 per year. Owned for 6 months (July to December), so charge = $2,000 × 6/12 = $1,000. Total depreciation charge for 20X4 = $7,200 + $1,000 = $8,200.
**Explanation**: A common error is treating all machinery as one pool and applying a single depreciation rate. Each asset (or batch of assets acquired at different times) must be depreciated separately based on its own cost, residual value, useful life, and ownership period. The accumulated depreciation at the start of the year ($14,400) is consistent with 2 years of $7,200 annual depreciation on the original machinery — this confirms the parameters are self-consistent.
