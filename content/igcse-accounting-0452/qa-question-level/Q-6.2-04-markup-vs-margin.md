# Question Q-6.2-04

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: What is the difference between markup and margin? Given a cost price and selling price, calculate both.

**Answer**: Markup and margin are both measures of profitability but use different bases: **Markup** = (Gross profit / Cost of sales) × 100% — expresses profit as a percentage of cost. **Margin** = (Gross profit / Revenue (selling price)) × 100% — expresses profit as a percentage of selling price. For example, if a product costs $60 and sells for $100: Gross profit = $40. Markup = ($40 / $60) × 100% = 66.7%. Margin = ($40 / $100) × 100% = 40%. The markup is always higher than the margin. They are mathematically related: Markup = Margin / (1 - Margin); Margin = Markup / (1 + Markup).

**Explanation**: The key is the denominator: markup uses cost, margin uses selling price. A common error is thinking a 50% markup equals a 50% margin — a 50% markup on $100 gives $150 selling price, but the margin is only 33.3% ($50 / $150).
