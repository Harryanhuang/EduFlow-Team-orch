# Question Q-5.5-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: How does the appropriation account change when a new partner is admitted or an existing partner retires during the financial year?

**Answer**: When a partner joins or leaves mid-year, the financial year is split into two periods — pre-change and post-change. For each period: 1) Net profit is apportioned on a time basis (unless actual pre/post-change figures are available). 2) Interest on capital and partners' salaries are calculated based on the partners active during each period and their capital balances at that time. 3) Residual profit for each period is shared using the profit-sharing ratio that applied during that period. The retiring partner receives appropriations only up to the retirement date; the incoming partner receives appropriations only from the admission date. Additional adjustments may include: goodwill valuation and allocation among partners' capital/current accounts, revaluation of assets and recording the surplus in partners' accounts in the old profit-sharing ratio, and preparation of the retiring partner's final settlement account.

**Explanation**: Time-apportionment is essential. A common error is applying the new profit-sharing ratio to the whole year. Always split at the date of change and apply the correct ratio to each period. Goodwill and revaluation are separate from the appropriation account but often tested together.
