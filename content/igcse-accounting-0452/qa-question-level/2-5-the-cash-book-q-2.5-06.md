### Question Q-2.5-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is a bank overdraft? How is it shown in the cash book?
**Answer**: A bank overdraft occurs when the business withdraws more money from its bank account than is available, creating a negative bank balance. It is a liability (the business owes the bank money). In the cash book, a bank overdraft appears as a credit balance in the bank column — the credit side total exceeds the debit side total. When balancing the bank column, the overdraft balance b/f is shown on the debit side (opposite to a normal positive balance).
**Explanation**: An overdraft is a liability, so its balance is credit. A common error is thinking a bank overdraft should show as a negative figure in the debit column — it is a positive credit balance in the bank column.
