# Question Q-6.4-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A bank assesses a loan from a small manufacturer: current ratio 1.4:1, acid test 0.8:1, gross margin 32%, net margin 9%, ROCE 15%, gearing 45%. The economy is entering a recession. What additional information should the bank request, and what concerns should be raised?

**Answer**: In a recession context: 1) **Liquidity concern** — current ratio 1.4:1 is below 1.5:1, acid test 0.8:1 means the business cannot cover current liabilities without selling inventory. In recession, inventory may be harder to sell. 2) **Revenue risk** — recession reduces demand. The bank should request sales order books, customer concentration data, and revenue forecasts. 3) **Gearing at 45%** — moderate but concerning in recession. Request existing loan terms, repayment schedules, and whether rates are variable. 4) **Cash flow projections** — request 12-month forecasts under recession scenarios (e.g. 20% revenue decline). 5) **Security** — what assets are available and their realizable value in a downturn. 6) **Customer base** — whether customers are in recession-resistant sectors. 7) **Cost structure** — fixed vs variable costs (fixed are harder to reduce). The bank should also request: management's recession survival plan, contingency funding, and sensitivity analysis.

**Explanation**: Ratios are a starting point; the recession context changes everything. A common error is assessing ratios without considering the economic environment.
