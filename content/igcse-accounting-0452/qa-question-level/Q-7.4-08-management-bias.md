# Question Q-7.4-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Management wants to present a favorable picture to attract investors. They consider: (a) delaying recognition of a probable lawsuit loss, (b) using an optimistic useful life estimate for assets, (c) presenting revenue growth prominently while downplaying increasing debt. Evaluate each against qualitative characteristics.

**Answer**: (a) **Delaying lawsuit loss**: Violates **faithful representation** — statements would be incomplete and biased. Violates **neutrality** — omission designed to achieve favorable result. May violate **relevance** if loss is material. (b) **Optimistic useful life**: Violates **faithful representation** — estimate is not neutral (biased towards lower depreciation, higher profit) and may not be free from error. Violates **verifiability** — independent observers would not agree. (c) **Revenue prominent, debt downplayed**: Violates **faithful representation (neutrality)** — presentation is biased. Reduces **understandability** — users may not get a balanced view. If debt is buried in notes, reduces **completeness**. All three represent fundamental failure of faithful representation and neutrality. The qualitative characteristics protect users from this type of manipulation.

**Explanation**: Neutrality is key to faithful representation. A common error is focusing on relevance while ignoring that biased presentation violates faithful representation regardless of whether information is technically included somewhere.
