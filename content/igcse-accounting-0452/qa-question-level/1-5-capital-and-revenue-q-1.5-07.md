### Question Q-1.5-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A business spends $8,000 on extending a building (capital expenditure) but records it as repairs (revenue expenditure). State the effect on: (a) net profit, (b) non-current assets, (c) capital.
**Answer**: (a) Net profit is understated by $8,000 because the $8,000 is treated as an expense instead of an asset. (b) Non-current assets are understated by $8,000 because the building extension is not recorded in the asset register. (c) Capital is understated because the lower profit means less profit is added to capital in the statement of financial position.
**Explanation**: All three figures are affected because the misclassification flows through the financial statements. A common error is only identifying one effect — the question asks for all three.
