### Question Q-1.4-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Rearrange the accounting equation to show how capital is calculated.
**Answer**: Capital = Assets - Liabilities (C = A - L). This shows that capital (owner's equity) is the residual interest in the assets of the business after deducting all liabilities.
**Explanation**: This form is useful when you know total assets and total liabilities but need to find the owner's equity. A common error is writing C = L - A — this would give a negative capital when assets exceed liabilities, which is illogical.
