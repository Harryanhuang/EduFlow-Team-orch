# Question Q-5.5-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Distinguish between a partner's capital account and current account. When is each used, and how do they appear in the statement of financial position?

**Answer**: The **capital account** records the partner's permanent investment — the original capital contributed plus any additional capital introduced. It normally remains unchanged unless partners agree to alter capital contributions. The **current account** records the partner's accumulated profits minus drawings, salaries, interest on capital, and interest on drawings. Each year, the partner's share of profit is credited to the current account, and drawings and interest on drawings are debited — the balance fluctuates annually. Two methods exist: 1) **Fixed capital method**: uses both accounts separately — capital stays fixed, all profit/drawing movements go to the current account. 2) **Fluctuating capital method**: uses only one combined account where capital AND profits/drawings are all recorded together. The method depends on the partnership agreement. In the statement of financial position, fixed capital shows both capital and current account balances for each partner separately.

**Explanation**: The distinction matters for exam presentation. A common error is recording profit share directly in the capital account when the fixed capital method is specified. Always check the question for "fixed capital" or "fluctuating capital" wording.
