### Question Q-2.6-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the imprest system of petty cash?
**Answer**: The imprest system is a method of managing petty cash where a fixed amount (the imprest amount) is set aside at the beginning of a period. As petty cash payments are made during the period, the cash balance reduces. At the end of the period, the total amount spent is reimbursed (topped up) from the main cash/bank account, restoring the imprest amount for the next period.
**Explanation**: The imprest system ensures control — the amount available at any time is fixed and predictable. A common error is thinking the imprest amount is spent and not replenished — the key feature is that it is restored each period.
