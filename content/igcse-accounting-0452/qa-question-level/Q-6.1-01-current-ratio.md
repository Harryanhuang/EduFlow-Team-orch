# Question Q-6.1-01

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is the current ratio? How is it calculated and what does it measure?

**Answer**: The current ratio is a liquidity ratio that measures a business's ability to pay its short-term debts (current liabilities) using its short-term assets (current assets). It is calculated as: Current ratio = Current assets / Current liabilities. A ratio of 1.5:1 to 2:1 is generally considered healthy — the business has $1.50 to $2.00 of current assets for every $1.00 of current liabilities. A ratio below 1:1 suggests the business may struggle to meet short-term obligations. A ratio that is too high may indicate idle resources (e.g. excess inventory or uncollected receivables). It is expressed as a number (e.g. 1.8) or ratio (e.g. 1.8:1).

**Explanation**: The current ratio includes ALL current assets, including inventory. A common error is reversing the formula. Always check whether inventory should be included — if the acid test ratio is requested, inventory is excluded.
