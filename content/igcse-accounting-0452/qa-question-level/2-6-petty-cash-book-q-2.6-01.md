### Question Q-2.6-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is petty cash? Give three examples of petty cash expenditures.
**Answer**: Petty cash is a small amount of cash kept by a business for minor, everyday expenses. Examples: postage stamps, office supplies (pens, paper), taxi fares, tea/coffee for the staff room, small repairs, delivery charges. Petty cash avoids the need to write cheques or make bank transfers for very small amounts.
**Explanation**: Petty cash is for small, frequent payments where writing a cheque would be impractical. A common error is including large purchases in petty cash — petty cash is for minor amounts only, and what qualifies as "minor" depends on the business's imprest amount.
