### Question Q-5.1-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: What is markup? A business buys goods for $80 and sells them for $120. Calculate the markup amount and markup percentage.
**Answer**: Markup is the percentage increase on the COST price to arrive at the selling price. It measures how much profit is added to the cost of goods.
Markup amount = Selling price - Cost price = $120 - $80 = $40.
Markup percentage = (Markup amount / Cost price) × 100% = ($40 / $80) × 100% = 50%.
The business marks up the cost by 50% to arrive at the selling price. If cost is $80 and markup is 50%, selling price = $80 + (50% × $80) = $80 + $40 = $120.
Markup is always based on cost as the denominator. A 50% markup means the selling price is 150% of cost.
**Explanation**: A common error is calculating markup as a percentage of selling price — that is margin, not markup. Markup = Gross profit / Cost of goods sold. Margin = Gross profit / Sales (revenue). The denominator is different, so the percentages are different even though the gross profit amount is the same.
