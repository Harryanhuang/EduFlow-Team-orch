# Question Q-7.4-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A lender assesses a loan using financial statements prepared under different accounting policies than their own: borrower uses FIFO, straight-line, revenue on delivery; lender uses AVCO, reducing balance, revenue on cash receipt. Discuss how comparability and understandability are affected and what the lender should do.

**Answer**: **Comparability** is affected — different policies make direct ratio comparison misleading. FIFO vs AVCO affects inventory values and cost of sales; straight-line vs reducing balance affects asset values and depreciation; revenue recognition timing affects reported revenue and profit. The lender cannot directly compare ratios without adjusting. **Understandability** is affected — the lender needs to understand the borrower's policies to interpret figures correctly. If policies are not clearly disclosed, the lender may misinterpret the position. **What the lender should do**: 1) Read notes to understand accounting policies. 2) Adjust borrower's figures to a common basis (recalculate under AVCO, reducing balance). 3) Focus on trends within borrower's own statements (comparable if policies consistent). 4) Consider whether borrower's policies are appropriate for the industry. 5) Request additional information if unclear or manipulation is suspected.

**Explanation**: Comparability requires consistent policies. A common error is comparing ratios directly without adjusting for policy differences.
