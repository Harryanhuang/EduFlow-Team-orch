# Question Q-5.5-02

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is an appropriation account? How does it differ from an income statement?

**Answer**: An appropriation account is a financial statement that shows how the net profit of a partnership is distributed (appropriated) among the partners. It starts with the net profit from the income statement, then deducts appropriations (interest on capital, partners' salaries) and adds back interest on drawings to arrive at the residual profit, which is divided in the profit-sharing ratio. It differs from the income statement in three key ways: 1) The income statement calculates profit from operations (revenue minus expenses); the appropriation account distributes that profit. 2) Appropriations (partners' salaries, interest on capital) are NOT expenses — they do not appear in the income statement. 3) The income statement shows business performance; the appropriation account shows ownership distribution.

**Explanation**: The critical distinction is that appropriations distribute profit; they do not generate it. A common error is treating partners' salaries as operating expenses in the income statement — they are distributions of profit and belong only in the appropriation account.
