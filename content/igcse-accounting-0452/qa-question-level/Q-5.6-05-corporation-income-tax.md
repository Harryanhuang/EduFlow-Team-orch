# Question Q-5.6-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: How is corporation income tax treated in a limited company's financial statements?

**Answer**: Corporation income tax is the tax a limited company pays on its taxable profit. Treatment: 1) Shown as a separate line in the income statement — deducted from profit before tax to arrive at profit after tax (profit for the year). 2) Calculated on taxable profit, which may differ from accounting profit due to tax adjustments (disallowable expenses, capital allowances). 3) Unpaid corporation tax appears as a current liability in the statement of financial position. 4) Corporation tax is NOT shown in the appropriation account — dividends are paid from profit after tax. 5) In the appropriation account, the starting figure is profit after tax, not profit before tax. 6) Proposed dividends (declared but not yet paid) are disclosed in the notes, not recorded as a liability.

**Explanation**: Corporation tax is a significant expense. A common error is confusing corporation tax (company-level) with personal income tax (individual-level). In exam questions, the tax rate or amount is usually provided. Always deduct tax before showing appropriations.
