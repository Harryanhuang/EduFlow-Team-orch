# Question Q-6.2-02

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is the net profit margin? How does it differ from the gross profit margin?

**Answer**: The net profit margin measures the percentage of revenue that remains as net profit after deducting ALL expenses (cost of sales plus operating expenses). It is calculated as: Net profit margin = (Net profit / Revenue) × 100%. It differs from the gross profit margin in that: 1) Gross profit margin only considers cost of sales; net profit margin considers all expenses. 2) The gap between the two margins represents the proportion of revenue consumed by operating expenses. 3) A business can have a healthy gross profit margin but a low net profit margin if operating expenses are too high. The net profit margin reflects overall efficiency in converting revenue into profit.

**Explanation**: Net profit margin is always lower than gross profit margin. A common error is confusing net profit with gross profit — net profit is after ALL expenses.
