# Question Q-5.6-01

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is a limited company? How does it differ from a sole trader in terms of legal status, liability, and ownership?

**Answer**: A limited company is a business entity that is legally separate from its owners (shareholders). Differences from a sole trader: 1) **Legal status**: A limited company is a separate legal person — it can own property, sue, and be sued in its own name. A sole trader and the business are the same legal entity. 2) **Liability**: Shareholders have limited liability — they can only lose what they invested. A sole trader has unlimited liability — personal assets are at risk. 3) **Ownership**: A limited company is owned by shareholders who hold shares; ownership can be transferred by selling shares. A sole trader owns the business entirely and personally. 4) **Management**: A limited company is managed by directors appointed by shareholders. A sole trader makes all decisions. 5) **Financing**: A limited company can issue shares to raise capital. A sole trader cannot.

**Explanation**: The key concept is separate legal personality. A common error is thinking limited liability means the company cannot fail — it can, but shareholders' personal assets are protected. The company, not the shareholders, owns the business assets.
