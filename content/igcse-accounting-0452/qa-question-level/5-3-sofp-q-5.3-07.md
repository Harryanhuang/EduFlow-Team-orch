### Question Q-5.3-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: Distinguish between a bank loan (long-term) and a bank overdraft in the statement of financial position. A business has: a bank loan of $40,000 repayable in 5 years; a bank overdraft of $5,000. Show how each appears in the SOFP.
**Answer**: A bank loan is a non-current liability (long-term liability) because it is repayable after more than one year. It appears in the "non-current liabilities" section.
A bank overdraft is a current liability because it is repayable on demand (within one year). It appears in the "current liabilities" section.

SOFP extract:
  Non-current liabilities:
    Bank loan (repayable in 5 years): $40,000
  Current liabilities:
    Bank overdraft: $5,000

Key difference: The bank loan represents borrowed funds with a fixed repayment schedule (long-term financing). The bank overdraft represents a temporary negative bank balance used for short-term cash management. In exams, a loan described as "repayable in X years" (where X > 1) is always non-current. The portion of a long-term loan due within 12 months may be shown as current — but IGCSE typically treats the full amount as non-current unless stated otherwise.
**Explanation**: A common error is putting both the loan and overdraft under current liabilities — only the overdraft is current. The loan is classified based on when it is repayable, not when it was taken. "Repayable in 5 years" means non-current.
