# Question Q-6.3-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A business's efficiency ratios: Inventory turnover: Y1 8 times, Y2 5 times. Receivables days: Y1 35, Y2 50. Payables days: Y1 40, Y2 60. Asset turnover: Y1 2.5, Y2 1.8. Analyze the trend and identify problems.

**Answer**: All ratios show significant deterioration: 1) **Inventory turnover** fell from 8 to 5 times (days increased from 45.6 to 73) — holding inventory much longer, suggesting over-ordering, declining demand, or obsolete stock. 2) **Receivables days** increased from 35 to 50 — credit control weakened; customers take 15 extra days to pay, straining cash flow. 3) **Payables days** increased from 40 to 60 — taking 20 extra days to pay suppliers, suggesting cash flow problems and potentially damaging relationships. 4) **Asset turnover** fell from 2.5 to 1.8 — generating less revenue per dollar of assets, indicating under-utilized assets or declining sales. Overall: the business is becoming less efficient in all working capital areas. The payables period (60) now exceeds receivables (50), meaning suppliers finance the business — unsustainable. This combination is a classic sign of cash flow distress and potential overtrading.

**Explanation**: Each ratio individually shows deterioration; together they paint a picture of systemic working capital failure. A common error is analyzing ratios in isolation without linking to the overall cash flow picture.
