# Question Q-7.2-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Expenditures: raw materials $120,000; wages $85,000; rent $30,000; computer system $25,000; installation $5,000; roof repair $12,000; production line upgrade (+20% output) $45,000; advertising $15,000; legal fees for patent defense $8,000; software license renewal $3,000. Calculate capital and revenue totals. If the upgrade was incorrectly classified as revenue, what is the effect?

**Answer**:

**Classification:**
- Raw materials $120,000 — Revenue
- Wages $85,000 — Revenue
- Rent $30,000 — Revenue
- Computer system $25,000 — Capital
- Installation $5,000 — Capital
- Roof repair $12,000 — Revenue
- Production line upgrade $45,000 — Capital (enhancement)
- Advertising $15,000 — Revenue
- Legal fees (patent defense) $8,000 — Revenue (defending rights, not acquiring/enhancing)
- Software license $3,000 — Revenue (annual subscription)

**Totals:**
- Capital = $25,000 + $5,000 + $45,000 = $75,000
- Revenue = $120,000 + $85,000 + $30,000 + $12,000 + $15,000 + $8,000 + $3,000 = $273,000

**If upgrade ($45,000) misclassified as revenue:**
- Revenue expenditure overstated by $45,000
- Capital expenditure understated by $45,000
- Profit understated by $45,000
- Non-current assets understated by $45,000
- Future profit overstated (no depreciation on $45,000)

**Explanation**: Always test each item against the three capital criteria. A common error is treating all legal fees as capital — defending a lawsuit is revenue because it maintains existing rights.
