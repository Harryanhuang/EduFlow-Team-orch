### Question Q-4.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is depreciation? Why do businesses charge depreciation on non-current assets?
**Answer**: Depreciation is the systematic allocation of the cost of a non-current asset over its useful life. It represents the consumption of the asset's economic benefits over time. Businesses charge depreciation for two main reasons: (1) Matching concept — to match the cost of the asset against the revenue it helps generate in each accounting period, giving a true and fair view of profit. (2) Prudence — to reflect that the asset's value is being used up, so the statement of financial position does not overstate the asset's worth. Depreciation is NOT a method of valuing the asset at its market price.
**Explanation**: A common error is thinking depreciation records a fall in market value — it does not. Depreciation is about cost allocation, not valuation. Even if the asset's market value rises, depreciation is still charged on the original cost.
