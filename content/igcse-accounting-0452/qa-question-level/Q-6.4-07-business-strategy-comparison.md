# Question Q-6.4-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Two businesses in the same industry: A — gross margin 45%, net margin 8%, current ratio 0.9:1, ROCE 25%. B — gross margin 35%, net margin 15%, current ratio 2.2:1, ROCE 12%. Interpret the differences and suggest what each does differently.

**Answer**: Both have different strategies. **Business A** has higher gross margin (45%) but lower net margin (8%), meaning operating expenses consume 37% of revenue. A likely invests heavily in marketing, staffing, or overheads — pursuing a premium/differentiation strategy. A's low current ratio (0.9:1) suggests tight liquidity — running lean with minimal working capital. Despite this, ROCE of 25% is outstanding. **Business B** has lower gross margin but much better cost control (net margin 15%, expenses at 20% of revenue), healthy current ratio (2.2:1), and ROCE of 12%. B likely pursues cost leadership — moderate margins, tight expense management, strong liquidity. A's strategy: high gross margin, high operating costs, lean liquidity, high returns. B's strategy: moderate gross margin, tight cost control, strong liquidity, moderate returns. An investor seeking returns might prefer A; a lender might prefer B.

**Explanation**: Always look at the gap between gross and net margins to understand operating expense levels. A common error is concluding A is 'better' due to higher ROCE without noting the liquidity risk.
