### Question Q-3.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the main purpose of preparing a trial balance?
**Answer**: The main purpose is to check that the total of all debit balances equals the total of all credit balances, confirming that the double entry bookkeeping has been arithmetically correct. It also serves as a convenient summary of all ledger balances from which the financial statements can be prepared.
**Explanation**: A trial balance checks mathematical accuracy, not completeness or correctness of classification. A common error is saying "a trial balance proves the accounts are correct" — it only proves that debits equal credits; many types of errors can still exist.
