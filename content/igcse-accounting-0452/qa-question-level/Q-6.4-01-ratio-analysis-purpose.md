# Question Q-6.4-01

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is ratio analysis? What are its main purposes in business?

**Answer**: Ratio analysis is the process of calculating and interpreting relationships between financial statement figures to assess performance, financial position, and trends. Main purposes: 1) **Performance evaluation** — assessing profitability, liquidity, and efficiency over time. 2) **Comparison** — comparing against competitors or industry benchmarks. 3) **Planning and budgeting** — using ratios to set targets and forecast. 4) **Decision-making** — helping investors, lenders, and managers make informed decisions. 5) **Control** — monitoring whether the business is meeting targets and identifying problems early. Ratio analysis converts raw financial data into meaningful indicators that are easier to understand and compare than absolute figures.

**Explanation**: Ratios make numbers comparable. A common error is thinking ratios tell you everything — they are a starting point for investigation, not a conclusion. Ratios show what happened, not why.
