### Question Q-4.3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A business bought a vehicle for $36,000 on 1 January 20X2. Depreciation is charged at 25% per annum using the straight-line method, residual value $6,000, useful life 4 years. On 31 December 20X3 (end of year 2) the vehicle is sold for $19,000. Calculate the profit or loss on disposal and show the disposal account.
**Answer**: Annual depreciation = ($36,000 - $6,000) / 4 = $7,500 per year. The asset was owned for exactly 2 years (1 Jan 20X2 to 31 Dec 20X3). Accumulated depreciation at disposal = $7,500 × 2 = $15,000. Carrying amount at disposal = $36,000 - $15,000 = $21,000. Sale proceeds = $19,000. Loss on disposal = $21,000 - $19,000 = $2,000 loss.
Disposal account:
Debit side: Vehicle (cost) $36,000 | Profit on disposal —
Credit side: Accumulated depreciation $15,000 | Cash (proceeds) $19,000 | Loss on disposal (SPL) $2,000
Total debits: $36,000. Total credits: $15,000 + $19,000 + $2,000 = $36,000. Account balances.
**Explanation**: A common error is forgetting that the accumulated depreciation must be removed from the books separately from the cost. The disposal account shows both the cost going out (debit) and the accumulated depreciation being cleared (credit). The loss balances the account — it is not an extra entry but the difference that makes debits equal credits.
