### Question Q-5.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A business has a gross profit margin of 40%. If the cost of goods sold is $72,000, calculate: (a) sales, (b) gross profit.
**Answer**: Margin of 40% means gross profit = 40% of sales. Also, COGS = Sales - Gross profit = Sales - 40% of Sales = 60% of Sales.
(a) COGS = 60% of Sales. $72,000 = 0.60 × Sales. Sales = $72,000 / 0.60 = $120,000.
(b) Gross profit = Sales - COGS = $120,000 - $72,000 = $48,000. (Alternatively: 40% × $120,000 = $48,000.)
Verification: Margin = $48,000 / $120,000 = 40%. Markup = $48,000 / $72,000 = 66.67%.
**Explanation**: A common error is calculating sales as $72,000 × 1.40 = $100,800 — this confuses margin with markup. A 40% margin means COGS is 60% of sales (not that sales is 140% of cost). When working with margin, always express COGS as a percentage of sales: COGS% = 100% - margin%. Then Sales = COGS / COGS%.
