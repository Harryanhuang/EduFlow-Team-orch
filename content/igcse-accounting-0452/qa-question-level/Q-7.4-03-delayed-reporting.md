# Question Q-7.4-03

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A business prepares accurate and complete financial statements but delays publication by 6 months. Which qualitative characteristics are affected and why?

**Answer**: **Timeliness** is primarily affected — information available 6 months late may no longer influence decisions. Economic conditions and financial circumstances may have changed significantly. **Relevance** is also affected — information relevant at year-end may become less relevant by publication date. For example, receivables collectible at year-end may become uncollectible. **Comparability** may be reduced if competitors publish within 3 months — users cannot make timely comparisons. **Faithful representation** is NOT affected — the information is still accurate and complete. However, faithful representation alone cannot compensate for loss of timeliness and relevance.

**Explanation**: Timeliness and relevance are linked — delayed information loses relevance. A common error is thinking accurate information is always useful regardless of timing.
