### Question Q-1.2-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Why does a bank need accounting information before lending money to a business?
**Answer**: A bank needs accounting information to assess: 1) The business's ability to repay the loan (profitability and cash flow). 2) The value of assets available as security (collateral). 3) The existing level of debt (gearing) to determine if the business is already over-borrowed. 4) Liquidity ratios to check short-term payment ability. Without this information the bank cannot evaluate credit risk.
**Explanation**: Banks are primarily concerned with repayment ability, not profit maximisation. A common error is saying "banks want to know if the business is profitable" — profitability is relevant only insofar as it supports loan repayment.
