### Question Q-4.3-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: How does the treatment of disposal of non-current assets appear in the financial statements? A company has the following for the year ended 31 December 20X4: Opening balance of non-current assets at cost $100,000, opening accumulated depreciation $40,000. During the year: purchased new equipment $20,000; disposed of equipment (cost $15,000, accumulated depreciation $9,000) for $7,500; depreciation charge for the year $12,000. Show the cost, accumulated depreciation, and carrying amount sections of the statement of financial position and the disposal impact on the statement of profit or loss.
**Answer**:
Non-current assets — Cost: Opening balance $100,000 + Additions $20,000 - Disposals $15,000 = Closing balance $105,000.
Non-current assets — Accumulated depreciation: Opening balance $40,000 + Charge for the year $12,000 - Eliminated on disposal $9,000 = Closing balance $43,000.
Carrying amount at 31 Dec 20X4: $105,000 - $43,000 = $62,000.
Verification: Opening carrying amount = $100,000 - $40,000 = $60,000. Movement: Additions $20,000 - Disposals (carrying amount) $6,000 - Depreciation charge $12,000 = Net change $2,000. Closing = $60,000 + $2,000 = $62,000. Matches.

Statement of profit or loss impact: Depreciation expense $12,000 + Profit on disposal ($7,500 - $6,000 = $1,500 profit, so net charge is reduced by $1,500). The profit on disposal of $1,500 appears as other income (or netted against depreciation expense).

**Explanation**: A common error in the statement of financial position is adding the disposed asset's cost to the closing balance or forgetting to eliminate its accumulated depreciation. The accumulated depreciation on the disposed asset ($9,000) is SUBTRACTED from the accumulated depreciation column — it is being removed from the books, not added. In the reconciliation: Closing carrying amount = Opening + Additions - Disposals (at carrying amount) - Depreciation charge.
