# Question Q-7.3-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Compare the effects of changing from FIFO to AVCO during rising prices versus falling prices. How does the price trend affect the direction and magnitude of the impact?

**Answer**: During **rising prices**: FIFO assigns oldest (cheapest) costs to cost of sales, newest (most expensive) to closing inventory. So FIFO gives lower cost of sales → higher profit, and higher closing inventory → higher assets. Switching FIFO to AVCO: cost of sales increases, profit decreases, inventory decreases. During **falling prices**: FIFO assigns oldest (most expensive) costs to cost of sales, newest (cheapest) to closing inventory. So FIFO gives higher cost of sales → lower profit, and lower closing inventory → lower assets. Switching FIFO to AVCO: cost of sales decreases, profit increases, inventory increases. **Magnitude** depends on: 1) Rate of price change — faster changes create larger differences. 2) Inventory turnover — faster turnover reduces the difference. 3) Size of inventory relative to total assets. The price trend determines whether the change increases or decreases profit and assets.

**Explanation:** The same policy change has opposite effects depending on the price trend. A common error is assuming FIFO always gives higher profit — it only does so during rising prices.
