# Question Q-6.1-02

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is the acid test (quick) ratio? How does it differ from the current ratio?

**Answer**: The acid test ratio (also called the quick ratio) is a stricter measure of liquidity that excludes inventory from current assets, because inventory may not be quickly converted to cash. It is calculated as: Acid test ratio = (Current assets - Inventory) / Current liabilities. Alternatively: (Cash + Receivables + Short-term investments) / Current liabilities. It differs from the current ratio by excluding inventory, providing a more conservative view of immediate liquidity. A ratio of 1:1 is generally acceptable — the business can meet short-term obligations without selling inventory. For businesses with fast inventory turnover (e.g. supermarkets), a lower ratio may be acceptable.

**Explanation**: The acid test is more stringent. A common error is including prepaid expenses in the numerator — prepayments cannot be converted to cash and should be excluded. Only the most liquid assets count.
