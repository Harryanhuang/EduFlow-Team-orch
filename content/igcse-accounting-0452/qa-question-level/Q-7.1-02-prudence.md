# Question Q-7.1-02

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is the prudence concept? How does it apply to inventory valuation and doubtful debts?

**Answer**: The prudence concept requires that assets and profits should not be overstated, and liabilities and losses should not be understated. When uncertain, anticipate losses but not gains. Applications: 1) **Inventory valuation** — valued at the lower of cost and net realizable value (NRV). If NRV falls below cost, the loss is recognized immediately. If NRV exceeds cost, the gain is not recognized until sale. 2) **Doubtful debts** — an allowance is created for receivables that may not be collected, anticipating a possible loss. 3) **Contingent liabilities** — if loss is probable, it is provided for. 4) **Contingent assets** — not recognized until virtually certain. Prudence prevents an overly optimistic financial picture.

**Explanation**: A common error is confusing prudence with pessimism — prudence does not mean deliberately understating; it means being cautious when there is genuine uncertainty.
