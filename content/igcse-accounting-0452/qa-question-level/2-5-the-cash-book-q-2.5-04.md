### Question Q-2.5-04
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is discount received? On which side of the cash book is it recorded?
**Answer**: Discount received is a reduction in the amount the business pays to a supplier, given by the supplier for prompt payment. It is recorded on the credit side of the cash book in the discount column because it represents a saving (income) to the business — the business pays less than the full amount owed.
**Explanation**: Discount received is a gain and appears on the credit side. A common error is confusing it with discount allowed — discount allowed (we give) is debit; discount received (we get) is credit.
