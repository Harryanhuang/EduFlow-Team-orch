# Question Q-6.4-04

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: Why is comparing ratios across different industries misleading? Give examples.

**Answer**: Comparing ratios across industries is misleading because business models, operating cycles, and capital structures differ fundamentally. Examples: 1) **Gross profit margin**: A luxury retailer may have 60%, a supermarket 5-10%. The supermarket compensates with much higher inventory turnover. 2) **Current ratio**: A supermarket may have below 1:1 (fast inventory turnover, delayed supplier payments — negative working capital cycle). A manufacturer may need 2:1 (inventory takes months to produce and sell). 3) **Asset turnover**: A service business may have 10+ times (few physical assets). A capital-intensive business (airline) may have 0.5-1 times. 4) **Gearing**: A utility at 70% may be safe (stable cash flows); a tech startup at 30% may be risky (uncertain revenue). Meaningful comparisons require same industry, similar size, and similar market conditions.

**Explanation**: Always compare like with like. A common error is ranking businesses across industries by a single ratio without understanding the business model context.
