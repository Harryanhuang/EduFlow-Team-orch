# Question Q-7.1-07

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A business has used FIFO for 10 years. Management wants to switch to AVCO for more accurate inventory cost measurement. The change would reduce reported profit by $15,000. Discuss whether this is acceptable and what disclosures are required.

**Answer**: The change is potentially acceptable but must meet criteria and disclosures. **Acceptability**: Consistency requires policies remain the same, but change is permitted if it provides more relevant and reliable information. AVCO may better reflect cost flow for interchangeable items. The change should not be motivated by profit manipulation. **Required disclosures**: 1) Nature of the change (FIFO to AVCO) in the notes. 2) Reason for change (more accurate cost reflection). 3) Financial effect — profit under FIFO would have been $15,000 higher. 4) If practicable, restate prior year comparatives under AVCO. **Evaluation**: Acceptable if the primary motive is improved accuracy. However, switching after 10 years requires strong justification as users are accustomed to FIFO-based trends. The $15,000 impact should be assessed relative to total profit — if significant, users need clear disclosure.

**Explanation**: Consistency does not prohibit change — it requires transparency. A common error is stating the change is "not allowed" because of consistency. The key is justification and disclosure.
