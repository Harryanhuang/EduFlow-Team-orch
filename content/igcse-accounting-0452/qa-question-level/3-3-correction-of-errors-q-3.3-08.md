### Question Q-3.3-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: The trial balance did not agree and a suspense account was opened with a debit balance of $200. All errors have now been identified and corrected except one remaining error. After posting all known corrections, the suspense account still shows a debit balance of $75. What does this tell you? Write the journal entry that will clear the remaining suspense balance if the final error is identified as: a sales day book overcast (total overstated) by $75.
**Answer**: The remaining $75 debit balance in the suspense account after all known corrections means the unidentified error causes debits to exceed credits by $75. A sales day book overcast by $75 means the sales total was $75 too high — the Sales account (credit) was credited $75 too much. This means credits exceeded debits by $75, which would normally create a $75 debit balance in the suspense account — matching exactly what remains. Correction: Debit Sales $75, Credit Suspense account $75, Narration: "Correction — sales day book overcast by $75." After this entry, the suspense account balance becomes zero, confirming all errors have been identified and corrected.
**Explanation**: The suspense account balance after corrections equals the remaining unidentified error. A sales day book overcast means Sales (a credit account) is too high — to correct, we debit Sales to reduce it and credit Suspense to clear the balance. A common error is debiting Suspense instead of crediting it — the suspense account has a debit balance, so we need to credit it to reduce it to zero.
