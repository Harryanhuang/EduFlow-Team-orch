# Question Q-6.3-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Evaluate whether a fresh produce wholesaler and a luxury car dealer would have different expected efficiency ratios. Explain why.

**Answer**: Yes, ratios differ dramatically by industry. **Fresh produce wholesaler**: 1) Very high inventory turnover (15-20+ times, 18-24 days) — produce is perishable and must sell quickly. 2) Short receivables days (10-20 days) — many cash sales or very short credit terms. 3) Short payables days (15-30 days) — suppliers expect quick payment for perishable goods. 4) High asset turnover (5-8 times) — low capital investment relative to revenue. 5) Very short or negative working capital cycle. **Luxury car dealer**: 1) Low inventory turnover (3-5 times, 73-122 days) — high-value, low-volume items. 2) Longer receivables days (30-60 days) — credit sales and financing arrangements. 3) Moderate payables days (30-45 days). 4) Lower asset turnover (1-2 times) — significant capital in showroom and inventory. 5) Long working capital cycle (60-120 days). Efficiency ratio benchmarks are entirely industry-specific and reflect product nature, sales volume, and supply chain structure.

**Explanation**: A common error is applying the same benchmark to both businesses. Always consider product nature (perishable vs durable), sales volume, and supply chain.
