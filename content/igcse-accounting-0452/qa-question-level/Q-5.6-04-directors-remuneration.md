# Question Q-5.6-04

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: What is directors' remuneration? How is it treated in a company's financial statements, and how does this differ from partners' salaries?

**Answer**: Directors' remuneration is the total pay and benefits received by directors for their services, including salaries, bonuses, benefits in kind, and pension contributions. In the financial statements: 1) Directors' remuneration is an **expense** in the income statement (usually within administrative expenses). 2) It is deducted when calculating profit before tax. 3) It is NOT an appropriation of profit — directors are employees of the company. 4) The total must be disclosed in the notes to the financial statements (a legal requirement). This differs from partners' salaries in a partnership, which are treated as appropriations of profit (shown in the appropriation account) because partners are not employees — they are owners distributing profit to themselves.

**Explanation**: Directors are employees; partners are not. A common error is treating directors' remuneration as an appropriation — this is only correct for partnerships. Always place directors' pay in the income statement for a company.
