### Question Q-2.3-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why are cash sales not recorded in the sales day book? Where are they recorded instead?
**Answer**: Cash sales are not recorded in the sales day book because the sales day book is designed for credit sales — transactions where a customer owes money and will pay later. Cash sales involve immediate payment, so they are recorded directly in the cash book. Recording them in the sales day book would create a fictitious receivable.
**Explanation**: The sales day book total is posted to trade receivables control — recording cash sales there would overstate what customers owe. A common error is thinking all sales go through the sales day book.
