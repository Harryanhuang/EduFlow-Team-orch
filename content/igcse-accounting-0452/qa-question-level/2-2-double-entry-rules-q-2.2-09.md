### Question Q-2.2-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: "A business receives $600 cash from a credit customer. The accountant debits Sales and credits Cash." Is this correct? If not, explain the error and write the correct entry.
**Answer**: Incorrect. When a credit customer pays, the business receives cash (asset increases → debit Cash) and the customer's debt is reduced (asset decreases → credit Trade receivables / the customer's account). The correct entry is: Debit Cash $600, Credit Trade receivables $600. The error: debiting Sales would record a second sale that never occurred, and crediting Cash would record money going out when it actually came in. The accountant has reversed both sides and used the wrong accounts.
**Explanation**: Receiving cash from a debtor affects cash and receivables, not sales. Sales were already recorded when the original credit sale was made. A common error is re-recording sales when payment is received — the sale was recorded at the time of the original credit transaction.
