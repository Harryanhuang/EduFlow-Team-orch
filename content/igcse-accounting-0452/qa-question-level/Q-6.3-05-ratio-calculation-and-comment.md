# Question Q-6.3-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A business has: cost of sales $300,000, inventory $45,000, revenue $500,000, trade receivables $60,000, trade payables $50,000, purchases $320,000. Calculate all efficiency ratios. Comment on the working capital cycle.

**Answer**:
- Inventory turnover = $300,000 / $45,000 = 6.67 times
- Inventory days = ($45,000 / $300,000) × 365 = 54.8 days
- Receivables collection = ($60,000 / $500,000) × 365 = 43.8 days
- Payables payment = ($50,000 / $320,000) × 365 = 57.0 days
- Working capital cycle = 54.8 + 43.8 - 57.0 = 41.6 days

Comment: The business holds inventory ~55 days, collects from customers in ~44 days, and pays suppliers in ~57 days. The working capital cycle of 41.6 days means the business must finance operations for ~42 days. The payables period (57 days) exceeds receivables (44 days), which is positive — supplier credit partially funds operations. Inventory holding of 55 days may be high depending on industry.

**Explanation**: Always use matching figures: cost of sales for inventory, revenue for receivables, purchases for payables. A common error is mixing revenue with cost-based denominators.
