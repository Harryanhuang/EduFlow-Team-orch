### Question Q-5.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A business provides the following for the year ended 31 December 20X4: Sales $320,000; Returns inwards $12,000; Opening inventory $28,000; Purchases $180,000; Returns outwards $7,000; Carriage inwards $9,000; Carriage outwards $11,000; Wages $45,000 (of which $3,000 relates to next year); Rent $18,000 (accrued $2,000 at year end). The business's gross profit margin is 37.5%. Calculate: (a) net sales, (b) COGS, (c) closing inventory, (d) gross profit, (e) net profit.
**Answer**: (a) Net sales = Sales - Returns inwards = $320,000 - $12,000 = $308,000.
(b) Margin 37.5% = 3/8. Gross profit = 37.5% × $308,000 = $115,500. COGS = $308,000 - $115,500 = $192,500.
(c) COGS = Opening inventory + Net purchases + Carriage inwards - Closing inventory.
Net purchases = $180,000 - $7,000 = $173,000.
$192,500 = $28,000 + $173,000 + $9,000 - Closing inventory.
$192,500 = $210,000 - Closing inventory.
Closing inventory = $210,000 - $192,500 = $17,500.
(d) Gross profit = $115,500 (from b).
(e) Operating expenses: Carriage outwards $11,000 + Wages ($45,000 - $3,000 prepaid) $42,000 + Rent ($18,000 + $2,000 accrued) $20,000 = $73,000.
Net profit = Gross profit - Operating expenses = $115,500 - $73,000 = $42,500.
**Explanation**: A common error in comprehensive questions is not making all adjustments before calculating expenses — wages need the prepayment deducted and rent needs the accrual added. Also, carriage outwards is an operating expense (not in COGS). The gross profit margin applies to NET sales (after returns inwards), not gross sales. Always work in order: net sales → gross profit (from margin) → COGS → closing inventory (reverse) → operating expenses → net profit.
