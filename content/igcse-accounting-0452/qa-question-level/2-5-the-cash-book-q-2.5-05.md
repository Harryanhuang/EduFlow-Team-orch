### Question Q-2.5-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Ahmed owes $500. The business offers a 5% discount for payment within 14 days. Ahmed pays $475 within the period. Show how this is recorded in the three-column cash book.
**Answer**: Debit side: Cash column $475, Discount allowed column $25. The total of $500 ($475 + $25) settles Ahmed's account in full. The $475 is the actual cash received; the $25 is the discount allowed (5% of $500). The credit entry for the full $500 is posted to Ahmed's personal account in the receivables ledger.
**Explanation**: The discount column total is posted to the discount allowed account in the general ledger (debit). A common error is recording only $475 without the discount entry — the full $500 must be accounted for to clear the customer's account.
