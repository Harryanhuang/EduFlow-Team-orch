### Question Q-5.2-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A business has a trial balance showing rent paid $8,400 and insurance paid $3,600. At year end, rent is accrued $1,200 and insurance is prepaid $600. Show the adjusted expenses in the income statement and the statement of financial position entries.
**Answer**: Rent adjustment: Trial balance amount $8,400 + Accrual $1,200 = Adjusted rent expense $9,600 in income statement. Accrual $1,200 shown as current liability in statement of financial position.
Insurance adjustment: Trial balance amount $3,600 - Prepayment $600 = Adjusted insurance expense $3,000 in income statement. Prepayment $600 shown as current asset in statement of financial position.
Total impact on net profit: Rent accrual ADDS $1,200 to expense; insurance prepayment REDUCES expense by $600. Net additional expense = $1,200 - $600 = $600 reduction in net profit.
**Explanation**: A common error is not adjusting the trial balance figure — the trial balance shows CASH PAID, not the expense for the year. The income statement expense = trial balance amount + closing accrual - closing prepayment. Always adjust each item individually.
