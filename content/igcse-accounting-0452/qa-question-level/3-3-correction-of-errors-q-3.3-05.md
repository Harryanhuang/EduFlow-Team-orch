### Question Q-3.3-05
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A suspense account has a credit balance of $420. The following errors are discovered: (1) A payment of $180 to a trade payable was correctly credited to Cash but debited to the correct Trade payables account — however, the amount was entered as $810 in the Trade payables account. (2) An irrecoverable debt of $240 was written off correctly in the Trade receivables account but not in the Irrecoverable debts expense account. Prepare correcting journal entries and show the suspense account after correction.
**Answer**: Error (1): The debit to Trade payables was $810 but should have been $180 — overstated by $630. Correction: Debit Suspense account $630, Credit Trade payables $630, Narration: "Correction — Trade payables debited $810 instead of $180, excess reversed." The suspense account was credited $420 (meaning credits exceeded debits by $420). The credit balance in suspense arose because somewhere a credit was recorded without a matching debit. Error (1) contributed $630 of excess debit, which means it does not explain the $420 credit difference — it actually made the debit side bigger. Error (2): The credit to Trade receivables ($240) was made but the debit to Irrecoverable debts was missing — credits exceeded debits by $240, contributing to the suspense account balance. Correction: Debit Irrecoverable debts expense $240, Credit Suspense account $240. After both corrections: Suspense account started with credit $420. Correction (1) debited suspense $630. Correction (2) credited suspense $240. Net: $420 credit - $630 debit + $240 credit = $30 credit remaining. The remaining $30 credit balance means there is still $30 of unidentified error (credits exceed debits by $30).
**Explanation**: The key is tracking how each error contributed to the original suspense balance. A common error is not considering whether an error increased or decreased the imbalance before posting to suspense. The suspense account is cleared by posting the correcting entry on the opposite side.
