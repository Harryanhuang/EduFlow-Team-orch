### Question Q-2.1-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Why are source documents important in accounting? Give three reasons.
**Answer**: 1) They provide evidence that a transaction occurred, supporting the entries in the books of account. 2) They contain details (date, amount, parties, description) needed for accurate recording. 3) They serve as an audit trail — if a discrepancy is found, source documents can be checked to verify or correct the records. They also help prevent fraud by requiring documentary support for every entry.
**Explanation**: Source documents are the foundation of the accounting trail. A common error is saying they are "for filing" only — they are active tools for verification and accuracy.
