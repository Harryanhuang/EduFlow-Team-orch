# Question Q-7.3-02

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: Why might a business change its accounting policy? Give two legitimate reasons and one illegitimate reason.

**Answer**: Legitimate reasons: 1) **Improved relevance and reliability** — the new policy provides more useful information. E.g. switching FIFO to AVCO because items are interchangeable and AVCO better reflects average cost flow. 2) **Change in accounting standard** — a new or revised standard requires or permits the change. 3) **Change in business circumstances** — the old policy is no longer appropriate, e.g. the pattern of economic benefits from an asset has changed. Illegitimate reason: **Profit manipulation** — changing a policy solely to improve reported profit, such as switching depreciation methods to reduce the charge and boost profit in a poor year. This is misleading because it does not reflect genuine economic change.

**Explanation**: The test is whether the change provides better information, not whether it improves profit. Disclosure alone does not make a change legitimate.
