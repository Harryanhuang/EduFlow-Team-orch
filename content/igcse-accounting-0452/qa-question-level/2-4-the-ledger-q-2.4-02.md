### Question Q-2.4-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Describe the structure of a T-account in the ledger.
**Answer**: A T-account has the account name written at the top. The left side is the debit (Dr) side and the right side is the credit (Cr) side. Each entry on either side shows the date, the name of the corresponding account (the "other side" of the double entry), and the amount. T-accounts are used to calculate the balance on each account.
**Explanation**: The T-account is a visual representation of a ledger account. A common error is putting the wrong account name on the left side — the entry on the debit side shows the name of the account that was credited, and vice versa, because it represents the other half of the double entry.
