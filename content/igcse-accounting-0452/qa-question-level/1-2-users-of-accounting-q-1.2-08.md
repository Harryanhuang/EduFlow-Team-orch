### Question Q-1.2-08
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Why might a potential investor (someone who does not yet own shares) want accounting information about a business?
**Answer**: A potential investor wants accounting information to assess whether the business offers a good return on investment compared to alternatives. They look at profitability trends, dividend history, growth prospects, and financial stability to decide whether to invest. Without this information they cannot evaluate the risk and return of the investment.
**Explanation**: Potential investors are external users evaluating whether to commit capital. A common error is confusing potential investors with existing owners — both use accounting information but for different decisions (invest vs. continue holding).
