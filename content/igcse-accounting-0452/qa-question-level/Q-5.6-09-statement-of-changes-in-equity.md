# Question Q-5.6-09

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: A company has: ordinary share capital $400,000 ($1 shares), 6% preference share capital $100,000, share premium $80,000, general reserve $50,000, retained earnings at start $120,000. Profit after tax is $95,000. Directors propose a final ordinary dividend of 5 cents per share and pay the preference dividend. Prepare the statement of changes in equity.

**Answer**:

**Statement of Changes in Equity:**

| | Share capital $ | Share premium $ | General reserve $ | Retained earnings $ | Total $ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Balance at start | 400,000 | 80,000 | 50,000 | 120,000 | 650,000 |
| Profit for the year | — | — | — | 95,000 | 95,000 |
| Preference dividend (6% × 100,000) | — | — | — | (6,000) | (6,000) |
| Ordinary dividend (5c × 400,000) | — | — | — | (20,000) | (20,000) |
| **Balance at end** | **400,000** | **80,000** | **50,000** | **189,000** | **719,000** |

**Workings:**
- Ordinary dividend = 5 cents × 400,000 shares = $20,000
- Preference dividend = 6% × $100,000 = $6,000
- Retained earnings end = $120,000 + $95,000 − $6,000 − $20,000 = $189,000
- Total equity = $400,000 + $80,000 + $50,000 + $189,000 = $719,000

**Explanation**: All equity changes flow through retained earnings (except new share issues). Share capital and share premium only change when shares are issued. A common error is deducting dividends from share capital instead of retained earnings, or miscalculating the ordinary dividend (cents per share × number of shares).
