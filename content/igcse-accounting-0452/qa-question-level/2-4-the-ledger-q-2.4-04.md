### Question Q-2.4-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: The following entries appear in the Rent account: Debit side — Jan 1: Bank $600, Feb 1: Bank $600, Mar 1: Bank $600. Credit side — Mar 31: Income statement $1,800. What does the credit entry on Mar 31 represent?
**Answer**: The credit entry on Mar 31 of $1,800 represents the transfer of the total rent expense to the income statement at the end of the accounting period. The three debit entries total $1,800 ($600 x 3), which is the total rent paid. At the end of the period, expense accounts are closed by transferring their totals to the income statement (credit the expense account, debit the income statement).
**Explanation**: This is the "balancing off" process for expense accounts. A common error is thinking the credit entry is a refund — it is a bookkeeping transfer to close the account for the period.
