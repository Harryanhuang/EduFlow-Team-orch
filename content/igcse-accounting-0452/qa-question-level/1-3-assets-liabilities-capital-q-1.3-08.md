### Question Q-1.3-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: Explain why drawings are not treated as a business expense in accounting.
**Answer**: Drawings are withdrawals of cash or other assets by the owner for personal use. They are not expenses incurred in earning revenue — they represent a reduction of the owner's capital (equity), not a cost of operations. Expenses appear in the income statement; drawings appear in the capital section of the statement of financial position as a deduction from capital.
**Explanation**: The business entity concept separates the owner's personal transactions from the business's transactions. A common error is treating drawings as an expense because cash leaves the business — the key distinction is whether the outflow was for business operations or personal use.
