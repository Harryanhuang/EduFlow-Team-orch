# Question Q-7.5-06

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Nova Traders began business on 1 January 20X8. During January 20X8 the following transactions took place:

- Owner introduced capital in cash $60,000
- Bought equipment for cash $18,000
- Bought goods on credit from Delta Supplies $24,000
- Bought goods for cash $6,000
- Credit sales $40,000
- Cash sales $10,000
- Returns inwards from credit customers $2,000
- Received cash from credit customers $30,000
- Paid Delta Supplies $20,000 by cheque
- Rent paid by cheque $4,000
- Owner withdrew cash for personal use $5,000
- A cheque for $6,000 was entered in the cash book as wages
- Discount received from Delta Supplies $1,000
- Closing inventory at 31 January 20X8 was $8,000

Additional information:

1. Rent owing at 31 January 20X8 was $500.
2. Of the $6,000 cheque entered as wages, $5,000 was actually drawings by the owner. Only $1,000 related to wages.

Prepare:

1. The journal entry to correct the drawings error.
2. The income statement for the month ended 31 January 20X8.
3. The statement of financial position at 31 January 20X8.

**Answer**:

1. **Correction journal**
   - Dr Drawings $5,000
   - Cr Wages $5,000

2. **Income statement for the month ended 31 January 20X8**

   **Revenue**
   - Credit sales $40,000
   - Cash sales $10,000
   - Less returns inwards $(2,000)$
   - **Net sales $48,000**

   **Cost of sales**
   - Opening inventory $0
   - Credit purchases $24,000
   - Cash purchases $6,000
   - Goods available for sale $30,000
   - Less closing inventory $(8,000)$
   - **Cost of sales $22,000**

   **Gross profit $26,000**

   **Other income**
   - Discount received $1,000

   **Expenses**
   - Rent: paid $4,000 + owing $500 = $4,500
   - Wages: $6,000 was recorded, but $5,000 of this was actually drawings. Correct wages expense = $1,000.
   - **Total expenses $5,500**

   **Profit for the month = $26,000 + $1,000 - $5,500 = $21,500**

3. **Statement of financial position at 31 January 20X8**

   **Assets**

   **Non-current assets**
   - Equipment $18,000

   **Current assets**
   - Inventory $8,000
   - Trade receivables: Credit sales $40,000 - returns inwards $2,000 - cash received $30,000 = $8,000
   - Cash:
     - Capital introduced $60,000
     - Less equipment $(18,000)$
     - Less cash purchases $(6,000)$
     - Add cash sales $10,000
     - Add receipts from credit customers $30,000
     - Less payment to Delta Supplies $(20,000)$
     - Less rent paid $(4,000)$
     - Less drawings cash withdrawal $(5,000)$
     - Less cheque entered as wages $(6,000)$
     - **Cash at bank $41,000**

   **Total current assets $57,000**

   **Total assets $75,000**

   **Capital and liabilities**

   **Capital**
   - Capital introduced $60,000
   - Add profit for the month $21,500
   - Less drawings $(10,000)$
   - **Closing capital $71,500**

   **Current liabilities**
   - Trade payables: Credit purchases $24,000 - payment $20,000 - discount received $1,000 = $3,000
   - Rent owing $500
   - **Total current liabilities $3,500**

   **Total capital and liabilities $75,000**

**Explanation**: This version uses one consistent source of data: opening capital plus a list of January transactions, one end-of-period accrual, one normal drawings withdrawal, and one classification error. There are therefore two drawings effects in total: the original cash drawings of $5,000 and the extra $5,000 hidden inside the cheque posted as wages. After correction, wages expense is only $1,000, total drawings are $10,000, profit is $21,500, and closing capital is $71,500. Cash is reduced by both the separate drawings withdrawal and the $6,000 cheque, so total assets are $75,000 and the statement of financial position balances with liabilities of $3,500. A common mistake is to correct wages to $1,000 but forget that drawings must then total $10,000, not $5,000.
