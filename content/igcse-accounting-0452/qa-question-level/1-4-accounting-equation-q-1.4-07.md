### Question Q-1.4-07
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A business buys goods on credit for $5,000. State the effect on the accounting equation.
**Answer**: Assets (inventory) increase by $5,000 and Liabilities (trade payables) increase by $5,000. Capital is unaffected. The equation remains balanced: A increases by $5,000 and L + C also increases by $5,000 (all from L).
**Explanation**: Credit purchases increase both what the business owns (stock) and what it owes. A common error is thinking this affects capital — the purchase itself does not create profit or loss, it is simply an exchange of a promise to pay for goods.
