# Question Q-6.4-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: What non-financial factors should be considered alongside ratio analysis when assessing a business's performance?

**Answer**: Key non-financial factors: 1) **Management quality and experience** — skilled managers can turn around poor ratios. 2) **Employee morale and skills** — affects productivity and quality. 3) **Brand reputation and customer loyalty** — strong brands command premium pricing. 4) **Market conditions and competition** — a declining market makes even 'good' ratios concerning. 5) **Technological change** — good current ratios may mask vulnerability to disruption. 6) **Environmental and social factors** — regulatory compliance and sustainability affect long-term viability. 7) **Supplier and customer relationships** — strong relationships provide stability. 8) **Legal and regulatory environment** — changes may significantly impact future performance. 9) **Product quality and innovation pipeline** — affects future revenue. 10) **Economic conditions** — inflation, interest rates, exchange rates affect ratio interpretation.

**Explanation**: Ratios are quantitative; these factors are qualitative. A common error is relying solely on ratios for decisions. The best assessments combine both.
