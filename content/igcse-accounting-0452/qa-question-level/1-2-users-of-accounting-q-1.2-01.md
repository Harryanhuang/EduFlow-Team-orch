### Question Q-1.2-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Who are the users of accounting information? Classify them into two groups.
**Answer**: Users of accounting information are classified into: Internal users — owners, managers, employees who work within the business and use information for day-to-day decisions. External users — investors, banks/creditors, government/tax authorities, suppliers, customers, and the public who are outside the business but have an interest in its financial affairs.
**Explanation**: The key distinction is whether the user works inside the organisation. A common error is classifying investors as internal users — even though they own shares, they are external unless they are also involved in management.
