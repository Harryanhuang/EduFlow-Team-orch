### Question Q-5.4-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A partner retires from a partnership. Partners A ($30,000 capital), B ($25,000 capital), and C ($20,000 capital) share profits 3:2:1. C retires. Goodwill is valued at $18,000 and is to be raised in the books. C is to be paid the full amount due. Show: (a) C's amount due, (b) the journal entries, (c) the remaining partners' capital balances after C's retirement.
**Answer**: (a) C's amount due: Capital $20,000 + Share of goodwill $18,000 × 1/6 = $3,000. Total due to C = $23,000.
(b) Journal entries:
  Raise goodwill: Debit Goodwill $18,000; Credit A's capital ($18,000 × 3/6) $9,000; Credit B's capital ($18,000 × 2/6) $6,000; Credit C's capital ($18,000 × 1/6) $3,000.
  Pay C: Debit C's capital $23,000, Credit Cash $23,000.
(c) Remaining partners' capital:
  A: $30,000 + $9,000 = $39,000
  B: $25,000 + $6,000 = $31,000
  Total: $70,000

**Explanation**: A common error is not including C's share of goodwill in the amount due — the retiring partner is entitled to their share of goodwill because they helped build the business. Goodwill is shared in the OLD profit-sharing ratio (3:2:1) because it represents value created by all partners up to the retirement date. After C leaves, A and B may agree a new profit-sharing ratio.
