### Question Q-2.1-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is an invoice? Who issues it and when?
**Answer**: An invoice is a document issued by a seller to a buyer when goods are sold (or services provided) on credit. It lists the goods or services, quantities, prices, and total amount due. The invoice serves as a request for payment and as evidence of the transaction for both parties.
**Explanation**: An invoice is issued for credit transactions, not necessarily for cash sales. A common error is thinking an invoice means payment has been made — an invoice requests payment; it does not confirm payment. A receipt confirms payment.
