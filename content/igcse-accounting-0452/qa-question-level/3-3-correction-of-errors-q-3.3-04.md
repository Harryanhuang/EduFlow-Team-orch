### Question Q-3.3-04
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: The trial balance shows debits exceed credits by $310. A suspense account is opened. Later these errors are found: (1) A sale of $200 was recorded as $290 in both the Sales account and the customer's account. (2) A purchase of $120 was posted to the Purchases account but not to the Trade payables account. Write the correcting journal entries and show how the suspense account is cleared.
**Answer**: Error (1): The wrong amount was used on both sides — both should be $200, not $290. Correction: Debit Sales $90, Credit Trade receivables $90, Narration: "Correction — sale recorded as $290 instead of $200." This reduces both the overstate. Error (2): Only one side was posted. Correction: Debit Purchases $120, Credit Suspense account $120, Narration: "Correction — credit entry for purchase of $120 omitted." Now for the suspense account: it was opened with a debit balance of $310 (to make the trial balance agree). After correction (2), the suspense account is credited $120. The remaining $190 debit balance in the suspense account means there are still unidentified errors of $190. Error (1) did not affect the suspense account because both sides used the same wrong amount.
**Explanation**: Only errors that cause a trial balance imbalance involve the suspense account. Error (1) had equal debits and credits ($290 each) so it never caused an imbalance — it is corrected directly between the two accounts. Error (2) had only a debit (Purchases $120) with no credit, so the missing credit goes to the suspense account. A common error is correcting error (1) through the suspense account — it should not be.
