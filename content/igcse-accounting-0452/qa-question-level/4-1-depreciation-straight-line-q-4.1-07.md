### Question Q-4.1-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A business buys equipment for $25,000 on 1 January 20X1. Residual value $5,000. Useful life 5 years. Straight-line depreciation is used. On 30 June 20X3 the equipment is sold for $12,000. Calculate the profit or loss on disposal and show the journal entry.
**Answer**: Annual depreciation = ($25,000 - $5,000) / 5 = $4,000 per year. The asset was owned for 2.5 years (Jan 20X1 to Jun 20X3). Accumulated depreciation at disposal = $4,000 × 2.5 = $10,000. Carrying amount at disposal = $25,000 - $10,000 = $15,000. Sale proceeds $12,000. Loss on disposal = $15,000 - $12,000 = $3,000 loss (carrying amount exceeds proceeds).
Journal entry: Debit Cash $12,000, Debit Accumulated depreciation $10,000, Debit Loss on disposal $3,000, Credit Equipment (cost) $25,000. Narration: "Disposal of equipment sold for $12,000, loss on disposal $3,000."
**Explanation**: A common error is comparing the sale proceeds with the original cost instead of the carrying amount. The profit or loss on disposal is always: Sale proceeds minus carrying amount (cost minus accumulated depreciation to the date of disposal). The accumulated depreciation must be removed from the books at disposal, along with the original cost.
