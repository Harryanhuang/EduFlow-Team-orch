### Question Q-1.4-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: A business has assets of $120,000 and liabilities of $45,000. Calculate the owner's capital.
**Answer**: Capital = Assets - Liabilities = $120,000 - $45,000 = $75,000.
**Explanation**: Direct application of C = A - L. A common error is adding instead of subtracting, or confusing which figure is assets and which is liabilities.
