### Question Q-3.1-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A trial balance disagrees because the debit column exceeds the credit column by $1,080. Investigation reveals two errors: (1) a rent payment of $360 was recorded as Debit Rent $360 with no credit entry; (2) a cash sale of $360 was recorded as Debit Cash $360, Debit Sales $360 (instead of Credit Sales $360). Explain how these errors produce the $1,080 imbalance and what corrections are needed.
**Answer**: Error 1: The rent payment debit was recorded but no credit was made. Debits exceed credits by $360. Error 2: The cash sale should have been Debit Cash $360, Credit Sales $360. Cash debit is correct, but Sales was debited $360 instead of credited $360. This means debits are $360 too high AND credits are $360 too low, so debits exceed credits by $720 from this error. Combined: $360 + $720 = $1,080 debit excess. Corrections: 1) Credit Cash $360 to complete the rent entry. 2) Credit Sales $720 to reverse the incorrect debit ($360) and record the correct credit ($360).
**Explanation**: When an entry is on the wrong side (debit instead of credit), it creates a double imbalance: the wrong column gains an amount the right column loses. A common error is counting only one side of the wrong-side error, underestimating its contribution to the difference.
