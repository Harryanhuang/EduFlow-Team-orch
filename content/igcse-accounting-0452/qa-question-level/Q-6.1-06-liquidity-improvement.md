# Question Q-6.1-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: What measures can a business take to improve its liquidity position? Evaluate the advantages and disadvantages of each.

**Answer**: Key measures: 1) **Reduce receivables collection period** — offer early payment discounts, tighten credit control, factor receivables. Advantage: improves cash inflows quickly. Disadvantage: discounts reduce profit; strict terms may lose customers. 2) **Reduce inventory levels** — use just-in-time ordering, sell obsolete stock, improve forecasting. Advantage: frees cash and reduces storage costs. Disadvantage: risk of stockouts and lost sales. 3) **Extend payables payment period** — negotiate longer credit terms with suppliers. Advantage: retains cash longer. Disadvantage: damages supplier relationships; loses early payment discounts. 4) **Sale and leaseback of non-current assets** — sell assets and lease back. Advantage: immediate cash. Disadvantage: long-term lease costs may exceed sale proceeds. 5) **Increase long-term finance** — issue shares or take a long-term loan. Advantage: stable funding. Disadvantage: cost of capital; dilution of ownership. 6) **Reduce drawings or dividends** — retain more cash. Advantage: immediate improvement. Disadvantage: owners receive less return.

**Explanation**: Each measure has trade-offs. A common error is recommending a single solution — businesses typically use a combination. The best choice depends on the root cause.
