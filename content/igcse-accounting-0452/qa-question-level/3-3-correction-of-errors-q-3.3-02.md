### Question Q-3.3-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the general format of a journal entry used to correct an error? What must every journal entry include?
**Answer**: A correcting journal entry has: the date, the two (or more) accounts to be debited and credited with amounts, and a narration (brief explanation). Every journal entry must have equal total debits and total credits, and the narration should describe what error is being corrected. Example: to correct a purchase of $500 wrongly debited to Repairs instead of Purchases — Debit Purchases $500, Credit Repairs $500, Narration: "Correction of error — purchase wrongly debited to Repairs account."
**Explanation**: Journal entries are the formal way to correct errors in the ledger. A common error is writing the journal without a narration or without ensuring debits = credits. The narration is essential so that anyone reading the ledger later understands why the entry was made.
