# Question Q-6.2-05

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A business has revenue of $350,000, cost of sales $210,000, operating expenses $85,000. Calculate the gross profit margin, net profit margin, and ROCE if capital employed is $250,000. Comment on the results.

**Answer**:
Gross profit = $350,000 - $210,000 = $140,000
Gross profit margin = ($140,000 / $350,000) × 100% = 40.0%
Net profit = $140,000 - $85,000 = $55,000
Net profit margin = ($55,000 / $350,000) × 100% = 15.7%
ROCE = ($55,000 / $250,000) × 100% = 22.0%

Comment: The gross profit margin of 40% is strong — $0.40 retained from each dollar of revenue after cost of sales. Operating expenses consume 24.3% of revenue, leaving a net profit margin of 15.7%. The ROCE of 22% is good — $0.22 of profit per $1.00 of capital employed, likely exceeding the cost of borrowing. The gap between gross and net margins shows operating expenses are significant but manageable.

**Explanation**: Always show workings clearly. ROCE should use operating profit. A common error is using net profit after interest for ROCE.
