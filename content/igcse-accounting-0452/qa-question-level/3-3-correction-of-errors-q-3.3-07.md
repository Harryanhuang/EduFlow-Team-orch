### Question Q-3.3-07
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A business reported a net profit of $45,000. Later these errors were found: (1) A non-current asset purchase of $3,000 was debited to Purchases (expense). (2) A sale of $1,200 was completely omitted. (3) Rent expense of $800 was recorded as $80 on both sides. (4) Irrecoverable debts written off of $500 were debited to Trade payables instead of Irreconable debts expense. Calculate the revised net profit.
**Answer**: Start with reported profit $45,000. (1) Asset purchase $3,000 wrongly debited to Purchases — expenses are overstated by $3,000, so profit is understated by $3,000. Correction: add $3,000 to profit → $48,000. (2) Sale $1,200 omitted — revenue is understated by $1,200, profit is understated. Correction: add $1,200 → $49,200. (3) Rent recorded $80 instead of $800 — expenses understated by $720 ($800 - $80), profit overstated by $720. Correction: subtract $720 → $48,480. (4) Debts $500 debited to Trade payables instead of expense — expenses understated by $500 (the expense was never recorded), profit overstated by $500. Correction: subtract $500 → $47,980. Revised net profit: $47,980.
**Explanation**: Profit adjustment requires working through each error's effect on revenue or expenses. A common error is treating error (1) as having no effect because both sides are $3,000 — the trial balance agrees, but the profit is wrong because an asset purchase (capital expenditure) should not appear in the income statement as a purchase expense. Error (4) is also easy to miss: debiting Trade payables instead of the expense means the expense was never charged against profit.
