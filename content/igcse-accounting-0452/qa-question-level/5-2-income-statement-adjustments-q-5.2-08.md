### Question Q-5.2-08
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: From a trial balance, a business has: Sales $200,000; Purchases $150,000; Opening inventory $15,000; Wages $30,000; Rent $12,000. The business applies a markup of 25% on cost. Adjustments needed: (a) wages accrued $2,500; (b) rent prepaid $1,000. However, the closing inventory figure is missing. Calculate: (a) the COGS implied by the markup, (b) the missing closing inventory, (c) the net profit after adjustments.
**Answer**: (a) Markup 25% means Sales = 125% of COGS. COGS = $200,000 / 1.25 = $160,000. Gross profit = $200,000 - $160,000 = $40,000.
(b) COGS = Opening inventory + Purchases - Closing inventory. $160,000 = $15,000 + $150,000 - Closing inventory. Closing inventory = $165,000 - $160,000 = $5,000.
(c) Wages adjusted = $30,000 + $2,500 = $32,500. Rent adjusted = $12,000 - $1,000 = $11,000. Total operating expenses = $32,500 + $11,000 = $43,500. Net profit = $40,000 - $43,500 = -$3,500 (net loss of $3,500).
**Explanation**: This type of question requires working in order: use markup to find COGS, use COGS formula to find the missing closing inventory, then apply adjustments to expenses and calculate net profit. A common error is calculating wages or rent at their trial balance amounts without making the adjustments — wages must include the accrual ($32,500, not $30,000) and rent must exclude the prepayment ($11,000, not $12,000).
