# Question Q-7.1-09

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A sole trader's financial statements show: assets at cost less depreciation; a personal car (60% business, 40% personal) recorded at full value as business asset; inventory at selling price; a probable lawsuit loss ($25,000) not disclosed; depreciation method changed from straight-line to reducing balance with no disclosure. Identify concept violations and explain correct treatment.

**Answer**:

1) **Assets at cost less depreciation** — CORRECT. Follows going concern (assets used over useful lives) and accruals (cost allocated over periods benefited). No violation.

2) **Personal car at full value** — Violates **business entity concept**. Owner's personal affairs must be separate from the business. Only the 60% business portion should be recorded as a business asset. The personal 40% should be excluded or treated as drawings.

3) **Inventory at selling price** — Violates **prudence**. Inventory must be at the lower of cost and NRV. Valuing at selling price anticipates profit before sale. Correct treatment: cost (or NRV if lower).

4) **Probable lawsuit loss not disclosed** — Violates **prudence**. A probable loss should be provided for as a liability and expense. May also violate **materiality** if $25,000 is significant.

5) **Depreciation change with no disclosure** — Violates **consistency**. Changing the method is permissible if justified, but must be disclosed in the notes with reason and financial effect.

**Explanation**: Each concept addresses a different aspect of faithful representation. A common error is identifying the violation without explaining the correct treatment. Always state both what was wrong and how it should be corrected.
