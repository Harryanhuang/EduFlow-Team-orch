# Question Q-5.5-04

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: How is interest on drawings treated in partnership accounts? What is its purpose and how is it calculated?

**Answer**: Interest on drawings is charged to partners on amounts they withdraw from the business during the year. In the appropriation account, interest on drawings is ADDED BACK to net profit (increasing the distributable amount), because it represents money partners owe back to the partnership. Its purpose is to: 1) discourage excessive withdrawals that reduce the business's working capital, and 2) ensure fairness between partners who draw different amounts. Calculation: if specific dates are given, calculate interest from each drawing date to year end. If no dates are given, drawings are assumed to have been made on average at the midpoint of the year, so interest is charged for 6 months (e.g. $5,000 × 5% × 6/12 = $125).

**Explanation**: Interest on drawings increases distributable profit. A common error is deducting it like an expense — it must be added back to net profit. When individual drawing dates and amounts are provided, calculate each separately from the drawing date to year end.
