### Question Q-2.1-05
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the difference between a cheque and a receipt as source documents?
**Answer**: A cheque is a written order instructing a bank to pay a specified amount from the drawer's account to the payee. It is evidence of an intention to pay. A receipt is a document acknowledging that payment has been received. A cheque is issued before or at the time of payment; a receipt is issued after payment is received.
**Explanation**: A cheque is a payment instruction; a receipt is a payment confirmation. A common error is treating a cheque as proof of payment — the cheque may bounce, whereas a receipt confirms money actually changed hands.
