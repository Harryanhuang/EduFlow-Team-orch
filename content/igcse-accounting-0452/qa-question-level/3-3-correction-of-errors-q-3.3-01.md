### Question Q-3.3-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the purpose of a suspense account? When is it opened?
**Answer**: A suspense account is a temporary account opened when the trial balance does not agree — that is, when total debits do not equal total credits. It holds the difference so that the trial balance can be balanced while the errors are investigated. Once the errors are found and corrected, the suspense account balance reduces to zero. If the suspense account still has a balance after all errors are corrected, it means there are still unidentified errors.
**Explanation**: The suspense account is not a permanent account — it is a placeholder. A common error is thinking a suspense account is used to hide errors. It does not hide them; it temporarily holds the imbalance so bookkeeping can continue while errors are traced. The balance in the suspense account equals the amount of the trial balance difference.
