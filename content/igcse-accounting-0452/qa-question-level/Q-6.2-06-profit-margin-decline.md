# Question Q-6.2-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A business's gross profit margin has remained constant at 35% over three years, but its net profit margin has declined from 18% to 12% to 8%. What could explain this? What actions should the business take?

**Answer**: If gross profit margin is constant but net profit margin is declining, the problem lies in operating expenses increasing faster than revenue. Possible causes: 1) Rising overhead costs (rent, utilities, insurance). 2) Increasing administrative expenses (staff costs, professional fees). 3) Higher distribution or selling expenses. 4) Increased finance costs (interest on loans) from more debt. 5) One-off expenses (legal fees, restructuring). Actions: 1) Review all expense categories to identify fastest-growing areas. 2) Negotiate better terms with overhead suppliers. 3) Reduce discretionary spending. 4) Consider whether revenue growth justifies expense growth (economies of scale should reduce expense as a percentage of revenue). 5) Review staffing levels and operational efficiency.

**Explanation**: The constant gross margin means pricing and cost of sales are stable — the problem is operational efficiency. A common error is suggesting price changes when gross margin is already stable.
