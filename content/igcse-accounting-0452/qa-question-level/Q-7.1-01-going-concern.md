# Question Q-7.1-01

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What is the going concern concept? How does it affect the preparation of financial statements?

**Answer**: The going concern concept assumes that a business will continue to operate for the foreseeable future (at least 12 months) and will not be forced to close or liquidate. It affects financial statements: 1) Non-current assets are recorded at cost less depreciation, not break-up value, because they will be used over their useful lives. 2) Expenses are allocated over the periods they benefit (depreciation). 3) Long-term liabilities are classified as non-current. 4) Prepayments are recorded as assets. If going concern is doubtful, this must be disclosed and assets may need to be restated at realizable value.

**Explanation**: A common error is thinking going concern means profitable — it means continuing to operate. An unprofitable business can still be a going concern if it has sufficient funding.
