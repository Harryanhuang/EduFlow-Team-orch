# Question Q-7.4-04

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A business uses complex financial instruments. Management argues they should be excluded from financial statements because users would not understand them. Evaluate with reference to qualitative characteristics.

**Answer**: This argument misapplies understandability. Understandability requires clear presentation for users with reasonable knowledge — it does NOT mean complex information should be excluded. 1) **Relevance** — complex instruments are likely highly relevant because they affect risk profile and financial position. 2) **Faithful representation** — excluding them would make statements incomplete and not a faithful representation. 3) **Understandability** — complex information should be presented as clearly as possible, with appropriate disclosures in the notes. The solution is not exclusion but better communication. Relevant and faithfully represented information must be included; understandability guides how it is presented, not whether it is included.

**Explanation**: Understandability guides presentation, not inclusion. A common error is excluding complex information in the name of understandability — this violates faithful representation (completeness) and relevance.
