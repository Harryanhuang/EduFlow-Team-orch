### Question Q-2.5-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is discount allowed? On which side of the cash book is it recorded?
**Answer**: Discount allowed is a reduction in the amount a customer pays, given by the business to encourage prompt payment (e.g., "2% discount if paid within 14 days"). It is recorded on the debit side of the cash book in the discount column because it represents a cost to the business — the business receives less cash than the full invoice amount.
**Explanation**: Discount allowed is an expense and appears on the debit side. A common error is recording it on the credit side because it is a "discount" — the key is that it costs the business money, so it sits on the same side as other debits.
