# Question Q-6.3-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A business offers 30-day credit terms but its receivables collection period is 52 days. What are the implications and what actions could the business take?

**Answer**: A receivables period of 52 days against 30-day terms means customers take an average of 22 extra days to pay. Implications: 1) The business provides free finance to customers for 22 extra days. 2) Cash flow is strained — the business must fund operations while waiting for payment. 3) Increased bad debt risk — longer unpaid debts are more likely to default. 4) May need additional borrowing to cover cash shortfalls. Actions: 1) Strengthen credit control — reminders, prompt follow-up on overdue accounts. 2) Offer early payment discounts (e.g. 2% for payment within 10 days). 3) Conduct credit checks on new customers. 4) Set credit limits. 5) Consider receivables factoring — selling receivables for immediate cash. 6) Charge interest on overdue accounts.

**Explanation**: 22 days overdue is significant. A common error is suggesting stricter credit terms to all customers — this may lose good customers. Focus on improving collection efficiency, not reducing credit availability.
