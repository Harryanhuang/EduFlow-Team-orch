### Question Q-3.4-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: The cash book shows a balance of $4,500 (debit). The bank statement shows $3,800 (credit). The difference is caused by: (a) unpresented cheques of $1,200, and (b) a deposit of $500 not yet credited by the bank. Show the bank reconciliation statement starting from the bank statement balance.
**Answer**: Bank reconciliation statement as at the date:
Bank statement balance (credit) $3,800
Add: Unpresented cheques $1,200
Less: Deposit not yet credited ($500)
Updated cash book balance: $4,500

Unpresented cheques are cheques the business has issued (credited in the cash book) but the bank has not yet paid — they reduce the bank's view of available funds, so we add them back to the bank statement balance. The deposit not yet credited is money the business has recorded as received (debited in the cash book) but the bank has not yet cleared — we subtract it from the bank statement balance because the bank does not yet reflect it. $3,800 + $1,200 - $500 = $4,500, matching the cash book.
**Explanation**: A common error is adding unpresented cheques to the cash book instead of adjusting the bank statement side. Unpresented cheques are already in the cash book — the bank statement is the one that needs adjusting. The reconciliation starts from one balance and works toward the other.
