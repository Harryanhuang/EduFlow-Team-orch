# Question Q-5.5-08

**Difficulty**: Challenge

**Question**: Compare and contrast the financial reporting of a partnership with that of a sole trader. What additional accounts and considerations arise for partnerships?

**Answer**: Both sole traders and partnerships prepare an income statement and a statement of financial position using the same fundamental accounting principles (double entry, accruals, prudence). However, partnerships have additional reporting requirements: 1) **Appropriation account** — partnerships must show how profit is divided among partners; sole traders take all profit as drawings so no appropriation is needed. 2) **Multiple equity accounts** — partnerships maintain separate capital and current accounts for each partner, presented individually in the statement of financial position; a sole trader has a single capital account. 3) **Interest on drawings** — partnership drawings may carry interest, requiring additional appropriation entries. 4) **Profit-sharing documentation** — partnership accounts must comply with the partnership agreement, which may specify different treatments per partner. 5) **Partner change accounting** — on admission or retirement, additional calculations for goodwill, asset revaluation, and capital adjustments are required. 6) **Complexity of drawings** — partnerships must track drawings per partner and apply any agreed interest.

**Explanation**: The core financial statements are similar; differences arise from multiple owners. A common error is presenting partnership capital as a single figure instead of showing each partner's capital and current account separately.
