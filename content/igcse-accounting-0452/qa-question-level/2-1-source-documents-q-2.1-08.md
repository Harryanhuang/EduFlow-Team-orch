### Question Q-2.1-08
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A customer receives a credit note from a supplier. In the customer's books, does the credit note reduce purchases, trade payables, or both? Explain.
**Answer**: The credit note reduces trade payables because the customer owes less to the supplier. It also reduces purchases (or returns outwards) because the goods returned are no longer part of the purchase total. In practice, the customer debits trade payables (reducing the liability) and credits purchases returns/returns outwards.
**Explanation**: The credit note has a dual effect in the customer's books. A common error is thinking it only reduces payables — it must also affect the purchases figure to keep the double entry balanced.
