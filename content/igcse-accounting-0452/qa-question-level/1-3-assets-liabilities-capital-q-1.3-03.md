### Question Q-1.3-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is the difference between a current liability and a non-current liability?
**Answer**: A current liability is a debt that must be repaid within one year (e.g., trade payables, bank overdraft, short-term loans). A non-current liability is a debt that is not due for repayment until more than one year has passed (e.g., a mortgage repayable over 20 years, a five-year bank loan). The distinction is based on the time until payment is due.
**Explanation**: The one-year boundary is the same test used for assets. A common error is confusing the type of debt with its timing — a bank loan can be either current or non-current depending on when repayment is due.
