### Question Q-1.5-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is revenue expenditure? Give two examples.
**Answer**: Revenue expenditure is spending on the day-to-day running of the business — it maintains existing assets and generates revenue in the current period. Examples: paying wages, buying inventory for resale, repair and maintenance costs, rent, electricity bills.
**Explanation**: Revenue expenditure keeps the business operating but does not acquire or improve non-current assets. A common error is classifying repair costs as capital expenditure — repairs maintain the existing state; they do not enhance value.
