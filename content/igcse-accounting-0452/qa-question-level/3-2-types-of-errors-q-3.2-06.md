### Question Q-3.2-06
**Difficulty**: Standard
**Question**: A credit sale of $450 to customer Ali was correctly recorded in the Sales account but was posted as $540 in Ali's receivables account. Is the trial balance affected? What type of error is this?
**Answer**: The trial balance will NOT agree. The credit to Sales is $450 but the debit to Ali's account is $540 — debits exceed credits by $90. This is NOT one of the six undetectable errors; it is a transposition error ($450 vs $540) that causes an imbalance. The correct entry should have been Debit Ali (receivables) $450, Credit Sales $450.
**Explanation**: The amounts differ between debit ($540) and credit ($450), so the trial balance will show a $90 difference. A common error is calling this an "error of original entry" — original entry means the same wrong amount is used on both sides; here the amounts differ, so the trial balance catches it.
