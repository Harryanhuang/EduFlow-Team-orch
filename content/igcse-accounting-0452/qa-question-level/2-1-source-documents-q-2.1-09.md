### Question Q-2.1-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: A business cannot find the source document for a $2,000 payment recorded in its books. What are the risks and what should the accountant do?
**Answer**: Risks: 1) The payment may not have actually occurred — the entry could be an error or fraud. 2) Without a source document, the entry cannot be verified during an audit, potentially leading to a qualification of the accounts. 3) The business cannot prove the payment was legitimate if challenged by tax authorities. Action: The accountant should investigate by checking bank statements for a corresponding payment, contacting the supposed payee, reviewing authorisation records, and if no evidence is found, reversing the entry. The incident should be reported to management.
**Explanation**: Missing source documents undermine the reliability of financial records. A common error is simply saying "record it anyway" — every entry must be supported by evidence.
