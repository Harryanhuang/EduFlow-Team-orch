### Question Q-1.2-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What financial information does the owner of a business need and why?
**Answer**: The owner needs information about profitability (profit or loss), financial position (assets and liabilities), and cash flow. This helps the owner assess whether the business is making a return on their investment, whether their capital is growing or declining, and whether the business can meet its obligations. The owner also needs this information to decide whether to expand, withdraw funds, or close the business.
**Explanation**: Owners focus on return on investment and capital preservation. A common error is only saying "owners need to know profit" — they also need liquidity, solvency and cash flow information.
