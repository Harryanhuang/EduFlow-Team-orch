# Question Q-6.4-02

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What are the main limitations of ratio analysis?

**Answer**: Key limitations: 1) **Historical data** — based on past statements, may not reflect future performance. 2) **Different accounting policies** — businesses may use different methods (FIFO vs AVCO, different depreciation), making comparisons misleading. 3) **Window dressing** — management may manipulate year-end figures to present a better picture. 4) **Industry differences** — comparing across industries is meaningless because benchmarks vary. 5) **Single-point data** — balance sheet figures are a snapshot and may not be representative (e.g. seasonal businesses). 6) **Non-financial factors ignored** — ratios don't capture management quality, employee morale, brand reputation. 7) **Inflation effects** — historical cost accounting distorts ratios during high inflation. 8) **Qualitative context missing** — ratios show numbers but not the reasons behind them.

**Explanation**: Ratios are a tool, not an answer. A common error is listing limitations without explaining how each affects interpretation. Always link limitations to their impact on decision-making.
