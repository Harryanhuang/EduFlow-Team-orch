# Question Q-7.3-03

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A business changes from FIFO to AVCO. Opening inventory under FIFO was $40,000; under AVCO it would have been $36,000. Closing inventory under FIFO is $50,000; under AVCO it would be $44,000. Cost of sales under FIFO was $200,000. Calculate the adjusted cost of sales under AVCO and explain the effect on profit.

**Answer**: Under FIFO: Opening $40,000 + Purchases - Closing $50,000 = Cost of sales $200,000. So Purchases = $210,000. Under AVCO: Opening $36,000 + Purchases $210,000 - Closing $44,000 = Cost of sales $202,000. Alternatively: Difference = ($40,000 - $36,000) - ($50,000 - $44,000) = $4,000 - $6,000 = -$2,000. Cost of sales under AVCO = $200,000 + $2,000 = $202,000. Effect: Cost of sales is $2,000 higher under AVCO, so profit is $2,000 lower.

**Explanation**: Always adjust both opening and closing inventory. A common error is only adjusting closing inventory — opening inventory change also affects cost of sales.
