# Question Q-7.3-06

**Difficulty**: Standard

**Question**: A business used reducing balance for vehicle depreciation for 5 years, now wants straight-line for a more consistent charge. Vehicles cost $150,000, accumulated depreciation under reducing balance is $75,000. Under straight-line, it would have been $60,000. Discuss whether the change is justified and the financial impact.

**Answer**: **Justification**: Management's desire for a "more consistent charge" is potentially valid if the pattern of economic benefits is more even over time. However, the change should be justified by the pattern of benefits, not by profit manipulation. **Financial impact**: Accumulated depreciation difference = $75,000 - $60,000 = $15,000 more charged under reducing balance. NBV under reducing balance = $75,000; under straight-line = $90,000. If applied prospectively, the NBV of $75,000 is depreciated using straight-line over remaining life. Prior periods are NOT restated — the $15,000 difference remains. **Disclosure**: Nature, reason, and effect on current year's depreciation. Cumulative difference of $15,000 should be disclosed.

**Explanation**: Prospective application means current and future periods only — prior periods are not restated. A common error is restating prior accumulated depreciation when the change is prospective.
