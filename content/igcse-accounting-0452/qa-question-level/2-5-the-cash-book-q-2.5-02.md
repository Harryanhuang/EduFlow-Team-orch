### Question Q-2.5-02
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Describe the structure of a three-column cash book.
**Answer**: A three-column cash book has three columns on each side (debit/receipts and credit/payments): a discount column, a cash column, and a bank column. The debit side records receipts (discount allowed, cash received, banked amounts) and the credit side records payments (discount received, cash paid, bank payments). The cash column records physical cash; the bank column records bank transactions.
**Explanation**: The three columns on each side allow separate tracking of discounts, cash-in-hand, and bank balances. A common error is putting discount allowed on the credit side — discount allowed is a cost to the business and appears on the debit (receipts) side alongside the related customer payment.
