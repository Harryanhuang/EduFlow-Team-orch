### Question Q-1.2-09
**Difficulty**: Challenge
**Question**: "All users need the same accounting information." Do you agree? Justify your answer with reference to three different user groups.
**Answer**: Disagree. Different users have different needs: 1) A bank focuses on liquidity and gearing to assess loan repayment risk — it cares less about detailed revenue breakdowns. 2) A manager needs detailed cost analysis by department for operational control — summary profit figures are insufficient. 3) A tax authority needs accurate profit figures and allowable expense details for tax computation — growth projections are irrelevant. While all users rely on the same financial statements, each group extracts and interprets different information. This is why businesses produce both general-purpose financial statements and specialised management reports.
**Explanation**: The syllabus emphasises that users have different information needs. A common error is agreeing with the statement — the whole topic exists to show that needs differ. Strong answers reference specific user groups with specific information requirements.
