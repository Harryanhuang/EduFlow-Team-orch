# Question Q-5.5-03

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: How is interest on capital calculated and treated in partnership accounts? Why is it allowed?

**Answer**: Interest on capital is calculated by applying the agreed rate (e.g. 5% per annum) to each partner's capital balance. If capital changes during the year, interest is calculated on a time-weighted basis — for example, 5% on $10,000 for 6 months = $250. In the appropriation account, interest on capital is deducted from net profit BEFORE the residual profit is shared. It is allowed to compensate partners who have invested more capital, recognizing that capital has an opportunity cost and that partners contributing more capital deserve a return on that investment before profit is shared. This ensures fairness when partners have unequal capital contributions.

**Explanation**: Interest on capital is an appropriation, not an expense. A common error is calculating interest on the average capital when the agreement specifies opening capital, or forgetting to time-apportion when capital changes mid-year. Always check whether the question specifies opening, closing, or time-weighted capital.
