### Question Q-5.1-01
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: What is an income statement (statement of profit or loss)? Describe the structure of a trading entity's income statement, including the trading section.
**Answer**: An income statement (statement of profit or loss) is a financial statement that shows the financial performance of a business over a period of time — whether it made a profit or a loss. For a trading entity, the structure is:
Trading section: Sales (revenue) - Cost of goods sold (COGS) = Gross profit. COGS = Opening inventory + Purchases - Closing inventory.
The trading section shows the profit earned from the core trading activity (buying and selling goods).
Below the trading section: Gross profit - Expenses (wages, rent, insurance, etc.) + Other income = Net profit (or net loss).
Gross profit measures the profit from buying and selling before considering overheads. Net profit measures what remains after ALL expenses are deducted.
**Explanation**: A common error is including operating expenses (rent, wages, etc.) in the trading section — these appear BELOW the gross profit line. The trading section only includes items directly related to buying and selling goods.
