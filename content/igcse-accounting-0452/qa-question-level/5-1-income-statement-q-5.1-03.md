### Question Q-5.1-03
**Difficulty**: Foundation
**Question**: Prepare the trading section of the income statement for a business with the following: Sales $250,000, opening inventory $30,000, purchases $150,000, carriage inwards $5,000, returns inwards $8,000, returns outwards $3,000, closing inventory $35,000.
**Answer**:
Trading section for the year ended 31 December 20X4:

Sales ($250,000 - $8,000 returns inwards) = $242,000
Less cost of goods sold:
  Opening inventory: $30,000
  Purchases ($150,000 - $3,000 returns outwards): $147,000
  Carriage inwards: $5,000
  Less closing inventory: ($35,000)
  COGS: $30,000 + $147,000 + $5,000 - $35,000 = $147,000

Gross profit = $242,000 - $147,000 = $95,000

Key adjustments: Net sales = Sales - Returns inwards. Net purchases = Purchases - Returns outwards + Carriage inwards. Carriage inwards is a cost of acquiring goods and is added to purchases. Carriage outwards (delivery to customers) is an operating expense, NOT part of COGS.
**Explanation**: A common error is including carriage outwards in COGS — carriage outwards is a selling/distribution expense and appears BELOW the gross profit line. Only carriage inwards (cost of bringing goods into the business) is part of the cost of purchases.
