# Question Q-7.2-02

**Difficulty**: Foundation

**Question**: What are the three criteria for classifying expenditure as capital expenditure? Give examples of each.

**Answer**: Expenditure is capital if it results in: 1) **Acquisition of a non-current asset** — buying a new asset. Example: purchasing a delivery van, buying land, acquiring computer equipment. 2) **Addition (extension) to an existing asset** — adding to or extending to increase capacity. Example: building a factory extension, adding a new office wing, installing an additional production line. 3) **Enhancement or improvement to an existing asset** — improving to produce greater benefits (more output, longer life, better quality). Example: replacing a wooden floor with concrete in a warehouse, upgrading a machine for higher quality output, redeveloping a building for a new use. Expenditure that merely maintains or restores an asset to its original condition is revenue, not capital.

**Explanation**: The three criteria are acquisition, addition, and enhancement. A common error is classifying improvements as revenue — if expenditure increases capacity, quality, or useful life, it is capital.
