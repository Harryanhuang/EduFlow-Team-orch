A $5$ kg object is raised from the ground ($y = 0$) to a height of $y = 10$ m by a variable force $F(y) = 5gy + 2y$ N (upward). What is the change in potential energy of the object? (Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².)

## Options
A) $\Delta U = 490$ J
B) $\Delta U = 590$ J
C) $\Delta U = 100$ J
D) $\Delta U = 0$ J (path-independent, depends only on height)

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The change in potential energy for a conservative force is path-independent and depends only on the height change:

$$\Delta U = U_f - U_i = mg(y_f - y_i) = 5(9.8)(10 - 0) = 490 \text{ J}$$

The applied force $F(y) = 5(9.8)y + 2y = 49y + 2y$ does additional work beyond what is needed to lift the object. The work done by the applied force is:

$$W_F = \int_0^{10} (49y + 2y)\, dy = \int_0^{10} 51y\, dy = \left[\frac{51y^2}{2}\right]_0^{10} = \frac{51(100)}{2} = 2550 \text{ J}$$

The work done by gravity (a conservative force) is:

$$W_{grav} = -\Delta U = -490 \text{ J}$$

By the work-energy theorem: $W_{net} = W_F + W_{grav} = 2550 - 490 = 2060$ J = $\Delta KE$.

If the object starts at rest, $v_f = \sqrt{2 \cdot 2060/5} = \sqrt{824} \approx 28.7$ m/s.

The key concept: $\Delta U$ for a conservative force depends only on the endpoints, regardless of the actual force applied. The correct answer for the change in potential energy is $\Delta U = 490$ J. The correct answer is **A**.
