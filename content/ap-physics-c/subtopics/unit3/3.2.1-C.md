A particle of mass $m = 2$ kg experiences a conservative force $F(x) = -4x^3 + 4x$ N. The particle is released from rest at $x = 2$ m. What is the speed when it reaches $x = 0$? Verify that mechanical energy is conserved.

## Options
A) $v \approx 2.00$ m/s; energy is conserved at $E = 4$ J
B) $v \approx 2.83$ m/s; energy is conserved at $E = 8$ J
C) $v \approx 4.00$ m/s; energy is conserved at $E = 16$ J
D) $v \approx 5.66$ m/s; energy is conserved at $E = 32$ J

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The potential energy is found by integration:

$$U(x) = -\int F(x)\, dx = -\int (-4x^3 + 4x)\, dx = x^4 - 2x^2 + C$$

Choose $C = 0$: $U(x) = x^4 - 2x^2$.

**Initial position** ($x = 2$, at rest):

$$U(2) = (2)^4 - 2(2)^2 = 16 - 8 = 8 \text{ J}$$
$$KE(2) = 0 \implies E_i = 8 \text{ J}$$

**Final position** ($x = 0$):

$$U(0) = 0$$
$$E_f = \frac{1}{2}(2)v_f^2 + 0 = v_f^2$$

Conservation of mechanical energy: $E_i = E_f$:

$$8 = v_f^2 \implies v_f = \sqrt{8} \approx 2.83 \text{ m/s}$$

**Verification at intermediate position** $x = \sqrt{2}$ (where the particle has highest kinetic energy):

$$U(\sqrt{2}) = 4 - 4 = 0$$
$$KE(\sqrt{2}) = \frac{1}{2}(2)(v(\sqrt{2}))^2 = (v(\sqrt{2}))^2$$
$$E = (v(\sqrt{2}))^2 = 8 \implies v(\sqrt{2}) = \sqrt{8} \approx 2.83 \text{ m/s}$$

The particle has equal speed at $x = \sqrt{2}$ and $x = 0$ because the potential energy has the same value at both points — a hallmark of energy conservation in a conservative potential. The correct answer is **B**.
