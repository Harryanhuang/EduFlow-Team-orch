A car of mass $m = 1500$ kg accelerates from rest with a constant acceleration $a = 1$ m/s². What is the instantaneous power delivered by the engine at $t = 8$ s, when the speed is $v = 8$ m/s?

## Options
A) $P = 6.0$ kW
B) $P = 12$ kW
C) $P = 24$ kW
D) $P = 48$ kW

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For constant acceleration, the driving force needed (ignoring friction and air resistance):

$$F = ma = 1500(1) = 1500 \text{ N}$$

The instantaneous power at the instant when $v = 8$ m/s:

$$P = F \cdot v = 1500 \times 8 = 12000 \text{ W} = 12 \text{ kW}$$

The correct answer is **B**.

**Note on units**: $1 \text{ W} = 1 \text{ J/s} = 1 \text{ N·m/s}$. The product $F \cdot v$ has units of N·(m/s) = (kg·m/s²)(m/s) = kg·m²/s³ = W (watts).

**Distinction**: instantaneous power at one instant ($P(t_0)$) differs from average power over an interval ($\bar{P} = W_{total}/\Delta t$). At $t = 8$ s, $P_{instant} = 12$ kW, while $\bar{P}$ from $t = 0$ to $8$ s would equal $\frac{1}{2}mv^2/t = (0.5)(1500)(64)/8 = 6000$ W $= 6$ kW — exactly half the instantaneous value.
