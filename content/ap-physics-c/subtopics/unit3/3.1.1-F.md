A variable force $F(x) = 3x^2 - 2x$ N acts on an object moving from $x = 1$ m to $x = 4$ m along the x-axis. How much work is done by the force?

## Options
A) $W = 54$ J
B) $W = 42$ J
C) $W = 48$ J
D) $W = 60$ J

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Work is the definite integral of force over displacement:

$$W = \int_{x_i}^{x_f} F(x)\, dx = \int_1^4 (3x^2 - 2x)\, dx = \left[x^3 - x^2\right]_1^4$$

At $x = 4$: $64 - 16 = 48$
At $x = 1$: $1 - 1 = 0$

$$W = 48 - 0 = 48 \text{ J}$$

The work done is $48$ J, which is the area under the $F(x)$ curve from $x = 1$ m to $x = 4$ m. The correct answer is **C**.
