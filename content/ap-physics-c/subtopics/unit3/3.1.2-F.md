A $4$ kg block starts at rest and is pushed across a horizontal surface by a force that does $W_{net} = 32$ J of net work. What is the block's final speed?

## Options
A) $v_f = 2.00$ m/s
B) $v_f = 4.00$ m/s
C) $v_f = 6.00$ m/s
D) $v_f = 8.00$ m/s

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The work-energy theorem states that the net work equals the change in kinetic energy:

$$W_{net} = \Delta KE = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 - \frac{1}{2}mv_i^2$$

Since $v_i = 0$:

$$W_{net} = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 \implies v_f = \sqrt{\frac{2W_{net}}{m}}$$

$$v_f = \sqrt{\frac{2(32)}{4}} = \sqrt{16} = 4.00 \text{ m/s}$$

The block reaches a final speed of $4.00$ m/s. The correct answer is **B**.
