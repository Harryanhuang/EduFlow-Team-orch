A $5$ kg object accelerates uniformly from $v_i = 2$ m/s to $v_f = 6$ m/s over $\Delta t = 5$ s. What is the average power delivered to the object during this interval?

## Options
A) $P̄ = 6.4$ W
B) $P̄ = 12.8$ W
C) $P̄ = 16.0$ W
D) $P̄ = 32.0$ W

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The work done on the object equals the change in kinetic energy:

$$W_{net} = \Delta KE = \frac{1}{2}m(v_f^2 - v_i^2) = \frac{1}{2}(5)(6^2 - 2^2) = \frac{1}{2}(5)(36 - 4) = \frac{1}{2}(5)(32) = 80 \text{ J}$$

The average power is the total work divided by the time interval:

$$\bar{P} = \frac{W_{net}}{\Delta t} = \frac{80}{5} = 16.0 \text{ W}$$

The average power delivered to the object during this 5-second interval is 16.0 W. The correct answer is **C**.

For uniform acceleration, $a = (6 - 2)/5 = 0.8$ m/s² and $F = ma = 5(0.8) = 4$ N. The instantaneous power at $t$ is $P(t) = Fv(t) = 4(2 + 0.8t) = 8 + 3.2t$. The average over $[0, 5]$ is $\frac{1}{5}\int_0^5 (8 + 3.2t)\, dt = \frac{1}{5}[8t + 1.6t^2]_0^5 = \frac{1}{5}(40 + 40) = 16$ W. The two methods agree.
