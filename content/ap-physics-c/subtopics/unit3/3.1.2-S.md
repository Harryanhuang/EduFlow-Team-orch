A $2$ kg block starts with initial speed $v_0 = 3$ m/s on a horizontal frictionless surface. A variable force $F(x) = 4x - 2$ N (with $x$ in meters) acts on the block in the direction of motion. What is the block's speed at $x = 4$ m?

## Options
A) $v_f = 4.00$ m/s
B) $v_f = 5.00$ m/s
C) $v_f \approx 5.74$ m/s
D) $v_f = 8.00$ m/s

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Using Newton's second law in the form $F = m \frac{dv}{dt} = m \frac{dv}{dx}\cdot\frac{dx}{dt} = mv\frac{dv}{dx}$:

$$F(x) = mv\frac{dv}{dx}$$

Separate variables and integrate:

$$\int_{v_0}^{v_f} v\, dv = \frac{1}{m}\int_{x_0}^{x_f} F(x)\, dx$$

$$\frac{1}{2}(v_f^2 - v_0^2) = \frac{1}{m}\int_0^4 (4x - 2)\, dx$$

Compute the work integral:

$$\int_0^4 (4x - 2)\, dx = \left[2x^2 - 2x\right]_0^4 = (32 - 8) - 0 = 24 \text{ J}$$

Then:

$$\frac{1}{2}(v_f^2 - v_0^2) = \frac{24}{2} = 12 \implies v_f^2 - v_0^2 = 24$$
$$v_f^2 = 9 + 24 = 33 \implies v_f = \sqrt{33} \approx 5.74 \text{ m/s}$$

The block reaches a speed of about $5.74$ m/s at $x = 4$ m. The correct answer is **C**.

The chain-rule manipulation $a = dv/dt = (dv/dx)(dx/dt) = v(dv/dx)$ is the heart of the calculus-based derivation of the work-energy theorem — it converts Newton's second law (in time) into a position-based equation that can be integrated directly.
