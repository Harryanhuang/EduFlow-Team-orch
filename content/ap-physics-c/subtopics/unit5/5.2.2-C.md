---

A solid disk of mass \(M = 3.00\text{ kg}\) and radius \(R = 0.400\text{ m}\) rotates freely about its central axis with an initial angular velocity \(\omega_0 = 8.00\text{ rad/s}\). A second solid disk of mass \(m = 0.500\text{ kg}\) and radius \(r = 0.200\text{ m}\), initially not rotating, is dropped coaxially onto the first disk. The two disks adhere and rotate together as a single rigid body. What is their final angular velocity \(\omega_f\)?

- A) \(6.40\text{ rad/s}\)
- B) \(7.68\text{ rad/s}\)
- C) \(9.60\text{ rad/s}\)
- D) \(5.33\text{ rad/s}\)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The friction between the two disks is an internal force; with no external torque about the rotation axis, $dL/dt = \tau_{\text{ext}} = 0$, so angular momentum is conserved.

**Moments of inertia of each disk:**
$$I_1 = \tfrac{1}{2}MR^2 = \tfrac{1}{2}(3.00)(0.400)^2 = 0.240 \text{ kg·m}^2$$
$$I_2 = \tfrac{1}{2}mr^2 = \tfrac{1}{2}(0.500)(0.200)^2 = 0.0100 \text{ kg·m}^2$$
$$I_{\text{total}} = I_1 + I_2 = 0.250 \text{ kg·m}^2$$

**Initial angular momentum** (only disk 1 contributes since disk 2 is at rest):
$$L_i = I_1\omega_0 = (0.240)(8.00) = 1.92 \text{ kg·m}^2/\text{s}$$

**Final angular momentum** (combined rigid body):
$$L_f = I_{\text{total}}\omega_f = (0.250)\omega_f$$

**Conservation $L_i = L_f$:**
$$\omega_f = \frac{1.92}{0.250} = 7.68 \text{ rad/s}$$

The integral form $L = \int \vec{r} \times \vec{v}\, dm$ confirms that the total angular momentum of the coupled system is unchanged by the internal frictional impulse during the brief slip-coupling phase. Option A (6.40 rad/s) is the common mistake of using $L_i/I_1 = 8.00$, ignoring the additional moment of inertia from the second disk.
