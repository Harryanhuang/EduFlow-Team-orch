A yo-yo of total mass $M = 0.100$ kg consists of two identical solid disks of radius $R = 0.0500$ m, connected by a light axle of radius $r = 0.0100$ m. The combined moment of inertia about the central axis is $I = MR^2 = 2.50 \times 10^{-4}$ kg·m$^2$. A string is wound around the axle; the free end is held fixed as the yo-yo is released from rest. The string unwinds without slipping. What is the magnitude of the angular acceleration $\alpha$ of the yo-yo?

## Options
A) $50.3$ rad/s$^2$
B) $37.7$ rad/s$^2$
C) $24.5$ rad/s$^2$
D) $63.0$ rad/s$^2$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The yo-yo falls vertically while the string unwinds from the axle. The tension $T$ in the string exerts a torque about the center of mass via the axle radius $r$.

**Translational (Newton's second law, down positive):**
$$Mg - T = Ma$$
where $M = 0.100$ kg is the total mass of the yo-yo.

**Rotational (torque about the center of mass):**
The tension $T$ acts at the axle radius $r$, producing
$$\tau = Tr = I\alpha$$

**Moment of inertia:** The combined moment for two solid disks about the central axis is
$$I = MR^2 = (0.100\ \text{kg})(0.0500\ \text{m})^2 = 2.50 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{kg·m}^2$$

**Kinematic constraint (no slipping, calculus-based):** The linear distance descended $s$ relates to the angle turned $\theta$ by $s = r\theta$ (arc length at the axle radius). Differentiating:
$$v = \frac{ds}{dt} = r\frac{d\theta}{dt} = r\omega, \qquad a = \frac{dv}{dt} = r\frac{d\omega}{dt} = r\alpha$$

**Solve the coupled system:** From the rotational equation,
$$T = \frac{I\alpha}{r} = \frac{I(a/r)}{r} = \frac{Ia}{r^2}$$

Substitute into the translational equation:
$$Mg - \frac{Ia}{r^2} = Ma$$
$$Mg = a\left(M + \frac{I}{r^2}\right)$$
$$a = \frac{Mg}{M + I/r^2}$$

Compute $I/r^2$:
$$\frac{I}{r^2} = \frac{2.50 \times 10^{-4}}{(0.0100)^2} = \frac{2.50 \times 10^{-4}}{1.00 \times 10^{-4}} = 2.50\ \text{kg}$$

Then:
$$a = \frac{(0.100)(9.80)}{0.100 + 2.50} = \frac{0.980}{2.60} = 0.377\ \text{m/s}^2$$

Convert to angular acceleration:
$$\alpha = \frac{a}{r} = \frac{0.377}{0.0100} = 37.7\ \text{rad/s}^2$$

**Answer: B** — $37.7$ rad/s$^2$.

Option A ($50.3$ rad/s$^2$) is the result of mistakenly using the disk radius $R = 0.0500$ m instead of the axle radius $r = 0.0100$ m in the torque equation, giving $\tau = TR$ instead of $\tau = Tr$. Since $R/r = 5$, this error inflates $\alpha$ by roughly the same factor. Option C ($24.5$ rad/s$^2$) equals $\frac12 g$, a free-fall confusion. Option D ($63.0$ rad/s$^2$) neglects the moment-of-inertia correction in the denominator.
