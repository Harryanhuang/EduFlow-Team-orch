A thin uniform rod of length $L = 1.50$ m and mass $M = 3.00$ kg rotates about an axis perpendicular to the rod through **one end** of the rod. What is its moment of inertia?

## Options
A) $1.50\ \text{kg}\cdot\text{m}^2$
B) $2.25\ \text{kg}\cdot\text{m}^2$
C) $3.38\ \text{kg}\cdot\text{m}^2$
D) $0.750\ \text{kg}\cdot\text{m}^2$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For a thin rod of uniform linear density $\lambda = M/L$, each infinitesimal segment $dr$ at distance $r$ from the rotation axis has mass:

$$dm = \lambda\,dr = \frac{M}{L}\,dr$$

The moment of inertia is the integral of $r^2 dm$:

$$I = \int r^2\,dm = \int_0^L r^2 \cdot \frac{M}{L}\,dr = \frac{M}{L} \int_0^L r^2\,dr$$

Evaluate the integral:

$$\int_0^L r^2\,dr = \left[ \frac{r^3}{3} \right]_0^L = \frac{L^3}{3}$$

Therefore:

$$I = \frac{M}{L} \cdot \frac{L^3}{3} = \frac{1}{3}ML^2$$

Substitute the given values:

$$I = \frac{1}{3}(3.00\ \text{kg})(1.50\ \text{m})^2 = \frac{1}{3}(3.00)(2.25) = 2.25\ \text{kg}\cdot\text{m}^2$$

The moment of inertia is $2.25\ \text{kg}\cdot\text{m}^2$, corresponding to choice **B**.

Note: Be careful not to confuse this with the rod rotating about its center, where $I = \frac{1}{12}ML^2 = 0.1875\ \text{kg}\cdot\text{m}^2$ — a much smaller value because mass is distributed closer to the axis.
