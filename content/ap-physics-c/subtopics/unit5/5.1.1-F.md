A uniform rod of length $L = 2.00$ m and weight $W = 10.0$ N is hinged at one end and supported by a horizontal rope at the other end, making a $30.0°$ angle with the rod. What is the tension in the rope? (The rope pulls perpendicular to the rod.)

## Options
A) $T = 2.50$ N
B) $T = 4.33$ N
C) $T = 5.00$ N
D) $T = 8.66$ N

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Treat the rod's weight as a force of $W = 10.0$ N concentrated at its center ($L/2 = 1.00$ m from the hinge), or equivalently as a distributed load along the rod.

**Using the distributed-force principle (calculus-based):**

Each infinitesimal segment $dr$ at distance $r$ from the hinge carries load $dF = (W/L)\,dr$. The contribution to torque from this segment about the hinge is $d\tau = r \cdot dF$ (since the rope force is perpendicular).

Let $T$ be the tension in the rope pulling at $r = L$ perpendicular to the rod. Equilibrium about the hinge requires zero net torque:

$$\tau_{\text{net}} = T \cdot L - \int_0^L r \cdot \frac{W}{L}\,dr = 0$$

Evaluate the integral:

$$\int_0^L r \cdot \frac{W}{L}\,dr = \frac{W}{L}\cdot \frac{L^2}{2} = \frac{WL}{2}$$

So:

$$T \cdot L = \frac{WL}{2} \implies T = \frac{W}{2} = \frac{10.0}{2} = 5.00 \text{ N}$$

The tension in the rope is $T = 5.00$ N. Note: the $30.0°$ angle is irrelevant when the rope pulls perpendicular to the rod — this is the calculus version of the standard torque-equilibrium problem, where we explicitly integrate $d\tau = r dF$ rather than treating the weight as a point force.
