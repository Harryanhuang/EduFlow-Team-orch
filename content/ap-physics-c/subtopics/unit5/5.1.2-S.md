A solid cylinder of mass $M = 3.00$ kg and radius $R = 0.200$ m rolls **without slipping** down an incline of angle $\theta = 30.0^\circ$. What is the magnitude of its linear acceleration down the incline?

## Options
A) $2.45$ m/s$^2$
B) $4.90$ m/s$^2$
C) $3.27$ m/s$^2$
D) $1.63$ m/s$^2$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
For a rigid body rolling without slipping down an incline, the no-slip constraint couples the translational and rotational motion. Let the $+x$-axis point down the incline.

**Translational (Newton's second law):**
$$Mg\sin\theta - f = Ma$$
where $f$ is the static friction force (up the incline).

**Rotational (torque about the center of mass):**
Only the friction force $f$ exerts a torque about the center,
$$\tau_{\text{net}} = fR = I\alpha$$
where $\alpha$ is the angular acceleration.

**Kinematic constraint (no slipping):** For rolling without slipping, the point of contact is instantaneously at rest, giving
$$a = R\alpha$$
This is the calculus-based relation: the arc length traveled is $s = R\theta$, so the linear speed is $v = \frac{ds}{dt} = R\frac{d\theta}{dt} = R\omega$, and acceleration is $a = \frac{dv}{dt} = R\frac{d\omega}{dt} = R\alpha$.

**Moment of inertia:** For a solid cylinder about its central axis,
$$I = \tfrac12 MR^2$$

**Eliminate $f$ and $\alpha$:** From the rotational equation,
$$f = \frac{I\alpha}{R} = \frac{I(a/R)}{R} = \frac{Ia}{R^2}$$

Substitute into the translational equation:
$$Mg\sin\theta - \frac{Ia}{R^2} = Ma$$

Solve for $a$:
$$a = \frac{Mg\sin\theta}{M + I/R^2} = \frac{g\sin\theta}{1 + I/(MR^2)}$$

With $I = \frac12 MR^2$:
$$\frac{I}{MR^2} = \frac{\frac12 MR^2}{MR^2} = \frac12$$

Thus:
$$a = \frac{g\sin\theta}{1 + \frac12} = \frac23 g\sin\theta$$

Substitute numbers:
$$a = \frac23 (9.80\ \text{m/s}^2)(\sin 30.0^\circ) = \frac23 (9.80)(0.500)$$
$$a = \frac23 (4.90) = 3.27\ \text{m/s}^2$$

The frictionless sliding case $a = g\sin\theta = 4.90$ m/s$^2$ (option B) is the common distractor â€” it ignores rotational inertia entirely. Option A ($2.45$ m/s$^2$) equals $\frac12 g\sin\theta$, and option D ($1.63$ m/s$^2$) equals $\frac13 g\sin\theta$, arising from incorrect moment-of-inertia factors.
