A rod of mass $M = 2.00$ kg and length $L = 3.00$ m is hinged at $r = 0$. The gravitational load intensity along the rod varies as $w(r) = w_0(r/L)$, where $w_0$ is set so that the total weight equals $Mg$. A horizontal force $F$ acts perpendicular to the rod at the free end ($r = L$) to maintain horizontal equilibrium. Find $F$.

## Options
A) $F = 13.1$ N
B) $F = 6.53$ N
C) $F = 19.6$ N
D) $F = 9.80$ N

## Answer
A

## Explanation
**Total mass constraint:** The load intensity $w(r) = w_0(r/L)$ must satisfy
$$Mg = \int_0^L w(r)\,dr = \int_0^L w_0\frac{r}{L}\,dr = \frac{w_0}{L}\cdot\frac{L^2}{2} = \frac{w_0L}{2}$$

Solving: $w_0 = \frac{2Mg}{L} = \frac{2(2.00)(9.80)}{3.00} = 13.07$ N/m.

**Torque from the distributed load (the key integral):**
$$\tau_w = \int_0^L r\,w(r)\,dr = \int_0^L r\cdot w_0\frac{r}{L}\,dr = \frac{w_0}{L}\int_0^L r^2\,dr = \frac{w_0L^2}{3}$$

Substituting $w_0 = 2Mg/L$:
$$\tau_w = \frac{(2Mg/L)L^2}{3} = \frac{2MgL}{3} = \frac{2(2.00)(9.80)(3.00)}{3} = 39.2 \text{ N·m}$$

**Equilibrium:** $\tau_F = F\cdot L = \tau_w$, so
$$F = \frac{\tau_w}{L} = \frac{2Mg}{3} = \frac{2(2.00)(9.80)}{3} = 13.07 \text{ N} \approx 13.1 \text{ N}$$

For comparison: a uniform load $w(r) = Mg/L$ gives $F = Mg/2 = 9.80$ N (option D), which is the common mistake of assuming the center of force is at $L/2$ regardless of distribution. With the load concentrated toward the free end, $w(r) \propto r$, the effective center of pressure shifts to $r_{\text{eff}} = \tau_w/(Mg) = 2L/3$, requiring $F = (Mg \cdot 2L/3)/L = 2Mg/3$.
