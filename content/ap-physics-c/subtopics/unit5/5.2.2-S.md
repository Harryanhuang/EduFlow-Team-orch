A figure skater performs a spin. With her arms extended, her moment of inertia is \(I_1 = 3.50\text{ kg·m}^2\) and her angular velocity is \(\omega_1 = 1.50\text{ rad/s}\). She then pulls her arms in, reducing her moment of inertia to \(I_2 = 1.20\text{ kg·m}^2\). Assuming no external torques act on the skater, what is her final angular velocity \(\omega_2\)?

- A) \(2.62\text{ rad/s}\)
- B) \(5.25\text{ rad/s}\)
- C) \(4.38\text{ rad/s}\)
- D) \(3.00\text{ rad/s}\)

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Conservation of angular momentum applies because the only torques are internal to the skater's body. Newton's second law for rotation gives $\tau_{\text{ext}} = dL/dt$; with $\tau_{\text{ext}} = 0$, $L$ is constant.

$$L_1 = L_2 \quad\Rightarrow\quad I_1\omega_1 = I_2\omega_2$$

$$\omega_2 = \frac{I_1\omega_1}{I_2} = \frac{(3.50)(1.50)}{1.20} = \frac{5.25}{1.20} = 4.375 \text{ rad/s} \approx 4.38 \text{ rad/s}$$

Although kinetic energy is not conserved (the skater does work pulling in her arms), angular momentum is strictly conserved. Option B (5.25 rad/s) is the common mistake of dividing $I_1\omega_1$ by $\omega_1$ instead of $I_2$.
