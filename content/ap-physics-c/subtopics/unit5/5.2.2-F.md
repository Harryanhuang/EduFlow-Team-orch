A solid disk of mass \(M = 4.00\text{ kg}\) and radius \(R = 0.500\text{ m}\) rotates about its central axis with a constant angular velocity \(\omega = 6.00\text{ rad/s}\). What is the magnitude of the angular momentum of the disk?

- A) \(1.50\text{ kg·m}^2/\text{s}\)
- B) \(6.00\text{ kg·m}^2/\text{s}\)
- C) \(3.00\text{ kg·m}^2/\text{s}\)
- D) \(12.0\text{ kg·m}^2/\text{s}\)

## Answer
C

## Explanation
For a solid disk, the moment of inertia is $I = \tfrac{1}{2}MR^2$. With $M = 4.00$ kg and $R = 0.500$ m:

$$I = \tfrac{1}{2}(4.00)(0.500)^2 = \tfrac{1}{2}(4.00)(0.250) = 0.500 \text{ kg·m}^2$$

The angular momentum is $L = I\omega$:

$$L = (0.500 \text{ kg·m}^2)(6.00 \text{ rad/s}) = 3.00 \text{ kg·m}^2/\text{s}$$

The general integral form $L = \int r^2 \omega\, dm$ confirms this: since $\omega$ is uniform across the rigid body, $L = \omega \int r^2\, dm = \omega I$.
