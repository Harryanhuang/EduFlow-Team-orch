A solid disk of mass $M = 2.00$ kg and radius $R = 1.00$ m rotates freely about a horizontal axle through its center. A mass $m = 1.00$ kg hangs from a rope wound around the rim of the disk. The system starts from rest. What is the angular acceleration of the disk?

## Options
A) $\alpha = 4.91$ rad/s$^2$
B) $\alpha = 9.80$ rad/s$^2$
C) $\alpha = 2.45$ rad/s$^2$
D) $\alpha = 3.27$ rad/s$^2$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
**Step 1: Write Newton's second law for the hanging mass** (positive downward)

$$mg - T = ma$$

where $T$ is the rope tension and $a$ is the linear acceleration of the mass downward.

**Step 2: Write the rotational dynamics equation for the disk**

For a solid disk, $I = \frac{1}{2}MR^2$. The tension $T$ provides a tangential torque:
$$\tau = TR = I\alpha$$

**Step 3: Apply the rolling-winding constraint**

The rope unwinds from the rim without slipping, so the linear acceleration of the mass equals the tangential acceleration of the rim:
$$a = R\alpha$$

**Step 4: Eliminate $T$ and $a$ to solve for $\alpha$**

From the torque equation: $T = \frac{I\alpha}{R} = \frac{(1/2)MR^2\alpha}{R} = \frac{1}{2}MR\alpha$

From the force equation: $mg - \frac{1}{2}MR\alpha = mR\alpha$

$$mg = \left(m + \frac{M}{2}\right)R\alpha$$

$$\alpha = \frac{mg}{R\left(m + M/2\right)} = \frac{(1.00)(9.80)}{(1.00)\left(1.00 + 2.00/2\right)} = \frac{9.80}{2.00} = 4.90 \text{ rad/s}^2 \approx 4.91 \text{ rad/s}^2$$

The tension is $T = \frac{1}{2}MR\alpha = \frac{1}{2}(2.00)(4.91) = 4.91$ N, which is less than $mg = 9.80$ N as expected — the hanging mass accelerates downward, so $T < mg$.
