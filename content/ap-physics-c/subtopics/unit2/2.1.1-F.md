A particle of mass $m = 2$ kg is subject to a time-dependent force $F(t) = 6t^2 - 4t$ N. The particle starts from rest at $t = 0$. What is the particle's velocity at $t = 3$ s?

## Options
A) $v(3) = 10$ m/s
B) $v(3) = 18$ m/s
C) $v(3) = 27$ m/s
D) $v(3) = 36$ m/s

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Newton's second law in calculus form:

$$F(t) = m\frac{dv}{dt} \implies \frac{dv}{dt} = \frac{F(t)}{m}$$

$$\frac{dv}{dt} = \frac{6t^2 - 4t}{2} = 3t^2 - 2t$$

Integrate with respect to time, using $v(0) = 0$:

$$v(t) = \int_0^t (3t'^2 - 2t')\, dt' = \left[t'^3 - t'^2\right]_0^t = t^3 - t^2$$

At $t = 3$ s: $v(3) = 27 - 9 = 18$ m/s.

The calculus-based approach: force determines the rate of change of velocity ($dv/dt$), and integration over time recovers the velocity function. The correct answer is **B**.
