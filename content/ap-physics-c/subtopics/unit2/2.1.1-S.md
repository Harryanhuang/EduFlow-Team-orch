A $0.50$ kg mass is acted upon by a force $F(t) = 4\sin(2t)$ N. The mass starts from rest at $t = 0$. What is the velocity at $t = \pi/4$ s?

## Options
A) $v \approx 1.66$ m/s
B) $v \approx 2.83$ m/s
C) $v = 4.00$ m/s
D) $v \approx 5.66$ m/s

## Answer
C

## Explanation
From Newton's second law:

$$\frac{dv}{dt} = \frac{F(t)}{m} = \frac{4\sin(2t)}{0.5} = 8\sin(2t)$$

Integrate with respect to time, starting from rest:

$$v(t) = \int_0^t 8\sin(2t')\, dt' = 8\left[-\frac{\cos(2t')}{2}\right]_0^t = -4\cos(2t) + 4\cos(0) = 4 - 4\cos(2t)$$

$$v(t) = 4(1 - \cos(2t))$$

At $t = \pi/4$ s: $2t = \pi/2$ and $\cos(\pi/2) = 0$.

$$v(\pi/4) = 4(1 - 0) = 4.00 \text{ m/s}$$

The calculus approach: the time-varying sinusoidal force produces an acceleration $a(t) = 8\sin(2t)$, which is integrated to give velocity. At the instant $t = \pi/4$, the velocity reaches $4.00$ m/s. The correct answer is **C**.
