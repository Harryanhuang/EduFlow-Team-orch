A block of mass $m = 3$ kg is pulled across a rough horizontal surface by a horizontal force $F = 20$ N. The coefficient of kinetic friction is $\mu_k = 0.25$. What is the block's acceleration? (Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².)

## Options
A) $a = 2.78$ m/s²
B) $a = 3.60$ m/s²
C) $a = 4.22$ m/s²
D) $a = 6.67$ m/s²

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Since the applied force is purely horizontal, the normal force equals the weight:

$$F_N = mg = 3(9.8) = 29.4 \text{ N}$$

The kinetic friction force opposes the motion:

$$f_k = \mu_k F_N = 0.25(29.4) = 7.35 \text{ N}$$

The net horizontal force is:

$$F_{net} = F - f_k = 20 - 7.35 = 12.65 \text{ N}$$

Applying Newton's second law:

$$a = \frac{F_{net}}{m} = \frac{12.65}{3} \approx 4.22 \text{ m/s}^2$$

The block accelerates at approximately 4.22 m/s². The correct answer is **C**.
