A $0.50$ kg object falls from rest through a medium with quadratic drag $F_{drag} = -cv^2$ where $c = 0.010$ kg/m. What is the terminal velocity, and what is the speed after falling for $t = 2.0$ s? (Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².)

## Options
A) $v_T = 31.3$ m/s; $v(2.0) = 14.5$ m/s
B) $v_T = 22.1$ m/s; $v(2.0) = 15.7$ m/s
C) $v_T = 31.3$ m/s; $v(2.0) = 8.72$ m/s
D) $v_T = 22.1$ m/s; $v(2.0) = 8.72$ m/s

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Newton's second law with quadratic drag (downward positive):

$$m\frac{dv}{dt} = mg - cv^2$$

Terminal velocity when $dv/dt = 0$: $v_T = \sqrt{\frac{mg}{c}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.50(9.8)}{0.010}} = \sqrt{\frac{4.9}{0.010}} = \sqrt{490} \approx 22.1$ m/s.

Solve the ODE by separation of variables:

$$\frac{dv}{g - \frac{c}{m}v^2} = dt \implies \int_0^v \frac{dv'}{g(1 - v'^2/v_T^2)} = \int_0^t dt'$$
$$\frac{v_T}{g}\int_0^{v/v_T} \frac{du}{1 - u^2} = t \implies \frac{v_T}{g}\tanh^{-1}\left(\frac{v}{v_T}\right) = t$$
$$v(t) = v_T\tanh\left(\frac{gt}{v_T}\right)$$

At $t = 2.0$ s:

$$\frac{gt}{v_T} = \frac{9.8(2.0)}{22.1} = \frac{19.6}{22.1} \approx 0.886$$

$$\tanh(0.886) = \frac{e^{0.886} - e^{-0.886}}{e^{0.886} + e^{-0.886}} = \frac{2.425 - 0.412}{2.425 + 0.412} = \frac{2.013}{2.837} \approx 0.709$$

$$v(2.0) = 22.1 \times 0.709 \approx 15.7 \text{ m/s}$$

The terminal velocity is about 22.1 m/s, and after 2 seconds the speed is about 15.7 m/s — roughly 71% of terminal velocity. The correct answer is **B**.

Compare with linear drag: for the same $v_T = 22.1$ m/s, linear drag would give $v(2) = 22.1(1 - e^{-9.8t/22.1}) = 22.1(1 - e^{-0.886}) = 22.1(0.588) \approx 13.0$ m/s. Quadratic drag reaches a higher fraction of $v_T$ at the same time because the drag force grows more slowly at low speeds ($v^2$ vs $v$).
