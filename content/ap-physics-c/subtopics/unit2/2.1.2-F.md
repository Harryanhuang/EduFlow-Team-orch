Two blocks of masses $m_1 = 3$ kg and $m_2 = 5$ kg are connected by a massless string over a frictionless pulley (Atwood machine). Block $m_1$ hangs on the left ($x$ decreases) and block $m_2$ hangs on the right ($x$ increases). Find the acceleration of the system and the tension in the string.

## Options
A) $a = 2.45$ m/s²; $T = 36.8$ N
B) $a = 2.45$ m/s²; $T = 29.4$ N
C) $a = 4.90$ m/s²; $T = 29.4$ N
D) $a = 4.90$ m/s²; $T = 36.8$ N

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Apply Newton's second law to each mass. The heavier mass ($m_2 = 5$ kg) accelerates downward, the lighter mass ($m_1 = 3$ kg) accelerates upward, with the same magnitude $a$.

For $m_1$ (accelerating upward):
$$T - m_1g = m_1a \implies T - 3(9.8) = 3a \implies T = 3a + 29.4$$

For $m_2$ (accelerating downward):
$$m_2g - T = m_2a \implies 5(9.8) - T = 5a \implies T = 49 - 5a$$

Equate the two expressions for $T$:

$$3a + 29.4 = 49 - 5a \implies 8a = 19.6 \implies a = 2.45 \text{ m/s}^2$$

Substitute back: $T = 3(2.45) + 29.4 = 7.35 + 29.4 = 36.75$ N.

Check: $T = 49 - 5(2.45) = 49 - 12.25 = 36.75$ N. Consistent.

The acceleration is $a = 2.45$ m/s² and tension is $T \approx 36.8$ N. The correct answer is **A**.
