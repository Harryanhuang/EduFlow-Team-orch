In a double Atwood machine, masses $m_1 = 2$ kg and $m_2 = 2$ kg hang from the left and right sides of a movable pulley of negligible mass. The movable pulley itself hangs from a fixed pulley via a string with a counterweight $m_3 = 3$ kg. The rope length constraint gives $a_1 + a_2 + 2a_3 = 0$, where $a_3$ is the acceleration of the movable pulley (equal to the acceleration of $m_3$). What is the acceleration of the counterweight $m_3$? (Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².)

## Options
A) $a_3 = 0$ m/s²
B) $a_3 = 0.70$ m/s²
C) $a_3 = 1.40$ m/s²
D) $a_3 = 2.80$ m/s²

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Let all accelerations be positive upward. The unknowns are $a_1, a_2, a_3, T, T_0$.

**Equations of motion:**
(1) $m_1 = 2$ kg: $T_0 - m_1g = m_1a_1 \implies T_0 - 19.6 = 2a_1$
(2) $m_2 = 2$ kg: $T_0 - m_2g = m_2a_2 \implies T_0 - 19.6 = 2a_2$
(3) $m_3 = 3$ kg: $T - m_3g = m_3a_3 \implies T - 29.4 = 3a_3$
(4) Movable pulley (massless): $T = 2T_0$
(5) Rope-length constraint: $a_1 + a_2 + 2a_3 = 0$

From (4) into (3): $2T_0 - 29.4 = 3a_3 \implies T_0 = 14.7 + 1.5a_3$

From (1) and (2) (since $m_1 = m_2$): $a_1 = a_2$ and $2a_1 = T_0 - 19.6 \implies a_1 = a_2 = \frac{T_0 - 19.6}{2}$

Substitute $T_0 = 14.7 + 1.5a_3$:
$$a_1 = a_2 = \frac{14.7 + 1.5a_3 - 19.6}{2} = \frac{1.5a_3 - 4.9}{2} = 0.75a_3 - 2.45$$

Constraint (5):
$$(0.75a_3 - 2.45) + (0.75a_3 - 2.45) + 2a_3 = 0$$
$$0.75a_3 + 0.75a_3 + 2a_3 - 2.45 - 2.45 = 0$$
$$3.5a_3 = 4.90$$
$$a_3 = \frac{4.90}{3.5} = 1.40 \text{ m/s}^2$$

The counterweight $m_3 = 3$ kg accelerates upward because the combined mass ($m_1 + m_2 = 4$ kg) below the movable pulley exceeds $m_3$, but the pulley geometry means $m_3$ moves at half the rate of the net imbalance. The correct answer is **C**.

Alternatively, using the derived formula for the double Atwood machine:
$$a_3 = g \cdot \frac{4m_1m_2 - m_3(m_1+m_2)}{m_3(m_1+m_2) + 4m_1m_2} = 9.8 \cdot \frac{16 - 12}{12 + 16} = 9.8 \cdot \frac{4}{28} = \frac{9.8}{7} \approx 1.40 \text{ m/s}^2$$
