A $2.00\,\text{kg}$ block oscillates on a spring with $k = 50.0\,\text{N/m}$ and amplitude $A = 0.300\,\text{m}$. What is the kinetic energy when the block is at $x = 0.150\,\text{m}$?

## Options
A) $0.563\,\text{J}$
B) $2.25\,\text{J}$
C) $1.69\,\text{J}$
D) $0.562\,\text{J}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The total energy is $E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2 = \frac{1}{2}(50.0)(0.300)^2 = \frac{1}{2}(50.0)(0.0900) = 2.25\,\text{J}$.

The potential energy at $x = 0.150\,\text{m}$ is:

$$U(x) = \frac{1}{2}kx^2 = \frac{1}{2}(50.0)(0.150)^2 = \frac{1}{2}(50.0)(0.0225) = 0.5625\,\text{J}$$

By conservation of energy, the kinetic energy is:

$$K = E - U = 2.25 - 0.5625 = 1.6875\,\text{J} \approx 1.69\,\text{J}$$

Alternatively, using calculus: $U(x) = -\int_0^x F\,dx' = -\int_0^x (-kx')\,dx' = k\int_0^x x'\,dx' = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$, confirming the potential energy.

Option A is the potential energy $U$, not the kinetic energy. Option B is the total energy $E$, not accounting for the remaining potential. Option D is a rounding error.

The answer is **C**.
