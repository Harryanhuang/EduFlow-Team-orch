A mass-spring system has spring constant $k = 100\,\text{N/m}$ and amplitude $A = 0.200\,\text{m}$. What is the total mechanical energy of the system?

## Options
A) $2.00\,\text{J}$
B) $0.50\,\text{J}$
C) $20.0\,\text{J}$
D) $4.00\,\text{J}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The total mechanical energy of an undamped SHM oscillator is conserved and equals the maximum potential energy stored in the spring:

$$E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2 = \frac{1}{2}(100)(0.200)^2 = \frac{1}{2}(100)(0.0400) = \frac{1}{2}(4.00) = 2.00\,\text{J}$$

Option B (0.50 J) results from using $E = ½kA$ instead of $½kA^2$. Option C (20.0 J) results from forgetting the factor of ½ or squaring incorrectly. Option D (4.00 J) is the value of $kA^2$ without the factor of ½.

The answer is **A**.
