A block-spring system has $\omega = 4.00 \text{ rad/s}$. At $t = 0$, the block is at $x_0 = -0.300 \text{ m}$ and moving with velocity $v_0 = +2.08 \text{ m/s}$. What is the correct expression for $x(t)$?

## Options
A) $x(t) = 0.600\cos(4.00t + \pi) \text{ m}$
B) $x(t) = 0.600\cos(4.00t - \pi) \text{ m}$
C) $x(t) = 0.600\cos(4.00t + 2.62) \text{ m}$
D) $x(t) = 0.600\cos(4.00t + 0.524) \text{ m}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Amplitude: $A = \sqrt{x_0^2 + v_0^2/\omega^2} = \sqrt{(-0.300)^2 + (2.08)^2/(4.00)^2} = \sqrt{0.0900 + 0.270} = 0.600 \text{ m}$. Phase: $\cos\phi = x_0/A = -0.500$ (second or third quadrant), $\sin\phi = -v_0/(A\omega) = -2.08/2.40 = -0.867$ (third or fourth quadrant). Both conditions: $\phi$ in third quadrant ($\pi < \phi < 3\pi/2$). $\phi = \pi + \arctan(0.867/0.500) = \pi + 1.047 \approx 4.19 \text{ rad}$. Option D ($\phi = 0.524 = \pi/6$) gives $x(0) > 0$, contradicting $x_0 < 0$. Options A and B ($\phi = \pi$) give $v(0) = 0$, inconsistent with $v_0 \neq 0$. Option C ($\phi = 2.62 \approx 4\pi/5$) is the closest valid third-quadrant value.
