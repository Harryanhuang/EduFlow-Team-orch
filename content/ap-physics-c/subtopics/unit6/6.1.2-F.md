A block-spring system oscillates with $\omega = 8.00\,\text{rad/s}$ on a frictionless horizontal surface. At $t = 0$, the block is pulled to $x = 0.250\,\text{m}$ and released from rest. What is the amplitude?

## Options
A) $0.125\,\text{m}$
B) $0.250\,\text{m}$
C) $0.500\,\text{m}$
D) $2.00\,\text{m}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
From the initial conditions:

$$v(0) = -A\omega\sin\phi = 0 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \sin\phi = 0 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \phi = 0$$

$$x(0) = A\cos(0) = A = 0.250\,\text{m}$$

When the block is released from rest, all energy is potential at $t = 0$ and the block is at its turning point (maximum displacement). The amplitude equals the initial displacement exactly.

The answer is **B**.
