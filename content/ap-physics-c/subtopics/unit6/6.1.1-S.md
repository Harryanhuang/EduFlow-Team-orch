A block of mass $m = 0.200\,\text{kg}$ is attached to a horizontal spring with force constant $k = 80.0\,\text{N/m}$ and oscillates on a frictionless surface. What is the angular frequency $\omega$ of the oscillation?

## Options
A) $10.0\,\text{rad/s}$
B) $20.0\,\text{rad/s}$
C) $400\,\text{rad/s}$
D) $6.28\,\text{rad/s}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
From Newton's second law for the spring:

$$m\,\frac{d^2x}{dt^2} = -kx \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \frac{d^2x}{dt^2} + \frac{k}{m}\,x = 0$$

Comparing with $\frac{d^2x}{dt^2} + \omega^2 x = 0$:

$$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} = \sqrt{\frac{80.0}{0.200}} = \sqrt{400} = 20.0\,\text{rad/s}$$

Option A (10.0) results from an arithmetic error ($k/m = 400$, not 100). Option C (400) is $\omega^2$, not $\omega$ -- the common error of omitting the square root. Option D (6.28) is approximately $2\pi$, which is a period-related quantity, not the angular frequency.

The answer is **B**.
