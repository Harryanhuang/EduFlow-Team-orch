A small asteroid is at rest at a very large distance from Earth. It begins to fall toward Earth under gravity alone. What is the speed at which it strikes Earth's surface (ignoring atmospheric drag)? Earth mass $M_E = 5.97 \times 10^{24}\,\text{kg}$, Earth radius $R_E = 6.37 \times 10^6\,\text{m}$.

$G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\,\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$

## Options
A) $7.91 \times 10^3\,\text{m/s}$
B) $1.12 \times 10^4\,\text{m/s}$
C) $5.59 \times 10^3\,\text{m/s}$
D) $1.58 \times 10^4\,\text{m/s}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The asteroid starts at rest at infinity, so its initial total mechanical energy is zero:

$$E_i = K_i + U_i = 0 + 0 = 0$$

At the surface:

$$U_f = -\frac{GM_E m}{R_E}, \quad K_f = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

Energy conservation ($E_f = E_i$) gives:

$$\frac{1}{2}mv^2 - \frac{GM_E m}{R_E} = 0$$

$$\frac{1}{2}mv^2 = \frac{GM_E m}{R_E}$$

$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2GM_E}{R_E}}$$

This is exactly the escape velocity from Earth. Substituting:

$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(5.97 \times 10^{24})}{6.37 \times 10^6}} = \sqrt{\frac{7.96 \times 10^{14}}{6.37 \times 10^6}} = \sqrt{1.25 \times 10^8} = 1.12 \times 10^4\,\text{m/s}$$

Option A (7.91 × 10³) is the orbital speed $v = \sqrt{GM/R}$, which misses the factor of 2. Option C (5.59 × 10³) is half the correct value. Option D (1.58 × 10⁴) is $\sqrt{2}$ times the correct value, as if one used $v = \sqrt{4GM/R}$.

The answer is **B**.
