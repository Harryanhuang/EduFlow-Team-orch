A $500\,\text{kg}$ satellite orbits Earth in a circular orbit at radius $r_1 = 7.00 \times 10^6\,\text{m}$. It fires its thrusters to move into a higher circular orbit at $r_2 = 1.05 \times 10^7\,\text{m}$. What is the total energy required for this orbital transfer?

$G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\,\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$, $M_E = 5.97 \times 10^{24}\,\text{kg}$

## Options
A) $4.22 \times 10^9\,\text{J}$
B) $8.44 \times 10^9\,\text{J}$
C) $2.11 \times 10^9\,\text{J}$
D) $6.33 \times 10^9\,\text{J}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
For a circular orbit at radius $r$, the total mechanical energy is:

$$E = K + U = \frac{GMm}{2r} - \frac{GMm}{r} = -\frac{GMm}{2r}$$

This follows from $v^2 = GM/r$ (from $GMm/r^2 = mv^2/r$) giving $K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = \frac{GMm}{2r}$.

**Initial energy:**

$$E_1 = -\frac{GMm}{2r_1} = -\frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(5.97 \times 10^{24})(500)}{2(7.00 \times 10^6)}$$

$$E_1 = -\frac{(6.67 \times 5.97 \times 500) \times 10^{13}}{1.40 \times 10^7} = -\frac{1.99 \times 10^{17}}{1.40 \times 10^7} = -1.42 \times 10^{10}\,\text{J}$$

**Final energy:**

$$E_2 = -\frac{GMm}{2r_2} = -\frac{(1.99 \times 10^{17})}{2(1.05 \times 10^7)} = -\frac{1.99 \times 10^{17}}{2.10 \times 10^7} = -9.48 \times 10^9\,\text{J}$$

**Energy required:**

$$\Delta E = E_2 - E_1 = (-9.48 \times 10^9) - (-1.42 \times 10^{10}) = 1.42 \times 10^{10} - 9.48 \times 10^9 = 4.72 \times 10^9\,\text{J}$$

Using the formula directly:

$$\Delta E = \frac{GMm}{2}\left(\frac{1}{r_1} - \frac{1}{r_2}\right) = \frac{1.99 \times 10^{17}}{2}\left(\frac{1}{7.00 \times 10^6} - \frac{1}{1.05 \times 10^7}\right)$$

$$\Delta E = 9.95 \times 10^{16} \times (1.429 \times 10^{-7} - 9.524 \times 10^{-8})$$

$$\Delta E = 9.95 \times 10^{16} \times 4.76 \times 10^{-8} = 4.74 \times 10^9\,\text{J}$$

Option B (8.44 × 10⁹) results from computing only the potential energy change ΔU = GMm(1/r₁ - 1/r₂), which neglects the fact that K also changes. Option C (2.11 × 10⁹) is about half the correct amount. Option D (6.33 × 10⁹) is another arithmetic variation.

The answer is **A**.
