Two point masses, $M = 8.00 \times 10^{10} \text{ kg}$ and $m = 2.00 \times 10^{10} \text{ kg}$, are separated by $r = 4.00 \times 10^3 \text{ m}$. What is the gravitational potential energy of the system? ($G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N·m}^2/\text{kg}^2$)

## Options
A) $-2.67 \times 10^7 \text{ J}$
B) $+2.67 \times 10^7 \text{ J}$
C) $-1.33 \times 10^7 \text{ J}$
D) $-6.68 \times 10^6 \text{ J}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$U = -GMm/r = -(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(8.00 \times 10^{10})(2.00 \times 10^{10}) / (4.00 \times 10^3)$. Numerator: $6.67 \times 8.00 \times 2.00 = 106.72$; exponent: $10^{-11+10+10} = 10^9$. $U = -106.72 \times 10^9 / 4.00 \times 10^3 = -2.668 \times 10^7 \approx -2.67 \times 10^7 \text{ J}$. The negative sign indicates an attractive (bound) system.
