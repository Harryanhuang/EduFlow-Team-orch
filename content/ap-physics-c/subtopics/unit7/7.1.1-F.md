Two spheres of mass $M = 100\,\text{kg}$ and $m = 50.0\,\text{kg}$ have their centers separated by $r = 2.00\,\text{m}$. What is the magnitude of the gravitational force between them?

$G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\,\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$

## Options
A) $8.34 \times 10^{-8}\,\text{N}$
B) $1.67 \times 10^{-7}\,\text{N}$
C) $3.34 \times 10^{-7}\,\text{N}$
D) $6.67 \times 10^{-8}\,\text{N}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Applying Newton's law of universal gravitation:

$$F = \frac{GMm}{r^2} = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(100)(50.0)}{(2.00)^2}$$

$$F = \frac{6.67 \times 10^{-11} \times 5000}{4.00} = \frac{3.335 \times 10^{-7}}{4.00} = 8.34 \times 10^{-8}\,\text{N}$$

Option B (1.67 × 10⁻⁷) results from using $r = 1.00$ m (half the separation). Option C (3.34 × 10⁻⁷) is $GMm$ before dividing by $r^2$. Option D (6.67 × 10⁻⁸) comes from using $m = 100$ kg instead of $5000$ kg for the product $Mm$.

The answer is **A**.
