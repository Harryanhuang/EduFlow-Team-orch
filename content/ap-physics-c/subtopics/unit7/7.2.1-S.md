A satellite is placed in a circular orbit at a height $h = 3.60 \times 10^5\,\text{m}$ above Earth's surface. Earth's radius is $R_E = 6.37 \times 10^6\,\text{m}$ and its mass is $M_E = 5.97 \times 10^{24}\,\text{kg}$. Find the satellite's orbital speed.

$G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}\,\text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$

## Options
A) $7.91 \times 10^3\,\text{m/s}$
B) $7.68 \times 10^3\,\text{m/s}$
C) $1.12 \times 10^4\,\text{m/s}$
D) $5.59 \times 10^3\,\text{m/s}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The orbital radius from Earth's center is $r = R_E + h = 6.37 \times 10^6 + 0.36 \times 10^6 = 6.73 \times 10^6\,\text{m}$.

For a circular orbit, the gravitational force provides centripetal acceleration:

$$\frac{GM_E m}{r^2} = m\frac{v^2}{r}$$

Canceling the satellite mass $m$ and solving for $v$:

$$v = \sqrt{\frac{GM_E}{r}} = \sqrt{\frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(5.97 \times 10^{24})}{6.73 \times 10^6}}$$

$$v = \sqrt{\frac{3.98 \times 10^{14}}{6.73 \times 10^6}} = \sqrt{5.91 \times 10^7} = 7.69 \times 10^3\,\text{m/s}$$

Option A (7.91 × 10³) is the orbital speed at Earth's surface ($r = R_E$), not accounting for the orbital altitude. Option C (1.12 × 10⁴) is escape velocity $\sqrt{2GM/R_E}$, approximately $\sqrt{2}$ times the surface orbital speed. Option D (5.59 × 10³) results from a calculation error.

The answer is **B**.
