A projectile is launched from a cliff of height $h = 50$ m with initial speed $v_0 = 30$ m/s. The range function is $R(\theta) = (v_0\cos\theta)\left(\frac{v_0\sin\theta}{g} + \frac{\sqrt{(v_0\sin\theta)^2 + 2gh}}{g}\right)$. What is the approximate optimal launch angle that maximizes the range? (Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².)

## Options
A) $\theta \approx 30^\circ$
B) $\theta \approx 35^\circ$
C) $\theta \approx 40^\circ$
D) $\theta \approx 45^\circ$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The optimal launch angle solving $dR/d\theta = 0$ for non-zero $h$ is:

$$\theta_{opt} = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{v_0}{\sqrt{v_0^2 + 2gh}}\right)$$

Substituting $v_0 = 30$ m/s, $g = 9.8$ m/s², $h = 50$ m:

$$\theta_{opt} = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{30}{\sqrt{900 + 2(9.8)(50)}}\right) = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{30}{\sqrt{900 + 980}}\right) = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{30}{\sqrt{1880}}\right)$$
$$= \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{30}{43.36}\right) = \tan^{-1}(0.692) \approx 34.7^\circ$$

We can verify numerically:

For $\theta = 35^\circ$: $\sin 35^\circ \approx 0.574$, $\cos 35^\circ \approx 0.819$
$$t_f = \frac{30\sin 35^\circ + \sqrt{(30\sin 35^\circ)^2 + 2gh}}{g} = \frac{17.21 + \sqrt{296.2 + 980}}{9.8} = \frac{17.21 + 35.72}{9.8} = 5.40 \text{ s}$$
$$R(35^\circ) = (30\cos 35^\circ)(5.40) = 30(0.819)(5.40) = 132.7 \text{ m}$$

For $\theta = 40^\circ$: $R = 30(0.766)(5.72) = 131.4$ m.
For $\theta = 30^\circ$: $R = 30(0.866)(5.07) = 131.7$ m.

The $35^\circ$ launch produces the greatest range. This is less than the standard $45^\circ$ for flat ground because the extra height $h$ reduces the time needed to reach the ground, favoring a lower angle to maximize horizontal velocity. The correct answer is **B**.
