A particle starts from rest at $x = 0$ and has acceleration $a(t) = 6t - 4$ (m/s²). What is its position at $t = 3$ s?

## Options
A) $x(3) = 9$ m
B) $x(3) = 18$ m
C) $x(3) = 27$ m
D) $x(3) = 36$ m

## Answer
A

## Explanation
First, integrate acceleration to find velocity. Using $v(0) = 0$:

$$v(t) = \int_0^t (6t' - 4)\, dt' = 3t^2 - 4t$$

Next, integrate velocity to find position. Using $x(0) = 0$:

$$x(t) = \int_0^t (3t'^2 - 4t')\, dt' = t^3 - 2t^2$$

Evaluating at $t = 3$ s:

$$x(3) = (3)^3 - 2(3)^2 = 27 - 18 = 9 \text{ m}$$

The answer is $9$ m, which corresponds to option **A**.
