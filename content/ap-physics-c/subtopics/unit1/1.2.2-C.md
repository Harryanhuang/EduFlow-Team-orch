Particle A starts at the origin: $\vec{r}_A(0) = 0$, $\vec{v}_A = 2.00\hat{i} + 1.00\hat{j}$ m/s (constant). Particle B starts at $\vec{r}_B(0) = 4.00\hat{i}$ m, $\vec{v}_B = 1.00\hat{i} + 2.00\hat{j}$ m/s (constant). Find the time of closest approach and the minimum separation distance.

## Options
A) $t = 1.00$ s, $d_{min} = 2.33$ m
B) $t = 1.50$ s, $d_{min} = 1.50$ m
C) $t = 2.00$ s, $d_{min} = 2.83$ m
D) $t = 3.00$ s, $d_{min} = 3.16$ m

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The relative position function is:

$$\vec{r}_{B/A}(t) = \vec{r}_B(t) - \vec{r}_A(t) = [4.00\hat{i} + (1.00\hat{i} + 2.00\hat{j})t] - (2.00\hat{i} + 1.00\hat{j})t$$
$$= (4.00 - 1.00t)\hat{i} + 1.00t\hat{j}$$

The relative velocity is:

$$\vec{v}_{B/A} = \vec{v}_B - \vec{v}_A = (1.00 - 2.00)\hat{i} + (2.00 - 1.00)\hat{j} = -1.00\hat{i} + 1.00\hat{j}$$

The squared distance is $d^2 = |\vec{r}_{B/A}|^2$. Minimizing $d^2$ by setting its time-derivative to zero:

$$\frac{d}{dt}|\vec{r}_{B/A}|^2 = 2\vec{r}_{B/A} \cdot \frac{d\vec{r}_{B/A}}{dt} = 2\vec{r}_{B/A} \cdot \vec{v}_{B/A} = 0$$

This gives the perpendicularity condition:

$$\vec{r}_{B/A} \cdot \vec{v}_{B/A} = (4.00 - t)(-1.00) + t(1.00) = -4.00 + 2t = 0$$

Solving: $t = 2.00$ s.

At this time:

$$\vec{r}_{B/A}(2.00) = (4.00 - 2.00)\hat{i} + 2.00\hat{j} = 2.00\hat{i} + 2.00\hat{j}$$
$$d_{min} = |\vec{r}_{B/A}(2.00)| = \sqrt{2.00^2 + 2.00^2} = \sqrt{8.00} \approx 2.83 \text{ m}$$

The perpendicularity condition $\vec{r}_{B/A} \cdot \vec{v}_{B/A} = 0$ means the relative velocity has no component along the relative position vector at closest approach — the particles are neither approaching nor receding at that instant. The correct answer is **C**.
