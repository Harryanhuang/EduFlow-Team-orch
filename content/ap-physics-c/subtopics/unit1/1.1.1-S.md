A particle's velocity is given by $v(t) = 3t^2 - 6t$ (m/s), and at $t = 0$ its position is $x(0) = 4$ m. What is the particle's displacement from $t = 0$ to $t = 3$ s, and what is its position at $t = 3$ s?

## Options
A) Displacement $= 0$ m; $x(3) = 4$ m
B) Displacement $= 9$ m; $x(3) = 13$ m
C) Displacement $= -9$ m; $x(3) = -5$ m
D) Displacement $= 4$ m; $x(3) = 8$ m

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Displacement is the definite integral of velocity:

$$\Delta x = \int_0^3 (3t^2 - 6t)\, dt = \left[t^3 - 3t^2\right]_0^3 = (27 - 27) - 0 = 0 \text{ m}$$

The particle's position at $t = 3$ s is:

$$x(3) = x(0) + \Delta x = 4 + 0 = 4 \text{ m}$$

The particle returns to its starting position. Physically, it moves in the positive direction for $t < 2$ (since $v > 0$ when $0 < t < 2$), then reverses and moves in the negative direction for $t > 2$ (since $v < 0$ when $t > 2$), covering equal distances in each direction.
