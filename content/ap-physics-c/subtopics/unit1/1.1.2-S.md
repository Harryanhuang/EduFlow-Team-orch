A particle moves with velocity $v(t) = t^2 - 5t + 6$ (m/s). What are the particle's displacement and total distance traveled from $t = 1$ to $t = 4$ s?

## Options
A) Displacement $= \frac{3}{2}$ m; Distance $= \frac{11}{6}$ m
B) Displacement $= \frac{11}{6}$ m; Distance $= \frac{3}{2}$ m
C) Displacement $= \frac{3}{2}$ m; Distance $= \frac{13}{6}$ m
D) Displacement $= \frac{13}{6}$ m; Distance $= \frac{11}{6}$ m

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Factor $v(t) = t^2 - 5t + 6 = (t-2)(t-3)$. Zeros at $t = 2$ and $t = 3$ s. On $[1,4]$:
- $1 \le t < 2$: $v > 0$
- $2 < t < 3$: $v < 0$
- $3 < t \le 4$: $v > 0$

**Displacement** (net definite integral):

$$\Delta x = \int_1^4 (t^2 - 5t + 6)\, dt = \left[\frac{t^3}{3} - \frac{5t^2}{2} + 6t\right]_1^4$$

At $t=4$: $\frac{64}{3} - 40 + 24 = \frac{64}{3} - 16 = \frac{16}{3}$
At $t=1$: $\frac{1}{3} - \frac{5}{2} + 6 = \frac{2 - 15 + 36}{6} = \frac{23}{6}$

$$\Delta x = \frac{16}{3} - \frac{23}{6} = \frac{32 - 23}{6} = \frac{9}{6} = \frac{3}{2} \text{ m}$$

**Distance** (integral of $|v(t)|$, splitting at zeros):

$$\text{Distance} = \int_1^2 v\, dt + \int_2^3 (-v)\, dt + \int_3^4 v\, dt$$

Region $[1,2]$: $\left[\frac{t^3}{3} - \frac{5t^2}{2} + 6t\right]_1^2$

At $t=2$: $\frac{8}{3} - 10 + 12 = \frac{8}{3} + 2 = \frac{14}{3}$
Value: $\frac{14}{3} - \frac{23}{6} = \frac{28 - 23}{6} = \frac{5}{6}$

Region $[2,3]$: $\left[\frac{t^3}{3} - \frac{5t^2}{2} + 6t\right]_2^3$

At $t=3$: $9 - \frac{45}{2} + 18 = 27 - \frac{45}{2} = \frac{9}{2}$
Value: $\frac{9}{2} - \frac{14}{3} = \frac{27 - 28}{6} = -\frac{1}{6}$ (absolute: $\frac{1}{6}$)

Region $[3,4]$: $\left[\frac{t^3}{3} - \frac{5t^2}{2} + 6t\right]_3^4$

Value: $\frac{16}{3} - \frac{9}{2} = \frac{32 - 27}{6} = \frac{5}{6}$

$$\text{Distance} = \frac{5}{6} + \frac{1}{6} + \frac{5}{6} = \frac{11}{6} \text{ m}$$

Displacement $= \frac{3}{2}$ m, Distance $= \frac{11}{6}$ m. The correct answer is **A**.
