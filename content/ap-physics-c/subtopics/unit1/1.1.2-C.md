The velocity of a particle is given by the piecewise function:

$$v(t) = 
\begin{cases} 
2t^3 - 6t^2, & 0 \le t \le 2 \\[4pt]
8t + 4, & 2 < t \le 4 
\end{cases}$$

What is the instantaneous jerk $j = \frac{da}{dt}$ on the left side approaching $t = 2$ s, and is the acceleration continuous at $t = 2$ s?

## Options
A) $j = 12$ m/s³; acceleration is continuous at $t = 2$
B) $j = 12$ m/s³; acceleration is NOT continuous at $t = 2$
C) $j = 6$ m/s³; acceleration is continuous at $t = 2$
D) $j = 6$ m/s³; acceleration is NOT continuous at $t = 2$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
**Left side** ($t \to 2^-$): $v(t) = 2t^3 - 6t^2$

$$a(t) = \frac{dv}{dt} = 6t^2 - 12t$$
$$j(t) = \frac{da}{dt} = 12t - 12$$

At $t = 2^-$: $j(2^-) = 12(2) - 12 = 12$ m/s³.

**Right side** ($t \to 2^+$): $v(t) = 8t + 4$

$$a(t) = \frac{dv}{dt} = 8 \text{ m/s}^2$$

Now check acceleration continuity at $t = 2$:
$$a(2^-) = 6(2)^2 - 12(2) = 24 - 24 = 0 \text{ m/s}^2$$
$$a(2^+) = 8 \text{ m/s}^2$$

Since $a(2^-) \neq a(2^+)$, the acceleration has a jump discontinuity at $t = 2$ s. The jerk from the left is $12$ m/s³. The correct answer is **B**.
