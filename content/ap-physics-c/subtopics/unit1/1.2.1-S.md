A projectile is launched with parametric equations $x(t) = 25.0t$ and $y(t) = 34.3t - 4.90t^2$ (meters, $t$ in seconds). What is the speed of the projectile at $t = 2.00$ s?

## Options
A) $25.0$ m/s
B) $29.0$ m/s
C) $33.6$ m/s
D) $44.1$ m/s

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Differentiate $x(t)$ and $y(t)$ to find the velocity components:

$$v_x(t) = \frac{dx}{dt} = 25.0 \text{ m/s} \quad (\text{constant})$$
$$v_y(t) = \frac{dy}{dt} = 34.3 - 9.80t \text{ m/s}$$

At $t = 2.00$ s:
$$v_y(2.00) = 34.3 - 9.80(2.00) = 34.3 - 19.6 = 14.7 \text{ m/s}$$
$$v_x(2.00) = 25.0 \text{ m/s}$$

The speed is the magnitude of the instantaneous velocity vector:

$$v = |\vec{v}(2.00)| = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2} = \sqrt{(25.0)^2 + (14.7)^2} = \sqrt{625 + 216} = \sqrt{841} = 29.0 \text{ m/s}$$

The key calculus operations are:
1. $v_x = \dot{x} = 25.0$ (constant, since $a_x = d^2x/dt^2 = 0$)
2. $v_y = \dot{y} = 34.3 - 9.80t$ (first derivative of quadratic vertical position)
3. Speed $= \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2}$ (magnitude of the velocity vector)

The correct answer is **B**.
