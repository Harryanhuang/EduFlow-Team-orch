The velocity of a particle is given by $v(t) = 2t^3 - 8t$ (m/s). At which positive time does the particle change direction, and what is its acceleration at that instant?

## Options
A) $t = 2$ s; $a = 8$ m/s²
B) $t = 2$ s; $a = 16$ m/s²
C) $t = 2$ s; $a = 4$ m/s²
D) $t = 4$ s; $a = 16$ m/s²

## Answer
B

## Explanation
A particle changes direction when its velocity passes through zero (i.e., when the v(t) curve crosses the t-axis and changes sign).

Set $v(t) = 0$: $2t^3 - 8t = 2t(t^2 - 4) = 0$, giving $t = 0$ and $t = 2$ s. The positive non-zero root is $t = 2$ s.

Instantaneous acceleration is the derivative of velocity:

$$a(t) = \frac{dv}{dt} = 6t^2 - 8$$

At $t = 2$ s: $a(2) = 6(4) - 8 = 24 - 8 = 16$ m/s².

Graphically, the v(t) curve crosses from negative to positive at $t = 2$ s, and the slope of the tangent to the v(t) curve at that point is 16 m/s². The correct answer is **B**.
