A projectile is launched from ground level with initial speed $v_0 = 30$ m/s at an angle $\theta = 30^\circ$ above the horizontal. What is the magnitude of the velocity vector at $t = 2$ s? (Use $g = 9.8$ m/s².)

## Options
A) $|\vec{v}| \approx 18.7$ m/s
B) $|\vec{v}| \approx 22.1$ m/s
C) $|\vec{v}| \approx 26.3$ m/s
D) $|\vec{v}| \approx 30.0$ m/s

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The parametric equations are:

$$x(t) = (v_0\cos\theta)t, \quad y(t) = (v_0\sin\theta)t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$

Differentiate to obtain velocity components:

$$v_x = \frac{dx}{dt} = v_0\cos\theta, \quad v_y(t) = \frac{dy}{dt} = v_0\sin\theta - gt$$

With $v_0 = 30$ m/s, $\theta = 30^\circ$:
$$v_x = 30\cos 30^\circ = 30(0.8660) = 25.98 \text{ m/s}$$
$$v_y(t) = 30\sin 30^\circ - 9.8t = 30(0.5) - 9.8t = 15 - 9.8t$$

At $t = 2$ s:
$$v_y(2) = 15 - 19.6 = -4.6 \text{ m/s}$$

The speed is the magnitude of the instantaneous velocity vector:

$$|\vec{v}(2)| = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2} = \sqrt{(25.98)^2 + (-4.6)^2} = \sqrt{675 + 21.16} = \sqrt{696.16} \approx 26.38 \text{ m/s}$$

The answer is approximately 26.3 m/s, which corresponds to option **C**.
