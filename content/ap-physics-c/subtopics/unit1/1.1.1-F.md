A particle moves along the x-axis with position given by $x(t) = 2t^3 - 9t^2 + 12t$ (in meters, $t$ in seconds). What are the instantaneous velocity and acceleration at $t = 2$ s?

## Options
A) $v = 0$ m/s, $a = -6$ m/s²
B) $v = 0$ m/s, $a = 6$ m/s²
C) $v = 2$ m/s, $a = 6$ m/s²
D) $v = 2$ m/s, $a = -6$ m/s²

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Instantaneous velocity is the first time-derivative of position:

$$v(t) = \frac{dx}{dt} = 6t^2 - 18t + 12$$

Instantaneous acceleration is the derivative of velocity (second derivative of position):

$$a(t) = \frac{dv}{dt} = 12t - 18$$

Evaluating at $t = 2$ s:

$$v(2) = 6(2)^2 - 18(2) + 12 = 24 - 36 + 12 = 0 \text{ m/s}$$
$$a(2) = 12(2) - 18 = 24 - 18 = 6 \text{ m/s}^2$$

The particle is momentarily at rest ($v = 0$) but has a positive acceleration of $6$ m/s², meaning it is momentarily at a turning point and about to move in the positive x-direction.
