A $3.00$ kg object moves to the right at $4.00$ m/s on a frictionless surface, colliding head-on with a stationary $2.00$ kg object. The two objects stick together after the collision. What is the speed of the combined mass immediately afterward?

## Options
A) $1.60$ m/s
B) $2.00$ m/s
C) $2.40$ m/s
D) $3.00$ m/s

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The total initial momentum of the two-object system is

$$p_i = m_1 v_{1i} + m_2 v_{2i} = (3.00)(4.00) + (2.00)(0) = 12.0\text{ kg·m/s}.$$

During the collision the contact forces $F_{12}(t)$ and $F_{21}(t)$ are internal. By Newton's third law, $F_{12}(t) = -F_{21}(t)$ for all $t$, so their impulses cancel:

$$\int (F_{12} + F_{21})\,dt = \int 0\,dt = 0.$$

The net external force on the system is zero, therefore total momentum is conserved:

$$p_f = (m_1 + m_2)\,v_f = p_i.$$

$$v_f = \frac{p_i}{m_1 + m_2} = \frac{12.0}{3.00 + 2.00} = \frac{12.0}{5.00} = 2.40\text{ m/s}.$$
