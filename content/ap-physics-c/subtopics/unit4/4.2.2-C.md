A 2.00 kg cart carries 3.00 kg of sand and moves at 6.00 m/s on a frictionless horizontal track. The sand leaks out of a hole in the bottom at a constant rate of \(0.400\text{ kg/s}\). As the sand falls, it exits the cart with zero horizontal velocity relative to the ground — that is, the sand simply drops straight down.

What is the speed of the cart 5.00 seconds after the sand begins to leak?

(A) 3.60 m/s
(B) 6.00 m/s
(C) 10.0 m/s
(D) 4.00 m/s

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The general momentum principle for a variable-mass system is

$$\sum F_{\text{ext}} = m\frac{dv}{dt} + v_{\text{rel}}\frac{dm}{dt}.$$

The leaking sand leaves the cart with zero horizontal velocity relative to the ground, i.e. $v_{\text{sand,ground}} = 0$. Therefore $v_{\text{rel}} = v_{\text{sand,ground}} - v_{\text{cart}} = -v$, and the thrust equation becomes

$$\sum F_{\text{ext}} = m\frac{dv}{dt} + (-v)\frac{dm}{dt} = \frac{d(mv)}{dt} - v\frac{dm}{dt} + (-v)\frac{dm}{dt}$$

which simplifies exactly to

$$\sum F_{\text{ext}} = \frac{d(mv)}{dt} - v\,\frac{dm}{dt}.$$

Equivalently, writing the rocket equation in the form

$$F_{\text{ext}} + v_{\text{rel}}\frac{dm}{dt} = m\frac{dv}{dt},$$

and noting $v_{\text{rel}} = 0$, gives $F_{\text{ext}} = m\,dv/dt$. With $F_{\text{ext}} = 0$ on the frictionless track,

$$m\frac{dv}{dt} = 0 \;\Longrightarrow\; \frac{dv}{dt} = 0,$$

so the cart's velocity is constant. At $t = 5.00$ s the remaining on-board mass is $m = 2.00 + 3.00 - (0.400)(5.00) = 3.00$ kg, but $v$ has not changed:

$$v(5.00\text{ s}) = v(0) = 6.00\text{ m/s}.$$

Physically, the cart-sand system has zero net external horizontal force, so total horizontal momentum is conserved. The sand that has already left carries zero horizontal momentum, so the remaining cart+remaining-sand must keep the original $6.00$ m/s; the missing momentum has been carried away by sand that has already dropped out.
