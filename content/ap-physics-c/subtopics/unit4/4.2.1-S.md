A $2.00$ kg object initially at rest is acted upon by $F(t) = (5.00\text{ N})\sin\!\left(\dfrac{\pi t}{0.500}\right)$ for $0 \le t \le 0.500$ s. What is its speed at $t = 0.500$ s?

## Options
A) $1.59$ m/s
B) $0.796$ m/s
C) $2.50$ m/s
D) $0.500$ m/s

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Compute the impulse as the time integral of the force:

$$J = \int_0^{0.500} 5.00 \sin\!\left(\frac{\pi t}{0.500}\right) dt$$

Use the substitution $u = \pi t/0.500$, so $du = (\pi/0.500)\,dt$ and $dt = (0.500/\pi)\,du$. The limits become $u(0)=0$ and $u(0.500)=\pi$:

$$J = 5.00 \cdot \frac{0.500}{\pi} \int_0^{\pi} \sin u\, du = \frac{2.50}{\pi}\bigl[-\cos u\bigr]_0^{\pi} = \frac{2.50}{\pi}(1-(-1)) = \frac{5.00}{\pi} \approx 1.59\text{ N·s}$$

By the impulse-momentum theorem, $J = \Delta p = m v_f$ (initial velocity is zero), so

$$v_f = \frac{J}{m} = \frac{1.59}{2.00} \approx 0.796\text{ m/s}.$$
