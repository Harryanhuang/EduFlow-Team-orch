Two objects move toward each other on a frictionless horizontal track and undergo a one-dimensional elastic collision. Object 1 has mass \(m_1 = 1.50\text{ kg}\) and initial velocity \(v_{1i} = +3.00\text{ m/s}\). Object 2 has mass \(m_2 = 2.50\text{ kg}\) and initial velocity \(v_{2i} = -0.600\text{ m/s}\).

What is the final velocity \(v_{1f}\) of object 1 after the collision?

(A) \(+0.50\text{ m/s}\)
(B) \(-1.50\text{ m/s}\)
(C) \(+2.50\text{ m/s}\)
(D) \(-0.50\text{ m/s}\)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The collision forces between the two objects are internal, so $\int \vec F_{\text{ext}}\,dt = 0$ and total momentum is conserved:

$$m_1 v_{1i} + m_2 v_{2i} = m_1 v_{1f} + m_2 v_{2f}$$
$$(1.50)(3.00) + (2.50)(-0.600) = 1.50\,v_{1f} + 2.50\,v_{2f}$$
$$4.50 - 1.50 = 1.50\,v_{1f} + 2.50\,v_{2f}$$
$$3.00 = 1.50\,v_{1f} + 2.50\,v_{2f}. \quad (1)$$

Because the collision is elastic, kinetic energy is also conserved:

$$\tfrac12 m_1 v_{1i}^2 + \tfrac12 m_2 v_{2i}^2 = \tfrac12 m_1 v_{1f}^2 + \tfrac12 m_2 v_{2f}^2$$
$$\tfrac12(1.50)(9.00) + \tfrac12(2.50)(0.36) = \tfrac12(1.50)\,v_{1f}^2 + \tfrac12(2.50)\,v_{2f}^2$$
$$6.75 + 0.45 = 0.75\,v_{1f}^2 + 1.25\,v_{2f}^2$$
$$7.20 = 0.75\,v_{1f}^2 + 1.25\,v_{2f}^2. \quad (2)$$

From (1): $v_{2f} = 1.20 - 0.60\,v_{1f}$. Substituting into (2):

$$0.75\,v_{1f}^2 + 1.25(1.20 - 0.60\,v_{1f})^2 = 7.20.$$

Expanding and simplifying yields $2\,v_{1f}^2 - 3\,v_{1f} - 9 = 0$, whose roots are $v_{1f} = 3.00$ (the rejected initial-velocity solution) and

$$v_{1f} = -1.50\text{ m/s}.$$
