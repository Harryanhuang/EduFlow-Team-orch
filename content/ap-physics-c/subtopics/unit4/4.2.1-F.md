A $4.00$ kg object initially at rest experiences a force $F(t) = 12.0 - 6.00 t$ (in N) for $0 \le t \le 2.00$ s. What is its speed at $t = 2.00$ s?

## Options
A) $12.0$ m/s
B) $8.00$ m/s
C) $6.00$ m/s
D) $3.00$ m/s

## Answer
D

## Explanation
The impulse imparted is the definite integral of the force over the time interval:

$$J = \int_{0}^{2.00} F(t)\,dt = \int_{0}^{2.00} (12.0 - 6.00t)\,dt$$
$$= \left[12.0t - 3.00t^2\right]_{0}^{2.00} = [24.0 - 12.0] - [0] = 12.0 \text{ N·s}$$

By the impulse-momentum theorem:

$$J = \Delta p = m(v_f - v_i) \quad\Rightarrow\quad v_f = \frac{J}{m} = \frac{12.0}{4.00} = 3.00 \text{ m/s}$$

The average force is $\langle F \rangle = 12.0/2 = 6.00$ N over the $2.00$ s interval. The impulse is $\langle F \rangle \cdot \Delta t = 12.0$ N·s, matching the integral result because $F(t)$ is linear.
