Vector $\vec{A} = 3 \hat{i} + 4 \hat{j} \text{ m}$ and $\vec{B} = 5 \hat{i} - 2 \hat{j} \text{ m}$. What is the magnitude of $\vec{A} + \vec{B}$?

## Options
A) $5.0 \text{ m}$
B) $7.0 \text{ m}$
C) $8.5 \text{ m}$
D) $12.0 \text{ m}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
$\vec{A} + \vec{B} = (3+5)\hat{i} + (4-2)\hat{j} = 8\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} \text{ m}$. Magnitude: $|\vec{A}+\vec{B}| = \sqrt{8^2 + 2^2} = \sqrt{64+4} = \sqrt{68} \approx 8.25 \text{ m}$. Closest to C ($8.5 \text{ m}$). With exact values: $\sqrt{68} = 2\sqrt{17} \approx 8.246$, closest to C.
