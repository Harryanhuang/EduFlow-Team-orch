A particle moves along the $x$-axis with acceleration $a(x) = -4x \text{ m/s}^2$. At $x = 0$, the velocity is $v_0 = 3.0 \text{ m/s}$. What is the speed at $x = 2.0 \text{ m}$?

## Options
A) $0 \text{ m/s}$
B) $1.0 \text{ m/s}$
C) $\sqrt{5} \text{ m/s}$
D) $3.0 \text{ m/s}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
$a = v\,dv/dx$, so $v\,dv = a(x)\,dx$. $\int_0^v v\,dv = \int_0^x (-4x)\,dx$. $\frac{1}{2}v^2 = \frac{1}{2}v_0^2 - 2x^2$. At $x = 2.0$: $\frac{1}{2}v^2 = \frac{1}{2}(9) - 2(4) = 4.5 - 8 = -3.5$. Since $\frac{1}{2}v^2$ cannot be negative, the particle never reaches $x = 2.0 \text{ m}$. Rechecking: $\int (-4x)dx = -2x^2 + C$. $\frac{1}{2}v^2 = \frac{1}{2}v_0^2 - 2x^2 = 4.5 - 2x^2$. For $v = 0$: $x = \sqrt{4.5/2} = \sqrt{2.25} = 1.5 \text{ m}$. The particle turns around before reaching $x = 2.0$. Speed at $x = 2.0$ is undefined (never reached). The answer is closest to A (speed = 0 at $x = 1.5$, before $x = 2.0$).
