A ball is thrown upward at $20 \text{ m/s}$ from the ground ($g = 10 \text{ m/s}^2$). At what time(s) is it at height $15 \text{ m}$?

## Options
A) $t = 1.0 \text{ s}$ only
B) $t = 3.0 \text{ s}$ only
C) $t = 1.0 \text{ s}$ and $t = 3.0 \text{ s}$
D) $t = 0.5 \text{ s}$ and $t = 3.5 \text{ s}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
$y = v_0t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$. $15 = 20t - 5t^2 \Rightarrow 5t^2 - 20t + 15 = 0 \Rightarrow t^2 - 4t + 3 = 0 \Rightarrow (t-1)(t-3) = 0$. Two valid times: $t = 1.0 \text{ s}$ (on the way up) and $t = 3.0 \text{ s}$ (on the way down).
