A ball is thrown horizontally at $15 \text{ m/s}$ from a $45 \text{ m}$ cliff ($g = 10 \text{ m/s}^2$). How far from the base of the cliff does it land?

## Options
A) $15 \text{ m}$
B) $45 \text{ m}$
C) $60 \text{ m}$
D) $90 \text{ m}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Vertical: $y = \frac{1}{2}gt^2 \Rightarrow 45 = \frac{1}{2}(10)t^2 \Rightarrow t = 3 \text{ s}$. Horizontal: $R = v_{0x}t = 15 \times 3 = 45 \text{ m}$.
