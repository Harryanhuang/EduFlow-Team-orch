A particle moves in the $+x$ direction with constant positive acceleration. Which set of graphs is correct?

## Options
A) $x$-$t$: straight line, $v$-$t$: straight line, $a$-$t$: constant positive
B) $x$-$t$: parabola opening up, $v$-$t$: straight line, $a$-$t$: constant positive
C) $x$-$t$: straight line, $v$-$t$: parabola, $a$-$t$: parabola
D) $x$-$t$: parabola, $v$-$t$: parabola, $a$-$t$: straight line

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Constant positive acceleration means $a$-$t$ is a horizontal line above zero. Integrating: $v(t) = v_0 + at$ is a straight line (positive slope). Integrating again: $x(t) = x_0 + v_0t + \frac{1}{2}at^2$ is a parabola opening upward. This matches option B.
