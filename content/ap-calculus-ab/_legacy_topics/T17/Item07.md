The function $f$ is twice differentiable with $f(0) = 1$, $f'(0) = 0$, and $f''(x) = x^2 - 4$. Which of the following statements is true about $x = 0$?

A) $f$ has a local maximum at $x = 0$
B) $f$ has a local minimum at $x = 0$
C) $f$ has a point of inflection at $x = 0$
D) The second derivative test is inconclusive at $x = 0$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Since $f'(0) = 0$, $x = 0$ is a critical point. Apply the Second Derivative Test: $f''(0) = 0^2 - 4 = -4 < 0$, so $f$ is concave down at $x = 0$. A negative second derivative at a critical point indicates a local maximum. Therefore $f$ has a local maximum at $x = 0$.
