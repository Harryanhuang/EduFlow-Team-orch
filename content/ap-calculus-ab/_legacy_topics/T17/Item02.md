The function $f$ is continuous and has the values $f'(2) = 0$ and $f''(2) = -3$. Which of the following is the correct classification of the point $(2, f(2))$?

A) Local minimum
B) Local maximum
C) Point of inflection
D) Cannot be determined from this information

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The Second Derivative Test states: If $f'(c) = 0$ and $f''(c) < 0$, then $f$ has a local maximum at $c$. Since $f'(2) = 0$ and $f''(2) = -3 < 0$, the point at $x = 2$ is a local maximum. A point of inflection requires a change in concavity, which $f'' = -3$ alone does not indicate.
