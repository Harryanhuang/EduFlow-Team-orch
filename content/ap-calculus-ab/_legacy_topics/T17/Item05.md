If $f''(x) < 0$ on the interval $(a, b)$, which of the following best describes the graph of $f$ on this interval?

A) Concave up and increasing
B) Concave up and decreasing
C) Concave down and increasing
D) Concave down and decreasing

## Answer
C

## Explanation
When $f''(x) < 0$, the graph of $f$ is concave down. The sign of $f''$ only tells us about concavity, not about whether the function is increasing or decreasing. Both increasing and decreasing functions can be concave down. Without information about $f'$, we cannot determine the monotonicity.
