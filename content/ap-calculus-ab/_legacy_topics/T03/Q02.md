## Question
Classify each discontinuity as removable, jump, or infinite:
(a) \( f(x) = \dfrac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1} \) at \( x = 1 \)
(b) \( g(x) = \dfrac{1}{x + 3} \) at \( x = -3 \)
(c) \( h(x) = \begin{cases} 0, & x < 2 \\ 1, & x \geq 2 \end{cases} \) at \( x = 2 \)

## Answer
(a) Removable — \( \displaystyle\lim_{x \to 1} f(x) = \lim_{x \to 1} (x+1) = 2 \) exists, but \( f(1) \) is undefined.
(b) Infinite — as \( x \to -3^\pm \), \( g(x) \to \pm\infty \) (vertical asymptote).
(c) Jump — left-hand limit is 0, right-hand limit is 1; both finite but unequal.
