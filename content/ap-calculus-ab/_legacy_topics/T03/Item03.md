Let
\[
f(x) = \begin{cases}
x^2 + k, & x < 2 \\
5, & x \geq 2
\end{cases}
\]
For what value of \( k \) is \( f \) continuous at \( x = 2 \)?

## Options
A) \( k = 1 \)
B) \( k = 3 \)
C) \( k = 5 \)
D) \( k = 9 \)

## Answer
A

## Explanation
For continuity at \( x = 2 \), we need \( \displaystyle\lim_{x \to 2^-} f(x) = \displaystyle\lim_{x \to 2^+} f(x) = f(2) \). The left-hand limit is \( \displaystyle\lim_{x \to 2^-} (x^2 + k) = 4 + k \). The right-hand limit and function value are both 5. Setting \( 4 + k = 5 \) gives \( k = 1 \).
