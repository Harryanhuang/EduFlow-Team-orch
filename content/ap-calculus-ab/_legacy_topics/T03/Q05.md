## Question
Let
\[
f(x) = \begin{cases}
x^2 - 1, & x < 2 \\
2x + k, & x \geq 2
\end{cases}
\]
Find the value of \( k \) that makes \( f \) continuous at \( x = 2 \).

## Answer
Left-hand limit: \( \displaystyle\lim_{x \to 2^-} (x^2 - 1) = 4 - 1 = 3 \).
Right-hand limit: \( \displaystyle\lim_{x \to 2^+} (2x + k) = 4 + k \).
Function value: \( f(2) = 2(2) + k = 4 + k \).

For continuity: \( 3 = 4 + k \), so \( k = -1 \).
