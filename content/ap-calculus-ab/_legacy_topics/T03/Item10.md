The function \( f \) is continuous on the closed interval \( [0, 4] \). If \( f(0) = -3 \) and \( f(4) = 5 \), which of the following must be true?

## Options
A) \( f(2) = 1 \)
B) The equation \( f(x) = 0 \) has at least one solution in \( (0, 4) \).
C) \( f \) has an absolute maximum at some point in \( (0, 4) \).
D) \( f'(c) = 2 \) for some \( c \in (0, 4) \).

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By the Intermediate Value Theorem (IVT), since \( f \) is continuous on \( [0, 4] \) and \( -3 < 0 < 5 \), there exists at least one \( c \in (0, 4) \) such that \( f(c) = 0 \). Option A is not guaranteed—the IVT only ensures a value is taken, not where. Option C may be true but is not guaranteed by the IVT alone (the max could be at an endpoint). Option D requires differentiability and the Mean Value Theorem, not continuity alone.
