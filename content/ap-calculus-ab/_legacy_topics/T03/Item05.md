Classify the discontinuity of \( f(x) = \dfrac{x^2 - 9}{x - 3} \) at \( x = 3 \).

## Options
A) Jump discontinuity
B) Removable discontinuity
C) Infinite discontinuity
D) The function is continuous at \( x = 3 \).

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Factoring: \( f(x) = \dfrac{(x-3)(x+3)}{x-3} = x + 3 \) for \( x \neq 3 \). The limit as \( x \to 3 \) is 6, which exists. However, \( f(3) \) is undefined (division by zero). Since the limit exists but the function is not defined at the point, the discontinuity is removable. The graph is the line \( y = x + 3 \) with a single hole at \( (3, 6) \).
