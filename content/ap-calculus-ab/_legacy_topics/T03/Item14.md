Let \( f \) be a function continuous on \( [1, 5] \) with \( f(1) = -2 \) and \( f(5) = 3 \). Which of the following statements must be true?

I. The equation \( f(x) = 0 \) has at least one solution in \( (1, 5) \).
II. The equation \( f(x) = 1 \) has at least one solution in \( (1, 5) \).
III. The equation \( f(x) = 4 \) has at least one solution in \( (1, 5) \).

## Options
A) I only
B) I and II only
C) II and III only
D) I, II, and III

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By the IVT, since \( f \) is continuous on \( [1, 5] \), \( f \) takes every value between \( f(1) = -2 \) and \( f(5) = 3 \). Statement I: \( 0 \in [-2, 3] \), so yes. Statement II: \( 1 \in [-2, 3] \), so yes. Statement III: \( 4 \notin [-2, 3] \), so no—the IVT does not guarantee this (and a linear function from \( -2 \) to \( 3 \) would never reach 4).
