Let
\[
f(x) = \begin{cases}
3x - 1, & x \leq 1 \\
x^2 + kx, & x > 1
\end{cases}
\]
For what value of \( k \) is \( f \) continuous at \( x = 1 \)?

## Options
A) \( k = 1 \)
B) \( k = 2 \)
C) \( k = 3 \)
D) No such value of \( k \) exists.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
For continuity at \( x = 1 \), the left-hand limit must equal the right-hand limit: \( 3(1) - 1 = 2 \) and \( 1^2 + k(1) = 1 + k \). Setting them equal: \( 1 + k = 2 \), so \( k = 1 \).
