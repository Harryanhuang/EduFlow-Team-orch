A population of insects grows at a rate proportional to its current size. If the population is 200 at time \(t = 0\) and 350 at \(t = 5\) days, write and solve the differential equation to find the population as a function of time.
The differential equation is \(\frac{dP}{dt} = kP\). Solving gives \(P(t) = 200e^{kt}\). Using \(P(5) = 350\): \(350 = 200e^{5k} \implies e^{5k} = 1.75 \implies k = \frac{1}{5}\ln(1.75)\). So \(P(t) = 200e^{t\ln(1.75)/5} = 200(1.75)^{t/5}\).
