Find all equilibrium solutions of \(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = y^{2} - 4\) and classify each as stable or unstable. Then, if \(y(0) = 1\), find the particular solution and determine \(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty} y(x)\).
Equilibria: \(y = \pm 2\). \(y = 2\) is unstable (solutions move away on both sides). \(y = -2\) is stable (solutions approach from both sides). For \(y(0) = 1\): \(y = \frac{2(3 - e^{4x})}{3 + e^{4x}}\). As \(x \to \infty\), \(e^{4x} \to \infty\) and \(y(x) \to -2\).
