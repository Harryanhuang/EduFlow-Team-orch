A cup of coffee at \(185^{\circ}\)F is placed in a room at \(70^{\circ}\)F. After 8 minutes, the coffee is at \(140^{\circ}\)F. Use Newton's Law of Cooling to write the differential equation and find the temperature of the coffee after 20 minutes.
Differential equation: \(\frac{dT}{dt} = -k(T - 70)\) with \(T(0) = 185\). Solution: \(T(t) = 70 + 115e^{-kt}\). Using \(T(8) = 140\): \(140 = 70 + 115e^{-8k} \implies e^{-8k} = \frac{70}{115} = \frac{14}{23} \implies k = -\frac{1}{8}\ln(14/23)\). At \(t = 20\): \(T(20) = 70 + 115e^{-20k} = 70 + 115(e^{-8k})^{2.5} = 70 + 115(14/23)^{2.5} \approx 70 + 115(0.400) \approx 116^{\circ}\text{F}\).
