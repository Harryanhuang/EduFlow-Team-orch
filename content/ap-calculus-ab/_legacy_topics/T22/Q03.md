Find the general solution of \(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = 2y(3-y)\) using separation of variables. Leave your answer in implicit form.
Separating gives \(\frac{dy}{y(3-y)} = 2\,dx\). Using partial fractions, \(\frac{1}{y(3-y)} = \frac{1/3}{y} + \frac{1/3}{3-y}\). Integrating yields \(\ln\left|\frac{y}{3-y}\right| = 6x + C\), or equivalently \(\frac{y}{3-y} = Ce^{6x}\).
