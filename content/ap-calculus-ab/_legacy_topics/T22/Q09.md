State the existence and uniqueness theorem for first-order differential equations. Then determine whether the theorem guarantees a unique solution through the point \((0, 1)\) for the differential equation \(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \dfrac{x}{y}\).
The theorem: if \(f(x, y)\) and \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\) are continuous in a rectangle containing \((x_0, y_0)\), then the IVP has a unique solution near \(x_0\). For \(\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x}{y}\), \(f(x, y) = \frac{x}{y}\) and \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = -\frac{x}{y^2}\). At \((0, 1)\), \(f\) is continuous but \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = -\frac{0}{1} = 0\) is also continuous. Both are continuous in a neighborhood of \((0, 1)\) (e.g., the rectangle \(|x| < 0.5, |y-1| < 0.5\)), so the theorem guarantees a unique solution through \((0, 1)\).
