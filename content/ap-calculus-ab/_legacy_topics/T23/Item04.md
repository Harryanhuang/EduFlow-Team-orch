Newton's Law of Cooling states that the rate of temperature change of an object is proportional to the difference between its temperature and the surrounding temperature. If a cup of coffee at $95^\circ$C is placed in a room at $20^\circ$C, which differential equation models the coffee's temperature?

A) $\dfrac{dT}{dt} = k(T - 20)$

B) $\dfrac{dT}{dt} = k(95 - 20)$

C) $\dfrac{dT}{dt} = -k(T - 20)$

D) $\dfrac{dT}{dt} = k(95 - T)$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Newton's Law of Cooling: $\dfrac{dT}{dt} = -k(T - T_{env})$, where $T_{env} = 20^\circ$C is the ambient temperature. The negative sign indicates the temperature decreases (approaches ambient). When $T > 20$, $T - 20 > 0$, but $dT/dt < 0$ (cooling), so the negative sign is required.
