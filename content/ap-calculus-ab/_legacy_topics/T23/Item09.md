A bank account earning continuously compounded interest grows according to $\dfrac{dA}{dt} = 0.04A$. If \$5000 is deposited initially, how long will it take for the account to reach \$10,000?

A) $\dfrac{\ln 2}{0.04}$ years

B) $\dfrac{\ln 2}{4}$ years

C) $\dfrac{2}{0.04}$ years

D) $\dfrac{10{,}000}{5000 \cdot 0.04}$ years

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Solving $dA/dt = 0.04A$ with $A(0) = 5000$ gives $A(t) = 5000e^{0.04t}$. Setting $A(t) = 10{,}000$: $10{,}000 = 5000e^{0.04t}$, so $2 = e^{0.04t}$, and $t = \dfrac{\ln 2}{0.04}$ years. This equals approximately 17.33 years.
