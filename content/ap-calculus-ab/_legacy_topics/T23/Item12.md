A drug is administered intravenously with concentration decaying according to $\dfrac{dC}{dt} = -0.1C$, where $C$ is in mg/mL and $t$ is in hours. If the initial concentration is $C(0) = 2$ mg/mL, what is the concentration after 3 hours?

A) $2e^{-0.3}$ mg/mL

B) $2e^{-0.1 \cdot 3}$ mg/mL

C) $2e^{-0.03}$ mg/mL

D) Both A and B are equivalent

## Answer
D

## Explanation
Solving $\dfrac{dC}{dt} = -0.1C$ with $C(0) = 2$ gives $C(t) = 2e^{-0.1t}$. At $t = 3$: $C(3) = 2e^{-0.3}$. Option A writes this as $2e^{-0.3}$, while Option B writes it as $2e^{-0.1 \cdot 3}$. These are mathematically equivalent. The answer is D.
