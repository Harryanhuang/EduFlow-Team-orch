The population of a city was 50,000 in 2020 and grows at a rate proportional to its size. If the population grows to 65,000 by 2025, what will the population be in 2030?

A) 78,000

B) 84,500

C) 91,000

D) 97,500

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Let $P(t) = P_0 e^{kt}$. With $P(0) = 50{,}000$ and $P(5) = 65{,}000$: $65{,}000 = 50{,}000e^{5k}$, so $e^{5k} = 1.3$, giving $k = \dfrac{\ln(1.3)}{5}$. For 2030 ($t = 10$): $P(10) = 50{,}000e^{10k} = 50{,}000(e^{5k})^2 = 50{,}000(1.3)^2 = 50{,}000(1.69) = 84{,}500$.
