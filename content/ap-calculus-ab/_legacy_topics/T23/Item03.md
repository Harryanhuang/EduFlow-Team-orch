Carbon-14 has a half-life of 5730 years. The decay constant $k$ for the exponential decay model $\dfrac{dN}{dt} = kN$ is:

A) $k = \dfrac{\ln 2}{5730}$

B) $k = \dfrac{1}{5730}$

C) $k = \dfrac{\ln 2}{2 \cdot 5730}$

D) $k = 5730 \cdot \ln 2$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
For exponential decay $N(t) = N_0 e^{kt}$, after one half-life $t = T_{1/2}$, we have $N(T_{1/2}) = N_0/2$. So $N_0/2 = N_0 e^{k \cdot T_{1/2}}$, giving $1/2 = e^{kT_{1/2}}$, so $kT_{1/2} = \ln(1/2) = -\ln 2$, thus $k = -\dfrac{\ln 2}{T_{1/2}}$. The negative sign is included in the differential equation; the magnitude is $\dfrac{\ln 2}{5730}$.
