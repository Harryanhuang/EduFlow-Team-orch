Let $A(x) = \int_2^x f(t) \, dt$, where $f$ is continuous on $[2, 7]$. Which of the following statements is true?

A) $A(2) = 0$
B) $A(7) = f(7) - f(2)$
C) $A(x)$ is always negative on $[2, 7]$
D) $A'(x) = f(x) - f(2)$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
By the definition of an accumulation function with lower limit $a = 2$, we have $A(2) = \int_2^2 f(t) \, dt = 0$. This is a fundamental property of accumulation functions: $A(a) = 0$ when the upper and lower limits are equal. Option B is incorrect because $A(7) = \int_2^7 f(t) \, dt$, which is not simply $f(7) - f(2)$. Option C cannot be determined from the information given. Option D is incorrect because $A'(x) = f(x)$ by the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, Part 1.
