Using the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, Part 2, evaluate $\int_1^4 (2x^2 + 3) \, dx$.

A) 14
B) 42
C) 45
D) 54

## Answer
D

## Explanation
First, find the antiderivative: $\int (2x^2 + 3) \, dx = \frac{2x^3}{3} + 3x + C$.

Then evaluate from 1 to 4:
$F(4) = \frac{2(4)^3}{3} + 3(4) = \frac{2(64)}{3} + 12 = \frac{128}{3} + 12 = \frac{164}{3}$

$F(1) = \frac{2(1)^3}{3} + 3(1) = \frac{2}{3} + 3 = \frac{11}{3}$

$\int_1^4 (2x^2 + 3) \, dx = \frac{164}{3} - \frac{11}{3} = \frac{153}{3} = 54$
