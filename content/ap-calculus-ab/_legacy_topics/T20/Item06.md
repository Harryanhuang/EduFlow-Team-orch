Let $A(x) = \int_3^x f(t) \, dt$. If $f$ is continuous, then $A'(5)$ equals

A) $\int_3^5 f(t) \, dt$
B) $f(5)$
C) $f(5) - f(3)$
D) $f'(5)$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, Part 1: if $A(x) = \int_a^x f(t) \, dt$, then $A'(x) = f(x)$ for all $x$ in the interval where $f$ is continuous.

Therefore, $A'(5) = f(5)$.

Option A represents $A(5)$, not $A'(5)$. Option C has no general validity. Option D is the derivative of $f$, not relevant here.
