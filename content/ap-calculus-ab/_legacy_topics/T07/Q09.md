A particle's position is $s(t) = t^2 \cos t - \dfrac{\sin t}{t}$ for $t > 0$. Find the velocity at $t = \pi$.

$v(t) = s'(t) = 2t \cos t - t^2 \sin t - \dfrac{\cos t \cdot t - \sin t \cdot 1}{t^2}$.

At $t = \pi$: $\cos \pi = -1$, $\sin \pi = 0$.

$v(\pi) = 2\pi(-1) - 0 - \dfrac{(-1)(\pi) - 0}{\pi^2} = -2\pi + \frac{\pi}{\pi^2} = -2\pi + \frac{1}{\pi}$.
