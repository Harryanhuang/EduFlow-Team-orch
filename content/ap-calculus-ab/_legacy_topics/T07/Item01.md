If $f(x) = x^2 \sin x$, what is $f'(x)$?

## Options
A) $2x \cos x$
B) $2x \sin x + x^2 \cos x$
C) $x^2 \cos x$
D) $2x \sin x - x^2 \cos x$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Using the product rule: $\frac{d}{dx}[u \cdot v] = u'v + uv'$. Let $u = x^2$ and $v = \sin x$. Then $u' = 2x$ and $v' = \cos x$. So $f'(x) = 2x \sin x + x^2 \cos x$.
