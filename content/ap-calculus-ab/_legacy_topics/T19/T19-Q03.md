<question>
Write the midpoint Riemann sum with $n$ subintervals for $\int_0^4 f(x)\,dx$. Define $\Delta x$ and the $x$-coordinates used.
</question>
<answer>
The width of each subinterval is:
$$\Delta x = \frac{b - a}{n} = \frac{4 - 0}{n} = \frac{4}{n}$$

The midpoint Riemann sum with $n$ subintervals is:
$$M_n = \sum_{i=1}^{n} f(\bar{x}_i)\,\Delta x$$

The midpoint of the $i$-th subinterval is:
$$\bar{x}_i = a + \left(i - \frac{1}{2}\right)\Delta x = \left(i - \frac{1}{2}\right)\frac{4}{n}$$

The $x$-coordinates used are the midpoints: $\bar{x}_1, \bar{x}_2, \ldots, \bar{x}_n$, each lying halfway between $x_{i-1}$ and $x_i$ on the partition of $[0, 4]$.
</answer>
