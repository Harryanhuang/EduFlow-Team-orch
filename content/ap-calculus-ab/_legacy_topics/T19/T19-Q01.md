<question>
What does the definite integral $\int_a^b f(x)\,dx$ represent geometrically? Include the concept of signed area.
</question>
<answer>
The definite integral $\int_a^b f(x)\,dx$ represents the signed area between the graph of $y = f(x)$ and the $x$-axis over the interval $[a, b]$.

- **Above the $x$-axis**: Area is counted as positive.
- **Below the $x$-axis**: Area is counted as negative.

The integral sums these signed contributions, giving a net result (positive, negative, or zero) rather than a total geometric area.
</answer>
