**Question:** What does the notation \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L\) mean in words? Explain what it does and does not require about the value of \(f(a)\).

**Answer:** The notation means "as \(x\) gets arbitrarily close to \(a\) (but not equal to \(a\)), the value of \(f(x)\) gets arbitrarily close to \(L\)." The limit is about the behavior of \(f\) near \(a\), not at \(a\). The limit does not require \(f(a)\) to be defined, nor does it require \(f(a) = L\). For example, if \(f(x) = \frac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1}\), then \(f(1)\) is undefined, but \(\lim_{x \to 1} f(x) = 2\).
