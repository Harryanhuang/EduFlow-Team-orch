**Question:** Let \(f(x) = \begin{cases} x^2, & x < 2 \\ ax + b, & x \geq 2 \end{cases}\). For what values of \(a\) and \(b\) does \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 2} f(x)\) exist? Show that there are infinitely many such pairs and describe the relationship between \(a\) and \(b\).

**Answer:** For the limit to exist, we need \(\lim_{x \to 2^-} f(x) = \lim_{x \to 2^+} f(x)\). Left-hand limit: \(\lim_{x \to 2^-} x^2 = 4\). Right-hand limit: \(\lim_{x \to 2^+} (ax + b) = 2a + b\). Setting equal: \(2a + b = 4\), or equivalently \(b = 4 - 2a\). Any pair \((a, b)\) satisfying this relationship works. For example: \((a, b) = (0, 4)\), \((1, 2)\), \((2, 0)\), \((-1, 6)\). There are infinitely many solutions because we have one equation in two unknowns — the constraint is a line in the \((a, b)\)-plane.
