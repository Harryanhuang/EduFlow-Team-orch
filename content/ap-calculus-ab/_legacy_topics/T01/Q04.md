**Question:** The graph of \(f(x)\) shows a removable discontinuity at \(x = 3\): the curve approaches \(y = 7\) from both sides, but there is a hole at \((3, 7)\) and a filled dot at \((3, 2)\). Find \(\lim_{x \to 3} f(x)\), \(\lim_{x \to 3^-} f(x)\), \(\lim_{x \to 3^+} f(x)\), and \(f(3)\). Explain the distinction.

**Answer:** \(\lim_{x \to 3} f(x) = 7\), \(\lim_{x \to 3^-} f(x) = 7\), \(\lim_{x \to 3^+} f(x) = 7\), and \(f(3) = 2\). The limit (both one-sided and two-sided) equals 7 because the curve approaches \(y = 7\) from both sides. The function value \(f(3) = 2\) because that is where the filled dot is placed. This demonstrates that the limit is independent of the function value at the point — a key conceptual distinction in calculus.
