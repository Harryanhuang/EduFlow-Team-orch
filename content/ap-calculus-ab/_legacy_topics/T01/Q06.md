**Question:** A table gives values of \(f(x) = \dfrac{\cos x - 1}{x}\) near \(x = 0\). Complete the table and use it to estimate \(\lim_{x \to 0} f(x)\). Then explain why relying solely on a table can sometimes be misleading.

**Answer:** Table values:

| \(x\)     | \(-0.1\)   | \(-0.01\)  | \(-0.001\) | \(0.001\)  | \(0.01\)   | \(0.1\)    |
|-----------|------------|------------|------------|------------|------------|------------|
| \(f(x)\)  | 0.049958   | 0.0049999  | 0.000500   | -0.000500  | -0.0049999 | -0.049958  |

The values approach 0 from both sides, so \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\cos x - 1}{x} = 0\). Tables can be misleading because: (1) the chosen \(x\)-values might miss important behavior (e.g., rapid oscillation between sampled points); (2) rounding errors can obscure the true trend; (3) for functions with sharp features, the table might not sample close enough to the target point. Numerical evidence supports but does not prove a limit.
