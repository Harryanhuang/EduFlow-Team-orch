The graph of function \(h(x)\) is shown below (described). The graph passes through the point \((1, 3)\) with no breaks, holes, or jumps at that point. What is \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 1} h(x)\)?

## Options
A) 0
B) 1
C) 3
D) The limit does not exist.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Since the graph of \(h(x)\) is continuous at \(x = 1\) and passes through \((1, 3)\), the limit equals the function value: \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 1} h(x) = h(1) = 3\). For a continuous function at a point, the limit as \(x\) approaches that point equals the function's value there.
