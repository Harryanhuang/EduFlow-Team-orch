Let \(f(x) = \dfrac{1}{(x - 5)^2}\). Which statement correctly describes \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 5} f(x)\)?

## Options
A) The limit is 0.
B) The limit is 1.
C) The limit is \(\infty\).
D) The limit does not exist because \(f(5)\) is undefined.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
As \(x \to 5\), the denominator \((x - 5)^2\) approaches 0, and since it is squared, it is always positive. Therefore \(\dfrac{1}{(x - 5)^2}\) grows without bound: \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 5} f(x) = \infty\). This is an infinite limit. Note that on the AP Calculus exam, writing "\(\infty\)" is the preferred way to describe this behavior (more informative than saying "does not exist").
