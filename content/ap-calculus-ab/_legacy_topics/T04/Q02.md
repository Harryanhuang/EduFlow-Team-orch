Evaluate $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{3x^2 - 2x + 1}{x^2}$. Explain why this limit is an infinite limit and identify the vertical asymptote.

## Answer
As $x \to 0$, the numerator approaches $1$ and the denominator approaches $0^{+}$ (since $x^2 > 0$ for all $x \neq 0$). Therefore $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{3x^2 - 2x + 1}{x^2} = +\infty$. Since the function grows without bound as $x$ approaches $0$ from either side, $x = 0$ (the $y$-axis) is a vertical asymptote of the graph.
