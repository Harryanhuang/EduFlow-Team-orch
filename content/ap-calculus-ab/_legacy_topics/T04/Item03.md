Let $f(x) = \dfrac{2x - 5}{x + 3}$. What is $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} f(x)$?

## Options
A) $0$
B) $2$
C) $-3$
D) $\infty$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For a rational function where the degrees of numerator and denominator are equal, the limit at infinity equals the ratio of the leading coefficients. Here both have degree 1, and $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{2x - 5}{x + 3} = \frac{2}{1} = 2$. Equivalently, divide numerator and denominator by $x$:
$\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{2 - 5/x}{1 + 3/x} = \frac{2 - 0}{1 + 0} = 2$.
