Let $f(x) = \dfrac{x^2 - 4x + 3}{x^2 - x - 6}$. At which $x$-values does the graph of $f$ have a vertical asymptote?

## Options
A) $x = -2$ only
B) $x = 3$ only
C) $x = -2$ and $x = 3$
D) $x = 1$ and $x = 3$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Factor numerator and denominator:
$$f(x) = \frac{(x-1)(x-3)}{(x+2)(x-3)}.$$
The denominator is zero at $x = -2$ and $x = 3$. At $x = 3$, the factor $(x-3)$ cancels with the numerator, so $x = 3$ is a removable discontinuity (hole), not a vertical asymptote. At $x = -2$, the numerator is $(-2-1)(-2-3) = (-3)(-5) = 15 \neq 0$, so the denominator approaches zero while the numerator does not. Thus $x = -2$ is the only vertical asymptote.
