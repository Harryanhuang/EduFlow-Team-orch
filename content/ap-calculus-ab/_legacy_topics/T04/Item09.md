Evaluate $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{\sqrt{4x^2 + 1}}{3x - 2}$.

## Options
A) $\dfrac{1}{3}$
B) $\dfrac{2}{3}$
C) $\dfrac{4}{3}$
D) $\dfrac{1}{6}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For $x > 0$, $\sqrt{4x^2 + 1} = x\sqrt{4 + 1/x^2}$. Divide numerator and denominator by $x$:
$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{x\sqrt{4 + 1/x^2}}{x(3 - 2/x)} = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{\sqrt{4 + 1/x^2}}{3 - 2/x} = \frac{\sqrt{4 + 0}}{3 - 0} = \frac{2}{3}.$$
