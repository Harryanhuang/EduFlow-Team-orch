The table gives values of a continuous function $f$:

| $x$ | $-2$ | $0$ | $1$ | $3$ | $5$ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $f(x)$ | $-5$ | $2$ | $-1$ | $4$ | $-3$ |

Using only the Intermediate Value Theorem, determine the minimum number of distinct solutions the equation $f(x) = 1$ must have on the interval $[-2, 5]$.

## Answer
Apply IVT on each subinterval, checking whether $1$ lies between consecutive function values:
- On $[-2, 0]$: $f(-2) = -5 < 1 < 2 = f(0)$. IVT guarantees at least one $c \in (-2, 0)$ with $f(c) = 1$.
- On $[0, 1]$: $f(0) = 2 > 1 > -1 = f(1)$. IVT guarantees at least one $c \in (0, 1)$ with $f(c) = 1$.
- On $[1, 3]$: $f(1) = -1 < 1 < 4 = f(3)$. IVT guarantees at least one $c \in (1, 3)$ with $f(c) = 1$.
- On $[3, 5]$: $f(3) = 4 > 1 > -3 = f(5)$. IVT guarantees at least one $c \in (3, 5)$ with $f(c) = 1$.

Minimum number of distinct solutions: **4**.
