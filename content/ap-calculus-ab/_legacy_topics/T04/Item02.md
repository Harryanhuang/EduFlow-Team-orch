Evaluate $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 2^{+}} \frac{x + 1}{x - 2}$.

## Options
A) $-\infty$
B) $\infty$
C) $3$
D) The limit does not exist, and the function does not approach $\infty$ or $-\infty$.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
As $x \to 2^{+}$, the numerator $x + 1 \to 3$ (positive), and the denominator $x - 2 \to 0^{+}$ (small positive). A positive quantity divided by a small positive quantity grows without bound, so the limit is $\infty$.
