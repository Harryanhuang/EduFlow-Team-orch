Find the horizontal asymptote(s) of $f(x) = \dfrac{4x^3 + x - 7}{2x^3 - 5x^2 + 3}$ by evaluating $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} f(x)$ and $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to -\infty} f(x)$.

## Answer
Both numerator and denominator have degree 3. The limit at infinity (in both directions) is the ratio of leading coefficients:
$$\lim_{x \to \pm\infty} \frac{4x^3 + x - 7}{2x^3 - 5x^2 + 3} = \frac{4}{2} = 2.$$
The horizontal asymptote is $y = 2$.
