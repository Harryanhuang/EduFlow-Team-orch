Let $f(x) = \frac{\sin x}{x}$ for $x \neq 0$, and define $f(0) = 1$ by continuity. Is the linearization $L(x) = 1$ at $x = 0$ an overestimate or underestimate for $x = 0.1$? Justify using the second derivative of $f$ at 0.
Underestimate; $f''(0) = -\frac{1}{3} < 0$, so $f$ is concave down near $x = 0$, and a tangent line to a concave-down curve lies above the curve: $L(0.1) > f(0.1)$. Thus $L(x)$ underestimates $f(x)$ for $x > 0$ near 0.
