Let $g(x) = \ln x$ for $x > 0$. What is the value of $\left(g^{-1}\right)'(0)$?

## Options
A) $0$  B) $1$  C) $e$  D) The derivative does not exist.

## Answer
B) $1$

## Explanation
Since $g(1) = \ln 1 = 0$, we have $(g^{-1})'(0) = \frac{1}{g'(1)}$. Since $g'(x) = \frac{1}{x}$, we get $g'(1) = 1$. Therefore $(g^{-1})'(0) = \frac{1}{1} = 1$. Note that $g^{-1}(x) = e^x$, and $(e^x)'|_{x=0} = e^0 = 1$, confirming the result.
