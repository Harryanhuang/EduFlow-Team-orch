Find the value of $\frac{d}{dx}\left[\arctan(x)\right]$ at $x = 1$. Explain how this relates to the slope of the graph of $y = \arctan(x)$ at that point.

## Answer
$\frac{1}{2}$

## Explanation
$\frac{d}{dx}[\arctan(x)] = \frac{1}{1 + x^2}$. At $x = 1$: $\frac{1}{1 + 1^2} = \frac{1}{2}$. This means the tangent line to $y = \arctan(x)$ at $x = 1$ has slope $\frac{1}{2}$, indicating the function is increasing but at a moderate rate at that point.
