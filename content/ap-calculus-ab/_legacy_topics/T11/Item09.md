The function $f$ is differentiable and strictly increasing. Selected values of $f$ are given in the table.

| $x$ | $f(x)$ |
|-----|--------|
| 2   | 5      |
| 5   | 8      |
| 8   | 13     |

If $f'(2) = 1$ and $f'(5) = 2$, what is $(f^{-1})'(8)$?

## Options
A) $\frac{1}{2}$  B) $\frac{1}{3}$  C) $2$  D) $5$

## Answer
A) $\frac{1}{2}$

## Explanation
Since $f(5) = 8$, we have $(f^{-1})'(8) = \frac{1}{f'(5)} = \frac{1}{2}$. The values $f'(2) = 1$ and the other table entries are distractors.
