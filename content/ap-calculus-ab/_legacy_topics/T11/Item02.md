The function $f$ is differentiable and strictly increasing. The table gives selected values of $f$ and $f'$.

| $x$ | $f(x)$ | $f'(x)$ |
|-----|--------|---------|
| 1   | 4      | 3       |
| 2   | 7      | 5       |
| 3   | 11     | 2       |
| 4   | 16     | 6       |

What is the value of $(f^{-1})'(7)$?

## Options
A) $\frac{1}{5}$  B) $\frac{1}{3}$  C) $\frac{1}{2}$  D) $5$

## Answer
A) $\frac{1}{5}$

## Explanation
Since $f(2) = 7$, we have $(f^{-1})'(7) = \frac{1}{f'(2)} = \frac{1}{5}$.
