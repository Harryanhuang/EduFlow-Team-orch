Find the equation of the tangent line to the curve y = ln(x) at x = e.

## Answer
y = (1/e)x

## Explanation
The point on the curve: when x = e, y = ln(e) = 1, so the point is (e, 1).
The slope: dy/dx = 1/x, so at x = e the slope is 1/e.
Tangent line: y - 1 = (1/e)(x - e), which simplifies to y = (1/e)x - 1 + 1 = (1/e)x.
