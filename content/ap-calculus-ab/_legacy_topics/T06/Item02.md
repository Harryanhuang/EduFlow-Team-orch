What is the derivative of f(x) = 3x^4 - 2x^2 + 7?

## Options
A) 12x^3 - 4x
B) 12x^3 - 4x + 7
C) 3x^3 - 2x
D) 12x^4 - 4x^2

## Answer
A) 12x^3 - 4x

## Explanation
Apply the power rule term by term: d/dx[3x^4] = 12x^3, d/dx[-2x^2] = -4x, d/dx[7] = 0 (derivative of a constant is 0). So f'(x) = 12x^3 - 4x.
