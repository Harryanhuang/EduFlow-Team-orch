Let f(x) = x^3 - 3x^2 + 2.

(a) Find all critical points of f.
(b) Determine the intervals where f is increasing and where f is decreasing.
(c) Find the equation of the tangent line at the local maximum.
(d) For what value(s) of k does the line y = k intersect the graph of f at exactly two distinct points?

## Answer
(a) f'(x) = 3x^2 - 6x = 3x(x - 2). Critical points: x = 0 and x = 2.
(b) f'(x) > 0 when x < 0 or x > 2 (increasing). f'(x) < 0 when 0 < x < 2 (decreasing).
(c) Local maximum at x = 0: f(0) = 2. Tangent line: y = 2 (horizontal, slope = 0).
(d) f(0) = 2 (local max), f(2) = 8 - 12 + 2 = -2 (local min). The line y = k intersects at exactly two points when k = 2 or k = -2 (the line passes through a local extremum, touching there and crossing once more).

## Explanation
Critical points come from f'(x) = 0. The sign chart of f' determines increasing/decreasing behavior. A horizontal tangent at a local extremum means the tangent line is y = the y-value. For a cubic, a horizontal line through a local extremum intersects the curve at exactly two points (the extremum is a tangent/double root and there is one more crossing).
