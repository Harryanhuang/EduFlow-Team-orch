Evaluate: \(\displaystyle \int (4x^3 - 2x + 7)\,dx\)

## Options
A) \(x^4 - x^2 + 7x + C\)
B) \(12x^2 - 2 + C\)
C) \(x^4 - x^2 + 7x + C\)
D) \(4x^4 - x^2 + 7x + C\)

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Apply the power rule for integration: \(\int x^n\,dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C\) for \(n \neq -1\).

\[
\int 4x^3\,dx = x^4,\quad \int -2x\,dx = -x^2,\quad \int 7\,dx = 7x
\]

So \(\int (4x^3 - 2x + 7)\,dx = x^4 - x^2 + 7x + C\). Option B is the derivative, not the antiderivative.
