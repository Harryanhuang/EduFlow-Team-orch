Find all values of $k$ such that the following limit exists:

$\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2 + kx - 6}{x - 2}$

For each such value of $k$, evaluate the limit.

## Answer
$k = 1$, and the limit equals $5$.

## Explanation
For the limit to exist (removable discontinuity rather than vertical asymptote), the numerator must equal zero when $x = 2$:

$2^2 + 2k - 6 = 0$
$4 + 2k - 6 = 0$
$2k = 2$
$k = 1$

With $k = 1$: $x^2 + x - 6 = (x + 3)(x - 2)$

$$\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{(x + 3)(x - 2)}{x - 2} = \lim_{x \to 2} (x + 3) = 5$$
