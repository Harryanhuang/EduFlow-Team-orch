Evaluate the limit:

$\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sqrt{x + 1} - 1}{x}$

## Options
A) $0$
B) $\dfrac{1}{4}$
C) $\dfrac{1}{2}$
D) $1$

## Answer
C) $\dfrac{1}{2}$

## Explanation
Rationalize by multiplying by the conjugate:

$$\frac{\sqrt{x + 1} - 1}{x} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{x + 1} + 1}{\sqrt{x + 1} + 1} = \frac{(x + 1) - 1}{x(\sqrt{x + 1} + 1)} = \frac{x}{x(\sqrt{x + 1} + 1)}$$

For $x \neq 0$:
$$= \frac{1}{\sqrt{x + 1} + 1}$$

$$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1}{\sqrt{x + 1} + 1} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1} + 1} = \frac{1}{2}$$
