Evaluate the limit:

$\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{4 - x}{\sqrt{x} - 2}$

## Options
A) $-4$
B) $-2$
C) $2$
D) $4$

## Answer
A) $-4$

## Explanation
Method 1 — Rationalize the denominator. Multiply by $\dfrac{\sqrt{x} + 2}{\sqrt{x} + 2}$:

$$\frac{4 - x}{\sqrt{x} - 2} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{x} + 2}{\sqrt{x} + 2} = \frac{(4 - x)(\sqrt{x} + 2)}{x - 4}$$

Since $4 - x = -(x - 4)$:

$$= \frac{-(x - 4)(\sqrt{x} + 2)}{x - 4} = -(\sqrt{x} + 2)$$

$$\lim_{x \to 4} -(\sqrt{x} + 2) = -(2 + 2) = -4$$
