Let $f(x) = x^3 - 6x^2 + 9x$ on the interval $[0, 3]$. What value(s) of $c$ in $(0, 3)$ are guaranteed by the Mean Value Theorem?

## Options
A) $c = 1$ only
B) $c = 2$ only
C) $c = 1$ and $c = 2$
D) $c = 3/2$ only

## Answer
C) $c = 1$ and $c = 2$

## Explanation
$f$ is a polynomial, so MVT applies. Average rate of change:
$f(0) = 0$, $f(3) = 27 - 54 + 27 = 0$
$(f(3) - f(0))/(3 - 0) = 0/3 = 0$

$f'(x) = 3x^2 - 12x + 9$. Set $f'(c) = 0$:
$3c^2 - 12c + 9 = 0$
$c^2 - 4c + 3 = 0$
$(c - 1)(c - 3) = 0$
$c = 1$ or $c = 3$

Only $c = 1$ is in $(0, 3)$. ($c = 3$ is on the boundary, not in the open interval.)

Correction: $c^2 - 4c + 3 = 0$ gives $c = 1$ or $c = 3$. Only $c = 1 \in (0, 3)$.

Answer: A) $c = 1$ only.
