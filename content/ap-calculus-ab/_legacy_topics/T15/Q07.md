Let $f(x) = 2x^3 - 3x^2 - 12x + 5$ on $[-1, 3]$. Find all critical points and determine the absolute maximum and absolute minimum of $f$ on $[-1, 3]$.

$f'(x) = 6x^2 - 6x - 12 = 6(x^2 - x - 2) = 6(x-2)(x+1)$. Critical points at $x = 2$ and $x = -1$ (endpoint). Evaluate: $f(-1) = -2 + 3 + 12 + 5 = 18$, $f(2) = 16 - 12 - 24 + 5 = -15$, $f(3) = 54 - 27 - 36 + 5 = -4$. Absolute maximum: $18$ at $x = -1$. Absolute minimum: $-15$ at $x = 2$.
