Let $f$ be a function that is differentiable at $x = a$. Which of the following statements must be true?

## Options
A) $f$ is continuous at $x = a$.
B) $f$ is continuous on some open interval containing $a$.
C) $f''(a)$ exists.
D) $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} f(x)$ does not exist.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
If $f$ is differentiable at $x = a$, then $f$ must be continuous at $x = a$. This is a fundamental theorem: differentiability implies continuity at the same point. However, differentiability at a single point does not guarantee continuity on an interval (B), does not guarantee the second derivative exists (C), and in fact guarantees that $\lim_{x \to a} f(x)$ DOES exist and equals $f(a)$ (making D false).
