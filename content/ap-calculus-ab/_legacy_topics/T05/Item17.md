The graph of $y = f(x)$ is shown below.

```
  y
  |
 4|     ____*
  |    /    \
 2|   /      \___
  |  /             \
 0|_/_______________*___ x
  |  -2    0    2    4
  |  *
 -2|
```

Assume the graph has a sharp corner at $x = 0$ and a horizontal tangent at $x = 2$.

(a) Estimate $f'(-1)$ by approximating the slope of the tangent line.

(b) What is the value of $f'(2)$? Explain.

(c) At which $x$-values in the visible domain is $f$ not differentiable? Justify each answer.

(d) Is $f$ continuous at $x = 0$? Is $f$ differentiable at $x = 0$? Explain the relationship.

## Answer
(a) At $x = -1$, the graph appears to pass through approximately $(-2, -1)$ and $(0, 2)$, so the secant line (approximating the tangent) has slope:
$$f'(-1) \approx \frac{2 - (-1)}{0 - (-2)} = \frac{3}{2} = 1.5$$

(b) $f'(2) = 0$ because the problem states there is a horizontal tangent at $x = 2$, and the derivative equals the slope of the tangent line.

(c) $f$ is not differentiable at $x = 0$ because there is a sharp corner (the left-hand and right-hand derivatives do not agree). If there are other corners or cusps visible, those would also be points of non-differentiability.

(d) $f$ appears to be continuous at $x = 0$ because the graph has no break, jump, or hole there. However, $f$ is NOT differentiable at $x = 0$ due to the sharp corner. This illustrates that continuity at a point does NOT guarantee differentiability at that point.

## Explanation
This multi-part problem integrates graphical derivative estimation, recognition of horizontal tangents, identification of non-differentiability from visual features, and the continuity-differentiability relationship. It tests CED 2.1, 2.2, and 2.3 skills.
