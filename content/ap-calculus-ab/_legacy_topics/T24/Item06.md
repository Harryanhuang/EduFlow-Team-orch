A particle moves along the x-axis with velocity v(t) = 6 minus 2t, where v is in cm/sec and t is in seconds. The total distance traveled by the particle from t = 0 to t = 4 is

## Options (if mcq)
A) 8 cm
B) 10 cm
C) 12 cm
D) 16 cm

## Answer
B

## Explanation
First find when velocity changes sign: v(t) = 6 minus 2t = 0 when t = 3.
From t = 0 to 3: v is positive, so distance = integral from 0 to 3 of (6 minus 2t) dt = [6t minus t squared] from 0 to 3 = 18 minus 9 = 9.
From t = 3 to 4: v is negative, so distance = integral from 3 to 4 of |6 minus 2t| dt = integral of (2t minus 6) dt = [t squared minus 6t] from 3 to 4 = (16 minus 24) minus (9 minus 18) = negative 8 minus (negative 9) = 1.
Total distance = 9 + 1 = 10 cm.
