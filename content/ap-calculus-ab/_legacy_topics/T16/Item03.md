If $f'(c) = 0$ and $f'(x)$ changes from positive to negative at $x = c$, which of the following must be true?

## Options
A) $f$ has a local minimum at $x = c$.
B) $f$ has a local maximum at $x = c$.
C) $f$ has a point of inflection at $x = c$.
D) $f$ is constant at $x = c$.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
By the First Derivative Test, if $f'(c) = 0$ and $f'$ changes from positive to negative at $x = c$, then $f$ has a local maximum at $x = c$. The function transitions from increasing to decreasing at this critical point.
