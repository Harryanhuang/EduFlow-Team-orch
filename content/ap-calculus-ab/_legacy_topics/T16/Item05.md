Let $f(x) = x^3 - 3x$. Find all critical points of $f$, and determine the intervals on which $f$ is increasing and decreasing.

## Answer
Critical points: $x = -1$ and $x = 1$.

Increasing on $(-\infty, -1) \cup (1, \infty)$.
Decreasing on $(-1, 1)$.

## Explanation
$f'(x) = 3x^2 - 3 = 3(x^2 - 1) = 3(x + 1)(x - 1)$. Setting $f'(x) = 0$ gives $x = -1$ and $x = 1$. Test intervals: For $x < -1$, pick $x = -2$: $f'(-2) = 3(4 - 1) = 9 > 0$, so increasing. For $-1 < x < 1$, pick $x = 0$: $f'(0) = -3 < 0$, so decreasing. For $x > 1$, pick $x = 2$: $f'(2) = 3(4 - 1) = 9 > 0$, so increasing.
