The graph of $f'(x)$ is shown below. The graph has $x$-intercepts at $x = -2, 0, 2$; it is positive on $(-\infty, -2)$ and $(-2, 0)$, and negative on $(0, 2)$ and $(2, \infty)$. No other information is given.

(a) Find all critical points of $f$.
(b) On what intervals is $f$ increasing? Decreasing?
(c) Find the $x$-coordinates of all local extrema of $f$ and classify each.

## Answer
(a) Critical points: $x = -2, 0, 2$.
(b) Increasing on $(-\infty, 0)$; decreasing on $(0, \infty)$.
(c) Local maximum at $x = 0$; no local extremum at $x = -2$ or $x = 2$.

## Explanation
(a) Critical points occur where $f'(x) = 0$ or $f'$ is undefined. From the graph, $f'(x) = 0$ at $x = -2, 0, 2$.
(b) $f'(x) > 0$ on $(-\infty, 0)$, so $f$ is increasing there. $f'(x) < 0$ on $(0, \infty)$, so $f$ is decreasing there.
(c) At $x = -2$, $f'$ does not change sign (stays positive), so no local extremum. At $x = 0$, $f'$ changes from $+$ to $-$, so local maximum. At $x = 2$, $f'$ changes from $-$ to $-$, so no local extremum.
