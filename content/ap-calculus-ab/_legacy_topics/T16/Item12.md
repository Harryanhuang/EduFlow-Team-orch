Let $g$ be a function such that $g'(x) = x^2(x - 5)$. Which of the following correctly describes the local extrema of $g$?

## Options
A) Local maximum at $x = 0$; local minimum at $x = 5$
B) Local maximum at $x = 5$; local minimum at $x = 0$
C) Local minimum at $x = 5$; no local maximum
D) Local minimum at $x = 0$; no local maximum

## Answer
C

## Explanation
$f'(x) = x^2(x - 5)$. Critical points: $x = 0$ and $x = 5$. Sign analysis: For $x < 0$, $x^2 > 0$ and $(x - 5) < 0$, so $f'(x) < 0$. For $0 < x < 5$, $x^2 > 0$ and $(x - 5) < 0$, so $f'(x) < 0$. For $x > 5$, both factors positive, so $f'(x) > 0$. At $x = 0$, $f'$ does not change sign (stays negative), so no local extremum. At $x = 5$, $f'$ changes from negative to positive, so local minimum at $x = 5$.
