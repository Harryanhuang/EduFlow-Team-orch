A rectangle has a perimeter of 60 cm. Let $x$ be the length of one side. Express the area $A$ as a function of $x$, find all critical points, and determine which gives a maximum area.
$A(x) = x(30 - x) = 30x - x^2$ for $0 < x < 30$. Critical point at $x = 15$. This gives maximum area of $A = 225$ cm$^2$ (the dimensions are $15 \times 15$, a square).
