Let $f(x) = x^2 - 4x + 3$. On which interval is $f$ decreasing?

## Options
A) $(-\infty, 2)$
B) $(2, \infty)$
C) $(-\infty, \infty)$
D) $(0, 4)$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$f'(x) = 2x - 4$. Setting $f'(x) = 0$ gives $x = 2$ as the critical point. For $x < 2$, $f'(x) < 0$, so $f$ is decreasing on $(-\infty, 2)$. For $x > 2$, $f'(x) > 0$, so $f$ is increasing on $(2, \infty)$.
