A rectangle is inscribed under the curve $y = 16 - x^2$, with its base on the x-axis. The vertices on the curve have x-coordinates $-a$ and $a$, where $a > 0$.

(a) Write a function in terms of $a$ that gives the area of the rectangle.
(b) Find the value of $a$ that maximizes this area.
(c) What is the maximum area?

## Answer
(a) $A(a) = 2a(16 - a^2) = 32a - 2a^3$

(b) $a = \frac{4}{\sqrt{3}}$

(c) $A_{max} = \frac{256\sqrt{3}}{9}$

## Explanation
(a) The height of the rectangle at $x = a$ is $y = 16 - a^2$. The width is $2a$. So $A = (2a)(16 - a^2) = 32a - 2a^3$.

(b) $A'(a) = 32 - 6a^2 = 0$, so $a^2 = \frac{32}{6} = \frac{16}{3}$, giving $a = \frac{4}{\sqrt{3}}$.

(c) $A_{max} = 32\left(\frac{4}{\sqrt{3}}\right) - 2\left(\frac{4}{\sqrt{3}}\right)^3 = \frac{128}{\sqrt{3}} - 2\left(\frac{64}{3\sqrt{3}}\right) = \frac{128}{\sqrt{3}} - \frac{128}{3\sqrt{3}} = \frac{384 - 128}{3\sqrt{3}} = \frac{256}{3\sqrt{3}} = \frac{256\sqrt{3}}{9}$.
