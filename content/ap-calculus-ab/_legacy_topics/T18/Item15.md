A rectangular field will be fenced on three sides, with one long side being an existing wall. If the area enclosed must be 1800 square meters, what is the minimum length of fencing needed?

A) $60\sqrt{3}$ m
B) $90\sqrt{2}$ m
C) $60\sqrt{6}$ m
D) $120$ m

## Answer
D

## Explanation
Let $x$ be the length parallel to the wall and $y$ the length of each perpendicular side. Area: $xy = 1800$. Fencing: $F = x + 2y = x + \frac{3600}{x}$. $F' = 1 - \frac{3600}{x^2} = 0$, so $x^2 = 3600$, $x = 60$. Then $y = 1800/60 = 30$. Minimum fencing: $F = 60 + 2(30) = 120$ meters. By AM-GM: $F = x + 3600/x \ge 2\sqrt{3600} = 120$, with equality at $x = 60$.
