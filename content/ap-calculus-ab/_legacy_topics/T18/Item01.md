A rectangle has a fixed perimeter of 60 centimeters. Which of the following expressions represents the area of the rectangle in terms of its width $x$?

A) $A = 60x - 2x^2$
B) $A = 30x - x^2$
C) $A = x(30 - x)$
D) $A = 60x - x^2$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For a rectangle with width $x$ and length $y$, the perimeter constraint is $2x + 2y = 60$, so $x + y = 30$ and $y = 30 - x$. The area is $A = xy = x(30 - x) = 30x - x^2$. Both options B and C are algebraically equivalent.
