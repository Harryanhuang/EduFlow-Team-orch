What is dy/dx at the point (1, 2) on the curve x³ + y³ = 9?

## Options
A) -1/4
B) -1/2
C) 1/4
D) -2

## Answer
A) -1/4

## Explanation
Differentiate implicitly: 3x² + 3y²(dy/dx) = 0. Solve: dy/dx = -x²/y². At (1, 2): dy/dx = -1/4. Verify the point is on the curve: 1³ + 2³ = 1 + 8 = 9. ✓
