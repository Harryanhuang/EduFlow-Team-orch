What is d²y/dx² of the curve x² + y² = 1 at the point (1/2, √3/2)?

## Options
A) -8√3/9
B) -4√3/3
C) -2√3
D) -√3/3

## Answer
A) -8√3/9

## Explanation
From dy/dx = -x/y, apply the quotient rule: d²y/dx² = [(-1)(y) - (-x)(dy/dx)]/y² = [-y + x(dy/dx)]/y². Substitute dy/dx = -x/y: d²y/dx² = -(x² + y²)/y³. Since x² + y² = 1: d²y/dx² = -1/y³. At y = √3/2: d²y/dx² = -1/(√3/2)³ = -8/(3√3) = -8√3/9.
