At which points on the curve x² + y² = 4x - 2y is the tangent line vertical?

## Options
A) (2, -1 + √5) and (2, -1 - √5)
B) (2 + √5, -1) and (2 - √5, -1)
C) (4, 0) and (0, -2)
D) (1, 0) and (3, -2)

## Answer
B) (2 + √5, -1) and (2 - √5, -1)

## Explanation
Differentiate implicitly: 2x + 2y(dy/dx) = 4 - 2(dy/dx). Solve: dy/dx(2y + 2) = 4 - 2x, so dy/dx = (4 - 2x)/(2y + 2) = (2 - x)/(y + 1). Vertical tangent when denominator = 0: y + 1 = 0, so y = -1. Substitute into curve: x² + 1 = 4x + 2, giving x² - 4x - 1 = 0. By the quadratic formula: x = (4 ± √20)/2 = 2 ± √5. The points are (2 + √5, -1) and (2 - √5, -1).
