Find the second derivative d²y/dx² of the circle x² + y² = r² in terms of x and y.

<answer>
d²y/dx² = -r²/y³
</answer>

<explanation>
From dy/dx = -x/y, apply the quotient rule: d²y/dx² = [(-1)(y) - (-x)(dy/dx)]/y² = [-y + x(dy/dx)]/y². Substitute dy/dx = -x/y: d²y/dx² = [-y - x²/y]/y² = -(x² + y²)/y³. Since x² + y² = r²: d²y/dx² = -r²/y³.
</explanation>
