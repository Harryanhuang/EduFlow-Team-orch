Given x^(2/3) + y^(2/3) = a^(2/3), where a > 0 is a constant, find dy/dx in terms of x and y.

## Answer
dy/dx = -(y/x)^(1/3) = -y^(1/3) / x^(1/3)

## Explanation
Differentiate both sides: (2/3)x^(-1/3) + (2/3)y^(-1/3)(dy/dx) = 0. Subtract (2/3)x^(-1/3): (2/3)y^(-1/3)(dy/dx) = -(2/3)x^(-1/3). Divide by (2/3)y^(-1/3): dy/dx = -x^(-1/3) / y^(-1/3) = -y^(1/3) / x^(1/3) = -(y/x)^(1/3).
