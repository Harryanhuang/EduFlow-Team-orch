What is dy/dx at the point (π/4, π/8) on the curve cos(x + 2y) = 0?

## Options
A) -1/2
B) -2
C) 1/2
D) 1

## Answer
A) -1/2

## Explanation
Differentiate implicitly: -sin(x + 2y)(1 + 2·dy/dx) = 0. At (π/4, π/8): x + 2y = π/4 + π/4 = π/2. Since sin(π/2) = 1 ≠ 0: 1 + 2·dy/dx = 0, so dy/dx = -1/2. Verify: cos(π/4 + π/4) = cos(π/2) = 0. The point is on the curve.
