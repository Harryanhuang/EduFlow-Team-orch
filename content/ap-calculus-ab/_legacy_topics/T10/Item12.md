At which point(s) on the curve x²y + y² = 5 is the tangent line horizontal?

## Options
A) (0, √5) and (0, -√5)
B) (√5, 0) and (-√5, 0)
C) (1, 2) and (-1, -2)
D) (0, 5) and (0, -5)

## Answer
A) (0, √5) and (0, -√5)

## Explanation
Differentiate implicitly: 2xy + x²(dy/dx) + 2y(dy/dx) = 0. Solve: dy/dx = -2xy/(x² + 2y). Horizontal tangent when numerator = 0: x = 0 (since y ≠ 0). Substitute x = 0 into the curve: y² = 5, so y = ±√5. The points are (0, √5) and (0, -√5).
