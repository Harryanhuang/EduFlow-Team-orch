A particle moves along a straight line with position $s(t)$, velocity $v(t)$, and acceleration $a(t)$. Complete the following table by stating what each condition implies about the particle's motion:

| Condition | Motion |
|---|---|
| $v(t) > 0$ | ? |
| $v(t) < 0$ | ? |
| $v(t) = 0$ | ? |
| $a(t) > 0$ and $v(t) > 0$ | ? |
| $a(t) > 0$ and $v(t) < 0$ | ? |
| $a(t)$ and $v(t)$ have the same sign | ? |
| $a(t)$ and $v(t)$ have opposite signs | ? |

## Answer
| Condition | Motion |
|---|---|
| $v(t) > 0$ | Particle is moving right (or in the positive direction) |
| $v(t) < 0$ | Particle is moving left (or in the negative direction) |
| $v(t) = 0$ | Particle is momentarily at rest |
| $a(t) > 0$ and $v(t) > 0$ | Particle is moving right and speeding up |
| $a(t) > 0$ and $v(t) < 0$ | Particle is moving left and slowing down |
| $a(t)$ and $v(t)$ have the same sign | Particle is speeding up |
| $a(t)$ and $v(t)$ have opposite signs | Particle is slowing down |
