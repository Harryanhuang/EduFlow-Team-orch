The function $C(t)$ gives the temperature (in degrees Celsius) of a cooling metal bar $t$ minutes after it is removed from a furnace. Which of the following statements best interprets the meaning of $C'(5) = -3$?

## Options
A) The temperature of the metal bar after 5 minutes is $-3$ degrees Celsius.
B) The temperature of the metal bar is decreasing at a rate of 3 degrees Celsius per minute at $t = 5$ minutes.
C) The temperature of the metal bar decreases by 3 degrees Celsius every 5 minutes.
D) The average rate of change of the temperature over the first 5 minutes is $-3$ degrees Celsius per minute.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The derivative $C'(5) = -3$ represents the **instantaneous rate of change** of the temperature at $t = 5$. The units of $C'(t)$ are degrees Celsius per minute. The negative sign indicates the temperature is decreasing. So at $t = 5$ minutes, the temperature is decreasing at 3 degrees Celsius per minute. Choice (A) misinterprets the derivative as a function value. Choice (C) describes a constant rate over an interval, not an instantaneous rate. Choice (D) describes an average rate of change, not an instantaneous one.
