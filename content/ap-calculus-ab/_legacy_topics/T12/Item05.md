Let $P(t)$ represent the population of a city in thousands, where $t$ is measured in years since 2020. Which of the following represents the average rate of change of the population from 2022 to 2025?

## Options
A) $P'(3)$
B) $\displaystyle\frac{P(5) - P(2)}{5 - 2}$
C) $\displaystyle\frac{P(2025) - P(2022)}{2025 - 2022}$
D) $P(5) - P(2)$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The average rate of change of $P$ from $t = a$ to $t = b$ is $\frac{P(b) - P(a)}{b - a}$. Since $t = 0$ corresponds to 2020, the year 2022 corresponds to $t = 2$ and 2025 corresponds to $t = 5$. The average rate of change is $\frac{P(5) - P(2)}{5 - 2}$, measured in thousands of people per year.
