The rate of change of the price $P(t)$ of a commodity is modeled by $P'(t) = 6 - 2t + 0.5\sin(t)$ dollars per week for $0 \leq t \leq 8$. The initial price is $P(0) = 20$ dollars.

(a) At what time $t$ is the price increasing most rapidly?
(b) At what time $t$ is the price decreasing most rapidly?
(c) What is the net change in price from $t = 0$ to $t = 8$?
(d) Is the price ever increasing at a rate faster than 7 dollars per week? Justify.

## Answer
(a) Price increases most rapidly when $P'(t)$ is maximized. $P''(t) = -2 + 0.5\cos(t)$. Setting $P''(t) = 0$: $\cos(t) = 4$, which has no solution. Since $-2 + 0.5\cos(t) \leq -2 + 0.5 = -1.5 < 0$ for all $t$, $P'(t)$ is always decreasing. So the maximum rate of increase occurs at the left endpoint $t = 0$: $P'(0) = 6 + 0 = 6$ dollars per week.

(b) Since $P'(t)$ is strictly decreasing, the minimum rate occurs at the right endpoint $t = 8$: $P'(8) = 6 - 16 + 0.5\sin(8) = -10 + 0.5\sin(8) \approx -10 + 0.5(-0.989) \approx -10.49$ dollars per week. The price is decreasing most rapidly at $t = 8$.

(c) Net change = $\int_0^8 P'(t)\, dt = \int_0^8 (6 - 2t + 0.5\sin(t))\, dt = [6t - t^2 - 0.5\cos(t)]_0^8 = (48 - 64 - 0.5\cos(8)) - (0 - 0.5) = -16 - 0.5\cos(8) + 0.5 = -15.5 - 0.5\cos(8) \approx -15.5 - 0.5(-0.146) \approx -15.43$ dollars.

(d) From part (a), the maximum value of $P'(t)$ is $P'(0) = 6$, which is less than 7. So the price is never increasing at a rate faster than 7 dollars per week.
