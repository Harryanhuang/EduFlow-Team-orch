The function $C(t)$ represents the amount of a chemical (in grams) dissolved in a solution at time $t$ minutes. Interpret the meaning of $C'(10) = -0.5$ in context, including units.

## Answer
At $t = 10$ minutes, the amount of chemical dissolved in the solution is decreasing at a rate of 0.5 grams per minute. The units of $C'(t)$ are grams per minute, and the negative sign indicates the quantity is decreasing.
