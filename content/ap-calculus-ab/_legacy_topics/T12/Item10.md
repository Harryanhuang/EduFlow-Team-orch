The velocity of a particle moving along the $x$-axis is given by $v(t) = e^t - 2$ for $0 \leq t \leq 3$. The particle is at position $x = 5$ when $t = 0$. What is the position of the particle at $t = 2$?

## Options
A) $e^2 - 4$
B) $e^2$
C) $e^2 + 1$
D) $e^2 + 3$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Position: $x(2) = x(0) + \int_0^2 v(t)\, dt = 5 + \int_0^2 (e^t - 2)\, dt = 5 + [e^t - 2t]_0^2 = 5 + (e^2 - 4) - (1 - 0) = 5 + e^2 - 4 - 1 = e^2$.
