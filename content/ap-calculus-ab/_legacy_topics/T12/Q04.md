The rate of water flow into a tank is $R(t) = 20 + 10\sin(t)$ liters per minute for $0 \leq t \leq 10$. The tank initially contains 50 liters.

(a) Write an expression for the total amount of water in the tank at time $t$.
(b) How much water is in the tank at $t = 6$ minutes?
(c) At what rate is the water level changing at $t = \pi$ minutes?

## Answer
(a) $W(t) = 50 + \int_0^t (20 + 10\sin(u))\, du = 50 + [20u - 10\cos(u)]_0^t = 50 + 20t - 10\cos(t) + 10 = 60 + 20t - 10\cos(t)$ liters.

(b) $W(6) = 60 + 120 - 10\cos(6) = 180 - 10\cos(6) \approx 180 - 10(0.960) \approx 170.4$ liters.

(c) The rate of change is $R(\pi) = 20 + 10\sin(\pi) = 20 + 0 = 20$ liters per minute.
