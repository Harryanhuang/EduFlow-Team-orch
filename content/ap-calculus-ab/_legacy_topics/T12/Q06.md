A particle moves along the $y$-axis with velocity $v(t) = t\cos(t)$ for $0 \leq t \leq 2\pi$. The particle is at position $y = 3$ when $t = 0$.

(a) For what values of $t$ is the particle moving upward? Downward?
(b) What is the position of the particle at $t = \pi$?
(c) Write, but do not evaluate, an expression for the total distance traveled by the particle from $t = 0$ to $t = 2\pi$.

## Answer
(a) Moving upward when $v(t) > 0$: $t\cos(t) > 0$. Since $t > 0$ on $(0, 2\pi]$, we need $\cos(t) > 0$. This holds for $0 < t < \pi/2$ and $3\pi/2 < t < 2\pi$. Moving downward when $\cos(t) < 0$: $\pi/2 < t < 3\pi/2$.

(b) $y(\pi) = y(0) + \int_0^\pi t\cos(t)\, dt$. Using integration by parts: $u = t$, $du = dt$, $dv = \cos(t)\, dt$, $v = \sin(t)$. $\int t\cos(t)\, dt = t\sin(t) - \int \sin(t)\, dt = t\sin(t) + \cos(t) + C$. So $y(\pi) = 3 + [\pi\sin(\pi) + \cos(\pi)] - [0 + \cos(0)] = 3 + (0 - 1) - (1) = 3 - 2 = 1$.

(c) Total distance = $\int_0^{\pi/2} t\cos(t)\, dt + \int_{\pi/2}^{3\pi/2} -t\cos(t)\, dt + \int_{3\pi/2}^{2\pi} t\cos(t)\, dt$.
