A man 6 feet tall walks away from a streetlight 15 feet high at a rate of 4 ft/s. Let $x$ be the length of his shadow and $d$ be his distance from the streetlight. What is $\frac{dx}{dt}$?

## Options
A) $\frac{4}{3}$ ft/s
B) $2$ ft/s
C) $\frac{8}{3}$ ft/s
D) $4$ ft/s

## Answer
C) $\frac{8}{3}$ ft/s

## Explanation
By similar triangles: $\frac{6}{x} = \frac{15}{x + d}$, so $6(x + d) = 15x$, giving $9x = 6d$, so $x = \frac{2}{3}d$.

Differentiating with respect to $t$: $\frac{dx}{dt} = \frac{2}{3}\frac{dd}{dt}$.

Since $\frac{dd}{dt} = 4$ ft/s (the man's walking speed), we have:
$\frac{dx}{dt} = \frac{2}{3} \cdot 4 = \frac{8}{3}$ ft/s.
