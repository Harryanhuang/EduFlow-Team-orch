The edge length $s$ of a cube is increasing at a rate of 2 cm/s. At the instant when $s = 4$ cm, find the rate at which the surface area of the cube is increasing. Recall that the surface area of a cube is $SA = 6s^2$.

## Answer
$\frac{d(SA)}{dt} = 96$ cm$^2$/s

## Explanation
$SA = 6s^2$. Differentiating with respect to $t$:
$\frac{d(SA)}{dt} = 12s \frac{ds}{dt}$

Given $\frac{ds}{dt} = 2$ and $s = 4$:
$\frac{d(SA)}{dt} = 12(4)(2) = 96$ cm$^2$/s
