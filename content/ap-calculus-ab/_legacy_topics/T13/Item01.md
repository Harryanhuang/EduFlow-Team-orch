The radius $r$ of a circle is increasing at a rate of 3 cm/s. At the instant when $r = 5$ cm, what is the rate of change of the area of the circle with respect to time?

## Options
A) $15\pi$ cm$^2$/s
B) $25\pi$ cm$^2$/s
C) $30\pi$ cm$^2$/s
D) $45\pi$ cm$^2$/s

## Answer
C) $30\pi$ cm$^2$/s

## Explanation
The area of a circle is $A = \pi r^2$. Differentiating both sides with respect to $t$:
$\frac{dA}{dt} = 2\pi r \frac{dr}{dt}$

Given $\frac{dr}{dt} = 3$ and $r = 5$:
$\frac{dA}{dt} = 2\pi(5)(3) = 30\pi$ cm$^2$/s
