A rectangle has length $\ell$ and width $w$, both changing with time. At a certain instant, $\ell = 8$ cm, $w = 5$ cm, $\frac{d\ell}{dt} = 3$ cm/s, and $\frac{dw}{dt} = -2$ cm/s. What is the rate of change of the area of the rectangle at that instant?

## Options
A) $-1$ cm$^2$/s
B) $1$ cm$^2$/s
C) $11$ cm$^2$/s
D) $34$ cm$^2$/s

## Answer
A) $-1$ cm$^2$/s

## Explanation
$A = \ell w$. By the product rule:
$\frac{dA}{dt} = \ell \frac{dw}{dt} + w \frac{d\ell}{dt}$

Substituting: $\frac{dA}{dt} = 8(-2) + 5(3) = -16 + 15 = -1$ cm$^2$/s
