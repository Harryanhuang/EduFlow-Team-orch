If f(x) = cos(x^3), what is f'(x)?

## Options
A) -sin(x^3)
B) -3x^2 sin(x^3)
C) 3x^2 sin(x^3)
D) -3x sin(x^3)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Outer: cos(u), inner: u = x^3. d/dx[cos(u)] = -sin(u) * du/dx = -sin(x^3) * 3x^2 = -3x^2 sin(x^3).
