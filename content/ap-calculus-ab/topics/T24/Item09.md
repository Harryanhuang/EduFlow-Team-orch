The base of a solid is the region in the xy-plane bounded by the curve y = 4 times the quantity x(1 minus x), the x-axis, x = 0, and x = 1. Cross-sections perpendicular to the x-axis are equilateral triangles with one side in the base. The volume of the solid is

## Options (if mcq)
A) square root of 3 over 15
B) square root of 3 over 10
C) square root of 3 over 5
D) 2 times square root of 3 over 5

## Answer
D

## Explanation
The side length of the equilateral triangle at position x is the vertical distance: s = 4x(1 minus x).
Area of equilateral triangle = (square root of 3 / 4) times s squared = (square root of 3 / 4) times 16 x squared (1 minus 2x + x squared) = 4 times square root of 3 times (x squared minus 2x cubed + x to the fourth).
V = integral from 0 to 1 of Area dx = 4 times square root of 3 times integral from 0 to 1 of (x squared minus 2x cubed + x to the fourth) dx = 4 times square root of 3 times [x cubed/3 minus x to the fourth/2 + x to the fifth/5] from 0 to 1 = 4 times square root of 3 times (1/3 minus 1/2 + 1/5) = 4 times square root of 3 times [(10 minus 15 + 6)/30] = 4 times square root of 3 times (1/30) = (2 times square root of 3)/5.
