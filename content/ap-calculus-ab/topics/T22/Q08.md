A 500-gallon tank initially contains 200 pounds of salt in solution. Brine containing 0.5 pounds of salt per gallon flows in at 3 gallons per minute, and the mixture flows out at 3 gallons per minute. Write the differential equation for the amount of salt \(S(t)\) and find \(S(30)\).
Rate in: \(3 \times 0.5 = 1.5\) lb/min. Rate out: \(3 \times \frac{S}{500} = \frac{3S}{500}\) lb/min. So \(\frac{dS}{dt} = 1.5 - \frac{3S}{500}\). This is linear: \(\frac{dS}{dt} + \frac{3}{500}S = 1.5\). Integrating factor \(e^{3t/500}\): \(S(t) = 250 + Ce^{-3t/500}\). Using \(S(0) = 200\): \(200 = 250 + C \implies C = -50\). So \(S(t) = 250 - 50e^{-3t/500}\). At \(t = 30\): \(S(30) = 250 - 50e^{-0.18} \approx 250 - 50(0.835) \approx 250 - 41.75 = 208.25\) lb.
