According to the existence and uniqueness theorem for first-order differential equations, what condition guarantees that the initial value problem \(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = f(x, y)\), \(y(x_{0}) = y_{0}\) has a unique solution in some interval containing \(x_{0}\)?

A) \(f\) is continuous at \((x_{0}, y_{0})\)
B) \(f\) and \(\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial y}\) are continuous in a rectangle containing \((x_{0}, y_{0})\)
C) \(f(x, y)\) is defined for all real numbers \(x\) and \(y\)
D) \(y_{0} = 0\) and \(f\) is a polynomial in \(x\)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The existence and uniqueness theorem for first-order ODEs states that if \(f(x, y)\) and \(\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\) are both continuous in an open rectangle containing the initial point \((x_{0}, y_{0})\), then there exists a unique solution \(y = \phi(x)\) passing through that point. Continuity of \(f$ alone (option A) guarantees existence but not uniqueness.
