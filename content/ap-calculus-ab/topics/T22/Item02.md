Which of the following slope fields is consistent with the differential equation \(\frac{dy}{dx} = x\)?

A) Line segments with positive slope in the first and second quadrants, negative slope in the third and fourth quadrants
B) Line segments with slope equal to the x-coordinate at every point
C) Horizontal line segments at \(y = 1\) and vertical line segments at \(x = 0\)
D) Line segments with slope proportional to \(y\) rather than \(x\)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The differential equation \(\frac{dy}{dx} = x\) specifies that the slope at any point \((x, y)\) depends only on the x-coordinate, not on y. At \(x = 0\) the slope is 0; at \(x = 1\) the slope is 1; at \(x = -1\) the slope is \(-1\). This means line segments at all points sharing the same x-value have the same direction, forming vertical "columns" of parallel segments.
