Which of the following is the general solution to the differential equation \(\frac{dy}{dx} = 6x^{2}\)?

A) \(y = 12x + C\)
B) \(y = 2x^{3} + C\)
C) \(y = 18x^{3} + C\)
D) \(y = 6x^{3} + C\)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Integrating both sides with respect to x:
\[\int \frac{dy}{dx}\,dx = \int 6x^{2}\,dx\]
\[y = 6 \cdot \frac{x^{3}}{3} + C = 2x^{3} + C\]
No initial condition is given, so C is an arbitrary constant representing the family of all solutions.
