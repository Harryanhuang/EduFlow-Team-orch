What is the derivative of $y = \arcsin(2x)$?

## Options
A) $\frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 4x^2}}$  B) $\frac{2}{\sqrt{1 - 4x^2}}$  C) $\frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 2x^2}}$  D) $\frac{2}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}}$

## Answer
B) $\frac{2}{\sqrt{1 - 4x^2}}$

## Explanation
Using the chain rule: $\frac{d}{dx}[\arcsin(u)] = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - u^2}} \cdot u'$. With $u = 2x$ and $u' = 2$, we get $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{1 - (2x)^2}} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{1 - 4x^2}}$.
