Let $g(x) = \arccos(x^2)$. What is $g'(x)$?

## Options
A) $\frac{-2x}{\sqrt{1 - x^4}}$  B) $\frac{2x}{\sqrt{1 - x^4}}$  C) $\frac{-1}{\sqrt{1 - x^4}}$  D) $\frac{-2x}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}}$

## Answer
A) $\frac{-2x}{\sqrt{1 - x^4}}$

## Explanation
$\frac{d}{dx}[\arccos(u)] = \frac{-1}{\sqrt{1 - u^2}} \cdot u'$. With $u = x^2$ and $u' = 2x$: $g'(x) = \frac{-1}{\sqrt{1 - (x^2)^2}} \cdot 2x = \frac{-2x}{\sqrt{1 - x^4}}$.
