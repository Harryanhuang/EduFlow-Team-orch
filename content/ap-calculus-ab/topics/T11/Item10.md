Let $h(x) = e^{2x} \cos(3x)$. Find $h''(x)$, the second derivative of $h$.

## Answer
$h''(x) = e^{2x}(-5\cos(3x) - 12\sin(3x))$

## Explanation
First derivative (product rule): $h'(x) = 2e^{2x}\cos(3x) + e^{2x}(-3\sin(3x)) = e^{2x}(2\cos(3x) - 3\sin(3x))$. Second derivative: $h''(x) = 2e^{2x}(2\cos(3x) - 3\sin(3x)) + e^{2x}(-6\sin(3x) - 9\cos(3x)) = e^{2x}(4\cos(3x) - 6\sin(3x) - 6\sin(3x) - 9\cos(3x)) = e^{2x}(-5\cos(3x) - 12\sin(3x))$.
