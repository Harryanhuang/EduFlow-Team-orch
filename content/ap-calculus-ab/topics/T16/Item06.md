Let $f(x) = x^3 - 6x^2 + 9x + 2$. Which of the following gives the local extrema of $f$?

## Options
A) Local maximum at $x = 1$; local minimum at $x = 3$
B) Local minimum at $x = 1$; local maximum at $x = 3$
C) Local maximum at $x = 2$; local minimum at $x = 4$
D) Local minimum at $x = 1$; no local maximum

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$f'(x) = 3x^2 - 12x + 9 = 3(x^2 - 4x + 3) = 3(x - 1)(x - 3)$. Critical points: $x = 1$ and $x = 3$. Sign analysis: For $x < 1$, $f'(x) > 0$. For $1 < x < 3$, $f'(x) < 0$. For $x > 3$, $f'(x) > 0$. By the First Derivative Test: $f'$ changes from $+$ to $-$ at $x = 1$ (local maximum), and from $-$ to $+$ at $x = 3$ (local minimum).
