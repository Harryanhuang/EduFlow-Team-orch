Let $f(x) = x e^{x}$. What are the intervals of increase and decrease of $f$?

## Options
A) Increasing on $(-\infty, -1)$; decreasing on $(-1, \infty)$
B) Increasing on $(-1, \infty)$; decreasing on $(-\infty, -1)$
C) Increasing on $(-\infty, \infty)$
D) Decreasing on $(-\infty, \infty)$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Using the product rule: $f'(x) = e^x + xe^x = e^x(1 + x)$. Since $e^x > 0$ for all $x$, the sign of $f'$ depends on $(1 + x)$. $f'(x) < 0$ for $x < -1$, so $f$ is decreasing on $(-\infty, -1)$. $f'(x) > 0$ for $x > -1$, so $f$ is increasing on $(-1, \infty)$. At $x = -1$, $f' = 0$ and $f'$ changes from negative to positive, so there is a local minimum at $x = -1$.
