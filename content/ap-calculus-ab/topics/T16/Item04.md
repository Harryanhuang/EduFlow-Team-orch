The function $f$ is continuous on $[0, 6]$ and differentiable on $(0, 6)$. The table below gives selected values of $f'(x)$.

| $x$ | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 |
|-----|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $f'(x)$ | $-2$ | $-1$ | $0$ | $1$ | $2$ | $1$ | $0$ |

On which interval is $f$ increasing?

## Options
A) $(0, 2)$
B) $(2, 6)$
C) $(0, 6)$
D) $(4, 6)$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
From the table, $f'(x) > 0$ for $x \in (2, 6)$ (the values are 1, 2, 1, and $f'(6) = 0$). Since $f'(x) > 0$ on $(2, 6)$, the function $f$ is increasing on this interval. On $(0, 2)$, $f'(x) < 0$, so $f$ is decreasing there.
