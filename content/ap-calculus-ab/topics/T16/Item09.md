Let $f$ be a function with derivative $f'(x) = (x - 2)^2(x + 1)$. Find all critical points of $f$, determine the intervals of increase and decrease, and classify any local extrema using the First Derivative Test.

## Answer
Critical points: $x = -1$ and $x = 2$.

Increasing on $(-1, \infty)$; decreasing on $(-\infty, -1)$.

Local minimum at $x = -1$. No local extremum at $x = 2$.

## Explanation
Critical points occur where $f'(x) = 0$: $(x - 2)^2(x + 1) = 0$ gives $x = 2$ and $x = -1$. Sign analysis: For $x < -1$, $(x - 2)^2 > 0$ always and $(x + 1) < 0$, so $f'(x) < 0$. For $-1 < x < 2$, $(x - 2)^2 > 0$ and $(x + 1) > 0$, so $f'(x) > 0$. For $x > 2$, both factors positive, so $f'(x) > 0$. At $x = -1$, $f'$ changes from $-$ to $+$, so local minimum. At $x = 2$, $f'$ does not change sign (stays positive), so no local extremum.
