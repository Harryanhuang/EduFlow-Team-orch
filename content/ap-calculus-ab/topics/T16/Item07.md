The graph of $f'$, the derivative of $f$, is a parabola opening upward with $x$-intercepts at $x = -2$ and $x = 4$. At what value(s) of $x$ does $f$ have a local minimum?

## Options
A) $x = -2$ only
B) $x = 4$ only
C) $x = -2$ and $x = 4$
D) $x = 1$ only

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Since $f'$ is a parabola opening upward with roots at $x = -2$ and $x = 4$: $f'(x) > 0$ for $x < -2$, $f'(x) < 0$ for $-2 < x < 4$, and $f'(x) > 0$ for $x > 4$. At $x = -2$, $f'$ changes from $+$ to $-$, so $f$ has a local maximum. At $x = 4$, $f'$ changes from $-$ to $+$, so $f$ has a local minimum.
