The graph of $f'(x)$ is shown below (described as a cubic opening downward with zeros at $x = -3, 1, 4$; positive on $(-3, 1)$; negative on $(-\infty, -3) \cup (1, 4) \cup (4, \infty)$). The graph has a local minimum of $f'$ at $x = -1$ and a local maximum of $f'$ at $x = 2.5$. Describe the behavior of $f$: intervals of increase/decrease and all local extrema.
$f$ is increasing on $(-3, 1)$; decreasing on $(-\infty, -3) \cup (1, \infty)$. Local maximum at $x = 1$; local minimum at $x = -3$. No local extremum at $x = 4$ (where $f' = 0$ but does not change sign).
