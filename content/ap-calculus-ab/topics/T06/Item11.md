The position of a particle moving along a line is given by s(t) = t^3 - 6t^2 + 9t, where t is measured in seconds and s in meters.

(a) Find the velocity of the particle at t = 2 seconds.
(b) Find the acceleration of the particle at t = 2 seconds.
(c) Is the particle speeding up or slowing down at t = 2? Justify your answer.

## Answer
(a) v(t) = s'(t) = 3t^2 - 12t + 9. v(2) = 12 - 24 + 9 = -3 m/s.
(b) a(t) = v'(t) = 6t - 12. a(2) = 12 - 12 = 0 m/s^2.
(c) At t = 2, v(2) = -3 and a(2) = 0. Since a(2) = 0, the velocity is not changing at that instant. The particle is neither speeding up nor slowing down at the exact instant t = 2. (For t just before 2, a < 0 and v < 0, so it was speeding up; for t just after 2, a > 0 and v < 0, so it begins slowing down.)

## Explanation
Velocity is the first derivative of position. Acceleration is the second derivative. A particle is speeding up when velocity and acceleration have the same sign, and slowing down when they have opposite signs. At t = 2, v = -3 (negative) and a = 0, marking the transition point.
