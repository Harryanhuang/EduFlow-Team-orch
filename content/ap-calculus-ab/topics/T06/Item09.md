If f(x) = 2x^(-1/2) + x^(1/2), what is f'(4)?

## Options
A) 1/8
B) 1/4
C) 3/8
D) -1/8

## Answer
A) 1/8

## Explanation
f'(x) = 2*(-1/2)*x^(-3/2) + (1/2)*x^(-1/2) = -x^(-3/2) + (1/2)x^(-1/2).
At x = 4: -4^(-3/2) + (1/2)*4^(-1/2) = -1/8 + (1/2)*(1/2) = -1/8 + 1/4 = 1/8.
