<question>
If \(y = \ln(\sin(2x))\), find \(\dfrac{dy}{dx}\) and evaluate it at \(x = \dfrac{\pi}{6}\).
</question>
<answer>
Apply the chain rule through three layers: \(y = \ln(u)\), \(u = \sin(v)\), \(v = 2x\).

\[\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{\sin(2x)} \cdot \cos(2x) \cdot 2 = 2\cot(2x).\]

Evaluate at \(x = \dfrac{\pi}{6}\):

\[2\cot\!\left(\frac{\pi}{3}\right) = 2 \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{2\sqrt{3}}{3}.\]
</answer>
