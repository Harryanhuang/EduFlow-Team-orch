<question>
Let \(f\) be a twice-differentiable function. Write a formula for the second derivative of

\[h(x) = f(x^2 + 1)\]

in terms of \(f'\), \(f''\), and \(x\).
</question>
<answer>
First derivative (chain rule):

\[h'(x) = f'(x^2 + 1) \cdot 2x.\]

Second derivative (product rule + chain rule):

\[h''(x) = \frac{d}{dx}\bigl[f'(x^2 + 1)\bigr] \cdot 2x + f'(x^2 + 1) \cdot 2.\]

Now \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\bigl[f'(x^2 + 1)\bigr] = f''(x^2 + 1) \cdot 2x\), so

\[h''(x) = (f''(x^2 + 1) \cdot 2x) \cdot 2x + 2f'(x^2 + 1).\]

Simplify:

\[h''(x) = 4x^2\, f''(x^2 + 1) + 2\, f'(x^2 + 1).\]
</answer>
