At which point(s) on the astroid x^(2/3) + y^(2/3) = 4 does the curve have a vertical tangent?

## Options
A) (0, 8) and (0, -8)
B) (8, 0) and (-8, 0)
C) (4, 4) and (-4, -4)
D) (2, 2) and (-2, -2)

## Answer
A) (0, 8) and (0, -8)

## Explanation
Differentiate implicitly: (2/3)x^(-1/3) + (2/3)y^(-1/3)(dy/dx) = 0. Solve: dy/dx = -y^(1/3)/x^(1/3). Vertical tangent when x = 0 (dy/dx undefined). At x = 0: y^(2/3) = 4, so y = ±8. The vertical tangents are at (0, 8) and (0, -8).
