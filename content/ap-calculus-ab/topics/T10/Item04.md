Given the equation xy + sin(y) = 1, find dy/dx in terms of x and y.

## Answer
dy/dx = -y / (x + cos(y))

## Explanation
Differentiate both sides with respect to x using the product rule and chain rule: y + x(dy/dx) + cos(y)(dy/dx) = 0. Factor out dy/dx: dy/dx(x + cos(y)) = -y. Therefore dy/dx = -y / (x + cos(y)).
