Find all points on the curve x² - xy + y² = 3 where the tangent line is horizontal.

<answer>
(1, 2) and (-1, -2)
</answer>

<explanation>
Differentiate implicitly: 2x - y - x(dy/dx) + 2y(dy/dx) = 0. Solve: dy/dx = (y - 2x)/(2y - x). Horizontal tangent when y - 2x = 0, i.e., y = 2x. Substitute into the curve: x² - x(2x) + (2x)² = 3 → 3x² = 3 → x = ±1. When x = 1: y = 2. When x = -1: y = -2. Points: (1, 2) and (-1, -2).
</explanation>
