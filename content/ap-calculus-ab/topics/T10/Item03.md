What is the equation of the tangent line to the curve x³ + y³ = 3xy at the point (3/2, 3/2)?

## Options
A) y = -x + 3
B) y = x
C) y = -2x + 9/2
D) y = -x/2 + 9/4

## Answer
A) y = -x + 3

## Explanation
Differentiate implicitly: 3x² + 3y²(dy/dx) = 3y + 3x(dy/dx). Solve: dy/dx = (y - x²)/(y² - x). At (3/2, 3/2): dy/dx = (3/2 - 9/4)/(9/4 - 3/2) = (-3/4)/(3/4) = -1. Tangent line: y - 3/2 = -1(x - 3/2), giving y = -x + 3.
