What is d²y/dx² of the circle x² + y² = 1 at the point (3/5, 4/5)?

## Options
A) -125/64
B) -64/125
C) -25/16
D) -5/4

## Answer
A) -125/64

## Explanation
From dy/dx = -x/y, differentiate again: d²y/dx² = [(-1)(y) - (-x)(dy/dx)]/y² = [-y + x(dy/dx)]/y². Substitute dy/dx = -x/y: d²y/dx² = -(x² + y²)/y³. Since x² + y² = 1: d²y/dx² = -1/y³. At y = 4/5: d²y/dx² = -1/(64/125) = -125/64.
