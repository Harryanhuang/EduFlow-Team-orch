What is d²y/dx² of the curve x² + y² = 1 at the point (1/√2, 1/√2)?

## Options
A) -2√2
B) -√2
C) -1/√2
D) -4

## Answer
A) -2√2

## Explanation
From dy/dx = -x/y, differentiate again: d²y/dx² = [(-1)(y) - (-x)(dy/dx)]/y². Substitute dy/dx = -x/y: d²y/dx² = -(x² + y²)/y³. Since x² + y² = 1: d²y/dx² = -1/y³. At y = 1/√2: d²y/dx² = -1/(1/(2√2)) = -2√2.
