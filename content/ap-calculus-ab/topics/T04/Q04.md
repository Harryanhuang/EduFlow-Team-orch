Let $f(x) = \dfrac{x^2 - 9}{x^2 + x - 6}$. Find all vertical asymptotes of $f$. For each vertical asymptote, evaluate the left-hand and right-hand limits. Be careful to distinguish removable discontinuities from vertical asymptotes.

## Answer
Factor: $f(x) = \dfrac{(x-3)(x+3)}{(x+3)(x-2)}$. The denominator is zero at $x = -3$ and $x = 2$. At $x = -3$, the factor $(x+3)$ cancels with the numerator, so $x = -3$ is a removable discontinuity (hole). At $x = 2$, the numerator is $(-1)(5) = -5 \neq 0$, so $x = 2$ is a vertical asymptote.

One-sided limits at $x = 2$:
- $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 2^{+}} f(x) = \lim_{x \to 2^{+}} \frac{x-3}{x-2} = \frac{-1}{0^{+}} = -\infty$
- $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 2^{-}} f(x) = \lim_{x \to 2^{-}} \frac{x-3}{x-2} = \frac{-1}{0^{-}} = +\infty$

The only vertical asymptote is $x = 2$.
