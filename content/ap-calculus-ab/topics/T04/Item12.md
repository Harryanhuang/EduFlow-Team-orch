Let $f(x) = \dfrac{3x^2 - 12}{x^2 - 4x + 3}$. Which of the following describes the end behavior of $f$?

## Options
A) $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = 3$ and $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to -\infty} f(x) = 3$
B) $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = 0$ and $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to -\infty} f(x) = 0$
C) $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = \infty$ and $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to -\infty} f(x) = -\infty$
D) $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = 3$ and $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to -\infty} f(x) = -3$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Both numerator and denominator have degree 2. The ratio of leading coefficients is $3/1 = 3$. Thus:
$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{3x^2 - 12}{x^2 - 4x + 3} = \lim_{x \to -\infty} \frac{3x^2 - 12}{x^2 - 4x + 3} = 3.$$
The horizontal asymptote is $y = 3$ in both directions.
