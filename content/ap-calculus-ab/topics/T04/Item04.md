Evaluate $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 3^{-}} \frac{2x + 1}{x - 3}$.

## Options
A) $\infty$
B) $-\infty$
C) $7$
D) The limit does not exist as a finite value and is not $\infty$ or $-\infty$.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
As $x \to 3^{-}$, the numerator $2x + 1 \to 7$ (positive), and the denominator $x - 3 \to 0^{-}$ (small negative). A positive quantity divided by a small negative quantity approaches $-\infty$.
