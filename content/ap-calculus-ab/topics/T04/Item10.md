If $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = 4$ and $f(x) = \dfrac{ax^2 + bx + 1}{2x^2 - 3x + 5}$, what is the value of $a$?

## Options
A) $2$
B) $4$
C) $8$
D) $1$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Since the degrees of numerator and denominator are both 2, the limit at infinity is the ratio of leading coefficients: $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{ax^2 + bx + 1}{2x^2 - 3x + 5} = \frac{a}{2}$. Setting this equal to 4 gives $\frac{a}{2} = 4$, so $a = 8$.
