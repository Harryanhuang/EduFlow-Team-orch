A population of fish in a lake grows according to the logistic model $\dfrac{dP}{dt} = 0.1P\left(1 - \dfrac{P}{1000}\right)$, where $P$ is measured in fish. What is the carrying capacity?

A) 100 fish

B) 500 fish

C) 1000 fish

D) 10,000 fish

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The logistic differential equation is $\dfrac{dP}{dt} = rP\left(1 - \dfrac{P}{K}\right)$, where $K$ is the carrying capacity (the maximum sustainable population). Comparing, we see $K = 1000$. At $P = K$, the growth rate $dP/dt = 0$, so the population stops growing and levels off at 1000 fish.
