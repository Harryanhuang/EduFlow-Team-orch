A substance has a half-life of 8 hours. If 80 mg is present initially, how much remains after 24 hours?

A) 10 mg

B) 20 mg

C) 40 mg

D) 5 mg

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Using exponential decay: $N(t) = N_0 \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^{t/T_{1/2}} = 80 \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^{24/8} = 80 \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^3 = 80 \times \dfrac{1}{8} = 10$ mg. Alternatively, using the continuous model: $N(t) = 80e^{-kt}$ where $k = \ln(2)/8$, so $N(24) = 80e^{-3\ln(2)} = 80/8 = 10$ mg.
