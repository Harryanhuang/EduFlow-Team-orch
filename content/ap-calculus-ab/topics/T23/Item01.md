Which differential equation represents exponential growth?

A) $\dfrac{dP}{dt} = kP$ where $k > 0$

B) $\dfrac{dP}{dt} = -kP$ where $k > 0$

C) $\dfrac{dP}{dt} = kP^2$ where $k > 0$

D) $\dfrac{dP}{dt} = k$ where $k > 0$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The differential equation $dP/dt = kP$ with $k > 0$ represents exponential growth because the rate of change is proportional to the current population. When solved, this yields $P(t) = P_0 e^{kt}$, which grows exponentially over time. Option B represents exponential decay. Option C represents nonlinear (quadratic) growth. Option D represents linear growth.
