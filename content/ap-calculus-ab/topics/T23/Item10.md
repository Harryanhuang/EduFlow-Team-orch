A hot object at $100^\circ$C is placed in a room at $25^\circ$C. According to Newton's Law of Cooling, its temperature $T(t)$ satisfies $\dfrac{dT}{dt} = -0.2(T - 25)$. What is the equilibrium solution?

A) $T = 100^\circ$C

B) $T = 25^\circ$C

C) $T = 75^\circ$C

D) $T = 0^\circ$C

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The equilibrium solution occurs when $dT/dt = 0$, which means $T - 25 = 0$, so $T = 25^\circ$C. This is the ambient room temperature, which makes sense: when the object's temperature equals the surroundings, it stops changing. This is called the steady-state solution.
