Let
\[
f(x) = \begin{cases}
2x + 1, & x < 3 \\
7 - x, & x \geq 3
\end{cases}
\]

(a) Find \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 3^-} f(x)\).
(b) Find \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 3^+} f(x)\).
(c) Does \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 3} f(x)\) exist? If so, what is it? If not, explain why.

## Answer
(a) 7
(b) 4
(c) The limit does not exist because the left-hand and right-hand limits are not equal.

## Explanation
(a) As \(x \to 3^-\), we use \(f(x) = 2x + 1\), so \(\lim_{x \to 3^-} f(x) = 2(3) + 1 = 7\).
(b) As \(x \to 3^+\), we use \(f(x) = 7 - x\), so \(\lim_{x \to 3^+} f(x) = 7 - 3 = 4\).
(c) Since \(7 \neq 4\), the left-hand and right-hand limits disagree, so \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 3} f(x)\) does not exist.
