Let \(f(x) = x^2 + 1\). What is the value of \(f(3)\) compared with \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 3} f(x)\)?

## Options
A) \(f(3) = 10\) and the limit is 9.
B) \(f(3) = 9\) and the limit is 10.
C) \(f(3) = 10\) and \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 3} f(x) = 10\).
D) \(f(3)\) is undefined and the limit is 10.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
For the polynomial \(f(x) = x^2 + 1\), we have \(f(3) = 3^2 + 1 = 10\). Polynomials are continuous everywhere, so \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 3} f(x) = f(3) = 10\). This reinforces that for continuous functions, the limit and the function value agree.
