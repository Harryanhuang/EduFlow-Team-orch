The table below gives selected values of a continuous function \(p(x)\).

| \(x\)    | 0.9  | 0.99 | 0.999 | 1.001 | 1.01 | 1.1  |
|----------|------|------|-------|-------|------|------|
| \(p(x)\) | 2.71 | 2.97 | 2.997 | 3.003 | 3.03 | 3.31 |

(a) Estimate \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 1} p(x)\). Justify your answer using the data.
(b) Explain why the function being continuous at \(x = 1\) supports your estimate.

## Answer
(a) 3
(b) Since \(p\) is continuous at \(x = 1\), \(\lim_{x \to 1} p(x) = p(1)\). The table values approach 3 from both sides.

## Explanation
(a) From the left: 2.997 is within 0.003 of 3. From the right: 3.003 is within 0.003 of 3. The data support \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 1} p(x) = 3\).
(b) Continuity at \(x = 1\) means \(\lim_{x \to 1} p(x) = p(1)\). Since the table suggests the limit is 3, continuity guarantees \(p(1) = 3\).
