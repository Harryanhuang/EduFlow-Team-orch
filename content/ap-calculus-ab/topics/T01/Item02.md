The table below gives values of a function \(g(x)\) near \(x = 3\).

| \(x\)    | 2.9   | 2.99  | 2.999 | 3.001 | 3.01  | 3.1   |
|----------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| \(g(x)\) | 5.810 | 5.980 | 5.998 | 6.002 | 6.020 | 6.210 |

Based on the table, which is the best estimate of \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 3} g(x)\)?

## Options
A) 3.0
B) 5.81
C) 6.0
D) The limit does not exist.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
As \(x\) approaches 3 from both the left and the right, \(g(x)\) approaches 6.0. The values get closer to 6 as \(x\) gets closer to 3: 5.998 and 6.002 are within 0.002 of 6. This numerical evidence supports \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 3} g(x) = 6\).
