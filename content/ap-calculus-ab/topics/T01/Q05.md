**Question:** Given the graph of a function with a jump discontinuity at \(x = -1\), a vertical asymptote at \(x = 2\), and a hole at \(x = 5\), determine all points where the two-sided limit does not exist. For each, classify the type of discontinuity and explain.

**Answer:** The two-sided limit does not exist at \(x = -1\), \(x = 2\), and \(x = 5\):
- At \(x = -1\) (jump discontinuity): \(\lim_{x \to -1^-} f(x) \neq \lim_{x \to -1^+} f(x)\). The left and right limits exist but are different finite values.
- At \(x = 2\) (infinite discontinuity / vertical asymptote): At least one one-sided limit is \(\pm\infty\). The function grows without bound near this point.
- At \(x = 5\) (removable discontinuity / hole): The limit exists (\(\lim_{x \to 5} f(x) = L\) for some \(L\)), but \(f(5) \neq L\) or \(f(5)\) is undefined. Note: the two-sided limit actually does exist here — only the function value disagrees. The question asks where the limit DNE; at a removable discontinuity the limit exists. So the limit DNE only at \(x = -1\) and \(x = 2\).
