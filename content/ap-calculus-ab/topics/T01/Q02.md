**Question:** Can change go on forever? Explain how the concept of a limit addresses Zeno's paradox: if you must always travel half the remaining distance to a destination, how do you ever arrive? Use limit notation in your explanation.

**Answer:** The limit concept resolves this paradox. If the total distance is \(D\) and at each step you travel half the remaining distance, after \(n\) steps the total distance traveled is \(D\left(\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{4} + \cdots + \frac{1}{2^n}\right) = D\left(1 - \frac{1}{2^n}\right)\). As \(n \to \infty\), we have \(\lim_{n \to \infty} D\left(1 - \frac{1}{2^n}\right) = D\). Even though there are infinitely many steps, the infinite sum converges to a finite value. The limit captures the idea that "infinitely many small changes" can accumulate to a finite result.
