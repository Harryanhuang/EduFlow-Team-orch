A function \(g\) is defined by \(g(x) = \dfrac{\sin x}{x}\) for \(x \neq 0\). The table below shows values of \(g(x)\) near \(x = 0\).

| \(x\)       | \(-0.1\) | \(-0.01\) | \(-0.001\) | \(0.001\) | \(0.01\) | \(0.1\) |
|-------------|----------|-----------|------------|-----------|----------|---------|
| \(g(x)\)    | 0.9983   | 0.99998   | 1.00000    | 1.00000   | 0.99998  | 0.9983  |

Based on this table, which conclusion is best supported?

## Options
A) \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 0} g(x) = 0\)
B) \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 0} g(x) = 1\)
C) \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 0} g(x) = g(0)\)
D) The limit does not exist because \(g(0)\) is undefined.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The table shows that as \(x\) approaches 0 from both sides, \(g(x) = \dfrac{\sin x}{x}\) approaches 1. The values 0.99998 and 1.00000 are within 0.00002 of 1. This is numerical evidence for the important result \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\sin x}{x} = 1\), even though \(g(0)\) itself is not defined in the given formula.
