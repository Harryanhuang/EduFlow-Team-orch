The graph of \(y = f(x)\) has a hole at \(x = -2\), where the curve approaches the point \((-2, 5)\) from both sides but \(f(-2) = 1\) (a filled dot at \((-2, 1)\)). What is \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to -2} f(x)\)?

## Options
A) 1
B) 5
C) The limit does not exist.
D) The limit equals \(f(-2)\).

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The limit asks what value \(f(x)\) approaches as \(x\) gets close to \(-2\), not what \(f(-2)\) equals. The graph shows the curve approaching \(y = 5\) from both sides, so \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to -2} f(x) = 5\). The fact that \(f(-2) = 1\) (the filled dot) is irrelevant to the limit. This is the key distinction between \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x)\) and \(f(a)\).
