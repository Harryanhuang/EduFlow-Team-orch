Find the derivative of $f(x) = \sin x \cdot \cos x$. (Hint: use product rule or first simplify.)

Using product rule: $f'(x) = \cos^2 x - \sin^2 x = \cos(2x)$.

Alternatively, $f(x) = \frac{1}{2}\sin(2x)$, so $f'(x) = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 2 \cos(2x) = \cos(2x)$.
