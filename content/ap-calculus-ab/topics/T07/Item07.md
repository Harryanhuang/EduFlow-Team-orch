If $f(x) = \cot x$, what is $f'(x)$?

## Options
A) $\csc^2 x$
B) $-\csc^2 x$
C) $\sec^2 x$
D) $-\sec^2 x$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Since $\cot x = \frac{\cos x}{\sin x}$, apply the quotient rule: $u = \cos x$, $v = \sin x$, $u' = -\sin x$, $v' = \cos x$. Then $f'(x) = \frac{-\sin x \cdot \sin x - \cos x \cdot \cos x}{\sin^2 x} = \frac{-(\sin^2 x + \cos^2 x)}{\sin^2 x} = \frac{-1}{\sin^2 x} = -\csc^2 x$.
