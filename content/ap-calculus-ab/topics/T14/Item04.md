If $f$ is twice differentiable and $f''(x) > 0$ for all $x$ near $a$, then the linearization $L(x)$ of $f$ at $a$ satisfies:

## Options
A) $L(x) > f(x)$ for $x \neq a$ (overestimate)
B) $L(x) < f(x)$ for $x \neq a$ (underestimate)
C) $L(x) = f(x)$ for $x \neq a$
D) Cannot determine from the given information

## Answer
B) $L(x) < f(x)$ for $x \neq a$ (underestimate)

## Explanation
When $f''(x) > 0$, the graph of $f$ is concave up. A tangent line to a concave-up curve lies below the curve, so the linearization underestimates the function value.
