The expression $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{x^3}{e^x}$ is an indeterminate form of which type?

## Options
A) $\dfrac{0}{0}$
B) $\dfrac{\infty}{\infty}$
C) $0 \cdot \infty$
D) $\infty - \infty$

## Answer
B) $\dfrac{\infty}{\infty}$

## Explanation
As $x \to \infty$, both $x^3 \to \infty$ and $e^x \to \infty$, so the limit has the form $\frac{\infty}{\infty}$, which is an indeterminate form suitable for L'HĂŽpital's Rule.
