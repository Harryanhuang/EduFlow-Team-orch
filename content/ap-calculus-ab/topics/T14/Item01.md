The linearization of $f(x) = \sqrt{x}$ at $a = 4$ is:

## Options
A) $L(x) = 2 + \dfrac{1}{4}(x - 4)$
B) $L(x) = 2 + \dfrac{1}{2}(x - 4)$
C) $L(x) = 2 + (x - 4)$
D) $L(x) = \dfrac{1}{4}(x - 4)$

## Answer
A) $L(x) = 2 + \dfrac{1}{4}(x - 4)$

## Explanation
The linearization of $f$ at $a$ is $L(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x - a)$. Here $f(4) = 2$ and $f'(x) = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}$, so $f'(4) = \frac{1}{4}$. Therefore $L(x) = 2 + \frac{1}{4}(x - 4)$.
