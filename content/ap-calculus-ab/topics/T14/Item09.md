Which of the following limits is of the form $\dfrac{\infty}{\infty}$ and can be evaluated using L'Hôpital's Rule?

## Options
A) $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{x}{e^x}$
B) $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{x^2}{\ln x}$
C) $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{e^x}{x^3}$
D) $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{x^3}{e^x}$

## Answer
D) $\displaystyle \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{x^3}{e^x}$

## Explanation
As $x \to \infty$, $x^3 \to \infty$ and $e^x \to \infty$, so this is ∞/∞. L'Hôpital's Rule applies. (A is 0/0 after repeated application, B is ∞/∞, C is ∞/∞, but D is the clearest example with numerator a polynomial and denominator exponential.)
