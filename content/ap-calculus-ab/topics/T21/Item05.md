Evaluate: \(\displaystyle \int \frac{1}{x}\,dx\)

## Options
A) \(\frac{1}{x^2} + C\)
B) \(\ln|x| + C\)
C) \(\frac{\ln|x|}{x} + C\)
D) \(-\ln|x| + C\)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The antiderivative of \(\frac{1}{x}\) is the natural logarithm. For \(x > 0\), \(\int \frac{1}{x}\,dx = \ln x + C\). For \(x < 0\), \(\int \frac{1}{x}\,dx = \ln(-x) + C\). Combining both cases: \(\ln|x| + C\).
