Evaluate the definite integral \(\int_0^1 x\sqrt{x^2+1}\,dx\) using substitution.

Let \(u = x^2 + 1\), then \(du = 2x\,dx\) and when \(x = 0\), \(u = 1\); when \(x = 1\), \(u = 2\).

\[
\int_0^1 x\sqrt{x^2+1}\,dx = \frac{1}{2}\int_1^2 u^{1/2}\,du = \frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{2}{3}u^{3/2}\right]_1^2 = \frac{1}{3}(2^{3/2} - 1) = \frac{1}{3}(2\sqrt{2} - 1)
\]
