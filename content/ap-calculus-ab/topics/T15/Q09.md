Let $f$ be a function continuous on $[a, b]$ and twice differentiable on $(a, b)$. Suppose $f(a) = f(b) = 0$ and $f'(a) > 0$. Prove that there exists at least one $c \in (a, b)$ such that $f''(c) < 0$.

By Rolle's Theorem on $[a, b]$, $f'(c_1) = 0$ for some $c_1 \in (a, b)$. Since $f'(a) > 0$ and $f'(c_1) = 0$, by the Mean Value Theorem applied to $f'$ on $[a, c_1]$, there exists $c \in (a, c_1)$ such that $f''(c) = (f'(c_1) - f'(a))/(c_1 - a) = (0 - f'(a))/(c_1 - a) < 0$, since $f'(a) > 0$ and $c_1 - a > 0$.
