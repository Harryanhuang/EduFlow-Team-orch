A runner's position (in meters) at time $t$ (in seconds) on the interval $[0, 10]$ is given by $s(t) = t^2 + 2t$. Use the Mean Value Theorem to show that the runner's instantaneous velocity must equal 12 m/s at some time $c \in (0, 10)$.

$s$ is a polynomial, continuous on $[0, 10]$ and differentiable on $(0, 10)$. Average velocity = $(s(10) - s(0))/(10-0) = (120 - 0)/10 = 12$ m/s. By MVT, there exists $c \in (0, 10)$ with $s'(c) = 12$. $s'(t) = 2t + 2 = 12$, so $t = 5$ seconds.
