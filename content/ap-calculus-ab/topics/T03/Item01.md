For a function \( f \) to be continuous at \( x = a \), which of the following must be true?

## Options
A) \( f(a) \) is defined and \( \displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} f(x) \) exists.
B) \( \displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a) \)
C) \( f'(a) \) exists.
D) \( f \) is continuous on some open interval containing \( a \).

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The definition of continuity at a point requires exactly three conditions: (1) \( f(a) \) is defined, (2) \( \displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} f(x) \) exists, and (3) \( \displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a) \). Option B concisely captures all three conditions, since if the limit equals \( f(a) \), both the function value and the limit must exist. Option A is insufficient because the limit might not equal \( f(a) \). Option C describes differentiability, which is stronger than continuity. Option D describes continuity on an interval, not at a point.
