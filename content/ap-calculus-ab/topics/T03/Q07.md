## Question
Let \( p(x) = x^3 - 3x + 1 \).

(a) Use the Intermediate Value Theorem to show that \( p(x) = 0 \) has at least one solution in each of the intervals \( (-2, -1) \), \( (0, 1) \), and \( (1, 2) \).
(b) Conclude how many real roots \( p \) has.

## Answer
(a) Evaluate at the endpoints:
- \( p(-2) = -8 + 6 + 1 = -1 < 0 \), \( p(-1) = -1 + 3 + 1 = 3 > 0 \). By IVT, a root exists in \( (-2, -1) \).
- \( p(0) = 1 > 0 \), \( p(1) = 1 - 3 + 1 = -1 < 0 \). By IVT, a root exists in \( (0, 1) \).
- \( p(1) = -1 < 0 \), \( p(2) = 8 - 6 + 1 = 3 > 0 \). By IVT, a root exists in \( (1, 2) \).

(b) Since \( p \) is a cubic polynomial (degree 3), it has at most 3 real roots. We found 3 distinct intervals each containing at least one root, and these intervals are disjoint. Therefore \( p \) has **exactly 3 real roots**.
