## Question
Suppose \( f \) and \( g \) are both continuous on \( [a, b] \), with \( f(a) < g(a) \) and \( f(b) > g(b) \).

(a) Prove that there exists at least one point \( c \in (a, b) \) such that \( f(c) = g(c) \).
(b) Apply this result to show that the equations \( \cos x = x \) and \( e^x = 3 - x \) each have at least one real solution.

## Answer
(a) Define \( h(x) = f(x) - g(x) \). Since \( f \) and \( g \) are continuous on \( [a, b] \), their difference \( h \) is also continuous on \( [a, b] \).

- \( h(a) = f(a) - g(a) < 0 \) (since \( f(a) < g(a) \)).
- \( h(b) = f(b) - g(b) > 0 \) (since \( f(b) > g(b) \)).

By the IVT applied to \( h \), since \( h \) is continuous on \( [a, b] \) and \( h(a) < 0 < h(b) \), there exists \( c \in (a, b) \) such that \( h(c) = 0 \), i.e., \( f(c) = g(c) \).

(b) For \( \cos x = x \): Let \( f(x) = \cos x \) and \( g(x) = x \), both continuous on \( [0, 1] \). \( f(0) = 1 > 0 = g(0) \) and \( f(1) = \cos 1 \approx 0.54 < 1 = g(1) \). By part (a), a solution exists in \( (0, 1) \).

For \( e^x = 3 - x \): Let \( f(x) = e^x \) and \( g(x) = 3 - x \), both continuous on \( [0, 1] \). \( f(0) = 1 < 3 = g(0) \) and \( f(1) = e \approx 2.718 > 2 = g(1) \). By part (a), a solution exists in \( (0, 1) \).
