Which of the following functions is continuous at every real number?

## Options
A) \( f(x) = \dfrac{x}{x^2 + 1} \)
B) \( f(x) = \dfrac{x^2 + 1}{x} \)
C) \( f(x) = \dfrac{1}{x^2 - 1} \)
D) \( f(x) = \tan x \)

## Answer
A

## Explanation
A rational function is continuous wherever its denominator is nonzero. For (A), \( x^2 + 1 \geq 1 > 0 \) for all real \( x \), so the denominator is never zero and the function is continuous everywhere. For (B), the denominator is zero at \( x = 0 \). For (C), the denominator is zero at \( x = \pm 1 \). For (D), \( \tan x \) is undefined at \( x = \dfrac{\pi}{2} + n\pi \) for any integer \( n \).
