The graph of \( f \) has a hole at \( x = 3 \) but is otherwise smooth and unbroken near that point. Which type of discontinuity does \( f \) have at \( x = 3 \)?

## Options
A) Jump discontinuity
B) Removable discontinuity
C) Infinite discontinuity
D) Oscillating discontinuity

## Answer
B

## Explanation
A removable discontinuity occurs when \( \displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} f(x) \) exists but either \( f(a) \) is undefined or \( f(a) \neq \displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} f(x) \). A "hole" in the graph is the classic visual signature of a removable discontinuity. A jump discontinuity would show the left- and right-hand limits disagreeing. An infinite discontinuity would involve the function growing without bound (a vertical asymptote).
