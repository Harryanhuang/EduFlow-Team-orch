A particle moves with position s(t). If v(t) = s'(t) and a(t) = s''(t), explain how you use concavity (acceleration) to determine when the particle is speeding up versus slowing down.
The particle speeds up when velocity and acceleration have the same sign (both positive or both negative), because |v| is increasing. The particle slows down when v and a have opposite signs, because |v| is decreasing. When a = 0, the speed is momentarily constant (neither speeding up nor slowing down).
