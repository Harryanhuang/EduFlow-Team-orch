A continuous function $f$ has $f''(x) > 0$ on the interval $(-1, 3)$. Which of the following statements must be true about the graph of $f$ on this interval?

A) $f$ is increasing
B) $f$ is decreasing
C) $f$ is concave up
D) $f$ has a local maximum

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The second derivative test tells us that when $f''(x) > 0$, the graph of $f$ is concave up. This is the definition of concavity. The sign of $f''$ tells us about concavity, not monotonicity (which is determined by $f'$), and does not directly indicate local extrema. Only option C must be true.
