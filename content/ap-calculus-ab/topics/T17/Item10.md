If $f''(x) = \frac{1}{x^2 + 1}$, which statement must be true?

A) $f$ is concave up for all $x$
B) $f$ is concave down for all $x$
C) $f$ has exactly one point of inflection
D) $f$ has no points of inflection

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Since $f''(x) = \frac{1}{x^2 + 1}$, we note that $x^2 + 1 > 0$ for all real $x$, so $\frac{1}{x^2 + 1} > 0$ for all $x$. Therefore $f''(x) > 0$ everywhere, meaning $f$ is concave up on its entire domain. Since concavity never changes (and never becomes negative), there are no points of inflection.
