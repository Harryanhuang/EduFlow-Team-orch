Which of the following is a necessary condition for a point $(c, f(c))$ to be a point of inflection?

A) $f'(c) = 0$
B) $f''(c) = 0$
C) The concavity changes at $c$
D) $f(c) = 0$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The definition of a point of inflection is a point where the concavity of the function changes. While $f''(c) = 0$ or $f''(c)$ undefined is often a preliminary check, the actual requirement is that the concavity changes at that point. The condition $f''(c) = 0$ is not necessary (concavity can change when $f''$ is undefined), and $f'(c) = 0$ or $f(c) = 0$ are unrelated to inflection points.
