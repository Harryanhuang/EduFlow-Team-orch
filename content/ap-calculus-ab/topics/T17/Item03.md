A point where $f''(x) = 0$ and the concavity of $f$ changes is called

A) A critical point
B) A point of inflection
C) A vertical asymptote
D) A corner

## Answer
B

## Explanation
A point of inflection is defined as a point on the graph where the concavity changes. This occurs when $f''(x) = 0$ (or is undefined) and the sign of $f''$ changes from positive to negative or vice versa. A critical point is where $f'(x) = 0$ or is undefined, which is not necessarily related to concavity.
