A function $f$ has $f'(x) = 3x^2 - 12$ and $f''(x) = 6x$. What are the $x$-coordinates of all points of inflection for $f$?

A) $x = 0$ only
B) $x = 2$ only
C) $x = -2$ only
D) $x = -2$ and $x = 2$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Points of inflection occur where $f''(x) = 0$ or is undefined, AND the concavity changes. Here $f''(x) = 6x = 0$ at $x = 0$. Testing the sign of $f''$ on either side of $x = 0$: for $x < 0$, $f''(x) < 0$ (concave down); for $x > 0$, $f''(x) > 0$ (concave up). Since the concavity changes, $x = 0$ is the only point of inflection.
