A car travels along a straight road. Its velocity is given by $v(t) = 4t - t^2$ miles per minute for $0 \leq t \leq 4$.

(a) What is the car's acceleration at $t = 1$? Include units.
(b) Is the car speeding up or slowing down at $t = 1$? Justify.
(c) Find the total distance the car travels from $t = 0$ to $t = 4$.

## Answer
(a) $a(t) = v'(t) = 4 - 2t$. At $t = 1$: $a(1) = 2$ miles per minute squared.

(b) At $t = 1$: $v(1) = 4 - 1 = 3 > 0$ and $a(1) = 2 > 0$. Since velocity and acceleration have the same sign, the car is speeding up.

(c) $v(t) = t(4 - t) = 0$ at $t = 0$ and $t = 4$. On $(0, 4)$, $v(t) > 0$, so the car never changes direction. Total distance = $\int_0^4 |v(t)|\, dt = \int_0^4 (4t - t^2)\, dt = [2t^2 - \frac{t^3}{3}]_0^4 = 32 - \frac{64}{3} = \frac{32}{3} \approx 10.67$ miles.
