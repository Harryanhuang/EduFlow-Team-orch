A right circular cylinder has a fixed height of 10 cm. Gas is being pumped into the cylinder, causing the radius to increase. The volume $V$ of the cylinder is given by $V = 10\pi r^2$. If the volume is increasing at a rate of $5\pi$ cm$^3$/s when the radius is $r = 3$ cm, what is $\frac{dr}{dt}$?

## Options
A) $\frac{1}{18}$ cm/s
B) $\frac{5}{36}$ cm/s
C) $\frac{1}{6}$ cm/s
D) $\frac{5}{6}$ cm/s

## Answer
A) $\frac{1}{18}$ cm/s

## Explanation
$V = 10\pi r^2$. Differentiating: $\frac{dV}{dt} = 20\pi r \frac{dr}{dt}$

Given $\frac{dV}{dt} = 5\pi$ and $r = 3$:
$5\pi = 20\pi(3)\frac{dr}{dt} \implies \frac{dr}{dt} = \frac{5\pi}{60\pi} = \frac{1}{18}$ cm/s
