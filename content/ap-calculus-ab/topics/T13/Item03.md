A spherical balloon is being inflated so that its volume increases at a constant rate of $20$ cm$^3$/s. Using $V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$, what is the rate of change of the radius $\frac{dr}{dt}$ when the radius $r = 3$ cm? (Round to 3 decimal places.)

## Options
A) 0.088 cm/s
B) 0.177 cm/s
C) 0.265 cm/s
D) 0.531 cm/s

## Answer
B) 0.177 cm/s

## Explanation
$V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \implies \frac{dV}{dt} = 4\pi r^2 \frac{dr}{dt}$

Given $\frac{dV}{dt} = 20$ and $r = 3$:
$\frac{dr}{dt} = \frac{20}{4\pi (3)^2} = \frac{20}{36\pi} \approx 0.177$ cm/s
