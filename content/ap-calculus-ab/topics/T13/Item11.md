A conical tank (vertex down) has height 12 ft and base radius 4 ft. Water is flowing into the tank at a rate of 3 ft$^3$/min.

(a) Express the radius $r$ of the water surface in terms of the height $h$ of the water using similar triangles.

(b) Express the volume $V$ of water in the tank in terms of $h$ alone.

(c) Find $\frac{dh}{dt}$ when $h = 3$ ft. (Round to 4 decimal places.)

## Answer
(a) $\frac{r}{h} = \frac{4}{12} = \frac{1}{3}$, so $r = \frac{h}{3}$.

(b) $V = \frac{\pi}{27}h^3$

(c) $\frac{dh}{dt} \approx 0.9549$ ft/min

## Explanation
(a) Similar triangles give $\frac{r}{h} = \frac{4}{12} = \frac{1}{3}$, so $r = \frac{h}{3}$.

(b) $V = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h = \frac{1}{3}\pi\left(\frac{h}{3}\right)^2 h = \frac{\pi}{27}h^3$

(c) $\frac{dV}{dt} = \frac{\pi}{9}h^2 \frac{dh}{dt}$

$3 = \frac{\pi}{9}(9)\frac{dh}{dt} = \pi\frac{dh}{dt}$

$\frac{dh}{dt} = \frac{3}{\pi} \approx 0.9549$ ft/min
