A 10-foot ladder leans against a vertical wall. The bottom of the ladder slides away from the wall along the horizontal ground. Let $x$ be the distance from the wall to the bottom of the ladder and $y$ be the height of the top of the ladder on the wall. Which equation correctly relates $\frac{dx}{dt}$ and $\frac{dy}{dt}$?

## Options
A) $\frac{dx}{dt} + \frac{dy}{dt} = 0$
B) $x\frac{dx}{dt} + y\frac{dy}{dt} = 0$
C) $2x\frac{dx}{dt} - 2y\frac{dy}{dt} = 0$
D) $x\frac{dy}{dt} + y\frac{dx}{dt} = 100$

## Answer
B) $x\frac{dx}{dt} + y\frac{dy}{dt} = 0$

## Explanation
By the Pythagorean theorem: $x^2 + y^2 = 10^2 = 100$.
Differentiating both sides with respect to $t$:
$2x\frac{dx}{dt} + 2y\frac{dy}{dt} = 0$
Dividing by 2: $x\frac{dx}{dt} + y\frac{dy}{dt} = 0$
