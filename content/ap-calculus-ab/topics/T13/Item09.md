A 13-meter ladder rests against a vertical wall. The bottom of the ladder is being pulled away from the wall at a rate of 0.8 m/s.

(a) Write an equation relating the distance $x$ (in meters) from the wall to the bottom of the ladder and the height $y$ (in meters) of the top of the ladder on the wall.

(b) Find $\frac{dy}{dt}$ when $x = 5$ meters.

(c) At the instant when $x = 5$ meters, is the top of the ladder moving up or down the wall? At what rate?

## Answer
(a) $x^2 + y^2 = 169$

(b) $\frac{dy}{dt} = -1$ m/s

(c) The top is moving down the wall at 1 m/s.

## Explanation
(a) By the Pythagorean theorem for the right triangle formed by the wall, floor, and ladder: $x^2 + y^2 = 13^2 = 169$.

(b) Differentiating: $2x\frac{dx}{dt} + 2y\frac{dy}{dt} = 0$, so $\frac{dy}{dt} = -\frac{x}{y}\frac{dx}{dt}$.

When $x = 5$: $y = \sqrt{169 - 25} = \sqrt{144} = 12$.

$\frac{dy}{dt} = -\frac{5}{12}(0.8) = -\frac{4}{12} = -1$ m/s.

(c) Since $\frac{dy}{dt} < 0$, the top is moving down the wall at 1 m/s.
