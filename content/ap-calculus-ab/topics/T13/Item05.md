Water flows into a right circular cone of height 12 cm and base radius 5 cm. The volume of water in the cone is increasing at a rate of 8 cm$^3$/s. Using similar triangles, the radius $r$ and height $h$ of the water satisfy $\frac{r}{h} = \frac{5}{12}$. What is $\frac{dh}{dt}$ when $h = 6$ cm? Round to 3 decimal places.

## Options
A) 0.407 cm/s
B) 0.815 cm/s
C) 1.222 cm/s
D) 1.630 cm/s

## Answer
A) 0.407 cm/s

## Explanation
$V = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$. Since $\frac{r}{h} = \frac{5}{12}$, we have $r = \frac{5h}{12}$.
Substituting: $V = \frac{1}{3}\pi \left(\frac{5h}{12}\right)^2 h = \frac{25\pi}{432}h^3$

$\frac{dV}{dt} = \frac{25\pi}{144}h^2 \frac{dh}{dt}$

Given $\frac{dV}{dt} = 8$ and $h = 6$:
$\frac{dh}{dt} = \frac{8}{\frac{25\pi}{144}(36)} = \frac{8 \cdot 144}{25\pi \cdot 36} = \frac{32}{25\pi} \approx 0.407$ cm/s
