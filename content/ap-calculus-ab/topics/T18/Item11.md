The graph of $f'$ is shown below. Which statement about $f$ is true?

(Assume the graph of $f'$ has x-intercepts at $x = -2, 1, 3$ and is positive on $(-2, 1)$ and $(3, \infty)$, negative on $(-\infty, -2)$ and $(1, 3)$.)

A) $f$ has a local maximum at $x = 1$.
B) $f$ has a local minimum at $x = 3$.
C) $f$ has a local minimum at $x = -2$.
D) $f$ has a local maximum at $x = 3$.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
$f$ is increasing when $f' > 0$ and decreasing when $f' < 0$. $f' < 0$ on $(-\infty, -2)$, so $f$ decreases up to $x = -2$. $f' > 0$ on $(-2, 1)$, so $f$ increases from $x = -2$. Thus $f$ has a local minimum at $x = -2$. At $x = 1$ and $x = 3$, $f'$ is zero but does not change sign, so no local extrema there.
