The second derivative of a function $f$ is $f''(x) = (x - 1)^2(x + 2)$. Which statement is true about the concavity of $f$ on the interval $(-3, 4)$?

A) $f$ is concave down on $(-3, -2)$ and concave up on $(-2, 4)$.
B) $f$ is concave up on $(-3, 1)$ and concave down on $(1, 4)$.
C) $f$ is concave down on $(-3, -2)$ and $(-2, 1)$ and concave up on $(1, 4)$.
D) $f$ is concave up on $(-3, -2)$ and $(-2, 1)$ and concave down on $(1, 4)$.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$f'' > 0$ means concave up; $f'' < 0$ means concave down. $f''(x) = (x - 1)^2(x + 2)$. $(x-1)^2 \ge 0$ always. Zeros at $x = 1$ (double root, sign doesn't change) and $x = -2$ (single root, sign changes). Test intervals: $(-3, -2)$: pick $x = -2.5$, $(-3.5)^2(-0.5) < 0$, concave down. $(-2, 1)$: pick $x = 0$, $(-1)^2(2) > 0$, concave up. $(1, 4)$: pick $x = 2$, $(1)^2(4) > 0$, concave up. So concave down on $(-3, -2)$, concave up elsewhere (except at $x=1$ where it is an inflection point candidate but sign doesn't change).
