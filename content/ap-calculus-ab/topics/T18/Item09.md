A point $(x, y)$ on the line $3x + y = 12$ is closest to the origin $(0, 0)$. What is the distance from the origin to this point?

A) $\frac{6}{\sqrt{10}}$
B) $\frac{12}{\sqrt{10}}$
C) $6\sqrt{10}$
D) $12\sqrt{10}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Minimize distance $d = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$, or minimize $d^2 = x^2 + y^2$. From constraint $y = 12 - 3x$, substitute: $d^2 = x^2 + (12 - 3x)^2 = x^2 + 144 - 72x + 9x^2 = 10x^2 - 72x + 144$. $d^2$' = $20x - 72 = 0$, so $x = 3.6$. $y = 12 - 10.8 = 1.2$. Distance: $d = \sqrt{3.6^2 + 1.2^2} = \sqrt{12.96 + 1.44} = \sqrt{14.4} = \sqrt{\frac{144}{10}} = \frac{12}{\sqrt{10}}$.
