The derivative of a function $f$ is given by $f'(x) = (x - 2)^2(x + 1)$.

(a) Find all critical numbers of $f$.
(b) On what intervals is $f$ increasing or decreasing?
(c) Find the x-coordinates of all local extrema of $f$.

## Answer
(a) $x = -1, 2$

(b) Increasing on $(-1, \infty)$; Decreasing on $(-\infty, -1)$

(c) Local minimum at $x = -1$; No local extremum at $x = 2$

## Explanation
(a) Critical numbers when $f'(x) = 0$ or undefined: $(x - 2)^2(x + 1) = 0$, so $x = -1, 2$.

(b) Test intervals: $(-\infty, -1)$: pick $x = -2$, $f'(-2) = (-4)^2(-1) = 16(-1) < 0$, decreasing. $(-1, 2)$: pick $x = 0$, $f'(0) = (-2)^2(1) = 4 > 0$, increasing. $(2, \infty)$: pick $x = 3$, $f'(3) = (1)^2(4) = 4 > 0$, increasing.

(c) At $x = -1$: $f'$ changes from negative to positive, so local minimum. At $x = 2$: $f'$ does not change sign (always nonnegative), so no local extremum.
