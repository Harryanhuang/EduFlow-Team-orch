The function $f(x) = x^3 - 3x^2 - 9x + 4$ has a local maximum at $x =$ ?

A) $-1$
B) $1$
C) $3$
D) $5$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
$f'(x) = 3x^2 - 6x - 9 = 3(x^2 - 2x - 3) = 3(x - 3)(x + 1)$. Critical points at $x = -1$ and $x = 3$. Second derivative: $f''(x) = 6x - 6$. At $x = -1$: $f''(-1) = -12 < 0$, local maximum. At $x = 3$: $f''(3) = 12 > 0$, local minimum.
