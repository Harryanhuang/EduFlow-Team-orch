A box with a square base and no top has a volume of 32 cubic inches. The material for the base costs $3 per square inch and the material for the sides costs $2 per square inch. Which of the following gives the total cost $C$ of the box in terms of the side length $x$ of the base?

A) $C = 3x^2 + \frac{128}{x}$
B) $C = 3x^2 + \frac{256}{x}$
C) $C = 3x^2 + \frac{64}{x}$
D) $C = 3x^2 + \frac{192}{x}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Let $x$ be the side of the square base and $h$ the height. Volume: $x^2 h = 32$, so $h = \frac{32}{x^2}$. Base area: $x^2$ at $3 per in squared: cost = $3x^2$. Four sides: each has area $xh$, total area $4xh = 4x \cdot \frac{32}{x^2} = \frac{128}{x}$ at $2 per in squared: cost = $2 \cdot \frac{128}{x} = \frac{256}{x}$. Total cost: $C = 3x^2 + \frac{256}{x}$.
