The volume $V(t)$ of water in a tank (in liters) at time $t$ (in minutes) is modeled by $V(t) = 50 - 4t - t^2$ for $0 \leq t \leq 5$.

(a) Find the average rate of change of volume over $[1, 3]$.

(b) Find the instantaneous rate of change at $t = 2$ using the limit definition.

(c) At what time does the tank appear to be emptying fastest? Justify.

<question>
The volume $V(t)$ of water in a tank (in liters) at time $t$ (in minutes) is modeled by $V(t) = 50 - 4t - t^2$ for $0 \leq t \leq 5$.

(a) Find the average rate of change of volume over $[1, 3]$.

(b) Find the instantaneous rate of change at $t = 2$ using the limit definition.

(c) At what time does the tank appear to be emptying fastest? Justify.
</question>
<answer>
(a) Average rate of change:
$$\frac{V(3) - V(1)}{3 - 1} = \frac{(50 - 12 - 9) - (50 - 4 - 1)}{2} = \frac{29 - 45}{2} = \frac{-16}{2} = -8 \text{ L/min}$$

(b) Instantaneous rate at $t = 2$:
$$V'(2) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{V(2 + h) - V(2)}{h}$$
$$V(2 + h) = 50 - 4(2 + h) - (2 + h)^2 = 50 - 8 - 4h - (4 + 4h + h^2) = 38 - 8h - h^2$$
$$V(2) = 50 - 8 - 4 = 38$$
$$V'(2) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{38 - 8h - h^2 - 38}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{-8h - h^2}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0}(-8 - h) = -8 \text{ L/min}$$

(c) $V'(t) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{V(t+h) - V(t)}{h}$. Using the power rule (which derives from the limit definition): $V'(t) = -4 - 2t$. This is a linear function that becomes more negative as $t$ increases. The most negative value on $[0, 5]$ occurs at $t = 5$: $V'(5) = -4 - 10 = -14$ L/min. So the tank is emptying fastest at $t = 5$ minutes, at a rate of 14 L/min.

(The negative sign means the volume is decreasing; "fastest emptying" means the most negative rate of change.)
</answer>
