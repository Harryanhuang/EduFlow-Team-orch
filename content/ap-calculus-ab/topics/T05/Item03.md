Let $P(t)$ represent the population of a city in thousands, where $t$ is measured in years. Which of the following represents the instantaneous rate of change of the population at $t = 5$?

## Options
A) $\dfrac{P(5 + h) - P(5)}{h}$
B) $\displaystyle\lim_{h \to 0} \dfrac{P(5 + h) - P(5)}{h}$
C) $\displaystyle\lim_{t \to 5} P(t)$
D) $\displaystyle\lim_{h \to 5} \dfrac{P(5 + h) - P(5)}{h}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The instantaneous rate of change of $P$ at $t = 5$ is $P'(5)$, which is defined as $\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{P(5 + h) - P(5)}{h}$. Option A gives the average rate of change over an interval of length $h$, not the instantaneous rate. Option C is the limit of the function value, not a rate. Option D has the wrong limiting process.
