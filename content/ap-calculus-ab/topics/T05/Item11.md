The graph of $y = f'(x)$, the derivative of $f$, is shown below. At which value of $x$ is the tangent line to the graph of $f$ horizontal?

```
  f'(x)
    |
  2 |   *---------*
    |  /           \
  0 |-/-----*-------\--
    |/      |2        \
 -2 |       *          *
    |
    +--1----+----3----+--4--> x
```

## Options
A) $x = 1$
B) $x = 2$
C) $x = 3$
D) $x = 4$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The tangent line to $f$ is horizontal when $f'(x) = 0$. From the graph, $f'(x) = 0$ at $x = 2$ (the graph crosses the x-axis there). At $x = 1$, $f'(1) > 0$. At $x = 3$, $f'(3) < 0$. At $x = 4$, $f'(4) < 0$.
