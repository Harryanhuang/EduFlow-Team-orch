Which of the following is equivalent to $f'(x)$?

## Options
A) $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{f(x) - f(0)}{x}$
B) $\displaystyle\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h}$
C) $\displaystyle\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x) - f(h)}{x - h}$
D) $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to h} \frac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The derivative $f'(x)$ is defined as $\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h}$. This is the standard limit definition of the derivative. Option A computes $f'(0)$, not $f'(x)$. Option C does not match the difference quotient form. Option D has an incorrect limit condition.
