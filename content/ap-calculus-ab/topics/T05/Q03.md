Explain in words what $f'(5) = 30$ means if $f(t)$ represents the distance (in meters) a runner has traveled after $t$ seconds.

<question>
Explain in words what $f'(5) = 30$ means if $f(t)$ represents the distance (in meters) a runner has traveled after $t$ seconds.
</question>
<answer>
At $t = 5$ seconds, the runner's instantaneous velocity (instantaneous rate of change of position) is 30 meters per second. This means that at that exact moment, the runner is covering distance at a rate of 30 meters per second. It does NOT mean the runner traveled 30 meters in 5 seconds or 30 meters total — it describes how fast the distance is changing at that precise instant.
</answer>
