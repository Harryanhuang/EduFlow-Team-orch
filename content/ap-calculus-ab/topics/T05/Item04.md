The graph of $y = f(x)$ is shown below. At which labeled point is the derivative $f'(x)$ closest to zero?

```
        |
     C  |        B
    *   |       *
        |      /
   -----|-----/---- A *
        |    /
        |   /
        |  *
        | D
```

## Options
A) Point A
B) Point B
C) Point C
D) Point D

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The derivative $f'(x)$ represents the slope of the tangent line. At point A, the graph appears nearly horizontal, meaning the tangent line has a slope close to zero. At points B and D, the graph has a noticeably positive slope. At point C, the graph has a negative slope.
