Write the difference quotient that gives the slope of the secant line through the points $(a, f(a))$ and $(a + h, f(a + h))$ for $f(x) = \sqrt{x + 1}$ at $a = 3$.

<question>
Write the difference quotient that gives the slope of the secant line through the points $(a, f(a))$ and $(a + h, f(a + h))$ for $f(x) = \sqrt{x + 1}$ at $a = 3$.
</question>
<answer>
The difference quotient is:

$$\frac{f(3 + h) - f(3)}{h} = \frac{\sqrt{(3 + h) + 1} - \sqrt{3 + 1}}{h} = \frac{\sqrt{4 + h} - 2}{h}$$

This expression represents the slope of the secant line. As $h \to 0$, it approaches the slope of the tangent line at $x = 3$.
</answer>
