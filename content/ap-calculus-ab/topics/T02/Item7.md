Evaluate the limit:

$\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\dfrac{1}{x + 2} - \dfrac{1}{2}}{x}$

## Answer
$-\dfrac{1}{4}$

## Explanation
Combine the numerator fractions:

$$\frac{1}{x + 2} - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{2 - (x + 2)}{2(x + 2)} = \frac{-x}{2(x + 2)}$$

$$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\dfrac{-x}{2(x + 2)}}{x} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{-x}{2x(x + 2)} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{-1}{2(x + 2)}$$

$$= \frac{-1}{2(2)} = -\frac{1}{4}$$
