When computing the area between x = y² and x = 4, it is simplest to integrate with respect to:

## Options
A) x, from 0 to 4
B) y, from −2 to 2
C) x, from −2 to 2
D) Either variable gives the same difficulty

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The curves x = y² and x = 4 are naturally expressed as functions of y. Setting y² = 4 gives y = ±2. Integrating ∫₋₂² (4 − y²)dy is straightforward. Integrating with respect to x would require splitting at x = 0 and using ±√x, which is more complex. Answer is B.
