For dy/dt = y(1 − y), what are the equilibrium solutions and their stability?

## Options
A) y = 0 unstable, y = 1 stable
B) y = 0 stable, y = 1 unstable
C) Both y = 0 and y = 1 are stable
D) Both y = 0 and y = 1 are unstable

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Equilibrium: y(1−y) = 0 gives y = 0 and y = 1. For y slightly above 0 (e.g., y = 0.1): dy/dt = 0.1(0.9) > 0, so y increases away from 0 → unstable. For y slightly below 1 (e.g., y = 0.9): dy/dt = 0.9(0.1) > 0, so y increases toward 1 → stable. Answer is A.
