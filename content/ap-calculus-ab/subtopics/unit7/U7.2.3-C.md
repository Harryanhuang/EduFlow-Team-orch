---For dP/dt = kP with k > 0 and P > 0, what can be said about the concavity of the solution curve?

## Options
A. The graph of P(t) is always concave up for P > 0
B. The graph of P(t) is always concave down for P > 0
C. The graph has an inflection point at P = 0
D. The graph is linear

## Answer
A

## Explanation
dP/dt = kP means the rate of change is proportional to the current value. The second derivative is d²P/dt² = k(dP/dt) = k²P. Since P>0 and k²>0, the second derivative is always positive, so the curve is always concave up.
