---What is the general solution to the differential equation dP/dt = kP with initial condition P(0) = P0?

## Options
A. P(t) = P0 e^{-kt}
B. P(t) = P0 e^{kt}
C. P(t) = P0 + kt
D. P(t) = P0 kt

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Solving dP/dt = kP: separate dP/P = k dt, integrate to get ln|P| = kt + C, so P = Ae^{kt}. With P(0)=P0, we get A=P0, so P(t) = P0 e^{kt}.
