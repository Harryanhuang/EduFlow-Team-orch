What is $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{x^2 + x}{x^2 - x}$?

## Options
A) -1
B) 0
C) 1
D) The limit does not exist.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Direct substitution gives $\frac{0}{0}$, an indeterminate form. Factor out $x$ from both numerator and denominator:
$$\frac{x^2 + x}{x^2 - x} = \frac{x(x + 1)}{x(x - 1)} = \frac{x + 1}{x - 1}$$
for $x \neq 0$. Therefore,
$$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{x^2 + x}{x^2 - x} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{x + 1}{x - 1} = \frac{0 + 1}{0 - 1} = -1.$$
