What is $\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sqrt{x + 4} - 2}{x}$?

## Options
A) 0
B) $\frac{1}{4}$
C) 4
D) The limit does not exist.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Direct substitution gives $\frac{0}{0}$. Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate $\sqrt{x + 4} + 2$:
$$\frac{\sqrt{x + 4} - 2}{x} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{x + 4} + 2}{\sqrt{x + 4} + 2} = \frac{(x + 4) - 4}{x(\sqrt{x + 4} + 2)} = \frac{x}{x(\sqrt{x + 4} + 2)} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x + 4} + 2}$$
for $x \neq 0$. Therefore,
$$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sqrt{x + 4} - 2}{x} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{4} + 2} = \frac{1}{4}.$$
