What is $\lim_{x \to 4} \frac{\sqrt{x} - 2}{x - 4}$?

## Options
A) 0
B) $\frac{1}{4}$
C) 4
D) The limit does not exist.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Direct substitution gives $\frac{0}{0}$. Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate $\sqrt{x} + 2$:
$$\frac{\sqrt{x} - 2}{x - 4} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{x} + 2}{\sqrt{x} + 2} = \frac{x - 4}{(x - 4)(\sqrt{x} + 2)} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x} + 2}$$
for $x \neq 4$. Therefore,
$$\lim_{x \to 4} \frac{\sqrt{x} - 2}{x - 4} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{4} + 2} = \frac{1}{4}.$$
