The function $f$ is defined on the closed interval $[0, 4]$. The graph of $f$ consists of two line segments and is shown below.

(Note: The graph has vertices at $(0, 2)$, $(2, 0)$, and $(4, 2)$, forming a V-shape with a corner at $(2, 0)$.)

Which of the following statements is true?

## Options
A) IVT guarantees that $f(x) = 1$ has exactly two solutions in $(0, 4)$.
B) IVT does not apply on $[0, 4]$ because $f$ is not differentiable at $x = 2$.
C) IVT guarantees that $f(x) = 1$ has at least one solution in $(0, 4)$.
D) IVT does not apply because $f(0) = f(4) = 2$.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The graph of $f$ is continuous on $[0, 4]$ (it has no breaks, jumps, or holes). The corner at $(2, 0)$ does not affect continuity; it only affects differentiability. Since $f$ is continuous on $[0, 4]$, IVT applies.

$f(0) = 2$ and $f(2) = 0$. Since $0 < 1 < 2$, IVT guarantees at least one $c_1$ in $(0, 2)$ with $f(c_1) = 1$.
$f(2) = 0$ and $f(4) = 2$. Since $0 < 1 < 2$, IVT guarantees at least one $c_2$ in $(2, 4)$ with $f(c_2) = 1$.

Therefore, IVT guarantees that $f(x) = 1$ has at least one solution in $(0, 4)$. In fact, there are exactly two solutions (one on each line segment), but IVT only guarantees "at least one," not "exactly two."

A is false because IVT does not guarantee exactly two solutions.
B is false because IVT requires continuity, not differentiability.
D is false because IVT applies regardless of whether $f(a) = f(b)$; it only requires $f$ to be continuous on $[a, b]$.
