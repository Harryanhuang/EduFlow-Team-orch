Which of the following statements best describes the meaning of $\lim_{x \to 3} f(x) = 5$?

## Options
A) The value of $f(3)$ is 5.
B) As $x$ gets closer to 3, the values of $f(x)$ get closer to 5.
C) The function $f$ is defined at $x = 3$.
D) The function $f$ is continuous at $x = 3$.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The notation $\lim_{x \to 3} f(x) = 5$ means that as $x$ approaches 3 (from either side), the values of $f(x)$ approach 5. It does not guarantee that $f(3) = 5$ (A), that $f$ is defined at 3 (C), or that $f$ is continuous at 3 (D). The limit describes the behavior of $f(x)$ near $x = 3$, not necessarily at $x = 3$.
