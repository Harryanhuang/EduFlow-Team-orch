Which of the following functions has a limit that does not exist at $x = 0$ because of oscillation?

## Options
A) $f(x) = \frac{1}{x}$
B) $f(x) = \sin\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)$
C) $f(x) = \frac{|x|}{x}$
D) $f(x) = \frac{x^2}{x}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
A) $\frac{1}{x}$ has an infinite discontinuity at $x = 0$ (not oscillation).
B) $\sin(1/x)$ oscillates infinitely between $-1$ and $1$ as $x \to 0$, so the limit does not exist due to oscillation.
C) $\frac{|x|}{x}$ has a jump discontinuity at $x = 0$ (left limit $-1$, right limit $1$).
D) $\frac{x^2}{x} = x$ for $x \neq 0$, so the limit is $0$.
The oscillation case is B.
