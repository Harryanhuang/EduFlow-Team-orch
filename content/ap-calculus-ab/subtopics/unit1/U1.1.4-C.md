The function $f$ is defined for all real numbers. Which of the following statements about limits is false?

## Options
A) If $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = \infty$, then $\lim_{x \to a} f(x)$ does not exist.
B) If $\lim_{x \to a^-} f(x) = 3$ and $\lim_{x \to a^+} f(x) = 3$, then $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = 3$.
C) If $\lim_{x \to a} f(x)$ does not exist, then $f$ is undefined at $x = a$.
D) If $f$ is continuous at $x = a$, then $\lim_{x \to a} f(x)$ exists.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
A is true: infinite limits are a type of non-existent limit (or at least not a finite limit).
B is true: equal left and right limits imply the two-sided limit exists and equals that common value.
C is false: a limit can fail to exist even when $f(a)$ is defined. For example, $f(x) = \begin{cases} 1 & x < 0 \\ 2 & x \geq 0 \end{cases}$ has $f(0) = 2$ defined, but $\lim_{x \to 0} f(x)$ does not exist because the left and right limits differ.
D is true: continuity at $a$ requires the limit to exist (and equal $f(a)$).
Therefore, C is the false statement.
