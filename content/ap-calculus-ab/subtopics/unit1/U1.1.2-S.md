Let $f$ be the function defined by
$$f(x) = \begin{cases} x^2 + 1 & \text{if } x < 2 \\ 3x - 1 & \text{if } x \geq 2 \end{cases}$$
Which of the following statements is true?

## Options
A) $\lim_{x \to 2} f(x) = 5$
B) $\lim_{x \to 2} f(x) = 3$
C) $\lim_{x \to 2} f(x)$ does not exist.
D) $\lim_{x \to 2} f(x) = 0$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
For the limit to exist, the left-hand and right-hand limits must be equal. Left-hand limit: $\lim_{x \to 2^-} f(x) = 2^2 + 1 = 5$. Right-hand limit: $\lim_{x \to 2^+} f(x) = 3(2) - 1 = 5$. Since both equal 5, $\lim_{x \to 2} f(x) = 5$.
