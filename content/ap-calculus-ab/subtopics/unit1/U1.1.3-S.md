If $\lim_{x \to 3} f(x) = 4$, $\lim_{x \to 3} g(x) = 0$, and $\lim_{x \to 3} h(x) = 2$, what is $\lim_{x \to 3} \frac{f(x) \cdot h(x)}{g(x) + 1}$?

## Options
A) 0
B) 4
C) 8
D) The limit does not exist.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Using limit laws: the denominator limit is $\lim_{x \to 3} [g(x) + 1] = 0 + 1 = 1 \neq 0$, so the quotient rule applies. The numerator limit is $\lim_{x \to 3} [f(x) \cdot h(x)] = 4 \cdot 2 = 8$. Therefore, $\lim_{x \to 3} \frac{f(x) \cdot h(x)}{g(x) + 1} = \frac{8}{1} = 8$.
