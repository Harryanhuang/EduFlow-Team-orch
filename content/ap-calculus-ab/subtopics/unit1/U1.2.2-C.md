What is $\lim_{x \to 1} \left( \frac{1}{x - 1} - \frac{2}{x^2 - 1} \right)$?

## Options
A) $-\frac{1}{2}$
B) 0
C) $\frac{1}{2}$
D) The limit does not exist.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Combine the fractions by finding a common denominator, which is $x^2 - 1 = (x - 1)(x + 1)$:
$$\frac{1}{x - 1} - \frac{2}{x^2 - 1} = \frac{x + 1}{x^2 - 1} - \frac{2}{x^2 - 1} = \frac{x + 1 - 2}{x^2 - 1} = \frac{x - 1}{x^2 - 1} = \frac{x - 1}{(x - 1)(x + 1)} = \frac{1}{x + 1}$$
for $x \neq 1$. Therefore,
$$\lim_{x \to 1} \left( \frac{1}{x - 1} - \frac{2}{x^2 - 1} \right) = \frac{1}{1 + 1} = \frac{1}{2}.$$
