Suppose $\lim_{x \to 1} f(x) = 3$ and $\lim_{x \to 1} g(x) = 0$. Which of the following limits must exist?

I. $\lim_{x \to 1} [f(x) + g(x)]$
II. $\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}$
III. $\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{g(x)}{f(x)}$

## Options
A) I only
B) I and II only
C) I and III only
D) I, II, and III

## Answer
C

## Explanation
I: By the sum rule, $\lim_{x \to 1} [f(x) + g(x)] = 3 + 0 = 3$, so I exists.
II: The quotient rule requires the denominator limit to be nonzero. Since $\lim_{x \to 1} g(x) = 0$, $\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}$ does not necessarily exist (it could be infinite or not exist depending on the behavior of $f$ and $g$ near $x = 1$). So II does not necessarily exist.
III: Since $\lim_{x \to 1} f(x) = 3 \neq 0$ and $\lim_{x \to 1} g(x) = 0$, we have $\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{g(x)}{f(x)} = \frac{0}{3} = 0$. The quotient rule applies because the denominator limit is nonzero. So III exists.
Therefore, I and III only is correct.
