Let $f$ be the function defined by
$$f(x) = \begin{cases} x + 2 & \text{if } x < 1 \\ 4 & \text{if } x = 1 \\ x^2 + 1 & \text{if } x > 1 \end{cases}$$
What type of discontinuity does $f$ have at $x = 1$?

## Options
A) Removable discontinuity
B) Jump discontinuity
C) Infinite discontinuity
D) No discontinuity; $f$ is continuous at $x = 1$.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Left-hand limit: $\lim_{x \to 1^-} f(x) = 1 + 2 = 3$. Right-hand limit: $\lim_{x \to 1^+} f(x) = 1^2 + 1 = 2$. Since the left and right limits exist but are not equal ($3 \neq 2$), $f$ has a jump discontinuity at $x = 1$. The function value $f(1) = 4$ is irrelevant to the classification since the two-sided limit does not exist.
