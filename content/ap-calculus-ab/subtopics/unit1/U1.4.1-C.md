The function $f$ is continuous on the closed interval $[0, 4]$. The table below gives selected values of $f$.

| $x$ | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
|-----|---|---|---|---|---|
| $f(x)$ | 3 | 1 | -1 | 2 | 5 |

For how many values of $k$ in the interval $[0, 4]$ does the equation $f(x) = k$ have at least one solution?

## Options
A) 2
B) 3
C) 4
D) The answer cannot be determined from the given information.

## Answer
D

## Explanation
The Intermediate Value Theorem guarantees that for any $k$ between $f(a)$ and $f(b)$, there exists at least one solution to $f(x) = k$ in $(a, b)$. However, IVT only applies to values between the minimum and maximum of $f$ on an interval. 

From the table, we know $f$ takes values 3, 1, -1, 2, and 5. The minimum value in the table is -1 and the maximum is 5. IVT guarantees that for any $k$ between -1 and 5, there is at least one solution. But we cannot determine the exact range of $f$ on $[0, 4]$ from just five points. $f$ might have a minimum lower than -1 or a maximum higher than 5 between the sampled points. Therefore, we cannot determine exactly how many values of $k$ in $[0, 4]$ have solutions. The answer is D.

Note: A common trap is to count the number of distinct $f(x)$ values in the table (5) or to assume the range is exactly $[-1, 5]$. Neither is justified by the given information.
