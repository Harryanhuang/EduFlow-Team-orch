If $\lim_{x \to 2} f(x) = 3$ and $\lim_{x \to 2} g(x) = 5$, what is $\lim_{x \to 2} [f(x) + g(x)]$?

## Options
A) 8
B) 15
C) 2
D) The limit does not exist.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
By the sum rule for limits, if $\lim_{x \to 2} f(x)$ and $\lim_{x \to 2} g(x)$ both exist, then $\lim_{x \to 2} [f(x) + g(x)] = \lim_{x \to 2} f(x) + \lim_{x \to 2} g(x) = 3 + 5 = 8$.
