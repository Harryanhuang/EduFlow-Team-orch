Which of the following is a necessary condition for a function $f$ to be continuous at $x = a$?

## Options
A) $f$ is differentiable at $x = a$.
B) $\lim_{x \to a} f(x)$ exists.
C) $f(x) > 0$ for all $x$ near $a$.
D) $f$ is increasing on an interval containing $a$.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For $f$ to be continuous at $x = a$, three conditions must be satisfied: (1) $f(a)$ is defined, (2) $\lim_{x \to a} f(x)$ exists, and (3) the limit equals $f(a)$. Therefore, the existence of the limit is a necessary condition. Differentiability (A) implies continuity but is not required. (C) and (D) are unrelated to continuity.
