Let $f$ be the function defined by
$$f(x) = \begin{cases} 2x + 1 & \text{if } x < 3 \\ k & \text{if } x = 3 \\ x^2 - 2 & \text{if } x > 3 \end{cases}$$
For what value of $k$ is $\lim_{x \to 3} f(x) = f(3)$?

## Options
A) 5
B) 7
C) 9
D) No such value exists.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For $\lim_{x \to 3} f(x) = f(3)$, the limit must exist and equal $f(3) = k$. Left-hand limit: $\lim_{x \to 3^-} f(x) = 2(3) + 1 = 7$. Right-hand limit: $\lim_{x \to 3^+} f(x) = 3^2 - 2 = 7$. Since both equal 7, $\lim_{x \to 3} f(x) = 7$. Therefore, $k = 7$.
