What is $\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2 - 5x + 6}{x - 2}$?

## Options
A) 0
B) -1
C) 1
D) The limit does not exist.

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Direct substitution gives $\frac{4 - 10 + 6}{2 - 2} = \frac{0}{0}$, an indeterminate form. Factor the numerator: $x^2 - 5x + 6 = (x - 2)(x - 3)$. For $x \neq 2$, $\frac{x^2 - 5x + 6}{x - 2} = x - 3$. Therefore, $\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2 - 5x + 6}{x - 2} = \lim_{x \to 2} (x - 3) = 2 - 3 = -1$.
