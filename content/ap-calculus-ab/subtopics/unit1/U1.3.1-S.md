Let $f$ be the function defined by
$$f(x) = \begin{cases} x^2 - 1 & \text{if } x < 2 \\ 3 & \text{if } x = 2 \\ 2x + 1 & \text{if } x > 2 \end{cases}$$
At which of the following points is $f$ continuous?

## Options
A) $x = 0$ only
B) $x = 2$ only
C) $x = 0$ and $x = 2$
D) $f$ is continuous at all real numbers.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
At $x = 0$: $f(0) = 0^2 - 1 = -1$. Since $0 < 2$, $f$ is given by a polynomial on $(-\infty, 2)$, so $f$ is continuous at $x = 0$.
At $x = 2$: $f(2) = 3$. Left-hand limit: $\lim_{x \to 2^-} (x^2 - 1) = 3$. Right-hand limit: $\lim_{x \to 2^+} (2x + 1) = 5$. Since $3 \neq 5$, the limit does not exist at $x = 2$, so $f$ is not continuous at $x = 2$.
Therefore, $f$ is continuous at $x = 0$ only.
