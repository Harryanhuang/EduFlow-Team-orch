Let $f$ be the function defined by
$$f(x) = \begin{cases} x^2 + k & \text{if } x < 2 \\ 3x + 1 & \text{if } x \geq 2 \end{cases}$$
For what value of $k$ does $\lim_{x \to 2} f(x)$ exist?

## Options
A) 1
B) 3
C) 5
D) 7

## Answer
B

## Explanation
For the limit to exist, the left-hand and right-hand limits must be equal. Left-hand limit: $\lim_{x \to 2^-} f(x) = 2^2 + k = 4 + k$. Right-hand limit: $\lim_{x \to 2^+} f(x) = 3(2) + 1 = 7$. Setting equal: $4 + k = 7$, so $k = 3$.
