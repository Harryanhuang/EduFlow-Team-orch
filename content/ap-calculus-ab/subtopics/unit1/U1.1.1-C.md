Let $f$ be a function such that $\lim_{x \to 2} f(x) = 7$. Which of the following statements must be true?

I. $f(2) = 7$
II. $\lim_{x \to 2^-} f(x) = 7$
III. $\lim_{x \to 2^+} f(x) = 7$

## Options
A) I only
B) II only
C) II and III only
D) I, II, and III

## Answer
C

## Explanation
If $\lim_{x \to 2} f(x) = 7$, then by definition the left-hand limit and right-hand limit must both equal 7. So II and III are true. However, the limit does not require $f(2) = 7$; the function could be undefined at $x = 2$ or could have a different value there. Therefore I is not necessarily true. The correct answer is II and III only.
