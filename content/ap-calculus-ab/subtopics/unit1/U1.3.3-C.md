If $f$ is continuous on $[0, 3]$ and $g$ is continuous on $[1, 4]$, on which of the following intervals is $f(x) + g(x)$ guaranteed to be continuous?

## Options
A) $[0, 4]$
B) $[1, 3]$
C) $[0, 3]$
D) $[1, 4]$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
The sum of two continuous functions is continuous on the intersection of their domains of continuity. $f$ is continuous on $[0, 3]$ and $g$ is continuous on $[1, 4]$. The intersection is $[0, 3] \cap [1, 4] = [1, 3]$. Therefore, $f(x) + g(x)$ is guaranteed to be continuous on $[1, 3]$.

Option A is incorrect because neither function is guaranteed continuous on all of $[0, 4]$ (e.g., $g$ may not be continuous on $[3, 4]$ and $f$ may not be continuous on $[0, 1)$). Options C and D are supersets of the guaranteed interval.
