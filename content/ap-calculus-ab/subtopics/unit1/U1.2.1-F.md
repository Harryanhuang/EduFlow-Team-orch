What is $\lim_{x \to 3} (2x^2 - 5x + 1)$?

## Options
A) 2
B) 4
C) 10
D) 22

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Since $f(x) = 2x^2 - 5x + 1$ is a polynomial, we can use direct substitution:
$\lim_{x \to 3} (2x^2 - 5x + 1) = 2(3)^2 - 5(3) + 1 = 18 - 15 + 1 = 4$.
