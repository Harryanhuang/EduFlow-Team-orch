Find the minimum distance from the point (0,1) to the parabola y=4-x^2.

## Options
A) 11/4
B) sqrt(11)/4
C) 3
D) sqrt(11)/2

## Answer
D

## Explanation
Let the point on the parabola be (x, 4-x^2). Distance to (0,1): D=sqrt(x^2+(4-x^2-1)^2)=sqrt(x^2+(3-x^2)^2)=sqrt(x^2+9-6x^2+x^4)=sqrt(x^4-5x^2+9). Minimizing D is equivalent to minimizing f(x)=x^4-5x^2+9. f'(x)=4x^3-10x=2x(2x^2-5). Setting f'(x)=0 gives x=0 or x=±sqrt(5/2). f(0)=9, f(±sqrt(5/2))=(25/4)-25/2+9=25/4-50/4+36/4=11/4. The minimum is 11/4, so D_min=sqrt(11/4)=sqrt(11)/2. Option D is correct. Option A is the minimum of f(x), not D. Option B incorrectly computes sqrt(11/4). Option C is f(0), not the minimum.
