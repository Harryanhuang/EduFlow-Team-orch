According to the First Derivative Test, which statement is correct?

## Options
A) If f' changes from positive to negative at c, then f has a local minimum at c.
B) If f' changes from negative to positive at c, then f has a local maximum at c.
C) If f' changes from negative to positive at c, then f has a local minimum at c.
D) If f' does not change sign at c, then f has a local minimum at c.

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The First Derivative Test states that if f' changes from negative to positive at a critical point c, then f has a local minimum at c. Option C states this correctly. Option A is wrong because f' changing from positive to negative indicates a local maximum, not minimum. Option B is wrong because f' changing from negative to positive indicates a local minimum, not maximum. Option D is wrong because if f' does not change sign, there is no local extremum.
