Let f(x)=x^3+3x on [-2,2]. Consider the following statements:
I. There exists c in (-2,2) such that f'(c)=4.
II. There exists c in (-2,2) such that f'(c)=0.
III. There exists c in (-2,2) such that f'(c)=(f(2)-f(-2))/(2-(-2)).
Which statements are true?

## Options
A) I only
B) II only
C) I and III only
D) I, II, and III

## Answer
C

## Explanation
The average rate of change is (f(2)-f(-2))/(2-(-2))=(8-(-8))/4=16/4=4. Since f'(x)=3x^2+3, solving 3c^2+3=4 gives c=±1/√3, both in (-2,2). So statement I is true. For statement II, f'(x)=3x^2+3≥3>0, so no c satisfies f'(c)=0; statement II is false. Statement III says f'(c)=(f(2)-f(-2))/(2-(-2))=4, which is the MVT conclusion and is true. Statements I and III are both true, making C the correct answer.
