Let f(x)=x^4-4x^3. Consider:
I. f has a local minimum at x=0.
II. f has a local minimum at x=3.
III. The Second Derivative Test is inconclusive at x=0.
Which statements are true?

## Options
A) I and II only
B) I and III only
C) II and III only
D) I, II, and III

## Answer
C

## Explanation
f'(x)=4x^3-12x^2=4x^2(x-3). Critical points at x=0 and x=3. f''(x)=12x^2-24x=12x(x-2). f''(0)=0, so the Second Derivative Test is inconclusive at x=0. f''(3)=12(3)(1)=36>0, so x=3 is a local minimum. For x=0, we use the First Derivative Test: f'(-1)=-4-12=-16<0, f'(1)=4-12=-8<0. Since f' does not change sign at x=0, there is no local extremum at x=0. Statement I is false. Statement II is true. Statement III is true because f''(0)=0 makes the Second Derivative Test inconclusive. Therefore II and III are true, making C the correct answer.
