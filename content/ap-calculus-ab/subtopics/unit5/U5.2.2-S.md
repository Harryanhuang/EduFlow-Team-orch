Find the local minimum value of f(x)=x^3-3x^2+5.

## Options
A) 5
B) 1
C) 2
D) -1

## Answer
B

## Explanation
f'(x)=3x^2-6x=3x(x-2). Critical points at x=0 and x=2. Sign analysis: f'(-1)=3+6=9>0, f'(1)=3-6=-3<0, f'(3)=27-18=9>0. So f' changes from positive to negative at x=0 (local max) and from negative to positive at x=2 (local min). The local minimum value is f(2)=8-12+5=1. Option B is correct. Option A gives the local maximum value. Option C gives the x-coordinate of the local minimum, not the value. Option D is incorrect.
