Let f(x)=x^3-3x^2-9x+2. Using the First Derivative Test, consider:
I. f has a local maximum at x=-1.
II. f has a local minimum at x=-1.
III. f has a local minimum at x=3.
Which statements are true?

## Options
A) I only
B) II only
C) I and II only
D) I and III only

## Answer
D

## Explanation
f'(x)=3x^2-6x-9=3(x^2-2x-3)=3(x-3)(x+1). Critical points at x=-1 and x=3. f''(x)=6x-6. f''(-1)=-12<0, so x=-1 is a local maximum. f''(3)=12>0, so x=3 is a local minimum. But the question asks for the First Derivative Test, not the Second Derivative Test. Using the First Derivative Test: f'(-2)=3(4+4-3)=15>0, f'(0)=-9<0, f'(4)=3(16-8-3)=15>0. So f' changes from positive to negative at x=-1 (local max) and from negative to positive at x=3 (local min). Statement I is true. Statement II is false because x=-1 is a local maximum, not minimum. Statement III is true. Therefore I and III are true, making D the correct answer.
