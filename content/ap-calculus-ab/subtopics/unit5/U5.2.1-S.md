Determine the intervals on which f(x)=x^3-6x^2+9x is increasing and decreasing.

## Options
A) Increasing on (-∞,1), decreasing on (1,3) and (3,∞)
B) Increasing on (-∞,1) and (3,∞), decreasing on (1,3)
C) Increasing on (-∞,1), decreasing on (1,3)
D) Decreasing on (-∞,1), increasing on (1,3) and (3,∞)

## Answer
B

## Explanation
f'(x)=3x^2-12x+9=3(x^2-4x+3)=3(x-1)(x-3). The critical points are x=1 and x=3. Testing intervals: for x<1, f'(0)=9>0 so f is increasing; for 1<x<3, f'(2)=-3<0 so f is decreasing; for x>3, f'(4)=9>0 so f is increasing. Thus f is increasing on (-∞,1) and (3,∞), decreasing on (1,3). Option B is correct. Option A has the wrong interval for decreasing. Option C misses the interval (3,∞). Option D incorrectly states the function is decreasing on (-∞,1).
