Which sequence correctly describes the four-step optimization problem-solving method?

## Options
A) 1) Identify quantity and assign variables, 2) Write primary equation, 3) Use constraint to reduce variables, 4) Find critical points and verify.
B) 1) Find critical points, 2) Write primary equation, 3) Identify quantity, 4) Verify optimal solution.
C) 1) Assign variables, 2) Find critical points, 3) Write equation, 4) Verify.
D) 1) Find critical points, 2) Use constraint, 3) Write primary equation, 4) Verify.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The standard four-step optimization method is: 1) Identify the quantity to be optimized and assign variables, 2) Write a primary equation for the quantity to optimize, 3) If the primary equation has multiple variables, use a secondary equation (constraint) to reduce to one variable, 4) Find the critical points and verify the optimal solution. Option A lists these steps correctly. Option B has the steps out of order. Option C omits the constraint reduction step. Option D incorrectly suggests finding critical points before setting up the equation.
