Let f(x)=x^4+4x^3+5. Consider:
I. f has an inflection point at x=-2.
II. f has an inflection point at x=0.
III. If f''(c)=0, then f has an inflection point at x=c.
Which statements are true?

## Options
A) I only
B) II only
C) I, II, and III
D) I and II only

## Answer
D

## Explanation
f'(x)=4x^3+12x^2, f''(x)=12x^2+24x=12x(x+2). Setting f''(x)=0 gives x=0 and x=-2. Sign analysis: f''(-3)=12(-3)(-1)=36>0, f''(-1)=12(-1)(1)=-12<0, f''(1)=12(1)(3)=36>0. f'' changes sign at both x=-2 and x=0, so both are inflection points. Statements I and II are true. Statement III claims f''(c)=0 is sufficient for an inflection point, which is false in general (e.g., f(x)=x^4 at x=0 has f''(0)=0 but no sign change). Answer is D.
