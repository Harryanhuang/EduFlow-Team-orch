Let f(x)=x^4-4x^3+4x^2. Consider the following statements:
I. f has a local minimum at x=0.
II. f has a local maximum at x=1.
III. f has a local minimum at x=2.
Which statements are true?

## Options
A) I, II, and III
B) I and II only
C) II and III only
D) I and III only

## Answer
A

## Explanation
f'(x)=4x^3-12x^2+8x=4x(x^2-3x+2)=4x(x-1)(x-2). Critical points at x=0,1,2. Sign analysis: f'(-1)=-4(-2)(-3)=-24<0, f'(0.5)=2(0.5)(-0.5)(-1.5)>0, f'(1.5)=6(1.5)(0.5)(-0.5)<0, f'(3)=12(3)(2)(1)>0. So f is decreasing on (-∞,0), increasing on (0,1), decreasing on (1,2), increasing on (2,∞). For statement I: f is decreasing on (-∞,0) and increasing on (0,1), so x=0 is a local minimum; true. For statement II: f is increasing on (0,1) and decreasing on (1,2), so x=1 is a local maximum; true. For statement III: f is decreasing on (1,2) and increasing on (2,∞), so x=2 is a local minimum; true. All three statements are true, so A is correct.
