Let f(x)=x^3 on [1,5]. By the Mean Value Theorem, there exists c in (1,5) such that f'(c) equals the average rate of change. What is the value of c?

## Options
A) sqrt(31/3)
B) 31
C) 0
D) 124

## Answer
A

## Explanation
The average rate of change is (f(5)-f(1))/(5-1)=(125-1)/4=124/4=31. By MVT, there exists c in (1,5) with f'(c)=31. Since f'(x)=3x^2, solve 3c^2=31 to get c^2=31/3, so c=sqrt(31/3) which lies in (1,5). Option A is correct. Option B gives the average rate itself. Option C solves f'(c)=0. Option D solves f'(c)=f(5)-f(1) without dividing by the interval length.
