In an optimization problem with a constraint, what is the standard AP Calculus AB approach?

## Options
A) Solve the constraint for one variable and substitute into the objective function.
B) Use Lagrange multipliers to find the optimal solution.
C) Differentiate the constraint equation to find critical points.
D) Add the constraint to the objective function and differentiate.

## Answer
A

## Explanation
When an optimization problem has a constraint equation relating variables, the typical method is to solve the constraint for one variable and substitute into the objective function, reducing the problem to a single variable. Option A describes this correctly. Option B describes Lagrange multipliers, which is beyond AP Calculus AB. Option C is incorrect because constraints are used to reduce variables, not to find critical points directly. Option D is incorrect because you don't differentiate the constraint itself.
