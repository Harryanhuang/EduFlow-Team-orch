$\displaystyle\int \frac{\ln x}{x}\,dx =$

## Options
A) $\dfrac{(\ln x)^2}{2}+C$
B) $\dfrac{1}{x^2}+C$
C) $\ln(x^2)+C$
D) $\dfrac{\ln x}{x^2}+C$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Let $u=\ln x$, so $du=\frac{1}{x}\,dx$. Then $\int \frac{\ln x}{x}\,dx = \int u\,du = \frac{u^2}{2}+C = \frac{(\ln x)^2}{2}+C$.
