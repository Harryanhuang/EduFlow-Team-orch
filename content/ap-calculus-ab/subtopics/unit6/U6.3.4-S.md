$\displaystyle\int_0^{\pi/2} \cos^2 x\sin x\,dx =$

## Options
A) $-\dfrac{1}{3}$
B) $-1$
C) $\dfrac{1}{3}$
D) $1$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Let $u=\cos x$, so $du=-\sin x\,dx$. When $x=0$, $u=1$; when $x=\pi/2$, $u=0$. Then $\int_0^{\pi/2} \cos^2 x\sin x\,dx = \int_1^0 u^2(-\,du)=\int_0^1 u^2\,du=\Bigl[\frac{u^3}{3}\Bigr]_0^1=\frac{1}{3}$.
