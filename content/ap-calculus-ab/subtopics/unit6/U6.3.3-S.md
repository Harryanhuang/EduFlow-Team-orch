$\displaystyle\int_0^{\pi} \sin^2 x\,dx =$

## Options
A) $\pi$
B) $\dfrac{\pi}{2}$
C) $0$
D) $2$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Use the identity $\sin^2 x = \frac{1-\cos(2x)}{2}$. Then $\int_0^{\pi} \sin^2 x\,dx = \int_0^{\pi} \frac{1-\cos(2x)}{2}\,dx = \frac{1}{2}\Bigl[x-\frac{\sin(2x)}{2}\Bigr]_0^{\pi}=\frac{1}{2}(\pi)=\frac{\pi}{2}$.
