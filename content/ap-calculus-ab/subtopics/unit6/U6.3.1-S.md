$\displaystyle\int \frac{x^2+1}{x}\,dx =$

## Options
A) $\dfrac{x^2}{2}+\ln x+C$
B) $\dfrac{x^2}{2}+\ln|x|+C$
C) $\dfrac{x^2}{2}+\dfrac{1}{x^2}+C$
D) $x^2+\ln|x|+C$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Rewrite and integrate term by term: $\int\frac{x^2+1}{x}\,dx = \int\Bigl(x+\frac{1}{x}\Bigr)\,dx = \frac{x^2}{2}+\ln|x|+C$.
