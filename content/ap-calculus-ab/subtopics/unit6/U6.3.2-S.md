$\displaystyle\int_0^2 \frac{x}{x^2+4}\,dx =$

## Options
A) $\ln 2$
B) $\ln 8-\ln 4$
C) $\dfrac{1}{2}\ln 8$
D) $\dfrac{1}{2}\ln 2$

## Answer
D

## Explanation
Let $u=x^2+4$, so $du=2x\,dx$ and $x\,dx=\frac{du}{2}$. When $x=0$, $u=4$; when $x=2$, $u=8$. The integral becomes $\int_4^8 \frac{1}{u}\cdot\frac{du}{2}=\frac{1}{2}\bigl[\ln|u|\bigr]_4^8=\frac{1}{2}(\ln 8-\ln 4)=\frac{1}{2}\ln 2$.
