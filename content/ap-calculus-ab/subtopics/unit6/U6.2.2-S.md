What is the value of $\displaystyle\int_0^{\pi/4} \sec^2 x\,dx$?

## Options
A) 0
B) $\frac{1}{2}$
C) 1
D) $\sqrt{2}$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Since $\frac{d}{dx}\bigl(\tan x\bigr)=\sec^2 x$, an antiderivative of $\sec^2 x$ is $\tan x$. Thus $\int_0^{\pi/4} \sec^2 x\,dx = \bigl[\tan x\bigr]_0^{\pi/4}=1-0=1$.
