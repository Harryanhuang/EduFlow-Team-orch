If $f(x)=\int_0^{x^2} \sqrt{1+t^3}\,dt$, then $f'(x)=$

## Options
A) $\sqrt{1+x^3}$
B) $2x\sqrt{1+x^6}$
C) $2x\sqrt{1+x^3}$
D) $\sqrt{1+x^6}$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Let $F(x)=\int_0^x \sqrt{1+t^3}\,dt$. By FTC Part 1 and the chain rule, $\frac{d}{dx}\bigl[F(x^2)\bigr]=F'(x^2)\cdot 2x = \sqrt{1+(x^2)^3}\cdot 2x = 2x\sqrt{1+x^6}$.
