The table gives selected values of a continuous, increasing function $f$ on $[0,2]$:

$x$: 0, 0.5, 1, 1.5, 2
$f(x)$: 1, 1.6487, 2.7183, 4.4817, 7.3891

What is the trapezoidal sum approximation of $\int_0^2 f(x)\,dx$ using these subintervals?

## Options
A) 5.896
B) 6.389
C) 7.389
D) 8.896

## Answer
A

## Explanation
On $[0,2]$, $\Delta x=0.5$ and the subintervals are $[0,0.5],[0.5,1],[1,1.5],[1.5,2]$. The trapezoidal rule gives $\frac{0.5}{2}\bigl[f(0)+2f(0.5)+2f(1)+2f(1.5)+f(2)\bigr]=0.25\bigl[1+2(1.6487)+2(2.7183)+2(4.4817)+7.3891\bigr]=0.25(23.5854)=5.896$.
