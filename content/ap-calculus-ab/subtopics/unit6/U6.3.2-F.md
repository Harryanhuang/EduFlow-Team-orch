$\displaystyle\int x(3x^2+1)^4\,dx =$

## Options
A) $\dfrac{(3x^2+1)^5}{5}+C$
B) $\dfrac{(3x^2+1)^5}{30}+C$
C) $\dfrac{(3x^2+1)^5}{6}+C$
D) $5(3x^2+1)^4+C$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Let $u=3x^2+1$, so $du=6x\,dx$ and $x\,dx=\frac{du}{6}$. Then $\int x(3x^2+1)^4\,dx = \int u^4\,\frac{du}{6}=\frac{u^5}{30}+C=\frac{(3x^2+1)^5}{30}+C$.
