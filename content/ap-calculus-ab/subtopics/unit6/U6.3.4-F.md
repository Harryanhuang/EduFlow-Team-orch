$\displaystyle\int_0^2 \frac{x}{x^2+1}\,dx =$

## Options
A) $\ln 5$
B) $\dfrac{1}{2}\ln 5$
C) $\dfrac{1}{2}\ln 2$
D) $2\ln 5$

## Answer
B

## Explanation
Let $u=x^2+1$, so $du=2x\,dx$. When $x=0$, $u=1$; when $x=2$, $u=5$. The integral becomes $\int_1^5 \frac{1}{u}\cdot\frac{du}{2}=\frac{1}{2}\bigl[\ln|u|\bigr]_1^5=\frac{1}{2}\ln 5$.
