Let $g(x)=\int_2^x \sin(t^2)\,dt$. What is the value of $g'(\sqrt{\pi})$?

## Options
A) 0
B) 1
C) $-1$
D) $\sqrt{\pi}$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
By the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus Part 1, if $g(x)=\int_2^x \sin(t^2)\,dt$, then $g'(x)=\sin(x^2)$. Therefore $g'(\sqrt{\pi})=\sin(\pi)=0$.
