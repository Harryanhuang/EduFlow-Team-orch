If $h(x)=\int_{x}^{x^2} e^{t^2}\,dt$, then $h'(x)=$

## Options
A) $e^{x^4}-e^{x^2}$
B) $2x\,e^{x^4}$
C) $2x\,e^{x^4}-e^{x^2}$
D) $e^{x^2}(2x-1)$

## Answer
C

## Explanation
Write $h(x)=\int_{x}^{x^2} e^{t^2}\,dt = \int_{0}^{x^2} e^{t^2}\,dt - \int_{0}^{x} e^{t^2}\,dt$. Differentiating with FTC Part 1 and the chain rule: $h'(x)=e^{(x^2)^2}\cdot 2x - e^{x^2}=2x\,e^{x^4}-e^{x^2}$.
