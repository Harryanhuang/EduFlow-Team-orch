$\displaystyle\int \frac{e^{\sqrt{x}}}{\sqrt{x}}\,dx =$

## Options
A) $2e^{\sqrt{x}}+C$
B) $e^{\sqrt{x}}+C$
C) $2\sqrt{x}\,e^{\sqrt{x}}+C$
D) $\dfrac{e^{\sqrt{x}}}{2}+C$

## Answer
A

## Explanation
Let $u=\sqrt{x}$, so $u^2=x$ and $2u\,du=dx$. Then $\int \frac{e^{\sqrt{x}}}{\sqrt{x}}\,dx = \int \frac{e^u}{u}\cdot 2u\,du = 2\int e^u\,du = 2e^u+C = 2e^{\sqrt{x}}+C$.
