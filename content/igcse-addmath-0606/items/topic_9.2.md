Question: Convert to Cartesian: x = 3t, y = t² + 1.
Answer: y = (x/3)² + 1 = x²/9 + 1
Difficulty: F

**Item 2 [F]**
Question: Locus of points equidistant from A(1, 2) and B(4, 6).
Answer: 3x + 4y = 23.5
Difficulty: F
Topic: 9.2
Explanation: Equidistant means PA = PB. Squaring both sides: (x−1)²+(y−2)² = (x−4)²+(y−6)² → x²−2x+1+y²−4y+4 = x²−8x+16+y²−12y+36 → −2x−4y+5 = −8x−12y+52 → 6x+8y = 47. Solving correctly: Left side: x²+y²−2x−4y+5. Right side: x²+y²−8x−12y+52. Equating: −2x−4y+5 = −8x−12y+52 → 6x+8y = 47. So 3x+4y = 23.5. Tags: locus, equidistant, perpendicular bisector
**Item 3 [S]**
Question: Cartesian equation of x = 2 cos θ, y = 3 sin θ.
Answer: x²/4 + y²/9 = 1
Difficulty: S
Topic: 9.2
Explanation: cos θ = x/2, sin θ = y/3. Using cos²θ + sin²θ = 1: (x/2)² + (y/3)² = 1 → x²/4 + y²/9 = 1. This is an ellipse.
Tags: parametric equations, ellipse, Cartesian

**Item 5 [S]**
Question: x = t + 1/t, y = t − 1/t. Find Cartesian equation.
Answer: x² − y² = 4

Explanation: x + y = 2t, x − y = 2/t. Multiply: (x+y)(x−y) = 4 → x² − y² = 4.

**Item 6 [S]**
Question: Locus of points equidistant from y = 3 and (0, −1).
Answer: Parabola y = 1 − x²/8
Difficulty: S
Topic: 9.2
Explanation: Distance to the line y = 3 is |y − 3|. Distance to point (0, −1) is √(x²+(y+1)²). Setting equal: |y − 3| = √(x²+(y+1)²). Squaring: (y−3)² = x²+(y+1)² → y²−6y+9 = x²+y²+2y+1 → −6y+9 = x²+2y+1 → −8y = x²−8 → y = 1 − x²/8. So the locus is the parabola y = 1 − x²/8.
Tags: locus, parabola, definition
**Item 7 [C]**
Question: x = 2 sin θ, y = cos 2θ. Find dy/dx.
Answer: dy/dx = −2 sin θ / cos θ = −2 tan θ
Difficulty: C
Topic: 9.2
Explanation: dx/dθ = 2 cos θ, dy/dθ = −2 sin θ. dy/dx = (dy/dθ)/(dx/dθ) = (−2 sin θ)/(2 cos θ) = −tan θ.
Tags: parametric equations, derivative, differentiation
**Item 8 [C]**
Question: Tangent to x = t², y = t³ at t = 2.
Answer: 3x − y = 4
Difficulty: C
Topic: 9.2
Explanation: At t = 2: x = 4, y = 8. dx/dt = 2t = 4, dy/dt = 3t² = 12. dy/dx = (dy/dt)/(dx/dt) = 12/4 = 3. Tangent equation: y − 8 = 3(x − 4) → y − 8 = 3x − 12 → 3x − y = 4.
Tags: parametric equations, tangent, differentiation
**Item 9 [C]**
Question: Locus where product of distances from (a, 0) and (−a, 0) equals a².
Answer: x⁴ + y⁴ + 2x²y² − 4a²x² = 0
Difficulty: C
Topic: 9.2
Explanation: √((x−a)²+y²) × √((x+a)²+y²) = a² → ((x−a)²+y²)((x+a)²+y²) = a⁴ → Expand: (x²−2ax+a²+y²)(x²+2ax+a²+y²) = a⁴. This is (x²+y²+a²)² − (2ax)² = a⁴ → (x²+y²+a²)² − 4a²x² = a⁴ → (x²+y²)² + 2a²(x²+y²) + a⁴ − 4a²x² = a⁴ → (x²+y²)² + 2a²(x²+y²) − 4a²x² = 0. QED.
Tags: locus, product of distances, complex locus
