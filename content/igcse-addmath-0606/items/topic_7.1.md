**Item 1 [F]**
Question: Without using a calculator, find the exact value of sin 30° + cos 60°.
Answer: 1
Difficulty: F

Explanation: sin 30° = 1/2 and cos 60° = 1/2. So sin 30° + cos 60° = 1/2 + 1/2 = 1.

**Item 3 [S]**
Question: Without a calculator, find tan 60° × sin 30° × sec 45°.
Answer: √6/2
Difficulty: S
Topic: 7.1
Explanation: tan 60° = √3, sin 30° = 1/2, sec 45° = 1/cos 45° = 1/(√2/2) = √2. So tan 60° × sin 30° × sec 45° = √3 × (1/2) × √2 = √6/2.

**Item 5 [S]**
Question: Given sin θ = 3/5 and θ is acute, find cos θ.
Answer: 4/5
Difficulty: S
Topic: 7.1
Explanation: Using the Pythagorean identity sin²θ + cos²θ = 1: cos²θ = 1 − (3/5)² = 1 − 9/25 = 16/25. Since θ is acute, cos θ > 0, so cos θ = 4/5.
Tags: trigonometry, Pythagorean identity, acute angle, finding cos from sin
**Item 6 [S]**
Question: Prove that sin²θ/(1 − sin²θ) = tan²θ.
Answer: sin²θ/(1 − sin²θ) = sin²θ/cos²θ = tan²θ ✓
Difficulty: S
Topic: 7.1
Explanation: Using the Pythagorean identity 1 − sin²θ = cos²θ. So sin²θ/(1 − sin²θ) = sin²θ/cos²θ = (sin θ/cos θ)² = tan²θ. QED.
Tags: trigonometry, Pythagorean identity, trigonometric identities, proof
**Item 7 [C]**
Question: Solve 2 sin²θ + cos θ = 1 for 0° ≤ θ ≤ 360°.
Answer: θ = 0°, 120°, 240°, 360°
Difficulty: C
Topic: 7.1
Explanation: Using sin²θ = 1 − cos²θ: 2(1 − cos²θ) + cos θ = 1 → 2 − 2cos²θ + cos θ = 1 → 2cos²θ − cos θ − 1 = 0 → (2cos θ + 1)(cos θ − 1) = 0. So cos θ = 1 or cos θ = −1/2. cos θ = 1 → θ = 0°, 360°. cos θ = −1/2 → θ = 120°, 240°. Total: 4 solutions.
Tags: trigonometry, solving trig equations, Pythagorean identity, quadratic in cos
**Item 8 [C]**
Question: cos x = −1/3, x in quadrant II. Find exact value of sin 2x.
Answer: −4√2/9
Difficulty: C
Topic: 7.1
Explanation: In QII, sin x > 0. sin²x + cos²x = 1 → sin²x = 1 − 1/9 = 8/9 → sin x = √8/3 = 2√2/3. sin 2x = 2 sin x cos x = 2 × (2√2/3) × (−1/3) = −4√2/9.
Tags: trigonometry, double angle formula, exact values, quadrant II
**Item 9 [C]**
Question: Prove: (sec θ − tan θ)(sec θ + tan θ) = 1.
Answer: (sec θ − tan θ)(sec θ + tan θ) = sec²θ − tan²θ = 1 + tan²θ − tan²θ = 1 ✓
Difficulty: C
Topic: 7.1
Explanation: Using the difference of two squares: (sec θ − tan θ)(sec θ + tan θ) = sec²θ − tan²θ. Using the Pythagorean identity for trigonometry: 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ. Therefore sec²θ − tan²θ = 1 + tan²θ − tan²θ = 1. QED.
Tags: trigonometry, Pythagorean identity, difference of squares, identity proof
