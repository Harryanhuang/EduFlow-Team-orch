# Topic 9.2 — Parametric Equations and Loci

## Basic Info
- **Topic ID:** 9.2
- **Domain:** Coordinate Geometry
- **Core/Supplement:** Supplement
- **Weight in exam:** Medium (Paper 2)

## Knowledge Points
1. Parametric equations: x = f(t), y = g(t)
2. Converting parametric to Cartesian (eliminate the parameter)
3. Sketching curves from parametric equations
4. Equation of tangent from parametric form
5. Loci: set of points satisfying a given condition
6. Standard loci: circle, perpendicular bisector, angle bisector, parabola
7. Combined loci problems
8. Parametric equations of standard curves

## Key Formulas / Methods
- To eliminate parameter t: express t in terms of x or y, or use trig identities
- For locus problems, translate the geometric condition into algebraic form
- Distance from a fixed point: √((x−a)² + (y−b)²) = constant

## Common Errors
- Incorrect elimination of the parameter
- Forgetting domain restrictions when eliminating
- Not expressing the locus condition correctly algebraically
- Confusing parametric curves with Cartesian forms

## Difficulty Band: F:2|S:4|C:3
- F: convert simple parametric to Cartesian, identify basic loci
- S: parametric equations of standard curves, tangents at parametric points
- C: complex loci, combined conditions, curve sketching from params
