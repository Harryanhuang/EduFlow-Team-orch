# Topic 6.1 — The Binomial Expansion

## Basic Info
- **Topic ID:** 6.1
- **Domain:** The Binomial Expansion
- **Core/Supplement:** Core
- **Weight in exam:** Medium (Paper 1 and Paper 2)

## Knowledge Points
1. Binomial theorem for positive integer n: (a + b)^n = Σ C(n,r) a^(n−r) b^r
2. Binomial coefficients: nCr = n!/(r!(n−r)!)
3. Pascal's triangle and symmetry: nCr = nC(n−r)
4. General term: T_(r+1) = nCr a^(n−r) b^r
5. Expansions in ascending powers of x for (1 + x)^n
6. Finding coefficients of specific terms
7. Using approximations with binomial expansion

## Key Formulas
- (a + b)^n = Σ C(n,r) a^(n−r) b^r for integer n ≥ 0
- C(n,r) = n!/(r!(n−r)!)
- T_(r+1) = nCr a^(n−r) b^r

## Common Errors
- Forgetting to include all terms in the expansion
- Incorrectly calculating nCr values
- Not applying the formula to the right values of a and b
- Confusing r (term number from 0) with T_r

## Difficulty Band: F:2|S:4|C:3
- F: expand simple binomials, use Pascal's triangle
- S: find specific coefficients, expand (1+x)^n
- C: general term problems, combinations, approximations
