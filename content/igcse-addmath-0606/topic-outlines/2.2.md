# Topic 2.2 — Exponential and Logarithmic Equations

## Basic Info
- **Topic ID:** 2.2
- **Domain:** Logarithms and Exponentials
- **Core/Supplement:** Core
- **Weight in exam:** Medium-High (Paper 1 and Paper 2)

## Knowledge Points
1. Solving exponential equations using logarithms
2. Solving equations of the form aˣ = b where a > 0
3. Solving logarithmic equations using laws of logs
4. Change of base formula: logₐx = (logₜx)/(logₜa)
5. Solving simultaneous equations involving exponentials and logarithms
6. Applications of exponential and log equations (growth, decay)

## Key Formulas
- To solve aˣ = b: take log both sides → x log a = log b → x = (log b)/(log a)
- Change of base: logₐx = log x / log a (any base, commonly 10 or e)
- logₐb × logₐc = logₐ(b^c) is NOT a valid law

## Common Errors
- Forgetting to apply log to both sides of an equation
- Applying log to individual terms instead of the whole expression
- Incorrectly assuming log(a+b) = log a + log b
- Not checking solutions in logarithmic equations (logs require positive arguments)

## Difficulty Band: F:2|S:4|C:3
- F: solve simple exponential equations, convert log equations to exponential
- S: solve equations requiring log laws, use change of base
- C: solve complex exponential equations, solve simultaneous equations with logs
