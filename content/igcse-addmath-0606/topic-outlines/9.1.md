# Topic 9.1 — Equation of a Straight Line and Circle

## Basic Info
- **Topic ID:** 9.1
- **Domain:** Coordinate Geometry
- **Core/Supplement:** Core
- **Weight in exam:** Medium (Paper 1 and Paper 2)

## Knowledge Points
1. Equation of straight line: y = mx + c, gradient-intercept form
2. General form: ax + by + c = 0
3. Gradient m = (y₂ − y₁)/(x₂ − x₁)
4. Equation from gradient and point: y − y₁ = m(x − x₁)
5. Perpendicular lines: m₁ · m₂ = −1
6. Parallel lines: m₁ = m₂
7. Angle between two lines
8. Equation of a circle: (x − a)² + (y − b)² = r²
9. General form: x² + y² + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0, centre (−g, −f), radius = √(g²+f²−c)
10. Intersection of line and circle

## Key Formulas
- Circle: (x − a)² + (y − b)² = r²
- General circle: x² + y² + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0
- Centre = (−g, −f), radius = √(g² + f² − c)

## Common Errors
- Confusing centre (a, b) with equation form (x − a)² + (y − b)² = r²
- Forgetting to square the radius when writing equation
- Not completing the square correctly when converting general form
- Mixing up perpendicular gradient condition

## Difficulty Band: F:2|S:4|C:3
- F: find equation of line given two points, write circle equation
- S: perpendicular/parallel lines, line-circle intersection
- C: hardest circle problems, combined line and circle geometry
