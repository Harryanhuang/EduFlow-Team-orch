# Topic 5.2 — Sum to Infinity and Convergence

## Basic Info
- **Topic ID:** 5.2
- **Domain:** Sequences and Series
- **Core/Supplement:** Supplement
- **Weight in exam:** Medium-High (Paper 2)

## Knowledge Points
1. Condition for convergence: |r| < 1 for geometric series
2. Sum to infinity of geometric series: S_∞ = a/(1 − r) for |r| < 1
3. Divergence when |r| ≥ 1: no finite sum
4. Applications: repeating decimals, infinite geometric constructions
5. Using S_∞ to find r given a and S_∞
6. Relationship between consecutive terms: T_n/T_(n−1) → r as n → ∞

## Key Formulas
- S_∞ = a/(1 − r) (valid when |r| < 1)
- |r| ≥ 1 → series diverges (no sum to infinity)

## Common Errors
- Applying S_∞ formula when |r| ≥ 1
- Forgetting that a is always the first term, not any other term
- Confusing sum to infinity with partial sums
- Not checking the convergence condition

## Difficulty Band: F:2|S:4|C:3
- F: evaluate S_∞ given a and r, identify convergence
- S: find missing parameters using S_∞ formula, repeating decimals
- C: proof of convergence condition, applications to limits
