# Topic 2.1 — Laws of Logarithms and Exponentials

## Basic Info
- **Topic ID:** 2.1
- **Domain:** Logarithms and Exponentials
- **Core/Supplement:** Core
- **Weight in exam:** Medium (Paper 1 and Paper 2)

## Knowledge Points
1. Laws of indices: aᵐ × aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ, aᵐ ÷ aⁿ = aᵐ⁻ⁿ, (aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ
2. Zero and negative indices: a⁰ = 1, a⁻ⁿ = 1/aⁿ
3. Fractional indices: a^(m/n) = ⁿ√(aᵐ)
4. Exponential functions: y = aˣ, properties, graphs
5. Logarithms: definition, y = logₐx ⟺ aʸ = x
6. Laws of logarithms: log(ab) = log a + log b, log(a/b) = log a − log b, log(aⁿ) = n log a
7. Using logarithms to solve exponential equations

## Key Formulas
- aᵐ × aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ
- aᵐ ÷ aⁿ = aᵐ⁻ⁿ
- (aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ
- logₐ(aᵐ) = m
- a^(logₐx) = x
- log(ab) = log a + log b
- log(a/b) = log a − log b
- log(aⁿ) = n log a

## Common Errors
- Confusing log(a+b) with log a + log b
- Forgetting that log without base implies base 10 or base e
- Not applying laws of logs correctly in both directions
- Mixing up log and exponential forms

## Difficulty Band: F:2|S:4|C:3
- F: simplify indices, convert between log and exponential form
- S: use laws of logs, solve simple exponential equations
- C: solve complex exponential equations using logs, prove log identities
