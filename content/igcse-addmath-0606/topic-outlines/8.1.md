# Topic 8.1 — Differentiation: First Principles and Polynomial Derivatives

## Basic Info
- **Topic ID:** 8.1
- **Domain:** Calculus
- **Core/Supplement:** Core
- **Weight in exam:** High (Paper 1 & 2)

## Knowledge Points
1. Limit definition of derivative: f'(x) = lim[h→0] [f(x+h) - f(x)] / h
2. First principles differentiation of simple polynomials
3. Power rule: d/dx(xⁿ) = nxⁿ⁻¹
4. Constant rule: d/dx(c) = 0
5. Sum/difference rule: d/dx(f ± g) = f' ± g'
6. Product rule: d/dx(uv) = u·v' + v·u'
7. Quotient rule: d/dx(u/v) = (v·u' - u·v') / v²
8. Chain rule: d/dx(f(g(x))) = f'(g(x)) · g'(x)

## Key Formulas
- Power rule: d/dx(xⁿ) = nxⁿ⁻¹
- Product rule: d/dx(uv) = u·v' + v·u'
- Quotient rule: d/dx(u/v) = (v·u' - u·v') / v²
- Chain rule: dy/dx = dy/du · du/dx

## Common Errors
- Forgetting to apply chain rule to composite functions
- Sign errors in quotient rule numerator
- Confusing f'(x) notation with f(x) notation
- Not simplifying final answers

## Difficulty Band: Hard (F:2|S:4|C:3)
- F: power rule on monomials, basic polynomials
- S: product rule, quotient rule, chain rule on powers
- C: chain rule on trig/exponential, nested composite functions
