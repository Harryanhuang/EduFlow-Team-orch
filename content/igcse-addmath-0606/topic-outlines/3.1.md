# Topic 3.1 — Quadratic Equations

## Basic Info
- **Topic ID:** 3.1
- **Domain:** Quadratics and Polynomials
- **Core/Supplement:** Core
- **Weight in exam:** High (Paper 1 & 2)

## Knowledge Points
1. Solution by factorisation (two linear factors)
2. Solution by quadratic formula: x = (-b ± √(b²-4ac))/2a
3. Completing the square: (x + p)² = q → x = -p ± √q
4. Nature of roots: discriminant b²-4ac > 0 (two real), = 0 (equal), < 0 (no real)
5. Sum and product of roots: α+β = -b/a, αβ = c/a
6. Forming equations given roots

## Key Formulas
- Quadratic formula: x = (-b ± √(b²-4ac)) / 2a
- Discriminant: Δ = b² - 4ac
- Sum of roots: α + β = -b/a
- Product of roots: αβ = c/a

## Common Errors
- Forgetting to check for common factors before factorising
- Sign errors in completing the square
- Incorrectly applying the ± sign
- Using discriminant incorrectly (mixing up >0 vs <0)

## Difficulty Band: Medium (F:2|S:4|C:3)
- F: factorise to find roots, simple integers
- S: quadratic formula with surds, completing the square with fractions
- C: discriminant analysis, sum/product of roots, forming equations
