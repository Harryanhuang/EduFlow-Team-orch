# Topic 5.1 — Arithmetic and Geometric Sequences and Series
## QA Question Level File

**1. [F]** Find the 10th term of the arithmetic sequence 3, 7, 11, ...
**2. [F]** Find the sum of the first 8 terms of the arithmetic series 2 + 5 + 8 + ...
**3. [S]** The 3rd term of a geometric sequence is 12 and the 6th term is 96. Find the common ratio.
**4. [S]** Find the sum of the first 6 terms of the geometric series 3 + 6 + 12 + 24 + ...
**5. [S]** An arithmetic sequence has first term 5 and common difference 3. Find the smallest n for which the sum exceeds 200.
**6. [S]** Insert two arithmetic means between 4 and 10.
**7. [C]** The sum of three consecutive terms of an arithmetic sequence is 21, and their product is 336. Find the terms.
**8. [C]** A geometric series has first term 5 and common ratio 2. Find the least number of terms required for the sum to exceed 1000.
**9. [C]** Prove that the arithmetic mean of two positive numbers a and b is greater than or equal to their geometric mean.
